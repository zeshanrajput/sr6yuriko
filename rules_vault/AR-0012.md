## Item 3: Report from Dizzy D 

These things are tight. They put a lot of work on the frame, so that thing will hold up. Suspension’s good, too. I think I could get 5,000 kilos in there, if I had something dense enough. Space is good, too. Yeah, it’s not a semitruck, but six of them could carry about what a semi-trailer carries, and if they have fifty on the road at all times, lining up six is possible. Or you use semis for your big runs, these guys for your smaller ones. 

The customizability is really impressive. The folding seats are no real trick, but the nanotech shelving that can extend from the sides is pretty slick. Sturdy, too—I’d say you could get two to three hundred kilos on each one. 

But my favorite part of this thing is the top. Built into the roof are six separate insulated compartments, two rows of three compartments each. The panels covering each compartment fold up with a simple command. The compartments keep hot things hot and cold things cold—they can even add a little heat or cooling, depending on what the compartment contains and the weather outside. In front of these compartments are two more compartments, each one holding a modified Quadrotor drone. As the vehicle goes about its rounds, the Quadrotors can pick up and deliver foodstuffs, adding that service to taxi and parcel service. And the icing on the cake is the Crawler drone that sits underneath the van most of the time. What this means is you’re riding along and you see a Taco Temple and suddenly get really thirsty, you can quickly punch up an order for a Really Big Drink, a Quadrotor will fly out and pick it up, drop it in an insulated compartment (they have cup holders, natch), then you can open the compartment from beneath and fetch the drink. Are you driving and can’t get to the back? Then have the Crawler drone stored underneath the van emerge, enter the van by rolling down the back window, fetch the drink, and bring it to you. The level of customization and automation is impressive. 

Now, you asked me to give the basic rundown of what it does and not anything else, but I feel like I should pass on something one of my team members said when they saw me working on this: This is a Tamanous dream machine. It’s big and it’s customizable, meaning you could hold multiple bodies plus any needed equipment in there. The insulated compartments on top plus the extreme drone integration throughout means you could slide a body into the van at point A, watch the van drive off, and you’d have about 1 million nuyen worth of organs in the insulated compartments by the time it arrived at its destination. There are a million possible uses for these vans, and I have no evidence that this is one that anyone is considering. But my friend was impressed by the possibility and couldn’t stop talking about it, so I thought it was worth mentioning.
