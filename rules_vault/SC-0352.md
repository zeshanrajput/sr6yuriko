## Backgrounds

The face fatale can come from all walks of life. Rich or poor, elf or troll, male (“homme fatal”) or female (“femme fatale”) or other (“personne mortelle”), the face fatale is able to read peopleand change personalities like a chameleon to get them to do what they want. They could have had a rough childhood or a relatively easy one (they are running the shadows, after all). The face fatale should have something in their past that explains why they manipulate people like they do. It could be a specific event, a means of survival, or just how they grew up interacting with people.

The face fatale can come from a wide variety of backgrounds, but a few don’t fit very well. The face fatale is a social creature, and military or mercenary life isn’t a good fit. Combat isn’t their first option or probably even in their top five, and fraternization can often lead to punishment or worse. For a face fatale who somehow winds up in the military, a side jaunt to intelligence would be a better option; spies and face fatales are often in bed together, literally. Additionally, the resources needed to be a good hacker or magician are going to hamper the ability of the face fatale to be good at their job.

On the other hand, a corporate background will work well for a face fatale. The maxim “it’s not what you know but who you know” is very often true in the corporate world, where networking can be the key to short-term and long-term success. A corporate face fatale could be in the shadows due to a misstep that cost them a cozy corp position, or they might have decided to freelance and have more control over their future. The face fatale should still have a lot of friends and contacts in the corporate world, assets that could be very useful as a shadowrunner.

Another source of face fatales in the shadows is people rising straight from the streets. Surviving on the streets isn’t easy, but a face fatale can negotiate their way through the go-gangers, the slumlords, and the police without earning many enemies. The streets are often a primer for the shadows, as the two are closely linked and cross over in many ways. The face fatale from the streets is moving up in the world, increasing the risk but also the reward.

One of the primary tools of the face fatale is romance. This has many levels of commitment, from winking at the bartender to get a free drink, to flirting with the police officer to get out of a ticket, all the way to marrying a wealthy tycoon to get access to their bank accounts. Romance isn’t a fairy tale for them—it’s just another tool of the trade. Their personal feelings aren’t a consideration; if the target needs to be seduced, then they turn on the charm and make themselves into whatever their target needs them to be. Whether that means they become passive or aggressive, demure or forward, it’s about making the mark feel they’ve found what they are looking for. If they can get some enjoyment out of it, all the better. But in the end, it’s just a job, not a real relationship.
