## Frame Job

The Dissonant technomancer’s team is hired to frame a person for committing a crime. The employer would prefer that the nature of the crime would be limited to illegal Matrix activity. They are not interested in having the runners murder someone so they can pin the crime on the target. Ideally, the crime should be one that economically inconveniences a powerful authority, such as a megacorporation. That would provide the least risk of unintended consequences to innocent parties while also providing significant motivation for an authority to go after the target of this frame job.

The target is an up-and-coming executive in charge of several important Matrix research and development projects. The target is also a highly skilled decker or technomancer and is highly security-conscious. It will be difficult to lure them into a compromising situation or to plant false evidence that will hold up with enough strength to incriminate them. Such a person is not without enemies, however, which include rivals within their own corporation as well as outside of it.

The complicating twist to this run is that the target is aware that runners have been hired to act against them. The target believes the runners mean to extract them or steal their most critical research data, and they have hired a shadowrun team of their own to investigate, track, and if possible, eliminate the threat. The enemy team will immediately begin with some legwork, doing Matrix searches and checking with their contacts about the PCs. The PCs might be tipped off by a fixer or info broker contact that inquiries are being made about them. Resourceful PCs might be able to find out information about the team that is after them in return. After this, the opposing team will attempt to track down and observe the PCs without being observed. If a confrontation is forced, or if the PCs present them with a tempting opportunity for an ambush, the runner team will attempt to attack and either capture or kill them. They’ve been offered extra nuyen if they capture at least one of the runners alive.

For the opposing shadowrun team, use a mix of runners, but be sure to include a competent decker or technomancer or two.

If the PCs successfully complete this run, the dissonant technomancer may take either the Dissonance Diffusion or Dissonance Spike quality for 5 Karma (instead of 10). This should also be a relatively high-paying job for the PCs, with a nuyen payout roughly 150 percent of what they are accustomed to receiving.
