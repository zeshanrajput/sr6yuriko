## Broken Ground

At the end of summer of 2082, a Yakutian Ambassador-at-Large, a lynx shapeshifter named Lumas, moved into the mountains above Lake Frohlika on the far northeastern edge of Baikal. They brought with them a considerable force intent on not only breaking the Saban-Zaga’s hold over the lake, but also re-establishing a Yakutian presence.

- For obvious reasons, the Russians never acknowledged the Yakutians as being a legitimate nation. It’s been an uneasy

ceasefire ever since; at least around the lake. It was an open secret that every group, including the Saban Zaga, was backed by Russian military commanders, sympathetic oligarchs, or Voryfunded groups who sought access to the lake’s water rights. **>** Red Anya

Lumas called in assistance from another ambassador, named Arlen (a raven). Approximately a month later, Arlen came from the Akitka range northwest of the lake. The forces they marshaled between them effectively elevated the region’s political unrest to a stage not seen since the original war. Open conflict in the northern communities of Severobaikalsk and Nizhneangarsk caused evacuations and ended the light rail lines’ ability to connect the northern territories with the southern lake shed.
