## An Audience with the King

The team is hired to seek an audience with King Carnage, the nosferatu warlord boss of the Crimson Carnage in Miggon. This is a dangerous task, as the Crimson Carnage have not been known to make deals with anyone who they might consider to be food. Finding the lair is not difficult—it is a fortress built from the ruins of a shopping center, very close to one of the stops made by the Death Train. Getting near the lair isn’t too hard, though the likelihood of encountering a patrol of ghouls led by a vampire lieutenant is high. Getting into the lair and securing an audience without being fed on is just short of impossible. The runners may choose to fight their way in, which will require some intense combat. They may attempt to impress or intimidate the ghouls and vampires to be granted passage. Characters who are not Infected may not gain or spend Edge on any Social skill tests made against members of the Crimson Carnage. If the runners have an Infected contact who is willing to travel with them and handle negotiations, the runners may gain and spend Edge on Social skill tests.

Regardless of the method used, acquiring an audience with the king will require three successful

tests or efforts. These might be combat encounters against patrols or guards, attempts to intimidate or impress the vampire lieutenants, or trying to sneak into the lair. After succeeding at three of these tests or encounters, the runners are eventually granted an audience and are escorted (or manage to burst in) to the king’s throne room. In the case of a combat encounter, the runners are counted as successful only if none of them took physical damage. They don’t simply succeed by surviving—they must prove that they are not to be trifled with.

Then the runners must present their case to King Crimson, who will hear them out unless they attack him. King Crimson will find their visit to be an amusing pre-dinner diversion, and he is willing to wait to find out what brought them here before appreciating the food delivery. The runners must make an extended Social skill test using either Con or Influence + Charisma. The base threshold for this test is 5, increased by 1 for each time the runners failed in an attempt to secure an audience. The interval for the extended test is five minutes. If the runners roll a glitch, increase the threshold by 2. If the runners fail to reach the threshold within three tests, or if a critical glitch is rolled, King Carnage grows hungry and impatient and declares that dinner is served—and the runners are the main course! The only option at this point is to escape. If the runners achieve the threshold in time, King Carnage tells them that he will consider what they have said and offers them safe passage from his lair. As soon as the runners get outside, they will be attacked by hungry ghouls and a vampire, as the King never promised safe passage outside of the lair.

Whether the negotiations succeed or fail, the runners are paid in full if they managed to secure an audience with King Carnage—provided they are willing to share the details of their experience. If they failed to secure an audience but at least made a serious effort, they are only paid half the fee. If the runners decide to bring along an Infected NPC to help, the NPC will insist on receiving an equal share of the pay.
