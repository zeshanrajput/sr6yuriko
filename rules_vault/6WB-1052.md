## Prepping

## For a Game

One of the fastest ways for GMs, especially new ones, to bring their game to a screeching halt is to do little or no preparation for it. While every GM eventually develops their own game-running style, there are a few basics that everyone should follow, no matter what your skill level and experience are.

But when running a pre-generated (pre-gen) adventure, a GM should always _read the adventure first._ Doing so not only helps keep the game flowing, but also allows a GM to identify potential problems or obstacles that may come up. For example, an adventure with heavy combat may not be the adventure for a group or runner team with little to no combat skills.
