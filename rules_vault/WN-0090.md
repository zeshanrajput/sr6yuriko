## Jails and Prisons

Following an arrest, criminals are booked and held at the local NYPD precinct. Generally, these are small facilities only able to accommodate a few dozen inmates. After they appear before a judge, they are transferred to Randalls Island where they are held in one of the three minimum, or two moderate, security prisons that make up Randalls Island Correctional Center while they await trial. Convicted felons and other high-risk inmates are sent to Roosevelt Island and one of the five prisons located there, ranging from maximum security to ultra-max.
