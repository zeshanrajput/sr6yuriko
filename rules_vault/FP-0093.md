## **Shorting the Future**

**One thing to remember about the Great Depression that hit the old United States in the late 1920s was that while it was a time of misery and suffering for most of the nation, some people made a fortune off of the events.**

**Let’s talk about Joseph Kennedy Sr., who made money on insider trading and market manipulation in the heady days of the 1920s. Unlike many other investors, though, he thought the market was overvalued, so he short-sold many stocks, betting on the market to collapse. In the frst six years of the Depression, he grew his wealth by more than forty times. In today’s terms, he would have been a multi-billionaire.**

**Some of the Kechibi code experiences showed us just how willing some people are to make money off widespread suffering.**

- **To be fair, the corps have been showing that for a long time.**

- **> Old Crow**

**The more people know about Convergence— particularly some of their old colleagues in Brokerage X—the more they’re going to try to fnd ways to be the ones left standing after Convergence’s “something” happens.**

**While they made money off of Brokerage X activities in the past, Convergence’s upper management are not happy that people would use their work to forestall disaster in order to make some money. They will pay good money to have people track down anyone who is making money off of their predictions of disaster.**

- **They will then take their resources and use them to pay the shadowrunners. Not a bad system.**
