## **Tactics**

**Bishop is not a ruthless man. He’s not a master planner like Knight, nor a high-stakes gambler like Richard Villiers. Bishop is a negotiator, and he doesn’t agree with the idea that the world is made up of winners and losers. He frmly believes that both sides can win, that agreements can be made, and that once a contract is signed, it should be followed. “A deal’s a deal,” as they say, and like most of us from the shadows, he understands that a man’s worth is only as good as his word. This makes him somewhat vulnerable to the more aggressive business types out there, but at the same time it builds loyalty and connections. He doesn’t have to fght you on his own—he has an entire network of people who will step up to help him out. It’s slower, but the patient gains build up over time. There’s a banking commercial in Philadelphia that he was in, talking about Ares fnancial services, where he brought up Benjamin Franklin’s quote that compound interest is the most powerful force in the world. His history in the city proves he believes that, too.**

**This isn’t to say he’s afraid to move fast and break things when needed, it’s just that he prefers a slower, safer approach. When things fall apart, he moves quickly, as you can see with his initial position after the Crash, but he already had pieces in place, ready to move rather than make it up on the fy. He’s the sort of guy who practices for hours for a ten-minute speech to make sure it goes perfectly, but he keeps a few jokes in his back pocket in case he gets thrown off. More than anything else, however, he’s a listener. Bishop wants to talk to people, in person, where he can get a good read on them. Subtle shifts in vocal infection, eyes fickering or blinks that never show up in Matrix feeds, the style of clothing worn, any type of tell that can lay out who he’s dealing with and how to approach them is a weapon in his arsenal, but none are as important as him sitting there, hands clasped, eyes open, mouth closed. He’s curious about** _**you**_ **, and what you want, and he’s content to let you talk as much as you want. He’s a good listener, and people love that.**
