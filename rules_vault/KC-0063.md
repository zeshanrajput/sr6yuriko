## Notable Scotsprawl Locations

**Royal Mile:** Stretching from Edinburgh Castle on the west to the Parliament building on the east, this is the oldest section of Edinburgh, full of history and heritage—and at least two notable banks (the Bank of Scotland and the Royal Bank of Scotland).

**New Town:** North of the Royal Mile and near the Water of Leith; many Scotsprawl corporate buildings are located here, especially the financial giants.

**Portobello:** Edinburgh is not always thought of as a beach town, but this stretch on the east coast offers sandy beaches and all the luxury trappings for the wealthy residents of the sprawl.

**Bington:** Between Edinburgh and Glasgow are several conical slag heaps of mining waste, locally known as bings. Bington is the area built among those heaps, and as you might guess, it’s not desirable real estate. Many corporate executives take helicopters from Edinburgh to Glasgow specifically to avoid areas like Bington and not subject their cars to whatever the residents might throw at them. If you want to hide from the all-seeing eyes of the Big Ten—or if you’re looking to find those who are trying to lie low—this is the place to go.

**Merchant City:** East of downtown Glasgow, this area used to hold industrial factories and warehouses, but they were abandoned as Scottish manufacturing sagged. Artists took over the area, and now gentrification is taking hold, though it has not yet totally altered the neighborhood. That means it is a mix of bohemian types and corporate professionals.

**Inchimanky:** A portmanteau of the name of a village (Inchinnan) and a slang term for dirty (manky), this is a label applied to the area north of Glasgow Airport. It has access to the airport, the River Clyde, and the M8, so a lot of manufacturing is based here to take advantage of that access. That’s pretty easy to tell by the odor and thickness of the air.
