## **Kripa Grover**

**If there’s one central person in the Tiding that outsiders have a grudge against, it’s Kripa Grover. Grover has been in the information-gathering business a long time—he spent twenty years at Aegis Cognito before he left and became a “freelance consultant.” Publicly, he claims no attachment or relation to the Tiding, but everyone knows that’s bulldrek. He left Aegis because he could get more money and more freedom with the Tiding’s work, and by all reports he enjoys his current life very much.**

**His departure, though, left a range of bad feelings. First of all, Aegis is never thrilled to lose an operative still in their prime, especially one with a wide range of contacts and knowledge. They’ve been known to take extreme measures to keep what they feel to be their proprietary knowledge from getting out in the wild, so Grover may have to keep an eye out for Aegis assassins.**

**He’s planned for this, though, by trying to make himself more valuable to Aegis alive than dead. He’s made sure his network of contacts has remained available to Aegis, while also ensuring Aegis knows that if anything happens to him, his network will clam up. Additionally, he maintains collegial relations with some former co-workers— he shares some information with them, they share some back. So far, this arrangement has proven mutually benefcial enough that neither side wants to end it.**

**As you might guess by the discussion of Grover’s network, his specialty is human intelligence. Either he knows a person, or he knows a person who knows a person. While other people might dive into the Matrix when looking for a piece of data, Grover jumps on his commlink and makes call after call. He may end up talking to someone half a world away who is tired and confused about who they are talking to and why, but it never takes Grover long to convince people that he is friendly and worth talking to.**

- **Name-dropping and ego-stroking are tools that never go out of style.**

**> Ms. Myth**
