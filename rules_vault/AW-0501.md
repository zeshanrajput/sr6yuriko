## Applying Metaplanar Translation

Traveling through a metaplanar passageway that uses physical transference will enforce a different version of reality on the traveler—unless the attunement effect of metaplanar translation has been inhibited in some way. Materializing on a metaplane will also have a similar effect, but the adapted body is created from scratch with that method, not transformed. The translation can be relatively minor if the reality paradigm is close to one the travelers are coming from. Minor changes may be subtle—little things like knowing the local languages or clothing that changes style to match the local fashions. Other changes may be extreme when the reality paradigm between two locations is really far apart, things like changing the basic body of the traveler into a radically different form. For example, traveling to a fire-based metaplane may give the traveler a body made of lava that is covered in flame, while traveling to the Hive without proper protection triggers a Kafkaesque transformation into a giant anthropomorphic insect. Regardless of how extensive the changes are, they only last until the traveler leaves the metaplane.

The primary rule for metaplanar translation is that only the appearance changes—everything brought into a metaplane will still work as it normally does. Adaptations to gear will try to match the local reality paradigm as closely as possible but will be limited by the ambient mana of the metaplane in some ways. In completely non-technological but high-mana metaplanes, this may mean that weapons become extensions of the traveler’s attuned body, either some physical feature such as claws or what appears to be a magical attack as high-velocity projectiles appear from thin air. In lower-mana metaplanes, there may not be enough energy for complete translations of gear, and many items will keep their original appearance, even if that type of item doesn’t “exist” in the local reality. Other gear on higher-mana metaplanes, especially drones, may manifest as insects, birds, dogs, pack mules, or spirit-like constructs, or a motorcycle may become a horse. These all still respond to the commands of the traveler and operate exactly like the original drone—just think of it as very good realistic features. Again, metaplanes with lower mana may not completely transform the appearance of drones and other technological gear, focusing on survivability in the local environment over changing the appearance. Pre-industrial reality paradigms with higher mana may change pistols and rifles into projectile weapons like repeating hand crossbows and longbows. While the profiles remain the same, the perceived form changes. Metaplanes with higher levels of technology may only alter the aesthetic of how equipment looks.



A gun is still a gun in a steampunk, dieselpunk, or atompunk reality, but exactly what that gun looks like can vary a lot.

Metaplanar translation can be inhibited with a number of techniques, and there may be numerous reasons for wanting to do this. The Metaplanar Stasis spell (below) exists to transport samples and material back to the physical realm unaltered so they can be studied, but it has also proven useful as a safe way to transport true elements through passageways. Enchantments can be added to portals so they stop translation, and the environments of metaplanes can be altered with ritual magic—beyond anything the players can achieve on their own. The best example of this is the bubble of reality Ares has created in the Hive that allows travelers to exist there without evanescence or translation. The metaplane of Dis also seems to have some type of enchantment that stops incoming travelers from fully translating to the reality of the metaplane. Speculation is that this is a defense the Disians put in place to discourage immigration and protect them from any possible invasion. And then there’s the elephant in the room—the material realm does not enforce any metaplanar translation on entities traveling into it. They always arrive exactly as they are in their home metaplanes. Theories about why this effect does not exist in the material realm are as varied as the ones regarding the nature of the Dweller on the Threshold—another feature unique to the manasphere of the material realm.
