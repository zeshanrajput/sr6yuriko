## **Mundane**

**First off is mundane critters. These critters lack any Awakened or Emerged powers—no magic or Resonance, and they exist more or less as mother nature intended (with some interference by breeding and domestication efforts by metahumans over the centuries). Mundane critters tend to be the least dangerous ones out there, both because they lack powers and because they have the good sense to stay the frag out of your way. Even mundane predators avoid metahumans, except in unusual cases such as extreme hunger, a rowdy mating time, or when they are protecting their young.**

**Most pets are mundane, and the same goes for livestock that are raised for food and other products made from critter bits. Dogs are widely used for a variety of labor and security tasks. Many mundane animals are used for a variety of dayto-day purposes, and you’ve probably learned to ignore them or take them for granted. There are pigeons, bats, insects, spiders, raccoons, mice, rats, snakes, and more living in your city— they’re all around you. As a runner (or security consultant), you can take advantage of this, using drones (with realistic features) or trained critters to get close to and observe locations that you normally wouldn’t be allowed access to. Frag, I can remember one run in which the team wandered right past security cameras by using a Physical Mask spell to look like raccoons—the cameras saw them, but the software algorithm monitoring the cameras ignored them and didn’t alert the guards.**
