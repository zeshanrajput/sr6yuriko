## Biotechnology & Agriculture

Our sprawl has a decade-long history of biotech research and production. They concentrate in the East New Orleans Business Zone. This was long dominated by Cross Biomedical, until the corp was butchered after the second Matrix crash. Now the big player in town is Universal Omnitech. They took over former Cross core company BioGenesis Unlimited, which now form the local core of UO. They research in the fi elds of biopharmaceuticals, geneware, symbionts, and bioware. The core aspect of their breakthroughs is repeated expeditions into the bayous and deep sea to search for unknown para-fl ora and -fauna, which might bring us the next VITAS vaccine. Their other subsidiaries are Riveros Applied Mimetics and Confederated Pharma.

- And these are just the big names. In the shadows of the megacorps exist a microcosmos of smaller chemical, service, and logistic companies, circling around the petrochemical industry. Some hope one day to sit at the adult table, but most get gobbled up by the megas before they get the chance.

- Snopes

- The real winner is Krupp Specialist Engineering. They have a plant in Baton Rouge, which provides specialized, heavy machinery and holds most of the plant construction and maintenance contracts in the area.

- Mr. Bonds

Other biotech giants on the spot are Biogene Technologies (Yakashima), Genecraft Biodesign (Renraku), the Genesis Consortium, Genetique (Aztechnology), Schering Pharma (Z-IC), Shiawase Laboratories, and Vital Pharmaceuticals (Horizon). One local corp I want to point out is Crescent Pharmaceuticals, which has been working on a HMHVV inhibitor, which would make it possible for HMHVV-Infected to not infect other metahumans. The rumor is that it is being led by a vampire.

Noticeably we are seeing a shift from oil drilling to deep-sea mining. New ore refi neries and steel mills were built next to the oil refi neries. Most of them are owned by Krupp Manufacturing, others are from Confederate Steel, and Acerías Aztlán (Aztechnology). Offshore Krupp SE uses old oil rigs as storage and operation bases for mining drones, in cooperation with another S-K subsidiary, Trans-Oceanic Mining.

When it comes to medical care, New Orleans is divided between two big players, DocWagon and Info Santé (former Cross medical service, now owned by Ares), and one smaller one, BuMoNA, which was originally brought here by AG Chemie and holds contracts with most German and various European corps.

- This Scandinavian corp is one of their “second tier subsidiaries”. The current shareholders are S-K 56 percent, new German AA Trikon 14 percent, Erika 12 percent, Mærsk 10 percent, and the Frankfurt Bank Association 5 percent. Remember that S-K and the FBA are sworn enemies and S-K’s semi-alliance with Mærsk only works because Lofwyr relies heavily on their shipping operations, after previous partner, Lusiada, was acquired by Spinrad.
