## **Matrix Surveillance: We’re Watching**

_**Surveillance, by any usual means, of a person of interest in an investigation is permitted in principle. The agents in charge of surveillance must be located on public property or property belonging to Lone Star Security Services or their parent company. Surveillance must not have the effect or cause of hindering persons of interest in an investigation**_ **. —Lone Star Security Services Procedure Manual, revised 2080**

**Matrix forensics has its place, but where GridSec shines is surveillance. In anticipation of potential crime, we have agents deployed to monitor certain subjects, servers, or trends. This capacity also comes in handy after the fact, through active surveillance of a perp or through publicly accessible information, like activities on social media. We’re increasingly trained to do OSINT, which is a fancy acronym to describe what any of you already do when you’re trying to fnd out whether your ex is happier with her new partner than they were with you.**

**We don’t need a warrant to search your social media, and this often allows us to reconstruct agendas and get an initial analysis. A simple mandate, or even direct agreements for certain OmniStar products (check your user contracts), gives us access to your social media data, such as your geolocation. At the end, we can obtain a whole range of contact metadata like that. We know who you’ve communicated with, when, and how often. This gives us an overview of networks and contact fows. In some cases, we already have so much information with the metadata that we don’t even need to know what was actually said or done to pin you for your crime.**

**> We have exactly the same logic and approach when we enquire about our contacts. More on that in the Smooth Operations upload. > Bull**

**We also make extensive use of the archives of these media outlets, which enables us to obtain information on changes in behavior. To dumb it down: When a perp plans a big hit, they tend to stop posting on their social.**

**When we need to be more proactive than digging into the Matrix, that’s when we call the Department of Surveillance. They can track all the people involved in the case. They’re geniuses at camoufage, disguise, and social engineering, and they’ve got SOTA gear at their disposal. Nothing and nobody can escape them. The only problem is that it costs the Department a fortune, which it bills the city. So you have to be able to justify it. The surveillance rules are pretty damn complicated, too.**

- **Another important point is the reference to “by any usual means,” which is interpreted in case law as ordinary or low-tech means. As soon as you want to use magical or sophisticated techniques, you must request a court order. Even a microscopic drone isn’t considered “usual” means, for instance. That’s why most detectives end up shadowing their suspects themselves or pay runners. But that comes with the risk of having the evidence rejected in court. Even evidence recovered indirectly and legitimately afterward is fruit of the poisonous tree. The defendant will leave the courtroom a free person if they can prove that the evidence against them was discovered from a tainted source.**

**> Legal Eagle**

**The Matrix is also incredibly helpful with research and verifying facts. To avoid speculation when faced with the vast amount of data that clues reveal, technology facilitates fnding correlations and links between them, and clarifes situations.**

**But there’s a catch. If data is properly indexed and categorized, we can draw inferences with a speed and effciency that would make Hercule Poirot’s “petite cellules grises” swoon. But conversely there is still the problem of “garbage in, garbage out.” I see a growing generation of detectives relying too heavily on this technology, so extolled by the so-called predictive tools’ salesmen. It hurts me to point out the obvious: This doesn’t work on its own. If you can get your hands on this cuttingedge equipment, good for you. But if you don’t have the professionalism of my forensic guys and a real ability to differentiate between correlation and causation, you’re going to burn yourself out having more than one iron in the fre. Or simply send innocent people to rot in our prisons.**
