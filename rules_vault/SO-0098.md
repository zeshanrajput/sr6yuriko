## Scaling

I feel I’ve covered this somewhat under obstacles, where scaling up is inadvisable. It is possible to scale this down to sowing distrust with a particular individual, from a bodyguard to a majordomo. Undermining such a relationship can actually take more work, as there is likely more intimacy to be understood. That same level of detail can work to your advantage, however, as it provides less ambiguous openings and more specific soft points. A relationship between the mark’s spouse and their trusted lieutenant can be exploited to suggest infidelity, where an accountant’s books can be tampered with to suggest incompetency or theft. These can still work to provide you with exploitable destabilizations in the target’s life.

Another way to escalate this is to supplant the security or needed individual. If you can be next in line for the client to turn to, you have them essentially in the palm of your hand. This, of course, takes far more time building up a relationship, especially if you want to avoid being seen as the potential instigator of this newfound disloyalty. You’re selling security where it’s just been violated.

In these cases, you have to immediately provide a solution to a problem that’s set them spinning. Panic makes people seek relief, and providing that relief might not be questioned in the moment. Their suspicion can likely escalate after the initial stress dies down, but you can do a lot in that window of opportunity, especially if you can keep them hemmed in on all sides and keep providing the security they suddenly lack.
