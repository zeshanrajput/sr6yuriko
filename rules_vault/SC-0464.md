## The Job

After a series of incidents where people outside the shadows suffer as collateral damage, or in a single but highly dramatic instance, the character has accepted the Selective Numbing quality. It will be useful, as some of the people are now coming after him. Some of the social adept’s victims (or just one big victim) have compared notes and have hatched a plan to get their revenge. Can they con a con artist?

The former marks know they can’t just pull a simple con on the social adept—no variation on the Nigerian Prince scheme will work here. Instead, they present a setup designed to appeal to a social adept. They say there is going to be a big fundraising gala, but they’re worried some competitors are going to attend the gala and spread slander about their organization. So they want to hire the social adept to go to the gala, monitor conversations, and counter any slander the social adept hears.

In reality, the gala is a trap, staffed entirely by the victims and their friends and contacts. Their job is to build evidence for an elaborate frame. They’ll try to recover any fingerprints the adept leaves on glasses, record lots of samples of them speaking for digital manipulation, and take pictures of them with people who will play a role in their eventual gambit.

At some point in the evening, the police will be called. A valuable necklace, worth 250,000 nuyen (that’s the claim, at least—in truth, the prop necklace is worth only about 250 nuyen), has been taken from the neck of one of the attendees while she was briefly napping in a back room. Soon after the police arrive, they receive a stack of evidence pointing toward the social adept. Their fingerprints are all over the room where the necklace was taken. There are audio recordings of them talking about the necklace and doing everything short of saying they will steal it. And there are (manipulated) pictures showing them pocketing the necklace, and plenty of eyewitnesses willing to back up the pictures.

The social adept will now have to do some fast talking to get out of this. In all truth, they’re probably not looking at a lot of jail time, but they’re definitely looking at a trip to the station and some police grilling. Any shadowrunner knows that can take a wrong turn, so it would be good to avoid it if possible.
