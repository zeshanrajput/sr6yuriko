## The Job: Part Two

The next part of the run can be as hard as the gamemaster wants to make it. If Dr. Hernandez realized there was only one team of expendable assets hired, word may have been sent to the other team of shadowrunners to prevent the players from returning to the base. Alternatively, additional security may have been sent to Topside, or the Leopard Guard may have set up a defensive perimeter around the portal at Airlock to stop them from re-entering the facility. Perhaps this becomes a running fight through the caverns as the team tries to escape to the surface. Once out of Aztechnology’s clutches, the team still needs to evade the Ares patrols on the island, make their way across the bay, and finally disappear into Battery City.

Once free and clear of Governors Island, the team realizes that Mr. Seven has already left the drone, probably as soon as they had access to the Matrix, and the pilot had been operating it during the extraction. It will follow the team around or sit wherever the team leaves it, waiting for the Null to return and re-animate it. Mr. Seven has been busy after they left and has already contacted Cat to make the final arrangements for the data transfer. A half-hour after returning, the team gets the Matrix address and access codes for a secure host. The location is in the middle of the Mojave Desert and doesn’t seem to be tied to any physical structure. In fact, it’s way out in the wild Matrix. If they go check it out, it’s full of the erased files on MCT’s activities in Denver—enough data to fill a rating 6 optical brick (p. 53, _Hack & Slash_ ). Shibata Consulting was indeed the cover for the black ops projects run in Denver.
