## Honey from the Comb

This adventure hook works best with the honeypot and the messenger backgrounds but is easily adapted to any team who enjoys social manipulation as a tactical option but is ready for magical violence.

The fixer has a contract. The client could be a gang known for BTL distribution, a concerned citizens watch committee, or a corporate section head who has gotten word of compromised workers and doesn’t want word getting back to their superior. Someone is selling a new beetle chip called Honey—very potent, dangerous, and highly addictive. It seems to have the added hazard of some kind of psychotropic conditioning element, but none of the chips have been able to be scanned or reverse-engineered. When it’s plugged in, the chip self-deletes after running, defies external observation, and leaves the user with no memory of what happened, only a warm sense of acceptance and bliss—and of course the desire to do it again. Any attempt to crack it without jacking in triggers its self-deletion protocols. The chips themselves are cheap Kong chips, mass-produced third-party blanks for commercial use, untraceable in their ubiquity. Without any way to backtrack on the technical side, it’ll take a field op to draw out the dealers and work their way up the food chain to identify and eliminate the source. The client can offer an interview with the user who inadvertently tipped them off or simply give them what they’ve learned, namely: The dealer doesn’t appear to have any criminal affiliations, the chips are cheap, and they are sold in a relatively quiet zone appropriate to the client (barrens, residential, just outside a corp housing complex, etc.).

Hunting will likely come down to legwork or undercover baiting, working up a chain of dealers and distributors, and using guile or intimidation to move link to link. The ultimate path is up to you, but it can end in any location you like so long as it can house a chip-print operation. Crates of blanks and basic programming suites don’t say a lot for the resources or sponsor of this operation, but it is defended by an insect flesh form (p. 68, _Street Wyrd_ ). The social investigation theme of the adventure should make this sudden shift a surprise, and it poses a salient combat challenge for any infected, as insect attacks count as spirits for purposes of regeneration. If the programming suite can be accessed, the BTL program can be observed and its purpose divined, conditioning users into a subconscious desire to be infested as hosts, likely resulting in good merges. This news should alarm the crew, as an unknown number of users may have already been claimed and melted back into society as hosts. Worse, who designed these? Where is the queen? And is this operation taking place anywhere else? Feel free to take this adventure wherever you wish. The client, if they have the connections or resources, may pass them up the chain to qualified hunting organizations, who may have additional use for the team in running the bugs to ground. Happy raiding!
