## Dino-technology

Look, someone was bound to get around to this eventually, despite the existence of an entire literary genre and film series designed as a cautionary tale against it. Someone had to go and make dinosaurs. The brilliant (some would say insane) idea to combine advanced techniques of biopaleontology with modern genetic engineering techniques in order to rebuild (to approximate, actually) the genetic sequences of dinosaurs paid off. An Ares subsidiary has been developing their dinosaur-growing laboratories for years, but they aren’t the only megacorporation to invest their R&D resources on this. Progress advanced considerably due to a recent influx of newly acquired genetic samples. This is still a relatively well-kept secret, but Ares now has a number of R&D installations where they are testing and training newly created dinosaurs. From the intel I’ve been able to gather, it seems that these genetically engineered critters are highly resistant to being tamed and trained, but with recent advances in beast-controlling technology, their potential for security and warfare applications has opened up a bit. Naturally big, strong, tough, and deadly—and under the full control of their owners—they are well-suited beasties for heavy combat. For these reasons, most of the dinosaurs that have been engineered are the big ones. I doubt you’ll see them on the open market for a long time, if ever, so don’t expect to be able to go out and buy your own dino-pet. Even if you manage to steal one, you’re better off finding a buyer—and I doubt that will be difficult, if you have the right connections—because they’re difficult to keep secret, and you can bet your last nuyen that whoever you took it from will be very eager to get it back. If the critter isn’t being controlled and commanded by software, it probably has a rigger riding its brain. If you have a nova-hot hacker on your team and happen to encounter one of these critters on a run, you might just be able to turn it against its owners. Don’t expect it to be an easy hack job, though. They’re likely to be protected by a firewall enhanced by an M-TOC or a technomancer—maybe both.

**Game Information:** Because these critters are the result of genetic engineering from the very beginning, all possible genetic augmentation has gone into their very existence, and no genetic augmentations may be applied to them. When a dinosaur is created, roll 4D6 on the Genetic Mishaps Table.

Dinosaurs require a specialized, oversized growth vat that is not available for purchase. If it were, it would cost at least one million nuyen and have an Availability of 12(I). The supplies needed for the artificial womb and growth vat process cost 1,000 nuyen times the dinosaur’s Body attribute per day. The genetic formula for creating a dinosaur is a heavily protected proprietary secret, andit is never stored on hosts or devices that are connected to the Matrix. Acquiring one would mean doing a run on one of Ares’ most secret, secure, and heavily guarded laboratories.

Dinosaurs are resistant to being tamed and trained. No Edge may be spent on Training tests to tame or train them, and they roll (Willpower + Intuition) x 2 to resist such attempts.

Dinosaurs are unavailable for purchase by PCs.
