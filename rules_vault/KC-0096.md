## Cultural Gap

The local culture is nothing like anything anyone from a major sprawl is used to. They are in a place that saw the rise of magic at the exact moment it was needed to face one of the worst epidemics in history. Many Lagosians firmly believe in their gods, their faith, and the divinity of their holy men. While the curse spouted by the local fruit vendor after you insult the gods doesn’t have any real magic, it was heard by several other locals who spread the word, and suddenly no one wants to help you, no one picks up your calls for a taxi, and the price of everything suddenly became double or triple of what it was before. This sudden lack of luck can certainly seem like magic. This cultural divide comes with the risk of unseen insults and misunderstandings. Any time there is a potentially stressful or tense interaction between the runners and the locals, make a Charisma + Intuition (3) test. Failing to meet the threshold gives the locals a point of Edge to use against the runners. If the roll glitches, they have insulted the local and they cease providing assistance to the runners. A critical glitch is a curse-worthy offense, meaning the runners just made some enemies in whatever part of town they’re in.
