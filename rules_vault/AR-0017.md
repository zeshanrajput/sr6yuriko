## TALES FROM THE UCAS 

gates keep cars out at certain hours of the day, but for the most part there is no barrier between cemetery and town. On 6[th] Street, a few small houses look across the road to gently sloped land with neat rows of gravestones rising up the hill. Some thicker trees and the lack of streets act as something more of a barrier to the north. There is no trace of ostentation in the cemetery. No large crypts. Or small ones, really. Just a collection of headstones, and those that dare poke their heads up more than ten centimeters above the ground eventually feel shamed by the disapproval of their peers, so they crack and slink back down toward the earth. 

So why am I talking so much about this place? Because it’s the other major gathering place besides the funeral home (funeral home and cemetery as the main gathering places—this is goth paradise, right?). Two or three times a week, around midnight, at least a dozen people walk into the cemetery. They straggle out some time after that, in small groups rather than all together. Why do they do it? The answer is simple: to commune with Satan. 

I promise you that’s not a joke. Unlike the funeral home, the cemetery—or a nearby area, since they actually go to a spot northwest of the cemetery—is outdoors and not well guarded. No roving spirits, nothing, really. I don’t know if the people didn’t think about it, or if they’re supremely confident that no one can mess up what they’re doing. The night I went there, fourteen people walked to a small clearing behind the cemetery. They were dressed in whatever they had been wearing that day (I assume) and sitting quietly on the ground. While they sat there, the air wavered and shimmered, then an orange glow appeared. That glow solidified into rocks that shone with red heat. It was a semi-transparent image of a cavern that looked extremely warm, but I felt no temperature change when it appeared. They sat silently, gazing at the apparition. 

Then, Satan appeared. 

Well, maybe not Satan. I didn’t catch the thing’s name. But its skin was red, it had massive horns on top of its head, a thick, jutting jaw, and a tail that ended in an arrowhead shape. So he was close enough. He stood in front of the gathered people—towered, really—and basked in their gaze. I heard hints of speech, but not enough to know anything that was being said. It all came from the people. Satan made no sound. 

The apparition lasted about two hours. The first person who left departed about fifteen minutes after the end of the vision. The last left two hours later. 

I came back to the cemetery one night when no one had gathered. Nothing appeared. 

Are they making it appear? What is it? Does it communicate with them? Does it have anything to do with the funeral home? 

Those are questions I have not been paid enough to answer.
