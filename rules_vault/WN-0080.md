## A-Rated Corporations

Remember Art Dankwalther? He was just a guy until a dragon died and left him a metric ton of nuyen. He thought that meant he had power. He spent a serious chunk of that wealth on shadowruns meant to give him even more power, and we all know how that ended—with a Thor shot from the sky. A-rated corps are run by powerful people who cannot understand why they don’t have that second A and the extraterritoriality attached to it. An AA Zürich-Orbital Gemeinschaftsbank rating is the minimum requirement to reach extraterritorial status, and achieving that is as much about socio-political positioning as it is about a diverse multinational portfolio. In fact, there are dozens of A-rated corporations that are larger—either in volume of NYC business or sheer market presence— than AA corps. Nevertheless, they cannot leverage that into a second A. So they come to the city or put down stakes in the surrounding boroughs in order to be close enough to the Corporate Court to get noticed. Here are a few of those corps working to get noticed.
