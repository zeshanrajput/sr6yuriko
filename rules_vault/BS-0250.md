## Tactile Sensitivity

Each treatment covers an area roughly the size of a human hand. Rather than the hedonistic increase in volume that cosmetic modification brings, tactile sensitivity instead focuses on detail, allowing a slower, more detailed “reading” of tactile information. Fully four times as many nerve endings are present, with the gain somewhat turned down to prevent overwhelming sensation, allowing for a more precise location of stimulation. This makes it simple to detect otherwise undetectable markings on virtually any surface. With practice, a user can read the keys on a keyboard or number pad in absolute darkness due to the indentations on each key, or they can feel when tumblers fall into place in a combination lock. These ancient technologies are rarely needed in today’s world, but it’s nice to know that the option’s there if you need it.



Characters with tactile sensitivity gain 1 point of situational Edge for lock picking or any touchbased Perception tests.
