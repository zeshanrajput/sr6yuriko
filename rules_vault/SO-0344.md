## Game Information

This feature must be designed as part of the outfit’s manufacturing process and can’t be applied as an after-market modification. The transforming garment’s rating determines the number of different configurations it can take. Every surface is

treated with electrochromic material, and nanotech fabric is used sparingly to allow for the creation and sealing of cuts. It’s also stain-resistant, self-cleaning, and self-repairing.

Every transforming garment is custom-designed and tailored to the individual. Each setting must be pre-defined.

For each rating, choose one outfit or armor type. In addition to describing its appearance, the user must also choose the type of outfit: cheap clothes, blue collar, casual wear, business casual, uniform, power suit, or très chic. The Social Rating from that outfit applies when the garment takes that form.

Outfits with a rating, such as uniforms, power suits, and très chic, are more complex and expensive to incorporate. Define the desired rating and add its cost to the overall cost when purchasing the transforming garment.

The advanced materials technology utilized in this garment is astonishing, allowing for a transformation of color, texture, and cut. It cannot, however, create and destroy matter. If one configuration is much smaller than another, the garment will become bunchy and dense in the smallest configuration while being thin and light in the largest mode. For that reason, the relative size of each configuration of a transforming garment must be similar. No matter how much nuyen a user has to throw at the clothing designer, they can’t make a bikini that transforms into jeans and an armor jacket.

If a form is chosen to emulate a uniform or provide camouflage, it provides a point of Edge on Con and Stealth tests to blend in or avoid detection.

Armor can be designed as a transforming garment as well, though it takes up capacity equal to rating x 2. When designing transforming armor, use the form that has the highest DR bonus as the baseline. Only armor that has a base DR bonus of +4 or less can be designed as transforming armor. Each configuration must specify the form it takes, be it armor jacket, lined coat, armor clothing, or what have you. The DR bonus provided is one less than the bonus usually provided by the armor’s form. Additional armor modifications that are included must be incorporated into every configuration, so gear slots that don’t look out-of-place on your armor jacket are going to be awfully weird when you change it to the armored evening dress. If the armor chosen provides a bonus or penalty to Social Rating, it applies when the transforming garment takes that form. The cost of each type of armor is added to the overall cost of the transforming garment.
