## **Astral Stealth Tips**

## **Posted by: Hemdale**

**Most secure locations have some form of Awakened security. All it takes is one on-duty security magician to make your mission more complicated. Many magicians can astrally project in order to patrol, but spirits are more regularly used for this. If the summoner gets disrupted or killed, their spirits are no longer an issue. Their bio-monitor will likely alert mundane security forces if this happens, but they won’t be able to make a report about exactly what happened until they’re back on their feet. If a spirit gets popped, the summoner becomes immediately aware that something just took it out. Then they’re likely to alert mundane security that there’s been an astral intrusion, and can call up another spirit to go fnd out what happened. This doesn’t take very long at all, so if you do pop a security spirit, don’t stick around unless you’re itching for a fght against a well-prepared opponent.**

**Sneaking past astral forms can be tricky, but it’s not impossible. If you know there’s a security mage or spirits keeping an eye on astral space, you can most likely avoid them with the help of an astral spotter of your own. Just like other guards and security critters, astral watchers can only watch one place at a time—if they’re on patrol or watching a location that isn’t critical to your objective, you can stay out of their line of sight. This is tricky if you don’t have someone on your team who can spot for you, so if you know this is going to be a problem, I recommend outsourcing an astrally projecting scout to back you up. Unless they know the Mindlink spell, it can be diffcult to keep in touch with them. Sometimes they can send a spirit along with your team that can materialize and relay messages. Subcontractors are often ridiculously expensive and tend to be less reliable than full team members, so be very careful about relying on this. I’d honestly rather run blind to the astral than rely on unreliable astral support, but the option is out there.**

**Fortunately, most astral security is watching for magical and astral security threats, not mundane ones. The best way to deal with it is to stay under the astral radar. The most useful advanced magical ability a mage on your team can learn is masking. This metamagic technique is not something an amateur mage is likely to know, since it requires initiation. If you’re a shadowrunning mage, learning masking should be one of your highest priorities when you advance your magical abilities. If you have a mage on your team, use peer pressure to get them to learn masking if they don’t know it already. Masking allows a mage to hide their aura so**

**that it appears to be mundane. They can also use it to conceal the auras generated by spells and magic items. This is super important. If you don’t appear to be magical or have any spells running, most astral security will ignore you unless you start causing a ruckus. If you want to be able to use any magic to enhance your capabilities, even alchemy drek, it will give you away while it’s active—unless your mage can mask it.**

**If masking isn’t an option, or if you need to sneak about in astral space, you can still pull it off. Something worth noting is that physical objects—even transparent ones—block astral line of sight. If you can make use of walls, windows, and other cover, you should be able to stay out of sight even as a glowing astral form. Things that are alive tend to glow in astral space, so take advantage of foliage such as trees and bushes. These can partially screen you on the astral in a similar fashion as fog or smoke might hide you from visual observation. It isn’t a hundred percent reliable, but I’ve seen it work.**
