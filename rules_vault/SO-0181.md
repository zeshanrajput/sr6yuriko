You might have a fixer, ganger, or other contact, but whether there’s a high degree of trust between you and your contacts is going to vary. Okay, moving on.

The best way to get started in a new place is to use the network you have. Your existing contacts may have friends in other cities, or in different lines of work. Make sure your contact will trust that friend with their life, because you’re inevitably going to be doing the same thing.

The second part is building a rapport with this person. This is where you really need to be cautious. Get as much information as you can on the individual. Do they like drugs, sex, nuyen, free tickets to urban brawl, or something more exotic? Are there parts of their life that could be used against them in a way that would hurt you? What motivates someone is going to be the best way to form a positive relationship, but it could also prevent you from being sold out. With frag-up number two, we knew the security guard was highly motivated by cash. We could’ve worked with that and made a deal. Sometimes if you’re out in the barrens, food or ammunition is going to be more valuable than straight nuyen.

Once you have information that should help you cover at least part of your hoop, then it’s time to start working with them. Are they buying you a new AK-97? Meet in a place that’s safe and you both agree on, or at least compromise, before you make the trade. Also, try the fraggin’ gun out before handing over the nuyen! Ask me how I know… If it’s a new Mr. (or Ms.)Johnson, then try and learn about the job you’re taking and aim small, or reasonably so. Don’t try to get the big score to retire on because that’s the one where it’s cheaper for the Mr. Johnson to kill you than pay you.
