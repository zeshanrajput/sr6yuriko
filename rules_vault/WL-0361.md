## **Wampus**

## **(Felis effingo)**

**The legend of the wampus originates from Native American mythology, with various tribes having their versions of the creature’s story. There are similar stories around the globe; a common element to the lore is a person using a wild cat’s pelt as camoufage so they can watch a sacred ceremony. When discovered, they are condemned by the elders. In some traditions, the wampus is considered a symbol of protection or a guardian spirit, attempting to stop others from making a similar mistake, while in others it is a fearsome monster to be avoided.**

**Starting in 1920, various locations within the United States reported sightings of a strange catlike creature murdering local animals. Major media outlets of the time claimed to have spotted a “wampus cat” from Georgia to North Carolina. These sightings continued through the 1960s and grew in savagery, forcing locals to impose curfews and arm people to protect cattle and other livestock. It is now believed that these sightings were actually Awakened wampus, but due to the low magic of the Fifth World, they could not fully utilize their powers and only achieved a hybrid form.**

**A wampus can be a North American bobcat, European lynx, Eurasian lynx, or African caracal with the ability to transform itself into a fully clothed adolescent metahuman. In this form, they can stand 0.8 to 1.3 meters tall and weigh 25 to 30 kilograms. They can move bipedally and have fully functioning arms and hands. Research has proven that a wampus can only maintain its bipedal form for four to six hours a day.**
