## Alternative Methods of Analysis

One of the benefits of living in the current year is the wonderful tech available. Relying on tried-and-true methods of observing facial and body movements is still important, but we’ve also developed technology to further assist us. Polygraph tests were once considered state of the art at detecting whether someone was lying based on blood pressure, pulse rate, respiration, and sweat; all while strapped to a chair and asked a series of questions. It wasn’t completely accurate, but it was another piece of data.

Today, we have modern equipment that can detect pulse rate, respiration rate, and skin salinity from a camera. This, combined with bio-sensors, can be employed to determine blood pressure while olfactory scanners can detect any pheromone emissions. At the same time, cameras can track micro expressions in eye movement and dilation and feed that data into algorithms that can help decipher body language.

- The same trick of controlling your tells can also work in these situations. The sensors can give you a more exact reading, but in an uncontrolled environment, the data is a lot noisier than you would think. Microvariations of one-tenth of a degree or a quiver of two-to-three ppm of carbon dioxide are measurable, but they might not give you a clear indication of whether they’re lying to you if the natural fluctuations in the room are around the same. One thing that always seems to be pretty easy to detect is arousal.
