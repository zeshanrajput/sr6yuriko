## **Siberian Bee**

## **(Apis siberius)**

**There are some Awakened species that make you think they came back into the world to help return the balance of nature and to smooth over the damage that metahumanity has done to the planet during its short reign. And then there are things like the Siberian bee, an Awakened version of the** _**Apis mellifera**_ **, a transplanted species with only three centuries of life in the region.**

**During a study, Netcat was in deep, and our facility was attacked by TerraFirst! jackbags who didn’t do their research on how well these subjects were treated and how every step in the process set them up for reentry into their natural community. But that’s besides the point.**

**This Awakened cousin to the basic species is signifcantly larger and shows a unique affnity for strong electromagnetic felds. They build their hives near power stations, transformers, and generators. The larger the feld, the larger the hive and the larger the queen. Early research showed the hive able to protect itself through a powerful electrical discharge. The smallest hive on record**

**The collective of hares presented no fewer than eight different access points to different Resonance Realms. Netcat had never seen anything like it before.**

**One might wonder the value of this, other than being able to cut back on noise in the wilds, but**



**produces a large enough charge to give a metahuman a good jolt, and the largest hive to date caused the death of Dr. Hills Gershwin, host of** _**The Hills are Alive**_ **tridcast and renowned troll bodybuilder turned apiologist. Later events resulted in the discovery of the Siberian bee’s ability to perform a focused, short-range, electrical discharge attack while swarming and the ability for a hive or swarm to super-charge a pair of bees to land on a target and “sting” them with a recorded discharge between 1.87 and 9.6 microcoulombs. That means somewhere between extreme discomfort and enough to cause cardiac arrest.**

**Other than providing the foundation for a really neat hobby where you have to wear a beesuit with some high-end non-conductivity weave in it, the Siberian bees are being used as security forces in some remote spots. A few generators, a pre-built hive, and bang, instant wandering security, not to mention a delicious supply of quite unique honey that is not only sweet and delicious but also carries the occasional mouth jolt of a small battery.**

**Bet you’re wondering how these things got me, eh? I was in the wrong place at the wrong time. I was doing some “subject release” work alongside some other nature-minded folks. There was a little holding facility protected by the Siberian cold and a few hives of these bees. Thankfully, the shifters we were freeing had a skilled medic and healer among them. My biomonitor recorded 5.2 microcoulombs. I was toast. Probably the closest to a permanent dirtnap I’ve come, and it was all from two chonky, fuzzy, cute honey bees landing on me when I got a little too close to their hive.**
