## The ITCC

Everybody knows the ITCC (Internetwork Transmission Control Council) runs the broadcast world. The ITCC is a regulatory body formed by the major broadcasters, a.k.a. their parent megacorps, watching over one another and their content to ensure quality and fair play. Ha! What a lot of you scrags don’t know is that the ITCC has only existed since 2022. Before then, the U.S. government had a regulatory body called the Federal Communications Committee, or FCC, where they bossed the corporations around. Can

you even imagine them trying that today? At any rate, the ITCC has expanded across all of North America, working in concert with the NANs and in full control of the rest. Keep in mind, only the major networks get a seat on the council—the smaller guys just do what they’re told from above. This keeps a tight control over what you can see or hear. If a small fish gets too uppity and starts trying to give out legit information, they’re shut down hard. Oligarchy! It’s what’s for breakfast.
