## Report 5: Interim report from agent 43 (Teslacoil), Omaha, NE, UCAS, June 18, 2081 

Missouri Valley is amazing. A- _ma_ -zing! It’s got a theater where you can watch trids, five shows daily, and it’s called the Rialto! Really! The buildings are all, like, one hundred fifty years old or so. Some are falling apart, some are in great shape. The Omaha sprawl never managed to reach this far, so it stayed a small town. It helped that some corp higher-ups moved there and dropped some coin to keep it spruced up. Some of them are gone now, but their work remains. 

So what’s left? Weird stuff, that’s what. There’s a funeral home in the downtown area that’s getting way more foot traffic than a funeral home should. And the people going in and out look way too jolly. And sometimes military. But not UCAS military, or at least not all of them are. I don’t know where they’re from—uniforms are not my thing—I just know they’re not familiar. 

But it’s not just the people. There are supplies going in there. Wooden crates, regular boxes. All sorts of things that are definitely not coffins. What’s in them? Beats me, because I can’t get into the building. Four spirits—four!—watching the place at all times. In a pissant town of a few thousand, who do they have that can keep four spirits on a regular rotation? And not weak ones. I tried to banish one, and it drifted toward me and slapped me in the face, hard, before I could so much as weaken it. 

So something’s going on there, and I haven’t been able to find out what, is the summary. 

But let’s talk about the rest of the town. You’ve got downtown—classic, one-street drag with a concentration of two- and three-story buildings. Then you’ve got a bunch of spread-out houses—some of them nice, some of them rundown, the kind of house that passes from generation to generation as the family members creatively find ways to stay out of the gaze of the powers of the world. A lot of the time they’re manual laborers—farm workers, plumbers, carpenters, electricians—who work independently, getting business by word of mouth. Some of them are smalltime criminals. They don’t steal or hurt any of their fellow residents, so their activities—drug dealing, insurance scams, identity theft—are winked at or overlooked by the other residents. 

The nicer houses hold the corp suits who can handle the long commute to Omaha, your local car and farm equipment dealer, the mayor, and a few other residents who have managed to accumulate some cash one way or another. Their places are nice, and they all have fire pits, so that there is a pleasant smoky smell in town on summer evenings. 

Then there is the cemetery.
