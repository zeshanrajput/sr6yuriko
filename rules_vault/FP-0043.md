## **The Principals**

**==> picture [233 x 4] intentionally omitted \<==**

**Naming names, the most dangerous of games. Membership isn’t exactly hidden, but it is spoken about as if the individual is either an eccentric, part of a rare order, or a leper, but with a title you just can’t ignore. While there are only 78 cards in the deck, the membership is estimated to be in the mid-hundreds. Possession of a card is not a prerequisite for membership, but rather a desire to possess a card and the means to do so. From what I’ve found, about half of the members are there to faunt their wealth, another quarter there to spite someone else, and the fnal quarter are the true cartomancers, those who seek to possess the power of the cards, not just the power to impress others with the lengths to which one will go to show off.**

**That said, I’ll focus on names of cartomancers here, but if you are hired to do anything dealing with this artifact, you could very well be dealing with a member of this society. If you fnd their name in here, be extremely wary, as they are the truest of fanatics.**
