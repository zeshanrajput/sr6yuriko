## News From Paris

Posted by: Sim-Eon \<04-12-83/2309:55>

I don’t have all the details yet, but I want to pass along some good news for a change. I just received a message about the Paris underground—as in literally under the actual ground—regarding the destruction of a second Disian Harvester. It was under the Louvre, and as a bonus, it wasn’t operational yet, so it didn’t destroy the center of downtown Paris when it went offline.

A Disian calling himself the Emissary has been active in Paris for some time now. While claiming to be an ambassador sent to promote peaceful interactions between the metaplanes, rumors have circulated that he was building an organization in the city. Once the existence of the Harvesters was confirmed, talk ran rampant that the Emissary was building one of these devices somewhere in Paris. I’d also heard rumors for a while now that Grey Cell and another, as yet unnamed, allied organization were operating in Paris. Rumor was they also suspected a Harvester being constructed somewhere in the city and had their agents searching for it.

Regular reports started surfacing about a team of runners blundering around the arrondissements of Paris searching for something, but somehow avoided drawing the attention of Marianne. I assume they were searching for the location of the

Harvester. In hindsight, the Louvre would have been the obvious location. During the Week of Death, the headquarters of the Atlantean Foundation was the only location in Paris to be hit by the Disian proxies in an organized assault. The damage to the Louvre and death of most of the staff caused the museum to close their doors to the public. A skeleton crew of survivors from the attack (probably in league with Disians the entire time) were said to be managing the reconstruction. All in all, a perfect scenario for the secret construction of a giant mystical device designed to drain the world’s mana.

From what I was able to find out, the point man of the Grey Cell’s operation was one Monsieur Gris (Mr. Grey? Not very creative). He was a relatively new Johnson on the Paris scene, and with very little digging, I was able to find out he was actually Henri Leroux: well-known Parisian philanthropist, powerful hermetic mage, elder in the local Stonecutters Guild, published theoretical alchemist, and the director of _la Fondation des Fabricants_ , the local branch of the Maker Foundation (who are reportedly funded by the Zhigul Makers, so that is perhaps the unnamed second organization in league with the Grey Cell). You’ll notice I’m using past tense, as Henri Leroux passed away in his alchemy laboratory just a few days ago.

The story circulating the shadows is that old Leroux had recreated one of Damian Knight’s metaplanar disruptors used to block access to the Bug Hives. Leroux had only managed to build the device after his team of hired runners stole the main components from MCT, but the extraordinary effort required to recreate the device killed him. Darker rumors talk of blood magic and that the old dwarf powered up the device with his own life essence. Regardless of how he did it, his team of handpicked runners made their way into the cavern under the Louvre after his death and detonated the device.

While the Harvester wasn’t operational yet and Paris was spared the fate of Delta-041, the magical fallout from the detonation of the device itself was still substantial. Where there was once a magical focus point at the double pyramid of the Louvre in the Parisian astral landscape (as I had been told, anyway), there is now a mana dead zone inside the range of a powerful mana ebb—an astral void also fills the cavern where that Harvester was located. I’ve been told the effects of the detonation extend far beyond the Louvre, and cover most of the First Arrondissement in a lesser mana ebb. If I understand the basics of the underlying theory correctly, the metaplanar pathway between Paris and Dis is now blocked, preventing another device from being built anywhere in the surrounding area.
