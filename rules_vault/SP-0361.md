## **Using Units of Mana in Sorcery**

**The frst step to casting a spell is to determine how much magic to use. This is set by the number of units of mana expended, and this number takes the place of the Magic attribute for that spell. The maximum number of units that can be initially applied to a spell is 2 x (Willpower + Initiate Grade). The units used also determine the Attack Rating of a Combat spell (units used + Willpower), the base DV of indirect Combat spells (one half of the units used), setting the drain damage as Stun or Physical, the range of Detection spells, and any other effects that depend on the caster’s Magic, such as the Structure rating of a Mana Barrier.**

**After a spell is cast, additional units of stored mana can be channeled into it to increase its effect. Adding 2 units of mana to the spell can Amp Up a Combat spell by +1 DV or increase the Area of Effect of a spell by 2 meters. The maximum number of adjustments that can be added to a spell is the native Disian’s Willpower. Note that the drain generated by the spell is increased as normal with the adjustments. After casting, units of mana can be used to offset drain, with each unit used directly reducing the drain on a point-for-point basis. Any drain remaining after being reduced by expending units is resisted by two times the Willpower attribute.**

**Instead of the normal mechanic for sustaining spells, they must be sustained with stored mana. This duplicates the Focused Concentration quality but requires one unit of mana per combat turn per spell. The penalty for sustaining a spell does not apply, and there is no set limit on the number of spells that can be maintained this way. Due to the way spells are sustained, Disians do not beneft from the Focused Concentration quality.**
