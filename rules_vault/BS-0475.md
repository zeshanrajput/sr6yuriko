## Bio-Drone Harness

More invasive than a critterjack, the bio-drone harness combines specialized adaptations of a critterjack, simrig, and skillwires so that a critter can be taken over and controlled—either by a rigger or a twisted equivalent of a drone pilot module. Due to the ethical concerns this technology raises, it’sunlikely to hit the consumer market in most places and is most often used for security or warfare applications.

**Game Information:** A critter with a bio-drone harness can be controlled and commanded by an RCC, or a rigger can jump into the critter just as if it were a drone. This overtakes and subsumes the critter’s own will and awareness when active, and any critter powers that utilize its Magic or Resonance attribute are unavailable—only its physical capabilities are useable while under control. While a bio-drone harness is inactive, the critter comes back to itself and may act normally. Activating the harness requires a Major Action. Each time the bio-drone harness is activated, make an Opposed test using the harness’s rating x 2 versus the critter’s Logic + Willpower—no Edge may be spent to influence this test on either side. If the critter resists, the bio-drone harness does not activate. The owner can try again with another Major Action, with a cumulative –1 dice-pool penalty for each consecutive attempt. After one hour, this penalty resets. On a critical glitch, the critter rejects the harness completely and goes berserk, and the bio-drone harness will never work on the critter again.

The rating of a harness defines the Pilot rating of the bio-drone. The maximum rating of a skillsoft or autosoft is limited by the rating of the harness, whether the software is being run on the harness itself or from an RCC.

A bio-drone’s base initiative is Reaction + Pilot, with 4D6 Initiative Dice. When a rigger is jumped in, the bio-drone uses that rigger’s VR Initiative.
