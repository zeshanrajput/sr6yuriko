## Scales 

“The scales are out of balance in your facility,” the Prime agent said with calm certainty in an unplaceable accent. His eyes were hidden behind gold-rimmed smartglasses, and every line of his crisp, black suit was razor-sharp. 

He wasn’t the only one in an expensive suit, of course. The handful of security officers at the site wore their logo-emblazoned tactical gear, but the facility director, assistant director, the vice executive, and the heads of sales and human resources were all dressed appropriately for a high-profile business meeting. Cheaper suits abounded among the building’s small administrative assistant pool, which included both of the department heads and the three section heads. Abbas, the agent’s own assistant on this operation, was in a suit much closer to that of a section head. Most of the work-a-day staff wore shirts and ties, but not anything that _he_ would call a _suit_ . He found it adorable that they tried, even if their ill-fitting, low-budget, results disgusted him. 

_His_ suit had a tiny winking lapel pin on it, along with cufflinks and a tie pin. All were of the purest gold, and more importantly, all of them bore the distinctive, restricted markings of Saeder-Krupp Prime’s troubleshooters: the Drachenshatten. 

Behind his gleaming golden lenses, none of them could tell when he was looking at them, and so they all assumed he _was_ the entire time. They all withered beneath that gaze. They all fidgeted. They all squirmed. Everyone but the agent in his razor-sharp black suit and his golden glasses, and his Matrix support assistant, Abbas, were terrified. Only the two of them weren’t afraid. 

The Prime agent smiled, and it was terrible to behold. 

“Saeder-Krupp Prime has been sent to restore that balance.” 

More fidgeting. Nervous swallows. A murmur of fear crept across the room—even to the tanned, flawless, executives. One of them took a half-step forward and tried his best to smile confidently and appear useful. 

“Sir, please allow me to offe—” 

“No,” the agent held up one gloved finger—just one, not even to his own lips—and the entire room quieted. Dozens of people silenced as thoroughly as though a spell had been cast to muffle them. Many of them were quite accustomed to following orders with absolute and immediate obedience, but several were much more accustomed to giving them. The Prime agent had let the assistant director start to speak so that he _could_ silence him, so that they could all _witness_ it, so that everyone there could see how effortlessly and naturally the second most powerful employee in this office could be silenced. 

“I do not need whatever you were going to offer. I do not need your words, your thoughts, or your suggestions. Abbas and I, and all of you, are not ‘synergizing’ today. We are not ‘brainstorming.’ This is not the formation of a committee, it is an announcement. I am _declaring_ things, not beginning a conversation.” He took his time to let the words sink in, then showed his teeth with another smile. “You may nod if that is clear.” 

“Micronetics is a valuable part of the Saeder-Krupp brand, as you all well know. This office is a valued part of Micronetics’ day-to-day operations, as you also well know. You take pride in those facts, I am certain, and you must also certainly acknowledge you are paid handsomely for your services and loyalty.” 

He waited again. They nodded. 

“But _also_ as you all well know, this facility has been targeted not once, but twice, in just the last three weeks, by corporate espionage freelancers. All of whom escaped, if not unharmed.” He found the word “shadowrunners” distasteful, and so he did not use it.
