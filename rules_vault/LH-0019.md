While losses during the Week of Death have and continue to be somewhat offset by the recruitment and support gained through the newly embraced “Defender Movement,” we’re still facing distinct tactical disadvantages across the board. We’re still short on personnel, and our logistics networks are still recovering. Intelligence reports regarding the Disians’ updated timetable for their Final Project are unfortunately forcing our collective hands, and Project Nemesis is very much a “come as you are” affair.

We do have some advantages, however.

First, because the Disians failed to complete all or even most of their objectives, they left sufficient enemy forces able, and more than willing, to launch counter-offensives … with extreme prejudice. Second, the Week of Death attacks revealed several vulnerabilities in our security protocols and procedures. These holes have been plugged with new protocols. Third, as part of those new protocols, we’re reorganizing even more into a decentralized structure, which further mitigates the damage done if our cells and networks are compromised or destroyed.

Fourth, and arguably the most important, is that coordination has been enhanced as formal alliances with Evo, Horizon, Renraku (although ties remain contentious), and the Draco Foundation, among others, have been finalized. This includes the creation of a definitive command and control structure. Before the Week of Death, certain anti-Disian allies had agreed to work together in a loose coalition, but not within a functional chain of command. This hampered initial operations as certain elements preferred to “handle things their way” while differentiating protocols for intelligence sharing and executing operations led to problematic situations, the Week of Death being the prime example.
