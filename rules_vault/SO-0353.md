## Social Rating Example TWO

Jazz is a bit out of his element. He’s surrounded by angry Halloweeners in the Redmond Barrens. Someone convinced them that Jazz and his team geeked a bunch of their pals, and he’s going to be hard-pressed to convince them not to attack. Jazz is still wearing his fancy suit, and the ganger lieutenant has a total Social Rating of 3. In the previous example, this would benefit Jazz, but this is one of those situations in which having a lower Social Rating grants the Edge, and the ganger’s Social Rating is far lower than Jazz’s. The ganger gets a point of Edge on the opposed Influence (Negotiation) + Charisma test.

In these situations, the side with a Social Rating of four (or more) lower than their opponent is awarded Edge. This applies when an Influence (Intimidation) test is made, but it’s based on the gamemaster’s discretion. Any Social test that involves the SINless, contacts with street types, gangers, ghouls, and the like may also invoke this rule. Some of the scarier-looking armor and fashion provides a penalty to Social Rating, and this is where that could be a benefit rather than a hinderance. It’s also one of the rare cases in which having a lower Charisma attribute provides an advantage, albeit a rather small one considering that a character with a low Charisma is likely to also have a much lower dice pool for Social skill tests.

Note that some clothing and qualities provide a penalty to Social Rating except when having a lower Social Rating is an advantage. These include scavenged rags, cheap clothes, and the Hot Mess quality.
