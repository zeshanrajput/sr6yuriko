## **Wild Interactions**

**If you encounter a potentially hostile critter in the wild, don’t just reach for your guns immediately—you might be able to de-escalate the situation. This is especially ideal if a noisy altercation might give away your position on a run, but conserving your ammo is almost always worth a try.**

**First thing is, don’t bother trying to interact with a critter unless you have some idea of what they are. Depending on the critter, the right approach might be intimidation or appeasement. If you can convince a critter that messing with you isn’t worth the risk, they may wander off and leave you alone. This approach tends to work very well with most mundane predators, but don’t even think about trying to scare off a bullyphant.**

**If you understand what motivates a critter, you stand a much better chance of getting through to it. Does it just want food? Either give them some or fnd a way to redirect their appetite at a different target. Is it protecting its territory? Convince the critter you aren’t there to challenge it, and slowly back away. Does it have young nearby? Run for your life! These aren’t solid rules for every situation, just a few examples. Just like when you’re negotiating with a metahuman, knowing what a critter wants puts you in a much stronger position.**

**That said, if running or hiding is an option, consider doing that frst. Every encounter is a risk, and it’s important to remember that wild animals are called wild for a reason.**
