## Making an Easier Read

Everyone is looking for an edge. This is especially true when it involves other metahumans and making sure we get the right offer, the right information, or simply avoiding being on the receiving end of a bad deal. Not everyone can be born with special gifts like magic to help them read a person. Instead, most are left with their own powers of observation to determine an opponent’s intent.

Normally, people aren’t going to allow themselves to be strapped into a polygraph machine during a negotiation, but there are newer and better technologies available to help determine if someone is lying. Piecing together your own set of lie-detecting equipment is about assembling the right sensors and having the right training to put that equipment to use. Of course, there’s also the consideration of being able to use this tech and gear in real time, though with the help of specialized intelligent software that’s not as difficult as it was in the past.

**Body position, small movements, and facial expressions** all tell a story about a target’s current mood and emotions. Getting a read requires a clear and unobstructed view of the target, although cameras and other imaging equipment can assist with this. In addition, thermographic vision provides information on the target’s body temperature, which can fluctuate when anxious or lying.

**Microphones** provide a way to monitor data points like heart rate and vocal tone. Heart rate will tend to elevate when the target feels pleasure, or if they’re attempting to lie. Vocal pitch will quiver when the speaker is under stress or when telling falsehoods. Using these sensors requires an environment quiet enough that the heartbeat and tones can be detected. This means that a noisy room or other technology can defeat these sensors.

**Olfactory scanners** can detect pheromones or other chemicals expelled from the body, such as when exhaling, coughing, or during excessive perspiration. Or if the target is excited or aroused. But these are only part of the data set. Knowing why such things are being expelled is also important.

**Atmospheric sensors** can show the operator respiration levels and body temperature for a given environment; however, this type of sensor is generally difficult to calibrate due to the wide variability of parameters and other participants in the room. Including these sensors can help, but you don’t want to rely on these alone.

While having such technology available offers distinct advantages, the right training to interpret the data is still necessary. There are three main science knowledge skills that can be useful in reading someone’s intentions: **Kinesiology, Psychology,** and **Physiology** . These areas of study can help provide a baseline to understand the types of movements, behaviors, and expressions which can reveal clues to a target’s intent. Kinesiology will primarily rely on cameras as a technology tool, while Physiology benefits from the use of olfactory and atmospheric sensors. Psychology is often used to create a profile based on reading the target’s behaviors, but information received from microphones can help complete the profile.

Besides learning these skills, one can always rely on additional technology. Software packages exist that combine multiple knowsofts together with an intelligent agent to analyze all the incoming sensor feeds in real time. These packages are expensive and are what corps, clandestine organizations, or trained intelligence operatives use when conducting interrogations.

When using these skills, sensors, and technology packages, users gain a point of Edge for use on a Judge Intentions test for each method that hasn’t been countered. For example, if cameras and a microphone are being employed, but the target has an active white noise generator, then a point of Edge can be awarded for the test as long as the user has training in Kinesiology. An additional point of Edge can also be gained for each of the four technology and knowledge tools being employed. This also allows a user to bypass normal Edge limits.

So, what can someone do to prevent technology or skills from reading their darkest secrets? Fortunately, there are pieces of equipment that interfere and combat the very technologies being relied on. Clothing and cosmetics can block visual sensors, devices can generate noise at the right frequencies and volumes to disrupt microphones, and perfumes and other chemicals can obscure the body’s natural emissions.
