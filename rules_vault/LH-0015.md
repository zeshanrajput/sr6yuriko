## The Disian

## Shadow War Begins

Following the Week of Death, the Disians had several advantages. First, they had seized the initiative and put the anti-Disians on their back foot. Second, they held most of the cards in terms of media and the overall narrative. This allowed the Disians to paint their enemies as terrorists, criminals, and something for the general public to rally against.

- So, another Tuesday.

- Bangswitch

- Except this time, everyone **is** out to get you. Makes it harder to maneuver if you’re worried that granny running the local soykaf stand is about to turn your hoop in because she thinks you **look** like a terrorist.

- Brutus Ex Mechina

- Yeah, and you better believe several anti-meta types used the situation to their full advantage.

- 2XL



- It’s funny, there still hasn’t been a bulldrek, I mean official, reason given by the media, government, or corporation for the reason behind the “terrorist attacks.”

- Cosmo

- Of course not, the unknown factor keeps people paranoid and afraid. Why waste a perfectly good crisis?

- Kay St. Irregular

Third, they had—and still have—the numbers. Backed by at least three AAA-rated corporations (Aztechnology, MCT, and Wuxing), with rumored support from others (Saeder-Krupp and Shiawase), and elements from multiple national governments, the Disians have a formidable force of personnel and material to draw from. And that doesn’t even include the number of shadowrunners and other irregular assets they can hire. Fourth, being embedded as they are, the Disians can wield considerable political power to control military forces by using them against their enemies or forcing them to simply stand down.

- That’s why you haven’t seen large military groups being deployed. Not only is it a huge drain on resources, but someone will eventually start asking the wrong questions about what they’re doing and why. Being told to shut up and follow orders only goes so far. And the last thing the Disians want is, say, the UCAS military finding out about what **really** happened to their missing colleagues in III Corps. Better to keep them stood down, out of the way, and ignorant.

- Colonel Cobra

- Same reason why the International Mercenary Association was targeted by a false flag operation designed to paralyze the vast majority of mercenary units, some of which are on par (if not better) than some national or corporate militaries. If you can’t take out a merc the traditional way, hit them where they’re the weakest: in their contracts.

- Picador

- Some of us choose to fight anyway. Contracts are no good if everyone is dead.

- Iron Cav Actual

And lastly, reports indicate that the Harvester Network is alarmingly close to completion, or close enough to where the Final Project can be initiated. And once that happens, the game is over. These factors allowed the Disians to keep the pressure up and, for the most part, kept the anti-Disian forces on their back foot. But it hasn’t been easy, and it’s certainly been costly.
