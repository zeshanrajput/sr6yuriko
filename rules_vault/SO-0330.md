## Pixel Tags

Originally invented to create multimedia spectacles when used in the millions, pixel tags are extremely small devices (approximately the size of a stealth tag) that are capable of emitting different colors of light upon command. Large groups or batches of them are usually controlled by a proprietary device, but they can be individually slaved to their user’s PAN. Their function is simple: emit light of a designated color. Individually, they are barely noticeable in a well-lit area, but used in larger numbers they can function as a screen. Used subtly, they can create realistic illusions on surfaces.
