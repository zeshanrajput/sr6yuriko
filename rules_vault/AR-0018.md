## TALES FROM THE UCAS 

## What It Means 

I’m tempted to just type “hell if I know” and leave it at that. And in truth, that’s all I have to say about Missouri Valley, because I’ve got nothing beyond that write up. As far as the rest goes, there are challenges, but all I see is opportunity. We have the gang and Lone Star connections to keep us appraised of developments (Vicar is out of our control, of course, but we have ways of addressing that), and if we can keep Lone Star directed properly, the anarchists shouldn’t be much of a problem either. We need to use our influence to keep them pointed at each other, districting and fighting until we’re ready to make our move. There will be short-term chaos, but if we use our time wisely, no one will get ahead of us until we’re ready to make our move. 

Some of the trick will be making sure people don’t come back from the outside. If we make the area look the right level of undesirable, they won’t be tempted to reclaim the area. 

That may mean doing something about Buzzard. They can’t get any more investment, and they certainly can’t be profitable—or at least not significantly so. Taub needs to be taken down a peg. We know what he wants to be, and it’s what we can’t let him be.
