## News from the Unseelie Court

Posted by: Frosty \<04-18-83/1521:31>

I saw that Elijah has already made a post on the situation unfolding in the Seelie Court, but I thought I should fill in a few details and add some information from another point of view.

While I’ve always tried to stay neutral regarding the intrigues of the Courts, I suppose that assisting Harlequin and the Ebon Queen has put me at odds with the Seelie Court. I’ve been labeled an Unseelie sympathizer and a terrorist. But, looking at the direction the Seelie Court is taking with Dis, I think maybe I’m a little proud of that title.

I’ve already posted some of this, but I suppose a short recap wouldn’t hurt. The current situation started back in ’79 when Lord Gwyn, the former Eolaí, or guide, of the Unseelie Court, brought news to Harlequin that Aina Dupree, whom Gwyn was calling the Ebon Queen, was alive and being held as a prisoner on a distant metaplane. Harlequin was enraged by the news and set about making plans to free her with a passion, or perhaps mania. He had long believed that she had died during the closing of the Watergate Rift. Something he had blamed Ghostwalker for, so much so that he started a war against the Great Wyrm in an attempt to carry out some form of revenge.

It took a few months to make the arrangements and get a team in position on Fauth-Doshgoi. The final part of the plan was the simplest, but also the most dangerous. We had learned early on that Aina’s jailer was Queen Alachia and she would need to be distracted for the plan to succeed. Harlequin was to keep her occupied during the extraction by luring her to an isolated glade. I don’t know what transpired during their meeting, but I do know it ended in violence. The team of runners on Fauth-Doshgoi were successful in freeing Aina and brought her through the gateway Harlequin had created. I also realized something had gone



wrong when Harlequin didn’t return. I managed to make my way to the “glade” where they had met and found the ground shattered and scoured for dozens of meters in all directions, but no trace of Harlequin or Alachia.

Lord Gwyn took charge of Aina’s recovery, and I started a search to find out what happened to my mentor. While Alachia was not seen in the Court after the altercation, it was obvious that she was still active behind the scenes. I quickly found out I was unwelcome in the Seelie Court. In fact, the entire metaplane became unsafe for me to travel through. While unable to visit the Court personally, I heard the news that Garbh Filíochta had been replaced as the Court Jester by a handsome man wearing a golden prosthetic ear and going by the name Gan Duine. In the Court’s peculiar Béarlagair of Sperethiel, that’s something like “one with no name,” or John Doe. My connections soon confirmed that this person was in fact Harlequin, but without any knowledge of his past or even knowing his own identity.

This was the beginning of a long, frustrating process to try to find out exactly what had happened and what could be done about it. Aina eventually took Lord Gwyn’s place as an Eolaí of the Unseelie and started spending her time among the giant oaks of Brocéliande, in the shadowy moss-carpeted halls they frequent. Aina and I spent nearly two years chasing down clues about “Gan Duine’s” condition, without success. Ironically it was Alachia’s current ally, the Disian known as the Emissary, who provided the information we needed. He had been active in the Seelie Court for some time, attempting to sway them into an alliance with Dis. This creature approached Aina with the same proposition, and as a reward for her support, offered to arrange Harlequin’s freedom. Without committing either way, she eventually learned the details of Harlequin’s confinement, and we formulated a plan to free him on our own.

After the post in _Scotophobia_ , I recruited a team of top runners and secured the help of the Queen of Roses’ son, a fae living in this world with the power to open metaplanar gates. By evading the watchers at the known passages into Brocéliande, the team slipped into the Court unnoticed, freeing both Harlequin’s physical shell and the coimeádaí holding his mind. Aina performed a ritual to return his memories, but I’m not sure how successful it was. While he does have his memories and is sometimes the Harlequin I know, he seems lost within the flow of time. One moment he’s lucid and in the present, the next he seems to be reliving the events of a lost age where he is Caimbeul, a Knight of the Crimson Spire, or perhaps in later ages as the wanderer claiming to be the Last Knight of the Crying Spire.

After the extraction, Alachia stepped up her actions against the Ebon Queen and the Unseelie in general. Her personal campaign against Aina started soon after Aina’s rescue from Fauth-Doshgoi, but it had remained in the shadows. Now all pretense of civility or courtly manners was gone; it was warfare, and it was out in the open. Several courtiers suspected of ties to the Unseelie had disappeared, others were forced to flee, and several factions went underground as Alachia loyalists ruthlessly stamped out dissent. Soon, she started bringing mercenaries in through the Yellowstone rift. Many believed her plan was to scour the fractured metaplane of Brocéliande with them to hunt down the Ebon Queen and Harlequin.

The Emissary must have seen his opportunity and offered his assistance to Alachia—if she could sway the Court into forming an alliance with Dis. After Alachia put her considerable political power to the task, it didn’t take long for Lady Brane Deigh to formalize an alliance. A Disian portal was built out in the wilderness near the Seelie Court. Disian creatures and servants flowed through the portal and created a base of operations. More mercenaries, seemingly from many different metaplanes, were also brought in. With the help of Disian magic and their chimeric constructs, they started moving out across Brocéliande searching for the Unseelie.

At the Disian base of operations, a large device started to rise above the treetops. Alachia let it be known that the Disians had confirmed, beyond any doubt, that the mana of the Courts was leaking into the Sixth World. Further, she announced that the “Court” had decided to accept the Disians’ offer of help, stopping the mana leakage by severing the link between the metaplanes and permanently closing the Yellowstone rift. While the site is ridiculously well defended, Aina and a few other powerful members of the Unseelie have managed to get a look at the device being constructed. It’s their opinion that it is actually intended to rip open the rift and bring the Courts in full contact with the Sixth World, allowing its mana to be consumed by the Disians through their harvester network. If anyone out there reading this has any knowledge of Disian “technology” and can get a look at this thing, I would love to have some more opinions about what it actually is.

As for other news, I’ve heard that the Unseelie in general are highly motivated to oppose the Seelie Court’s current activity and are fighting back against the mercenary forces moving through Brocéliande. Engaging them with hit-and-run skirmishes for weeks now, bleeding them white while suffering almost no casualties. I also have reliable word that they are quickly mobilizing to oppose Dis on other fronts. As masters of asymmetric combat, the Unseelie will most likely use the same tactics to attack the least-defended Disian positions in the material plane, assuming that it will draw DiRESISTANCE, ATsian resources away from other fronts such as the Courts. Regardless of where they choose to attack the Disians, it will be good news and welcome help for the defenders of the Sixth World.
