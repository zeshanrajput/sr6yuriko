## complex orders

In most circumstances, commanding a critter is straightforward and simple: “Attack that person,” “Fetch me that thing.” Simple orders are obvious and direct, with clear directions. Whenever the target of a command is not obvious, or if more than one command is being used at once (e.g., “attack that person and then bring me that thing they’re holding”), it counts as a complex order, and there is a chance that the critter might not understand. In this case, issuing the command requires a Major Action. As part of this action, you may make an Influence + Charisma test, and any hits are added to the critter’s Comprehension test.

When it comes to deciding whether or not a command counts as complex orders, use these three criteria as a guide:

- Can the critter currently perceive the target of the command? If not, it’s a complex order.

- Is the critter being given more than one command at the same time? If so, it’s a complex order.

- Does it seem to the GM that the situation is especially confusing to the critter? If so, it’s a complex order.
