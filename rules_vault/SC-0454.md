## Government

- **Common life modules:** Academy Training, Activist, Adept Training, Agent, Astral Explorations, Bodyguard, College/ University, Covert Ops, Detective, Guide, Investigator, IT Support, Lawyer, Magical Studies, Matrix Security, Medical Doctor, Military, Nurse, Office Manager, Patrol Cop, Politician, Pragmatic Magician, Professor, Security Guard, Technical School, Think Tank

A state or government background has a wide variance in how the social adept’s background developed, depending on which government they grew up under and for which one they worked. First, it’s worth noting that this background would cover many social adepts who served a state or other government function through corporate contractors. These corps have distinct cultures (or several, in newly formed conglomerates like OmniStar), especially for corporate citizens. Still, social adepts who are Lone Star officers are likely to see themselves as Seattle or Austin cops first, OmniStar citizens second.

There are countless government functions where a social adept could develop the background for their abilities, skills, and entrance to the shadows. Naturally, espionage security services are the natural fit. Governments need spies and investigators just like the megacorps do. Because their abilities are often overlooked, social adepts who grow up outside a megacorp are sometimes drawn into areas that aren’t obviously transferable to shadowrunning—like public health, education, welfare, or other metahuman services. The social adept could have become a government lawyer, diplomat, teacher, benefits adjuster, or office functionary.

There is also a grey area where governments and corporations operate together in intergovernmental and public-private partnerships even beyond typical government contracting/outsourcing of services, like policing to Lone Star or utilities to Shiawase Envirotech. Characters with such backgrounds and experiences would be encouraged to develop logic and memory-based abilities to recollect discrete elements and programs, and negotiation skills to maneuver their way through the web of external and internal relationships. Of course, this assumes that the character developed their official skills and roles. This is _Shadowrun_ , and your PC is probably not the kind of person who goes with the flow and finds success in the normal

world. Instead, they would develop skills and abilities to enhance Stealth, Con, and Judge Intentions in order to exploit their positions as spies, as the insider for syndicates that counterfeit documents and identities, and as fixers and fences shuffling property to unauthorized purchasers.

The primary distinction for a social adept with a government background is that in a world where megacorporate power is unmatched, there is a pervasive sense of inferiority and resentment likely to follow the character and motivate their actions. At the same time, that sentiment sits alongside an _esprit de corps_ built on interpersonal relationships and ideas that can seem foreign to many within the corps, syndicates, or the streets. As with the largest megacorporations and other legacy institutions, most government civil service is considered a long-term career for those who are admitted. Indeed, that is also one of the conflicts inherent in the background: the social adept would have an understanding, or at least some awareness, of this distinction and might develop their skills to navigate that environment.
