## **Security Critters**

**The frst thing you have to understand is how excited someone who designed a security system is when they throw something at invaders that the invaders didn’t anticipate. I’m being completely serious here when I say that sometimes they watch live feeds of their secured areas just to see the moment when the opposition is caught by surprise, and then they sit there and giggle. They do. I’ve heard it.**

**So that is one of the primary reasons you’ll fnd critters in security setups—to catch us off our guard. We may have our security legwork down pat—scan for drones, scan for spirits, scan for guards—and we overlook a critter who can cause us a problem. This is especially tricky when they enlist the services of critters who seem just like part of the normal fauna—security birds and small mammals are all the rage right now because they are so easy to overlook on a scan. You have your mage check all the astral bodies in the area, and they might not remember to see if that pigeon or squirrel is actually Awakened. Or it could be they** _**do**_ **check, but the facility keeps the secret weapons locked up most of the time, so the mage tells you everything is okay, and then you go in, and you see a crow and think nothing of it until it swoops down on you and pecks your eyes out.**

- **Cayman**

**It’s not only that they’ll try to sneak the critters on you—they’ll also throw curveballs when it comes to how the critters will interfere with your work. Obviously, a critter that tries to rip your throat out is the most direct form of anti-intruder action, but it’s far from the only thing they can do. Critters can interfere with your Matrix access, spy on you from a distance, and even mess with the local manasphere. Any function on your team could be messed with by a critter. What’s worse, unlike the attack on your throat, some of these other effects can happen at more of a distance, so the critter does not have to be directly in front of you to cause you a problem. The headaches they can cause are multiple.**

**As everyone here should know, though, every problem is also an opportunity. Anytime a setup tries to employ something different, there are ripple effects, and those provide you with different ways to interfere with what they are doing.**
