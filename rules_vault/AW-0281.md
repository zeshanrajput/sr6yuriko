## First Hunt

When I next returned to the Citadel, I quickly made my way to where I’d seen the first golem. I hid in the rafters again and settled in for a long wait, as I had no idea if my encounter was a chance event or some sort of patrol. Since I didn’t know the answer, I decided to not attack the first golem I encountered but wait to see if there was a pattern. I waited and watched for hours, and I quickly came to the conclusion that I’d just missed one when I arrived and settled into my hiding spot. Something else also became obvious while I was waiting— there were several types of golem.

I wasn’t prepared to tangle with the full range of types I saw—one lithe, almost thin to the point of appearing malnourished, and made of an incredibly dark metallic substance, one made of a silvery metallic substance that resembled a normal human in stature, one that appeared to be made of brass and walked with a determined but slow gait, and the dark steel or iron type that I’d seen before—I decided to wait until there was a single steel golem. I took the chance to assense all of them, as well as gather as much as I could with my detection spells, but my findings were somewhat inconclusive. All I could tell for sure was they were all powerful in their own way, but the most physically powerful was the huge steel-colored ones.

Finally, I managed to get a situation where the others, if they stayed on the same schedule I’d mapped out during my observations, were several minutes away, while a steel one was nigh on below me. This particular one had a large shield and an even larger sword—the fragging thing was as long if not longer than my halberd. But since the others I’d seen had smaller shields and spears long enough to be pikes for a normal-sized troll, I figured this was my best chance.

Stowing my halberd, I dropped on the golem from above in the hope that my razors would do some damage to the head and neck before it even knew I was there. But as the saying goes, no plan survives contact with the enemy, so I was not surprised that I didn’t get the drop on the thing. What did surprise me was the fact that it hopped to theside and brought its shield up. I was dumbfounded! There’s no Ghost-damned way that something that large should be that nimble on its feet. Thankfully, my instincts kicked in, so I was able to avoid the golem’s sword as it came in for a skull splitter strike straight down on me. After finishing my roll, I pulled my halberd and took up a high stance.

Part of me was gleeful at having such worthy prey, and part of me was worried at having such worthy prey. A four-meter-tall golem that had to weigh five hundred kilos shouldn’t be able to move as fast as this one did. Factoring in that we had no reach advantage over each other, plus it carried a shield that allowed it to parry and block my strikes with relative ease, I was slowly being pushed backward.

But as I always say, we are a melding of man and machine and magic. As the damn thing was recovering from another impossible move, I began throwing spells. First thing I found was that drain was sucking more than normal, almost as if the mana itself was agitated and didn’t want to flow through me as well as usual, but given that stealth was no longer a factor, I started to snarl and howl as I centered myself to be a better conduit for mana. The second thing I determined was that this golem was _really_ tough. It shrugged off some of my most powerful elemental spells, while clouts just bounced right off. Thankfully, direct-combat spells did the job, though it still resisted them as well as some mages I know. While ducking and rolling and parrying, I worked through my repertoire, digging deep to see what worked and what didn’t. The answer—changing the environment and confusing it—led me to use an ice sheet and some illusions, and I was finally able to get the upper hand.

As the steel golem fell to the ground, I let out a sigh. That had been a lot harder than I thought it would be and had taken significantly longer, too— to the point that I could hear large feet clanking on stone from both sides. Time for stealth again. I leaped back into the rafters and waited for the patrols to investigate and then move on.

By my estimation, it was almost three hours before all of the golems returned to their patrol routes and schedules. I learned some more as I watched and waited. The black golems are some kind of ninja assassin types, as several times they disappeared from sight only to appear again elsewhere seconds or minutes later. The silvery ones were swift and light on their feet and often had bows or crossbows, although some had melee weapons. They were also the only ones that seemed even close to detecting me, constantly scanning the rafters as if they knew I was there, but they couldn’t see me.

The brass ones—well, I soon realized they weren’t brass. They were orichalcum! And they were very well versed in sorcery and other magical

arts. At first, I dismissed their spellcasting as cute, but as I watched, my respect grew and grew. The three I saw used some potent spells and had learned some intermediate metamagics. Given what I saw, my temptation to maybe cast a spell at one of them diminished very quickly—I’d not seen evidence of apotropaic magical techniques, but given everything else I’d seen, I was not going to put it past them. The orichalcum golems also spoke, which surprised me so much I almost fell over.

Once it was finally safe (enough) for me to return to the tower and leave, I did so with all due haste. That steel golem had been a beast, literally as it pushed me as far as some of the more powerful paranormal critters I’ve ever faced; and I wasn’t looking forward to tangling with the other types. I knew I would have to, in order to classify them and their capabilities, but I would do that another time.
