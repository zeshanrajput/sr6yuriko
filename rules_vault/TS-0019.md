## **LEPs vs. SPs**

**What makes one corporation (or subsidiary) a law enforcement provider and another (maybe another subsidiary of the same corp) a security provider? What the frag is even the difference? For the most part, it comes down to authority and accountability. Corporate LEPs all operate under a government’s authority, basically subcontracting the authority that was originally granted to the government entity by legislation. This situation comes with some obligation to hold to the same standards as that original law enforcement entity, and the LEP can be accountable to that original government entity. After all, if the service gets bad enough, or something blatantly illegal is going on, the contract can always be canceled for noncompliance and given to the competition.**

**Many of the corporations with law enforcement provider contracts started as private security companies and still offer those services, and they often do it with the same personnel. As an example to explain how that works, let’s look at a hypothetical Knight Errant setup. The Ares subsidiary Knight Errant XYZ Municipal Law Enforcement accepts a contract to provide law enforcement services in XYZ’s municipal territory. The corporation is legally and contractually required to uphold XYZ’s laws as written in the books. Their employees also have the same obligations and will swear an oath to do just that. Another Ares subsidiary corporation called Knight Errant XYZ Security Services operates in the same territory and is an extraterritorial subsidiary of an AAA corporation. It and its employees are not obligated to uphold or even acknowledge XYZ’s laws. Trooper Smith can be transferred between the two subsidiary corporations, both under the Ares umbrella, and operate under two separate sets of rules. One day he may be a sworn offcer of the law, serving the public, and able to be held accountable for his actions. The next day he may be guarding a corporate property and will cheerfully shoot you in the face just for being there, probably getting a bonus for doing it.**

**> And, of course, when Trooper Smith “mistakenly” bludgeons some poor old woman to death for shopping while ork, he’s removed from the police force, only to be transferred to Ares’ Wolverine or Hard Corps’ subsidiary where his “special skills” will be better utilized. On paper, it’s a reprimand and Knight Errant “disavows Trooper Smith’s actions” and insists that “he is no longer an employee of Knight Errant,” giving the impression he’s been sacked, while in truth, he remains an Ares citizen and now the proud owner of new criminal SIN.**

**> Sim-Eon**

**So why the dual systems? The simple answer is proft. Holding a municipal law enforcement contract is usually a money-losing endeavor, but having that contract is a big advantage when selling much more proftable private security contracts. The pitch is that the municipal force will always be there as a backup for the private security offcers and the private security contract holder will get priority treatment from the municipal force. There may only be a dozen guards at the customer’s facility, but there are hundreds on patrol that are only a commcall away. In short, well-managed municipal contracts can yield a fortune for the parent corporation, way beyond the measly payment installment from city hall.**
