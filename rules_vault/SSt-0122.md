## **Swamps**

**==> picture [233 x 4] intentionally omitted \<==**

**Swamps and other wetlands are usually not put in the same class of hazardous environment as extreme cold and heat. After all, while few people live next to Antarctica or the heart of Death Valley, people have been known to live right next to a swampy area—or fll it in to make their city. The climate that produces a swamp is often amenable to human habitation, and of course swamps don’t lack for water, which means they have the essentials people need. They also, though, have plenty of dangers that could trap you if you wander into them too deeply or incautiously.**

**This became even more true after the Awakening. Another critical difference between swamps and the other environments discussed so far is that while life can be sparse in cold and desert areas, swamps absolutely teem with life, fora and fauna both. Even in the Fifth World, a fair amount of these species were venomous or otherwise dangerous, but since the Awakening, some of them have become even more effcient in the ways they deliver harm to people.**

**This means that the amount of constant environmental awareness you need in a swamp is tremendous. You must keep an eye on the plants around you, be on the lookout for any animal movement (especially one coming toward you), and watch your footing. If you’re walking, you frequently can’t see the land you’re stepping on, which means a sinkhole or tangle of plants could be just waiting to trip you up.**

**Spending the night in a swamp can be a hazard, too. The assortment of wildlife can get curious about you and your possessions (especially if you’re carrying food), so if you don’t have any sort of shelter, they might start poking their noses in your business. A hammock of some sort, if you had the foresight to carry one with you, can get you a bit off the ground and away from trouble.**

**As far as other essentials go, the good news is that swamps are full of food and water. You just have to be sure to use those elements correctly. Do not** _**ever**_ **drink water directly from the ground, pond, or anything else. The amount of harmful bacteria and toxins swimming in there are not worth the risk. Instead, boil the water or make sure you have some sort of purifcation device. And make it a good one—this is not a time to cheap out!**
