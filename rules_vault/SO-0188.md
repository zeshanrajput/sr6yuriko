## Information isn’t Free

There are two main topics here. First, why bother posting here? And second, how much is it going to cost to be a member and get access to the reports? The higher the subscription, the more detailed the reports are likely to be.

So why should you post? I was seriously wondering that myself, but I’ve since seen the value of it based on what I could do with it. I had to spend a little quality time in Atlanta recently and had more than a few new people I was working with. I ran a couple of queries at Shadowland BBS and was able to pick the right place to meet and also avoided working with someone who had a high chance of working with Humanis. At that point, I was sold. I’ve been submitting reviews since then. It’s the only way to make it better.

The second part concerns subscription levels and fees. You’ll notice the sub is probably going to be cheaper than any bribes you’ve had to make in the last month, so that’s a win in my book. There are four subscription levels, but only the top three levels can run queries. According to the promo material, the higher the subscription level, the better the software that runs the queries. But not just anyone can get higher access. You need someone to vouch for you—only those with solid rep scores can get higher access.
