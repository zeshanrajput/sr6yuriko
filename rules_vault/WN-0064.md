## Mitsuhama

**Local Subsidiaries:** American Art and Culture, Amtrak Incorporated, MediaSim, Pentagram Publishing, Takai Development Corporation

When the Corporate Court released the new corporate rankings placing MCT as the top corporation in the world, it had a withering effect on the company’s NYC market. It should come as no surprise that the company’s focus has been on trying to force the Corporate Court to merge their functions with the MDC. In other words, the Corporate Court would control Manhattan and MCT’s seat at the table would be all but certain. That plan of action isn’t polling well with anyone—especially not the AA corps who would lose their limited powers as a result.

MCT is trying to gain footing by any means necessary. They opened with a hearts-and-mindsstyle PR campaign and publicly rechristened Newtown as New Harlem in honor of the history of that section of the city. All the while, their Takai Development Corporation worked to purchase land through shell corporations and make MCT the largest landowner in Manhattan. This strategy hasn’t gone unnoticed, and the influence of the world’s top mega continues to shrink. It’s obvious to anyone watching that MCT is just looking for an excuse to resort to more drastic measures to regain power in the region.
