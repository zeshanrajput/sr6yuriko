## Brokerage X

Brokerage X knows how the game is played and is dedicated to using the rules against the corps. For years, they’ve been using runners to feed them insider information and then trading based on what they learn. They sold Ares short when they got advance word about the Excalibur’s weaknesses, then bought it on margin when they learned about the deal to move to Atlanta. They made a ton off the downfall of NeoNET and got decent ownership percentages of some surviving subsidiaries.

They are arriving at a challenging point. They have an impressive portfolio overall, and they have a voice in several proxy votes, if not in major corporate boardrooms. They are ready to start throwing their weight around, especially if they can engineer mergers between corporations that they own significant shares of, finally making them a significant minority owner, if not a majority. Once they reach that pinnacle, they’ll face multiple dilemmas. Do they tear the corp down to fulfill their anti-establishment mandate? Do they use it to build up more of a power base, allowing them perhaps more sweeping moves in the future while, in the meantime, playing exactly the kind of game they’re supposed to oppose? And do they remember the lesson of Art Dankwalther and what happens when you become big enough to make the corps nervous?

- They do, and that’s one reason they are far more decentralized than Dankwalther, as a lone individual, could ever be. But that comes with its own complications when it comes to decisionmaking.

- Kay St. Irregular
