## Shiawase Arms Nemesis II

Named after the Greek goddess of divine justice and vengeance, the original Nemesis was designed to be a user-friendly, person-portable infantry squad support weapon. Built around a high-rated safe-targeting system, the idea was to help prevent friendly-fire incidents among lower-tier or less-experienced troops while still providing a solid weapon for higher-tier experienced ones. On many counts, the Nemesis was a success. Among the units it was designed for, reports indicated that friendly-fire incidents did in fact decline among those who used it. Although those same reports indicated that the safe-system was somewhat prone to hacking and that the ammo-feed system was extremely susceptible to foreign objects and debris. The new Nemesis II for the most part has corrected these problems but is now made of stronger, lighter-weight materials similar to those used in the proposed “Mobile Warrior” line of weapons. The result is a weapon system that is twenty-five percent lighter than its predecessor. While still a decent weapon system, these lightweight materials don’t handle recoil like the previous model, making the Nemesis II slightly less accurate in combat.
