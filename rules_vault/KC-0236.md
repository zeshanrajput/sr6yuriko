## MCT

How could Mitsuhama Computer Technologies not take a look for the code? When you’re on top of the megacorporate rankings, you fight tooth and nail to stay there. And when you have the resources that MCT has, you can bet your favorite nuyen that they are hiring some deniable assets to hunt down and investigate any leads they come across. Being the trusting mega, they would totally not track the team they hire and then send their own team in after them if they tried to sell off the information. Never.

So what are they after? It’s gonna depend on which department is doing the hiring. There is definitely some inter-department competition going on. Most different parts of their organization are trying to find a copy so they can figure out how they can apply it to their own problem space or maybe even take down a rival. But there are a couple of specific departments that are spending big MCT scrip to get their hands on it.

Let’s start with one I was surprised by. Mitsuhama Magical Services is really pushing hard to get a copy of the code. When I dug into what the job would entail, it was a pretty simple run, but it was really light on what they wanted you to go find. If you squint really hard at the job offer, you could see that they were looking for the Kechibi Code. It makes sense in a way, because it’s not like the finger wigglers really understand the Matrix.



- Just because their area of expertise is outside the binary realm doesn’t mean they are oblivious to it. And before anyone asks, I have heard they wish to apply the algorithms against various arcane formulas to determine if it can recognize patterns that they have been unable to. This makes sense when you look at the complexity involved in spell, spirit, and metaplanar formulas.

- Ethernaut

- One of the more persistent rumors was that MCT was working with a group of (according to internal documents) “new advisors” on the potential for developing new kinds of “magical technology.” Nothing new there, but what I found interesting was a memo that came across my virtual desk indicating that MCT was experimenting with some kind of new “Matrix modeling” software code to assist with new magical formulas or equations (their words, not mine). Not sure how that would work, but MCT sent a hit team to take out some deckers in Osaka who got too close and let GOD take the credit. So whatever they’re working on, MCT is willing to kill for it.

- Pistons

The other main area that was paying out was a joint venture between Mitsuhama-Benguet Mining and Marubeni-Sumitomo Energy. The rumors around those jobs are that they’re looking for a way to get a leg up on Shiawase and boosttheir flagging energy biz. Given those two organizations are working together, it’s probably looking for petrol or radioactive materials. Some friends who took a shot at the job were investigated pretty heavily. Something about making sure they didn’t have any connections to GreenWar or the like. The team that got sent was definitely looking for software, but when they arrived at the target, the hashcode they were given turned up nothing.

The thing that puts a chill down my spine is wondering if any of their security teams got ahold of the Code. Can you imagine Zero-Zones where they start finding your weaknesses the second you step in? They probably see it as a cost savings because they won’t need to expend as much ammunition on an intruder. Or maybe now you get to survive because they know you’re vulnerable to being manipulated. It’s pretty fragged.

When you’re number one in the ranking, you don’t stay there by assuming your investments are smart and that a wide portfolio will prevent an attack. When you start hearing rumors that your market position is vulnerable, you need to figure out how, so you can spot the weakness and prevent it from impacting your business dealings.
