## Translation Ear

Renraku invented an entire cyberware niche with the release of their trademarked Translat-Ear ten years ago. Shiawase and Evo have since released their competing MoshiMoshiMimi and Babblefish products respectively. No matter the brand, translation ears fundamentally operate the same way: Smart systems detect perceived language and provide a real-time translation. Translation ears effectively allow the user to understand, but not speak, a foreign language. Because this understanding is at the basic skill level (p. 99, _SR6_ ), idioms and other figures of speech are translated directly rather than being put into context, so both slang and nuanced conversation can potentially confuse the listener. If the user also has an image link, this augmentation can translate written text as well.

**Wireless bonus:** Translation ears must be wirelessly enabled to function at all.
