## Zeta-Imperial Chemicals

CEO Katrina Thyssen served on the NEEC for years before cashing in for corporate wealth. Her time in the highest levels of government took her around the world, and often to NYC, where she met and dined with representatives of the Corporate Court and all its megas. Those relationships helped Z-IC win a majority vote to replace Areson the MDC. The corporation joined the MDC in order to cement their footing with the Corporate Court. Z-IC looks to be winding up for a run at taking over a handful of A and AA corps in order to further diversify their offerings in a bid to become an AAA corporation. Of course, that means having control of a voting share that belonged to JRJ, presently on loan to Spinrad Global. There were a few no votes cast their way—Saeder-Krupp in particular objected to the addition of a company whose oldest, and perhaps most substantial alliance, is with SpinGlobal.
