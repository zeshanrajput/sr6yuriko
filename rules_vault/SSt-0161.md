## **Interacting With the Law**

**Of course, sometimes you just get unlucky and can’t avoid a run-in with a badge. Say you have to steal something from a target’s Westwind, Your decker’s too busy watching reruns of** _**Blimp Samurai**_ **to hack the door and you don’t feel like waiting around, so you lean over and break the car’s window—but by sheer bad luck, a cop walks around the corner at that moment and spots you.**

- **How is probable cause different from reasonable suspicion?**

- **> DangerSensei**

**If that happens, your approach should be basically the same as before they rolled up: don’t give them a reason to be any more suspicious than they already are. Act the way “normal people” do around cops. Normies (especially wageslaves) have**

- **Don’t start with me, lawyer-man.**

- **Hard Exit**



**In cases like this, a little preparation can go a long way. If you’re going to steal something from someone’s car, dress like you can afford that car. If a cop spots you breaking in, show them how the (fake) car-control program on your commlink and complain about how it wouldn’t let you unlock the door. Trust me, the cop won’t bat an eye at something like that—they’re just going to chuckle at the poor bastard who locked themselves out of their car and go on with their day.**

- **For extra safety, you can go above and beyond and disguise yourself as the car’s actual owner, complete with fake SIN. I did that once, and the officer who found me took pity and called his friend in the cybercrime unit to unlock the car.**

- **Mika**
