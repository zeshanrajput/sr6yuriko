## What Remains 

The party’s over, and the band is going home. The real question is how thoroughly they cleaned up. 

I’m not foolish. I know they didn’t leave anything super-huge. But I don’t need anything super-huge. Not with the way things are now. Any little thing, anything that could give the Smuts an advantage, would be good. We know how to do this, we know where to look. Scour the training grounds to look for unexploded ordnance. Check closets and back rooms where a sloppy cadet might have left something. These are the possibilities—but to take advantage of them, we have to get in there. 

The physical barriers aren’t a problem. Some of them weren’t in great shape to begin with, and with no one to watch them, they’ll just take some time to get through. The astral security, in the form of spirits, is a little trickier, but that might just be a matter of time. The UCAS mages will summon them for a while, just to keep an eye on the place, but eventually they’ll have to focus elsewhere. It might be a matter of days, maybe weeks, but I don’t think it will be much longer than that. 

Then we figure out who gets what, and what they’ll do with it. 

The whole landscape is uncertain. People have been moving behind the scenes to consolidate power ever since the blackouts started, but most of them have been anxious not to draw attention to themselves. No one wants a target on their back just yet. But the time’s about here—the military’s gone, and there’s a power vacuum to fill. 

Everyone’s keeping a low profile—there are few recruiting posters or manifestos being circulated, and no clear plans of action. But that doesn’t mean things aren’t happening, and from my standpoint, it’s best to figure out what’s going on before massive balls of power start rolling downhill. 

I’ve got sources out gathering info, but we’re still at the bits and pieces phase where I assemble stuff and figure out what it means. So first, the stuff. Then I’ll try to type my way into figuring out what it means.
