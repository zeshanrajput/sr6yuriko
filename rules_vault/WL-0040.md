**Once the frst fossils of the** _**Smilodon**_ **cat were discovered in the 1830s, the population’s imagination was captured around this large, predatory cat that had fangs reaching twenty-fve centimeters long. **It differs from the Siberian tiger in its tendency to operate more in family groups. Siberian tigers are solitary hunters, with the exception of mothers with cubs. There was some allowance for daughters to be allowed in the same hunting territory, but this was not consistent. With the reappearance of the Siberian tiger, they have adopted a more communal behavior that is similar to that of the African lions. This strategy has been so successful that in some places, they have completely replaced the local population of Siberian tigers.**

**Saber-toothed cats have a number of ecosystems where they have been found. Some of the earliest sighted of the creatures was** _**Puma novalis**_ **in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains. This species closely resembles the mountain lion (** _**Puma concolor**_ **) in coloration but is signifcantly larger. Since this frst discovery, others have been found such as the saber-toothed leopard (** _**Panthera obsidianus**_ **), which is related to the jaguar (** _**Panthera onca**_ **) of Amazonia or the saber-toothed tiger (** _**Panthera tigris timor**_ **), which is related to the Siberian tiger (** _**Panthera tigris tigris**_ **). These new species share many of the same characteristics as their progenitor species. For example, the saber-toothed tiger is substantially larger than the saber-toothed jaguar.**

**Their coloration takes advantage of the tall grasses that make up their home territories. When on the hunt, it is nearly impossible to detect these stealthy animals. The adults in the family group will work in concert to ring in their prey, and the tigers will attack once the prey is looking the other way. The size of the family group feeds into how often they need to hunt. The average adult saber-toothed tiger needs between ten and ffteen kilograms of food per day. Because of this, the family groups are often limited to four or fve adults. Saber-toothed tigers hunt both during the day and night, as opposed to the Siberian tiger, which hunts primarily in the day.**

**Focusing on the saber-toothed tiger, the largest of the various cats, we fnd an effective grasslands hunter. This large predator is bulkier and shorter than the Siberian tiger, weighing up to 300 kilograms and having a maximum length of fve meters. The saber-toothed tiger’s skull features two prominent upper canines that can reach thirty centimeters in length, while the lower canines are reduced in size when compared to its close cousin.**
