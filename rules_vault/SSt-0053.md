## **Graceful Exits**

**The mark may have given you everything you want, but you still have to smoothly walk away without gaining any negative attention. Let’s talk about how to do that.**

- **So, getaways. We’re talking the aforementioned van with fake labels, right?**

- **Webwalker**

- **Oh, that’s just the surface. It’s so much more than the van. There are nearly infinite possibilities. Like blocking off some getaway streets. Or setting up a crisis that draws a huge crowd (think fireworks in the fertilizer factory) so you can slink away in the chaos. Or you can even make the van the getaway decoy while you putter away on a pizza-delivery scooter. There’s no one definite way to make a getaway. A little study will help you find the most effective way to get away, clean and easy.**

- **Crash**

- **Sure. But the Backalley Makeover is a fairly solid choice, too, though it might be a slow escape depending on the terrain, and a quick exit is almost always recommended since they’ll be scanning every fragger in a kilometer radius for the first thirty minutes after the alarm goes off.**

- **Kia**

- **I thought that was a short con?**

- **Webwalker**

- **Well, the getaway would likely fall under the short con, but it’s so fragging important. Ensuring a clean getaway, shaking a tail, safeguarding whatever data/people/critters you may have liberated. You wanna get out and get your dosh? Make sure you have a way to get away safely and unobtrusively that keeps the risk to you and your team minimal. (DON’T SKIMP ON PAYING YOUR WHEELMAN, for the love of all that is holy, unholy, and from Chicago! It’s a worthwhile investment.)**

- **Rainbowsmite**

**> Your love is two-thirds appreciated, Rainbow.**

- **Red**

**> Sure. But it also works for any situation you need a quick change. You don’t have to improv it, you can stash an outfit and props where you plan to make the switch. The janitor coveralls you were wearing? They can come off and burn in a prepared spot or bundle into a duffel if you want less residue and won’t get spot checked, and you’ve got civvies on underneath. Or a security uniform. Or a different disguise, with wigs and makeup and all kinds of stuff to entice or offend the nose and cover up the smell of blood and cordite and sewer.**

- **SimonSays**

- **You can also lace your ditched clothing with chems if you’re trying to avoid organic trackers. There’s some drug derivatives that completely kill a hound’s sense of smell for a period of time but then wear off within in an hour or so. Helps out down the line since the pooch can smell things normally. It helps sell the illusion that there was no trail.**

- **Rainbowsmite**

- **One of my favorite gadgets back when was the C^2 grenade. Take a chem grenade and fill it with Cleaner Cleaner or any other chemically disruptive compound, anything that disrupts any traces of you that could be used for forensic or ritual tracking. Toss liberally when making your getaway. Best part is that chem grenade shells are cheap and easy to get, and industrial solvent is so common as to be comical.**

- **Red**

- **This is where it’s fun to mix and match tactics. A really thorough escape takes advantage of corporate borders. Janitor van leaves Mitsuhama parking lot, goes into AZT parking garage. The sticker is removed, the paint job changes, the RIFD tags shifted, and the cargo spread out or replaced. If they find the van at all or pull you over, you’ve lost all the cleaning supplies. Now you’ve got boxes of stuffers to deliver, and certainly no guns or gear. Have a nice day, officer!**

**You can do this over and over for a few rides and over multiple corp borders if you have the time, inclination, and resources to justify such paranoia. AZT isn’t going to give MCT their security recordings or let their goons pursue anyone on their property. Don’t push it, though, because AZT will sniff out the juice of a run and take you in regardless. Same goes for any spot you take advantage of and the people who own it. It’s not exactly jurisdictional immunity, but it takes advantage of those who have it.**

- **Turbo Bunny**

- **Never discount one of your crew with a very young-sounding voice on speaker yelling “But Daddy, you said you’d take me to Stuffer Shack when you got home and I’m hungry!” and just walking out the door with an exasperated, haggard expression. You have no idea how many times that works.**

- **EB**

- **Combine the quick and the easily dismissed and you’ve got the best of every quick-con component. Season to taste and keep moving.**

- **Distractions are another good example of the short con as an exit. Confounding security by complicating their day at the worst possible moment (i.e., when you are on your way out the door with the goods) can go a long way toward securing your escape. If the drek hits the fan in a half-dozen areas dotted throughout the complex at the same time, you’ve just decreased the likelihood that you’ll be caught because the target only has so many resources to deploy. If you’ve got cash to burn (or wisely negotiated your deal to include expenses), a group of gangers breaking out into a theatrically staged firefight on the target’s stoop as you are headed out the back can work wonders for your chances of a clean getaway. Hell, find a few folks who are down on their luck, willing to make a quick buck, and don’t know enough about you to finger you after the fact, and pay them a pittance to harass or pester the folks at the gate. If you can afford some disposable drones, those come in pretty handy as a distraction. A solid decker can leave all sorts of gum in the gears of the local Matrix with just a little bit of prep time and a few minutes baked into the plan to incorporate it. A couple spirits deployed in the right place at the right time can wreak astral havoc and buy you a little breathing room. Just like with any tool, though, you want to be careful about what you leave in your wake that can be tracked back to you.**
