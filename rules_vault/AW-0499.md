## Materializing a Metaplanar Form

Astrally projecting into a metaplane or the Astral Sea, either by an astral quest or some type of astral passageway, grants the traveler the Materialization power, creating a metaplanar form that includes immunity to normal weapons. It is a process similar to a spirit materializing in the physical realm. The body created is a dual-natured reflection of their physical body. It is created from the material of the metaplane and adapted to the nature of that reality through metaplanar translation. Once materialized,

travelers may not astrally project—again—but can dematerialize and return to an astral form, moving within the version of the astral plane that overlaps the metaplane. Assensing may be used at any time, as the materialized form is always dual-natured. Just as in the astral plane, the Astral skill may be used as a defaulted skill while in this dual-natured state. Astral constructs carried along—such as foci, spells, or a branded vehicle—will also materialize and dematerialize with the traveler. Bound or summoned spirits who owe services to the traveler will only materialize in a compatible metaplane. For example, a spirit of water will not appear on the metaplane that has an aspect of fire.

The metaplanar form recreates an equivalent of any cyberware or bioware integrated into the traveler’s physical body. In addition, other physical items very familiar to the traveler, such as clothing, armor, weapons, electronics, and perhaps even drones may also be recreated as part of the metaplanar form. They may not look the same, due to metaplanar translation, but they will function as remembered. The maximum number of drones that can manifest is limited to the Logic attribute, and each attempt to manifest one requires a Memory (3) test. Vehicles will not normally manifest regardless of how well remembered they are, but those branded with True Fire (see p. 41) create an astral form that can be carried into the metaplanes as if they were a focus. These vehicles will appear with an aura of fire but will otherwise retain their normal appearance, regardless of the translation paradigm of the metaplane.

All manifested items are reflections created from the traveler’s memory and formed from the material of the metaplane; while they may function as remembered they are not the actual items. Any data collected or images recorded will not be transferred back to the original. Ammo expended or damage to the equipment will also not be carried over to the physical versions. These reflections of equipment are created from the material of the metaplane and will disappear when the traveler de-materializes. Gear that has materialized this way is considered “normal” on the metaplane, as it is created from the material of the metaplane. Materialized weapons are still normal weapons, and beyond the method of creation—and maybe operation—they are not in any way magical. Even translations that merge them with the traveler’s body do not make them magical attacks.

While manifesting on a metaplane, the physical body enters a coma-like state for as long as the mind is away in the metaplanes. Time spent in metaplanar form does not count toward the time limit for maintaining an astral form. There is no limit for time spent in the metaplanes, but extended stays may require some medical support for the physical body. The perception of time in the metaplanes does not run at the same rate as in the realworld. Weeks or even years spent in the metaplanes may just be hours in the real world. Metaplanar forms all have a single condition monitor that uses the larger of the Physical or Stun track. Damage to a metaplanar form may be healed as normal (unless it’s from a failed trial during an astral quest). The silver cord still connects to the physical body while in the metaplanes; the connection is weaker in some ways, stronger in others. Damage does not transfer between the two until they are rejoined, and any damage to the metaplanar form becomes Stun damage to the physical form after returning to the body. If a metaplanar form takes enough damage to go into overflow, that traveler suffers the same fate as a disrupted spirit. They are immediately sent back to their body in the physical realm. Damage to the physical body is not reflected in the metaplanar form, but if the body dies while the mind is away, the metaplanar form will disintegrate when the traveler attempts to return to the physical world. Return to the physical body from a metaplanar form is achieved by simply willing oneself to return. There is no test to return from a manifested form.
