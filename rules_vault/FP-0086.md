## **Brokerage X**

**Brokerage X people will tell you they’re not in the business of knowing the future—they’re in the business of making money. Further, they’ll also say that what sometimes seems like knowing about major events before they happen is actually just having inside information before almost anyone else.**

**Often, though, these are distinctions without a difference, as knowing some things about the future would absolutely help Brokerage X make money. But as their Kechibi-code-related activities showed, they are quite capable of being more proactive in their approach, manipulating events to get their desired result. And nothing helps you manipulate results more than knowing what’s going to happen before it does.**

**This contributes to a natural overlap between Brokerage X personnel and Convergence hackers and shows why Brokerage X is one of the primary sources of Convergence employees. Like the Seers’ Guild, Brokerage X personnel divide with Convergence in one signifcant matter, but instead of being disagreement in whether the event will happen, it’s about what to do about it. While Convergence is focused on averting disaster, Brokerage X would like to know what kind of disaster is looming before they decide if they want to stop it. There has always been a strain of burn-it-all-down mentality in Brokerage X, and that strain would like to see just who would be hurt by this upcoming event. If members of the Big Ten stand to take signifcant damage, Brokerage X could well be inclined to not just let the event happen, but encourage it.**

**> I’ve never mastered the art of using capitalist tools to bring down capitalism, but I wish Brokerage X members with that inclination the best of luck.**

**> Old Crow**
