## The Job

Neo-anarchists of the Storm Raven sect have gotten wind of what’s happening and want the runners to feed info to them. To win them over, they have a tip—an earth spirit is digging up some long-covered spots that have been rich with old corporate data from before the quakes. The tech may be a little behind and the mud a little crusty for slinging, but it all still sticks the same.

The target is the basements of the Arsenal near Fifth Avenue and 64[th] Street. The quake damaged access to the basement, but a free spirit of earth named Alfred has been going around and opening up access to buried locations all over the city. What data was held in the Arsenal has been lost to time, but the runners’ employer is interested in everything that is getting uncovered. This one just happens to be in a rather high-traffic, high-profile area, so they need a team of skilled shadowrunners to get in quietly and retrieve whatever data was being worked on.

This particular access point has already been claimed, or at least secured, by NYPD, Inc., in order to protect it from random looky-loos. There are only two NYPD, Inc., officers (use Lone Star Patrolman, p. 206, _SR6_ ) at the entrance. They can be bypassed via a quick knockout or some slick talking. They aren’t the brightest cops, and they’ve been feeling nervous ever since they started this assignment.

The access tunnel is a two-meter-by-three-meter hole about twenty-five meters from the north wall of the building, near the Delacorte Clock, with a steep angle of dirt that walks down to the foundation level of the Arsenal. At the foundation, another hole has been made, this one also two meters wide and three meters tall. It has been reinforced by an arch put in place by the spirit of earth who dug the hole.

The basement is large, just shy of the sixty-meter-by-fifteen-meter floor plan of the Arsenal itself. The walls and columns of the basement are thick, and the area itself has been out of use for decades. The basement access opens to a large but crumbling taxidermy shop with a single exit door and two large storage closets. Out the door is a hall that

The systems are a little out of date, requiring an Engineering + Logic (10, 1 minute) Extended test to get them back up and running. Inside, the systems in the regular rooms are just catalogues of the species present, items on loan, and items in progress, all dated years ago. The research station info is more interesting as it points in a specific direction of research. The zoo brought in early paranormal animals that displayed abilities specific to mental manipulation. Studies were performed by federal scientists, and clues in the files make references back to potential mind-altering drugs being delivered to or used by the CIA.

Formulas and data are all a little old but could net the team a solid 10,000 nuyen payout from the right corporate source. Then again, if the runners have a contact in the UCAS government that they trust enough, they could increase that payoff to 25,000 nuyen if they hand over all copies of the data.

These areas of the lab are not uninhabited and have long been used by a small pack of four vampires (p. 218, _SR6_ ) all led by a rather twisted free kindred spirit (Force 8, p. 148, _SR6_ ; add Fear and Movement). They’ve been able to come and go via the vents or astral travel, but now that a regular

door is open, they know someone will eventually come hunting for them.

They can be negotiated with, rather than fought, if the runners are willing to help them find another place to stay or to seal up the entrance Alfred made.
