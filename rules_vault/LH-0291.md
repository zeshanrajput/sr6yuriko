## The Darkest Place

## \<07-23-83/0000:05>

I remember everything, yet I can only recall some; it’s like my brain is protecting me because what I saw almost broke me.

The famed locus, the key to the Disians’ Final Project, was powered up and ready to receive the material plane’s stripped mana. The otherworldly swirl of colors within the darkness that went on for infinity. I only glanced at it and almost lost myself. The Disians in their regular form were at the edge of a ritual circle I could barely comprehend, yet I could feel the strongest magical energies I’d ever encountered.

And in the center of the circle was Hanna.

I barely remember the Red Samurai charging in with lightning speed, their swords cutting down every Disian in their path, their life essence bursting with their deaths … and sucked into the locus; their otherworldly screams echoing in my mind.

Only one remained: the Auditor. I suddenly yelled, just in time to save him from a samurai’s blade. I proclaimed what he had taken from me, and that I claimed the right to take his life, in a voice that contained more power than normal. The samurai nodded and stepped back.

Looking down at Hanna, seeing her body broken, I wanted to rant, to take my time doing to the Auditor what he’d done to her and so many others. But I remembered the box. Inside was a simple clear crystal the size of a thumb. I then acted without thinking and walked up to the wounded Auditor and tossed the crystal to him. As soon as he caught it, I shoved him with all of my might into the locus.

His screams echoed longer than the others, but I didn’t care. Several heartbeats later, cracks started to form in the locus as it shattered and collapsed into itself at the same time.

The next several minutes were a blur. We grabbed Hanna and made our way out of the compound, past the scores of dead, past the burning vehicles, and onto the open fields of crimson-stained ice. But that image didn’t phase me; the massive wave of mana energy surging toward us in a color-churning sky did.

In that moment I thought we’d failed.
