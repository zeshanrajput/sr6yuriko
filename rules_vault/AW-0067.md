## The Cardinal Poles

The Elemental planes, while separate, cannot be discussed completely without referring to all of them as a whole. Case in point—the elemental planes are so close together that they brush up against one another forming what are known as the cardinal poles. They are perceived as spherical spaces radiating from the junctions where two elements mix, creating a unique environment. The diameters of these poles vary, but they are at least large enough to accommodate a city.

The areas near any cardinal pole are, as you might expect, a bit weird. Surrounding each pole are the two elemental planes, splitting the horizon vertically, like two large windows. The mixing of elements differs but is stable for the associated spirits to interact.

**>** And at this point, I should interject that some may perceive this to be a hyper, bubble, or fractured metaplane as Dr. Gordon conceived. While he may be correct in one of those definitions, I suspect that there is much more to the elemental planes. In all our travels, myself, G-Nome, even Doctor Gordon combined, no one has seen such natural stable connections between metaplanes.

**>** Magister

If there is a concept of evolution/mutation for spirits, it happens at the cardinal poles, where some have adopted new abilities or a hybridization of both elements, becoming immune to some flaws and earning new ones. One thing for certain is that the mixture of elements makes it safe for both types of spirits to travel to. Because of this, the cardinal poles have become trade and diplomatic hubs for various spirits and beings, elemental and otherwise.
