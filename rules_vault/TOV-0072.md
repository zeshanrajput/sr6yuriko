## S pirit of Obatala

## (Spirit of Guardian)

Spirits of Obatala usually appear as crusading men dressed in all white. These spirits are often associated with creation or creativity, but also associated with drink, mistakes, and self-improvement. The orisha are associated with both the Rada and Petwo nanchon. Adherents who call upon spirits of Obatala may refer to them as Alabalashe, Baba Araye, Baba Arugbo, or Olufe, depending on the aspects they wish to invoke.

The Obatala orisha prefer the color white, but often this is paired with bold accents of red or purple. The typical ritual offerings include white hens, snails, white melon soup, and other white food. These offerings are often cooked with honey. Any ritual sacrifi ce involves animals that match the gendered aspect of the lwa that the summoner seeks to commune with.
