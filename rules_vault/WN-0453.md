## Aftermath

Be aware of any ramifications of what the PCs decide. This can be as simple as who they have built trust with and who is angry with them, but it’s also about who has what tech, whose standing has improved, and who is feeling more desperate. If Dis, for example, loses their portal under Governors Island, they will feel more desperate and thus will ramp up their activities. Any encounters the PCs have with Disians in the future should reflect this increased urgency. Similarly, if the PCs act to increase Mitsuhama’s cash crunch in any way, Mitsuhama will start acting more aggressively to close deals and find some short-term financial gains, with little regard to who they hurt while doing so.

Additionally, the gamemaster should be aware of who (if anyone) has access to Animus technology at the end of this campaign. Anyone who has it will use it to alter memories to their advantage, and even if the memories eventually revert back to reality, this has the potential to cause a variety of chaos that could make life quite interesting for the PCs for a while.
