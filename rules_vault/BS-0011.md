## What Is Essence?

Our understanding of Essence has evolved and changed over the years. Early on, it seemed to be a spiritual echo of a person, then it was a kind of

idealized mental picture manifested on the astral plane. At one stage, it was just the “shadow” of who you were.

One thing that has become more obvious over recent years is that our understanding is incomplete and still changing. Perhaps this is not surprising— the Awakening was not that long ago, and we have had to unlearn a lot of folk-knowledge that had sprung up in the years when magic was dormant.

Our early understanding of Essence was pretty good as a first approximation, but we have refined it since then. We initially thought that each metahuman started with a “pure” or “unblemished” Essence. Religious folks who looked into this often thought of it as a soul or spirit. In any case, the accepted wisdom was that Essence started “full” and could be diminished as the vicissitudes of life took their toll. It was thought that drug abuse and addiction polluted (or separated you from) your Essence, as did large-scale trauma and even cyberware. The rationalization was that as your physical body diverged from your idealized or “natural” body, this reduced your Essence. This is not entirely wrong, but there are gaps in this theory. When it didn’t work—when someone ended up with more or less Essence than the theory predicted—it initially was written off as being connected to past traumatic events or just a statistical outlier. The few people who didn’t have a reduced Essence but who had undergone augmentation were ignored or assumed to be a measuring mistake.

- That’s the way I always learned it. The amount you were born with was the most Essence you could get, and every implant reduced it.

**>** Bull

- Don’t forget, the previous research was generally put out by someone wanting to publish. You have to have a neat ribbon tied around those. You can’t have statistical anomalies messing up a perfectly good research paper.

**>** Neon Fireball

Research in this area is made harder because we are uncertain of the nature of Essence and how to determine whether a particular person’s Essence has been reduced. There is no measuring stick for Essence—any estimate of how much Essence there is relies on subjective observation by Awakened people, who often interpret what they see in different ways.

Anyway, it turns out that Essence is more complicated than we once thought. Our old understanding is like Newtonian physics—pretty good most of the time, but it breaks on certain edges. One odd thing we found is this: Some people who have had augmentations have not lost Essence because of them. Not many, but some. Once researchers found this, we realized that something was excitingly wrong about what we thought Essence was. Digging deeper, we found that those people who had augmentations but

The most obvious example is people who have altered their bodies to better fit their gender identity. Those alterations usually don’t alter the Essence of the person undergoing the procedure, at least not as much as they would otherwise.

When you realize that nature can screw up and make a body that doesn’t fit with an aura, then other things become apparent. There are, for example, a number of people who are a different metatype from their siblings. Some of those people choose to get metatype affirming surgery, whether that be bulking up to become an ork or troll, or shaving bones and muscle to appear more like an elf or dwarf. For some of those people, their aura is diminished by that surgery, for others it is not, and in fact when someone has surgery to affirm their Essence, it can result in a stronger, more vibrant aura.

We think this happens when you change the outer body to match the inner Essence.

This just changes the problem, though, because it raises a new question: What is the inner Essence? Short answer: We don’t know, and we may never be able to know. How do we define the fundamental nature of metahumanity? Are we changing over time? Is there just one foundational part of ourselves? Heavy questions, and not something that many people spend time thinking about. Most of the research in this area is conducted by scholars locked away in academic halls or groups who are attempting to meld mana with their corporate profits, such as Wuxing. These topcs are also explored by less-formal groups of Awakened.

So, what do we know? We have confidence about a few things:

We know that your Essence can change over time. Over the years, we have developed treatments to repair lost Essence. This shouldn’t be a surprise, in that everything alive grows and changes over time. Essence is like this—it evolves, grows, and changes as we get older. We don’t seem to be able to consciously control how Essence changes over time, but it reflects how we live.

We know that the different metatypes are different expressions of Essence, but it’s the same kind of stuff. We’re not even really sure what is happening, exactly, when auravores such as vampires drain the Essence from metahumans.
