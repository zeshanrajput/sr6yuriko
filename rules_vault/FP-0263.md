## **Making a Connection**

**The criteria are pretty straightforward:**

**1. You must be a member of North America’s indigenous people,**

_**and**_

**2. You must hate the Anglos and wish to see all of them off the continent.**

**And that’s it, really. There isn’t any half measure here. However, between saying you meet these two criteria and being allowed to join Warpath, there’s a bit of a gap.**

**Since the ’70s, we have had a much stricter vetting system for new recruits, although joining back in the ’40s and ’50s wasn’t that much easier, to be fair. Nowadays, the bulk of our membership comes from the ranks of those who support and sympathize with our cause. Those of interest will be observed and evaluated, and if they have shown their worth, we approach them with opportunities.**

**New members will be approached in person or in the Matrix, and if they agree to join, they will go through a probationary period under the watchful eye of several established members. Failure to properly demonstrate or prove their commitment to the cause can be deadly. While ex-Warpath members exist, they are quite rare and are only allowed to live once they have proven that they do not pose an ongoing risk to the organization.**

**New members are sent to one of our training facilities, where some indoctrination takes place, along with training in combat, explosives, survival, stealth, and infltration/exfltration skills. Training camps associated with the New Guard will also include economics and negotiation skills for some members to help acquire land.**

**While actually joining Warpath is quite diffcult, working for us is somewhat easier. Under the right circumstances, any members, even those of the Old Guard, will work with people from outside the organization. Obviously, it’s a lot easier if you are a known sympathizer or supporter so we don’t need to worry about your allegiance. And Native American runners or shamans/adepts linked to Native traditions have it a bit easier. Ultimately, nuyen is a good way to ensure some degree of cooperation.**

**Most jobs are quite dangerous, and we won’t go out of our way to help you if something goes wrong. The job you have will depend on who hires you.**

**Leif-Killers are less likely to hire runners, but if you happen to be hunting a former member of Winternight or Ex Pacis or are disrupting an operation run by those groups, they will gladly form a temporary alliance and cooperate with you for as long as needed. Impress them enough, and you might even get some information or future jobs out of it.**

**The Old Guard mostly work in-house. They rarely hire runners, and when they do, it’s usually to cause a distraction or get additional muscle for a tough job. Either way, it will likely be bloody and loud. They are quite competent, so it mostly makes up for the barely hidden hatred and spite.**

**The New Guard and the Coderunners are the ones who make the most use of runners. The jobs are generally not that different from what any Mr. Johnson would offer. You can expect just about anything, including theft, sabotage, wetwork, infltration, blackmail, crashing servers, or political terrorism. The Coderunners tend to be friendlier, but you can expect a high level of professionalism from both groups.**
