## Out to Sea

A cruise ship is stalled in the Caribbean. It has gone radio silent, and boats and planes approaching it have been fired at. Mr. Johnson wants the cyber ninja and their compatriots to get to the ship, find out what’s going on, and rescue some of the wealthier passengers.

Getting to the ship is the first hurdle. It is two hundred miles west of the southern tip of Florida, and both machine guns and missiles fire at any visible approach.

The ship itself has sixteen decks and holds more than five thousand passengers. If the cyber ninja and their team get aboard the ship, they’ll find that the passengers are a little puzzled about why they are not moving but generally content. The ship is fully functional and they keep being fed. They claim they have been in touch with family and co-workers, telling them they have been delayed (they haven’t; their communications have been intercepted and fake replies have been sent to them). They’ve heard gunfire from the ship, but they’ve been told it’s just to scare off pirates, and the situation is under control.

The cyber ninja is going to have to investigate the ship without letting the captain and crew know

what they are up to. As it turns out, the captain is responsible for this whole affair, and he had planned it well in advance. He has decided to secede from the entire world and use the cruise ship as the first part of a new island state. He believes that if he treats the passengers well enough, they will accept his plan when he gets around to explaining it to them.

He worked very hard to get a crew who is personally loyal to him, and so far they have not questioned his orders, partly because he has told them of dangers and rampaging pirates if they continue forward. Whether they will stay loyal when his story starts to fall apart is an open question. Clearly the captain is not fully sane, so dealing with him will be tricky. If he is out of commission, the pilot could probably bring the ship home. If both are out of commission, the situation becomes trickier. The runners might have to only get their targeted passengers home for now.
