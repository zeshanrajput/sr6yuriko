## playing Advice

People often think first of a PI’s skills with their fists or a gun, but in truth, those are secondary considerations, or perhaps tertiary. Their observational skills are important—they have to see details that other people miss, and they have to make connections that others may not (it helps, of course, if these connections are genuine instead of imagined). People skills are important, too, whether they prefer persuasion or intimidation. They need to find ways to get people to tell them things they don’t want to say.

But in truth, probably the unifying trait for all private investigators is sheer will. They may get bullied, dismissed, beat up, or otherwise mistreated, but they don’t give up. They will pursue the truth even when everyone, including their own common sense, tells them not to.

This dogged persistence should be a core part of playing a private investigator. When one plan doesn’t work, they come up with another. When no plans work, they go and talk to all the parties involved and see if they can shake things up.

Private investigators should be able to play face roles and combat roles, allowing them to take a flexible approach to many situations. While they’re willing to dive into a situation and play things by ear, they usually have a purpose for what they do. When they try to get a rise out of someone, it’s to see how they react. When they turn on the charm, it’s often to deflect suspicion. And when they pick a fight, it’s because either they’re desperate or they think they can win it.

Private investigators also think a lot about relationships. Their contacts are central to their work—they reach out to them as a matter of course at the start of any job. They are also always watching other people’s relationships and seeing how they can use them. Does an employee seem overly devoted to their boss—or overly disloyal? Can the private investigator ferret out who is involved in illicit affairs and turn that information to their advantage? Can they see who is bribing whom and navigate a path through those relationships? Those are the questions on their mind, and if they do a good job of answering them, they’ll succeed at their work.
