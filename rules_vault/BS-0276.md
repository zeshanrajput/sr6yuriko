## Geneware and Nanoware vs. Traditional Implants

A question we both hear a lot is: What good is geneware (or nanoware) when traditional implants do a better job? We’ll try to give a few answers here. First off, we’ll concede the point that in a one-to-one comparison, neither geneware nor nanoware can affect the same scale of change gained by cyber- or bioware. But that’s not their strong point—both are subtle technologies that provide equally subtle benefits. Both geneware and nanoware usually work well with most other augmentations, even those that typically don’t work with other implants. There are also several benefits gained with these technologies that can’t be duplicated with traditional ’ware. Nanoware is hard to detect, requiring a nanoscanner to notice its use. It is extremely versatile, as colonies can be swapped out very quickly. This tech can even be used without installing a nanohive, providing an Essence-loss-free augmentation. Geneware can be even harder to spot—and with shuffle, near impossible. It can provide direct benefits, making you slightly tougher, stronger, smarter, and faster, while also increasing your potential beyond what would normally be possible. This tech allows you to break your own biological limitations with a low Essence cost. Nanoware and geneware may not be your first choices, but they shouldn’t be ignored. They could give you a little extra advantage that puts you ahead of the competition.
