## Breadbox Case

A breadbox case is a slang term for a cyberdeck or cyberhack built with cheaper or out-of-date components, wired and hacked together to make them functional using a quantity-over-quality approach. A breadbox case cyberdeck or cyberhack is not portable. It takes up at least as much space as an old desktop computer, but most are sprawling affairs of devices that take up a whole room. It takes an hour to set up or break down a breadbox unit, and it’s utterly useless when not properly set up. The advantage of a breadbox case is that the nuyen cost for any of the cyberhack and cyberdeck hardware is reduced by fifty percent. You also

have effectively unlimited capacity for case modifications. This option can also apply to home commlink systems (commonly called dataterms), and megacorporations often use cyberdecks installed into larger terminals to make them less vulnerable to theft (commonly called cyberterms).
