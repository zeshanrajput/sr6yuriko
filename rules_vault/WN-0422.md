## The Job, part one

The level of opposition encountered when returning to Governors Island will depend a lot on how much noise the team made on their last trip (mission 27, p. 105). If the team slipped in undetected, there will not be any change in security (see mission 27 for details on the island’s defenses). If they got caught and stirred up the Ares hornets’ nest, perimeter security will now include an increased rotodrone presence, with patrols one minute apart, backed up by ground patrols of rigged Steel Lynxes armed with Stoner-Ares M202s and Roadmasters carrying ten-person squads crisscrossing the island in an alternating pattern.

The cave leading to the Airlock facility is unchanged regardless of Ares’ or Aztechnology’s

level of alertness; it is exactly as it was when the runners last passed through it. Aztechnology has not replaced the spirits guarding the cavern or moved any of the stones that were blocking the passageway. If the team was undetected during their scouting visit, the steel door to the portal is in the condition the runners left it in. If they were spotted by security on their last visit, the lock has been replaced with a rating 6 maglock tied into the Airlock host. In addition, a wired camera is now mounted above the door.

Past the door, the cavern buzzes with activity. The portal is flaring frequently as teams pass through in both directions, moving crates out and carrying them down to the dock. Other crews can be seen loading the staged crates into the small submarines. With the Aztechnology workers focused on their tasks, it won’t be difficult to slip through the active gate.
