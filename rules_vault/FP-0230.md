## **Making a Connection**

**There are two signifcant obstacles to connecting with or hiring the Tiding: getting them to respond to you and affording them.**

**As far as connections go, there’s a relevant saying from old-time Chicago politics: “We don’t want nobody nobody sent.” You may have the name or contact information of someone connected with the Tiding, but if you do not have a direct referral from someone Tiding people trust, you will not get anywhere. On top of that, the people they trust to give them referrals don’t let just anyone through the door. They ask lots of questions and always meet potential referrals face-to-face. They are people of means, so if they’re truly interested in a meeting, they can fy a long way to make it happen.**

**There are a few things they look for before they make a referral. First, you need to have money. You’re not going to hire the Tiding for anything under six fgures, and they want to make sure you have that before you meet with anyone. This means having six fgures to spare, not just barely having 100,000 nuyen in various investments and bank accounts. If you want a referral, you can’t just count on a nice suit or dress to make a good impression. You should be the real deal.**

**Second, the Tiding is not about doing basic research. They exist to help you learn things few people know, not to inform you on basics like who the largest shareholders of a particular corporation are. The people who are going to make the referral will have a conversation to see what you want the Tiding to do and what you know about the subject. If your subject matter expertise is shallow, you won’t get anywhere.**

- **I think you’re setting the bar too high here. It’s not that you need to know every little bit about the thing you want to learn more about—you mainly need to show that you know something that the general public doesn’t know. As long as you can make it clear that you have a leg up on John Q. Public, that should be enough.**

- **> Plan 9**

**Lastly, they’ll look at your character. This doesn’t mean you have to be a Boy Scout or anything. They know how the world works—they know certain amounts of dishonesty and double-dealing are par for the course. They’ll want to know if you’re going to abuse the power they might give you or if you’re going to screw them over. Evaluating those things is highly subjective, so they’ll be going with their gut. They’ll take some time in conversation feeling a potential referral out, getting to know as many aspects as they can.**

- **Depending on who you’re talking to, they also might cast some spells on you to see if they can trust you. If you block or counter them, your potential referral is dead in the water.**

- **Jimmy No**

**I already touched on the cost aspect—the Tiding won’t listen to any proposal that gives them less than 100,000 nuyen, and that’s a foor they rarely touch. Usually, their deals top a quarter million. If you really want to hire them, get ready to pony up.**

**But if you want to just talk to them? That rarely will get you anywhere. When they have information that could bring them hundreds of thousands of nuyen, they’re not going to just part with it in casual conversation. Even if you don’t get their best information, though, you can often get them to share a little more than the general public knows, if only because they act a little like drug dealers—they’ll give you the frst taste of the good stuff, hoping that’ll make you pay for more.**
