## Chaos in Real Time

## Posted by: Snopes

## Newsflash! Macao

Native genius Gu Guanyu for Yingyong Research Tasking has been seen with Light Clouds designer Becky Wu Ping at the Li Li Ji sushi bar in Macao City. Yu Ji Wen of Modern Fashions Ezine managed to interview the couple briefly before personal security for Guanyu ushered them out of the restaurant. When pressed for the delicious details, Ms. Wu Ping declared, “I have discovered a kindred soul in Gu, who reached out to me in order to discuss a new line of self-defense item-ware that will be sought after by the trendy and the safety-conscious alike. I hope to have many more opportunities in the future!”

Okay folks, it’s official. Bonds and I worked out a way to track strange fluctuations in exchange markets, and once we kicked it off, we were flagged almost immediately—not by Yingyong, but instead by Kanabo. And to be honest, we were amazed, because we didn’t realize Kanabo was even investment-enabled.

It started when we realized there were company evaluations that looked remarkably like reports we had both seen in the wake of the Megacorporate Audit. Kanabo’s supplier chains were in a state of heavy exchange, and we tracked them back to an operator who goes by the callsign of Keiretsu—someone associated with agents within Shiawase’s MFID subsidiary. We managed to link other primary trades to no fewer than half a dozen accounts overseen by Lei Yimu. So yes, Shiawase has agents actively involved in operations against Kanabo and their 14K investors. We also witnessed transactions involving other banking agencies throughout southeastern Asia. None of them large firms, but all of them vetted and bonded appropriately.

It was also happening at speeds that our own hardware was hard-pressed to keep up with. We both realized we were watching two versions of the code go up against each other. The financial numbers were impressive, but not so overwhelming as to trigger major alerts with the larger exchange bodies. All of it ended abruptly when the transactions originating from Keiretsu stopped. We do not know what happened, but in the end Kanabo had earned nearly 3,000,000 nuyen in inventory shares and liquid capital. Total trade war time: a mere forty-two seconds. We were lucky to have even caught it. More than 30,000,000 nuyen in shares were put into unstable trading waters by both sides, but Lei Yimu’s gang took the win this time. It wasn’t a nanosecond buyout, but if this were scaled up somehow, the ramifications are obvious.

- In fairness, we sat and watched markets for nearly four hours before catching this.

  - Mr. Bonds
