## Vampire’s Invitation/Fake Handyman Plot Hooks

The Invitation is going according to plan… until the mark puts a spanner in the works. The mark has some dark, dark secrets in the basement. The kind they’d kill to protect, or a collection the runners would fit perfectly in:

A Hot Day Play is going great until their spouse shows up. Talk fast and think faster, and remember: if they’re arguing, they might not be looking your way…

A Shell Shocker works great until your fake wounds are being examined by an off-duty medic.

A Missionary play backfires when the mark turns out to be a zealot of a different faith and decides to make the runner a convert by any means necessary.

Badges work great when you have authority they respect. So, what happens when there’s a drug lab or forgery operation or hostages hidden away and you’re too dangerous to be left alive?

sniper positions or hard access to datalines might be a reason. Maybe you only need to get in to shut down the local security, snatch the mark, or plant some sabotage. In that case, the mark might end up knocked out early on once you’re outside of prying sightlines. Or dead if you’re a fragger like that. This isn’t that uncommon a move for assassins, as I understand it.

The classic, however, requires a little bit of a team effort. At least one of you keeps the mark busy while the other one uses the bathroom or something, buying you a brief window to snatch some valuables, plant a bug or tap or bomb, map the location, snap some pics … raid the fridge …

Handymen can easily get the run of the house as the mark takes at least one of them on a tour of wherever they have trouble. Don’t worry if that’s where you need to be, like a terminal or Matrix router or something. Once they show you, one of you takes them somewhere else to mess around with another utility (“Oh, yeah, this might be linked to an R-84 limiter, old piece of Shiawase tech in fuse boxes. Can you take me to the fuse box?”). They’re flipping light switches, turning water on and off, anything you like that keeps them away from where the action really is. For bonus points, charge them for your time.

Census Takers and their escalated Badge variants can keep people in one room for an interview or audit (I swear that word is a psychological weapon) while the other inspects the property. Probably one of the most effective ones out there.

Missionaries and Hot Day Players are probably going to have the hardest time finding an excuse to leave the room outside of going to the bathroom, and that only lasts so long. I consider it a safe play, but also a rushed one.

Shellshockers might get someone to run to the other room for supplies, but their reflex is going to be helping, meaning you can’t control their behavior. Pretty good odds you’ll get in, but pretty dicey once you’re inside.
