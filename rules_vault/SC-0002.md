## Introduction

The world of shadows is vast and diverse. Every run is a puzzle with more than one solution—if you stay in the shadows, you’ll find the really is more than one way to skin a cat (you’ll also find some runners who take that saying far too literally). Going to steal a prototype? You could blast your way in, sneak in, charm your way past the guards, or hack the local host to give you permission to walk in as an authorized employee. Trying to remove a high-ranking corp employee from one company at the request of another? You could pummel them into submission, persuade them to want to leave, sneak them out, or convince them that what they thought was their life was a dream, and their new job is reality.

Creativity and imagination are invaluable in carrying out these various plans, but you also need the skills to help you travel in whatever direction you choose.

_Shadow Cast_ provides information on twenty archetypes who have skills that push some of the normal boundaries of shadowrunning. There are some that follow darker paths than many other shadowrunners, like the Dissonant technomancer or the Bloodpanther, who works the shadows while also serving the nation of Aztlan. There are characters to wield traditional skills with different twists, like the face fatale, who specializes in seduction or the street samurai blade fighter, who shuns firearms and prefers to operate with sharp steel in

close quarters. There are a few people you might not expect to see in the shadows, like the street doc, who most runners see at the end of a job when they need some stitches, or the academic mage, who seems more comfortable in a scroll-filled library than a trash-filled alley. These and more are all discussed in this book.

_Shadow Cast_ is not only about providing a broader range of character options, but also about providing tools for these options. Each archetype has a variety of backgrounds to help players and gamemasters think about where the character might come from and how that shapes their behavior. This includes suggestions on game stats that could be used for different backgrounds.

Each archetype also includes plot hooks and qualities to help flesh out and use the character, along with advice on playing the character. This latter section provides both strategy and tactics, helping players use the range of characters in this book in a variety of situations.

The end of the book has some cards showing sample character stat blocks for some of the archetypes in this book. This previews a full deck called the _Mug Book_ .

The goal of this book is simple: to help players and gamemasters experience the full range of characters in the Sixth World and to find fun ways to put those characters to use. Enjoy _Shadowrun_ ’s infinite variety!
