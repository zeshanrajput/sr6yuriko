## **Ice Spider**

## **(Theraphosidae arctos)**

**Got at least two solid hypotheses on where these things came from, and neither is going to help get rid of them—that takes fre and frepower. Two different breeds of these spiders technically exist. There is a northern ice spider in the Arctic region and a southern ice spider living on Antarctica. Both appear similar to the basic body shape and frame of a tarantula, but they are signifcantly larger (up to two meters in their head, abdomen, and thorax) and are covered in white and silver fur patterns better suited to their icy domains rather than the tarantula’s desert camo of black and brown. The northern ice spider preys primarily on large deer, rabbits, squirrels, the occasional fox, and when feeling particularly ferce, a wolf or two. The southern ice spider preys primarily on penguins and science researchers.**

**The remarkable similarities between the two species have led parazoologists to assume they have a common progenitor. This progenitor has come from one of two places, depending on whose hypothesis you favor. Dr. Jason Heidel Richmond offers a terrestrial hypothesis where these spiders actually came from labs that were utilizing them for research at both the North and South Poles, and they escaped, adapted to the local environment, became toxic from the environment they were exposed to, grew to the size that they are, and**

**So now we should talk about how I almost fell victim to an ice spider. We were doing research in Antarctica, and I was off doing an evaluation to see if a glacial rift that was forming due to natural ice degradation or whether it was some form of astral rift forming in the ice, causing it to split and melt as it shifted between metaplanes or locations. This particular location had a rather deep series of crevices and rifts for me to explore. Within, I discovered the presence of at least a dozen ice spiders. Their silken icy webs became suddenly abundant, and I decided it was better to look around than to start messing with their food-delivery service. It didn’t take long to fnd the cavern at the base of the crevasse that led to a pantry of sorts. Several large cocoons were suspended above with faint living auras still present in them, along with another half-dozen along the walls.**

**The place was also littered with bones, and I decided it was well past time to back out and bring in the thugs. It was not the best timing, and with radio contact unlikely to put it mildly, I could only speculate on what was happening when I heard the gunfre erupt from our basecamp. I hightailed it back as best I could in those conditions, but it’s never easy (or wise) to move too quickly in those icy regions.**

**The camps were ablaze, and the fres were the only thing keeping the spiders at bay. Several dozen, ranging in size from a baseball to a Eurocar, were rapidly leaping through the air from one tech and doctor to another. Security was close, but the spiders were too close to people and the equipment to use anything big or fashy. They kept it tight and pointed, focusing on the smaller subjects. They knew better than to kill them and thus stunned and penned as many as they could before the rest fed.**

**Let me just say, it was terrifying start to fnish. At least three of these were big enough for me to ride and could easily have fed me to a collection of their babies.**
