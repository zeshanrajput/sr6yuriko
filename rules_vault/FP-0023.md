## **Opposition Report**

**Dadeson: Who have you pissed off bad enough for them to want to wreck your little Asintmah allegiance?**

**Sarkenbleck: Alliance. We don’t owe allegiance to Asintmah, it is simply an iconography and core to stand around. A pure world without all of humanity’s dark corruption.**

**But to answer your question, yes. We have conficts with those who don’t see the troubled path the world ambles down. The list is long, as those who have “worked” to build themselves a niche of power through magic or selling transhumanism as the way to a better future do not want their world to crumble.**



**In truth, the worst among these are Evo, the Atlantean Foundation, the damn DIMR, MCT (especially their metaplanar research and Matrix research divisions), Renraku, the Seers Guild (and that new group of seers, Convergence), most of the NAN for one reason or another, Amazonia, Aztlan, both Tírs, and Yakut, just to name a few. If they’re a corp or country run by mages or those heavily focused on transhumanist and Matrix tech, then we’ve made problems for them.**

**We try to keep it light enough to avoid retaliation. Not out of fear, but out resource conservation. Wars don’t beneft progress. If we make a large move, it’s going to advance our agenda in a huge way.**

**Now, we do end up with our fair share of personal vendettas. Those who think they have the right to join any elite organization they want but are rejected by ours, usually cause more problems for us than the Big Ten. You won’t see a single face from the Brackhaven line at our meetings, and that has been problematic. While we align on a few points, we differ on something pretty fundamental, and that is enough to keep him out. He’s not working on an agenda for a better future for the majority, he’s just working a future that doesn’t include those he fears or doesn’t like.**

**Since we’re on them, let’s talk dragons. Obviously, pulling the plug on magic sends them back wherever they came from, but with GW doing some kind of plane-walking, our world doesn’t seem to be their only domain. At least for the greats, so the younger dragons take issue with us pretty regularly. One particular opponent is Urubia. Efforts and actions in and around Seattle have been stymied so far by her counter plays. She’s good, but she’s outmatched, and her days are numbered. She may think of herself as the Red Queen of the Barrens, but we’re already putting things in place to dethrone her.**

**We know Evo has an issue with our “Pure Body, Pure Mind” campaign, but so far, it’s surface level in our direction. We’ve been undermining their research ever since we got the inside lowdown from a monad named Darian Flynn. His security knowledge and access were pivotal in setting up some long-term plans utilizing both his access and monad tech.**
