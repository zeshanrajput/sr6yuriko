I met an absolutely slammin’ hacker onAnd there’s one other thing, if you’ll permit me a moment of ego: I got good.



**> Wait, Slamm-0’s asking permission to be arrogant now? That’s new. > Pistons**

**Getting good meant I didn’t need to do phishing scams that would get me into bank accounts with a few hundred nuyen. It meant I could fx my gear properly when it broke, rather than relying on a half-competent kludge. Best of all, it meant that just because someone was offering me work, I didn’t have to say yes. I could start using parameters—what did the job pay? What was the risk? Would it be fun?**

**If the job paid enough, I could afford to not work for a time. A lot of the time, it was a good idea to take some time off and let the heat die down. Assuming I negotiated properly, that downtime would be downright enjoyable. I could do what I wanted. That’s a great feeling—as long as you know what you want. That’s so important that I’m going to make it its own section.**
