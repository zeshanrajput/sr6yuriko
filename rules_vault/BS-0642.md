## Cybermancy Rules

Of all the dark arts and terrors of the Sixth World, cybermancy is among the worst. The unnatural fusing of magic and machine is so anathema to the natural order that its mere presence causes astral pollution. Frighteningly enough, cybermancy has made just as many steps forward as any other technology has in the past twenty years.

Cybermancy is a difficult and expensive process involving both mystical and technological expertise. It begins with enough augmentation to push a subject’s Essence to the very brink. Though medical science is advanced enough to keep a body alive even when the brain has long ceased functioning, the procedures involved with cybermancy are far more complex.

Normally, when one’s Essence becomes too low, they simply pass away, as their spirit or life force slips away. A strenuous and expensive blood magic ritual must take place in coordination with the surgery, complete with creation of a formula and a harrowing metaplanar quest. After this, a ritual takes place to create a sort of spiritual “net” that binds the fleeting spirit to the body. This net doesn’t allow the spirit to escape due to excessive implantation. This doesn’t only apply to cyberware—excessive bioware may cause the same result. Certain pieces of cyberware, in addition to a cocktail of apoptotic inhibitors, steroids, nanotech, and gene therapy, are required to keep the subject alive while their body keeps trying to die.
