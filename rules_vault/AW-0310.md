## The Bureaucracy

The bureaucracy of the Place of Clouds is run entirely by air and water spirits, and their approach is most accurately described as laissez-faire. They show no interest in restricting rights, including travel and mining rights, provided that a party makes an attempt to work with them. Visitors who show no deference to the bureaucracy are largely ignored, as are those who violate agreements. The bureaucracy makes no exclusive agreements and are more than happy to sell mining rights for the same island to as many parties as are willing to purchase them. The one thing that is almost impossible to get from the bureaucracy is any form of decisive action—including, or perhaps especially, enforcing agreements. This has been heavily exploited by magicians representing the interests of businesses and magical groups from Earth, but is perhaps most problematic in regards to the intrusions by Disians. The closest thing to getting the bureaucracy to enforce agreements or defend the Place of Clouds from hostile entities is the issuing of letters of marque. These letters of marque effectively deputize a party to oppose violators with force—though there is no more oversight of these privateers than of any other party acting within the hyper-metaplane.

The key thing to remember about rules and bureaucracy within the Place of Clouds is that they are nearly nonexistent and have no teeth. The environments themselves are beautiful and often tranquil, and the moods of the inhabitants easygoing and carefree. This makes it natural to feel relaxed and safe, but if anything bad happens to you, don’t expect anyone to come to your rescue. When conflict occurs, the spirits keep their distance.

The only place where the bureaucracy seems to have any binding or enforceable power is within the Spire, and this is largely limited to construction or renovation rights. If you wish to purchase, construct, or modify a building on the Spire (including the air docks), you must acquire permission from the bureaucracy. No changes are allowed without their approval, and unlike deals that affect the Place of Clouds beyond the Spire, the bureaucrats take them very seriously. If anyone attempts to build, modify, or destroy structures within the Spire, the changes they attempt will reform into their pre-established shape within moments.
