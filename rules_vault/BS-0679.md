## Forward into the Possible

The future of human engineering is not cybernetics, or genetic engineering, or even nanotechnology by itself. The future is the blending of these technologies. In order to erase the inefficiency of human systems, we must converge the various branches of scientific exploration to create new human structures that replace the functionality of our existing systems. This is evolution. This is also freedom in its purest form, because through bio-robotic mimicry, we are no longer beholden to the evolutionary shape of these biological functionary parts. Instead, we can shape the parts to match our needs, as well as the functional needs of our society.

We have the technology to decide what we want to be at a genetic level, and the old language of homo sapiens is not enough to describe that. We are already posthuman. What we are is a species in flux. What we are meant to become is not entirely biological and no longer isolated. Technology enhances our bodies and our minds. The next step is standardizing sidecar protocols and discovering how to use them to merge human thinking. Ideally, the personal identity must be preserved, but the individual must be part of the whole just as the honey bee is part of the hive.

We are mere decades away from human transience. Shifting consciousness to a new body will become the norm. Sidecar protocols—permanent merger with artificial intelligence—will become the norm. These changes will improve the quality of life of our species. We will speed communication faster than even the Matrix can offer. Linking as one, we will gain greater capabilities. We have the potential to form a hive mind and all the power and ability for advancement that comes with it. Imagine applying the power of linked human intellect to a problem. As I stated when I began, evolution is a response to the environment. Today that environment is changing rapidly, and we as humans must respond rapidly, unleashing the full potential of our genetic and technological understanding in order to keep up with a world that I believe threatens to grow beyond us. I also believe we are ready for that threat. We must stand ready to meet the challenges of our modern world and to shape humanity to meet those challenges.
