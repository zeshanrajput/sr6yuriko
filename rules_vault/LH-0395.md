## The Stone

Others have more details about the behind-thescenes stuff, but I can hit the basics.

Thanks to some shadow ops in Japan, a lot of data regarding MCT and their involvement with the Disians reached the right hands. As this was going on, fate dealt the Disians a bad hand. A young man named Tosh MacDonald was kidnapped to be used as a Harvester battery. No one knew, but Tosh was Toshiro’s youngest son, andhe was being clandestinely groomed to obtain his father’s MCT shares. Toshiro also had immense pride in the young man’s accomplishments without the benefits of the Mitsuhama name. So, when his son vanished, Toshiro took to the shadows to recover his son, but was too late. Tosh’s death sent Toshiro into a vengeful spiral. He discovered his company’s deep entanglement with the Disians, and in his extremely emotional state, he saw all of it as a dishonor to his family name.

- Considering the level of Disian infiltration within MCT, I’d say it was more accurate that the blinders came off. By then, Miriam Ito had effectively taken over and Toshiro realized his inactions let her.

- Sam-R-Eye

Enter the Empress. Backed by Shiawase, Toshiro came clean about his failures and those of his corporation. He then pleaded for a path to regain the honor of his family name via the right of seppuku. But the Empress wasn’t keen on such an empty effort. Shiawase and Renraku suggested that MCT fold and present reparations in the form of corporate assets be given to the other Japanacorps. But the Empress knew that wasn’t within Toshiro’s power and suggested a compromise: Toshiro would sacrifice his company rather than his life. He was also ordered to prevent Disian success.

And so, the stone was thrown, and the fall began. Toshiro started with his own sell-off. He sold all his stock in Mitsuhama. Immediately after that, with Renraku and Shiawase financial firms at the lead (selling within seconds of the closing bell after Toshiro sold), the world market saw the event and panic sold before the bottom dropped out. The shadows exploded that night with investment firms trying to get their trades on the exchange first. From what I’ve gathered, no fewer than 100 runner teams all tried to hit the trade exchanges at the same time. Within a minute of the opening bell, MCT’s stock value had dropped 77 percent.

- From a strategic standpoint, this did jack and drek to ensure the survival of the Sixth World. MCT forces were already entrenched and ordered to fight until the bitter end. In the final battle, none of this corporate posturing mattered. But it’s typical. We fight and die; they reap the benefits.

- Scattershot

When the fighting was over, the fire sale began. To cover their losses, MCT had to start selling pieces, and every corporate shark was there to nibble at the whale carcass. Disians inside the corp struggled to hold together their shadow logistics empire and the Defenders took advantage. Just about everyone sunk their teeth into MCT at some point and came off with a chunk of the giant. _Technically_ MCT is still the top megacorp, at least until the next corAFTERSHOCKSporate audit. In reality, MCT’s current ranking is actually closer to the bottom.
