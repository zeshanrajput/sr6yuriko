## Radar Sensor

An implanted radar sensor differs from aviation or military radars by using a shorter wavelength in the terahertz range and transmits in a linear frequency-modulated continuous wave (LFMCW). This type of radar is optimized for high-resolution imagery, low power requirements, and reduced background clutter. An implanted radar sensor functions as an ultrasound sensor (p. 277, _SR6_ ) with a sixty-degree field of view originating from the user’s forehead and has a maximum range of twenty meters. This form of “sight” has the added functionality of being able to see through any barriers or materials with structure rating 15 or less. Radar ignores all Invisibility and Silence statuses, but it is susceptible to jamming. The user loses 2 dice for radar-based Perception tests for every point of jamming noise they are subjected to. Radar signals are unsubtle; any Matrix user within fifty meters can triangulate the exact position of an active radar sensor with a successful Cracking (Electronic Warfare) + Logic (3) test. It takes a Minor Action to activate or deactivate the system.

**Cyberware overdrive:** A radar sensor can “see” for fifty meters during one Perception test. Gain two dice, one of which must be a wild die, for that test.

**Wireless bonus:** If the user also has a wirelessly enabled Math SPU, radar targeting data permits ranged attacks at a detected target while ignoring Blindness and Cover statuses, or any other visibility modifiers. Any barriers between user and target will still degrade the damage normally (see **Barriers** , p. 111, _SR6_ ).
