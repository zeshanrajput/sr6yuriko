## Bribes, Scams, and Pickpockets

**==> picture [17 x 158] intentionally omitted \<==**

**----- Start of picture text -----**<br>
SHADOWRUN: THE KECHIBI CODE<br>**----- End of picture text -----**<br>

Many Lagosians make a decent living off the outsiders that come through town, whether through scams or bribes. They are both just part of everyday life in Lagos. This means they’re part of the backdrop and setting that makes Lagos what it is, so they need to play a part in thisstory. In order to simplify some of these interactions, they can be abstracted with a few dice rolls. This doesn’t mean you can’t roleplay out some of the encounters to deliver this flavor, but daily dice rolls can help provide the flavor of the area without slowing the story down.

Bribes involve an Influence (Negotiation or Intimidation) + Charisma Opposed test to represent the interaction. For each net hit by NPCS, bribes cost the players 50 nuyen. If the players accumulate 10 net hits, they won’t have to make this test again. Having extra support in the area in the form of backup can provide a point of Edge to the party asking for the bribe. If the amount of support is exceptionally large, it can provide a +1 die bonus on the test. These bribes come in the form of airport taxes, tolls at chokepoints on streets, fees for talking to influential locals such as hawalas, politicians, crime bosses, and corp execs. A bribe that can’t be paid in currency can be paid in trade items, though the runners will receive fifty percent of their book value. If players lose the Opposed test but will not or cannot pay the bribes, the gamemaster should apply consequesnces, such as NPCs refusing to deal with difficult foreigners or a nonlethal melee scuffle in the street.

Scams involve a Con + Charisma vs. Intuition + Willpower Opposed test. For each net hit by NPCs, scams cost the players 50 nuyen. If the players accumulate 10 net hits, word gets around, and scammers stop targeting them. Having additional personnel in on the scam helping to support the initial story provides the scammer with a point of Edge. If there is a significant team in on the scam, a potential +1 dice pool modifier could be awarded. These scams come in many forms, including guide offers, fake respirators, fake filters, offers to meet important people, etc., and the scam artists are great at slinking back into a crowd if they’re made.

Pickpockets involve a Stealth (Palming) + Agility vs. Perception + Intuition Opposed test. If the NPC wins, the attempt costs the targeted player some small and liftable item—the more hits by the NPC, the larger the item that can be grabbed without being noticed. If the player wins, they can let the thief run away empty-handed or roll initiative. Don’t underestimate the talents of a skilled pickpocket. Stealing an assault rifle strapped to a character’s back or hanging off a shoulder might seem impossible, but the best pickpockets will manage it, especially in a large crowd, which is what most of Lagos hosts. Having extra support in the area in the form of backup can provide a point of Edge to the pickpocket team. If the amount of support is exceptionally large, it can provide a +1 die bonus on the test.
