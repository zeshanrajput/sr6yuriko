## Articulation Points

This is where we get back to the discussion from _Power Plays_ about ideology and the dominant socioeconomic and political ethos of people within the megacorporations and those outside looking in. It’s also useful to tie this in to Dr. Spin’s breakdown of the Big Ten’s individual corporate motivations. Corporatism makes it even more important than ever to consider how these are used to influence and drive behavior while the ideas of Struggle, Death, Order, Power, Consensus, Communications, Evolution, Balance, Family, and Radical (although that seems primed to change considerably now) define what buttons are being pushed and what ideas and motivations are being exploited in each megacorp. Amusingly enough, the global megacorporations’ rule has actually resulted in corporatism having less “market share” of ideology (socioeconomic and political) than its predecessor did before the Crash of ’29, when the age of the megacorporate rule began in earnest.

Social engineering, especially when it’s based on electronic communications like phishing, has been broken down, analyzed, and automated for decades. The three dominant interpersonal concepts used to draw out information or to manipulate action by someone are sympathy, trust, and fear. All of these pillars can be used to appeal to you—or manipulate you. Any decent social engineering effort would lean on some combination of these three, so we should take a moment to look at them.
