## Observation

I know a martial artist who once said that all combat is based on reading your opponent. The ability to recognize weakness and then exploit it is intrinsic to victory. In this, there is no difference between a physical altercation and a social exploitation. Find the soft spot, wait for the opening, and strike.

One of the most vital elements in a social target is the mark’s image, insecurities, and self-confidence. “Hunger and haunt,” is one of my favorite phrases used to describe it. All people see themselves as part of a hierarchy, even if they actively eschew such things. You can be an army of one and still self-evaluate your assets in comparison to others, whether it’s combat ability or financial freedom. No one wants for nothing. If anything, an abundance of one thing can stoke the fires for another.

Another point is the question of whether your mark is proactive or reactive. Some folks make things happen; others respond to external stimuli. We all have a mix of both, but you need to learn what that looks like for your target.
