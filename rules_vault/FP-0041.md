**> Glitch**

**This society masquerades within many names. Most generally refer to their members as cartomancers as a reference to the power of the Sixth World Tarot deck they seek to fnd, despite the challenge of tracking the various scattered cards. Within the society, they refer to each other as Echoes, in reference to their formal society name, the Echoes of Divination. So many others have come and gone in the hunt for these artifacts. While the fad has dwindled (though I should not call it a fad), the cards are still out there, and they continually evolve. As one foretelling unfolds or unravels, another is sculpted. While many of the original hunters have faded, either from life or interest, a collective of sorts has taken hold. This collective is interesting in that they aren’t your everyday cads going after every whisper and clue and making a muss, fuss, and mess of things more often than not. They take their time, they plot, ploy, and ply among their own and others, but they aren’t harmless academics.**

**I think the reasons I fnd these people so dangerous are because they’re fanatical, fantastically wealthy in cash and connections, and most have not even the slightest concept of personal restraint. The ability to do something is all that’s required for them to do it. There is never a thought of whether it** _**should**_ **be done, only that it can be—whether for their own personal gain, their opponents’ decline, or purely out of sheer amusement for amusement’s sake. When they get their hands on a card and the secrets to its activation, you can sure as drek get ready to see some storms on the horizon.**

**The cartomancers known to me are only a handful, but following them becomes easy once you spot the trails of bloody breadcrumbs they leave. Madness just begets more and more madness with this lot. The strangest thing about them is that they are not woven into any singular global threat or plot but can, on any whim, weave themselves right in because the cards have pointed them in the right direction. They are at one moment allied with the mad forces of Dis because the Fool has laid a vision, and then they see that vision made real with a suicide attack on a protected site or suddenly dissolved when the Queen of Cups shows them a different path.**

**They are as mercurial as the readings of the cards they seek.**
