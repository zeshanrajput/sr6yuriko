## **Asian Griffon**

## **(Alatusleo serpens)**

**Early studies assumed these were some cousin of the dragon or wyvern, but genetic samples revealed they are most closely related to the griffon. Usually found in the mountain ranges of the Asian continent, they have a more reptilian torso and rear legs but still bear avian forelegs. The head is scaled and the crown and long neck are ringed with spikes that then come closer together around the neck region and traverse spine to tail.**

**I didn’t get lifted by an Asian griffn, but instead I strolled into its lair with no expectations of fnding it home, however, it and its small family group were all home, and I demonstrated to them very quickly how quickly a little gnome could skydive. Then they proceeded to work with me on my aerial combat skills.**

**As dangerous as these are in the wild, they are worse when trained. Several remote research and mining operations run by Wuxing across Asia use them as site protection. With their three-dimensional dominance, they are perfect security critters.**
