## **Physical, Astral, or Matrix Evidence**

**Making connections with more tangential evidence is key to solving any investigation. The types of evidence range from magical in nature to things that are much more physical, like chemical or biological elements. How an investigative team uses these tools and ties them to people and locations creates a stronger idea of who the guilty party is and if they’ve found the right person. Just beware of falsifed evidence, as that can throw wannabe detectives off the trail.**

**Psychometry and the use of astral signatures in an investigation is essential when the nature of the crime involves magic. Reading and sensing the ripples in the manasphere can provide echoes of what happened in the past. It can give details like the emotions and motives that come into play. It’s possible to still detect these things even if the Awakened perpetrator took the time to clean the astral, and fnding an astral signature can provide a spirit with an easy way to track the people involved down.**

**Bank records and other fnancial instruments can be useful evidence. The transaction history is a window into the movements of the account holder. They can be checked for forgery or identity theft. If they are attempting to funnel funds to be laundered, sometimes that isn’t as clean as they wanted, leaving a trail back to the original account. This can be used to tie an individual to a location, or provide a stronger alibi, but only if you somehow can get access to these highly sensitive records.**

**Identifying the exact frearm used in a crime is a powerful piece of evidence when building a case. Striationa caused by rifing in the barrel leaves a sort of fngerprint on bullets that can be used to link crimes together and place an individual’s weapon at the scene of the crime. While it can link crimes together, tying it to an individual will require the gun.**

**There are eyes and ears everywhere that record everything around. Cameras and microphones are**

**a ubiquitous part of the Sixth World. These tools are used for facial, voice, and gait recognition. In the old days, this often required investigators to comb through hundreds of hours of footage and sound. But with modern tools such as intelligent agents and artifcial intelligence, the speed at which this can be processed means that criminals spend much less time on the streets. And beyond just what the cameras and microphones pick up, devices are tracking their locations, and this breadcrumb trail can be useful evidence.**

**An individual’s genetic code can be a powerful piece of evidence that ties them to a location or crime. Their DNA could be a blood drop, eyelash, or skin cells left behind. Modern law enforcement personnel are specifcally trained in the careful collection of these materials that can be left behind at any scene of the crime. And this tool is just as useful at exonerating the innocent.**

**The use of GSR or gunshot residue can be useful when placing an individual at the scene of a crime where frearms are used. The blowback of particles from the bullet can spray onto many individuals nearby. Modern chemical analysis provides a good relationship between the manufacturer of the ammunition and the detected residue on a person’s clothes, hands, or even vehicles.**

**Fingerprints can provide a valuable view into a crime scene, allowing the investigator to place individuals at specifc locations within the scene. The uniqueness of the prints provides a strong connection between a specifc individual and the evidence left behind, with an extremely low probability of two people having the same prints. Within the Sixth World this is trickier, as the prevalence of cybernetic hands and limbs make the presence of prints less likely.**

**Matrix access logs on hosts or on devices can be a useful source of evidence. Hosts have the capacity to store as many logs as necessary, while logs on a device tend to be much more limited in the duration of storage, although it’s rumored that the Deep Foundation does have every piece of information ever. System logs are useful in piecing together timelines when those systems are involved. Even in the case where a smart hacker goes in and cleans up after themselves, there is still a discrepancy when they exit the host, and that’s logged as well.**

**Other physical evidence that can be left behind ranges from footprints to scraps of clothing. Maybe the specifc make and model of car can be ascertained from the tire tracks in the mud. The victim could have DNA evidence under their fngernails. Someone’s commlink or vehicle will provide a wealth of evidence. A smart investigative team uses all these assets together to fully understand the situation and bring the criminals to justice.**
