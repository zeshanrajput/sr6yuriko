## The Whisper

Some people make, and some people take. That’s how you put soy on the board and kept yourself in coffin motels for years. Sure, you could have gotten some low-level production job in a factory with a stunning mortality rate, or sold your body on the streets, but there was a thrill to the lift, a rush from watching what no one else could see. You were a ghost, and you loved it. Maybe that’s why you dodged the bigger outfits, glorified protection rackets for criminals with high quotas and low benefits. And you figured you might as well join a corp. All of that went out the window the night you lifted the wrong stick.

She was faster than your eye could see, stronger than you could imagine. She was tireless, relentless, effortlessly pursuing you across all your best rooftop escape routes. Her eyes glowed, and you knew it wasn’t cyber. You slammed a secure door you knew was left unlocked after dark, one the dealers made use of, and breathed hard. You’d deal with them later. You’d be alive to deal with them later. And then the mist came through the cracks, and she was behind you. This was it: the last score. The big mistake that would end it all.

Only it wasn’t.

She saw your speed, your hunger, your potential. Your talents were wasted here, she said. Petty voyeurism, scant nuyen, and an inevitable early, unmourned death. There was so much better out there. Secrets worth stealing, valuable prototypes worth more money than you’d ever dreamed of to be lifted. Souls. You would steal blood and souls and bathe in gold, and you would do it forever.

You knew opportunity, and you knew this was it. What looked like a last mistake turned out to be the big break. You never looked back. Now you enforce AZT interests almost as a sideline to your career as a professional thief and spy. You acquire, relentlessly, because the only thing that defines you more than your curiosity and greed is your hunger, always, for more.
