## Handling the Corporate Angle and Their Employees

Corporations aren’t people in and of themselves, but the way you behave and how you interact on corpowned sites can have a direct correlation with how you’ll connect with the individuals who work there. Just being present on site and looking the part could get you treated as if you’re a member of the club. This requires you to do your legwork and understand what their culture represents and how it shows up.

- Each corporation has its own culture that defines how its employees operate. As an example, Renraku takes the worship of the Eternal CEO and the concepts of Bushido 2.0 to an extreme. It’s fully ingrained throughout everything they do as a company, and how most of their employees organize their life.

- **>** Haze

- Here’s another one: Horizon’s relentless focus on the zeitgeist. Your worth is purely based on how others perceive you and is integrated throughout their Consensus system. You’ll see this in their public dealings as well. Did you spot someone taking selfies at a dinner party? Most likely, they work for Horizon.

- Sim-Eon

When establishing a new corporate contact or network, I typically start from the outside. Find a bar, club, or whatever that’s frequented by their employees, then get to know them. Do favors for them. You need to fit in and be seen as part of the “in” crowd, not an outsider. Once you’re one of them, then it’s time to work on creating those deeper connections.

How you interact with wageslaves or lower corporate echelons will vary. Bribing a high-level executive is going to be significantly harder than the janitor making 2,000 nuyen a month. The motives of an executive are often far removed from what the average person is going to be dealing with on a day-to-day basis. Nine times out of ten, the exec is all about gaining (or maintaining) power within the company. Occasionally, though, you’ll see them worrying about something incredibly mundane, like a wayward family member. Or perhaps something more eccentric.

- I got one of those. I know this mega-rich fragger that spends his time shopping for vintage airplanes like someone else would shop for a new outfit. The only way you’re really going to catch his eye is with a shiny P-38 Lightning or Supermarine Spitfire, not with a few thousand nuyen. The difference between what the wealthy and the mega-rich want is kilometers apart.

- **>** Orbital DK

- What the frag? The other side of metahumanity is struggling to get food for tomorrow and desperate to put a roof over their head. A couple hundred nuyen will buy them some peace of mind. That’s not to say they won’t sell you out for a few hundred more, but it’s about knowing what’s on the street. By the way, that two hundred nuyen goes a lot farther if you’ve got a solid rep on the street.

- Borderline

- Are you new to the Sixth World, chummer? This kind of inequality is just another Tuesday.

- Shockwave

One group that’s beneficial to get in good with is corporate security. If you’ve built some rep with them, you’ve got a potential avenue to handle some issues during a run. Knowing the individual guards allows for knowing their potential for bribes, favors, and getting data you just shouldn’t have under penalty of getting shot … a lot. And if, or when, you get caught, they just _might_ be in a position to help you.

Most security, however, is going to stay professional and loyal to their corp. I don’t really understand why. The corporation or contract they’re dealing with isn’t really looking out for them. I’ve found that by really listening to them as individuals, I’ve been able to put some real and positive change into their lives that the corps never will.

- Don’t think you’re going to get super chummy with all corpsec officers. Most can smell trouble or bulldrek a kilometer away. You need to talk the talk if you’re going to convince them that you’re their chummer and want to buy them a beer. They’re not going to trust you right away.

Knowing a few shadowrunners while on the force can help get things moving and make sure certain jobs get done. Trading favors makes this world really work. But Juice’s right in that you want to approach them the right way or they’re going to be suspicious. Most won’t risk their payday and benefits, because it’s all they have.

Contacts among the shadows are invaluable to their investigations. And it’s a win-win situation—if you manage to hook up with one of them, a whole new world of opportunities opens up.

- **>** LSPD

It’s a similar situation for general corp employees. Some, if not most, are going to have a misappropriated loyalty to an uncaring corporation. But if you can find an in with them, you’ll gain some opportunities. When the time comes, you may find that certain doors are conveniently left unlocked. Or maybe they’ll take their break at a certain time. Such people are also great sources of paydata regarding corporate rumors and insider information. And when you do need to bribe someone, wageslaves and the local baristas are going to take a lot less nuyen to move than some upper-level manager who gets paid way too much.

- Every corporate location has backdoors and service entrances that keep the scut-workers away from the important people. Learn who they are and what they like, then do them some favors. With that newfound trust at your fingertips—and hopefully little love for the corp—you might be able to ask them for an out when things go sideways.
