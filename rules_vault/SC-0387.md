## Ex-Law Enforcement

This is one of the oldest stories in the profession. A police officer, or perhaps detective, puts in some time on the force, working in uniform until one day they set it aside. There are a lot of reasons they may have left—maybe they were corrupt, or not corrupt enough. Maybe they got fed up with the rules they constantly had to follow. Maybe they got wounded and couldn’t continue regular duty. Maybe they just hated the regimented hours. Or maybe they got tired of arresting the wrong people.

Whatever the case may be, the ex-cop hits the streets with some Influence skills, some police contacts, and usually a pretty big chip on their shoulder. The cops know them and may well hate them, but they know the cops, too, and sometimes their insider information alone is enough to keep you from having to spend a night in the clink.
