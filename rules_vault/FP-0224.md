## **The Big Ten**

**Again, this is complicated. The more they hear about the Tiding, the more determined high-ranking executives of the Big Ten are to block them from getting access to any of their secrets. Some executives want to go beyond that—they’d like to take the offensive, lash out against some magpies to convince them to get out of their line of work, and have the whole thing go away.**

**Yet, at the same time, the majority of the Tiding’s most signifcant clients are executives of the Big Ten.**

**As is the case with guns, people only dislike the Tiding when it’s aimed at them. Otherwise, they can’t help but fnd it useful.**

- **This truly is a wonderful irony. If the Big Ten could just agree that the Tiding was too large a threat to many of their secrets, and they agreed among themselves to end all contacts and contracts, the Tiding might shut down, because their remaining revenue might not be worth their while. **> Fianchetto**

**One element they obsess about when they hear Tiding agents are on the trail of something is not so much what they are after but who has hired them. Tiding activity was recently reported in Manhattan by some corp intelligence specialists, and while the belief eventually developed that they were looking into the activities and origins of Animus Construction, it wasn’t clear who asked them to do so. Lots of people wanted to know, but of course the Tiding protects that information carefully.**

- **Is it possible that the Tiding doesn’t know who ultimately hired them? After all, the whole point of us being hired by “Mr. Johnson” is that the identity of the person hiring us, and all of the people behind them, can be concealed.**

- **Chainmaker**

- **Possible, but not likely. First, the Tiding likes to know who is hiring them. That has a lot of bearing on how they’ll approach their target, how they’ll know where to look, how they’ll present what they find, and how they’ll protect the identity of their client while they search. For that reason, if they somehow don’t know where the money to hire them is coming from right off the bat, they’ll put their skills to work in finding out.**

- **Dr. Spin**

**One positive aspect of the Tiding, as far as the Big Ten are concerned, is that they seem to have no desire to be narcs. They certainly have uncovered evidence of illegal activity during their jobs, but, to date, they have shown no interest in reporting anything they fnd to law enforcement.**

- **They’re not above using that information as blackmail if it helps get them even more information, however.**
