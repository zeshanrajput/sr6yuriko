## CHEMICAL PRODUCTION FACILITY ATTACKED! 241 KILLED

_SAMARKAND —_ A Mitsuhama Computer Technologies–owned chemical production facility in Tepakul, approximately forty kilometers southwest of Samarkand in the Commonwealth of Turkestan, came under attack by what appears to be a western dragon of immense size.

The dragon, thought to be the one known as Sirrurg “the Destroyer,” was responsible for the destruction of EuroAir Flight 329 in 2041, as well as a swath of destruction during the Amazonian War in which the dragon devastated Borinquen and several Aztlaner cities.

Immediately prior to the attack, a major dust storm quickly developed, localized around the chemical plant. This storm disabled all communications as well as most wireless Matrix links not confined to within buildings. Security positions were also disabled.

Once the storm had cleared at 0438 local time, the attack continued. There were also reports of other individuals attacking the facility, but those reports are unconfirmed.

Spirits of air manifested within the compound, their winds stripping parts of the facility roofs and tossing light vehicles aside.

The dragon then tore into the facility, its fiery breath melting prefabricated structures while its claws ripped massive chunks out of concrete out the floor, as if it were searching for something.

Two attack VTOLs from a nearby military base were scrambled to intercept but were destroyed by air spirits when they got within five kilometers of the facility.

Reports say the dragon continued its attack until the main building’s superstructure collapsed upon itself. By the time the attack was over, no buildings were left standing. Chemical storage tanks were also destroyed, their contents leaking into the crater that was once the main facility. Any personnel who attempted to fight were strewn about the complex like dolls.

In total, 241 people were reported killed, 203 of whom were plant workers about to end their workday. Thirty-eight security personnel were also killed in the rampage.

A press release from MCT stated, “Earlier today, our facility in Tepakul was attacked by terrorist forces led by the great dragon known as the Destroyer. We are not aware why the dragon chose to attack our facility and end the lives of over two-hundred workers. But we will not rest until he, and those who worked with him, are brought to justice on the world stage. What transpired here is unacceptable, and Mitsuhama strongly and vehemently condemns his actions. We will not stand idly by while this criminal remains free.”

Aside from the loss of life, damage to the facility is estimated to be over several billion nuyen.
