## The Job

Many are not aware that Renraku is a major player in the ARCHology. Renraku likes it that way, but they also want to leverage this power into more public power in St. Louis and this funding boost, kept in illicit accounts, could definitely help them, right before they turn the code over to the Corporate Court to boost favor in that realm at the same time.



Mr. Johnson points the runners at Hiteki Taktikaro, a.k.a. Mako. Taktikaro is Renraku’s top corporate shadow operative in St. Louis. Mako needs some nuyen to pay the runners and has a keen target that will provide him with the additional funds he can use to pay the runners for the code.

Mako’s target is a current urban legend in St. Louis that Mako has discovered is true and far more vulnerable than others would think. Urban legends in St. Louis talk about a shipment of Confederate gold from the United States Civil War that was being moved up the Mississippi to pay off outlaw mercenary forces in the North who were intended to harry Union forces. It disappeared along the way—the most claim is that it was lost to a tornado that struck the ship carrying it.

Modern street gossip reports that somehow this gold was found by the Sea Dragon and has been brought to St. Louis to fund her efforts in the city and along the Mississippi River. While this gossip is actually true, not all of the gold she brought for payment was picked up. When the bars were being picked up, one of the people involved “dropped” four of the bars. She had an intention to pick them up later, but an unrelated incident left her in a coma.

The woman was an agent for Renraku infiltrating the Sea Dragon’s operation. She wasn’t high in the ranks, but Mako wasn’t certain her accident—a car wreck during the Blackout—was completely unrelated to her assignment. He waited several months before hiring a mage to delve into her mind while also being linked to him, hoping to gain direct interaction with her memories. He saw her accident, then delved further to see more of her work. After he got all the information he needed, Mako pulled the plug on her and killed the mage he used.

The location of the missing gold isn’t easy to access, but Mako is willing to risk the PCs’ lives knowing he can keep close tabs on them and possibly pull out a wounded runner and dig in their head for the location of the Kechibi Code if things go wrong.

Mako does not offer up the exact nature of the items being recovered. He simply tells the runners they are recovering a four historical artifacts of the American Civil War. They will recognize the artifacts by their CSA and 1863 markings. He offers no more specifics on the items. A Matrix Search with a threshold of 4 is enough to find the true nature of the artifacts.

The location of the artifact— Lennox-Sullivan Square—is the biggest challenge. The artifact was dropped off underwater in the basement of an apartment building that had its first floor and basement flooded by a shift in the Mississippi River. The building section that remained above the water was abandoned after the lower section flooded, but it has been since reoccupied. The neighborhood around the flood was also abandoned, and most of buildings nearby have collapsed into the river.

The megacorporate subdivision of Lennox-Sullivan Square (as presented by NeoNET) was constructed in the mid-’60s as housing and commercial space for NeoNET citizens in the city of St. Louis. The subdivision is located in between MacArthur Bridge, William L. Clay Sr. Bridge, I-44/55, and the Mississippi River. It is one of several residential projects that reimagined sections of riverfront from their industrial roots to a more modern aesthetic. The buildings within the project consisted of sixteen two-story structures and two ten-story residential towers. The shorter buildings contained retail space on the first floor and office space above that. The two towers were built close to the river and were connected by a series of bridges. These bridges were interesting because they were all angled. Each bridge connected an even-numbered floor in one tower to the next higher odd-numbered floor in the next, creating an artistic lattice. The river actually flowed into the space between the towers creating the centerpiece for a pleasant little nature area built for citizens to enjoy.

When NeoNET collapsed, it lost a large number of residents, but Novatech had continued putting in the bare minimum to maintain the property. Even though it was practically adjacent to downtown and the ARCHology, it became a bit rundown and actually started to become a common residence for shadowrunners using their better-than-average fake SINs.

Then, the Blackout occurred. Events along this section of the river set a few barges to break free. The freed barges hit the concrete walls built along the river and shattered the concrete. The construction process used for the ARCHology had caused some unseen damage to the soil structure, loosening it, and when the river flowed in through the broken concrete it started washing away the loosened soil, sinking foundations of all the structures. The towers settled a floor lower, but four of the shorter structures sank and collapsed. This was partially due to the water flowing in, and partially because a certain dragon was coming in to collapse the buildings in order to stabilize the ground.

After the event, the area was abandoned, as it was only accessible by water. The residents all moved out, the stores closed, and the area, sitting just outside of the DBC, has not recovered.

Mostly. Even though it’s close to the ARCHology, several riverfront ports, and high-profile locales, the towers have become a haven for local river rat gangs. They do smuggling jobs, materials storage, protection gigs, and myriad other criminal activities. They are in the way of the runners just swimming in and grabbing what they need.
