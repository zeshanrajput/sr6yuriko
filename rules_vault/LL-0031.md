## Positive Qualities 

## Drake 

You have been blessed or cursed with a dragon-touched heritage that allows you to shift into an anthropomorphic dragon, and you know it! You have already shifted at least once, you know how to control your powers, and you’ve got some idea you need to keep your head down and avoid detection. 

- **Cost:** 50 Karma 

- **Game Effect:** You may choose a drake type (eastern, western, etc). You count as a drake in addition to your usual metahuman classification, gain the Dracomorphosis power, and a pool of six dracomorphic adjustment points to modify your attributes with—all detailed later in this document. You also gain the downsides Unsteady Hands, Memorable, and Misshapen when in dracoform, and Most Wanted in both forms—also detailed below. True drakes gain the downside Mightiest of Slaves. 

- **Note:** This quality does not count toward the six total qualities limitation at character generation (p. 66, _SR6_ ). 

- **Game Effect:** At some story-appropriate moment, your gamemaster will remove this quality and replace it with the Drake quality. You immediately choose a drake type, and are then free to spend your six dracomorphic adjustment points as you wish. As soon as you have 6 Karma available, you _must_ undergo dracogenesis rank 1 (see below), at which time you gain your drake type’s special power. You do not gain one additional dracogenesis power for your first rank—you only gain your drake type’s special power. You may gain additional drake powers through further additional dracogenesis ranks. Even prior to this first transformation, you have the Most Wanted downside listed below—draconic scrying has led them to take an interest in you. After the first transformation, you gain the downsides Unsteady Hands, Memorable, and Misshapen (when in dracoform). True drakes are never latent drakes. 

- **Note:** This quality does not count toward the six total qualities limitation at character generation (p. 66, _SR6_ ).
