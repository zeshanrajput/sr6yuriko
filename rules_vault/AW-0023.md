## The Plane of Kin

While it is most commonly referred to as the Plane of Man by metaplanar scholars and aetherologists, I much prefer to use a term that is inclusive of all forms of metahumanity. The Plane of Kin (I often wish I could abbreviate the title to “the metaplane,” but alas, that would merely cause confusion) is perhaps the metaplane “closest” to the physical plane. It is anthropomorphic in nature, in that the history and actions of metahumanity have shaped the spirits and places within—or it is equally likely that metahumanity’s history and behavior has been influenced by the places and beings within the Kindred Plane. I’d love to understand this connection more deeply, but the question as it stands is one of the chicken-and-the-egg variety. There is perhaps no way to confidently explain how one of the metaplanes so closely lines up with our own plane of origin. Perhaps it is merely because it is the metaplane in closest proximity to the physical plane, which causes the two to bleed into one another. This is certainly true, but it does



not decisively answer my questions regarding the relationship between this metaplane and the ideas, symbols, dreams, and hubris of metahumanity.

When visiting the Plane of Kin, it can be easy to fall into the trap of forgetting that you are no longer in Kansas. There are buildings, including vibrant cities as well as ancient ruins, populated by spirits who seem very much like metahumans. These kindred spirits largely approve of metahuman travel into the metaplanes. If you are wise, polite, and willing to part with some of your Karma, I recommend that you make finding a guide among them your first order of business on your journey. Just as with metahumans, you must be careful who you deal with and who you avoid. Some of these kindred spirits are vengeful or even monstrous—these will wish you harm, though they might charm you with smiles and lies.

You can reach the Plane of Kin through the Astral Sea—in some cases the shores of this plane overlap with the shores of the physical plane, such that you never need enter the Astral Sea itself to travel there. If you are not an initiate capable of astral projection, you must find another way there. Fortunately, there is a nexus in North America that connects to a hyperplane within the Plane of Kin that is called the Hudson Valley. This valley is accessible via natural (or semi-natural) astral rifts. Simply shift your perception to astral space and look for places of heightened spirit activity. If you are particularly observant—geomancers are skilled at this—you might observe how the flow of mana is reinforced or disrupted in subtle ways that provide a trail to follow. While these rifts only allow access via astral projection, you will often find free spirits near them. Some of these free spirits are visiting for their own amusement, but others are in the business of metaplanar travel. If you find one of these rifts, all you need do is start up a conversation with some of the free spirits. If you’re polite and willing to part with some Karma, the spirit may be able to create a metaplanar gateway for you, or at least point you toward another free spirit that has that ability. Then all you need do is step through the gate, and your body, mind, and all of your immediate possessions are transported to the Hudson Valley.

You will find the Hudson Valley to be a busy, hospitable place that seems to resemble the United States of America as it was in the nineteenth century—with the exception that its inhabitants are far more diverse than what you would have found in that place and time in the physical world. One of its key features is a great river that flows along the extent of the valley.

Many travelers pass through Hudson Valley— some from the physical plane, but many more from other metaplanes. Here you will find not only kindred spirits, but spirits and astral travelers from nearly every plane that is near to us: faeries, beasts,

tree spirits, elementals, and even some of the more sinister creatures that come from the dark reaches of the Plane of Kin or Shadow. In the Astral Sea, the Hudson Valley extends out from the Plane of Kin like a river that winds through a number of other metaplanes. By traveling through the Hudson Valley, you can reach other places within the Plane of Kin more quickly, but you can also use it as a super-highway of sorts in order to reach the planes of Faerie, Beasts, Plants, and the elemental planes—even the Plane of Shadow, if you are desperate or foolish enough to travel there. It is through the Hudson Valley that megacorporations as well as individual magicians have learned to access and exploit the vast resources of reagents and other magical objects in order to profit by selling them on Earth. Most gates are too small to allow large vehicles to pass through to the metaplanes, but not all of them. It wouldn’t be a bad idea to bring a canoe with you, now that I think of it. Don’t be too surprised if you see an Aztechnology Aguilar escorting a convoy of all-terrain cargo vehicles from one place to another in Hudson Valley—just be sure to stay out of their way if you do.
