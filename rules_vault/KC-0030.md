## Trust

Every relationship, whether it is business or personal, involves some interactions that come without guarantees. Before you get a business contract inked, you need to engage in negotiations knowing the whole process could fall apart at any time. Obviously, without guarantees or contracts, you’re not going to make that investment of time unless you believe that the other party is bargaining in good faith and has something of value to offer. In other words, you have to trust them.

On a larger scale, a huge part of any effort to build a brand is to make that brand trusted. You want people to know that when they spend money on your brand, they will get the experience you promise. The exact nature of that experience varies from brand to brand, but the need to build trust does not. A trusted brand is the most valuable commodity in the business world, because it brings in everything you want—repeat business, positive word-of-mouth, and a willingness to spend more just for the name you put on a product.

In the shadows, our gut reaction is to scoff at this. Who are we to ever trust the corps? Yet we say this as we use our Renraku commlinks, drive our Ford automobiles, and fire our Ares weapons. We have brands we cling to and brands we reject outright. We say we don’t trust the corps, but we still rely on what they make to save our lives.
