## Collapse: The RhineRuhr-Megaplex

Things couldn’t be worse, or so it was thought after the Week of Death and the overreactions by domestic security forces against “terrorist hotspots” in a region with a long, sad history of violent clashes between corps and trade unions.

Then came Valentine’s Day 2083. In the middle of the famous Rheinänder carnival (when most hospitals were already overwhelmed by drunkards), hundreds of explosions in the Ruhrplex’s abandoned mines shook the region. The destruction of a nearly completed Harvester deep beneath Dortmund by Grey Cell-backed shadowrunners was only partly responsible. To protect their hidden facilities, the Disians triggered a fail-safe consisting of 235 separate bombs. It had long been suspected within the shadows that these bombs existed, but they were laughed off as a conspiracy theory. They consisted of industrial-strength mining charges placed at structurally critical points, and they had been lying dormant since the trade union struggles of the early 2000s. They were apparently identified by Disian agents and integrated into a network to cause maximum destruction when detonated. A humanitarian catastrophe of

ALLIED GERMAN STATES: LETsuch apocalyptic proportions would render all opposition too busy dealing with the crisis to organize effective resistance before the final phase of Disian operations began.

That plan worked perfectly.

The detonations caused large regions of the Ruhrplex to suddenly sink by 15 to 30 meters, taking low and tall structures with them. Additionally, the explosions destroyed large sections of the pump network that kept the Ruhrplex, which had already sunk below the groundwater level due to mining activities, dry.

What wasn’t immediately destroyed by what has been dubbed the Kollaps (Collapse), was filled with groundwater and river flooding after the April thaw. All the while, S-K’s global HQ Neu-Essen remained completely unaffected (although it was already 20 meters below groundwater level before the Kollaps). Some “valuable” areas were thrown into the gaping maw of the megas by the overburdened municipalities in exchange for the vague promise to “save” them, usually by whatever definition the megas wanted to apply.

Unwelcome areas, such as the Tusken Fallow (“Hauerbrache”), were simply abandoned and accounted for the main burden of losses. Overall, the Kollaps immediately killed tens of thousands, and hundreds of thousands died during the brutal winter. Over a million inhabitants lost their homes and became inner-German refugees. The better-off corporate citizens were taken care of, while the actual refugees weren’t. And that’s not including certain kinds of dispossessed and “dubious” people the surrounding communities (and countries) wouldn’t take in or wanted to get rid of as quickly as possible.
