## Renraku

**Local Subsidiaries:** Adams-Westlake Mediaworks, Architectural Dynamics, Champion Financial, FactFiles, GridGuide

No company benefited from the fall of NeoNET more than Renraku. For years, the company chased the Matrix infrastructure contract for the towers and surrounding Corporate Court properties. With NeoNET out of the way, Renraku’s lobbyists went to work quickly to secure that contract and many more held by the fallen megacorp.

Renraku’s New York operations represent an opportunity to step out of the shadow of Mitsuhama by branding themselves as the lead Japanacorp of North America. This has put them in direct conflict with Shiawase and Mitsuhama, but more curiously, Horizon. That last rivalry has a lot to do with how the corporation chooses to make its mark. Renraku’s corporate identity is based around the idea of need fulfillment. They want the consumer to think of Renraku anytime they feel a need for anything—be it information or a product. The corporation features New York as the core of that messaging through Lycan, a localized search provider with data links to GridGuide and other Renraku services. Lycan can provide New Yorkers with up-to-the-second information about any news, product, event, traffic situation, etc. within the borders of the city. That information is relayed to the end user with Renraku’s brand all over it. Thanks to their control of xGuide and the local Matrix, Renraku’s Lycan customers have faster access to data than any other provider can offer, and that is what has Horizon piping mad.

- Renraku’s battle with the Yakuza is well illustrated. Curiously, this does not appear to be the case with recent MDC addition, Nagato. The corporation is Yakuza-run, but this particular flavor of Yakuza doesn’t seem to be following the rules (or marching orders) of the Japanese home office.

- Mihoshi Oni

- You didn’t think we’d get through a conversation about Renraku without mentioning the Red Samurai, did you? Renraku habitually deploys units of the samurai in NYC, putting them on full display in front of buildings, moving executives through the city, even marching in many of the city parades. The goal here is simple: marketing. Renraku wants the world to associate them with top-flight security. They want people to know that the samurai mean safety, and by extension, Renraku is a corporation you can feel safe with.

- Hard Exit

- Let’s not forget that NeoNET didn’t just disappear. Novatech, Erika, and Transys all remain as top AA corporations. They know what Renraku did, and it doesn’t sit well with any of them. While they don’t have the power to challenge the corp directly, it’s an open secret that each is taking shots at Renraku in their own way.
