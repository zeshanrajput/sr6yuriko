**Early riggers were primarily relegated to the transportation side of things, but advances in drone technology have shifted that balance over in favor of recon and combat support. Frag, but most of the vehicles I use for transporting runners around are wireless—the equivalent of big drones. I love drones. They are by far the most useful and reliable tools that a runner could ever hope for. There are some drawbacks and vulnerabilities—we’ll get to those a bit later—but those are massively outweighed by the benefts. They come in all shapes and sizes, from the tiny bug-shaped Kanimushis to fully armed personnel carriers and everything in between. Some are ideal for snooping around, watching your back, spotting trouble, and keeping track of what’s going on. Some are useful for various technical tasks, like planting explosives, cutting through doors and walls, bypassing locks or sensors, maintaining and repairing your tech, and even providing medical care. Others provide excellent combat support, ideally from a distance or concealed positions. And then there’s vehicles rigged up like drones, which can help you get your team into position or out of trouble as needed. When it comes to rigging these days, drones are the name of the game. Sure, there are still some riggers who are primarily wheel jockeys who focus on the one vehicle they’re jacked into, but they’re really only good for transportation and intercept tasks— specialists who are only any good for one or two aspects of a run and sometimes aren’t even needed. Or maybe they’re just newbies who haven’t pulled together the considerable amount of scratch you need for a decent RCC and a bunch of drones.**
