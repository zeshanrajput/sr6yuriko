## Area Boys

Local thugs, street police, tribal warriors, guardian angels, ganger demons. The list goes on and on as to how different people in Lagos see the Area Boys. It varies from place to place and group to group, as the Area Boys are not a single unified entity. That is simply the name given to these gangs of Lagosian street fighters who play whatever role those with the power and money place upon them. In other areas of the world they would be gangs, but none of them ever identify themselves as part of any smaller group in such a way. They may be working for different power players in town, but the Area Boys moniker stays, no matter what. Now, the culture behind these local groups may vary, and that could be a way to identify them, butbeing Igbo or Yoruba isn’t added to the Area Boy identifier.

Throughout any runner’s time in Lagos, groups of Area Boys will play a range of parts. They’ll be part of toll roads and bribes at the airport that simply flavor the city. They’ll be sent to follow the runners as they investigate or sent to directly obstruct their path. They’ll be offered as guides or scouts or even backup if the runners feel the need to assault something and one of the local power players thinks helping is in their best interests. The Area Boys can be both enemies and allies.
