## **Keeping Contacts**

**The gear comparison I made earlier was to make the point that your resources as a face aren’t so much your equipment but your network of contacts. But it’s also important to recognize that your contacts aren’t just things to be used—they’re people. If you only see your contacts for what they can do for you, your relationship with them will be primarily transactional. This is okay in some cases, particularly with the ones you’ll be interacting with on a business level, but in other cases it will lead to resentment. In the middle of a run, it’s easy to become so focused on the job that you overlook this, so be sure to take an extra moment in each interaction with a contact to see them as a person and cultivate your relationship with them. Basic good manners can go a long way in this. Saying “please” and “thank you” doesn’t cost you a thing, and it’s a great way to show respect. Give them the chance to tell you about their interests and ask about their well-being from time to time. In this fast-paced world, it can be a rare thing to have someone pay attention to you. Taking time to listen and show that you care establishes a better connection, and it can also provide you with more opportunities to deepen that connection. With very few exceptions, we all crave meaningful connections with other people, and these are often in short supply. In short, don’t forget that your contacts are people, treat them with kindness and respect, and show them that you care about them. There’s no**

**better or faster way to cultivate and maintain your contacts than by doing just that.**
