## Laws

There are two key legal points that should be remembered for Lagos.

First and foremost, no one currently holds the policing contract for the city. There are individually contracted forces on Lagos and Victoria Island for corporate and private property based on the discretion of the owner, but not in the part of the sprawl where most of the people live. The Area Boys act as a pseudo-police force, but they operate more for their own benefit than for the greater good. This gives outsiders the idea that Lagos is some free-for-all anarchist zone, but that can’t be further from the truth. Without police, the locals tend to handle any and all transgressions in-house, or they report it to more powerful forces who prefer people not think the city is a dumping ground for chaos.

Second important point: Lagos does not participate in the Global SIN Registry. The lack of SINs is not the important part but rather the cascade of differences that come with it. This means most of the people in Lagos don’t have SINs to broadcast. Being SINless is the norm here, rather than the exception. They also don’t care if you broadcast yours, though broadcasting anything often makes you a target in most of Lagos.
