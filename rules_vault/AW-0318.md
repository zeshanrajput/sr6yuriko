## Privateers

Letters of marque are issued by the bureaucrats, often to anyone who is willing to purchase one. A letter of marque grants an airship the authority to attack known pirate vessels. The bureaucracy maintains a detailed list of all known airship pirate vessels, all of which are fair game to attack—even within the Spire’s protected airspace. The guardians of the Spire and the bureaucrats do not otherwise interfere with the pirate ships, even the ones that visit the Spire. In order to avoid collateral damage to the docks and other nearby airships, it is forbidden to attack any ship within 100 meters of the docks—even if it is a known pirate vessel. You might think that this would cause the airship pirates to avoid the Spire, but the opposite is true. Instead, it is the custom and tradition for pirate airships to arrive at the Spire in groups in order to deter attacks by local privateers. The guardians of the Spire allow them to pass but may alert any local privateers in case they’d like to try their luck. If a privateer attacks pirate ships within the Spire’s airspace, the guardians will create a perimeter around the battle to prevent other airships from being entangled in the conflict. If the pirate ships get close to the docks while under fire, the guardians will then call a halt to the battle—even ships with a letter of marque are treated as hostile combatants if they don’t stand down.

Most privateers don’t spend much time near the Spire, however, preferring to hunt closer to Pirate Island where they are much more likely to encounter lone pirate airships. Other privateers provide inexpensive (and sometimes even free) escort services for other airships that are likely to be targeted by pirates—mostly mining and cargo transport ships.
