## What Is a Metaplane?

Metaplanes are extensions of astral space—or more accurately, what we call astral space is an extension of the metaplanes. You might think of them as separate universes that are connected to one another. Some scholars have suggested that our own universe (the place I usually refer to as the physical plane) is but one of the metaplanes. I don’t think they’re too far from the truth, but there seem to be significant differences that make the physical plane stand out as unique from the places we call metaplanes.

One of the most significant differences is the cyclic nature of magic. More research over a longer period of time is required in order to confirm this, but I believe that the metaplanes do not have a cycle in which magic leaves and returns. Little enough is understood about this apparent cycle on the physical plane, but it seems to be entirely unique. What seems to happen among the metaplanes are gradual shifts that bring some of them closer together, forming conjunctions that make travel easier. They can also drift away and become more distant from one another, or eclipses can occur as one metaplane blocks access to another. When metaplanes connect, you can travel from one directly to another just by passing through a threshold where they meet. Sometimes this takes you through a newly created, fractured metaplane that blends the two parent metaplanes. Even if a demiplane is not created, areas near the overlap bleed into one another, taking on aspects of both metaplanes.

But let’s get back to that point about the cycle of magic in the physical plane. For me, and this is my personal theory, this provides the clearest definition for what a metaplane is. You see, the metaplanes aren’t just moving closer to (or farther apart

from) each other—they move toward and away from the physical plane as well! When enough of the metaplanes are close enough to our physical plane, such as the eight metaplanes currently adjacent to the material plane, the world awakens to magic. When they separate, mana levels decrease, and magic ultimately departs from the physical plane. This mystery will be studied much more deeply over time by many scholars, explorers, and curious magicians, but I feel safe drawing this conclusion from the data we have available: the metaplanes are where magic comes from. To put it another way, magic exists because the metaplanes have come close enough to overlap with the physical plane! Isn’t that exciting? Can there be any doubt that the metaplanes where spirits reside, and to which the first initiates learned the secrets to greater power, are the very well from which mana is drawn? I find this to be very exciting, and wonder what other great mysteries will be discovered through the study and exploration of metaplanes.
