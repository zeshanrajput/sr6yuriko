## FACES IN THE CROWD

A group’s face isn’t just the person who talks a lot or is friendly. Instead, they represent multiple types of social characters and approaches offering different characters and opportunities in game play. Those concepts may look similar in stats and skills, but depending on personality and playing style, they’ll play very differently. Before considering what type of face to play, determine the nature of the group or team. Is this group or team utilizing a more professional style (a.k.a. black trench coat) or something a bit more chaotic (a.k.a. pink mohawk)?

Below are six archetypes that can be played as a face and made into a player’s own. There is no onesize-fits-all approach to playing a face. A successful face is versatile and capable of adapting their style to meet the evolving needs of the group, its mission, and the external environment.
