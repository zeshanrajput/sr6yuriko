## Bötzsee, Strausberg Industrial District, 22:32:04

Through the oily smears on the windshield, little more could be seen than the massive metal gate. No need to announce herself: The turrets of the factory fortress surely watched the approach of her car closely. Apart from a few apartment blocks, Strausberg was a deserted wasteland, with only the glaring lights of the factory fortresses rising from its wild garbage dumps, poison-sludgefilled depressions, and gray patches of withered vegetation.

Zoé unplugged. Today’s ride was a standard run with as few surprises as Berlin allowed. She took her Altmayr shotgun from the rutted passenger seat and checked that it was loaded.

Through the thick downpour, she saw the console light on the factory gate change from red to green. Time to get out. She slung the Altmayr and grabbed the suitcase from the back seat, which would contain—as always—tightly packed poker chips from the Vesuv casino chain. Untraceable, the chips were a popular cash alternative in Berlin for any transaction that was better left unnoticed.

Hood pulled low over her face and body hunched to protect against the toxic broth they called “weather” here, the petite woman with the striking white tech dreads scurried into the shelter of the gate passage as a small door in the vehicle gate creaked open. She threw back her hood and pulled down her respirator to reveal her face. A gesture of courtesy that was denied her in reverse: Flanked by two armed guards with closed helmets, carrying a small cargo box between them, the contact approached her without removing his mask. Superfluous. She knew who it was anyway. “Have a nice day, too, Rashid.” Nodding. “Good evening, Zoé.” She handed over the suitcase, and the two helmet guys set the cargo box down in front of her.

“Anything to watch out for today? A kill order against me so your fine bigwig bosses can finally pinch the 200 for this courier run?” Twenty-one, twenty-two, twenty-too-long. “No, everything’s all right. Have a gute fahrt.” A good trip. Yeah.
