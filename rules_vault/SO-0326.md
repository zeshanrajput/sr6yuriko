## Micro Stealth Tags (Rating 1-6)

Micronization efforts have resulted in smaller stealth tags with improved capacity that are ideal for

espionage and surveillance work. Micro stealth tags are incredibly tiny—the largest are roughly the size of a small-print period, while the smallest are microscopic. They also have a particularly subtle Matrix presence, making them harder to detect than normal stealth tags. A sensor version is also available, with a single point of capacity for any single sensor type to be installed; it cannot be used in arrays.

Micro stealth tags have some limitations. First, they have a very weak wireless component and are easily jammed, even by the slightest amount of Matrix noise. Second, they have a limited, maximum twenty-four-hour lifespan once activated. Unlike other tags and devices, their settings are largely fixed. Once activated, they transmit their data to a paired device and stop working when they run out of power. This device is designated for when the tag is activated but can be hijacked or tapped by a decker or technomancer. Third, if recovered, micro stealth tags cannot be reused, but the data that is still stored within can be later recovered. Fourth, micro stealth tags have just enough data storage to record until they die.
