## The Tale to Today

In Lagos, corporations have long been able to despoil the environment with impunity—until recently. An environmental attorney named Eniola Okoro suddenly seems to have a ton of resources at her disposal: a team of attorneys, relationships with Lagos’ movers and shakers, and a pile of cash. The corporations of Lagos hear she benefitted from some dramatic stock-market gains. They want to know how she pulled this off and if she had any assistance. Then maybe they’ll shut her down or arrest her if she did anything illegal.

The runners can be hired by one of several sources while other potential employers stay in the mix with their assets to create problems with the investigation. The investigation is not straightforward but rather a mixed series of movements and encounters around the sprawl.

Fate stepped in and had a hand in distracting both of them. Okoro got a case involving hundreds of local kids getting sick that actually paid, and Obukanwe got a job offer from Brackhaven Investments that would require her to move to the Carib League. Both forgot about the money and program until a month later, when Okoro asked about her money because she needed food. Obukanwe sent her the account accesses, as she had no time to check it herself.

Okoro was shocked to find an account with a balance she could barely read, having never dealt with a nine-digit number. She contacted Obukanwe in a panic thinking it was a mistake. Obukanwe was slow to respond, and a day later when she addressed the confusion, it was ten digits. When the pair discovered the truth, they cashed out the investments and kept their heads down for a few weeks. Okoro then used some of the money on a case and saw what power the money held.

Obukanwe knew Okoro wasn’t going to stop, so she helped her with a plan. They decided to expand

Okoro’s law firm and invest in several other firms while opening an investment firm to hide the money. They differed in their strategies—Okoro wanted to use the money and resources to fuel further expansion, while Obukanwe cautioned her against it, warning it would draw too much attention.

Ignoring Obukanwe’s warnings, Okoro found another way to help Lagos with the money. She found a hawala named Hana Orobuku, now known as Ayeowo, and provided a huge influx of resources to Orobuku’s small hawala network, turning it into the largest in Lagos. The move was great for the people but created a massive power shift and drew attention.

The corps of Lagos can’t help but notice how Okoro has been throwing around money. She was an annoyance to them before all this, and they’re quite concerned that she’s about to become a major pain. If someone is helping her by pouring money into her coffers, they want to find out who is doing so. And why.
