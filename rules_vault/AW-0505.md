## Evanescence in Deep Metaplanes

Just as entities coming into the physical realm from the deep metaplanes have their Essence worn away by the energies of our reality, the same thing happens to travelers entering the deep and very deep metaplanes. The effect is most pronounced when astrally projecting into these deep metaplanes. The unprotected metaplanar form begins to break down almost immediately, with metaplanar translation not being as complete or providing the protection it does in the nearer metaplanes. In the deep metaplanes, the traveler’s Essence temporarily drops by 1 point every day of subjective time spent there. In the very deep metaplanes, this accelerates to 1 point lost per hour. The temporary loss is recovered at a rate of 1 point per hour after returning to the material plane. If a traveler’s Essence is reduced to 0, the metaplanar form is painfully disrupted as it’s ejected from the deep metaplane and violently forced back to the body. Similar to its function in the astral plane, a shadow cloak will protect the traveler here, but evanescence destroys the enchantments on the shadow cloak, permanently reducing the rating of the device.

Evanescence affects those physically traveling to the deep metaplanes in ways that are more subtle and far more horrific. Unless stopped in some way, metaplanar translation adapts the physical form into something that can survive in the metaplane, but the energies that cause evanescence are simply slowed, not stopped. Using the translated physical shell as a shield, the loss of Essence happens at 1 point per week, or 1 point per day in the very deep metaplanes. Unlike the loss suffered while projecting, this loss is permanent (remember that Magic




and Resonance are reduced by 1 for every point of Essence lost). In addition, the adaptations made to the physical form can also become permanent— the longer someone stays in the deep metaplane, the more likely they are to keep a physical souvenir from their stay. See p. 142, _Slip Streams_ , for a few examples of the changes that can be made permanent by exposing the unprotected physical form to evanescence.
