## Renraku

Renraku’s affinity for automation and efficiency makes Kechibi a prime target for them. The predictive analytics they already use bear a remarkable similarity to what Kechibi seems able to do. Being able to pick apart and analyze the code might allow them to streamline and advance those processes even further.

Unfortunately for Renraku, I think that some of the managers in their shadow-ops department have managed to get their grubby paws on the code, or at least an executable fragment of the code. You’d think that would be great for Renraku, right? No dice, these territorial, mid-level managers are way more interested in using the code to jockey for position within the organization than they are in giving it to their employer. How do I know they have it? I can’t be one hundred percent sure, but I managed to get my hands on an internal newsletter. The front page congratulated two managers for winning the lottery, commiserated with a division whose workers were all losing their jobs, and reported that through a bureaucratic error, sixty years of taxes that Renraku had avoided by becoming extraterritorial were somehow assigned to one particular manager. Renraku decided that it was a lot easier to get rid of that kind of headache than to fill in the forms needed to correct it, and they terminated that person’s employment to avoid any liability. There is now a bewildered former high-flying middle manager wandering the barrens with trillions of debt. No one wants to mess with him in case any of his debt rubs off on them. Did you know that many tax-collecting bodies have a formula that authorizes different levels of force when collecting debts? I didn’t, but I do know that Renraku is negotiating directly with multiple taxing bodies. Normally, a company like Renraku wouldn’t worry too much about what the government could do to them, but in these cases, other members of the Big Ten are happy to back up tax collection options, so that’s enough to keep them nervous.

Are you up for some pro-bono work? I can think of someone who will be very grateful toanyone who can convince any tax collector that they’ve made an error. He is a bit shy of cash at the moment, though.

In any case, I think it’s clear that someone was using the code here to get rid of their internal rivals, although I suppose it’s still possible that this was an actual genuine error by tax collectors, or maybe some sort of vengeful AI is loose within Renraku, but it looks related to me.

All this politicking has to be frustrating the accountants and financial planners within Renraku. I wouldn’t be surprised if the whole thing doesn’t trigger a wide-reaching restructure within the corp. Mind you, if people really do have the code, or even part of it, and want to resist, it might look like a game of urban brawl in a treehouse. Expect blood.
