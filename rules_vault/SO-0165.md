## Obstacles

The original name of the con comes from the use of hard currency before the nigh-universal use of digital money. The work was in transportation rather than documentation. While you can now do all of this very easily, hands-off and from home if you feel safe doing so, it’s a matter of bombarding the mark with false, speculative, or otherwise convincing secondary sources and research to make the sale, because the Matrix makes it very, very easy to do some research. You have to flood them with the idea that you’ve got the inside track and mainstream media won’t reveal such an incredible payout. And why not? The powers that be wouldn’t let people know that, and even if they did, this wouldn’t be an opportunity. The secrecy of it is the key to overcoming a lack of more official or recognized authorities.

The second risk is the currency itself. In the mass bureaucracy of modern economy, there’s a lot of anonymity in the crowd. But some, particularly corpscrip, can have a greater degree of being monitored. Plenty of corps and governments drop bots into the system to track its use for myriad purposes. One of those is fraud. Odds are low, but you want to cover your tracks as best you can.
