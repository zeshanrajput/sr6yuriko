## It’s Just Biz

Depending on a run’s duration, there’s a chance that one or more counter factions may seek out a player character’s contacts in reference to their current activities. This might happen during, or more often, after the run. The time it takes for a



faction to track the PC’s contact depends on the team’s current Heat Modifier. Whether the contact provides any information depends on their Loyalty and Favor Points. The gamemaster can use these factors to determine if their contact meets with the faction and if they provide information on the runner and/or their team.

Optionally, if the gamemaster wants to rely on the dice instead of story elements, roll 1D6 and compare against the current Heat Modifier. If the value of the die is lower than the Heat Modifier, then the contact meets with the faction. If the contact’s Loyalty and Favor Points are lower than the team’s current Heat Modifier, then the contact will also provide some information. If the contact meets with the opposition during the run, the Heat Modifier increases by 2. If this meeting occurs after the run, then their Heat is only increased by 1.

When should the contact flip or stay silent? This is decided using a Con- or Influence-based test using their loyalty rating +3 as the threshold. The character’s contact can add Loyalty or additional modifiers based on any Contact Keywords (see the Contact Keywords section) related to the defense test. The gamemaster can decide if the contact trades unused Favor Points for a dice pool bonus on the defense test. If the attacker is successful, then the contact will spill information to the faction. If the defender is successful, they stay silent or cover for the team.

Depending on the outcome of this test, the runner should then let their contact know that things are hot right now and that they appreciate the cover. The runner will need to spend 2 Favor Points to convince the contact, and this prevents the team from gaining any Heat, even if the contact has to meet with the faction.
