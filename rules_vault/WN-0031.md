## Washington Heights

- **Council Member:** Linda Brewer

- **Security Rating:** B

- **Typical Host Rating:** 2

- **Primary Corporate Funding:** Evo, Centurion, Marsh and Hound Ltd.

Before the quake, people hardly realized Manhattan existed past Harlem. The Heights, and neighboring Inwood, were forgotten bedroom communities inhabited by overlooked social and racial groups—not much has changed since then. These days there are a mix of corporate enclaves nestled among the clusters of brownstone apartments and homes, almost giving the area a suburban feel. You can spot the wealth in the district based on how tightly the houses are packed together and how high they rise toward the skyline. Near the border with New Harlem, the area is dense with apartment buildings rising as high as twenty stories. Moving toward the northern tip of NYC, the space between houses grows, and the security gets tighter. The zoning for anything north of 180[th] Street is eight stories or lower, and by the 190s, the apartment buildings give way to actual houses. At the very tip, the 196-acre Inwood Hill Park serves as a monument to the ancestral home of the Lenape tribe. Descendants of the tribe can still be found performing rituals on a weekly basis.

Residents of the Heights are predominantly metahuman, though that percentage sinks the further north you travel. Evo fire and medical workers from CrashCart represent the bulk of corporate citizenship in the area, with Aztechnology, Renraku, and Wuxing also funding corporate housing communities there. Smaller corporations have been making inroads in the area by funding schools, churches, and other civil initiatives.
