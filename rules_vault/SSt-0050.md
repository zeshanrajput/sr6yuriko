**Let me emphasize that last part: do not exist. A SIN is an indelible digital footprint. Shadowrunners happened because so many people couldn’t get a System Identification Number and be “real people” as far as legal recognition is concerned. Someone without a SIN is a squatter, a transient, a non-person. If they break into Megacorp B’s secret facility, they have no legal protections, meaning no lawyer, no representation, no rights. If they don’t exist, Megacorp B is not obliged to treat them any way but how they want. By the same token, nothing they say is admissible as evidence. They literally cannot betray Megacorp A, at least not in a legal sense that violates Corporate Council laws. They are absolutely and utterly disposable.**

**Allow me to remind you that any megacorporate employer is never your friend. You can’t afford their friendship. You can be useful, you can be a treasured asset, but you’ll never be a real person to them.**

**That’s a con. Right there. Deniability. Simple invisibility in the right light to someone. A con isn’t about being unseen, but unrecognized. You’re not fooling the eyes but the psychology of your mark. You belong right where you are, what you say is true and credible, and cooperation with you is in the best interests of the target. You’re inventing a person who doesn’t exist to take the fall for you. Just like your employer does.**

**The difference is that the fake you doesn’t exist and can’t catch heat. You’re simply a tool, a persona, created to do a specific job and be discarded at a future point. It’s not necessarily a victimless crime, but it has a lower body count, and if there’s a victim, it won’t be you.**
