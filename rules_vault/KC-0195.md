## Feng Shui of a Metropolis

It is no secret that Hong Kong is a remarkably busy metroplex. Historically a place of complicated business and foreign intrigue, it remains as complex today as it was before. Wuxing currently has the crown and Sharon might be as close to a Queen or Empress as it’ll get, but there are a lot of competitors. Business trends here go the way of most major hubs, of which east Asia has many. More than a hundred A-rated corporations call Hong Kong home, plying their services and products across the Chinese kingdoms and feudal lands, with a few of them steadily rising to join the AA-tier as soon as they can. The total number of unrated businesses are perhaps incalculable, especially in the more financially challenged districts such as Kwun Tong or Wong Tai Sin. And while Maersk is banished from the main core of Hong Kong, it still has several ports open to it in both Tuen Mun and Tai Po.

Of course, all the other megacorporations have offices and complexes both in the business core located in Wan Chai as well as the Central, Western, and Eastern districts on the island. Some of the Japanacorps have office towers on Hong Kong Island but are more concentrated in Yau Tsim Mong and central Kowloon City. The most notable of the megacorporate powers that seem to be working more in tandem with Wuxing these days is Gaeatronics. They had good relations at the height of the PPG and appear to be retaining them. The latter’s efforts to provide more power for the ever-famished Hong Kong districts and lessen the brunt of the massive waves that roll in with the seasonal typhoons have not gone unnoticed. Many of the sprawl’s Awakened and eco-friendly residents see the cooperation between the region’s largest megacorporation and the eco-friendly practices of the power provider as an agreeable direction.

The club scene is many-faceted, with all kinds of metahumans represented well, but the real heartbeat can best be felt in the party cores. Famous celebrities from across Asia and the Pan-Pacific nations add their glitter to the crowd and help keep the atmosphere lively. Then you can

throw in sports mega-athletes, a few astronauts, dozens of stellar musicians, and of course any number of wannabes and hangers-on. Industries and styles that started in the twentieth century have evolved to keep pace with the trends of the twenty-first, even after the devastation of earthquakes, VITAS, and dozens of typhoons. Nothing is going to keep Hong Kong down, and they have the magic to prove it.

One cannot speak of Hong Kong without mentioning its Vibe and the impact of the Wuxing geomancers upon the region. Since 2064, the number of earthquakes has diminished, with most no longer noticeable to the average person. Astral energies flow across Hong Kong, creating a sense of serene ebbs and flows, and many peoples and entities take advantage of the region. Neighborhoods along one or more of the ley lines, often called dragon lines here, find an odd balance each day. Even the more violent criminal elements stay clear of these neighborhoods because of the shifting forces at work and the Jingshen (spirits) and other Kai qi bi (beings of malicious intent) who congregate in many of them.
