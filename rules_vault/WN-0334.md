## Tracking the Code

The commcode Dr. Peters provides is not the kind that’s just listed in an online directory. Calling it leads to a few clicks and beeps, with no voice message. No one will call back unless the message left after the beep has the exact phrase, “memories are fleeting, even more so in these high-tech times.” Leaving that phrase (which would have to happen with dumb luck, as there is no way the PCs would know it) will result in someone calling back and being willing to set up a meeting, which would make the PCs’ lives easier. Since that is extremely unlikely, though, they’ll probably have to do things the hard way.

The best option for the PCs is to hack the device that answers the commcode, using either Brute Force or Probe and Backdoor Entry, then locate it using Trace Icon (p. 180–184, _SR6_ ). The opposing Willpower is 5. The PCs will need to succeed on this test at least twice. The first time, they will see that the device they are tracing currently sits in Brooklyn, way down near Coney Island. After about five minutes, they’ll lose the signal and will have to Trace Icon again. The device will have moved, heading toward Manhattan on Ocean Parkway. From this point on, the PCs will lose the trace every twenty minutes and will need to take another Trace Icon action. The device will generally continue along Ocean Parkway but may veer north and east as the PCs get closer.

When the PCs find the device, they’ll see it’s a commlink (Transys Avalon, device rating 6; for an extra obstacle, have some local hangers notice that an expensive commlink was dumped in the trash, and they went to claim it for themselves). There is no data on the phone—no personal information, no messages, no call log, nothing. Careful PCs will be able to pick up some clues from the link:

• The individual who dumped the commlink carelessly left a fingerprint on it. A corporate

contact could help them run it through some corporate databases, though for a fee (100 nuyen per megacorp database searched). Running the fingerprint through a Mitsuhama database reveals the fingerprint belongs to Oscar Aparicio, General Services, Corporate Level (that, of course, is corp-speak for “person likely involved in some shadow work”). If the runners call Mitsuhama and ask around, they’ll eventually be told that Aparicio is out of the office. If they mention Anima or Animus, they’ll be given an address where they might be able to find him: 400 West 181st Street.

- Hacking into the commlink can uncover its recent movements. It was at Coney Island for a few hours before the PCs traced it, but before that it was on the north end of Manhattan, near the former site of the Washington Bridge (also where 400 West 181st Street would be).

Returning that information to the Mr. Johnson who hired them will complete the mission and keep Mr. Johnson busy while they figure out what it means. The information they retrieved is worth a 5,000 nuyen bonus (increase the bonus by 500 nuyen per net hit on an Influence test), If all they got was the commlink, Mr. Johnson can figure out the information they missed, but they do not get the bonus.
