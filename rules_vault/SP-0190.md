## **Elimination of Treats**

**While it’s been talked about at length already, it is nonetheless an essential part of the Disian strategy and needs to be included here. Not only does the elimination of threats provide the obvious benefts, the mere threat or knowledge that otherworldly forces are possibly lurking in every shadow also provides a strategic and psychological beneft.**

**Much like the sniper pinning down a platoon, those under attack must now alter their plans and methods to avoid additional casualties while simultaneously forcing the issue of what to do regarding the current casualties. Both are meant to keep the opposition off-balance and force them to act from a position of disadvantage or, in many cases, their “Plan B.”**

- **Not to mention the losses of personnel and materiel. A smart adversary, which the Disians are, targets the biggest threats first and either eliminates them outright or ties them up in such a way that they’re no longer a threat, even if only briefly. Granted, not every attack accomplishes the first objective, but even a failure can accomplish the latter; contingency plans can only go so far in mitigating damage.**

- **Rifleman**

**Currently, this part of the Disian strategy is ongoing and shows no signs of abatement anytime soon. While shocking and brutal, the attacks perpetuated during the Week of Death were not quite as successful as intended. Numerous high-value targets have been eliminated or disrupted, but according to current data and projections, the Disians only succeeded in eliminating between ffty to sixty percent of their intended primary targets during their initial**



**strike. This has forced the Disians to engage in supplementary strikes, which are ongoing for the foreseeable future. While the attacks still signifcantly hurt and in some cases scattered enemy forces, it was far from the victory the Disians required.**

- **A significant number of anti-Disian forces have been scattered but not destroyed. Most of us already had emergency protocols in place for just such a situation. But we still must deal with the loss of materiel and, more importantly, personnel. Not to mention reestablishing networks, lines of communication, and regaining the initiative. Good news is that along the way, we’re picking up new recruits who are either now aware of the threat or have an axe to grind.**
