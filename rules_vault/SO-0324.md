## Lipguard

Originally designed as a lipstick seal enabling it to last longer and not leave behind smudges, this thin layer of invisible coating also prevents contact vector drugs and toxins from being absorbed. This application for preventing a poisoning is limited, because it only protects the area that is covered.

Applying it to more than a small patch of skin is wildly impractical, but still technically possible. Lipguard is not just for lips; it can be used on other parts of the body. For example, if a target somehow knows that they’re going to be the victim of a contact vector poison attack and knows exactly where and when the would-be assassin is going to strike, they can apply lipguard to help protect the targeted area or other parts of their body.

For assassins and other less scrupulous individuals, lipguard provides a unique option for attacking a target. By protecting their own lips by applying lipguard, they can then apply a layer of a contact-vector toxin or drug on its surface, and they’re able to deliver a poisoned kiss. The same method can be used to transmit a toxin or drug on the palm or other part of the body. Of course, the user must be extremely careful not to accidentally dose themselves in the process.
