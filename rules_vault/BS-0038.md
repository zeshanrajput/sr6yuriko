## Negative Quality: Dying Cyberware

Your cyberware is just about dead, and nothing can be done with the hardware other than to nurse it along and hope it doesn’t fail at a bad time.

**Bonus:** 5 Karma

**Game effect:**

- Designate an implant that costs one or more Essence. This implant is out of warranty and failing. A good mechanic or cyberdoc can identify problems with visible implants with a visual inspection.

- When using the implant, one die in the roll is replaced by a wild die.

- The runner must make an Edge test each month; if this test glitches, the implant fails and doesn’t function. If this would be lethal to the runner, the runner may survive but cannot succeed in any physical or mental test. They can call a friend to come and help them. An implant that has failed this way cannot be repaired, but it can be replaced.

- If the implant is successfully hit by a called shot, it is permanently disabled as if the runner had glitched the monthly Edge check.

The other thing to note here is that this is a hidden cost for retired runners. If an augmented runner retires without making a big final score, or at least enough nuyen to replace their implants every few years (or remove them), they’re likely to eventually get into a situation where their implants fail and they’re unable to fix them.

This can be a source of money for the less-ethical runner as well. If someone has ’ware they’re emotionally attached to but is no longer in service, or the parts are hard to come by, they might hire a team of runners to bring in someone who has more of that ’ware that can be used for spare parts. It’s not usually a money thing—if someone has money to hire runners, they normally have money to upgrade to the next model. It’s almost always some unbalanced sociopath who has a weird obsession or compulsion for a particular item. Maybe it’s the cyberspur that killed a drake, maybe it’s the first datajack they had implanted. It’s easy money, but if word gets around that you hunt retired runners because someone’s warranty expired, your name is going to be dirt.

- You ain’t going to survive long. For one thing, you’ll be on my personal shit list. I want to retire one day, and I don’t want someone coming after me for spares.
