## Same place, 1.33 kilometers higher, 23:38:43

The Roadmaster of Sigorski’s team, still slightly smoking in places, was on the scene faster than was possible using only his drone’s earlier position ping. He nodded appreciatively. Perhaps the team had prepared a crawling drone near the factory, which had attached itself to the cab’s underbody, perhaps they had shot an RFID tag at the cab, or perhaps the group’s magician—Tatzel?—had sent a spirit after the cab. Whatever the case, the team had not lost the trail and had been able to catch up to the cab by using the main streets.

Instead of thundering rashly into the Schattenhafen, the Roadrunner had approached in an exceedingly measured manner. A heat blob, unsteady from the drone’s point of view, had detached itself from the back of the vehicle, and with a quiet “whup-whup-whup,” fired three microgrenades toward the cab. Smart airbursts with range detonator, based on their accuracy, and two flash-bangs and a tear-gas grenade, based on the reaction of the two opponents near the cab.

Only now did the driver of the Roadmaster push the cruise control forward and speed toward the target, the separate heat blob continuing in a circular motion.
