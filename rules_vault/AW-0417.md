## The Power Plant

MCT has recently set up an outpost within the Wastes of Miggon in the ruins of what appears to be an old nuclear power plant that melted down. All of the ancient signs appear to be in Russian. The MCT team at the outpost consists of elite soldiers, magicians, and engineers, and they have wasted no time in getting their own structures up. Most denizens of Miggon avoid the power plant and the dead town that surrounds it due to the nature of its occupants—a particularly nasty type of toxic mutant animi that are easily mistaken for ancient lifeless corpses until they hear or smell your presence. The MCT team deals with this threat as they would any other annoyance—through the expenditure of ammunition. The toxic fallout ghouls attack in waves several times each day.

The power plant itself is massive, too huge for the MCT team to fully explore, let alone occupy more than a small fraction of. The MCT fortress is a small section with reinforced walls, with two entrances that are set up as kill zones. These kill zones provide clear fields of fire that allow the armed guards, mages, and turrets to target any approaching threat. They treat everything that approaches the fortress as a threat.

Within the power plant, the fallout ghouls lie in wait or prepare ambushes for any potential prey. Prey is scarce in Miggon, and so most of the ghouls within the outlying town lie in torpor. The ones within the power plant have been awakened by MCT’s activity and are restless and hungry. No matter how many are shot down in attacks against the MCT fortress, there always seems to be more of them to join the next wave.

Besides the MCT team and the fallout ghouls, almost nothing else lives within this region. Even the Crimson Carnage avoid this place (but only after several failed attempts to raid the MCT fortress), though the occasional Kr’u’Ba finds it to their liking. While some magicians have searchedthe power plant for unique reagents, the only ones to be found here are tainted, and only of use to a toxic magician. You would be wise to avoid anyone you observe harvesting reagents in such a place.
