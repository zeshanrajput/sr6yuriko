## Managing Community Standards

When planning a job or simply obtaining paydata, don’t overlook the residents of the community where you’re operating. Different communities are going to have varying and often contrasting categories of residents. But I’m going to talk about three larger groups rather than go in-depth about the various kinds of communities out there. And even those are going to vary in terms of socio-economic factors, religion, metatypes, and languages.

Let’s start the discussion in a little place known as the Barrens. Living here is cheap (if you call it living). Much of the time, it’s seen as just another piece of gang territory. Those who consider the Barrens their home could still prove valuable depending on what you’re looking for. They know who’s trouble and which houses are empty. Since no one wants to glance at someone who’s doing worse than they are, they’re effectively invisible. This makes them very good at being your eyes and ears on the street.

Next are the working-class neighborhoods. During the day, these places tend to be quiet. At night, you’re likely to see blue-collar folks trying to escape from the doldrums of the day. These are the people who make the world run. Without them, the rich will just elevate another group who fears sliding back down the economic ladder. These folks are excellent sources of information, and bribes can go a long way.

The last group are the “wealthy-and-theyknow-it types.” Even though they tend to have higher security around them, they can also provide valuable paydata if you just look the part and visit a Soybucks for a green soy latte. Gossip is a way of life and the privileged are also often easy to leverage, because when you’re loaded and bored, vices are your escape—and when you have a long way to fall, you’ll do anything to avoid taking the plunge.
