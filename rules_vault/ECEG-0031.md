**Tampa, Miami, and Savannah are bigger, Louisiana privatized its major ports back during** the Lynch era. The United Port Corporation of South Louisiana (UPCSL) took over the Ports of New Orleans, Greater Baton Rouge, South Louisiana, and Louisiana Offshore (an offshore platform connected via pipeline with the mainland). With the privatization and sale to JRJ, these ports became a single port that stretches all along the Mississippi River from the Gulf to the outskirts of the city and upriver to Baton Rouge. All the way down, you will fi nd container terminals, breakbulk cargo piers and terminals, ferry stations, and LNG as well as chemical terminals (not to mention the giant cruise terminal in Downtown). When JRJ bit the dust, Mærsk acquired their JRJ Sealand subsidiary. Then Fuchi, Mærsk and Gunderson became a fi ll-in owner for some time, until the former two collapsed in the late ’50s and early ’60s. Today, UPCSL is owned by Wuxing (55 percent), Mærsk Terminals (25 percent), KondOrchid (10 percent), and Symington-CWR (10 percent). Besides port logistics, coordination, tugging, and navigation service, UPCSL also offers warehouse space, renting out industrial real estate, as well as maintaining the channel depth of fi fteen meters, so it stays traversable for Panamax and Neopanamax ships. Lone Star holds the security contract, while the Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA) provides waterways inspection. Main exports are unprocessed and processed food, fuel, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, and refi ned minerals. Main imports are crude oil and gas, minerals, steel, agritech (fertilizers, seeds, etc.), electronics, and special machinery.
