## Drip Lightning falls

Fumaroles in the hillside along the southern edge of Mowich Lava Flow have killed off most of the vegetation and stained the soil white and yellow. Somewhere deep below, a coal seam is burning, and at night you can see a blue glow from them. On particular foggy days, drip lightning falls spit up sulfuric slime burning a bright blue. The whole hill is dangerous to approach due to the high levels of hydrogen sulfide and sulfur dioxide.
