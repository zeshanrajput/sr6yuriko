Oceans, seas, and lakes. At last count, roughly seventy percent of the planet’s surface is covered in water, with the world’s oceans making up somewhere in the region of ninety-six percent of the water on the planet. That’s a lot of water, to be sure, but it’s also the seat of life. The primordial oceans are where life began, and life still teems within the waters of the world. As Bubbles has repeatedly informed you fi ne folks of JackPoint, the world is utterly and irrevocably dependent upon the parts covered in this chapter for its very existence. The oceans, seas, and lakes of the world are absolutely vital to our way of life.

Water is also not a habitat suited to metahuman life. Not only do we need air to breathe, but we also are poorly adapted to living on or in the water. We are not powerful swimmers, we are not adapted to the cold or pressure one often fi nds in an aquatic environment. Light plays tricks under water, so seeing can be a challenge, too.
