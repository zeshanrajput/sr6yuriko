**My research points toward their defensive musk as the reason for the salt, since the musk is quite similar to chlorine gas. This is one of two unique defenses that protect the porcupine from predators. The second are the tail quills. With a ferce whipping of its tail, the volleying porcupine earns its name by hurling groups of quills at potential threats.**

**Back to the point of trying to RFID one when I didn’t realize I was working in one of those neutral zones. I have never dipped, ducked, and levitated so fast in my life, but I survived a potentially deadly encounter and tagged two piasma in one day. Win for me!**

**I also learned that the piasma is quite the skilled climber. Levitating to escape required haste and some aeronautical maneuvering in order to keep clear of one of these beasts scaling a tree like a squirrel.**

**These are never used in an offensive manner, as the volleying porcupine is an herbivore. They eat roots and bulbs in the warmer months and tree bark in the winter months. This occasionally leads to lethal levels of bark loss for a tree, and this makes the porcupine a threat to logging companies.**

**The volleying porcupine family structure is quite social. They live in burrows in groups of parents and children. A packed burrow could include up to six individuals, putting a serious strain on local food resources and potentially damaging or killing a large number of trees during the winter months.**

**Skills: Astral 3, Athletics 5, Close Combat 6, Outdoors 2, Perception 4, Stealth 3 Powers: Armor 8, Dual Natured, Enhanced Senses (Vision), Immunity (Cold), Natural Weapon**

**When encountered, a volleying porcupine will start by puffng up its crest to scare off a predator. If this doesn’t work, it will whip tail quills if the predator is still some distance away, or unleash its noxious musk if the predator is close. If attacked, the porcupine moves to put its quilled regions into the maw of its attacker. This method is almost one hundred percent effective, as nothing likes to bite down on a bunch of barbed spikes.**
