## Just Being a Mark

Sometimes you get scammed, and it has nothing to do with a run. Maybe it’s just the guy at the supermarket who helps you pick out the right flavor of krill-cakes. People simply try to make nuyen any way they can, and they’ll do their best to separate you from your cred. Other times, it’ll be related to biz.

My first story highlighting that idea is about a street doc who fragged me over. No one likes being a mark, and this one hurt. I’d been looking for a new doc while working in Chicago. So I did the usual and asked around about someone to help with minor tune-ups and some cheap upgrades on my ‘ware. A few days later, I got a name and commcode. I felt like this was a guy I could trust. The whole time I worked with

him, he swore he could get his hands on some high-end gear, and after a couple of months of us knowing each other, he says he’d managed to score a piece of ‘ware I’d been looking for. It was pricey, but I had been saving. So,I told him I’d get it installed.

The day of the surgery rolled around, and honestly I should’ve seen that something was fishy. Hindsight is always twenty-twenty. Sure, there was luggage behind the counter. Yeah, the doc had somehow booked another surgery immediately prior to mine so he couldn’t show me the new ‘ware. Either way, I was surprised when I woke up without my credstick and missing a couple augments with the doc nowhere to be seen.

Now, you probably have a few questions, like why he’d leave me alive and why didn’t I hunt him down? The answer to the first was that I always bring a friend for surgery, and I give them access to my biomonitor. The second part was that I did, and I spent a bunch of time and resources trying to get him. The best I could get out of my friend was “He said he got an emergency call and that you’d wake up in about an hour.” Other than that, he was gone.

The second story isn’t about me, but one of my regular crew. We were hired to work security for a party, but that was a cover for our real goal—one of the attendees needed to die. Our mage was having a fling with a local over the previous few weeks, and she just happened to show up at his favorite bar the day after our meet with Mr. Johnson. Now, the caster was careful and didn’t let anything slip. The only thing he told her was he couldn’t go on a date the night of our job because “he had plans.”

Come the night of the job, we were good to go. We had eyes on the target, and a friend was working the door. Everything was going to plan until she showed up. Suddenly, there was a lot of confusion and that’s when her team sprang into action and extracted our target. I don’t know whether her team knew anything about us, or that she thought we were security and picking one of our members as a target was the best way to handle the job. We completed the job, but it was a lot harder and cost us more nuyen to do it.

My friend was devastated and honestly didn’t know she was working against us until he tried to contact her after it all went down. She ghosted and hit him where it counts … right in the heart.

- It’s a good hustle. Even when someone doesn’t think they’ve revealed anything useful, they don’t realize that when you combine it with other information, it becomes valuable. Sorry for your friend, but pillow talk has doomed a ton of people.
