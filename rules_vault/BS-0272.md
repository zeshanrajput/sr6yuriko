## Leech Symbionts

Though Genesis is leading the field of ectosone technology, UniOmni/Renraku were the first to introduce leech ectosymbionts. Leech symbionts usually feature one or more neural shunts that create a link between the nervous systems of the host and symbiont.

**Carapace Leech:** Carapace symbionts possess a natural hardened shell and are implanted subdermally in critical areas (covering the chest and upper limbs), acting as a form of dermal armor (p. 71, _SR6_ ). Careful clothing can conceal the leech symbionts from view. This symbiont is incompatible with any other skin modifications.

**Cleaner Leech:** For a long time, the only leech symbiont in use was the cleaner, which is simply a modern take on a procedure used thousands of years ago. Flesh-eating insects, usually maggots but sometimes small beetles, are introduced to a wound, where they consume only the dead flesh. They clean the wound in a matter of days far more thoroughly than modern science can with tools. The insects only eat necrotic tissue, avoiding any damage to living flesh whatsoever. As dead flesh has no nerve endings, the procedure doesn’t so much as a tickle. Unfortunately, people tend to react badly to the sight, so dressing the wound is vital to the procedure’s success.

Cleaners are a temporary symbiont that provides +1 die to natural Healing tests until all damage is removed and +4 dice to resist secondary infection until all wounds are healed.

**Cypher Leech:** While most of the symbiont research is fairly old, there are a couple of new avenues that have started to show up. Some Emerged symbiotic leeches were discovered near power substations. When technicians went to perform routine maintenance on the software, they found that the firewalls of the local hosts were bolstered beyond what they should have been. This brought in some researchers from the parent company. With two weeks’ training, the leeches will boost the Firewall of a specific Matrix device. For this service, they only require sustenance from their host.

Cyphers are a symbiont that provides +1 to the Firewall rating. If the device it has been trained to protect is ever destroyed or swapped, it takes an additional two weeks to retrain them to protect a new device.

**Glow Leech:** This leech symbiont is mana-sensitive, possessing spliced glo-moss genes. Whenever an astral form passes through the host body, the symbiont trembles and visibly glows. Glow symbionts have also been known to light up when passing through mana barriers, a ward, or in the area of a spell. Allow the host to make a Perception + Intuition (2) test, with a +2 dice pool bonus. If the test succeeds, the character feels the symbiont vibrate, and the leech glows.

**Sac Leech:** This leech symbiont is essentially a living chemical gland (p. 57), designed to release its compound into the host’s system with a neural signal. Each sac leech can only produce a single compound, chosen when purchased, and the selection cannot be changed. These compounds must be non-magical. The sac leech produces a single dose every twenty-four hours and can hold as many as two doses at once.

Note that the implantation of a sac leech in no way provides immunity, or even improved resistance, from the substance for the user. The sac leech keeps the compound from leaking into the body naturally.

The sac leech’s cost is the base price of the gland plus an amount equal to the cost of one hundred doses of the chemical compound it produces. The Availability of the sac leech is 6, or equal to the compound created, whichever is higher, and is licensed or illegal if the chemical carries such a flag. A user may have several sac leeches. The sac leech may be connected to a cybernetic reservoir.
