## **Ultra Platinum Security**

**The frm’s specialty is providing “the best security you can hire” (and they spare no expenses to please their demanding clientele). According to their sales pitch, Aigle Royal agents are better trained, better equipped, and more importantly, know how to behave in society—or at least, better than your typical corporate security thug. They also know how to keep things quiet and deal with the actual threat without stopping the party.**

**Their SOTA gear is certainly the most obvious incarnation of their schtick. Their Kevlar-lined suits incorporate bio monitors that send an alert should something happen to the agent. Their eyewear also features top-of-the-line optics and**

**enhancements. In the unfortunate case of violence, the guards are suffciently armed (typically with submachine guns and fashbangs) and can often hold their own until a backup team’s arrival. They are also experts in drone surveillance when there aren’t enough agents to patrol their assigned area. These drones, usually small and simple models, are programmed with advanced scanning autosofts to patrol vents or small accessways that could be exploited by intruders.**

**Their Matrix security is tight but limited to their own personal gear. All devices, including detection systems, are protected by a dedicated Matrix server. If the client pays an extra fee, their devices can be bound to it and even monitored by spiders in addition to their own personal Matrix security. A hacker who tries to defeat the physical security and alter their target’s data is left with double the work (hacking the client’s and Aigle Royal’s host).**

- **Thanks to this strategy, Aigle Royal got a leg up on the Matrix, but it was almost by chance. They decided to protect their unit with a server and not a PAN or even an M-TOC, so they looked early on into “hardware hosts” (the ones we call Framework hosts nowadays). Having those first ones ever commercialized by Erika also went well with their PR’s bulldrek on using the best possible gear.**

- **Sim-Eon**

- **On the plus side for us, if their principals are at a remote location, they will deploy this mobile server in the vicinity to not compromise Matrix performance due to signal loss. Which means if you find the van or other vehicle carrying the server, take it out, and you’re good to go.**

- **Slamm-0!**

- **On this particular front, Aigle Royal’s most painful failure happened on their own turf. Their customer paid extra to ensure the safety of the data they carried on their yacht anchored in the port of Nice. A few members of Section 89 simply swam under the small vessel carrying the mobile server and set charges under it, while Aigle Royal guards were pointlessly scrutinizing their immediate surroundings. The minute the boat exploded, Section 89’s deckers jumped on the now-defenseless system like sharks on bloody prey.**

While this could be seen as a weakness, Aigle Royal pays above market rates to their subcontractors to ensure they have the very best available.**
