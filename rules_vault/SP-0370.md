## **Faustian Mages**

**Possessing a Faustian Crystal can create a wide assortment of adept-like abilities in the possessor, even if they’re mundane. These crystals can also duplicate the benefts provided by technological augmentations and provide a type of faux Essence when cyberware is removed from a Faustian by the Dis. They may also be used to duplicate the magical abilities of para-critters for a Faustian in a manner reminiscent of Disian chimera.**

**For mages, some of the crystal’s capacity may be left open, and the mage can draw on the units of mana to augment their own casting abilities. Unlike the fxed abilities, though, this pool of stored mana is depleted when used and must be replenished by their patron.**

**The true value of a Faustian Crystal becomes apparent when they are gifted to a mage who has access to the full range of magical abilities. In addition**

**Using units of mana from Faustian Crystals follows rules similar to those for native Disians. Ten**

**units of mana can be allocated from the maximum capacity of the crystal to generate a power point’s worth of adept abilities. The abilities are set by the Disian owner of the crystal, not the Faustian. In addition to adept powers, the Disian may use a Faustian Crystal to duplicate the effects of augmentations or critter powers, similar to how chimera is constructed. The benefts provided by bioware or cyberware devices can be duplicated with adept-like powers, at a rate of one unit of mana for each 0.1 point of augmentation duplicated. This can either be new abilities or replacements for technology removed as part of the Faustian’s bargain. If these abilities are replacing removed cyberware, the crystal also generates a faux Essence that temporarily replaces what was lost by the removed ’ware. Augmentations that are designed to interact with other technology, such as implanted commlinks, cyberjacks, and vehicle control rigs, cannot be duplicated.**

**Critter powers can also be duplicated with a Faustian crystal, However, without providing a full list of the units required to bind every critter power, the cost in units of mana is up to the gamemaster. Very minor powers like Enhanced Senses are duplicated by binding 1 unit. The most powerful abilities such as Concealment or Fear require 10 units of mana, while powers that fall in between like Animal Control or Mindspeech take 5 units of mana to hold in place.**

**Any units of mana left unbound in a crystal may be used to augment spellcasting abilities, if the Faustian has the ability to cast spells. Units of mana from a crystal can be added to a Sorcery + Magic test while spellcasting or as a ritual magic leader, increasing the effective Magic rating of that one spell or ritual by the number of units added to it. The maximum units of mana that can be added is the Faustian’s Willpower attribute. Units may also be used to offset drain on a one-to-one basis, or they may be added to any Counterspelling test, up to the Willpower limit. Units may not be used to Amp Up spells or increase their area of effect; the standard rules for that still apply.**

**Stored units of mana used to augment Sorcery tests are expended in the test; the only way for this pool of units to be replenished is for a Disian to transfer units of mana from their own internal storage into the crystal. The Disian can do this while in mental contact with the Faustian—the only requirement is that they must be in the same metaplane.**

**As noted on p. 75,** _**Astral Ways**_ **, all Faustians have trouble using the Astral skill, and all astral perception and combat tests must include a wild - die. The Faustian Crystal also creates some diff culty with summoning, so all summoned spirits gain an automatic +2 dice on their resistance rolls. Units of mana may not be added to any summoning tests; however, they may be added to Banishing tests, up to the Willpower Limit.**
