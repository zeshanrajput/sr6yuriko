## Know Your Tolerances

If you go to a hostile environment, you’ll have your body and your gear, and you need to know what each can handle. Acquaint yourself with tables on how long it takes for frostbite to set in at various temperatures, how long before sunburn starts at various times of day, that sort of thing. You need the same information for any piece of gear you take with you, whether it’s a fi rearm, cyberleg, cyberdeck, or vehicle. Just because it works every day in the sprawl doesn’t mean it will be at all reliable in the desert or on the ice. Know what your items are capable of before you ask them to do too much.

The streets of the sprawls we inhabit don’t often seem friendly when we’re in them, but if you wander off into the more extreme environments of the world, you’ll fi nd some of the things you took for granted or had never thought about are suddenly signifi cant concerns. In most sprawls, even on a cold or hot day, getting from one place to another is a matter of selecting the right coat or jacket and moving on your way. In polar regions, by contrast, if you do not thoroughly prepare proper clothing, you might not make it more than a kilometer or two before you start losing extremities—or worse.



- **And remember that some problems can be mitigated more easily than others. Worried about how your rubber tires will perform on ice? Get some chains on them. Need to make sure your machinery stays in motion in the cold? Get some grease designed for low temperatures. Driving into sub-zero temperatures? Then your engine is going to be sluggish and your battery range limited and there’s nothing you can do about it. If you think you can just power through that last one, you’re wrong.**

- **Turbo Bunny**
