There is a small black monad cat (Jinxy) stalking along the walls and pawing at the occasional hole in the baseboard. She gives the runners very little attention, but if she sees them, she immediately alerts Piper.

Piper Jane’s office door announces her company’s name and job in gold lettering on frosted glass. The space is small and very well organized, though it lacks character. There is a single desk located directly across from the door. Two wooden chairs with red velvet cushions face the desk, while the walls are lined with an array of other chairs and couches with no single sense of style connecting them all.

Piper is seated behind her desk and immediately rises to greet the runners if they buzzed in or if she is aware of them from her surveillance system, a.k.a. Jinxy.

Piper’s straight red hair is pulled back in a tight ponytail. Her face carries touches of mixed European and North-African decent, with blue eyes, a sharp but small nose, and a caramel complexion punctuated by a mix of freckles and a beauty mark above the left edge of her lip. She’s currently wearing a button-up blouse done up just shy of the collar, blue jeans that have seen better days, a bulky commlink (which you suspect is a cyberdeck) on her arm, glasses with the distinctive glow of AR feeds at the corners, and a thin, black-laced collar with what looks like a subvocal mic attached to it next to a central black gemstone. Piper’s office is filled with AROs only she can see through her glasses and that’s where the chaos lies, with dozens of case files open and data pinned in the virtual air everywhere.

She greets the team professionally and then offers seats anywhere in the office. She goes for a quick round of introductions, saying that Swen speaks highly of them (whether he does or not). She then gets down to the nitty-gritty.

She opens a desk drawer and pulls out a hard case credstick holder and opens the top to reveal twenty-five credsticks. She says there is 1,000 nuyen on each stick, and the money is theirs for a job well done.

Negotiations can be handled as usual with a 500 nuyen bump with each net hit, but she lets them know the extra might need to come in after she gets the data out to the right people. If they balk, she reminds them they’re not working for a mega—they’re working for the everyday citizen who just deserves to know the truth.

After payment details are settled, she goes into laying out the job. Seems an elite meeting of corp types are gathering for a sales pitch that involves some serious c-suite hanky-panky. She doesn’t have an in for what the new tech is, but she has a list of the invitees. She wants the runners to get access to the event and get footage of several varieties on the attendees and whatever it is they are trying out or buying.

The event is Friday evening at 19:00 at Villiers Tower, located on York between 90th and 89th streets. The tower is sixty stories tall, built post quake, named for Richard Villiers (and currently owned by Novatech), and houses offices for a variety of companies as well as a collection of expensive condominium properties on floors twenty through thirty and fifty through fifty-five. The corporate suite for the gathering is located on the fifty-eighth floor and belongs to Sol Seeker Enterprises (a subsidiary of Evo). Piper doesn’t know what the exact purpose is, but she knows invitations were extremely limited and very hush-hush. She won’t speculate on the purpose because she has no idea—that’s why she’s hiring runners.
