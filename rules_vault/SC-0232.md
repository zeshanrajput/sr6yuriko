## defensive decker

## playing advice

## Roleplaying

An effective defensive decker requires incredibly expensive hardware, years of training, and a heightened sense of vigilance. It’s not the sort of thing that attracts newbies and hotshot hackers. You’re the kind of person who is dedicated, careful, and willing to do your job without making noise. It’s all to easy for your allies to take you for granted, because when you do your thing well, not much seems to happen. It’s only when you frag up when they notice. The surface pressure can be a lot for you to bear, but it also feels good to prevent something bad from happening.
