**Matrix: Cap-Atos (A), Lagardère-Publicis (A), Vivendi (A), Foçëa**

**Bank, Finance, and insurances: Index-AXA (AA) Logistics, Transport: ESUS (AA), Groupe M Security (Transport): Securitax**

**Retail: Groupe M**

**Food: Les Mousquetaires (A) (but not Carrefour, which, like Dassault, belongs to Aztechnology)**

**Energy: France Énergie (A)**

**Water and waste management: Veolia Envirotech (Proteus)**

**Aerospace: Aesa (AA)**

**Luxury industry: L’Oréal (Z-IC), Louis Vuitton (A)**

**Industry: Air Liquide, Barytech (S-K), Sanof-Aventis (ZIC), Michelin (belongs to Merenati), Merenati (AA) Tourism: Michelin (through Groupe Med)**

- **Sim-Eon**

**By nature, the French Touch is an extremely diverse organization. As is the case with the AAAs, virtually any market has one or more of its members playing the feld. However, the name resonates particularly in fashion and the food industry, including wine and catering. This has often put members of the FT in the crosshairs of Aztechnology, especially NatVat, Shiawase’s SECCA Co-op Commercial, and AG Chemie (controlling the majority of Germany’s wine-growing districts), Dallmayr (Nebelherr’s A-rated food and catering corp), Meridional (they still try to promote Spanish goods), and Swarovski-Joop (German-Austrian A-rated luxury upstart). Despite the competition, the marketing of the consortium has done its best leveraging the food crisis of the late 2070s.**
