## Re alistic Animals

The intent of this approach is to provide a framework for players to design the animal they want to play. Zebra, capybara, panda, or cobra are among the valid choices. The spirit of Shadowrun shifters is to be real animals, though—not your favorite venomous turtle-bear. Alligators should come with Functional Tail (Paddle). Sapient snake shifters could expect to open doors or grab a commlink, so Functional Tail (Prehensile) would be appropriate. When in doubt, your GM has fi nal say.

GMs are also encouraged to let animals do what animals can do. Primates can climb and brachiate. Canines can track by scent. Alligators and dolphins can stay submerged far longer than metahumans. Felines will refuse to eat anything with medicine in it, no matter how much bacon grease you use. Even if an ability isn’t mechanically represented, animals are still able to do what they do naturally. If there is a chance of consequential failure, use an Agility + Athletics test for most physical acts or Intuition + Outdoors test for more instinctive actions, with a threshold determined by the GM.
