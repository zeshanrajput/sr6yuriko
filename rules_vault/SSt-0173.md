## **Matrix and Rigging Crimes**

**The laws governing Matrix crime haven’t evolved much since the early part of the century, mostly because actions like hacking security and taking control of someone else’s “computer system” (as most legislation still calls them) haven’t fundamentally changed since then, even though the tech’s a lot more advanced.**

**The development that applies to most runners where Matrix crime is concerned is the requirement to broadcast a legitimate SIN. This mostly applies on corporate property, but a handful of draconian nations (including Aztlán and both Tírs) have allowed similar laws to stand when they’re challenged.**

**consider whether cyberdecks were “weapons” for the purposes of charges like “assault with a deadly weapon.” Most courts have ruled that they are, so if you slap a Matrix crime decker with a data spike and give them a nosebleed, it’s treated the same way as if you punched them.**

**For riggers, the legal hoops can be a bit simpler to jump through. Different courts describe their reasons differently, but most of them agree that any actions a rigger takes remotely are treated as though the rigger had done them personally. Most jurisdictions also say that a rigger can be held responsible for drones acting on autopilot while slaved to their RCC. In other words, if your dogbrain drone shoots and kills someone, you’re the one getting charged with murder.**
