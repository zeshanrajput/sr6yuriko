## Combat

When it comes to combat, it’s time to let the combat specialist shine. You must concern yourselfwith the number one rule of combat in the Sixth World: “geek the mage!,” and you are not likely well-suited to deal with incoming bullets.

Stay in cover, hold back your actions for defensive and active counterspelling anytime actions. Watch what happens each round, searching for the best opportunities to act using your spells and spirits as efficiently and effectively as possible. In most cases the most efficient use of your magic is to buff your allies. Alchemical preparations are especially good for this, as they can be made ahead of time. When they are needed, the magician does not suffer drain or need to sustain spells. The drawbacks are that they are of short-term use, must be made ahead of time, which can require a lot of bookkeeping and preparatory dice-rolling. Use of the Rote Alchemy quality is a great way to quickly take care of this, as it allows you to buy hits instead of rolling dice at a more favorable ratio than normal.

Some of the most effective spells for buffing allies are Improved Invisibility, Increase Attribute,

and Increase Reflexes, but there are many other possible choices.
