## **Your Day in Court**

**If you’re determined to get in front of a judge and jury and try to convince them of your innocence, good luck. This fle isn’t long enough for me to give you actual legal advice, so I hope you found a good lawyer. Instead, let me tell you what you’re likely to see in the process of going to court.**

**When you frst get arrested, you’re given a SIN, because the courts can’t do anything if they don’t have a way to track you. You then sit around and wait for your arraignment, which is when you appear in court and get told what you’re formally being charged with. In places with laws against arbitrary arrest and detention, the prosecutor is required to lay charges against you within a certain time of being arrested, which can be anywhere from forty-eight hours to a month. In places without those laws, you might be waiting a while.**

**If you and your lawyer agreed to plead guilty in exchange for a reduced sentence, you’ll have a few more hearings, then start serving whatever your sentence is. If you plead not guilty, you go into pre-trial custody,” which is basically jail but without being convicted frst. Depending on how backed up the courts are, pre-trial can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few years. What happens there is outside of the scope of this fle.**
