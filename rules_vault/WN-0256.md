## DJ’s Off Day

The runners’ surveillance of DJ should start at midnight, but if they get distracted or delayed, just start the Stealth tests when they get on the job. Below, you will find blocks of time, activity, outfits, and scene notes, along with a note in parentheses on who DJ is impersonating, though this is not obvious until after the footage is edited and released. Small clues, like a distinctive style of hair or dress, may be offered.

It’s possible, even likely, that the players will intervene after one or more of DJ’s activities. If they do, Mr. Johnson will not reappear. A backup sleepwalker will be activated to pick up where DJ left off, and the remainder of these events will take place without the runners’ direct observation.

**00:00–01:45:** (Michael Simmons, Citigroup): DJ leaves his building at exactly 00:00 dressed in a pair of sweatpants and a grey t-shirt and climbs into the driver’s seat of a waiting Range Rover Model 2080 (p.297, _SR6_ ) and heads toward West Side. On 87th, between Amsterdam and Broadway, he parks the Range Rover on the street, remote pays the parking from his commlink, and climbs into the rear of the van. He emerges from the rear door in a Vashon Island suit but now wearing a

black wig styled in a modern corporate chic.

Through the rear door, the team can see the back of the Range Rover has several trunks and a bar of clothes hanging across the rear bed.

After exiting the van, DJ walks two blocks to a ground-floor jewelry store named Starlight Jewelers, enters the store, saunters to the counter, speaks with the salespeople for a few minutes, then pulls a silenced Ares Predator VI from his jacket and kills everyone in the shop in a calm, methodical fashion. After they are dead, he reloads and puts several rounds into their host terminal, killing the local host. He fires a few more shots into various cases and shoves a smattering of rings, bracelets, and necklaces into his jacket pocket. As he exits the store, he puts one last round into the keypad next to the door and proceeds to walk back to the van via a different route than he walked to it.

There are a handful of people on the street by the jewelry store as everything occurs, but none notice the activity within—except one who is standing nearby when DJ shoots the keypad. DJ gives that individual a little imaginary tip of his hat before he walks off.

DJ arrives back at the van at 01:45, climbs in the side door, and reappears up in the driver’s seat, black wig gone and a chauffeur’s cap in place as he drives off.

**01:45–04:50:** (Dutch Adams, NYPD, Inc.): DJ drives what seems like an aimless route all over Manhattan for about two hours. At 03:50, he parks the van and climbs into the back again. When he emerges from the side door, he is wearing an NYPD, Inc., uniform. He now has AR glasses and a brown wig.

In uniform, he approaches one of the checkpoints for Terminal and starts chatting with the security staff in place. If the runners can hear the conversation, they hear that he is talking about a tip he got from his sister “who’s really into trogs,” that the Terminal Velocity gang is going to make a push on this checkpoint to jump in some new members. He gets the five guards present all focused on the flow of people from inside before slipping a long thin blade from his belt. He then proceeds methodically murdering the guards by sliding the blade up into the back of their skulls leaning their bodies in such a way that when the others look back, all looks well. He manages to kill three of them easily before the fourth notices something is off. At that point, DJ draws his Colt America L36 and shoots the guard square in the forehead and then unloads the remaining rounds into the last guard as he spins to see what’s happening.

The crowd at this hour moving in and out are mostly mind-your-own-business types—runners, squatters, gangers, and third-shifters—but they all scatter or take cover when the shooting starts. DJ then walks away like nothing is going on, takes a



different route back to his van, climbs in the back at 04:45, and appears in the front seat back in his Vashon Island and chauffeur’s cap.

**04:50–09:30:** (Caitlyn Dawson, Trans-Orbital): DJ moves across the island and sits along the shore to watch the sunrise. He’s changed into an oversized red velvet pantsuit, large sunglasses, and a wide-brimmed red velvet hat. The cut of the pantsuit is definitely feminine and DJ’s movements and mannerisms match it well. After sunrise DJ does not get in the van, instead he takes a little white convertible sports-car, a Eurocar Westwind X80 parked near the van.

He drives the car into the heart of Times Square and then proceeds to floor it through an intersection of pedestrians crossing from all directions, before gunning it past the incident and into a parking garage where he parks the bloodied sports car, steps out, calmly walks to the elevator bank, goes up one floor, walks over to another elevator bank, and takes it up a few more floors. On that floor he climbs into the back of the van (which used Autopilot and GridGuide to get here) and emerges in the front seat wearing a long black wig, jeans, and a t-shirt. He then blacks out all the windows on the Range Rover before driving out of the parking garage.

**09:30–11:35:** (Flynn Ames, Maersk): From the garage, DJ crosses town yet again, swinging through a McHugh’s drive-thru for some breakfast. After parking near the docks in Southside, he heads closer to the water to sit on a bench and eat his breakfast. After he’s done eating, he tosses the garbage in a trash bin and keeps walking another 100 meters. He leans against a wall, watching the trash can for a bit. At 11:30, he pulls a small device (a detonator identifiable by anyone with Engineering or Firearms skill) from his pocket, extends an antenna, flips open a safety cover, and presses the button beneath causing the trash can to explode in a massive ball of white fire that envelops six people nearby and sends white-hot shrapnel tearing into another dozen people nearby.

DJ then drops the detonator in another trash can and calmly walks back to the Range Rover.

**11:35–14:20:** (Cullen Schwarz, Saeder-Krupp): With the van windows still blacked out, DJ drives away from Southside, heading north. He drives to a residential block near Terminal, emerges from the back in a very unique suit, designed to look like lizard scales. He’s also wearing a black wig with gold highlights. DJ walks a block and stops in front of a large building that was once a Catholic church but has since been sold to the Church of the Dragon Reborn. He stands in front of the doors until they open at 14:05 at which point he raises both hands and appears to cast a flamethrower spell. Fire streams through the open doors, engulfing a

group of children in the foyer, just leaving their learning circle. Moments later, the entire front of the building goes up in flames.

DJ then moves hastily back to the Range Rover, climbs in the back, and drives off.

Runners know DJ is not Awakened, and they can enhance the footage to see the cuffs of his jacket are slightly melted and scorched, one revealing the tip of a high pressure hose nozzle.

**14:20–16:45:** (Faith Davis, SpinGlobal): DJ drives the Range Rover to Downtown. He emerges in a stunning high-collared red evening gown, complete with proper padding, dark sunglasses, and a long blonde wig. He strolls through Downtown, catching eyes all over. At one point he stops to have a long conversation with a good-looking, tall, dark-haired man. He obviously offers flirtatious touches and gets a few in return. At the end of the encounter, DJ kisses the man deeply and passionately. The man looks a little surprised but smiles as he watches DJ walk away.

DJ then heads back to the Range Rover and drives off.

**16:45–18:00:** (Vera Spokane, Prometheus Engineering): DJ drives to the East Side, parks, and steps out in a white dress, styled after a Grecian toga, but wearing a head-to-toe green bodysuit, with an auburn, professionally highlighted wig and black heels. He walks into the front door of an Evo office, stays inside for twenty-five minutes sitting in the lobby bathroom, and then comes back out and walks back to the Range Rover.

**18:00–18:45:** (Jackson Ito, Renraku Computer Systems): DJ makes a quick drive over near Terminal and parks the van a few blocks away. When he steps out of the back, he is wearing a spiky black wig and a leather jacket with a collection of patches but an obvious large blank section where a gang emblem would be. He enters an alley and goes down a basement staircase. Beyond the door to the basement is an abandoned storage room with a hole in the back wall that leads to a service tunnel into Terminal. While down there, he hands over several credsticks and datachips to a variety of different gang and criminal syndicate members. He leaves the same way he came in and finds two NYPD, Inc., officers in the alley chatting and smoking. The pair start to question him. It does not go well, as DJ keeps answering with nonsense. As they prepare to bring him in for a psychiatric evaluation, the Range Rover arrives at the end of the alley and activates all of its lights in a random flashing pattern. The officers are blinded, and DJ pulls out an SCK Model 100 from his jacket and drops both officers while they are blinded.

He then hurries into the van and drives away.



**18:45–20:35:** (Kensai Okama, Sony Corporation): DJ drives around seemingly aimlessly again for an hour before stopping in the parking lot of an AR arcade, cleverly named ARcade, at the corner of 36th and Third Avenue. He exits the van in a finely tailored dark blue suit and grey wig carrying an AK-97 over his back. He enters ARcade and ascends to the second floor. From there, he opens fire, shooting across the way and hitting only an empty building. It’s more than enough to cause panic on the street below. Then he walks into the Range Rover and drives off.

**20:35–21:50:** (Ashlee Corsair, Horizon Corporation): DJ takes the van toward Central Park and parks close by the entrance on 90th Street. He exits the van wearing a mini-skirt, loose-fitting blouse, short heels, and a blonde wig styled in a sharp down-angled bob. He leisurely strolls into Central Park and proceeds to kick several random small dogs much to the terror of their owners. One owner does pull out a small handgun but doesn’t shoot at DJ since he walks off immediately after the puppy punt.

He leaves Central Park after kicking seven dogs. On the way out of the park, he stops at a street vendor, gets a couple hot dogs, and sits down to eat them on a bench on Central Park East. After he’s done eating, he heads over to the van and drives around the island randomly again.

**21:50–22:45:** (Kim Dolsoon, Nagato Corporation): DJ’s next stop is the New Whitby Hotel on 56th Street. It’s nice, but it’s also very exclusive and private. DJ parks down the street and steps out of the van in tall stiletto heels, fishnet stockings, a black three-quarters-length jacket, a narrow-brimmed trilby style cap, with a black wig of long, silky-straight hair.

DJ walks to a rear entrance of the hotel, accesses an AR keypad for entry, goes straight to the elevator, and proceeds to the fourteenth floor. He goes straight to room 1408, where an attractive early-twenties male human opens the door wearing nothing but boxer briefs and lets him in. The pair begin to make out, but DJ suddenly stops and breaks it up. He says he can’t do this anymore. The lover pleads, then gets angry and says if he can’t have her, no one can. He takes a knife from the kitchen, but DJ pulls a Tiffani Needler from his coat pocket and shoots the young man several times.

DJ then leaves the same way he came and climbs back into the van.

**22:45–00:00:** (Cade Scanlon, Shiawase): The van again heads toward Terminal, this time parking near Jane and 4th Street. DJ exits the back of the van wearing a white suit, white dress shoes, and a white fedora over white hair. He walks a block, and rather than entering Terminal, he walks

into Brilliantz!!!, a jewelry store that is a cover for a high-end sex club upstairs. He heads up to the club and engages in a wide variety of acts with a number of different women for just under an hour. He walks back out to the Range Rover, climbs in the back, and the van drives back to his address, where DJ steps out the side door at 23:59 wearing the same sweatpants and t-shirt he left in, then enters his building at exactly midnight.
