## Explosive Cosmetics

Chem sniffers are able to detect most explosives, so sneaking a bomb into a heavily secured social event typically isn’t an option. However, if the explosive in question can be broken down into several inert components that can be easily disguised as lipstick, eyeliner, and makeup setting powder, they can be later combined into an effective explosive. Not something powerful enough to cause a significant amount of death and destruction, but perfect for blowing a small hole through a wall, a lock off of a door, sabotaging delicate electronics, or creating a distraction.



Combining these kinds of chemical components requires the right skills to complete the delicate process, but no detonator is required. Once properly combined, the explosive will detonate on its own using the chemical reaction as a fuse and timer. How long this process takes and how powerful the resulting explosion is determined by the quantities and mixture of the explosive components in question. Use improper components in the wrong mixture and the compound may not work at all. Or it could immediately detonate in the user’s hands.
