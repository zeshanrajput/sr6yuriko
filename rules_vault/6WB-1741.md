## Molotov Cocktails

Berlin is hardly the only city in the world where Molotov cocktails are used. But here, these homemade flaming devices are used so often in conflicts that they need to be explained in terms of rules:

The throwing and burning properties of any

“Molly” (and any other improvised incendiary device) depend on many factors such as the size, weight, and handiness of the bottle; breaking properties of the glass; and the filling used. For a cocktail made from a bottle of Schnapps or Jabifu (Janz Billja Fusel, a homemade form of moonshine found in many Berlin pubs) with a cloth stopper, these rules apply:

**1. Preparation:** Each cocktail must be prepared in advance, which requires two Major Actions (remove the cap, insert the cloth plug).

**2. Lighting:** Before throwing it, the Molly must be lit with one (more) Major Action.

**3. Throwing:** A Molly is thrown just like a grenade, but with 1 die deduction due to its inaccurate flight characteristics. The Attack Rating is 11.

**4. Fwoosh:** A Molly has an unpredictable area of effect of (1D6+1) meters and must land on a hard surface to break (a head will do, but a car, a wall, or the floor is easier to hit). All targets within the area of effect take 2P damage and incur the status Burning 2.
