## Slapback

[Trigger: Failed Composure Test, Action: Major (A)] 2 Edge per One Net Hit

Sometimes people just snap. And while that doesn’t often solve any problems, at least it makes the other team pay for what they did. When a player character fails a Composure test, they may spend an action to respond in kind. For every 2 Edge points spent, the opponent suffers the same effect as if the attacker had scored one net on the test they just made. The attacker still suffers the effects of their action, but so does the target. The most Edge that can be spent on this is equal to the number of net hits the opponent scored when they triggered this action.

What exactly happens may not be clear in every circumstance, and the GM may declare that the circumstances don’t fit this action if there’s no apparent Slapback effect to apply.
