## **James Jiang (Wuxing)**

**I’ll start with perhaps the biggest wildcard in the bunch. Jiang’s nine-year term on the Z-OGB board expires at the end of the year, which means Wuxing will not have a representative on the board for a year. Board members are almost never appointed to a second nine-year term, so Jiang has the chance to go out with a bang. There is a lot of speculation about what kind of bang he might prefer.**

**Jiang does not need much from the bank or the corporate world in general. He was wealthy before**

**he joined the board, invested well, and he is in his sixties, so he could retire comfortably and not owe anything to anyone.**

- **Longevity treatments, which Jiang certainly could afford, could extend his career, but it’s not at all clear he wants to keep working. He may take the relative youthfulness with him into retirement.**

- **> Baka Dabora**

**If he’s not concerned about business networks and money, that means Jiang’s focus is likely on his legacy, and what greater legacy could there be than saving the world? I expect Jiang to be the most active in pushing for shifts in how Z-OGB operates. The largest problem for him is that the cause he will most likely advocate for is moving Z-OGB investments to Wuxing, hoping to turn their allegiance in this struggle, which may be seen as self-serving and a waste of resources.**

- **There’s a solution that is simple in conception and complicated in execution: encourage the investment to go to MCT instead of Wuxing, then make sure Wuxing partners with MCT. As I discussed in the Wuxing section, there are obstacles and grudges standing in the way of that happening.**

- **Mr. Bonds**
