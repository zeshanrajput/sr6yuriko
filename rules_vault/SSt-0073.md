## **Starting Out**

**If you’re looking to become a rigger, I have a few recommendations on how to stretch your initial pile of nuyen out. If you don’t already have a pile of nuyen, that’s your frst priority. Get a lot of it together, a bare minimum of 200k, before you even consider getting started. I suppose a single-vehicle pilot with a used control rig might be able to get by with less, but a serious rigger’s going to need a lot of scratch. Like I said before, you’ll need to cover the cost for your implant, an RCC, a vehicle or two, some drones, and autosofts. These recommendations are only applicable if you have a fnite amount of nuyen—let’s say in the 200k–500k range. If you’ve got a lot more to play with, then you should hire me as a consultant so I can help you make the most of that fortune you’re sitting on—or maybe just retire early and live a life of ease and luxury instead of risking your life in the shadows.**

**First thing to deal with is your control rig implant. Without one of these, you have no business calling yourself a rigger. It’s a highly advanced and very invasive direct neural interface that connects directly to your central nervous system and sensory grey matter, and it’s what allows you to perceive remote sensor data as physical sensations—and to control technology remotely as a direct extension of your own body. With a control rig, you can jack in and jump in to any vehicle or drone with a rigger interface. Every drone comes standard with a rigger interface, and any vehicle can be installed with one fairly cheaply. Control rigs come in three levels, with improved levels allowing for greater connection and control. I recommend starting out small, with a basic control rig that you can upgrade later. The benefts of an increased control rig are substantial, but not as substantial as having enough nuyen for the other stuff you’re going to need to get started. Don’t be too squeamish about going with a used model for your frst one, either— it’s a great way to save money for more toys. Down the road, you’ll want to upgrade to a higher level of control rig with an improved grade as well, but for starters just get your foot in the door with the basics. If you’re going to focus more on jumping into your main vehicle or drone most of the time rather than relying on lots of drones, it’s worth spending some additional nuyen on basic medullan parallel processors and the best simsense interface overdrive you can afford—at this stage, save money by going with used implants if you can fnd them. You can usually fnd a wuality simsense interface overdrive implant gently used for the same price as a new model that’s only had as good—and the degree of invasiveness isn’t too bad for this. Unless you’re trying to pack in as much cyberware as possible to double up as a street sam or decker, it’s always worth it to get secondhand augmentations when you’re getting started.**

**Next up is your rigger command console. Unlike with the control rig, don’t cheap out on this. Get the biggest, baddest one you can afford, with the highest-level frewall rating possible. Opposing hackers are your worst enemies as a rigger—especially technomancers, with their fragging fault sprites. The Proteus Poseidon is probably the best starter RCC for new riggers, coming in at a price point of 68k, and it’s worth every nuyen. If you can get the next step up, skip the Transys Eidolon—its frewall isn’t as good as the Poseidon’s—and go for the Ares Red Dog.**

**Between your augmentations and RCC, you’ve probably spent at least 100k. If you’re going with the low end of the budget range I recommend, that leaves you enough nuyen for a halfway decent ground vehicle, a combat drone or two, and some surveillance drones. What vehicles and drones you choose will depend on your location, goals, personal style, and how much nuyen you have available.**
