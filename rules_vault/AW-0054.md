## Air

There is no terrain within this metaplane. No horizon. It’s just a swirling mass of colored gasses. Variations in temperature and humidity create wind, which moves the gasses wherever it will. The sky has shades of illumination, but never true darkness, as only the density of clouds changes as the incandescent gas orbits the metaplane. The sky is often shades of blue, but it’s fickle with splashes of orange and purples when least appropriate. It’s more of a challenge here to see that much of what is moving is alive. We can look up into the sky and imagine dragons and unicorns. Here, the coalesced gases can do that on their own. It’s a very unnerving and disorienting environment to travel to. How do you stand _up_ ? Do you have to worry about falling? Is anything tangible?

To those first-time travelers, you’ll find that there is a pull of gravity, but it’s not toward the center—it’s toward Foehn, the earth-air cardinal pole. Gravity has no bearing on how air elementals construct their civilization, which can cause disorientation and nausea. Cities can take any form, sometimes imitating nature that metahumans are familiar with, sometimes exploring unique, abstract forms. They glide easily through the air, taking in the light that suffuses the whole plane. Travelers will also find that the wind currents circulate between the hotter Tramontane, the air-fire cardinal pole, and the cold Korikyu the air-water cardinal pole. It is a fascinating interconnection between the planes and their development. One wonders what other forces can cross the dimensions—or have the cardinal poles always existed?

Like colored ribbons floating in the breeze, the super-fluid highways are the first signs of civilization noted. Reaching one of them whisks you away like a fast-moving river, slowing only upon reaching Octahedron or any of the cardinal poles. It’s only when you get to one of these destinations that you see more familiar shapes of air elementals like sylphs. The wilder spirits have a very flexible, freeform shape that they use both offensively and defensively. The sapient spirits have a consistent form, with the capabilities of squeezing past solid objects like gas.

The great cities are of denser material, though light enough to float with varying translucency. Most

public places are built with large spaces, with arches and flying buttresses. Doors and windows are often viewed as silly concepts for beings with adjustable forms. The more private structures have a small slit that interrupts the integrity of the perimeter with material that can adjust the level of opacity.

Air elementals do not linger in thought or place. Stagnation is a common fear among them. They are sometimes viewed as omnipresent, though their speech may be hard to understand as the trains of thought behind the words are foreign to metahumans. They are fast in thought like fire, though they extend themselves in multiple directions—and not just following the paths of least resistance—to come up with multiple conclusions and plans. Not all are good ideas, as air elementals can easily get stuck in circular logic, or eddies.
