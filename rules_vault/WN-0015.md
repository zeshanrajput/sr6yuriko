## Points of Entry

The Metropolitan Transport Authority was formed in 1965 as a public benefit corporation, though it languished for years until the MDC redefined the power structure in the city. Afterward, the MTA became the controlling entity of all points of entry to the city. Officially, the MTA is now owned and operated by NYPD, Inc., but the management ranks are loaded with representatives from all major corporations that call NYC home.

The cozy relationship between the MTA and the corps is writ large, as MDC corporations have renamed nearly every single port of entry in a reflection of self-interest. That said, the MTA data pushers do an excellent job monitoring who and what moves through their city. Be it by air, land, or sea, nothing gets in without being noticed. If it isn’tsupposed to be here, then the right palms need to be greased to let it in.

Officially, the ports and public transit are secured by an NYPD subdivision known collectively as the Transit Police. In reality, that branch of the NYPD is staffed by bureaucrats and officers near retirement age and supplemented by sub-contracted Ares Hard Corps, Winter Systems, and Minuteman Security personnel dressed down in MTA uniforms.
