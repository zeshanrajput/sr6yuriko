## Sprite Fortress

A mysterious and hidden host has been found on the Matrix, and one of the defensive decker’s contacts has acquired the information needed to find it. This contact is not willing to risk exploring it but wants to hire the decker and their team to find out what is inside. The strange part is that it can’t be accessed through normal means—it seems to exist fully on the Foundation, and entering the host requires something a bit more special than your typical host run. The contact will also note that they have not heard any rumors of anyone coming back from attempting to hack the host, so the risk is high. The decker’s own hardware and skills won’t be enough. In order to give them the

best possible chance of success, the whole team will need to connect to the defensive decker’s cyberdeck through a specially modified hitcher jack so they can assist them.

_Note_ : Future sourcebooks may include rules about Foundation runs. You may refer to those rules if you have access to them or follow the suggestions included in this section.

When the runners jack in, they will appear before a tremendous virtual fortress. Its walls are thick and vast, and their perimeter indicates there is tremendous space inside. The environment will seem fully realistic, not at all like the Matrix everyone is used to. Around the fortress as far as the eye can see, the horizon is a featureless plain. Any attempts to explore away from the fortress inexplicably lead back to it.

When in this place, all of the runners have the same attributes and skills that they possess in the real world. Thinking about a piece of equipment they own in real life will cause it to appear, allowing them to bring along any weapons and ammunition they already own. Anything not owned by the runners in real life isn’t available to them here, but they can gear up beforehand however they like. Augmentations function as expected, but magicians are a little out of luck. No adept powers, spells, astral abilities, spirits, or magic abilities of any kind translate into this place. Technomancers are mysteriously unable to compile or call their registered sprites, even ally sprites. Runners have full access to their skills and may use them normally within the environment. In addition, the Cracking skill may be used in place of any other skill on a test.

Any damage taken while on this run is Physical biofeedback damage, resisted using Willpower + Firewall (using the defensive decker’s Firewall attribute). All runners gain the benefits of any of the defensive decker’s software, qualities, devices, or anything else that provides a bonus against biofeedback damage. If the decker falls unconscious, the run is over and everyone else experiences dumpshock.

The journey into the fortress will have several stages. The first stage is penetrating the wall. The wall is very resistant to direct assaults, seeming to take no damage from vicious assaults—even explosives. Searching for a secret entrance, however, will eventually provide an opening.

Next is the outer halls, which are regularly patrolled by floating obelisks of interlocking stones and symbols. The runners can either use Stealth to sneak past the patrols or fight them. The attributes and skills of these bizarre guards are all 6, with Initiative 12 + 1D6, AR and DR 12, a beam attack [DV: 3P, Attack Rating 12/12/12/12/12], and Physical and Stun condition monitors with 11 boxes each. Their dice pool for all tests is 12. They employ suicidally direct combat tactics. They ignore all wound modifiers and recover 3 boxes of damage to both Physical and Stun condition monitorsat the end of each combat round. If defeated, they vanish, then reform completely where they were last destroyed a minute later. Reformed guards behave as if they have forgotten the previous combat and will only attack if the runners stick around to be noticed.

Past the outer halls is a long series of inner chambers filled with a wide variety of strange denizens—some appear to be human, but most have an unusual appearance. The denizens go about various errands and tasks and don’t seem to be interested in stopping to chat. They aren’t hostile, but they don’t answer questions or offer help. If pressed or attacked, they will flee. If any are killed, all of them will stop what they are doing and flee in terror to various hiding places.

Eventually, the team reaches the center of the fortress, which is guarded by stern-looking sphinxes. The sphinxes will challenge the runners, asking unusual questions, but ultimately seeking to learn what the runners are hoping to find. Only once every runner has given an honest answer will they allow them to pass. If the runners attack the sphinxes, point out that they seem very powerful and likely to fight to the death. Use the stat blocks for the Eastern Dragon (p. 220, _SR6_ ) for the sphinxes, but without hardened armor or any critter powers.

Upon entering, the runners will learn the secret of the fortress: it is a sanctuary for free sprites, though even they do not know how the fortress got there—they claim to have just happened upon it. They filled it and dedicated it to their purpose, which is to be a safe place where free sprites can train, build up strength, and figure out what to do with that strength.

The corps, of course, would love to harness that strength for themselves—or possibly destroy it if they feel it might become a threat. Free Matrix advocates would like the fortress to be preserved as an anti-corp bulwark. The runners will have to decide whether to share this secret with their contact and get paid a lot of nuyen, or keep it secret. If the runners protect the secret, their contact pays them a small amount of nuyen for the disappointing lack of paydata, but each runner gets bonus Karma and gains a young free sprite contact with the types Matrix and Resonance, Connection 3 and Loyalty 5.
