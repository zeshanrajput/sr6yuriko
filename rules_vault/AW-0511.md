## a note on mana transfer

Gateways, portals, and rifts will often create an unaspected mana flow or a mana ebb in the vicinity of the passageway. Higher-mana metaplanes create a mana flow, while lower-mana metaplanes create ebbs. The intensity of ambient mana on the connecting metaplane will determine the level of the flow or ebb, as it will match the level of the connecting metaplane and extend 2D6 meters away from the passageway. The mana flow or ebb created by a passageway will linger for some time after the passageway is closed. Gradually dropping in intensity, moving from high, to medium, to low, to none, by one level every 1D6 hours until the effect dissipates entirely.

The altered mana level around a passageway can also help or hinder the Mana Siphon ritual (p. 140, Slip Streams). Natural mana ebbs count as a (low –1, medium –2, high –3) net hit reduction to the ritual, while natural mana flows (low +1, medium +2, high +3) count as a bonus to the total. This is in addition to any positive or negative effect the altered mana levels around the passageway already have on the casting of the ritual itself.

**High Mana** : Magic is abundant here, and the metaplane is filled with spirits, other magic-using entities, and devices that incorporate magic into whatever technology is available. The metaplane is a non-aspected medium mana flow where Edge actions and Edge boosts using magic cost 1 Edge less than normal. Any test that includes the Magic attribute also receives a +2 dice pool bonus.

**Very High Mana** : It seems like every creature, device, and even the landscape itself has some type of magical power, feature, or effect. The entire metaplane is considered a non-aspected high mana flow that benefits everyone. Any tests made here that include the Magic attribute automatically gain a point of Edge and a +4 dice pool bonus.
