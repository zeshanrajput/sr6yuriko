## Test Drive

Mr. Johnson has an interesting proposal for the gun-fu samurai: Take out a new prototype firearm for field evaluation. Mr. Johnson is willing to pay 5,000 nuyen per month the weapon is tested and will supply the samurai with any ammunition, of any type, they require. Mr. Johnson will also foot the bill for any necessary repairs. The downside is that the firearm will have an active gun-cam that will record all performance data. The prototype firearm in question will be the same as one the gun-fu street samurai already uses (with some minor cosmetic modifications). Stat-wise, all Attack Ratings will be increased by one, and the player character receives one additional Minor Action for re-loading. What Mr. Johnson doesn’t tell the gun-fu samurai that an AI has been embedded into the prototype’s smartgun system. This AI also provides an additional +2 dice to resist hostile Control Device Matrix actions. All of this sounds good until the AI starts to develop a sort of conscience. This may manifest in different ways, such as: refusing to shoot at certain targets, or changing ammo from lethal to less-than-lethal rounds (if applicable), or changing the targeting data just enough for the gun-fu samurai to miss the target. Or the AI develops a bit of a violent streak, switching to lethal rounds when less-than-lethal are called for. The AI could also switch firing modes to increase the chances of collateral damage. Perhaps, at various times, the AI could do all the above for no apparent reason other than to simply do it. No matter whathappens, Mr. Johnson will want all the data collected. Should the gun-fu samurai lose or maybe purposely discard or destroy the AI, Mr. Johnson will be most displeased and actively work against the gun-fu samurai in the future as well as expect the samurai to pay 50,000 for “loss of property.”
