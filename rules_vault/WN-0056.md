## Big Apple Corporations

The list of corporations that call NY home can fill a datastore. Most are passthroughs used to hide the assets of individual corporate citizens. Some are joint ventures between otherwise unaligned corporations, generally in service of a shared objective. With so many players, the NY corporate scene wound up bisected by a line of A-tier status. If you don’t have at least one “A” next to your name, then you’re hardly worth noticing. When the Corporate Court took up residence in the towers, in effect it defined that line. Unrated corps have no voice in the court, no interest stake in Z-O Bank, and thus no value to the corporations positioning for leverage within the city.

There is a hierarchy here, but it isn’t directly related to the number of As before your corporate name. A request from, say, Nagato, may get a more immediate response than a request from Ares. It is a question of leverage within the Manhattan Development Consortium. Given the way that council is presently shaped, there are AA-rated corps who have more influence on city affairs than some of the megas.
