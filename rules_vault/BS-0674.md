## Cybertechnology

The integration of human and machine continues unfettered. In the hundred and twenty years since the term “cyborg” was first coined, we have moved from pulp conceptualizations of stylish chrome machines, to the ubiquity of elective limbs and eye replacements. While cybertechnology is now commonplace, the commercial sector continues to operate decades behind what is possible. The fastest breakthroughs in cybertechnology are coming from the military industrial complex, and with due cause. At the core of any military operation is a soldier. The cost and time commitment of training a soldier create a negative value relationship based on the time any flesh-and-blood soldier may effectively serve their corporation or country. Cybernetics is the key to extending the amount of time a soldier may serve effectively in the field.

In the next five to ten years, you should see a proliferation of what my working group is referring to as Sidecar Protocols. This represents a series of embedded command structures or, in advanced cases, a limited artificial intelligence designed to supplement user control of cybernetic functions. In such a case where the user can no longer operate— be that through loss of consciousness or other mishap—the Sidecar will take over and allow the user to continue through their present function and pilot the user to a pre-programmed location, making use of all available cyberware and interfacing with any other connected systems under user control.

The central purpose of cybernetics is to enhance and replace human functionality. If you have a defective part or were born without a function, cybernetics may restore you to full capability. Such prosthetics are commonplace, though we have long recognized that the merger of man and machine comes at a cost. We generally tabulate that cost as the loss of the aural aspect generally referred to as Essence. The research on Essence being performed at the Istituto di Genetica e Biofisica and other lesser-known locations continues to yield surprising if confusing results. Where we once believed Essence to be a one-way denominator of our core humanity, that belief may be misguided. Here is what we do know for certain: The human body is a shell in which we hold our consciousness. What form that shell takes is slowly becoming a list of expanding options.

I believe the future of cybernetics will redefine the human form from a mechanical perspective. Non-human experimentation performed by Manadyne as well as by Evo, UO, and Aztechnology proves that the brain is the only biological part of the body necessary to maintain the self. With the brain suspended in a solution providing nutrients, the human concerns of food, air, and water are removed. Likewise, the barriers associated with those needs vanish. In short, the shell can be whatever we choose it to be, so long as proper steps are taken to maintain the biological brain and the non-biological essence of that core individual. Research and funding of the shell concept began in earnest four decades ago. In that time, the science has moved from the misnomer of a cyberzombie to a clearer understanding of purpose. With the presumptive loss of humanity challenged, we can begin to explore what is possible.

First, we must ask why are we engaging with cyberware? The difference between biological and cybernetic modification can be codified by purpose. Genetic modification, while reservable and even moldable, is a slow process. When we are shaping ourselves toward a purpose, the genetic modification option is best employed in long-term environments. The cybernetic option is designed for multirole modalities, and we can see in some professional environments that those modality packages are already being employed. A subject can be pre-fitted with interlocking mechanisms at the stem joints to allow for ease of modification.

**>** Elective paraplegia is already becoming more novel in industry jobs. Workers sign incentive deals to have their limbs permanently removed and are fitted with cranial packages to help them manage the replacement limb sets they are provided. The limb sets are assigned based on working conditions—Blue, Brown, etc., as I detailed in my biotech post. These customized limbs are upgradeable and reusable, and in the end you’re talking about people whose limbs literally belong to the corporation.

**>** The Smiling Bandit

In my own work with the military, the purpose is clear: creating an edge on the battlefield. Does that mean developing a situation where it is viable for a soldier to add a second set of arms to their body? Does it call for augmentations such as gills and webbed feet to provide faster transit through water? Does a soldier require wings and lighter bones in order to have a better vantage point? Given the different environments that may become a field of battle, you can see that this is not a onesize-fits-all approach. However, when we return to the idea of extending a soldier’s time in the field, it all boils down to a single advancement. The human body must and will be fully replaced by a cybernetic shell.

The goal of current military research is to create a combat-efficient body that is largely cybernetic in nature. That shell would be able to sustain the damage and specific environmental requirements of the battlefield. These shell constructions are not inherently humanoid in nature. They are mission- or purpose-specific creations. A corporation, which I will not name, is exploring the feasibility of a human brain being integrated fully into the operational framework of an extraplanetary vehicle. This would allow for a smaller, lighter, and faster exploration vehicle that could pioneer the next phase of manned space travel. Closer to home, Ares corporation has taken the first steps toward housing a human brain in a vehicle shell, specifically a tank. While this development is sure to have the military-industrial complex buzzing, more commercial integrations are possible. Situational transfer is possible. The shell you wear todaydoes not need to be permanent. Advancements in human-machine integration at the brain level are the key to all of this. We have long been restrained by the human form. This obstacle is eroding. With the presumptive loss of humanity challenged, we can begin to explore what is possible.
