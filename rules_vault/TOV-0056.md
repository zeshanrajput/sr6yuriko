## C onjuring

Voodoo is a possession tradition. The spirits summoned cannot materialize like those conjured by magicians from most other traditions. The lwa are selective in what they are willing to possess. They will only possess a prepared inanimate vessel, but not any living creature unless they have the Serviteur quality. This results in two valid possession targets: prepared inanimate objects that match with their preferred colors and symbols, and serviteurs. Gris-gris and zombies are created through special rituals which use the Conjuring skill. This doesn’t prevent them from being summoned and remaining in the astral.

Voodoo is very different from the approach of other magicians and their relationship to spirits. The practitioner of Voodoo doesn’t summon the lwa, but instead is appealing to the lwa to come and grant a hearing. To convince the spirit to appear, the presence of symbols, foods, sacrifi ces, and dance that appease that divine entity are used during rituals. If the lwa heeds the request of the caller, this does not mean that all wishes will be granted. Instead, the caller needs to convince the spirit that the request aligns with their values.

Each time the summoner asks for a service or boon, it takes a Minor Action. As a part of the action to give the command, the summoner must make an opposed Infl uence + Charisma or Con + Charisma vs the lwa’s Willpower + Intuition. If the summoner is unsuccessful, the spirit has declined to do the specifi c task. If the summoner’s request aligns with the aspects of the lwa, the gamemaster may provide a bonus point of Edge to the summoner to be used on the Social test. Failing the test does not expend a service, and the Minor Action remains expended.

The lwa prefer to be summoned as part of a ritual as opposed to summoning quickly through a single action. The lwa prefer that the summoning is part of a ritual in a hounfour. Like ritual spellcasting (p. 143, _SR6_ ), this involves selecting a leader, choosing which lwa to conjure, work in a hounfour (magical lodge), prepare an offering (reagents), and seal the ritual which takes fi fteen or more minutes. The summoner seals the ritual using a Conjuring + Magic vs. Force x 2 Opposed test and gains a point of Edge due to the use of the ritual. Conjurers are restricted to only summoning lwa. They are able to banish any spirit, including lwa. However, when a Voodoo conjurer wishes to banish a lwa, it is not a command but is instead an appeal to the lwa to leave. The use of the lwa’s symbols or preferred offerings grant the banishing magician a point of Edge that must be used on the Conjuring (Banishing) or drain resistance test. Magicians of the Voodoo tradition can banish non-lwa spirits normally, and magicians of other traditions can also banish lwa.
