## **The Seers’ Guild**

**I can speak of this from my conversations with fellow guild members. This relationship has a little tension because the guild feels Convergence is**

**poaching some of their best members, but Convergence has since clarifed that they are happy to have their employees retain guild membership, so that has helped. Most of the time the relationship between the two groups is quite collegial, as many seers very much enjoy sharing information about one another’s divinations.**

**The big divide between the two groups is that while Convergence is all about looking into the disaster they believe is in the horizon, the Seers’ Guild is not fully convinced of this event. Some have seen enough from Convergence and are on board, while others think Convergence seers are victims of confrmation bias—once they came to believe in their big “something,” they started interpreting everything in that light, so they can’t stop seeing signs that the thing they think is going to happen is in fact going to happen. For the most part, though, Convergence seers view this skepticism as a healthy check on their assumptions and eagerly reach out to seers who might attempt to rationally talk them down from their position.**
