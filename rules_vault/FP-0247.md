## **Resources**

**All of these plans and objectives are nice to have, but what kind of resources do we have access to? A lot less than we would like, for sure. This is defnitely our choke point, especially fnancial re-**

**sources, and one of the key reasons we have not been able to make as much progress as we would have liked.**

**Our income streams are minimal. We mostly get funds from our members and sympathizers. We also do fundraisers, the occasional blackmail, and ransoming. The Coderunners are also becoming quite adept at ransomware, embezzlement, and other Matrix-based fnancial crimes, but in the end, this money is not suffcient to cover all our needs. Between buying land, buying weapons and gear, and paying for the services of smugglers, coyotes, and runners, it doesn’t take long for money to run out.**

**In the end, compromises and choices are made, money is allocated, and people aren’t always happy, but until we can diversify and increase the output of our fnancial stream, that will be the main chokepoint in our operation.**

**So with that constraint in mind, we make do with what we have. Equipment-wise, we are surprisingly well stocked. It’s not always state-of-theart gear, but we have stockpiles across the continent. Most of them can be found in vacant bolt holes and safehouses. We also have more substantial stockpiles in our more permanent complexes that I mentioned earlier.**

**As I said, the gear is rarely recent. Hell, we still have stuff that goes as far back as the War of Liberation in the 2010s; it’s old, it’s clunky, but it still works well and is quite effective against technomancers, since they are unhackable. Most of it is a little more recent, bought from arms dealers, stolen, or liberated from trucks in the late ’60s and early ’70s. We have some armor and drones, favoring tracked and mid-sized drones.**

**Explosives are another big expense. We blow things up—that’s been our thing since the ’40s. So we favor explosives over general gear if we have to choose. While we have access to military-grade explosives, mostly through the black market or “lost” gear from the NAN’s armed forces, we actually have a bigger stash of commercial explosives, which are easier to obtain, as well as the chemicals required for home-cooked explosives. They’re usually not as powerful and not always as stable as the military-grade stuff, but we’ve become adept with mixing them. And let’s be honest, a nail bomb or two in a parade doesn’t need to be very powerful to be effective.**

**We have occasionally managed to make a few dirty bombs. They’re expensive, so we’ve used them sparingly. They’re effective, though, at keeping the governments wondering how many we have left and when we’ll be using them.**

**Finally, magical supplies. Whether reagents, telesma, fetishes, or foci, as a society with a strong magical tradition, this is quite important for us, and quite expensive. We have some talismongers and crafters within our ranks who help somewhat, but even the basic components are still fairly ex-**
