## **Making a Connection**

**The easiest way to make a connection to Convergence is to have an established record of being able to perceive something about the future. No matter what your tools—magic, hacking, an unerring intuition, whatever—if you can show that you can get a glimpse of what is about to happen, odds are Convergence will reach out to you soon. If they haven’t already.**

**The majority of us, though, don’t have that gift, so we have to rely on other ways to connect to Convergence. Most of us reading this are interested in getting work from Convergence, and the good news is there is plenty of it. I’ll get to more specifcs in the next section, but the broad view is that Convergence has a deep need for information (not dissimilar to the Tiding, discussed later in this posting). Information about the future often comes in incomplete snippets, and runners are exactly the people they want to fll in those gaps. The jobs are interesting, because they’re often a little different from the norm (“identify a man based on this sketch” rather than “kidnap the man in this picture”), but the problem is they are never straightforward. As you should understand by now, Convergence is not swimming in normal waters. As some diviners have described it, some of the things Convergence has noticed that have raised their alarms are fuctuations in the manasphere/aether/ what-have-you, and when you start playing with those sorts of things, they don’t stop fuctuating. To jump to another metaphor, once you’re in the river, you’re in it for the whole trip, even if it happens to run off a cliff. Which it often does.**

**This means that your simple identify-the-manin-the-drawing job is not going to stay simple. You might fnd out that there is a secret organization that desperately wants you to** _**not**_ **fnd the man and hinders your every move. You might fnd out that the man is not a person, but some sort of animated construct hosting a type of spirit you have never encountered. Or you might fnd out that the target changes his identity every week or so, and his reasons for doing so are going to make your life much more complicated. What is** _**not**_ **going to happen is that you clearly identify the person, fnd a name and address, send it to Convergence, collect your money, and go home. It’s never that simple.**

**That means that when Convergence looks for runners, creativity and fexibility are important. If you think you qualify, make sure your fxer talks about you in this way when presenting you to Convergence.**

**In the other direction, you may want to reach out to Convergence to get information from them, since they have a lot of it. This can be especially true if you think you or someone important to you**



**has appeared in one of their visions. You could try to arrange for a consulting visit with Convergence, but your bank account better be healthy. Consultations start at 1,000 nuyen per hour, and that’s with a fve-hour minimum. If you want just an hour, you’re still going to have to shell out 5,000 nuyen. And don’t think the consultant is just going to spill the beans about everything Convergence has about you or anyone else. They’ll say that in order to help you, they need to understand you more, so they’ll subject you to a number of probing questions, very much hoping to get more information out of the session than they give away. You’ll need to be on your toes to get any control of the conversation, and the meter will be running the whole time.**

**But we’re shadowrunners. We don’t pay thousands of nuyen to get what we want. We’re supposed to be paid, not pay. You want to play Convergence, not be played by them. So how do you do that?**

**The good news is that you know what Convergence wants—news about their “something.” The bad news is that their bulldrek detectors are well-honed, so don’t think you can just make up something to tell them and get their attention. You absolutely can sell them information—but it had better be good.**

**Probably the most effcient way to get them information they’re interested in is with the Sixth World Tarot. Each card is a sought-after artifact— even if images of it are all over the Matrix, the cards themselves are valuable to diviners. They want to study each card, stare at it, assense it, do whatever they can to understand it more. So if you get your hands on a tarot card, Convergence personnel would be happy to take a meeting with you. They’ll still try to steer the conversation to their interests without giving you information, asking you where you got the card, how you got the card, and so on, so you’ll have to work to get them to tell you things. At very least, though, if you successfully bring them a card, you have a good chance to get hired to bring them another one.**
