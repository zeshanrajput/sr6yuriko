## **Infernal and Internal Affairs**

**Since we’ve talked about dirty cops already, let me ask this: Is it the dirty cops you really hate? Odds are, the ones you fght could fall on either side of the spectrum, they’re doing their job, and you might kill them for it. The dirty ones are the ones who are most likely to be on your contacts list, the ones with a price tag you can meet. Bad cops cut deals with criminals, and you’re a criminal. You’re the dirt they get dirty with by dealing with** _**you.**_ **Or do you hate the ones who make sure they don’t get dirty? And then there are the good ones, the ones you can almost respect, the ones who try to do the ethical or moral thing.**

**Then there’s IA.**

**So, what’s an IA agent to you? Bad news is they’re the ones who make sure that the system works exactly how it’s supposed to. Read that again and again if necessary until it sinks in. Sure, they may get some bad people off the street, but if ever there was a candidate for propping up the system, no matter how stupid or clueless those running it are, IA is it.**

But they’re suddenly authorized to use “unconventional assets,” which grants operatives legal sanction and the capacity to claim you resisted arrest doing horrible drek. And if you kill any of them, then you’re a cop killer, and earn the extra attention of investigative bodies whose entire resource set is based around catching you. Good luck.**

**Civilians have a very romantic view of what IA investigators do. They think that we watch the watchers, that we’re the elite units that oust bad badges. It’s a nice idea, but it’s not very accurate. In reality, we’re the “Playing Catch-Up Squad,” always ten steps behind.**

**I’ve been in the squad just shy of ffteen years, and I can count on one hand how many times I’ve lived the dream of slapping bracelets on some dirty cop. Most of the time I’m leading investigations into frivolous claims. Someone didn’t like they were told to “shut up,” or the never-ending excessive force complaints. Someone thinks they can get two minutes of fame or payout because they had handcufs slapped on. It never ends! And it bogs down the legitimate complaints such as gross abuses of power. Too often those reports get buried among complaints of a cruiser driving too fast. It’s the reason a lot of honest badges don’t want anything to do with us, why we’re seen as the “enemy” among our colleagues. Having to grill some ofcer over how roughly he spoke to a double-murderer during the arrest doesn’t exactly inspire camaraderie and confdence among the ranks. Though, there’s another reason that bad badges are able to escape our judgment, and that’s because a lot of them are my colleagues.**

**IAB has so often been wielded by corrupt command staf that it’s a common joke among badges that we’re the goons of whoever is in power. A number of IA investigators have concocted completely false criminal complaints against other ofcers, to satisfy some internal political struggle or personal vendetta. It’s … disheartening, to say the least. The last arrest I made was of a woman I had known for ten years, a friend of mine. She’d taken a payout to frame a detective for criminal misconduct, because said detective was getting close to fguring out that his precinct was involved in racketeering. When I put the cufs on her she looked at me and asked, “What would you have done?”**

**I simply replied, “My job.”**

**—Three Tusk Trin, Crimson Crush**
