## The Belarusian Fire Drill

## Elements

It’s not quite that simple, of course. You need a few basic components. Last time I talked about this, I said you need haste, stress, and a prop. While I stand by that, let’s elaborate a little more. First, you need to blend in for the purpose of being beneath alarming notice. That is to say, if you were trying to infiltrate an office block, you should be wearing the uniform, proper office attire, or even a courier getup. Something everyone is used to seeing and subconsciously passes over in favor of something more interesting. In a corporate environment, you’ll get stares with mohawks and SMGs.

After that, you need a prop. This can be anything from a wrapped package to a hardcopy folder to a piece of equipment. It shouldn’t be clear exactly what it is, or what importance it has or to whom. You want to strike the balance between “it belongs” and “it’s above my paygrade.”

Stress and haste are somewhat interrelated. You’re moving fast under pressure, each feeding the other. A little sweat, a dark or worried expression, the stride that says you’re not going to run, but you would if you could. You can vary this depending on your prop. For example, delivery of prototype ammunition isn’t going to be jangled about. You’re going to be drenched in sweat and moving gingerly. I wouldn’t use this everywhere, but I’ve heard it works in some circumstances.
