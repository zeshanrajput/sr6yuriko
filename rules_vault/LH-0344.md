## Erma Werke chain weapons

The most popular market for chain-weapon prototypes ended up being Germany (specificalNEWly, and maybe ironically, with neo-anarchists), so much so that Saeder-Krupp is looking at producing its own versions of these weapons. Before the golden wyrm could respond, however, Evo moved to dominate the market by pouring production (and marketing) resources on its Erma Werke subsidiary and their line of powered chain weapons. This line started with the Hirschfänger (chainblade), the Kettenhund (chain-sword), and the Nachtwächter (chain-glaive).

The newest item breaking into the Wäfen brand lineup is the Kettenfaust (chain-fist), a gauntlet-vambrace combo with a 15-cm chain blade. When activated, the blade slides out of a sheath in the vambrace to a mount on the gauntlet at the bottom of the fist. Like other fist-hand weapons, these are often sold in pairs.
