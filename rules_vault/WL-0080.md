## **Sandworm**

## **(Vermes saxi aridus)**

**While not often found in mountainous regions, the sandworm is a dry-climate, desert-dwelling subspecies that is exceptionally (okay, supernaturally) good at garnering water in the driest of regions. This is actually due to a connection to the metaplane of water. They are actively hunted by local tribes for meat and water as well as by corporations and talismongers for use in telesma, foci, and reagents. Their movements soften and saturate the sands to such a degree that their trails are often riddled with quicksand. They are also a bit larger, six meters long on average, and thus make for some wide inadvertent traps.**
