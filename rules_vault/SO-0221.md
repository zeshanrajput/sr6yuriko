## Undoing the Damage

Sometimes the quickest way to deal with a turncoat is to turn them into a smoldering crater. Other times, the team will want to recover the relationship. The first step is always to figure out the team

was betrayed in the first place. Sometimes this is obvious because of all the evidence pointing back to the person—other times it will take some time for the team to determine who fragged them over. Once they’ve figured out who ratted them out, they’ll need to figure out how to handle it. This decision will probably be influenced by how that contact was turned. Maybe they were intimidated by a big, bad gang who kidnapped their sick aunt. Or they were conned by an embezzling CEO whose funds are currently frozen, and that CEO really needs their help in getting the funds transferred. The simplest answer might just be that someone offered them a pile of nuyen and that was good enough. It’s likely the team will decide how they respond based on what they find.

To get the contact back on the straight and narrow, they’ll need to eliminate the primary factor—recover the kidnapped family member, get the money back from the scam, or get that contact the drugs they need. It’s about removing the main thing that caused them to flip in the first place. Clearing that up gives the contact the freedom they need to make a rational decision, and the team is awarded Favor Points based on the impact. You’ve helped out your contact who now owes you big time, and they’re more likely to be loyal to you in the future.
