## How Gaps in Essence Happen

As I mentioned earlier, your aura is resilient to minor changes and can evolve over time. When your body is changed in ways that don’t match your inner Essence, it affects your aura as well. This is well established in cases of technological augmentation—everyone who knows anything about Essence knows your aura gets dimmer when you implant cyberware. The amount of dimming is also reasonably clear, and it appears to be consistent for implants. For example, two people each getting muscle replacement will (in the standardized opinion of experts in the field) each have their aura dimmed by the same amount. When too much of your aura is gone, there are other effects.

You can also lose Essence when your body undergoes changes other than augmentation. The damage done from significant trauma, from particularly damaging drugs, from long-term physical or emotional abuse, and from traumatic events such as torture or exposure to toxic magic can be significant and can sometimes manifest immediately, and other times take time to develop. To be fair, if you are in a situation where you are close enough to a toxic mage to have your aura affected, you may not be as worried about the insidious and damaging effect that their magic can have on you. If you have just escaped from a toxic and abusive relationship, you probably have bigger things on your mind than how shiny your aura is, but the effect is there, and it is noticeable to an experienced eye.

These gaps can be addressed in a number of ways. For physical changes, gene therapy or revitalization are definitely the way to go. For deeper, more emotional damage, aura therapy and traditional psychotherapy are both useful. These may not necessarily heal all of the damage, but they can help with some of it.

This is consistent with the previously described effect. These situations take you farther away from your true inner self; Essence is restored as you return to who you are.

The other thing to realize is that people can live their whole lives with Essence holes and never notice. They might live happy and fulfilling lives without caring that their aura has dimmed 7.3 percent. For most people, most of the time, a little Essence loss is perfectly normal. I have colleagues who think that, in fact, a person cannot learn and grow without damaging and then healing their Essence a little.
