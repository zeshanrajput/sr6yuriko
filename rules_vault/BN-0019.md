## Select Qualities and Design Your Shifter

During this step, you will set what type of animal you are as well as the impact of that animal on your nature—including any additional qualities, add-ons, and weaknesses. The fi rst action for this step is to select the Shifter positive quality. When you take this quality, you must choose one—and only one—of the four subcategories of shifter: furry, feathery, scaly, or fi n. The Shifter quality may only be taken once. Take note of the minimum Karma cost for your selection, as this is the starting cost for the quality as well as the minimum it can cost.

You gain all associated powers and weaknesses for your selected shifter subcategory. There are also optional positive and negative shifter qualities. These optional qualities let you customize your shifter to more closely match the animal form selected (see the **Realistic Animals** sidebar). The optional qualities adjust the cost of the Shifter quality: positive optional qualities increase the Karma cost and negative optional qualities decrease the Karma cost. The cost of the Shifter quality cannot be lower than the minimum listed cost. These optional qualities may only be taken at character creation, but the negative Shifter optional qualities can be bought off after play starts as normal. For more information, see the examples in the Designing Your Shifter sidebar.

Selecting the Shifter quality impacts your character’s Magic. If your character is not Awakened, you are considered the mundane version of a shifter and your Magic is automatically increased to 1 when the Shifter quality is selected. Unless you are Awakened, your Magic cannot be increased with Adjustment Points from Step Two.

All shifters gain the racial negative quality Allergy (Silver, Extreme) or Allergy (Gold, Extreme). This quality may not be bought off. Eagle shifters and Wareks (Leopard shifters from Africa) are known to have gold allergies, while most other shifters are allergic to silver.

Other positive and negative qualities affect both forms, as do adept powers, drugs, and spells. If there is some confusion or question whether something would work in animal form, the gamemaster has fi nal say.
