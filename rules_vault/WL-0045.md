## **Cetan Hawk**

**Like many of the newly discovered animals and creatures over the last seventy years, cetan hawks (** _**Buteo lakota**_ **) have deep roots in the traditions and stories of the local peoples. This animal has**

**deep roots in the history of Native American mythology. In the myths, the hawk is linked to grace and successful outcomes. It is also considered a master of both air and earth.**

**geese and swans. These creatures can be found in the grasslands.**

**The basic structure of the bird differs heavily from ostriches or emus. They have extremely stocky and strong legs, making them fast but not agile. It has wings, but these are too small to be used in fight and are used instead to help with balance. The demon duck stands nearly three meters tall and weighs eighty kilograms. Its massive beak weighs nearly fve kilograms by itself and has hardened edges it uses to crack open nuts and shells. The plumage of the demon duck is striped patterns of brown and black, while its head is covered in iridescent green. Its beak is typically yellow and orange in coloration.**

**The cetan hawk has an appearance similar to the red-tailed hawk. The head and tail of the cetan hawk are a brilliant red color, similar to the shade of a male cardinal. The underside of the hawk is mostly white and cream, while the center of the chest and bottom of the head is a dark brown, mimicking the outline of a smaller bird when viewed from below. The back of the wings and the back of the animal are shades of brown forming a speckled pattern.**

**The hawk is similar in size to the red-tailed hawk but has a slightly larger tail and smaller wings. It is about sixty centimeters in length with a wingspan of 110 centimeters. They weigh between one and one-and-a-half kilograms.**

**The demon duck is an herbivore, and the majority of its diet consists of tough, woody vegetables and hard seeds. Due to the size of the bird and the quality of its diet, it is grazing on food nearly all day. While the animal is normally aggressive against any large creature entering into its territory, this behavior is much stronger while nesting in the springtime near September, when it has eggs. After the chicks hatch, the mother remains to guard the chicks for the next six months before wandering off to let the chicks fend for itself.**

**The cetan hawk is inherently magical and uses this to aid in its hunt. The bird contains the ability to engulf its target in either air or earth, allowing it an easier hunt. It also uses its sharp senses to track prey up to three kilometers away. Its typical prey includes rodents, small birds, and other creatures that typically weigh under two kilograms.**

**Cetan hawks form stable bonded pairs for mating and continue to hunt together and raise chicks for many years. Breeding occurs in early March at the culmination of an elaborate sky dance between the pair. The females typically lay between two and three eggs, which take one month to incubate. Once the chicks hatch, it takes another month and a half before they are ready to take their frst fights. This is the cetan hawk’s most dangerous phase to metahumanity. If the pair feel that the nest is being threatened, they will engulf the target and dive bomb it with sharp talons.**

**The birds are typically solitary in nature, but many will exist over a set range. Probably the best way to describe the behavior is that they are social but do not really operate as a fock.**
