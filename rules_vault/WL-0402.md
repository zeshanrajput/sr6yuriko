## **Critter Special Training**

**Some gear and augmentations require a critter to receive special training. The maximum amount of special training a critter can learn is equal to their Intuition x 2. Special training does not count toward the maximum commands a critter can learn, and learning commands does not count toward a critter’s maximum amount of special training.**

**Providing a critter with special training requires at least a week of effort, during which time you must spend at least one hour per day with the critter, and a successful Training test. If a critter has already learned its maximum amount of special training, they may unlearn a previous special training as part of learning a new one—this requires no additional time or effort.**

**The following examples only cover some possibilities and are not intended to be a restrictive list.**

**Armor Training: The critter has been acclimated to wearing combat armor.**

**Augmentation Training: This allows the critter to take full advantage of an augmentation that requires special training. This generally does not include augmentations that provide passive physical bonuses, such as bone lacing or muscle replacement. Which augmentations require this training is covered in** _**Body Shop**_ **(p. 135–149).**

**AR Training: The critter can now recognize and understand AR messages, and they may be issued commands triggered by audio, visual, or simsense AR signals.**

**Power Training: Generally, a critter lacks the understanding to take advantage of capabilities granted by spells, complex forms, and critter powers (other than their own). If you order a spirit to provide your critter with the Movement power, for example, they are not likely to go as fast as they could, or they may just choose to stay still until the strange effect wears off. If you’d like your critter to become used to working with a power, spell, or other similar effect, you can train them for it. Each separate power or spell requires a separate special training. With this training, the critter becomes comfortable with the effect and can take full advantage without becoming confused or frightened by it.**

**Home Territory Training: A critter can be trained to treat a specifc area as their home territory. With this special training, a critter will not voluntarily leave their home territory unless specifcally commanded to do so. If they are forced to leave their home territory, they will return home as soon as they are capable of doing so. A critter can only have one home territory at a time—training them to treat another place as their home territory sets the new place as their home.**
