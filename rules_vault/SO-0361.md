## Specific Knowledge

If player characters are prepared with extremely relevant information that provides them with a substantial advantage, that may be grounds for gaining a point of Edge. Some examples of this might include knowing someone’s tell or nervous tic, knowing the perfect gift to bring, or dropping the right name at the right time.

In other cases, having a relevant Knowledge skill might be grounds for awarding Edge. This should generally be called upon in very specific cases, not just simply because a player character has a Seattle Bars skill when a meet is taking place in a bar (though if the meet is with the bar’s owner, it might actually apply). If a run involves current events or celebrities, a Current Events skill might grant a point of Edge. Whether or not a Knowledge skill should award Edge is ultimately at the GM’s discretion and may even relate to something the GM knows, but the player characters do not— such as having an Insect Spirits Knowledge skill when dealing with an insect spirit that is secretly masquerading as a metahuman, for example. For this reason (and others), it’s advantageous for a

GM to have a list of the player character’s Knowledge skills, contacts, and qualities at hand.
