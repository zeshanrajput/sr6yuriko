## The Messenger

Sometimes death isn’t really the objective of a killing, it’s just the clearest way to make a point. It might be as harmless as a puppy nailed to a garage door, a racehorse head in the sheets, or a family car bombed. Hell, sometimes they don’t even want you to make the kill. Blow up the car as they’re walking toward it. Poison them, but not quite to death, so the client can speak with them while their tongue is swollen with cantarella (oh yeah, you’ve got your own formula to replicate the Borgia classic). The fun ones are taking out the security around the target, _ping ping ping,_ oh, you’re all alone now, aren’t you? Think about why I didn’t kill you. Think about how I could. You aren’t safe unless you play ball. Yeah, you’re a killer, but you never kill the actual target. You kill their sense of security, the source of their resistance to obedience. To your thinking, death is a means of coercing action out of others. Your weapon is fear.

You were a natural choice for your sponsor. You already understood the art of fear. A banshee thrives on it, drinks its scent, builds a crescendo of sanity-tipping terror before taking what they need, and you already had a half-dozen mind-warping scenarios in your playbook before you got the ability to make marks piss themselves with a look. That might be what you like best: You don’t scream. They do.

Your tactics have only marginally changed. You love your ability to inspire supernatural horror, and you use it to herd your targets to where they can get the message. At the outer buffer of you, even where they can’t see you, they’ll subconsciously avoid your zone. You push them right where you want them. You’re the wolf and the shepherd at once. Life has never been so sweet, and it’s never going to end.
