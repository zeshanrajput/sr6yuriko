## Digitigrade Legs

Anthropomorphic cyberlegs that “bend backward” are popular variants on the standard cyberleg design. They are also known as satyr legs, raptor legs, or “Kid Stealth” legs. Contrary to first impressions, the knee does not actually bend backward, the ankle joint does. The limb stands on the ball of an extended foot and still possesses a hip and knee joint between thigh and shin that are both shortened to compensate for the rearrangement. If only one leg is replaced with a digitigrade leg, that leg functions as a standard but unconventional-looking cyberleg. If both legs are replaced with digitigrade legs, however, the user gains a bonus +1/+2/+1 meters to their movement and +4 dice for jumping tests. The user also becomes about 10 to 15 centimeters taller. Digitigrade legs function in all other ways as a full cyberleg (p. 288, _SR6_ ).

**Cyberware overdrive** : A digitigrade leg gains +2 Strength and +2 Agility for one test. These bonuses may exceed the augmented maximum. It requires 2 Minor Actions to use cyberware overdrive on both legs at once.
