## **Alchaeran Imperial Eagle**

**(Aquila heliaco arcanus)**

**Quartz didn’t want me to quit. My problem was that I only had food enough for a week, and that time was dwindling quickly. I decided to turn the tides. Rather than actively tracking, I started to wander and place random illusions around, mostly of prey species for the cat, but a few times I placed my own visage in a potentially harmful situation. At this point I caught sight of an illusion in the casting, did a quick track on the line of sight, and got my frst glimpse of Quartz.**

**While not directly at the top of my favorite things to study, alchera are most certainly in the top fve. Combine an alchera exploration with the discovery of the alchaeran imperial eagle, and it doesn’t matter what was up top last week, that’s my new favorite study.**

**We were studying an alchera in the Caucasus. It was a castle with multicultural and exocultural elements in the design. Local legends connected it to a rather unpleasant collection of werewolves, but not the shifter kind we are used to, more the horror-movie style. All of this was legendary conjecture that we kept at the back of our heads, and it also was why we had a rather bulky security detail. All those big goons didn’t do me any good when I slipped off to do some quiet digging and I came across a trio of alchaeran elves. They were an element of the local magic and very little threat to me, but my cockiness clouded my judgment. When a very real alchaeran eagle swooped down and pinned two of those elves by their helmeted heads to the hand-carved stone, I was face-to-beak with death. I was saved by the third elf taking a swing and then taking the beak over his face.**

**This famous Awakened mountain lion was crimson from head to toe, with the only exception being its piercing emerald-green eyes. It was nearly identical to the cat from the show, a sleek-bodied, small-framed mountain lion. It had been using a variation on the levitation spell to make itself light enough to be supported by its secondary tail and to use the thin upper branches of the trees to avoid being spotted. I only really managed it because I looked up in frustration at one point.**

**As soon as I spotted them, Quartz slowly levitated down from the tree to a spot in front of me. Through a very basic form of telepathy and an empathetic link, Quartz reached out and communicated with me. I was able to understand their inherent spellcasting abilities, which begin with illusions. Some, though, can perceive and learn spells cast by others.**

**I did the sane thing and ran to fnd a spot I could safely document the events from. I started with the look. Similar to the eastern imperial eagle, the alchaeran version sports two key variations. The wingtips on the alchaeran are black and trail a variable stream of wispy blackness depending on the detection of an alchera. Within an alchera, the wings seem to almost fow like a stream. The second feature unique to the alchaeran eagle are the eyes. When not in or near an alchera, they appear similar to a mundane eagle, but upon approach, the eyes slowly glow red and fnally erupt in fames as the creature crosses the arcane boundaries.**

**Before I left my studies, a month after a brief trip back to civilization (with an invisible Quartz), I had a fuller understanding and actual connection to Quartz that fueled my own magic in a similar fashion to my power focus.**

**But yes, I, like Sukie Redfower herself, have published nothing offcial on our fndings on this ability because of a fear of corporate exploitation of this ability. I’m putting it here now, because I have already seen in-house research by both MCT and Evo on the topic, so telling the shadow community seems only fair.**

**Subsequent research has demonstrated the creature gains sustenance primarily from the entire menu of alchera inhabitants and prefers to locate alchera and alcheral thins in order to hunt. This is one of the values it holds for arcanocorps, as it can be tracked to locate potential alchera for exploration and exploitation.**
