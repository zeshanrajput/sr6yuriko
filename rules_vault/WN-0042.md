## Terminal

- **Council Member:** Sebastian Krebs

- **Security Rating:** B to Z

- **Typical Host Rating:** 2/2/1

- **Primary Corporate Funding:** Zeta-Imperial Chemicals, Lucent Bioware, Matrix Assets Inc.

Zeta-Imperial Chemicals bought their way onto the MDC with a simple promise to clean up Terminal. For years this section of the city floundered as a walled-off DMZ that doubled as one of the handful of ways to get into the city. Terminal houses Penn Station, which serves as a bus terminal and rail hub for maglev train lines moving up and down the coast from Boston to as far as DeeCee. It also has Terminal Station, which brings in traffic from New Jersey, and serves as a local stop for the city’s elevated rail line.

The long-awaited rebuild is in progress, and the area immediately surrounding the station is already gentrified to the point where it is on par with New Harlem or any of the Villages of Lower Manhattan in terms of police presence. Walk a few blocks farther down and you’ll start seeing graffiti on the walls in real life and in AR. Walk any farther and you’ll find yourself in the middle of an open war between the local gangs, along with a mix of NYPD and Winter Systems security forces contracted by Zeta-Imperial Chemicals to reclaim the now-extraterritorial property the corporation purchased as part of their move to the district.

The NYPD won this policing contract back from Winter Systems when they took over the citywide contract; however, per arrangement with Zeta-Imperial Chemicals, the local precinct is only responsible for conducting patrols and dealing with standard crimes. The language of the agreement is important, because it holds Zeta-Imperial Chemicals responsible for “specified urban renewal projects,” which translates into Winter Systems being paid to take the zone back block by block.

- Winter Systems is literally getting away with murder here by claiming they are working to remove insurgents from sovereign territory. The fact is that only part of the work they’re doing is taking place on land covered by the Shiawase Decision. Most of the gangs being slaughtered are on UCAS soil, and local newshounds are sniffing all over this looking for any evidence of corporate wrongdoing or malfeasance.

**>** Sunshine

You can practically see the progress being made in Terminal from space. Look for the dark spot on the West Side of Manhattan locked in place by walls that extend to the water at 29[th] and 42[nd] , and the main wall running down Seventh Avenue from 42[nd] to 29[th] . Inside the zone, Zeta-Imperial Chemicals’ influence can clearly be seen by the lights of the shiny new complex near the center of the district. A mix of new and old buildings radiating outward from the new arcology has basic services, but everything near the walls and outside the so-called safe zone has been entirely cut off. The checkpoints are still up along Seventh Avenue at 34[th] and 42[nd] , though most of the time the cops barely look in your direction to see if you are the kind of person who ought to be leaving Terminal or the type who they need to pat down. It will stay that way until Zeta-Imperial Chemicals proves it can get security under control.

- Terminal was—is—how the poor people get into the city. Police response here is draconian. They know the citizens using Terminal aren’t wealthy enough to fight for their rights, nor do they matter enough to the corps they work for to be protected. Zeta-ImpChem’s operation here is essentially a money grab. If they can move the security dial just a little more it will be enough to attract lower-class workers fleeing Battery City, which means Zeta-ImpChem should eventually turn a profit.
