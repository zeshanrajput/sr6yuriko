## Shiawase Flamethrowers

Blaze decides to target them all with a sweeping attack without the wild die. Spray attacks are resolved similarly to a Full Auto attack, so Blaze makes a single Exotic Weapons + Agility roll and each targeted defender rolls their defense individually (unless the GM is using a grunt group and makes it one roll). Each defender can also use their own Edge against the attack. Comparing AR to each defender’s DR grants Blaze some easy Edge, but he can only keep two of the five Edge gained from the defenders. Blaze rolls 4 hits on the attack roll, while the GM rolls for each defender, getting 0, 1, 3, 4, and 6 hits. That means Blaze hits the first four for 8DV, 7DV, 5DV, 4DV, and misses the last one. The four biters that were hit with the flamethrower spray attack also have the Burning 5 status and will have to deal with that on their next turn, while the last biter is unharmed and not on fire.

Shiawase has expanded their flamethrower line to include several new options. Compared to the Blazer, the Incinerator increases the volume of fuel per area, but shortens the range. Other new options are the Fire Bug, a one-shot pistol flamethrower, and the Fire Starter, a small two-shot flamethrower designed from the ground up to be an under-barrel accessory on assault-style rifles. Both are smaller and less cumbersome than mounting the larger Blazer.

The Incinerator also introduces an option for a fuel tank carrier backpack that can also be used on the Blazer, but not the Fire Bug or Fire Starter. As a cost-savings measure, the Incinerator, Fire Bug, and Fire Starter all lack the change-on-the-fly ammo system of the Blazer.

On straight attacks, the first valid target on the line is the only one hit—no lining up two targets and hitting both as that still requires a wobbly or sweeping attack. Spray attacks also arch across a distance, so it’s the GMs call if it can go over an obstacle or if there is enough overhead clearance to make a shot. It’s also up to the GM to decide if the spray would hit anyone moving across the path of the spray. With the arching path it may be that only someone close to the shooter or the target may be at the right elevation to be hit, or maybe it’s just that the timing isn’t exact, and they pass before or after the actual attack.

**Game Rules:** Swapping out a fuel tank on these three weapons follows the standard rules for changing tanks and using backpack carriers. All versions of Shiawase’s flamethrowers use the Spray Attacks rules (p. 117, _SR6_ ) and deliver fire damage (p. 110, _SR6_ ). The damage and duration of the Burning status depends on several factors, but the Incinerator should start with a Burning 5 status, while the Fire Bug and the Fire Starter start at Burning 3.
