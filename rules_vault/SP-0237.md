## **Shadow Puppets**

**Before and after the war, both sides used proxies and deniable assets. Aztlan placed spies and other operatives in Amazonia, waiting for opportunities that could destabilize the country. Likewise, Amazonia conducts operations to identify, manipulate, and suborn these agents. But Amazonia carries out far fewer operations against Aztlan than it did before. They cannot afford to give Aztlan another justifcation to openly go on the offensive. This puts much of the initiative in Aztlan’s hands, and Amazonia is left on the defensive.**

**With the arrival of the Disians, Aztlan has become bolder and more aggressive. It seems to many in Amazonia that the time for fghting a shadow war is coming to an end—one way or another.**

**When it comes to shadowruns against Amazonia, extractions of politically powerful Awakened beings or agents of DISA (Department of Intelligence and Security of Amazonia, the nation’s secret police) are on the rise. This serves a double purpose of destabilizing Amazonia and providing the Disians with more fuel for their machines. The information extracted from the unlucky targets of these extractions is used alongside reports from agents in Amazonia to plan subsequent operations. The practice of locating and extracting vulnerable targets is nothing new, but the scale of it has ramped up to over fve times the previous level—a number that is likely a conservative estimate but is diffcult to confrm due to the nature of the business.**

- **Clockwork**

**Hualpa may have gone into a cloistered sequestration, but there are many other dragons who call Amazonia home. Aztechnology has convinced their Disian allies that the dragons who lead the Awakened nation of Amazonia are among the greatest threats to their plans. And to be fair, that’s probably true. Amazonia is not going to take the Disian invasion and harvesting of mana lightly. And so third-party operatives are being offered considerable sums of money and material support to take on dragon assassination contracts. So far, none of these have been successful, but the attempts have been violent and disruptive, causing both casualties and damage to the environment.**

**One of the main targets of these assassination contracts is Pedrinho, a dragon who operates out of Metrópole . Pedrinho is often entangled in local politics and is known for fnding the middle ground where those with anti-dragon sentiments and those with anti-metahuman sentiments can coexist. In the shadows, he’s known to be a frequent employer of shadowrunners—usually in his efforts to ensure stability among the disparate factions in Metrópole. Lately, he’s been hiring runners for more proactive jobs, working to identify and hunt down agents of Aztlan to learn what he can about their operations. Due to a nearly successful assassination attempt, he’s become wilier and more secretive of late, moving from place to place and rarely appearing in person among those he holds little trust for. Some say he’s becoming more paranoid by the day, and that he’s likely to begin suspecting treachery from the runners he works with, but so far these seem to just be rumors. I’ve uncovered no accounts of misplaced betrayals, and believe me, I’ve been watching for them.**



**São Paulo is perhaps the most metropolitan district of Metrópole, where there is the greatest concentration of megacorporate assets and arcologies. This makes it a vital district for Amazonia’s economy but also a point of vulnerability to operations from Aztlan. The alliances between Disians and the megacorps, most notably Ares and MCT, provide Aztechnology with greater opportunities. This is somewhat tempered by megacorp rivalries, but the Disians are apt at political manipulation and are likely to take advantage of the situation. Ares and MCT can act more freely within São Paulo than any Aztlan agents could. Though they are watched, they are not watched as closely as potential Aztlaner spies. This results in plenty of job opportunities for runners willing to work Ares or MCT targeting Amazonia’s interests. The Disians hope to destabilize Amazonia and cause infghting to prevent the country from opposing them. Many of the runs sponsored by Ares and MCT are designed to frame various parties, plant and spread misinformation, highlight inequity and injustice to foment public unrest, and uncover government corruption. These tasks are much less direct than the jobs offered by Aztechnology, and many of them even seem noble in comparison—particularly the ones that are meant to open peoples’ eyes to injustice and corruption. But at the root of these jobs are the Disians, and their ultimate goal is to drain our world of all life. A healthy and powerful Amazonia stands in their way, and they’ll stop at nothing to undermine that opposition. If you run the shadows in Amazonia, take extra care to dig deeper and understand what you’re signing on for. The last thing we need is more of you helping those things.**
