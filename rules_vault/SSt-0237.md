## **Keep Separate Business Separate and Respect Confidentiality**

**Sometimes, you might get to work with your housemate, and that’s all fne and good.**

- **Be sure, though, that your relationship can support exposure at both home and work. Some thrive in those conditions. Others don’t.**

- **Jimmy No**

**It doesn’t always work out that way, though. Take Netcat and me—there aren’t a whole lot of teams with two hackers, so we don’t get on the same jobs much.**



**Conventional couples often go out, do their work, come home, and talk about their day. We have some variants in that pattern. First, our hours are weird, and we may be gone for days or even weeks at a time. Second, when we talk about our day, we have to be a little guarded. We can talk about some of the interesting events, but anything that might identify the Mr. Johnson, client, or target has to be carefully scrubbed from our conversation. You can talk about spousal confdentiality and who can or can’t be forced to testify against each other, but there are concerns that go beyond legal niceties. We’re professionals, and we know that the best way to keep aspects of the job confdential are to not talk about them at all. It’s protection for us and protection for the other person—what they don’t know can’t be extracted from them.**
