## Contacts and Loyalty

While we talked about the groups of people you’re likely going to milk for information or small favors, you can’t depend on these like the ones you’ve worked with before and who really know you. Plus, the individuals on a job aren’t really going to go out on a limb for you like someone who owes you a favor.

Now, this won’t necessarily help you out right away, but a big social network helps when you don’t know what you’re going to do. Those giant webs of contacts are hard to manage and maintain, though—after all, trust must be fostered over time and repeatedly reinforced. That’s going to be the balancing act you must maintain while working. There are going to be times when you need to leave the city and all your old contacts won’t see you around—and remember: out of sight, out of mind.

So you need to balance those wide-versus-deep connections, and then make sure you have the right ones for any situation. And it’s not all about you, either. You’re part of a team for a reason. You might want to have deep connections to local law enforcement, while your street samurai has good smugglers and black-market operators the team can rely on.

The other aspect is trusting your contacts. You’ve got to have trust at some point or you’re going to fail a lot of jobs. You’re still going to get screwed over at some point, and it’s gonna hurt. Do your due diligence and cover your hoop, but don’t operate alone.

- I really haven’t seen many people get up in the morning and say, “I’m going to screw over some shadowrunners today.” Most of the time something bad has happened and the person is just trying to survive, same as you. Yeah, they sold you out, but if you survive, they owe you more than they can ever truly pay off. Also think about that when you’re selling out one of your contacts.

- Old Crow

- Man, when someone’s broken my trust, am I just supposed to take it? No. Frag that. You burn me and I’ll bring the heat back on you.

- Pistons

- I’m with Pistons on this one. Betray me and I’ll fry your brain in the Matrix the next chance I get.

- Sim-Eon

The primary reason for fostering all these relationships is to create plans for your runs.

Maybe they have the right paydata to get your way in. Or maybe they know who’s catering the mob boss’s daughter’s wedding. Those details matter and you’re not going to have all that information yourself. The second is that they could be the one that pulls your hoop out of the fire when things get hot, such as when you need a bolt hole to lay low for a while until the heat dies down.

The point is you can get a couple of these squared away ahead of time; you don’t have to improvise so much when the drek hits the fan. And it will hit the fan. Plus, your contacts are gonna be much happier if they aren’t left scrambling to figure out plan B on your exfil—it’s much better than having them say no while some goons break down the door you’re hiding behind.
