## Travel

Most travel in this plane is vertical. Great bathyspheres are built with modulating density layers, allowing them to sink like a stone or float into the air. These bathyspheres have independent inner and outer hulls so they can roll through cities like a marble. As most of the upper structures are made of ice or a form of aerogel, friction is minimal, giving the bathysphere efficient speed and momentum. Bathyspheres are the most common form of vehicle found at the cardinal poles, as they have the most flexible forms of travel.
