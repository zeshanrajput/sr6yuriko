## **Make the Critters a Better Offer**

**This can be an even longer-term operation, but if you can pull it off, the payoff can be enormous. The vast majority of security critters are not sentient (if they were, they’d have to be given pay and breaks and occasional time off and other things corporations hate), which often means the critters are not especially loyal. The trainers might think they have a special bond with their critters, but the critters are mostly looking for whoever will feed them, and that allegiance can shift quickly (don’t @ me, pet owners).**

**So if you can fnd a way to wherever the critters spend their downtime (if they have any), you might be able to give them a few snacks and interactions over time that lead them to like you. This doesn’t have to be a complete deprogramming—you don’t need them to turn on their trainers. You just want them to hesitate enough to let you pass, or at least abuse the trust you earned by taking them out quickly.**

- **You really could go for the long haul and see if you can get the critters to turn on their masters, but that takes some serious animal handling skills and a lot of time.**

- **Mika**

- **It can be fun, but the payoff is not worth the increased effort—at least, not to me.**

- **> G-Nome**

- **If they’re using cybered critters, sometimes you can hack them. Usually, most of their critical systems are isolated from wireless intrusion, but if you’re creative there’s still a lot you can work with—especially if you have plenty of time to prep.**
