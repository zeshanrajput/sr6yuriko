## Unintended Consequences

Modifying a critter’s genetics sometimes produces unintended—and unwanted—results. In some cases, the critter dies as an embryo, though some may survive only to develop an aggressive form of cancer early in life. For those that survive the genetic tinkering process but are not left unscathed, allergies and other weaknesses are not uncommon. If an imperfect specimen is detected early on, a megacorp will usually scrap it, but quite often these weaknesses go unnoticed until well after the critter has finished growing and begins its service.

Once a critter reaches adulthood, roll on the Genetic Mishaps table. For mundane critters, roll 1D6. For Awakened and emerged critters, roll 2D6. For critters that receive gene therapy treatments instead of being modified at the embryonic stage, add another 1D6 to this roll. Add +1 to the dice roll for each mundane genetic augmentation, and +2 for each Awakened genetic augmentation. Additionally, add +1 to the dice roll for every glitch rolled during the embryonic gestation period.
