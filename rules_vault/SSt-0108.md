## **> Hold your feathers, black bird.**

**So they took Jason in and cared for him. They always left him under supervision at their safehouse. They were very cautious with their weapons and even did some baby proofing of the place—to the extent of such tasks in a squat in the barrens. From what I’ve gathered, Jason was as happy as a kite with them.**

**Until an S-K assault team tracked the runners down after a job and stormed their safehouse. They escaped, but Jason and his nanny got caught in the crossfire.**

**Another lesson here to be learned: This was a wrong call. We are not living in trid-movies. Taking a child in, in our line of work, is not a good idea.**
