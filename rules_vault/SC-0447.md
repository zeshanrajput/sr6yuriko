## sniper

## playing advice

Snipers are a feared and respected archetype in the Sixth World. They are the unseen threat that dispenses death without warning. When operating inside their niche, they have as much lethality as any other archetype, if not more. Unlike the other, more well-rounded combat archetypes such as the street samurai, they are less comfortable being subjected to hostile firepower or reacting to unpleasant surprises. If a sniper is a killing machine, they are a finely tuned, high-performance one intolerant of getting grit in the gears—as opposed to being a rugged and unbreakable wrecking ball.

The sniper’s capabilities are built almost entirely on the two pillars of firepower and observation, and both of these capabilities will be valuable to almost any shadowrunning team. This mix makes snipers resemble a hybrid archetype combining the features of covert-ops specialists and weapon specialists. Snipers make natural partners with both archetypes, cooperating directly where their capabilities overlap and propping the other up where they diverge. They lend more firepower to combat but bring fewer social skills to the table than the former archetype, and less variety in backup artillery but more capability in sneaking and reconnoitering than the latter archetype.

For all the power held by an unseen, untouchable sharpshooter, their most dangerous weapon is their mind. Snipers identify exploitable gaps or lapses in security. Teams fortunate enough to have a sniper benefit from a tactician who minimizes the chance for unpleasant surprises during the mission. They’re the team’s guardian angel, keeping them safe with direct fire support as well as misdirecting opposition and reinforcements by strategically destroying materiel. And of course, few measures keep opponents’ heads down and unable to join the fight better than the knowledge that a sniper is out there, pointing a rifle at them.

Conventional snipers almost universally make use of magic or technological augmentations. Lethal and sleek, adepts make superb snipers. Most cybernetic augmentations can be replicated by various adept powers, but certain options are available to adepts that are difficult for technology to match. Smartgun technology can only enhance a rifle’s accuracy so much, and there are few ways to ascend into a sniper’s perch better than employing wall running. On the other hand, technological augmentations can offer mundane snipers unique advantages as well. Powerful cybereyes can reduce or even remove the need for a bulky optical scope on an already hard-to-conceal rifle. Where most combat archetypes embrace the same few usual augmentations such as wired reflexes and muscle replacement (or their bioware alternatives), snipers often have less need of them due to their tactics. Indeed, jumpy and twitchy muscles are an absolute hindrance to long-range marksmanship. Many snipers find more use in cyberware commonly employed by covert operators to enhance their ability to remain undetected. Other augmentations that permit them to remain still without cramping, alert and awake without being jittery, and free from biological needs to eat or excrete for extended periods of time are invaluable to snipers performing long stretches of observation while in camouflage.

Some runners of different archetypes might be categorized as snipers when using their tactics, even if not their signature rifle. Drone riggers in particular can duplicate the function of a sniper particularly well. Combat magicians are also quite capable rifle-less snipers, as spells and spirits are just as devastating over rifle-like ranges (if not longer, when weather conditions cooperate).
