## **Unseelie Court Basics**

**The most important thing to understand about the Unseelie Court is that it is exactly as old as the Seelie Court, for it exists almost entirely to oppose that body. Since the moment the Seelie Court came into existence, there was someone who thought the Court was a bad idea, and thus the Unseelie Court was born.**

**The second most important thing about the Unseelie Court is that it opposes the Seelie Court in all ways possible. This is not just a matter of disagreeing on some policies. This is complete opposition to the Seelie Court’s methods, to the point that the Unseelie Court doggedly refuses to take on any of the trappings of the Seelie Court. So while the Seelie Court is rigidly hierarchical, the Unseelie Court has no titles or ranks. The Seelie Court has strict protocols on who is allowed to do what and when. The Unseelie Court has none of that. The Seelie Court maintains rolls listing who is tied to which faction and regularly considers who can be added and who must be expelled. The Unseelie Court keeps no such records and has zero sense of who the members of its Court are. This is good for security—no one can break into your secret membership listing if it doesn’t exist—but also is a way of life. Having membership means having offcial organizational standards, and the Unseelie Court is opposed to such things.**

**To reiterate: There are no titles within the Unseelie Court. No ranks, no leaders, no members, no offcial proceedings. If you ever hear anyone call something a “meeting of the Unseelie Court,” they’re wrong or lying.**

- **That might be a little extreme. Any discussion of how to undermine the Seelie Court could be construed as a meeting of the Unseelie Court, and if people describe it as such, I won’t hassle them too much about it.**

**That, then, is a considerable list of what the Unseelie Court does not have or do. So what does it actually do?**

**Again: It opposes the Seelie Court.**

**If you have ever taken action against the Seelie Court, you have, at least briefy, become part of the Unseelie Court. If you coordinated your efforts with someone else, then you all conducted an Unseelie Court operation. And if you ever talked about problems you found with the Seelie Court, then you participated in Unseelie Court gossip.**

**This is why it’s utterly hilarious when Seelie Court operatives talk about “breaking the Unseel-**

**ie Court” or “dissolving the Unseelie Court operation.” That’s like trying to stomp out an ant colony with a toothpick, only the ants are more aware of each other than the Unseelie Court operatives are.**

**This is not to say that there are not some serious Unseelie Court operatives. There absolutely are some longtime Seelie Court fxtures who secretly are dedicated to undermining that Court, and they have engaged in initiative after initiative to accomplish that purpose. The Seelie Court would dearly love to fnd out who these people are, and if their identities were discovered, they would get highly visible show trials as Unseelie Court “leaders” and be publicly executed. But that still doesn’t mean the Unseelie Court has a structure or leaders.**

**> By Elijah’s definition, someone could be considered a member of the Unseelie Court while undermining the Seelie Court and then back in the Seelie Court if they resume normal duties for a time—when in truth they’d only be resuming those duties to prepare a good cover for future undermining. So in those cases, fulfilling Seelie Court duties actually become Unseelie activities. It just gets murkier, is what I’m saying.**
