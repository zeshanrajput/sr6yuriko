## Hounfour

Voodoo practitioners use a lodge known as a hounfour, oum’phor, or casa de santo. The name for the lodge varies based on the region of the practitioner. The center of the hounfour contains a central post called a poto mitan, or poteau-mitain. This post represents a cosmic axis or crossroad and is used to contact lwa. This post acts as the altar and is often decorated with specifi c patterns (veve) related to the lwa. The veve are traditionally fl our or cornmeal with increasing complexity based on the Force of the spirit. Located around the poto mitan are the ritual offerings, additional drawings, and other elements meant to please the spirits.

Wearers of the Ade Aabo gain a point of Edge when resisting possession, which must be used on the test, or it is lost. Any time the wearer of the cap takes damage, they must make an Edge (2) test to see if the headgear avoided being damaged. If the headgear is damaged, then it no longer provides any Edge for Possession tests.
