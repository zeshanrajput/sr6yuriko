## **The Cost of Due Process**

**Here’s where things can break down. See, attorneys charge based on how diffcult their client is to work with. For individuals in your line of work, these fees can be steep, due to all the trouble your job can create. But, for baseline purposes, we can work with some of these numbers.**

**For basic criminal defense, prices start on the low end around 3,500¥. This is for infractions**



**like lacking a proper license for job equipment, possession of small amounts of prohibited or illegal items, or simple assault for a bar fght or someone getting a little too mouthy with you at the club.**

**Defense of more serious offenses goes up from there, but for basic defense, expect to pay about 20,000¥ for the higher end stuff like major felonies or crimes that would likely put you away for a long time.**

**Understand, this is just for the basic defense. This will afford a couple of sessions with you, get you through the initial pretrial steps and into a discussion with the district attorney or corporate equivalent. I’ll be there during any and all interrogation attempts, and any questions they ask you outside of these offcial sessions cannot be related to your pending case. This cost estimate does not include any trial preparation or actual court appearances. If at all possible, avoiding a case going to trial is best for everyone involved, because it’s so much easier on everyone.**

**If all goes well, we’ll be able to get them on some technicality and get the whole case dismissed. If not, then hopefully we can get you out with time served (meaning the time you’ve already spent in jail awaiting their decisions counts toward your total sentence) and maybe a fne. If nothing else, you may have to do a little bit of time at a country club of a jail. Of course, this all assumes that your alleged crime was not heinous enough to warrant much more severe penalties, such as no bond.**

**Sometimes the cops and prosecutors think they have enough evidence to convict you on the spot, and they tend to throw their weight around in order to intimidate you into accepting their so-called bargain. In this case, it’s usually a bullish offer with an exorbitant prison sentence, a ridiculous fne, and a thinly veiled threat that the situation can get far worse if you do not cooperate. Don’t accept these without frst consulting your attorney! These tend to be quick and dirty and are meant to intimidate you into accepting terms right away. Remember, all they care about is getting an arrest and a conviction, not getting the actual culprit.**
