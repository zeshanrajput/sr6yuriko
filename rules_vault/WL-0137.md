## **Mycofundi**

**Also known as “gnomes” or “shroomies,” these fungal-based lifeforms appear as both capped and uncapped hominids between ffteen and ninety centimeters tall, often seen in earthy browns, greens, and yellows. Occasional specimens have bright red or orange bulbous caps, with anywhere from eight to twelve eyespots crowning their upper portions. They are a mutation of mycoprotein fungi after placed in contact with decaying oak, teak, and cherry trees in medium or high mana fow sites. They are usually encountered in groups from three to ffty. They display animal-level intelligence, capable of pack and herd behavior.**

- **To be clear, their mutation has not been repeated with any consistency. Some arcanofauna specialists report that they have been encountered in other metaplanes.**

- **Lyran**

**While not inherently hostile, they respond to threats by mutating spontaneously to survive.**

- **The fastest recorded mutation is under two seconds.**

- **Rikki**

**Known mutations include short limbs capable of throwing small items and sprouting as many as three pairs at once (brawlers), exploding spore caps emitting repulsive clouds of pungent odors (yellow caps), exploding blisters that ooze highly corrosive agents (smelts), piercing whistle-like screams that can both deafen and invoke nausea in most creatures with ears (choirs), and lastly transforming into lengthy sharp spines that are painful to step onto or if struck with when thrown (maltrops).**

- **This ability to toss others is real. I accidentally trekked into a good-sized colony while in the Broceliande, and some of those brawlers began tossing their maltrop pals like spiny shuriken. Even hurled a yellow cap into the backpack of my buddy Hank. Not fun at all.**

- **Stone**

**Mycofundi have been found globally, with confrmed sightings in the Americas, Central and Southern Europe, Central and Southern Africa, and most of Asia. First encountered in the forests of Tír Tairngire and the Native American Nations, myconids thrive in temperate and boreal forests, often found with lichen and moss in shaded spots, and they are noted for their ability to proliferate quickly among decaying biomatter, leaving behind healthier soils and setting up symbiotic relationships in the roots of many species of trees, tubers, and grasses. They become ambulatory when threatened or when a commune needs to spread out.**

**Exposure to the spores of mycofundi can develop into** _**candidemia**_ **, a blood infection for which there are a wide array of treatments, much as any other pathogen. Respirators are not fully effective, though blood fltration technologies are.**

- **If you’ve ever had a case of thrush, you’ve had a common form of candidemia. The illnesses can take on a variety of forms, and if left untreated can do immense damage to internal organs, including the artificial ones many of us have. Some people know of “hospital cough” or even “convention crud”—those are other common forms of candidemia.**

**> Butch**

**In a relaxed state, mycofundi usually stand between ten and thirty centimeters tall, with caps of equal diameter. When agitated or afraid, they begin to mutate. The known mutations are brawlers (found in groups of one to three), which receive additional pairs of limbs similar to Shiva Arms (p. 126,** _**Sixth World Companion**_ **); choirs (found in groups of six or more), which begin uttering a painful whistling scream that induces the Nauseated status (p. 52,** _**SR6**_ **, effective up to fve meters per member of the choir); maltrops that have an effect similar to the thorns dermal alteration (p. 121,** _**Sixth World Companion**_ **); smelts, which combine the qualities of Corrosive Spit and Defensive Secretion (p. 118,** _**Sixth World Companion**_ **); and yellow caps, which as their name suggests, change their caps to a brilliant yellow color developing Bioluminescence (p. 128,** _**Sixth World Companion**_ **). If their caps are broken or crushed in any manner, they emit Spores (p. 190) exposing all surfaces and individuals within three meters to their effects, and that of Nausea Gas (p. 122,** _**SR6**_ **) for one to six minutes. Additionally, exposure requires a resistance test repeated every twelve hours or develop** _**candidemia**_ **, a respiratory/blood disease. Respiratory and blood fltration augmentations or gear bonuses are half rating.**
