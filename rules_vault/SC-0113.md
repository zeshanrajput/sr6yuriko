## Old, Darke Things

This adventure works best with the Acolyte or Red Dervish background, but it should work well with any team that includes an Awakened character or has an interest in metaphysical threats, especially if they have access to Sacrificial metamagic.

Only a handful of people in the world know about the existential threat represented by the early blood mages of Aztlan or the deals they made that nearly ushered in a trans-dimensional apocalypse. Their employer—who can be a collector of arcane rarities, an AZT researcher who doesn’t know better, or even a magical ecologist investigating strange occurrences in the manasphere—certainly doesn’t know about the Bridge or the things that hunger beyond it. What they have heard of are certain points of strange mystical conjunction in Aztlan, aberrant foveae at ley line conjunctions where the mana should be at its most rich. More than that, unknown magical phenomena may have taken place, from radical reagents to spontaneous orichalcum formulation. They’d like an escort to the zone to investigate for themselves. As seeking Aztlan approval would strip them of any personal findings or credit, this has to be executed as stealthily as possible. If the team includes a rigger, this is a great chance to put their smuggling skills to the test and earn the team a bonus. Otherwise, the client arranges transport quietly into the badlands of Aztlan, far from the reach of the sprawls.

Arrival finds the team experiencing a strange attunement to blood magic. Not only has magic returned, but it feels extraordinarily potent in sacrificial alignment. Assensing, either the crew’s or the client’s, can reveal the point it seems to be emanating from, necessitating a dig. This can take as long as you feel is appropriate, from a day to a week, with the odd badland patrol posing a moment of tension (unless the group provokes an attack for some reason). The excavation opens into a massive cavern, many of the rock formations within holding small clues of ancient stonework in unrecognizable linguistic formats (unless one of the group is highly versed in so-called “Atlantean,” and even then it’s too piecemeal and faded to make out any definitive translations). Whether it’s at the center of the cavern or at the end of a dungeon crawl of archeological impossibilities and non-Euclidean geometry, a vast edifice awaits at its center. This artifact, a great egg at least ten meters tall and composed of an unknown type of stone, defies all attempts to gather a sample. Attempting to get close intensifies the depth of sacrificial attunement, and anyone with a mentor spirit that advocates for sacrifice feels as though the desire to spill blood is growing, yet the voice of their mentor becomes ever more silent.

At this point, the group is no longer alone. The team is not the only one looking for answers here. A cult seeking to investigate the site, an Aztechnology Jaguar Guard containment squad, a rival runner crew, a combination of the previous three, or any other appropriate challenge to the team enters the cavern with strict intentions of eliminating all competitors. At this point the goal is to escape with the client intact, by stealth or force. Should they succeed, the client is determined to go underground for a while, paying the fee and asking to stay in touch, gaining the group a contact with Connection 2 and Loyalty 4. Of course, the client may be on the watchlist of the competition who showed up, which can bring about all kinds of complications in the future. And of course, any runner who practices Sacrifice metamagic may have questions of their own, whether a quest for personal power or a crisis of faith.
