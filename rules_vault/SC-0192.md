## Exiled Hacker

You entered adulthood in good shape, putting hacking skills to use for a good wage. Maybe you worked in Matrix security for a corp, a government, a non-governmental organization, or even an organized crime outfit. Maybe you sculpted hosts, or maybe you did illicit hacking on your employer’s dime. Whatever the case, you built your hacking skills and made good money.

Those helped, but the circumstances you were dumped into were rough, full of people who only used bare-bones electronics and didn’t care what you could hack. You still used your hacking skills to survive—getting a free night in a coffin motel here, a little quick-and-dirty hacking run there— but you got into plenty of situations where your cyberdeck was not a threat, and you had to survive. You selected a way to fight and got better at it out of necessity. You knew you wouldn’t be the toughest person around, but you could be tough enough to get people to leave you alone.

Now you have two skillsets—the question is, can you figure out how to use them together?
