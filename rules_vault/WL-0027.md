## **Reagent Hunting**

**A subset of the natural encounters are those you deliberately seek out because there is something you want from the critter. This is a different type of operation. For starters, you’re not going to be able to just avoid the critter. If they just left the part of them that works as a reagent lying around, no one would be paying you thousands of nuyen to retrieve it.**

**Secondly, you need to know what you’re trying to get and how to get it. If you’re hunting a sandworm, you want a tooth. If you’re going after a rock lizard, you want blood and maybe some critical organs. If you’re chasing an aerial ostrich, you want feathers.**

- **Feathers? You mean, the things birds drop all the time? Didn’t you just say that you won’t find reagents simply lying around?**

- **> Traveler Jones**

- **This is part of the knowledge you need to collect. Once they’re detached from the bird, aerial ostriches don’t much care what happens to them—unless they observe you gathering them. Trust me—waiting for them to wander off before you start collecting is going to make things much easier.**

- **Winterhawk**

- **Generally speaking, the more magical a critter is, the more reagents can be harvested from them. Something like a century ferret is going to have little to harvest, perhaps a tail or some fur. If you take down a dragon, literally every piece is going to be good for something.**
