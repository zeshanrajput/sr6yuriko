## Game Rules for an Astral Quest

The first step in the astral quest is to decide on the goal of the quest. Goals can be gaining information like a magical formula or a spirit’s true name, learning a new metamagic, or uncovering some hidden truth. The quest may also be to gain a passageway to the deeper metaplanes or accomplish a task such as retrieving a disrupted spirit or permanently destroying a spirit on its home metaplane. Fixing the goal of the quest in the mind of the traveling mage requires a period of meditation represented by a Magic + tradition attribute + Initiate Grade (18, 10 minutes) Extended test. Any helpers being guided to the metaplanes by the questing mage do not have to undergo this preparation.

After this preparation, the initiate needs to astrally project and move away from the physical realm, leading anyone traveling with them. As the barrier to the metaplanes approaches, the quester will be challenged by the Dweller on the Threshold. To pass the trial presented by the Dweller, an initiate must face their own doubts, fears, and limitations, overcoming their secret demons. The gamemaster can present this in a variety of ways. Perhaps the test will be in a courtroom filled with shadowy accusers and judges, or it may be visions of past failures that need to be re-enacted creating a different outcome, or a stage play in front of anaudience acting out a deeply repressed fear to be confronted and overcome, and of course it may also be a manifestation of the Dweller itself interrogating the travelers about their intentions. Failure in this initial challenge will send the quester back to their physical body. Whatever form the challenge takes, it should be an opportunity for the gamemaster and player to explore the character. Alternatively, if a faster, simpler approach fits the style of play better, the challenge of the Dweller can be represented as a simple Magic + tradition attribute + Initiate Grade (4) test, with failure resulting in the quester forced to return to their body. They must then repeat the meditation, with a one-point higher threshold, before trying to pass the barrier again.

Once past the initial challenge, the travelers move directly to the trials of the quest itself. Depending on the goal of the quest, this may occur on a known metaplane, or a subdivision of a metaplane such as a bubble metaplane or hyper-metaplane. Once in the metaplane, the quester—and anyone else traveling along—will manifest a metaplanar form for the duration of the trials. The quest itself is represented as moving through a series of scenes or places with challenges that fit the nature of the quest’s goal and the environment of the metaplane. The number of places should be three to five on average, but could vary from as few as two for a very simple goal to as many as a dozen for a goal that is exceedingly important and difficult to obtain. The challenge of a scene or place will be a test using a specific skill, with a threshold set by the importance of the goal—usually the same as the number of scenes required for the quest. These tests are usually named plainly— for example, the Place of Charisma will represent a social challenge, while the Place of Battle requires a combat skill, and the Place of Magic may need a powerful spell to be cast. The last scene will include the goal of the quest, perhaps a tome containing the knowledge sought, a mentor willing to provide enlightenment, or an enemy that must be destroyed. Returning to the physical body at the end of a successful quest is simply willing oneself to return—there is no associated test.

Failing a trial along the way will add another scene to the quest, but it also weakens the metaplanar form of the quester. A failed test must be resisted with the higher of Body or Willpower, as if it were a blow having a damage code equal to the threshold of the failed test. Damage caused by a failed trial is tracked separately from the regular condition monitor in a ten-box quest condition monitor, and this damage may not be healed by skills, gear, or magic (though one point of it can be healed with a 4-Edge boost). Combat within an individual scene may also fill some condition monitor boxes, but this damage is applied to the regular condition monitor and does not fill any boxes

in the special quest condition monitor. If the primary quester fills any condition monitor, from any source, the quest has failed, and the initiate and any helpers who followed them on the quest are forced back into their physical bodies, with Stun damage equal to the boxes of damage suffered by their metaplanar form.

For more difficult astral quests, it’s important to take some helpers along, as the trials would be too arduous for a single traveler. It’s also a good way to get the entire group of players involved. Using teamwork tests on any of the trials raises the threshold of that trial by 1 per extra participant. A helper along on the quest may be the team leader of a test, but the quester must participate in any test, and the penalty for failure falls on the quester alone, regardless of who leads the test. Travelers who have come along to help may leave the quest at any time by stating a desire to return to their physical bodies, but they may not return to the quest if they leave.
