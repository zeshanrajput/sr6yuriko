## Kuznechik Airbike

Since Berlin’s unification, corporations have been trying everything they can to spread the blessings of the modern world far into the often neo-ludditic anarchic areas. Broken roads, gang road tolls, ideological transit bans, and looting of delivery vehicles have led companies in Berlin to rely even more heavily than elsewhere on aerial drones to deliver pizza, fashionable shoes, supermarket goods, and other drek to their customers. However, Berlin wouldn’t be Berlin if that wasn’t fraught with problems, too. Time and again, delivery drones are lost, hacked, or shot out of the sky, hijacked with nets and ropes while being unloaded, and then dismantled and sold for parts. More powerful models, on the other hand, are sometimes converted by mostly youthful tinkerers into a “rider drone.” Also called “Kuznechik” (Russian for grasshopper) or “Spatz” (German for sparrow), these things are basically flying drones with a mounted seat, a few handles to hold on to, and a rewiring of the controls that allows the daredevil pilot to directly control the drone via cable connection. With a vehicle control rig, if available, or blatantly suicidal with a joystick.
