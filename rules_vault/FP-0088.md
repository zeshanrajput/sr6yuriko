## **Headquarters**

**It’s easy to forget just how many kinds of divining there are. We know about the common ones—reading tea leaves (tasseography), gazing into crystal balls (scrying), reading palms (palmistry), and of course tarot cards (taromancy)—but there are a whole lot more forms out there. Reading signs from birds (orniscopy), reading the stars (astrology), staring into mirrors and pools of water (hydromancy), analyzing people’s body features (somatomancy), looking at numerical patterns (numerology), listening to the rhythm of thunder (brontomancy), watching a baby crawl, and one of my personal favorites, aspidomancy—divining the future by reading patterns in suits of armor.**

**While the UCAS is greatly diminished in power, they still have a considerable population and signifcant wealth at their disposal. With Ares leaving, they no longer have an AAA corporation headquartered in their borders, leaving a certain power void that Convergence sought to fll.**

**No two diviners perform their tasks the exact same way, so Convergence has facilities for as many of them as they can. Their headquarters have a wealth of tarot cards, a quality telescope, a pen of potential animal sacrifces in the basement, an array of tea leaves, balls made of the clearest crystal, and on and on. And yes, some fne quality suits of armor. It’s a joyous playground for diviners and perhaps their best recruiting tool.**

**> They also have a full custodial staff to clean up the messes. Poor souls. > Frosty**

**The important thing about the headquarters is separating what has value from what does not. The livestock, the crystal balls, and even the suits of armor are not all that valuable—and the suits are diffcult to swipe. The real value is the database of divinations they have, along with the hacked data they have retrieved, and what it can tell you about the future. They have two protections for this. First is standard Matrix security, frewalls and IC and spiders and so on. They have good defensive hackers, and they use them. Second, though, is they try to keep the hardcore analysis, the stuff that connects all the dots, off the Matrix host and on wired hosts only. So even if you manage to get on their private host and rummage around for a while, the data might seem random without the connective tissue Convergence staff has developed. It becomes all too easy to miss the forest for the trees.**
