## Culture

The culture of Lagos is one of economic disparity, along with an old-world religion that has gained a new following since the Awakening, and tribal cultures with rivalries older than many modern countries. Superstition, fears, poverty, oppression, and greed define the everyday behavior of the citizens of Lagos. They’re frequently honorable and do not tolerate outsiders looking down on them or disrespecting their culture. The people of Lagos need nothing from those who invaded their region. This includes outsiders, tourists (the few they have), corporations, corporate employees, and anyone who does not trace their lineage to the local tribes. Typical Lagosians are self-sufficient and proud of it. They’ll sacrifice to avoid feeling like they owe something to an outsider.
