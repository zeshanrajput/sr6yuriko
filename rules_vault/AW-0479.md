## Astral Gateway Ritual

(Anchored)

**Threshold:** 3 + (Distance Number)

This ritual causes forced projection and typically opens a temporary passageway to the Astral Sea or a metaplane selected by the ritual leader. Using the astral plane as a destination (a 0-distance journey) only causes forced projection and does not open a passageway. In order to open a passageway, the ritual leader must have previously visited that metaplane, have a guide, or have knowledge of its location in order to open a gateway to it. A spirit guide must be from the destination plane, and escorting the mage there counts as a service. A mage may also gain the location through in-depth study of the aura of a spirit or creature from that plane. Understanding the location of a metaplane through study of one of its natives is an Intuition + Astral test with a threshold based on the relative distance of the metaplane (2: adjacent, 4: near, 6: intermediate, 8: distant, 10: deep, and 12: very deep). Certain grimoires and tomes may also contain information on the locations of some metaplanes, but that information should be difficult to obtain (treat as a 1,000 nuyen ritual formula, but with an availability equal to the distance) and difficult to decipher. Note that these formulas are different from the maps that can be carried into the Astral Sea, though the more expensive maps can be used for this purpose as well as navigating the Astral Sea. Understanding the location of a metaplane from the description in a written formula takes a week of study and a successful Astral + Logic test. The threshold is equal to the distance number. Failed attempts can be retried, but a –2 dice pool modifier is applied for each new attempt.

Any objects within five meters of the astral gateway become dual-natured, and anyone, even non-Awakened individuals, is subject to forced projection. This ritual takes six hours to perform. The gateway lasts for a number of days equal to the net hits on the sealing test, and the gateway must remain in the place where it is opened.

This updated version of the ritual replaces the version used on p. 140, Slip Streams.
