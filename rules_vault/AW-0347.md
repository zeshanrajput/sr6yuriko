## Arrival at the Plane of Beasts

The next task ahead of us was to find some powerful local beast spirits who would be willing and able to either guide or transport us to the Island of Tandora. Fortunately for us, the river tributary continued well into Aerie—our first destination. As we neared the cluster of trees where the Council of Raptors met, I decided it would be worth cashing in a favor from a corvus to safely gain an audience. I won’t bore you with the details, except that it took us hours of discussion to find out that we needed permission directly from Queen Mystras. It seemed the expedition might be doomed, but a delegation of great eagles was present at the court. Hemdale’s gifts and flattery convinced them to carry us up the great mountain where the roc king Lightning-over-Water reigns with the double-headed eagle queen at his side. We were not permitted to speak with the queen directly, so we had to submit our petition through a representative. In most places other than the Plane of Air, metaplanar bureaucracy is incredibly tedious. In the Plane of Beasts, bureaucracy is largely nonexistent. We spent a few hours in a cave with the most splendid view of the terrain below, and then the news came back that our offering had been found acceptable, and that travel to the Island of Tandora would be permitted. I wasn’t sure exactly how we were going to get there—I assumed we’d either be pointed in the right direction or pass through a portal. I certainly wasn’t expecting another trip on the backs of great eagles. They carried us up and away from the mountain, deep into the clouds, where we lost all sight of land and sky. After what seemed like only a few minutes, we descended back through the clouds, and the Island of Tandora was visible beneath us.
