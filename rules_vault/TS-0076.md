## **Specialty Divisions**

**Specialty divisions can usually be described as one of four types: magical operations, Matrix operations, drone operations, and K9 or PK (for para-critter) operations. Various forces will use different names for these divisions, such as Lone Star’s Division of Paranormal Investigations, or the Division of Matrix Security, but the basic operation of a specialty division will generally be the same as what’s described here. The offcers who work in these divisions are in high demand and short supply. As a result, command will temporarily assign these offcers to different squads and cases as needed, moving them between Tactical, Investigation, Patrol, or even Support.**

**Magical Operations is composed of a very small number of full mages, usually in a command role, with a larger number of aspected mages and other Awakened individuals. These offcers are a mix of those limited to a single aspect of magic, like sorcerers or conjurers, as well as mystic adepts, social adepts, astral explorers, and those with simple astral sensitivity. Enchanters may also be employed but will often stay in the support departments. Magical operations tend not to include adepts who focus strictly on combat, attaching those individuals to TacDiv instead.**

**Offcers who work in magical operations are constantly in demand and will be assigned to an assortment of departments, wherever they are needed the most. An offcer from magical operations may spend their day attached to a SWAT team responding to high-threat calls or working with a forensics team collecting astral and psychometric impressions for a homicide investigation. Other offcers may spend their day projecting, patrolling astral space and watching for illegal magic use, or assisting with suspect interviewing and interrogation by sustaining an Analyze Truth spell.**

**K9 and PK Operations is usually the smallest specialty division of most forces. K9 units include regular mundane police dogs trained in a variety of tasks such as sniffng out drugs or explosives, containing rioting civilians, or tracking suspects across rural areas. Cybernetically enhanced K9 units may also be attached to SWAT units, assuming the handler has the combat training to work with the team. Regular and cyber K9 units may be deployed to patrol an area, augmenting foot patrols, or sent with TacDiv units for crowd control or demolitions.
