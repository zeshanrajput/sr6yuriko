**ways you can have your fortune told in New Orleans. However, sometimes you don’t trust** fate to give you a good outcome. That’s where the liar’s medium comes into play.

**==> picture [234 x 246] intentionally omitted \<==**

**==> picture [67 x 630] intentionally omitted \<==**

By stacking the deck and loading the dice, you aim not just to be shown what the fates will, but exactly what you need to see to buy a better future. A liar’s medium replaces the –2 drain on divinations with +1 Edge or, if the user really wants to play with fi re and cheat the deck big time, +2 Edge and +2 drain.

Creating a liar’s medium requires an existing divination fetish and “cheating” the tool—shaving the dice to weigh them more to one side, loading the deck so it’s easier to draw what you want, or similar tasks. Once completed, a new dram of reagents must be expended to secure the altered magic. Thus, creating one from scratch effectively requires two drams.
