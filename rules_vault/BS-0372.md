## Cyanide

- **Vector:** Ingestion, Inhalation, Injection

- **Speed:** Immediate

- **Duration:** 1 hour

- **Power:** 12

- **Effect:** Physical damage

Cyanide is a group of chemical compounds defined by a carbon atom triple-bonded to a nitrogen atom. It is one of the fastest-acting poisons, and itgenerally starts immediately with headaches, dizziness, and fast breathing, and is later followed with seizures, low blood pressure, and cardiac arrest. Cyanide poisoning works by inhibiting cells from transferring oxygen, blocking the mitochondrial electron transport chain. Victims of cyanide poisoning must resist (Power)P damage.
