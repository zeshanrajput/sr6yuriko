## The Central Plot Arc

## (Animus / CIA / Project Lazarus)

After the events of the 2080 blackouts, the UCAS government began looking for a weapon that could strike back against the megacorporations. Kent Burroughs, now the director of the CIA—also secretly the penultimate master of the Black Lodge—proposed Project Lazarus as a way to strike back against the Corporate Court and create a resurrection of UCAS prominence. The upcoming initial demonstration is being managed by Indica Meiers in Manhattan, overseen by Justin Bellflower, with the event organized by Celine and Illyran Ardrin. It includes a release of incriminating video footage. Simultaneous to the release of manipulated video footage of DJ’s criminal actions, sleeper agents, conditioned with Animus tech over the last year, will be activated inside the homes of people close to the Corporate Court. A small number of incidents were planned to appear random at first glance; trusted bodyguards turning on their charges, spouses attacking their families in the middle of the night, and senior managers wiping out billions of nuyen. These were primarily tests of the technology. Once it was shown to be successful, the CIA would then attempt social engineering through memory manipulation on a significant scale. People intending to make deals with the UCAS would suddenly find themselves amenable to more favorable terms than they previously considered. People intending reprisals or punishments against the UCAS would suddenly forget what they were planning to do. Negotiation by negotiation, interaction by interaction, the UCAS would give themselves the upper hand, building long-term power as they did so.

Not everyone within Animus Construction is on board with the implementation of Project Lazarus. Jonathan Blake believes that overuse of the tech by the UCAS government would eventually lead back to Animus, disrupting his plans. As a response, Blake and his secret partner, Miles Lanier, devise a plan to separate portions of the operation from what’s controlled directly by the CIA, then expose

Project Lazarus, destroying Animus Construction and leaving Blake in control of the remaining assets. Using a shadow operative, they gather several patients undergoing therapy at Animus Construction: Clinical Operations and release them in Central Park. This was the first step in the exposure plan, followed by tipping off Piper Jane about the initial Project Lazarus event sponsored by Celine and Illyran Ardrin, and then leaking information about a “Project Lazarus” laboratory.

Before the lab information could be leaked, a small laboratory using Animus technology had to be set up, ready to be found—one that exposed the basics of the technology and led directly to MCT and the CIA. Through a local fixer, Blake hired the Ancients to intercept a shipment of medical nano-forges coming from MCT as they were on the way to the main Animus Construction facility. The hijacking only partially succeeded, and the tech ended up in the hands of the NYPD. Fearing the exposure of MCT’s involvement, Teeg initiated a cleanup operation using the Null Sect to cover up the evidence. He also sent in a team of shadowrunners to retrieve the equipment—and they mostly succeeded—leaving Blake and Lanier scrambling to alter their plans. The Null Sect did a good job of erasing all evidence at the precinct, but Piper suspects there may be more to find and sends the runners searching for other video footage. This uncovers the involvement of some known MCT operatives entering—and then leaving—the precinct in the hijacked Roadmaster.

With Blake’s efforts to expose it having no real impact yet, Project Lazarus continues to follow the initial plan. The “demonstration” with DJ is set up, with the runners happening to be one of several teams secretly recording the events. The raw footage is reworked by the CIA labs and released. The immediate fallout of Project Lazarus draws in a number of power players including Buttercup, who tasks Taylor Dacopral and indirectly Rikki Nguyen to look into events. In short order, Rikki tracks down DJ and attempts to discover what happened. The Tiding (an independent covert information-gathering organization) is also interested in uncovering the events and will either attempt to extract DJ for their own investigation or may be willing to pool resources with the runners to uncover what’s going on. If the runners handle the Tiding correctly, they may even hear about Animus Construction for the first time (if they didn’t hear about it while engaged in the _Assassin’s Night_ campaign).

The efforts of the runners to uncover events have not gone unnoticed. Indica Meiers, running interference related to Project Lazarus for the CIA, hires an intermediary to pay Piper to “interview” the runners in an effort to discover what they know. She also unintentionally leaves a trail of evidence leading back to the CIA’s involvement.



The CIA is not the only Animus faction that has noticed the runners—they have also shown up on Jonathan Blake’s radar. After gathering every detail he can about the team, he arranges for information about them to be leaked to Buttercup. He then hires a local fixer to lure them into a meeting— first with him, then with Buttercup. Blake is trying to use the runners to steer Buttercup into helping expose MCT’s—and the Null Sect’s—involvement with Project Lazarus and eventually expose the CIA while diverting attention away from his and Lanier’s interests. Ms. Helena Chomsky, who is in charge of Buttercup’s security operations through Marsh and Hound Ltd., is actually working for Miles Lanier and will be in the meeting, secretly reporting back to Blake.

Coincidence always plays a role in events, and the runners pick up a few key pieces of information after gaining access to two secret labs. The data gained from the decades-old labs gives the runners some new insight into the CIAs early experiments on mind control. The data also helps point the way to the location of another possible lab—the Octagon. After tracking down this new lead, the runners learn that work with mental manipulation is ongoing and it’s tied to a name they may have heard from the Tiding—Animus Construction.

Having the data on these various programs is a long way from understanding the science behind it—or what people may be doing with it. The runners need an expert opinion, and their contacts point them to a BTL producer named Brinkvale. After helping him locate a previous patient, Brinkvale will explain to the team what the Animus memory-altering technology described in the data can do, and also what its limitations are. He then points them to another expert who may know more—his old boss, Dr. Sonya Peters, who is now an e-ghost inside the NYU Medical School host. While not providing a lot of additional technical information, she will mention that some people claiming to be from the UCAS government had tried to recruit her to work on something called Project Anima a few years back using the exact same technology—and she is able to provide some data on those people.

While searching for more information on Animus Construction and the CIA operatives running it, a recently retired colleague of the runners brings them a crucial clue. He reveals that his parents are secretly employed by Animus Construction. He overheard a conversation about a work meeting happening soon. Eavesdropping on the meeting produces no new clues, but if the work associate can be successfully followed, the other attendee will lead the team to the main facility of Animus Construction. If the team fails in their tailing attempt, Lady Luck will give them another chance. One of their Mr. Johnsons, El Guapo,

has intercepted a delivery heading to a secret facility. He hires the runners to make the delivery and get him information on what that facility is. The location in question turns out to be another secret facility of Animus Construction. Regardless of how the runners locate the facility, exploring it will confirm what the runners already suspect, but the prime clue about who may be behind events is an encrypted message requesting a meeting with a cryptic time and location. Unraveling the note and spying on the meeting will uncover who the actual players behind Animus Construction are, handing the runners the last pieces of the puzzle needed to expose the organization.

What they choose to do with the information gathered has the potential to create a storm that will shake the pillars of the corporate world, tearing down some while elevating others. The challenge will become surviving the storm long enough to collect a payday and then blend back into the shadows.
