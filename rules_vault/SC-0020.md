## Metaplanar Quest

This adventure hook works best with the privileged background but is easily adapted for any team with one or more Awakened player characters.

Magicians are known to travel on metaplanar quests as part of initiations and other magical works. These are normally solo ventures very personalized to the experiences and perceptions of the magician. While interesting, they provide the challenge of not easily involving more than one player in the adventure. Instead of tasking the mundane runners with guarding the magician’s body, involve them in a quest that takes the entire team physically into the metaplanes using a gate.

The objective for such a quest might be learning the true name of a free spirit, acquiring an exotic reagent for a unique ritual or focus, learning some secret knowledge that is unattainable through any other means, or rescuing another magician who has become trapped there.

The first part of such an adventure is gaining access to the metaplanes. Unless the employer provides this access, the team will either need to find and access a stable gate (p. 19, _Street Wyrd_ ) or use the assistance of a free spirit with the Astral Gateway power. Such spirits are likely to deal in magical goods, information, and Karma—nuyen rarely interests them much.

The second part of the adventure is traveling through the metaplane to the objective. Use unusual imagery, encounters with strange spirits, people, and creatures, unusual obstacles, and environmental hazards to set the strange tone for the metaplane. Use the stat blocks for appropriate types of spirits for most entities encountered, and feel free to be flexible with what powers they have. Provide a variety of challenges that the runners must use a variety of skills and tactics to overcome.

In most metaplanes that any metahumans are likely to travel to, most of the laws of physics apply. Magic functions normally, though there is no separate layer of astral space, so no astral projection or perception is possible. Technology also works as expected, with the exception of Matrix connectivity. There is no Matrix presence, and so no wireless bonuses are available. Drones and comms will only function within a 100-meter radius of a master device (commlink, cyberdeck, RCC, etc.). If the metaplane in question is the Court of



Shadows, technological devices are transformed into natural or low-tech versions but function effectively the same. An assault rifle might look like a crossbow, but it deals the same damage. Drones might look like dogs or griffins, but they are still treated as drones.

The third and final part of the adventure comes when the runners reach the location of their objective. This is nearly always a confrontation of some kind, which might require fighting, tricking, convincing, winning a game from, or even dancing with a powerful spirit. The metaplanes are often very reactive, and so the way the runners react and respond to any challenges is likely to set the tone for the engagement.
