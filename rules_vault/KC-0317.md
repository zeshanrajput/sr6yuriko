## The Job

Horizon needs more power hubs, and this is a huge opportunity to build one in Central North America. The contact is Alsiya. Her single name comes from her former life as a vocal performer before she turned into a corporate executive. She needs this to build Horizon St. Louis rather than presenting this to the Consensus, as the program will see the Kechibi Code and potentially use it globally, rather than just locally, or just erase it. Alsiya is the buyer, but she needs to toss a false front onto her interactions with the runner team. They are sent on a literal milk run in order to show some skills and make it look like she’s paying them for something else. She has a sense of humor and hires the runners to steal a load of milk from a specialty dairy outfit that still makes real milk. She also sees the security at a place like that as relatively easy to crack.

Things are never so easy.

The target is Defiance Dairy. This is one of the rare real dairy farms. See the **Defiance Dairy** sidebar for a little Matrix ad they run before you read on.

Now, for the truth. Defiance Dairy produces real milk. They do have cows wandering the grounds. Well, what look like cows. The surface cows are actually drones covered in cow skins. The interior cows are kept in an underground facility below the warehouse that can also be reached from the nearby quarry, a secret part of their property and their operation. These creatures are kept alive by drugs and technology and produce immense quantities of milk that is not as pure and clean as their ads promote. They still sell it for a pretty penny but that is not their main business.

Clean and fresh is what to expect from all of our products with the finest cheeses and several varieties of milk to tantalize a variety of palates.

Healthy, safe, and home grown, Defiance Dairy nourishes not only your body, but your very soul.

Defiance Dairy, in particular the quarry adjacent to their farmland, is an end point for one of North America’s most well-used smuggling routes that runs from Seattle, through Denver, and down to St. Louis. The smugglers come in using LAVs and VTOL craft that land in the quarry and get moved over into caves they use as hangars. The goods that come in with the smugglers are pulled off and delivered to St. Louis or to shippers on the Mississippi River to be taken downriver to New Orleans or off up the Ohio, or upriver to the UCAS cities along the Mississippi. All these goods are shipped in trucks disguised as delivery trucks for Defiance Dairy.

The dairy farm consists of a small retail facility near Route 94, an office building, a trio of stables with milking parlors for the cows to go during inclement weather, and two large loading warehouses.

The warehouses are 100 meters by 50 meters with two large bay doors on the short side. Inside is a series of docks. There are two sets of docks along the long walls and a parking region at the center of the warehouse. The docks start at the far end of the warehouses, where five large industrial elevators bring up goods, both illicit and dairy related, from the cavern below the warehouse. The delivery vans (use GMC Bulldog, p. 301, _SR6_ ) are kept in the warehouse until it’s time to ship to keep them out of the public eye.

Security around the building is provided by wireless cameras all around the interior and exterior of the warehouses. A series of hardwired cameras that cover the tunnels under the warehouse over to the quarry feed to the stables and the underground complex. The doors of the warehouse have maglocks (rating 5) with remote operation controlled via ARO keypad activated by a motion sensor near the doors.

The warehouse has ten workers (use Humanis Goons, p. 204, _SR6_ ; increase Strength to 4) loading

LET’Sand unloading trucks. They use one of four commlink-controlled pallet jacks or drive one of six forklifts. They also move trucks to and from the storage lot in the center of the warehouses. Only five of the workers in each warehouse come in for work while the others come up from down below.

Beneath the warehouse is a large cavern. The cavern has an array of tunnels that lead off in one direction. These lead to the quarry hangars. Small flatbed trucks make runs back and forth.

In the opposite direction of the hangars is a single tunnel that leads to the real milking facility. Large fans blow into this tunnel to both prevent the smell from reaching this area and to keep the methane produced by the cows from settling into this cavern and eventually the hangars. A group of eight workers cycle through working in the milking room. They wear full hazmat suits and spend their time checking feed tubes, excrement tubes, cow vitals, air quality, milk production, hormone levels, and clear out and replace cows that are not performing. This section of this place is like the worst slaughterhouse you could ever imagine, especially since all the cows have been skinned and covered in a numbing gel. Their legs have been removed and they are sustained by tubes with liquids of a variety of colors.

A single tunnel leads off on another wall. Down that tunnel is a large living complex that houses workers as well as smugglers looking to bed down before they make another run. This single tunnel has a series of side tunnels for different purposes. There is a large kitchen and dining hall at the first junction to the right. The next junction, to the left, leads off to a social area with seating and trids. The next ten junctions, staggered on the right and left, each lead to a series of ten small sleeping quarters, each with a bunk, chair, desk, and small storage locker for personal items and clothes.

The underground complex uses all the commlinks present to maintain the mesh network, but it is still flooded with noise (6 levels everywhere but the main chamber).
