## Allies and Rivals

When designing NPCs, it’s useful to determine if they have any allies or rivals. A rival could be someone competing over territory, skills, or some other factor that puts the two at odds, while allies are highly trusted individuals who show a large degree of loyalty and do what they can to assist. These relationship dynamics mean that both parties know a lot of information about the other, but the nature of that relationship can vary between

friendship and hostility. This dynamic doesn’t have to be mutual, either. One character may consider the other an ally or rival, and the other could be completely ignorant of their feelings.

Allies are considered to have a Loyalty of 5+. The contact who considers the other to be an ally will come to their aid when called upon. They also gain a bonus dice pool equal to Loyalty to resist any influence or con attempt.

Two individuals or groups who compete over the same objective are considered rivals. The nature of this relationship could be friendly in nature where each drives the other forward to great heights, or it can be more adversarial, based on bitterness and hostility. For the purposes of this section, friendly rivals would be better classified as allies, while rivalries should be reserved for combative relationships.

Contacts can have no more than three total allies and rivals each. Ally and rival relationships don’t need to be symmetrical, meaning that it’s okay to have more rivals than allies, or vice versa. Allies can be used to gain leverage over a target—when targeting an individual who has an ally, player characters who use that ally on any test to con or influence their target gain a point of Edge. For legwork or social tests involving rivals, the character must use a Favor Point to convince them to help their rival, even if it’s indirectly beneficial. Conversely, any work that would hurt the rival will grant a Favor Point as long as it’s successful.
