## Traditional Yakuza (Old Ways/Maasaki Watada)

Oyabun Akira Watada’s reign was marked by significant changes and internal strife. In 2067, ill health forced Watada to name his nephew, Hitori Hanzo, as acting kumicho. Hanzo officially succeeded him upon Watada’s death in 2068, but he was assassinated in 2070 shortly after putting down the Shotozumi Uprising. Maasaki Watada was named the new Oyabun of the Watada-rengo.

The Watada-rengo is the largest conglomerate of criminal organizations spanning across Japan, the Philippines, Australia, and Europe. Unlike a conventional conglomerate, the Watada-rengo operates akin to a collective of criminal factions, with the Oyabun of the Watada-gumi serving as its leader. Each Oyabun and kumicho within the conglomerate contributes a portion of their profits to the rengo, receiving protection and assistance in return.

Oyabun Maasaki Watada oversees collaboration between the factions, intervenes in disputes, offers resources and connections, and provides support in handling violent conflicts involving Watada-rengo members. Watada maintains a traditional stance and disapproves of the New Way, resulting in his gumi excluding women, metahumans, and mixed-heritage individuals. This ideology is mirrored to varying extents by other gumi within the Watada-rengo, with Watada silently endorsing Yakuza traditions by selecting new Oyabun from his supporters to take control of leaderless organizations.

Maasaki Watada, a staunch traditionalist, sought revenge against his New Way rivals. This sparked the formation of the Wanibuchi-gumi from the New Way clans in Neo-Tokyo, supported by the free spirt Jurojin. The breakaway dealt a significant blow to the Watada-rengo. So, to consolidate his power, Oyabun Watada halted expansion.

Until now, that is, when Maasaki Watada saw an opportunity amidst the chaos. While the Mafia and Vory were preoccupied with their internal strife, the Four Oyabun Rengo and the New Way groups were distracted. The time was ripe for the Watada-rengo to expand their operations.

Their first move established connections with disgruntled suppliers for both the Mafia and the Vory. Under Maasaki Watada’s direction, the Watada-rengo reached out to disillusioned street-level contacts and offered assurances of protection in exchange for allegiance to the Watada-rengo. Meanwhile, others leveraged new alliances to expand the Watada-rengo’s influence on foreign soil. They brokered deals with local criminal enterprises and offered support in exchange for profit shares. With each new partnership, Watada-rengo footholds expanded in the global underworld. Thus far, they’ve focused on more traditional Yakuza businesses, such as drugs, BTLs, gambling, prostitution, and protection rackets. They’ve preferred to leave things such as white-collar crimes to the inferior New Ways.

The Shotozumi-rengo of North America are part of the Old Yakuza tradition; nevertheless, they broke with the Watada-rengo about ten years ago because of the treatment from Oyabun Watada. They’ve been working against the Watada-rengo ever since, and it’s unclear what will happen as the Watada-rengo expands to North America.
