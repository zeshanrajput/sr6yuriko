## Conserving the Metaplanes

The metaplanes are vast places, filled with seemingly infinite numbers and variations of flora and fauna, spirits, and environments of every kind—vast cities, untouched wilderness, corrupted wastelands, and bizarre biomes. When you study the metaplanes, traveling and exploring as I do, you can be left with no doubt that they are real places that exist independently of our own minds and imagination, and yet even the elemental planes seem to influence or be influenced by some events that occur on Earth. The currencies of the elemental planes, for example, seem to fluctuate based



on earthly weather and climate patterns. Perhaps these are coincidences or evidence of some cosmic synchronicity, but it seems highly likely that metahumanity has some effect on the metaplanes, and the metaplanes have an effect upon us. I personally ascribe to the theory that the apparent cycle of magic in our world can be attributed to our physical plane’s proximity to the metaplanes in the Astral Sea. The metaplanes are not static as they drift along the Astral Sea. They are the source of all magic and mana, and when they are close to us, mana flows into our world through the shores of the Astral Sea, carried in like the high tide of a terran ocean. What causes the astral drift of the metaplanes, and whether or not it can be predicted or even adjusted, is unknown, but I believe that its discovery is merely a matter of time and effort.

In the twentieth century, metahumanity discovered that its actions had great effect upon the Earth’s climate, which in turn has a great effect on its ecosystems and the life forms that depend on them. It seems strange to me that there are still people living today who deny the fact that metahumanity has done such grave damage to its home, that environmental catastrophes are hoaxes spread by anti-capitalist undesirables. Then I think of the hubris of many magicians—hermetics, mostly— who choose to believe that the metaplanes are a projection of the mind, and that the spirits living there are not truly alive. This is a dangerous mindset, because it allows the magician to act freely with no care for the consequences of their own actions—why worry about whether or not you destroy lives and unbalance ecosystems in a dream world? It’s just imaginary, right? Wrong! So very, very wrong! Like every thought stunted by egocentric greed used to justify injustice throughout our history, the denial of the importance or even existence of the lives and agencies of those living in a new frontier, it is wrong. Not just wrong, but unconscionably filthy. If you are an adherent to the belief that the metaplanes are just magical thoughtrealms full of spirits to exploit and resources to be plundered, I sincerely hope that you change your mind.

Unfortunately, metaplanar “experts” making these claims have gained mainstream popularity. Their voices are boosted on the media by the megacorporations, who hope to profit greatly over the vast resources they are in the process of stripping from the metaplanes. Some have the resources to establish semi-permanent portals that allow personnel and vehicles to physically enter and exit the metaplanes. They send in armed convoys— some for the purpose of exploration and trade, others to set up or maintain outposts near rich deposits of reagents or important cities. If matters continue as they seem likely to, the spirits of the metaplanes will learn to fear and hate metahuman interlopers, and the metaplanes will become too dangerous for travel and exploration.

And so I implore to you, dear reader, to respect the metaplanes as you would respect all of nature. Accept that they are real places populated by sapient entities with lives of their own. Realize that what you do can have a lasting effect upon the metaplanes and the spirits who live there. Recognize that the vast resources present within the metaplanes are part of a unique ecosystem, and that by stripping them of those resources, you will bring about imbalance in ways that you are incapable of anticipating. You have an ethical obligation to preserve nature, both on the physical plane and in the metaplanes. We all do.
