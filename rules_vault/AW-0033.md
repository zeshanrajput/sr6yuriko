## Hacking the Metaplanes

But what about Matrix tech? There’s no Matrix on the metaplanes, right? So, what good is a cyberdeck? Well, the Matrix as we know it is rare on the metaplanes, but it exists in a few places. It’s more likely you’ll find something “Matrix-like,” while in other places you’ll get the dreaded “no Matrix signal detected” notification once you step through that gate. But don’t give up on finding a use for your cyberdeck there. This might come as a surprise to you, but there’s a lot of native tech on the metaplanes, even the ones with no Matrix equivalent. By native tech, I mean it was designed, developed, and manufactured on a metaplane, not brought in from outside. Don’t be so surprised—there are cities all over the metaplanes. Do you think the spirits just build them with psychokinesis? Well, I’m sure that comes in handy, but they also havecranes, cargo-haulers, and power tools. How do you think they send messages or store their data? Spirits living on the metaplanes might not generate electricity the same way we do, or even use electricity, but they find a way to make their tech work. I’ve also heard from a few technomancer friends that they can often tap into “something” out in the metaplanes, something that seems an awful lot like the Matrix, even if a decker can’t find it. The new UnITy stuff (Universal Information Theory) out of Denver calls it a noosphere. It seems like the data fields—nous energy—can be created by any type of organized data system. Even if it’s some type of magic device or clanking difference engine storing and processing the information, it does the same thing as when optical circuits or used as far as a technomancer is concerned. You’ll likely find all manner of strange tech on the metaplanes, as well as plenty of stuff that seems strangely familiar.

Even when there’s no Matrix equivalent to find, a lot of this tech still needs to talk to other tech in order to function. Sometimes they interact through the metaplane with no apparent connection points, like wireless Matrix tech. Sometimes you need to get close, or even run a data cable to it. Usually, these things don’t have universal data ports, so be sure to bring along a data tap. I’ve found that almost any metaplanar tech can be hacked if you directly connect a data tap—even when there don’t seem to be any wires to tap into, I just attach it to the spot that makes the most sense, and I’ve got access. In the more modern-looking cities scattered around, there are a variety of networks that are a lot like the Matrix. Some of them are like the old non-wireless Matrix, which means that you have to plug in somewhere, but then you can hack anything on the network. Others have a wireless network that works almost exactly like the IRL Matrix. On the elemental Plane of Earth, there are places where you can tap into the ground and hack things. It’s weirder than a dragon with ethics, but I’ve done it.

Keep your deck turned on (or your living persona, if you’re a technomancer), and scan for signals as you go. You’ll find more tech than you might think, and sometimes you can interface with things that don’t seem to be technological in nature. One of the most wizard things I’ve ever done was hack a waterfall so I could pause it, then reverse it so we could ride it up to where we needed to go like it was an elevator. Keep an open mind, and don’t forget to be creative.
