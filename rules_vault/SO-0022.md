## Power of a Hi-Vis Vest, Hard Hat, and Clipboard

On the topic of fitting in, one of the tricks I recommend is to dress for the part that no onewants to see. Showing up as an authority figure to complete inspections or hand out assignments can get you into where you’re not supposed to be. Let me tell a quick story to illustrate.

One time, we were breaking into a Gaeatronics power distribution center. This phase just required us to scope the place out, so we didn’t need to bring in any of the more illicit items from our kits. Our initial legwork at the local watering holes dug up some rumors about the place being overdue for a surprise inspection from corporate. In fact, they had been expecting one for the last six months. A little more research turned up the right disguise we would need. Then we set up some overwatch just in case the real inspectors decided to show up at the worst possible time. We waltzed in with convincing fake credentials and started issuing orders.

Now, you’re probably thinking about all the ways that this could fall apart. All it would have taken was a ring to the main offices and suddenly we’re caught with our pants down and no gear—but this is the power of a good plan! We had our decker lurking in the foreman’s commlink waiting for such a call. Since I was on point for talking, I immediately started pointing out things that needed fixing or that weren’t up to snuff. This put them on their back foot to the point they never even bothered to check with corporate. They just got out of our way!

The key there was knowing ahead of time that the foreman didn’t like confrontation, so we used that against him. We ended up dragging him through the entire plant, making him as uncomfortable as possible while I asked questions about why things weren’t in order. This created the perfect cover for my two other teammates to start taking pictures and planning the real run.

- It’s a pretty common con. But beware, corporate training has been created to try and teach the wageslaves to look for this. Lucky for us, no one watches those training trideos.

Having the right disguise helps sell that you’re supposed to be there, but it’s not foolproof. But dressing in a set of scrubs at a hospital isn’t a getout-of-jail-free card. One particular hospital we were infiltrating had a policy where all employees needed to transmit their SIN constantly. Again, this ended up getting factored into our plans, but it wouldn’t have been possible without solid legwork.
