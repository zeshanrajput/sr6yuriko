## Vocal Modification

The debate continues to rage across the music world about vocal range enhancement. Some insist that natural voices sound better and that those who are truly gifted should be celebrated. Others insist that there’s no discernible difference in expanded ranges and natural voices, unlike the distorted sound of cybernetic vocal enhancement, and that snobbery is the only reason for pushback. Those looking to make it in music have started altering these modifications, and the shadows have found other uses for them.

A character can only take one form of vocal modification.

**Banshee:** The most invasive vocal modification was first introduced by Aztechnology but couldn’t

find a market. That was until a low-profile rocker out of Ohio known as Bobby V got this modification and showcased it with his band the Banshees. Aztechnology saw the opportunity and threw money at the band. Other scream metal bands quickly followed. An untrained person can only make noises akin to an Aztec death whistle, but with a little practice, it allows a person to let out a scream that stops their fans dead on their feet.

**>** For the record, I don’t have any affiliation with Bobby V or the Banshees.

**>** Banshee

This biomodification causes targets to slow or stiffen up. It affects everyone who hears it within a radius of (Body x 5) meters around the user. The player makes a Con (Performance) + Body vs. Intuition + Willpower Opposed test. The rating of any area-effect sound-dampening devices, personal sound-dampening devices (such as cyberware, or earbuds with sound dampening), or hits scored by Hush or Silence spells, is added to the target’s Opposed test, with only the highest single modifier applying. If the target wins, they suffer no ill effects. If the attacker wins, the target gains Immobilized status. If the augmented individual gets 4 or more net hits, the target gains the Stilled status.

**Growler:** A cheaper version of Vocal Range Extender, it doesn’t provide the benefits of the Vocal Range Enhancer or the Vocal Range Extender. Instead, it causes the character to add a very low and primitive tone to their voice. The sound resonates with something in our amygdala that helps trigger a fear response (similar to what horror trids use to frighten audiences).

Individuals with this quality radiate the ultimate unnerving or even predatory vibe. This sensation is often described as discomforting, menacing, or even oppressive. Grants a bonus point of Edge on all Intimidation tests.

**Range Enhancer:** The character has access to a full eight octaves for singing. This provides 1 point of situational Edge for all performance tests.

**Range Extender:** A more expansive version of the range enhancer. While the base enhancement gives the user access to a full eight octaves for singing, the vocal range expander pushes the boundaries even further, allowing the user’s voice to reach into the infrasonic and ultrasonic, ranging from 20 Hz to 200 kHz, producing sounds beyond the standard metahuman hearing range. Combined with a similar hearing enhancement, users can speak with one another at a frequency others can’t hear, an obvious advantage.

In addition to the 1 point of situational Edge for all performance tests, it also allows for attempts to fool voice recognition systems or to imitate animal or artificial noises. Use an Opposed Con (Impersonation) + Charisma test against the biometric




system’s rating for the former, or against an NPC’s Perception + Intuition for the latter.
