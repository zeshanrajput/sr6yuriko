## The Costs 

- **Metahuman Quality:** First and foremost, playing a drake requires either the Drake positive quality (50 Karma) or the Latent Drake positive quality (25 Karma). 

- **Unsteady Hands:** Drakes gain the Unsteady Hands quality when in dracoform. 

- **Memorable:** Anyone who witnesses a drake change forms or use drake powers receives a +2 dice pool bonus on Memory tests (p. 67, _SR6_ ) to recall their appearance or other details about them or to remember having seen them before. Quite a few bystanders (and even once-friendly NPCs) will often feel very strongly about “dragons” suddenly appearing in their day-to-day life as well, and using drake powers or shifting form near them is a fine opportunity for a gamemaster to consider long-term role-play implications, the likelihood of the Prejudiced negative quality (or simply fear and paranoia) affecting NPCs dispositions, etc. 

- **Misshapen:** “Metahuman-Anthropomorphic Dragon” covers a lot of ground. There are a wide variety of body types just among humans, once elves, dwarves, and trolls are in the mix, and all of it is complicated by the body types of the four key types of drakes. Any given drake’s individual physical attribute modifications are taken into account as well. There is a wondrous variety of arm lengths, torso widths, shoulder shapes, wings, and everything else on display. Any time a drake shifts forms, clothing and armor they’re wearing is destroyed—as is any similar equipment (take your smartgoggles and gyromount harnesses off if you get the chance!).
