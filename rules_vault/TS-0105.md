## **You Don’t Technically Exist**

**==> picture [233 x 4] intentionally omitted \<==**

**Legally speaking, folks in your line of work tend to be given the short end of the stick when it comes to fair and proper representation in today’s legal systems. It comes with the territory, as I’m sure most of you know, but that comes with its share of ramifcations should you run afoul of law enforcement or corporate security in any way.**

**I get that not every runner is completely SINless. Some have corporate System Identifcation Numbers or some may even have national SINs granted to them from a very young age, but there still remains a vast community of people in any metroplex area, who are completely SINless, not just shadowrunners.**

**One major beneft the SINless enjoy is not having their records on any reliable fle anywhere. This can be very useful if you are trying to hide or disappear. The only data someone may have on you is comparative data from another instance where you left it, be it blood, fngerprints, getting your picture taken, or some other method of collecting information. Cops tend to be of one extreme or another when it comes to the SINless. They either take absolute advantage of them (as I’ll talk about soon enough), or they**

**simply fnd them too much of a hassle to deal with and can easily be convinced, usually through cold, hard cash or cred, to simply let a SINless person go. Being able to simply vanish can be a considerable advantage for an alleged professional criminal. But that anonymity comes at a price.**

**You have no real form of identifcation to provide for transactions as simple as being carded for buying booze. With no SIN, you can’t apply for or receive any real line of credit, nor can you have a normal bank account. Not that you’d want one. You can’t cross a national border easily, as you cannot provide proof of any kind of real residency that the customs offcials normally accept.**

**And should you get arrested, that cherished anonymity can work against you very quickly.**

**I’ve heard crooked cops say it to people they pick up as a threat: “You don’t technically exist.” It’s their way of scaring the individual into compliance. The implication being, if you don’t technically exist, then you never got brought into the station in the frst place, and should you disappear, no one will come looking for you and no one will miss you. It implies the cop can do whatever they want with you with no repercussions, because you possess no legal recourse to fght back. People who hear this phrase more often than not simply vanish. Others emerge days later with broken bones, or even missing fngers or limbs. Almost all of them have a hollow stare they’ll never shake.**

**Even in cases where someone isn’t being arrested, there are times when a LEP unit cruises into a largely SINless community and leans on them pretty hard. They’ll drive around really slowly, eyeballing all the locals, and ask inane questions until they see something they can easily construe as borderline illegal, at which point they get out of their car and strut up to the individuals in question. This whole time they have their hands on their weapons and have probably deployed their drone as well (if they have one). The cop then starts asking circular questions, trying to get the target of their harassment to give them a reason to arrest them. Failing that, they try to get the person being interrogated, I’m sorry “interviewed,” to roll on someone they consider an actual criminal. Of course, if this person says a word, they’re likely to get beaten up at best, and maybe even killed for being a snitch or rat. So, inevitably, either this person throws someone else to the cops, or they get hauled in on some trumped-up charge themselves. Either way, the cops put them in a position where they believe there is nothing they can do about it. This happens all the time when LEPs’ contracts are up for renewal, and the company wants to pad its numbers by saying they had some kind of crackdown on crime.**

- **Legal Eagle**

**In national jurisdictions enforced by a LEP, cops absolutely do not have the right to abuse you once you’re in their custody, and they have a duty to protect you and provide for your well-being once they take you in. This means providing reasonable shelter, food, water, sanitation facilities, and ability to clean yourself. They must also make “every reasonable attempt” to identify you with a SIN, be it from a national jurisdiction, a corporate enclave, or even a criminal record from another place on the planet. If they cannot fnd any information on you, they will issue you a temporary SIN to be used while in their system. This is equivalent to a mugshot, and only exists within their system for a certain amount of time, usually about two months after your stay in their facilities, before it is required to be purged. This SIN only becomes a permanent criminal SIN should you be convicted of a crime in a court or fnd a way out of LEP custody and fail to comply with a warrant.**

**Additionally, as a SINless individual, you’re entitled to an arraignment and a trial, if things make it that far. This trial may or may not be in front of a jury, but the more severe the alleged crime, the more likely a jury will become involved.**

**You’re also entitled to name your legal counsel. You may think you can represent yourself effectively, but I would strongly recommend you don’t, for a couple of reasons. First, the world of law is a convoluted spiderweb of arcane language, precedents, rulings, and loopholes. Just wrapping your mind around these requires a specifc way of thinking most people aren’t prepared for. Even if you do have legal training, you likely don’t have a staff of paralegals and other attorneys to sift through**

**mountains of data in a holistic way that even an agent on a powerful host cannot effectively do on its own. Second, you don’t get** _**me**_ **. **Corporations take their secrets very seriously and consider all confdential company business as matters of national security.
