## **Changes Due to Magic**

**Probably the most impactful change for law enforcement in the Sixth World was the return of magic. The rank-and-fle trooper, armed with little more than a pistol, simply couldn’t deal with a criminal who could shoot lightning from their fngertips, turn invisible, or summon a sentient tornado. The only real response law enforcement had was to fght fre with fre.**

- **Along those lines, pest and animal control services also had to adapt, and do it fast. Chasing a couple of raccoons and a pack of stray dogs is not the same job as dealing with a piasma that’s accidentally venturing into the city limits.**

- **Moreau**

**As magic use became more common, every force attempted to recruit Awakened assets and establish divisions for magical operations. It quickly became apparent that there were simply not enough trained mages to go around. The talent was rare, and those who could wield the full range of magical abilities were even rarer. Trained full mages were being heavily recruited by universities and corporate research departments.**

**The solution ended up being the use of street mages with no formal training, but plenty of raw power. Sometimes they were “recruited” as a condition of parole or attracted with offers of on-the-job training set up by these new divisions. Those with less broad magical talents were also sought out and integrated into police forces. Aspected sorcerers, conjurers, and enchanters are far more common than full mages but were generally overlooked for magical research and corporate work. The new police divisions specializing in magic found that these individuals were just as skilled, or in many cases more skilled, in their specialty as more diversely talented mages.**

**Another pool of overlooked Awakened talent was also put to good use by police forces, a practice that continues today. These were astral Sensitives who could see into the astral world, and Explorers, able to project astrally. Neither of these Awakened types have any other magical abilities, but for police work these talents proved to be enormously useful. Explorers could be sent out like astral patrol units, watching for illegal magic use and freeing up full and aspected mages for other assignments. Sensitives were attached to patrol units to spot spell use or magically active suspects, as well as help mundane detectives in their investigations.**

**With the numbers of Awakened individuals on a force always on the low side, the practice of moving these offcers around from department to department became common. Magical assets are sent out wherever they are most needed and then quickly moved to the next job. In a typical day, an offcer may be sent to an investigation to collect astral impressions, back to the station to help interrogate a suspect with a spell, summon a spirit to track down a witness, move out with a SWAT team on a high threat response, then fnish the day with a few hours of astral patrol, body on a cot and their astral form roving the city.**

- **Burnout among Awakened officers is particularly high because of this constant overextension of abilities. In fact, some of the earliest known burnouts were among those first aspected sorcerers working for Lone Star’s DPI.**

- **Red**

- **To be fair, it’s not so easy to say “no” when you’re fully aware of how a single powerful spirit could decimate an entire SWAT team that went out without magical support.**

- **Winterhawk**

- **This is also why most forces keep a few Awakened runners on retainer (or enslaved after a free “get of jail” card), so they can use them (or sacrifice them) as a last resort when their own resources run out.**

- **Jimmy Kincaid**
