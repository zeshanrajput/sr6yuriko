## Conjunction

For a long time, Ares was only capable of staging raids on the Hive via two methods: remote probes and Inhabitation agents. We’ll cover the less disturbing first.

Probes are simple: program basic AIs to go through, scan and retrieve, then have a few with the data come back while others set off tactical nuclear devices to cover their tracks and do damage. They even had some novel designs incorporating reclaimed bug chitin to supposedly offer some measure of camouflage, though this was found to be ineffective, especially given that the bugs operate on a hive mind and a blank wandering in is neither part of your hive nor a member of the competition. The data they brought back was instrumental to the establishment of the Colony.

The second was a point of conjecture in some of the darker corners of metaplanar theory: that if a bug can inhabit a human, then a human can inhabit a bug. Ares, evidently, accomplished this. They have a number of agents who have effectively made a one-way trip into the Hive. If it’s a good merge, they’re deep cover agents. If it’s a hybrid, they’re the specialists, scouts, and most acclimated teammates to have.

Establishing the Colony was their first real beachhead for future forays. Using encounter suits specially designed to ward off the worst environmental effects, both super-mundane and evanescence, they could have agents posted longterm. This made protracted engagements more possible, even if it required a lot of work to keep the Colony hidden.

Now, though, there is a kind of fast-track to the Hive. A metaplanar conjunction, sort of an alignment of distant steps between here and there, makes it possible to reach, assuming you have the right directions. A standard journey would still be too long to survive, but through the right means, it’s possible, with or without a portal, to make an assisted journey to the Hive.
