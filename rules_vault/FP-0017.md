## **Denver Shakeup**

**While this is related to the Dis events described above, there is enough separation between the two to warrant its own entry. The overlap comes through efforts to manipulate the manasphere, but some of the work performed in Denver took things to a new level of nightmare.**

**We know that magic and technology often exist in separate spheres, but engineers in Denver found a fendish overlap. Spirits, once materialized, can be damaged through physical means, especially if they are constrained from de-materializing. Some corporations in Denver—and no one wants to claim responsibility for this—developed machines where spirits would be summoned, forced to materialize, and then slowly tortured. Why? So that their agony could be used to taint the nearby**

**manasphere. What’s more, they partnered with the notorious Null Sect to develop this tech.**

- **Remember that this was not solely a technological feat. No technology exists to summon and constrain spirits. Conjurers were still needed to bring the spirits to our plane and make them materialize. Then the machine took over.**

**> Jimmy No**

**Does torturing sprits merely to warp a little manasphere sound monstrous to you? Yeah, it should. And you would not be alone in that assessment—remember that torturing spirits was part of what got Horizon in trouble in Vegas several years ago. So the companies doing these experiments started rushing to avoid the gathering anger, and they cut some corners when developing a machine that would … wreak some sort of havoc, though the nature of the havoc is not clear to me. In the end, thanks to some runners, it wreaked the wrong sort of havoc. The good news was, a metaplanar rupture that might have swallowed the whole city was stopped. The bad news was, damage to the manasphere was not averted, and a lot of that fallout seems to have settled in the Aurora Warrens, because they certainly needed more problems there. There was also a whole lot of Matrix damage that the city has not recovered from yet.**

**Considerable harm was also done to Ghostwalker’s reputation. Murmurs had been spreading that Ghostwalker had been negligent since the loss of the spirit of Denver, and the fact that the Null Sect was able to set up this experiment in his city over the course of multiple months only heightened perceptions that the great dragon was slipping.**

**Ghostwalker has to fgure out how to deal with a damaged city and wounded population. His normal style of order through pain and intimidation would likely cause unrest to increase, so he is looking at ways of using actual diplomacy to win people over. If you want to earn a favor from a great dragon, there are opportunities in Denver—should you want to side with one of the more notorious members of dragonkind.**

**While these are far from the only events happening in the world, they’re some big ones that should provide some grounding for what you’re about to read. So plunge ahead and prepare to think about all the ways a world can fall apart.**
