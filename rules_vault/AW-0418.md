## Aztechnology Enclave

Roughly halfway between Hubris and the Yagmin enclave is a build site where employees from Aztechnology are working to construct a small fortress. Before you get excited, they don’t seem to be interested in bringing Stuffer Shack to the metaplanes. Unless you explicitly have business with them, you do not want to encounter these people. They are led by a group of blood mages, and the enclave is protected by blood panther adepts. Most of the workers here are prisoners who are kept in line with the threat of being sacrificed or sold as food to the bone traders. Even the ones who serve well are ultimately sacrificed one way or the other. Rumors carried by the Yaggil report that when the Aztechnology entourage arrived, they paid a visit to King Carnage—and made a deal with him. What the terms of that deal might be, or even whether or not a deal was reached, is unknown. What corroborates this rumor is that the Crimson Carnage do not bother the Aztechnology outpost—though they have been seen riding near it. No one wants to get close enough to find out what they’re actually up to, but whatever it is, it’s got to be something horrific.

The Yasmal tribe is allied with the Yaggil. Their enclave is far from Hubris, close to the glass deserts where the Kr’u’Ba are numerous. Their enclave has been built from salvaged ruins brought from Hubris by train. Outside its walls is a desolate plain, with a horizon broken only by the spare leafless tree or the skeletal skyscrapers of Hubris in the distance. The Yasmal are not as welcoming as the Yaggil. The region surrounding the Yasmal enclave is the most dangerous wilderness in Miggon, with marauding bands of Infected on one side and the hostile Kr’u’Ba on the other. If a visiting party wishes to gain entrance to the Yasmal enclave, someone from that party must pass a trial by combat. This is most often an unarmed contest against a decently strong kindred spirit, but surviving a ride on the Death Train or retrieving the glowing heart of a slain Kr’u’Ba are the most prestigious methods of proving your worth.

The Yasmal trade for much of their food from the Yaggil, offering foci, alchemical preparations, reagents, and mercenary services in return. The Yasmal are particularly skilled at alchemy, and the preparations they create do not deteriorate as long as they remain within Miggon. They quickly lose their potency in a matter of minutes once they are taken outside of Miggon. If you prove yourself to the Yasmal, any form of alchemical preparation can be purchased from their alchemists.
