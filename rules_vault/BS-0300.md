## Transient-Only Applications

**Etchers:** Only available as a transient colony, etchers are used to inscribe a metallic pattern, temporarily or permanently, on the subject’s bones. A typical use is to inscribe a criminal SIN or some other type of detectable identifier. These marks can be detected by magnetic anomaly detectors (MADs) and magnetic resonance imagers (MRIs). Temporary bone markings break up after a preset time, but permanent treatments can only be removed with surgery that scrapes the bone clean.

Once injected, a bone marker takes eight hours to form, and each point of nanite volume can cover 100 square millimeters of bone.

**Markers:** Another type of transient-only application, these nanites are basically microscopic optical chips used for transporting valuable data without being detected. Data is divided up and downloaded into multiple nanites, building in redundancy against nanite loss. Later, a simple blood sample is all that is needed to collect enough nanites to reconstitute the data within. Each point of nanite volume can carry the same data as a standard datachip. Markers are designed to defeat nanoscanners: increase a nanoscanner’s detection threshold by +2, in addition to the colony size modifier.

**Taggants:** Unlike other nanites, a taggant nanite colony emits a signal that can be detected by any standard wireless device. This produces an ARO and icon in the Matrix.
