## Hotspot

## Columbia University (Morningside)

Located in the New Harlem area. When the MDC re-built Manhattan, Harlem was leveled and completely rebuilt. Everything reflected the corporate-friendly community of the future. Some of the old sites remained, like Columbia University. However, while appearances were kept up, the façade started to crack. The university’s enrollment went down, and crime increased.

The government and schools wanted enrollment up, while the corporations wanted their “Teen Out” program to be successful. The program was designed to allow older teen corp kids some time alone. It was designed to give the illusion of responsibility while university personnel kept an eye on teenage kids. It’s a rare instance when everyone worked together and an area was cleaned up. Since then, the college has regained its top-rank status.

Security on campus is good. There are numerous

cameras, and many buildings have wards to stop cheating and other things college students may be tempted to do. All building’s main entrances have MAD scanners (rating 4), but they are easy to get around.

A basic Matrix search will show there is an extensive system of tunnels that connects many of the buildings—students have explored and entertained themselves in them for generations. They mainly house infrastructure needs such as water, steam, electrical, and Matrix conduits. Additionally, many have been cordoned off due to safety and other concerns, though many students still use them for a variety of activities.
