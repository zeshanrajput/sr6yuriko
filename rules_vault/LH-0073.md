## Informational Warfare

Since the beginning of this shadow war, we’ve been observing what could be the beginning of a paradigm shift in how Horizon operates as a corporation. Pretty much everyone in the Sixth World, for better or worse, equates Horizon with entertainment. And while other corporations have their own longstanding entertainment divisions, Horizon is still the default when it comes to things like sims, trid shows, social media, and more importantly for this war: news. It’s no surprise that the news outlets owned by pro-Disians have been busy suppressing or spinning information regarding anything Disian as well as hammering anti-Disian groups such as Grey Cell.

And of course, Horizon’s vast media empire has been countering these claims since they joined Project Nemesis, maybe even before—discussion for another time. The point is, Horizon has been smart about it. Going directly against pro-Disian outlets threatens their credibility and makes them look like they’re simply pushing tabloid headlines. Instead, Horizon has simply been keeping the conversation going and not pushing a counter-narrative, but one that says, “We know some of these things are a hoax, but what’s the angle, what are they _really_ hiding?”

This simple strategy has kept people more vested and interested in stories that normally would have already died, forcing the pro-Disians to constantly defend and suppress and therefore open themselves up for mistakes and “gotcha” moments. Wear them down until they do or say something stupid.

- It also plays against the credibility of those who are normally trusted voices. If they’re made to look like they’re in on it, or hiding something, it’s damaging and thus keeps the story going because now they become part of the story.

Basically, Horizon has been moving beyond mere entertainment and has started to tip their hand at what they’ve always been the master of: information manipulation and coercion. And if this comes as a shock to anyone, shame on you for not paying attention … but also remember it.

There’s another aspect to Horizon that needs to be mentioned: how they’ve pretty much become the backbone for Project Nemesis’s intelligence-gathering ops. Yeah, people leading the Defender Movement are experts in their fields, but creating an intricate, complex intelligence-gathering network in such a relatively short time is next to impossible.

That is, unless you already have a structure in place and all you need to do is add people. That’s the other thing Horizon has helped facilitate. Who else has the capability of connecting and coordinating all the information the individual cells and groups collect _and_ making sure it makes sense?

And the fact we know (next to) nothing about it is a testament of how well it’s working.
