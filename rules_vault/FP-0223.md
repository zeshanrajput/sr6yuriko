## **Aegis Cognito**

**Aegis both belongs in this category, and it doesn’t. This has to do with the differences between information and other commodities. Let’s compare it to a car. Two people cannot possess the same car, but they can possess the same piece of information. If I tell you about my car, you get the information about the car, but you don’t get the car itself. The information is far more transferable. The value also works differently if the item is copied. If I have a piece of information you don’t and I tell it to you, the piece of knowledge generally becomes less valuable.**

- **The “generally” is important. Corporate secrets and the like, yes, become less valuable as they’re spread. But information like “this trid series is coming out soon and is going to be awesome” becomes more valuable as it spreads. So secrets become less valuable, and hype becomes more. You may think it’s easy to tell the difference between the two, but it isn’t always.**

- **Dr. Spin**

**If, on the other hand, you get a copy of the car, then we both have the full value of the car (minus depreciation for using it, of course, but let’s not go overboard on adding variables). So while copying a car preserves value for both of us, copying information does not.**

**If the information is stolen, it still loses some of its value, since stealing it from someone does not mean they forget about it. If it’s especially detailed, like blueprints for a prototype, then it will retain much of its value. However, if it’s information on, say, who was behind the plot to kill Johnny Spinrad, then the information becomes less valuable,**

**because one more person knows it, but stealing a car does not affect the value of the car, unless you immediately crash it into a telephone pole or something.**

**All this is to say that sometimes Aegis sees the Tiding as increasing the value of their business, and sometimes decreasing it. On the plus side, Aegis operatives know that Tiding magpies will fnd all sorts of hidden information, and if they cultivate sources among the magpies, they’ll have a channel to some solid data. On the negative side, they could well be diluting the value of things Aegis knows, or doing to Aegis what Aegis is attempting to do to them. So the game of spy versus spy is in high gear, as agents try to cultivate each other without giving their counterpart any solid information.**

- **Which means they try to give them information that seems solid but is actually empty nonsense. That sort of thing tends to fill the shadows with empty conspiracy theories, so be on the lookout.**

- **Thorn**

**While Aegis does not think the Tiding is large enough to pose an existential threat to them, they’ve managed to take some large contracts that Aegis would have preferred to have. Aegis is not going to take that lying down, and that will play into some of the information they pass to Tiding magpies. If they can get them to bite on fake information, they will be quite pleased.**

- **Even if they don’t present fake information to a client, the time they have to spend confirming or debunking it is considered a minor win by Aegis.**
