## Fear

We sometimes act like fear is a relatively new persuasion technique of the corps, a sign of our decadent world, but it’s not. Fear-based advertising

is as old as advertising itself. In ancient Pompeii, cup manufacturers warned customers that if they used inferior cups, they might be drinking dust. That isn’t a major fear, of course, but it speaks to the major intention of fear-based advertising—if you do not use our product, you are opening yourself to suffering.

The nature of the looming suffering depends on the product being pushed. It could be the promise that if you buy the wrong sort of alcohol, people you are attracted to will not find you appealing, or the gentle threat that if you purchase the wrong automobile or gun, you’ll die. The corps continue to develop the art of the threat, promising you isolation, poverty, and imprisonment if you don’t conform to the way of life they are selling. The world they have built helps them be quite persuasive.
