## Dis

Residents of Dis are, of course, very interested in the portal staying open and news of their manasphere’s condition staying secret. True to their nature, they’re not going to set about this task directly. The outcomes they seek, in order of preference, are: 1) the runners are fooled by their ruse; 2) the runners are convinced to be sympathetic to their cause; 3) the runners are intimidated; 4) the runners are bought off. The nuances of these approaches are discussed in the Tactics section.

_Sends_ : Faces, then spirits

_Resources_ : The first two steps require some of Dis’ earthly allies, so they send two different faces (use Entertainer, p. 167, _Sixth World Companion_ for the first one, Policlub Member, p. 170. _Sixth World Companion_ for the second). When and if it comes time for an attack, Dis sends a pack of ten Force 4 spirits of beasts (p. 148, _SR6_ ) with instructions to kill the runners and destroy their physical forms and possessions. Additional Powers for each spirit can be chosen by the gamemaster to make the encounter interesting and challenging.

_Tactics_ : The first operative, a man named Finley Crane, poses as a Draco Foundation operative eager to hear what the PCs have learned about Dis. He is active and engaged through the whole conversation, and he’ll reference Coppernickel and the PCs’ interactions with him from the previous mission (p. 123). They quality of their interaction with Coppernickel should affect Finley’s attitude toward them, but even if things with Coppernickel ended badly, Finley will be determined to talk things out. When the discussion is over, Finley tells the PCs that he and his colleagues can take it from there. He assures them that with the help of the Astral Space Preservation Society, they’ll be well equipped to look into the issue. He thinks the conflict might start to get hot, though, so he advises the PCs to keep their distance from this point on.

If this doesn’t work and the PCs are still poking around after Dis, they send in their second face, a woman named Esme Cordarelle. She openly presents herself as an agent of Dis, and she tries to explain the position of her metaplane. She admits Dis is in a difficult spot, but she says that is caused by being caught between powers. She says the runners must understand what that’s like. She says they have found a path to fixing things, though—all

they need is time and to be left alone. She tells the PCs that they can just walk away, don’t tell anyone anything, and they will have helped Dis.

If Cordarelle fails, then it’s time for an attack, and the spirits of beasts described above ambush the PCs.

The final effort from Dis is a return visit from Cordarelle, who makes the offer described below.

_Offer_ : Esme Cordarelle offers the PCs 120,000 to destroy any evidence they have gathered about Dis.

_Conditions for Attack:_ As described above, and also the spirits of beasts will attack sometime after the PCs sell any information about Dis unless they cover their tracks very carefully. They will also respond to a good threat—if the PCs threaten to share all the data they have with MCT (as long as they have a solid contact whose name they are willing to share), then Dis will back down, knowing that MCT could cause them significant problems.

_Ways to Play off Other Factions:_ Disians in Manhattan very much do not want Arleesh and company alerted to their activities, and they have extra incentive to shut them down. If the PCs are making a deal with Arleesh, the Disians will get more aggressive and unleash the beast spirits. The PCs can also use the threat of going to Arleesh as a bargaining chip, getting Cordarelle to raise her offer to 135,000 nuyen.

Additionally, as long as the PCs are on good terms with Aztechnology representatives, the Dis beast spirits will not attack, as they hope Aztechnology will be able to smooth things over before violence becomes necessary.

_Aftermath Attack Risk:_ Moderate. Dis is moving on big plans and don’t want them stalled, but they also can’t necessarily take time to deal with every potential speed bump—some of them, they’ll just run over.
