## Epicenter of Disputes

During that first encounter with a minotoad, we learned that the Plane of Battle is not unlike our own, though you can hardly see it with all the mountains and ash. Beyond the mountains is the Astral Sea, but crossing the mountains is next to impossible. Spirits who arrive here find themselves trapped in the metaplane.

- While Korikyu may house prisoners from the adjacent metaplanes, they are not the dangerous criminal type. The Mist Queen is apparently capable of exiling them much further away.

- Wayfinder

- With Dis involvement, there are rumors of spirits exiled here for political reasons or as leverage.

- Magister

- What about people?

- Carlisle

- So far, we haven’t gotten stuck in the metaplane. I want to point out, however, that an incident with Queen Candelia some time ago got some corporate wagemages stuck here for a few hours until Sigh-on-Wind calmed her down.

- Canis



Spirits find that their material form is susceptible to injury and death. More distant metaplanar beings such as bugs are equally cut off from the Hive once they arrive. It may be that this metaplane occasionally comes close enough to the Hive for them to attempt an invasion.

- In light of the nature of the metaplane, it sounds more like a honeypot than a natural metaplane—something to keep the spirits farther out in check.

- Magister

In another mystical and ironic puzzle, a few free spirits who have been disrupted on the material plane have ended up here in servitude to Domitian at the Coliseum, which I’ll talk about later. Not sure if there was some devil/dweller contract for their freedom, but it doesn’t look like it was worth it.

- Shedim also are very unlikely to stop on the Plane of Battle, though they are not unheard of, while bugs are quite common.

- **>** Ethernaut

The minotoad also explained how they are indeed the native species of the Plane of Battle, but they can occasionally be found on other metaplanes. Some minotoads I’ve interacted with don’t believe they were originally native to the Plane of Battle but were brought there and then “nativized” somehow. I have also heard the opposite from some other minotoads—that they originated here and then were taken to other metaplanes and turned into natives there. Honestly, I’ve never met a minotoad anywhere but on the Plane of Battle, so the current hypothesis is that they are indeed one of the native species. Especially as no one, to my knowledge, has heard of any actual way to become a native being of a metaplane other than being born/created/spawned on it.

When asked about the constant battles in the valley, the minotoad informed us that some come for conquest, some come to settle disputes by duel (through the arbitration of Dweller on the Threshold), and some come because they are exiled here. All find out that death is real here.

The minotoads live in the mountains around the valley and rarely go into that area except for battles between their own tribes or to take on any invading force. This provides a huge amount of real estate where there is no real need to worry about collateral damage. The lava bombs also pose a unique challenge to non-native species, and alongside the constantly falling ash and red-dirtladen rain, any damage done to the terrain within the valley is soon erased by the natural environmental processes of the plane.
