## Hotspots

## Knickerbocker Social Club

This meeting place of New York’s movers and shakers is a peaceful respite in the heart of Times Square. The 1906 building used to be known as the Knickerbocker Hotel before the elite social club purchased the location and forever closed its doors to the public. The fourteen-story building retains much of its high-end charm and splendor, with multiple meeting hall and bar levels, including a rooftop bar overlooking Times Square. The sub-basement of the hotel still has tunnels leading directly into the old Times Square train station, which partially collapsed during the ’05 quake and was never reopened.

A full staff is always present at the hotel alongside an army of discreetly dressed security personnel. Rumors persist of at least half a dozen ghosts roaming the halls of the facility, though none have shown any aggression toward the guests.
