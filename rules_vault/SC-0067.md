## The Belford Sanction

This adventure hook works best with the professional and the operator backgrounds but is easily adapted to any team that is prepped for precision deletions.

A reliable fixer for wetwork has a job lined up: a well-protected member of high society in Europe has been marked for liquidation. The objective is straightforward, but the setting is not, with the target currently taking part in the Grand Tour. This month-long transnational pleasure trip is where the rich, famous, and influential rub shoulders in a bacchanal of high-society entertainment. The Tour will begin before the team can reach the target’s location, and the sanction must be completed before it ends. While the kill can be public or private, the latter is preferable, considering the extraordinary security which will be at play and the waves it will make. This is a headline-maker, and the pay reflects that, with travel and expenses fully paid and enough on top of that to keep the team mobile or give them time to cool off.

Depending on the season where this takes place, the Summer Tour passes through gambling in Monaco, movies in Cannes, orgies in Venice, and concludes with an extravagant masked ball in Paris (the last chance to make the kill). If it’s winter, the Tour begins in Paris, moving on to skiing in Alpine Tyrol and Salzburg, nights of debauchery and antiquity in Prague, and finally concluding with the Parisian Grand Ball, again with a masquerade theme. This provides a number of set pieces and opportunities for both the hit and complications. Use your imagination. The rich and elite have topflight security at their beck and call, but they often have a craving to slip the leash of their handlers and have some “real fun.” Poisons, strangulation,

BANSHEEsniping, organized natural disasters, equipment malfunction—there are myriad methods, but every failed attempt will be noted by security, adding an additional point of Edge to every security element against the team for future attempts. The worst twist of all is that the target is a vampire with membership in the Ordo Maximus. If they get pushed too far, they’ll call in cybermantic and magical aid that escalates the difficulty dramatically. The fact they are a vampire only adds to the challenge, and with their masking, it’s unlikely the runners will know until techniques like poison fail to take the target down. The first shot is the easiest. Make it count.
