## Richland Technologies

Posted by: Nuyen Flow

Strategically located between Nashville, Tennessee, and Huntsville, Alabama, this CAS company has a long history. First formed in 1970 by the Richland family, the company was sold off and reacquired by the original owner multiple times. No one from the local area really ever understood what the company or family did, but they always seemed to travel in circles that exceeded locals’ expectations.

The only thing rural communities like to talk about more than people failing, is talking about why someone is successful. As such, the company and family’s growth was not without its darker rumors, such as connections to the shadier echelons of society. Depending on the year, and which local you were talking to, Richland Technologies had served as a front for the Mafia, Vory, Yakuza, CAS government, or globe-spanning secret societies. These stories, though unproven, added a layer of intrigue to the family’s history; a whisper of danger that seemed at odds with their public persona as upstanding pillars of the community.

Despite these rumors, or perhaps because of them, Richland Technologies thrived. The company wasn’t just a normal business, it had woven itself into being in the very fiber of the community its founder had grown up in and wanted to serve. When the second generation took over, it grew and became a local landmark. Its operations were sprawling and diverse, including construction, robotics, infrastructure, and military-type projects.

That growth landed them on NeoNET’s radar. NeoNET used runners and other large corporations’ tactics to force the Richlands to sell the company. The founders’ son stayed under the NeoNET yoke, and though he never moved up the NeoNET ladder, he gained the respect of the NeoNET leadership and accompanied them on many merger and acquisition deals.

When the Boston Lockdown and its aftermath came to light, it was clear that Richland Technologies wasn’t part of the Novatech recovery and consolidation plan. The Richlands moved quickly and

bought as many of Richland Technologies’ assets as they could, including the name. So, on October 2, 2081, it was once again a family-owned business, and rumors started flying the very next day. This is when I became aware of them and started digging.

I can see where some of the previous rumors started. The family members who went back to Richland Technologies were all well-educated and have been seen by a lot of interesting people. The granddaughter of the founder always wears a ring made from some old Russian coin. She’s fluent in Russian and commonly seen with members of the different Vory. Her brother on the other hand is fluent in Japanese, wears an ornate ring with an oni, and deals with members of various Yakuza gumi. Their father seems to have a wide range of contacts that makes no sense to me whatsoever.
