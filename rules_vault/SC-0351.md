## FACE FATALE

The face fatale is a fixture of espionage thrillers and noir detective stories, and for good reason. History offers many examples of personalities who changed the course of nations or reshaped entire cultures with their presence and force of will. but there are equally commanding people who thrive in obscurity, wielding their powers of manipulation and persuasion with more precision—not on the masses, but against individuals. For the face fatale, personality and appearance are weapons every bit as effective as a street samurai’s blade or smartgun. Understimate them at your peril.

While any face can be charming, the face fatale specializes in seduction. It doesn’t have to be sexual, but one way or another, they always make their target bend to their will and dance to their tune.

The typical face is a master of connections and bargaining. The face fatale isn’t so blunt or commercial. Their focus is narrower, a precision blade compared to the utility tool used by most faces. When the face fatale is at work, you won’t know it unless they want you to. Their use of flattery,

appeal, and manipulation will have their targets thinking that their desires and the face’s requests are one and the same. The face fatale will plant the seed of a suggestion and let the mark cultivate it until they become the motivated one behind the idea, subtly steered in the right direction by the face fatale. The face will know a person who can provide the information the team needs, while the face fatale will know a person who can direct the team to the person who knows. The face will call in favors, but the face fatale will call in debts or dust off just the right blackmail. The two often cross paths, but they aren’t interchangeable.
