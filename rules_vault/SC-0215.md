## Plot Hooks

## Watch the Throne

A new leader has risen to the top of the local Reality Hackers gang. The Reality Hackers are a large gang, and leadership definitely has its privileges. That means plenty of people are interested in this leadership position, and just because someone has claimed the top position doesn’t mean all conflicts are settled. Rival gangers, both in and out of the gang, are set to levy a series of assaults designed to test the new leader’s mettle and depose them if they don’t measure up. The job is simple in concept—help defend the new leader against these waves of attacks. The devil, of course, is in the details. If they can make it through the weekend without leaving a designated location (roughly one city block on Reality Hacker turf), the new leader’s position is confirmed. Whatever that means. What it means to you is nuyen and some powerful—if chaotic and dangerous—contacts, if it works out.

The terms of the leader’s testing turn out to be pretty harsh, the stakes being no less than their survival. Rivals will attempt to kill the new boss. Surviving without leaving the area represents the ultimate proof of their right to be the boss. Since all of their fellow gangers represent a threat, they’ll need to rely on their own abilities and some outside help. And because the Reality Hackers are as much a hacker gang as they are cybered-up thrill killers, plenty of the warfare will be electronic in nature.

When the runners arrive, the gang boss greets them along with a rowdy crowd of Reality Hackers. After an hour or so of partying, the gangers all leave the block, and the runners have two hours of grace time before the real party begins. The rules are simple: don’t leave the block, and don’t die. There are a number of buildings within the block, including a reinforced ganger hangout with a bar, cameras, maglocks, a few turrets, and even some drones, giving the defensive decker plenty to work with. Besides the obvious option, there are also several other buildings to choose from, and even some hidden rooms and tunnels.

Once the runners have had time to prepare, they’ll come under attack almost immediately. One Reality Hacker decker for each runner shows up in the Matrix and starts trying to brick devices using Data Spike actions. They’ll stick around and keep trying to break stuff until they’re defeated. The second attack comes quickly on the heels of the first; it’s a bunch of gangers from a rival gangwho have been allowed to try to kill the new boss. These gangers don’t work well together, but there are a lot of them, and they’re heavily armed.

At some point later during the weekend, the runners will be contacted via Matrix message with an offer to walk away, or a bonus if they agree to kill the boss themselves. If they take this option, add +2 to the heat modifier and award 1 less Karma at the end of the run.

Otherwise, one last assault happens almost at the end of the weekend, just an hour before the cutoff time. One of the other Reality Hacker lieutenants who had been in competition for the leadership role decides to try very hard to take out the boss. A coordinated assault of Matrix attack (data spikes from personas in VR mode) along with a few of the most dangerous and violent Reality Hackers is the final challenge to the new leader’s status.

Assuming the runners and the new boss survive, each runners gains a new contact with the types Street and Matrix, with Connection 4 and Loyalty 4. This contact could represent the gang boss, but might also be someone else within the Reality Hackers that approves of the outcome. The Reality Hackers have a few street docs and plenty of illegal Matrix tech. Any runners that accept their pay in augmentations or electronics receive double the pay, but only if they take all gear instead of cash.
