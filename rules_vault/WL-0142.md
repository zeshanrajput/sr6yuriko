## **Volleying Porcupine**

## **(Hystrix Sagittarius)**

**What it doesn’t work against is a creature that isn’t trying to bite it, like a drunk cyclops. Let’s go there now.**

**It is not wise to get into a drinking contest with a cyclops when studying volleying porcupines in southern Greece.**

**We’re drinking. I’m drunk. I’m making stupid claims about the defensive skills of this species I’m studying. The cyclops I’m drinking with, George-something-opapopadopalos, says he has moved these critters with his bare hands. Now, I get he’s a troll, but come on, no one wants to carry one of these things for any length of time. I tell him that and he says, “Bet me!”**

**That said, let’s talk about the critters before I tell you this story of my idiocy. Volleying porcupines closely resemble their progenitor species (the North African crested porcupine) in general appearance. The average subject is a little over a meter long with another quarter meter of tail wagging along behind them. They usually weigh in at about ffteen kilos and have similarly adapted hairs that grow together into dense quills. The quills near the rear and tail usually reach a length of twenty-fve centimeters, with quills along the nape of the neck and ridge of the back growing to half a meter.**

**I’m a sucker for a good bet, and I say I’ll cover his tab for a week if he can carry a volleying porcupine ffty meters. Now, this is dumb on so many levels, not the smallest of which is that I’m putting some innocent animal through no small amount of terror to win a bet.**

**Quite the impressive mohawk.**

**The rest of the fur is generally coarse and dark brown, with some variation between a standard brown on the lighter side and black. Sightings of albino subjects have been reported and confrmed, but this is the typical genetic abnormality. As a unique variation, these subjects are often the target of poachers working for eccentric clients who desire rare animals for their menagerie of things to show they’re special. In other words, there’s more money in snagging albinos. There is still decent money in gathering the basic species, as security frms use them, as do ecological protection organizations when trying to prevent logging and construction in wild areas. They are easily trained and manipulated using salt licks, as they have an intense dietary need for salt.**

**Well, Mother Nature has an in with karma, and I got my comeuppance for this moronic choice.**

**Off to the woods we go, where I know of a den of volleying porcupines. It’s night time, so they are out and about. I send George after one.**

**It puffs up. George keeps coming.**

**It sends a volley his way. George keeps coming. It sprays George with musk. George keeps coming. It turns its quills to George. George reaches around its fanks and scoops it up.**

**The porcupine starts to panic and starts finging quills everywhere. George starts walking for his ffty meters.**

**I start getting pelted with quills. I’m drunk. I got no spells up. I got no armor on because I was just**
