## Police Procedure

How cops operate depends on where the crime was committed. NYPD prefers not to engage criminals in the middle of a crowded street. In places like Midtown, Downtown, and Times Square, they follow a policy of locate, track, terminate—this means they’ll tag you after you commit a crime instead of confronting you directly in the middle of a busy street. Instead, they’ll use drones or AR tracking software to follow you, and then kick the case to that precinct. Depending on the severity of the crime, the response can range from a couple of officers knocking on your door to a fully kitted-up SWAT unit blasting through your walls.

- This is another example of how they deal with jurisdictional issues. If a wanted criminal decides to hide out in, let’s say,



Renraku’s Arcology, NYPD will use the pressure of public opinion to grease the wheels of justice. They’ll turn to the MDC and cry about Renraku harboring dangerous felons. Renraku will save face by unleashing the Red Samurai on said felon, and then touting their response as an example of just how little shit they are willing to take.
