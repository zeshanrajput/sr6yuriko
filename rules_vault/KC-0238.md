## Wuxing

Someone once tried to explain shipping to me once, talking about how fragile it all is. They talked about piracy and canals. They told me that having a single port blocked could cause a huge ripple effect. It’s really fascinating because it reminds me so much of the Matrix. If some script-kiddy starts trying to hack you while you are on a job, your job is probably going to go sideways. If the host that has the data you need is currently being overloaded with requests, it’ll be easy to get in, but it’s also going to be really hard to get anything done. Which is really just a long-winded way of me telling you that Wuxing is very interested in getting its hands on Kechibi.

Using Kechibi, they would be able to find weaknesses in the global economy and exploit them. Being the largest shipping corp means that they could bring the whole world to a halt. Or maybe they are looking at ways to prevent it from being used against them? They could potentially affect product values by speeding up or slowing down delivery of goods—even by just a few minutes. That level of market manipulation in the shipping business could frag any company reliant on moving goods, which is an awful lot of them. Wuxing isn’t the only company in the shipping game, but we’ve already talked about using Kechibi to find holes in an enemy company, so when you try to

ship something through a competitor like Maersk, you find the port of call is full or the fuel delivery is delayed.

Their other claim to fame is in the financial markets, where they own a large number of subsidiary financial institutions and banks. They own everything from Fidelity Mutual to Malaysian Independent Bank to Albion Mutual before you even consider Wuxing Financial Services. The Kechibi Code is toxic sludge to any of these subsidiaries and could leave the market in chaos. According to someone who worked catering at the latest Wuxing Financial offsite, they were talking about pooling their resources to stomp down on any group finding a copy of the code. They even heard a couple of execs conspiring to prevent other parts of Wuxing from getting ahold of it. So if you’re on a run to get your hands on the code, I would be extra aware of the Dà Dì Xiao elite troops that will likely be hot on your trail.

- A contact of mine who wants to remain anonymous was recently hired by Wuxing for a run to get their hands on Kechibi. They were given a data vault to extract the code from. They did the run, pulled the data off the node they were told to hit, and as they were walking out the door, the Dà Dì Xiao hit them. The vault was the first thing shot; their decker was the second (aren’t you supposed to geek the mage first?). Anyway, my friend was the only one to make it out alive. Their current thinking is that their “Mr. Wu” was with Wuxing Financial, and it was a setup the whole time.
