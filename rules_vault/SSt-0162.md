## **A Cold Drink**

**If you didn’t prepare for it or just got extra unlucky, you might fnd yourself in a situation where the cop has caught you red-handed in the middle of breaking a law. Luckily, you’ve still got options.**

**The frst of these options is good ol’ bribery. Most cops are just as underpaid as any other wageslave and will be happy to take a little side income if they think they can get away with it. You might have to negotiate with the cop over price, but if you’ve got a certifed credstick handy, you can buy your way out of trouble. If you’re lucky, you might even get a friend in the department out of it.**

**The second option is intimidation. Like most people, cops don’t like danger. More importantly, their bosses don’t like danger, because danger costs nuyen (in the form of expended ammo, medical bills, etc.). If you make yourself scary enough to not be worth the trouble of trying to intervene, many cops will pretend they didn’t see anything. This is especially true of the cops who live for bullying others—like most bullies, they’ll back down and look for an easier target if they think you’ll put up a fght.**

- **”Not worth the trouble” is the key, here. If you seem dangerous but the officer still feels they have to act, they’re more likely to call HTR than back down.**

- **Thorn**

**Finally, if you have a plausible enough story, you can try to lie your way out. You probably won’t convince the cop that your actions are completely above-board, but that doesn’t matter if you can buy yourself enough time to fnish your objectives and leave.**

**If something goes wrong at this point, your options are to run, fght, or let yourself be arrested. It’s up to your team to decide whether you have a chance at getting away or winning a shootout. No matter which you choose, Offcer Schmuckatelli is**

**sure to use his most powerful weapon—his commlink—and summon a few of his friends to help.**

- **If you do decide to run, try hopping through a corp’s extraterritorial property, especially if you’re running from someone that corp doesn’t like—the Japanacorps and Wuxing, S-K and SpinGlobal, Aztechnology and basically anyone else. Most corpsec won’t risk a scrap with another corp if there’s a risk of fallout from their higher-ups. Even better, hide a second vehicle there and switch out to lose your tail.**

- **Turbo Bunny**

- **You can also fit your vehicle with an amphib drive system and head for the nearest body of water. Pursuers never expect someone to drive into the water on purpose, and it’ll give you cover from any aerial drones they scramble to follow you.**

- **> Sounder**

**If you get away, good for you. If not … well, I’ll turn things over to DangerSensei (a former lawyer, before he grew a conscience and became a shadowrunner) to tell you what happens next.**
