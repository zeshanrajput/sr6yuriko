## The Talismonger’s Vacation

This adventure hook works best with the alchemist background but works almost as well for any team with a talismonger contact.

The talismonger contact of the academic mage PC reaches out to them with a request to employ their entire team for a unique job. The talismonger needs to leave town on a mysterious and difficult venture, and they need someone to run the shop in their absence. They will only be gone for a week or two and have prepared everything to make it as simple as possible. If the runners agree, they will be paid in magical foci, reagents, alchemical preparations, or some other magical services if they wish, or they may take a smaller payment in the form of nuyen (each runner may choose their own payment or leave it for later). Besides running the shop, which requires fairly little effort—ringing up customers, fulfilling orders from carefully marked packages, tidying up, etc.—there are three vital tasks that need to be completed.

The first task is maintaining the carefully calibrated laboratory, which is in the process of transforming a number of alchemically treated precious metals into an even more precious metal: orichalcum. Every day, an Awakened character must spend at least one hour working in the laboratory and make a successful Enchanting + Logic (2) test. If they fail, the batch is ruined, and the talismonger’s Loyalty rating will be reduced by one.



The second vital task is making a delivery to an eccentric customer. This customer is an unusual Awakened sapient being who does not like metahumans very much (except perhaps as a meal). They might be a vampire, a free spirit, a dragon, or a paranoid retired shadowrunner. Getting to them requires traveling out into the wilderness where hostile critters are known to roam. Upon arrival, the recipient will be suspicious of anyone who is not the talismonger. A password has been provided, but the customer is likely to attack from ambush before allowing an explanation. None of this is anticipated by the talismonger, of course. There are only detailed instructions for where and when to make the delivery, and what password or phrase to use for identification.

The third task will be to harvest reagents on a specific day or night, during some special circumstances (under the light of a full moon, while chanting or singing a specific verse, during the hour just before dawn, etc.). Someone with the Enchanting skill must spend an hour gathering these reagents, but there will be an unanticipated complication. A local gang has claimed the area as their turf, a rival talismonger claims exclusive rights to the reagents there, some magicians are preparing to perform a ritual nearby and demand privacy, or a pack of hungry ghouls are out on a grocery run.

If the runners are successful in completing all three tasks in a reasonably satisfactory manner, the talismonger’s Loyalty and Connection ratings both improve by one. All of the runners may add the talismonger as a contact, with a Loyalty rating one lower than the academic mage’s.
