## Let’s Make a Deal or Two

## The Big Picture

The team is contacted for a meeting at Darkest Hour, a new hotspot on the edge of the formerblackout zone at Biddle and North Sixth Street. The meeting time is set just before sunset to allow the runners to see the drastic difference between the city of St. Louis inside and outside the Deep Blackout Core. It’s a typical invite to meet Mr. Johnson, delivered by a fixer the team knows well. At Darkest Hour, they will discover Mr. Johnson is a very stern female ork with short blonde hair and very expensive Armanté suit.

Mr. Johnson informs the runners they need them to act as middlemen for a rather valuable bit of paydata (a copy of the Kechibi Code, natch). They will be given information about the paydata and a selection of individuals Mr. Johnson feels can make maximum use of it. Mr. Johnson stipulates the runners are required to connect with all of the prospective buyers to get the best offer. She offers a simple reason: the chaos created by this data being out on the market and eventually in the right hands benefits their benefactor and allows that individual to utilize some of the chaos the runners are creating to gain a bit stronger footing in the nation.

While negotiations and making deals is their main job, Mr. Johnson warns that once word of their actions hits the streets they will be a target, so trouble could possibly find them. The payment offer is also unique and reflects well with this multi-faceted job. The leads given by Mr. Johnson have variable levels of authority, and each may need some help in order to get this offer up the chain. These jobs and the sale of the paydata file are the moneymakers for this operation. Mr. Johnson contracts the runners for the negotiations and offers them a twenty-five percent cut of the selling price as pay. Negotiating increases the cut by 2.5 percent per hit, up to six hits maximum, which would give them a forty percent cut. It’s another manipulation to get the runners to talk to all the possible buyers in order to get the best possible offer.

Once out on the streets, the runners need to set up meetings with the contacts Mr. Johnson provided. None of the contacts are capable of making the deal on their own, and they will all ask for some symbol of loyalty or honesty, sending the runners off on another job to test them.

All of this is part of the plan set forth by Mr. Johnson’s benefactor. Mr. Johnson works for the Sea Dragon. She is trying to locate a copy of the Kechibi Code, and she wants to see the chaos that will ensue by sending the code into the tumultuous beginnings of independent St. Louis. She plans to use this chaos to benefit her own efforts to keep working her way into control of the new city-state via the shadows.

For Mr. Johnson, the job is a unique opportunity to do something a little different. They get to bring in the team and offer them something runners rarely get: a choice. They get a chance to sway the future of St. Louis, though if the power behind

the scenes gets their way, the choice the runners makes is just a way to kick off a shadow war and give one side a distinct advantage over their rivals. The job is very open ended, as the team is given the file and a collection of choices for its sale but told no specific choice is preferred. They are given a lead in each group, but it’s not as simple as a Matrix call, a money transfer, and a file transfer. Nothing in the shadows ever is.
