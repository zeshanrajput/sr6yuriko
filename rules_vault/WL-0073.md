## **Posted by: G-Nome**

**chians. SouthAm is famous for the Andes. Australia felds the Great Dividing Range, Blue Mountains, Australian Alps** , **and Flinders Range** , **just to name** a few. Europe has the Alps, Caucasus, and Pyrenees. Asia has the monster Himalayas, as well as the Tian Shan, the Caucasus, the Urals, the Hindu Kush, the Western and Eastern Ghats, the Karakoram, and the Pamir Mountains. The vastness of Africa has the Atlas Simien, Rwenzori, Virungu, and Drakensberg mountains, to name a few. Antarctica has the Transantarctic Range running across the continent. Each of these ranges has both evolutionary parallel species as well as unique inhabitants of their own. Many of the paracritters of the regions are shaped by the local legends, and often research shows them connected in some way to mana-specifi c metaplanes.

**I absolutely love the beauty and variety of life in the peaks and valleys** , **but as any and everyone** here should know, or at least suspect, all those wild places are increasingly trying to kill you. Mountainous terrain is not just a danger based on the paracritters but also provides plenty of opportunities to get killed by physics, especially gravity and thermal energy (or lack thereof). The local wildlife, especially the paranormal variety, take advantage of these opportunities and the dangerous surroundings to enhance their nutrient supply.

While the mountains of the world are dangerous, it’s something far more mundane that usually causes signifi cant issues here: traveling. Due to altitude changes, soil variations, fl ora growth, lack of developed paths, and the general similarity of the surroundings, fi nding your way around the mountains is a unique challenge. For mapsoft addicts who fi nd it hard to locate the Stuffer Shack a quarter-kilometer away using commlink directions, the mountains could easily mean certain death. Rarely is the path to travel between two points in the mountains a straight line, and it in fact usually involves three planes of motion rather than the standard two.

The thing about mountains and mountainous environments is their history as something divisive. Whether it’s the physical divide between biomes, the political and social divide between nations, or the psychological divide created by their impenetrable visage, the mountainous regions of the world are commonly areas of contention. This psycho-social phenomenon deeply affects the legends and tall tales related to mountains, and in our Awakened world, this shapes the mana and the paranormal life that arises in the region.
