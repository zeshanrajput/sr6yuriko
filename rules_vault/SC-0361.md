## Roadblock

Things seem to be going the right way on the mission until suddenly someone isn’t doing what they are supposed to be doing. The team’s goals are incompatible with those of the face fatale, though not necessarily directly opposite. The face fatale won’t be directly involved with the characters, but rather with an important NPC or two. This could be one of the character’s contacts or loved ones, but it would work best with someone that is involved in the particular mission.

The face fatale’s target should be expected to act in a certain way. Perhaps it is a courier who delivers on a predictable route or a security guard who takes a long break at a certain time or a wageslave who will provide a password for a small fee. They need not be major players in the mission but should be someone who the players think is predictable and key to a successful outcome. When they go to execute their play, however, they are in for a surprise. The target doesn’t react as expected, and the team either needs to abort or regroup (though it shouldn’t be a major red flag to the players’ target).

They’ll need to figure out what went wrong, and if they observe the face fatale’s target, they should catch sight of them. Perhaps the courier takes random side trips to be with the face fatale, or the security guard skips her break to spend more time with the face fatale after work. In the end, the players should have a pretty good idea that they need to talk with this new influence. Confronting the face fatale should be fairly tame; neither party is worried about the other’s end goal, as they both just have uses for the poor pawn stuck in the middle. That target may be coy at first, but in the end both parties should be upfront with what they

need from the target, even if they’re more reluctant to share why they want that result.

This run-in with the face fatale need not end on a sour note. The face fatale isn’t going to just roll over for the runners, however. They will have to make it worth the face fatale’s while. Perhaps the players can work with them so that both their goals are met. Or they may just share the spoils of one mission. In fact, this could be an excellent way to introduce an NPC or new player character to the group. Should the PCs resort to violence, the face fatale will make a hasty retreat and do their best to become a bigger thorn in the players’ side, especially with how easily they can manipulate their target at this point. In the end, this encounter with the face fatale should shake up the characters’ plans but not cause a great hardship or challenge to the bigger picture.
