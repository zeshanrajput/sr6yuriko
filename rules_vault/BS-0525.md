## Nano-Critter Sensor Array

Nano-sensors can be installed in birds, rodents, insects, or other tiny critters, which tap into the critter’s natural senses and translate that information into electronic sensor data. These sensor arrays include a combination of visual, auditory, heat, and motion sensors, but they have a very small storage capacity and short range. Once per minute, they transmit the previous minute’s worth of recorded data to a specified host or device on the Matrix, after which that data is deleted from the nano-critter sensor array’s memory cache so that new data can be recorded in its place. A Noise rating of 2 or higher is enough to jam the sensor array and prevent it from transmitting any data, but it will continue to attempt to transmit its memory cache each minute. It deletes its stored data each minute whether or not the upload succeeds.

If there are multiple nano-critter sensor arrays within 100 meters of each other, they are more resistant to being jammed. If six or more critters are in range, a Noise rating of 3 or higher is required to jam them. If ten or more critters are in range, they will only be jammed if the Noise rating is 4 or higher.
