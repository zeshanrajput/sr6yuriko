## **Going Commando**

**No matter how good your frewall is, a determined technomancer is going to eventually fnd a way around it. “Eventually” probably means under ten seconds. But hopefully you get a chance to notice that someone is messing with your drones. When you do, you need to go to your contingency plan. Depending on the op, your contingency plans will vary, but they all use the same trick—command your drones to go commando, and by that, I mean wireless mode off. You’ll want to preload some autosofts on your drones ahead of time so they won’t be completely useless relying on their dog-brains, and you’ll need to load in some instructions for what they do when they shut off their connection to the Matrix. Keep it simple, because they won’t be very effective. The most common contingency I use is RTB—return to base. I set up a predefned rendezvous point (sometimes several different ones, so I don’t have multiple drones heading to the same location). Important safety tip: Don’t have them return directly to your physical location, as you risk leading an enemy right back to where they can geek you. Have them go somewhere relatively safe—and safely away from you—so you can recover them. I like to give my drones a preset command to come back online for a short period of time every hour or so, which gives me the option to add them right back to my RCC and recall them that way.**

**Some alternative examples from my personal experience include using a medevac drone with a maneuvering and stealth autosoft to recover a fallen comrade or extraction target (I’ve done both more than once)—this particular toy also had chameleon coating and passive stealth features. If I’d had it running active on the Matrix—even running silent—someone would have spotted and hacked it. If you ever need to extract someone, it’s a tried-and-true technique that I’d trademark if I could—call it the “Clockwork Pickup” if you ever use it, and send me some royalties through a fxer. I also like to use this mode to get combat support or recon drones into position. I’ll either time them to come online at a specifc moment or set them up to passively listen for a command over radio or a line-of-sight laser transmission when I don’t know exactly when they’ll be needed. Then I just add them back to my RCC and take control. This works great for ambushes as well as spying.**

**Going commando also works great when using a micro-drone as a bug, by the way. Plant a Gnat or Kanmushi on a target and use it to track them, but they’ll eventually be detected even if they’re running silent. Instead of leaving them online, command them to record all sensor data, then go offine— wireless off, but still active. Then use a randomized algorithm to determine how long they remain offine. Each time they come online, they power on for only a few seconds—just long enough to transmit their data back to you and await an abort or recall**
