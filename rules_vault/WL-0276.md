## **Icedrake**

## **(Neovaranus algoris articus)**

**These large lizards resemble a young western dragon without wings. They are excellent climbers and easily stalk everywhere, including rough mountainous terrain, preying on mountain sheep,**

**==> picture [19 x 765] intentionally omitted \<==**

**goats, elk, and the occasional polar bear that crosses their path. They are aggressive hunters and passionately defend their territory—territory that can change on a whim and expand into an area simply because the icedrake fnds a cave or food supply it likes.**

**Along with crushing jaws, razor sharp claws, and a tail whip that can knock a troll to the foor, the icedrake’s most deadly weapon is its cold aura. This power cools the air directly around the icedrake’s body, along with its skin temperature, to a crisp –100° C, which is about halfway to the temperature of liquid nitrogen. That’s unpleasant when engaged in melee with the creature but a death sentence when one falls into a pit trap created on the ice or when one gets cornered among the tunnels, ruins, and underground cities they frequently inhabit in both the North and South Poles.**

**A few rumors had spread claiming that specimens with the ability to expel a chilling breath exist, but I haven’t seen any examples on my previous expeditions. This would change here, as several specimens located in this region have evolved.**

**Near-death experience number two, as in “too fraggin’ many!” We weren’t even doing our research on icedrakes when we came upon a fresh kill scene. It was easy for us to fnd, as it included a handful of herbivore specimens we had already tagged for a study on herbivore population fuctuation in the region via migration, sickness, and predation.**

**With a big percentage of our herbivore subjects no longer viable, we scrapped that study and instead decided to look into the feeding habits of the icedrake. We reached a safe camp spot and set up our primary research station. We did not realize we had set the station up in the territory of an icedrake we named Sir Galahad. They wander a bit, but when it came back, we found the entire exterior surface of the tent covered in frozen urine.**

**After we moved, we discovered Galahad chose to expand his territory with our movement but did not take into account another nearby icedrake we named Merlin, the frst of the icedrakes we encountered with the chilling breath. We found ourselves in the midst of a territorial dispute that happened to occur simultaneously with a runner team coming in to try to capture Merlin.**

**Merlin and Sir Galahad quickly joined forces against their new foes, which somehow included the innocent research team that was simply trying to flm the debacle of man and nature’s interaction.**

**We did learn something about icedrake combat techniques when Sir Galahad and Merlin both went after me frst. Whether this was due to my Awakened nature or my size I was initially unsure, but after they had successfully immobilized me, they moved up the size chain. They focused their defensive efforts on avoiding bigger foes, while teaming up against smaller prey. The three of us**

**==> picture [246 x 35] intentionally omitted \<==**

**who were immobilized in ice were lucky that icedrakes prefer to start eating their food live, if a bit chilled. After the others chased off the pair of icedrakes, they were able to thaw us and help prevent too much tissue damage with a little arcane healing.**

**Icedrakes are no joke, and they seem to be getting smarter—some have been spotted operating in small groups. The social arrangements of those groups is a subject for a future study, but frst we need to do a little more research on countermeasures for that cold breath.**
