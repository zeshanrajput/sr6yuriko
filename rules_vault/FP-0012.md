## **The Kechibi Code**

**Every few years, the various automated tools stock market traders use in their work go haywire and cause a fash crash, causing markets to lose billions or even trillions of dollars in short order. Every time, the various authorities overseeing the markets, led by people who lost high proportions of those billions, vow to crack down on the use of automated tools. They do, but immediately the tool developers start making changes to conform to the new regulations while also giving the traders the functionality they want. And a new crash approaches.**

**The point is, some sort of automated trading will always be a part of the market because those who are involved in the market like it. They like the convenience, they like the fact that tools can pay attention to more than they can, and they like to feel that the right tool, properly deployed, can outsmart everyone.**

**Enter the Kechibi code. While the exact mechanics and coding of this thing are still in dispute, I’ve looked at enough observations from people in the know that I think I can say the basics of what it does.**

**The Kechibi code is a meta-automator. Rather than directly engaging in automated trading, it infuences the tools that do the trading. It has a lot of tools to do this—social media bots, fake news generators, tweaks to economic forecast tools, and more. Its special gift is an ability to home in on areas where moderated tools are especially sensitive, making them react quickly to whatever change it generates and then having other tools react to** _**that.**_ **The results are rapid and cascading, panics coming from directions most traders have not anticipated. Banks have been crashed, corporations have been bankrupted, and personal fortunes have been made and lost.**

**With the amount of manipulation the Kechibi code is capable of, more and more people are discovering it does not have to be limited to fnancial manipulation. Politics is a ripe feld for its efforts, as is any area where social reputation matters.**

- **Does this mean corporate executives are looking to use it against one another in the ongoing scramble for power? You bet your hoop it does.**

- **Cosmo**

**Another thing that has become clear is that the Kechibi code is extremely adaptable. There are no indications that it is anything close to sentient, but its programming is very capable of adapting to the circumstances it is placed in. This is especially true when those deploying it can tell it what to look out for—if it knows what kind of changes it might have to make in advance, it is better prepared to make them.**

**> Which means that even something as advanced as the Kechibi code benefits from a smart, sentient programmer who can prepare it properly.**
