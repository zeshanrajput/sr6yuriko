_**Michael Bishop was born Michael Brown on May 4, 2030, in the Richard Allen Holmes projects in Philadelphia. He stepped up at ten years old when his father stepped out, picking up odd jobs to help support his mother and two sisters. He found time to study on the side, which got him through junior high, but he dropped out of high school to take on more work when his younger sister became pregnant. Hustling through three jobs, he supported the family while getting his G.E.D., passing business and accounting college classes online. He was able to parlay this into a job with Ares at twenty, working his way up over the next decade through discipline and long hours. While he was unknown to most of the corporation, he was mentored by several higher execs who appreciated his work ethic. He was a likely candidate for an executive position when disaster struck Ares. Crash 2.0 devastated the company, with several executives killed in accidents when the Matrix collapsed, others erased from the system. General chaos erupted. Michael stepped up again, taking charge of the situation. He might not have had the position, but he projected the authority, and more importantly the ability, to take over. A week of grueling twenty-hour days followed. He slept in his offce, sending out handwritten letters while Matrix communications were down, and providing the steady hand needed to get the ship through the storm. By week’s end, Michael, who had introduced himself as Bishop when he frst walked into the offce, was accepted as the leader he’d demonstrated he was—missing paperwork be damned.**_

_**Three months later, Damien Knight came to visit him in Philadelphia’s Corvis Tower to have a face-to-face meeting. Knight was interested in Mr. Bishop’s claim that he was a “distant cousin” of Knight and had been sent by him to take charge. The meeting lasted an hour, and when it was over, they held a joint press conference, with Knight praising his cousin’s hard work and saying how much his stewardship had impressed him. Mr. Bishop had shown that Knight’s original confdence in him was well-placed, and Knight offcially gave him his stamp of approval. Mr. Bishop spent the next twenty years turning the Philadelphia branch into the best-run in UCAS division outside of Detroit, soon taking over all of the operations of Pennsylvania and having infuence on everything done in the Northeast. From there …**_

Like most things in this - world, it’s partially true. Right.**

**In truth, Bishop is one of us—he’s a shadowrunner. Or, I suppose, a former one. He started as a street hustler, but wanted more money, so he started going outside the law. With a heart full of ambition, a head for numbers, and a silver tongue, he became a face in short order, assembling small teams to work the Philly shadows when he was in his late teens. He quickly fgured out the risk-toreward ratio, and he switched gears to become a fxer. Three years of that, and a regular Johnson from Ares plucked him out of the streets to move into the corp towers. He got cover as a drone in accounting while learning the Mr. Johnson ropes, where he had a knack for getting runners on board that was counteracted by his unwillingness to go for betrayals. That softness kept him from rising as far as he might have gotten, but it got him loyalty and respect from the teams he hired. When the Crash went down, he was able to call in favors, get doors opened, and get himself into the big chair in Philly. That frst week, potential rivals found themselves out of records, out of money, or out of luck, and they either fell in line or fell off the grid. I don’t have any evidence of him removing players from the board for good, but you don’t get where he did without some blood on your hands.**
