## **>** Netcat

That would be the final unpleasantry, yes. But again, under ideal circumstances, detailed genetic analysis can be performed in less than twenty-four hours with the proper equipment. Nanite programming would take about an hour along with concocting the proper genetic mix for the transgenic upgrade that maximizes biocompatibility. Ideally, this should be a slow drip infusion over a period of hours while the subject is sedated. For more serious procedures, the patient may be put into a medically induced coma. After about aweek, a patient should be prancing around on their digitigrade legs.

But in poorer locations, patients will get an injection, a fistful of painkillers (I recommend slab for those who need to go comatose), and a kick in their hoop to get them out the door. Hopefully, those drugs will not mix poorly with the transgenic infusion. Upgrades at this level could include a couple of beds in a back-room clinic with restraints so patients don’t hurt themselves too much.
