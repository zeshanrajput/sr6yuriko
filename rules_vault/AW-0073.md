## Fire

The Metaplane of Fire is a dynamic land of heat and light. The terrain constantly churns with the varying viscosity of lava. Some of it boils like an ocean, other parts crust over to form islands. Some of the islands collide, forming volcano-like structures with glowing fountains of liquid heat. Others thicken into the ash wastes, where few permanent structures form. The sky is thick with haze and ash, illuminated by dancing auroras and flickering embers. Those unfamiliar with the metaplane can easily get lost on their own. The cacophony of sounds leads to many sleepless nights. To go from the near-silence of earth to the thrashing of fire is a shock to the system. First-time impression is that this is hell. This is incorrect. It’s more of a fastpaced, energized world.

Some of the flora and fauna have short lifespans, befitting the dynamics of the metaplane. A mote lives, breeds, and dies is a matter of seconds as the lava cools just enough for the eggs to hatch. A brief window of life, and all you can see is the brief flashes of their birth/death to know of their existence.

There are a few biomes that persist, if not in the same place or size all the time. The ash wastes are several square kilometers where the various energetic winds cancel each other out and release soot and ash. It can be a meter thick in places, collecting in black piles of anti-snow. The shard fields are the solidified remains of terrain after being blown around by a storm. It is like a frozen sea with razor-sharp waves. Shard fields can form when the liquid terrain is blown over cliffs, forming beautiful yet fragile bridges. And the forest groves of kindling spiral like twisted carbon snakes, growing dense enough to be a reprieve from the heat of the day. They also hold on to the ground longer from the random rivers of fire that cut through. Each tree bears one or two glowing fruits. They feel dense and heavy. Kindling put all their energy into the fruit over a long period of time; whenthe fruit is ripe, the old tree dies and the fruit falls to the ground, sinking deep into the pyria firma. Then in a great explosion, viscous fluid erupts and solidifies into a new kindling tree, ready to grow new fruit.

While there are few physical structures denoting settlements, there are still signs of civilization. Vast caravans travel across the metaplane. These range from the simple errhine-pulled wagons of the ardor to the behemoth infernal engines, burdened by chaotically lashed industrial colonies. They travel mostly from cardinal pole to cardinal pole, with the occasional meanderings of the ardor. The few structures are the empyrean keeps, monolithic structures of basalt housing small kingdoms. But while constituting a structure, even these are on the move, as the foundations float on rivers of lava with a multitude of banners to catch the thermal winds. The largest of these keeps is Oblimarch, a floating city of five thousand that follows the molten river that circles Cupellation.

- The exceptions to this are still the cardinal poles. They are fixed points in an ever-changing landscape, which is why they are so important in any trade or spiritual negotiations. To the spirits of fire, they are places of rest and celebration after traveling for so long and far.
