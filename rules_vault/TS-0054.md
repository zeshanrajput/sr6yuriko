## **Standard Issue**

**Like any job, the right know-how and gear can make the difference. It’s no different for anyone working a beat for a LEP. The specifcs may differ, but the training and gear largely remains the same across most LEP corporations.**

**First comes training. As I said before, most jurisdictions require any offcer in the employ of a LEP to be POST certifed. I say “offcer” here to differentiate between the actual cops and the civilian support staff who aren’t certifed peace offcers. These are the receptionists, feet mechanics, building security, dispatchers, and so on. These individuals can be POST certifed, but generally these positions are not required to have that certifcation. Although those in charge of one of these departments tend to be for various legal reasons.**

**Of course, there’s always an exception to the rule. POST is mainly a North American thing, but every nation has its own version of these certifcations. Regardless, this specifc type of certifcation is more or less expected, mostly because those places carry over at least a shadow of the law enforcement traditions and expectations from years past.**
