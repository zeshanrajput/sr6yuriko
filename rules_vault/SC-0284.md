## Parent Trap

This adventure hook is designed for the rich kid background, but it could be adapted to work for any well-rounded team of shadowrunners. A wealthy executive has been extracted, kidnapped along with their spouse from their own home, by a team of violent shadowrunners. The PCs are hired to find and retrieve the asset, with a lesser payment offered for eliminating them instead. If the drone rigger has the rich kid background, it turns out that the people they’ve been hired to rescue are their own parents. Otherwise, the targets are Mr. Johnson’s parents. Either way, they’ll need to pull off a rescue without letting the targets know that their family is involved in the biz.

As for finding the other runners, that won’t be too hard. The team is hiding out in a warehouse with their captives. They’ve been hired to sit on the targets for three days while they wait for further instructions. The PCs can track the runners down using some Matrix legwork, utilizing their contacts, or through whatever means they pursue. As long as it makes sense and the dice rolls go well enough, the PCs should be able to find the runners. The hard part is going to be getting the targets safely away from them. For the enemy runners, be sure to include a mage, a hacker, a drone rigger, and several combat specialists such as a street samurai or weapons expert. They should be a formidable threat that requires the PCs to come up with a creative approach. However it goes down, it’s bound to get messy. If the targets somehow realize who is behind their rescue, there could be other future complications. But if the run is successful, it pays well. The PCs also gain a megacorporate fixer (the person who hired the team) with the types Corporate and Government, Connection 5, Loyalty 1, and one favor each.

This adventure hook is designed for the omniscient background but works well for any team with surveillance drones or equivalent capabilities. The PCs are hired by a fixer with well-known ties to the entertainment industry. Their client is an entertainment agent at their wits’ end. Their most popular star has come under some dire threat, and they refuse to cooperate with their security team. This means the agent has turned to a fixer to hire a team of shadowrunners to provide overwatch and protection. They must keep track of the star at all times, but discreetly. If the star discovers they are being watched and protected, they will attempt to slip away. Surveillance drones and sensor devices are the ideal method, but the PCs must also be prepared to intervene in case of a threat. The nuyen payment for this job is significant, and the prospect of keeping tabs on a celeb might be of interest as well.

The discreet surveillance can be resolved with a few Perception and Stealth tests, with a threshold of 3. The chase rules for shadowing (p. 180, _Double Clutch_ ) could be a perfect fit for this scenario. The star will mostly stay within a security zone rating of A or better but will go slumming in a D zone once or twice.

At some point, the threat will appear. A team of runners, gangers, cultists, or appropriate antagonists will attempt to kidnap the star. If the PCs have succeeded in keeping track of the star without being noticed, they will be ready and aware of the attack. But if the star noticed their unwanted security detail, they’ll already be captured by the time the PCs notice, and the chase will be on!

If the PCs rescue the star, they are grateful. The PCs gain the star as a contact with the types Corporate and Media, Connection 6, Loyalty 1, and one favor each.
