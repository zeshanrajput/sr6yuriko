## Emergency

Within the last decade, awareness has grown that synthetic intelligence not only exists, but its numbers have exploded from the Big Three involved in Crash 2.0 to legions of more advanced but less omnipotent systems that are clearly connected to the Deep Resonance or Dissonance or

the Foundation or some combination of the three. And there are almost certainly some that are complete wildcards.

With awareness of AIs, the question of their motivation has only grown deeper. We often understand other humans even when we have strong disagreements because we know what they are pursuing. Perhaps they seek to obtain material goods, or power, or companionship, or other elements that often have a strong physical component. These desires are different for an AI—though as we have learned, not as different as we might have assumed. AIs will not fight a war to procure or preserve food supplies, but their existence can still be threatened, and they might still react out of fear of erasure. They might not conduct physically intimate relations as metahumans do, but they are still capable of forming deep emotional bonds and going to extreme lengths to preserve those bonds.

Every time a conflict with AIs emerges, we have to ask two questions: First, what do they want? And second, how confident are we that we will understand the first answer when we get it?
