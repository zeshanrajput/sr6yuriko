## An Offer You Can’t Refuse

The runners are provided with a limited map of the Knickerbocker club layout. Their map tells them what floor the safe and isolated computer are located on in the club and provides directions from the main area of the party to where they need to go. The runners will be directed to the thirdfloor hall, where a small party of about fifty welldressed people is dancing to the music of a string quartet. Following their map, the team will need to discreetly slip through the east doors and head down the corridor to a room at the far end.

When the runners walk into the room, a large, white-haired man is standing at the bar. Runners with Politics, Seattle People, Business Leaders, or similar Knowledge skill can make a Perception + Intuition (2) test to recognize him as Bellevue mayor and Centurion CEO Jonathan Blake, He welcomes them and calmly encourages them to have a drink. He says, “I arranged for you to meet me here this evening. I thought it was important to do it this way, so that you would appreciate the level of information I have about you and how easy it was to lure you into this situation. Knowledge is leverage in this town. You know something I don’t, and I’m interested in balancing the scales.”

Blake is bold, but he is not reckless. He has a six-man Seraphim security detail waiting steps away (use Seraphim Avenging Angel, p. 208, _SR6_ ). He doesn’t, however, reveal the existence of that team. He allows the runners to set the tone of the interaction up front. He iterates that they will be paid in full for the conversation and that it will be brief, because there are other matters that need to be addressed. He wants to know who the Mr. Johnson was who brought them to NY. He is merely looking to confirm this information, so if they lie, he will know. He uses their lie as an example to show them that he knows quite a bit about how and when they came to be running in NY, exactly what they were doing before they got there, as well as what they’ve been doing since they arrived. He appears confident and sympathetic to the situation they are presently in. He explains exactly how much danger they are in if they decide not to cooperate.

He explains that he manipulated Eager into believing the shadowrun was legitimate as a way to discreetly connect with the runners. After all, he suspected they might refuse his advances, and

that simply could not happen. The main reason he needs to speak to the runners is because a highly placed individual—higher even than Blake—also wants to speak to them. This individual has questions. He needs them to give specific answers to those questions, and he will tell them what to say.

He asks the runners a handful of questions. Once he’s satisfied with their answers, he informs them that he has sold them. Leased them, actually. Per arrangements with business associates of his, he has arranged for the runners to be delivered to the Empire State Building to answer questions from Evo board member and free spirit Buttercup and her associates. They will be asked about work they did and work they did not do that was attributed to them. He wants them to lie and take credit for a particular job, and he tells them specifically what to say.

Blake tells the runners to tell Buttercup that they were hired by a Mr. Johnson to take out a hacker going by the name “Double A.” If asked if they know who the Mr. Johnson worked for, they are to say, “Matrix Assets Inc.” Blake explains that he will have ears in the space and will know whether or not they comply with his request. Their pay is contingent on their completing the task.
