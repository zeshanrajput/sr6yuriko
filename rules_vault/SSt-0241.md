## **The Person Who Doesn’t Know**

## **Or, so you want to lead a double life!**

**In my experience, very few people get into this lifestyle deliberately. They stumble into it. Sometimes, they desperately need a place to live, and the only situation they can fnd that works at all is a roommate situation. Other times, they meet someone—maybe at a restaurant, maybe at a club, maybe somewhere else—and sparks fy, and things happen, and they think it will just be a one-night thing but then there are other nights, then they’re staying over longer, and the next thing you know they’ve moved in together, and they’ve never really talked together about who does what for a living.**

**The biggest thing you need is a cover story for how you get paid and why you disappear for odd periods of time. Consultant or executive coach are good options—job description can be vague, work ebbs and fows according to client needs, and sometimes you need to travel to be on site for a few days. Bone up on some of your corporate jargon, and you should be able to pass okay.**

**There are two things, though, that you’re going to need to come to terms with: You’re going to be putting people you care about in unwitting danger, and cracks are going to show in your façade. The frst one is just a matter of how much your ethical sense can handle. The second deserves a more detailed discussion.**

**We all know how intimidation works. Sometimes you have information but not enough to arrest someone. Other times you have plenty of information, but for various reasons you want someone nervous instead of arrested or imprisoned. So you have someone pay a visit to your target’s home. It’s especially fun to visit the person’s family when they’re not around, as it leaves both your target and their housemates feeling nervous, angry, and scared.**

**That’s going to happen at some point. Someone menacing will drop by your home and question the people who live there, and you’re going to need to explain what’s going on. You can call it a mix-up or something, and maybe that will work, until it happens again. And you have to explain it again. Then a person who you really want to trust you won’t trust you, for the very good reason that you’re lying to them. It’s a tough way to live, and I don’t like it.**

- **We’re all living a lie, usually more than one, so I don’t know what the big deal is here. You do what you need to do to survive and keep the things that are important to us. And let’s be honest, significant others lie to each other all the time. It’s what makes relationships work. So stop being so angsty over all this.**

- **Clarion**

- **Wow, even I think that’s messed up.**

- **Kane**
