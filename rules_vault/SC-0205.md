## decker fighter

## playing Advice

The challenge of playing a hybrid character is simple: You often feel like you are not as good at your tasks as specialist characters, so you feel you cannot out-fight a street samurai or out-hack a technomancer. That may be true, but it’s also the wrong way to look at it. You’re not trying to best people at their game—you’re trying to make them play yours. That means you hack the street samurai and punch the technomancer.

Your hacking skills may have trouble getting past top-flight firewalls, but only a small part of the world has that. Your targets should be the commlinks, cameras, building maintenance devices, and even drones and vehicles that are only loosely protected. You may not be hacking to get the most protected paydata, but you can hack to cause chaos. Use cameras to get a better understanding of your surroundings than your opponents, and use other devices to change the surroundings in ways others don’t expect. If anyone you’re going against hasn’t protected their weapon or cyberware enough, punish them. Shut things off, force them to reboot, and otherwise mess up their plans.

If another hacker is giving your team trouble, your job may not be to defeat them in cybercombat. Instead, delay them until you can bring your superior combat abilities to the fight. Laying them out with a swift punch neutralizes them as surely as bricking their cyberdeck, and it puts you in the arena where you might have the advantage.

Another way you can gain an advantage is focus. Don’t try to master every area of combat or every area of hacking. Pick where you want to be strong and dedicate yourself to it. For example, are you a brawler or a shooter? Street samurai can get away with being both, but you should focus on one. Pick a style and weapon you like and specialize in that area. The two bonus dice you get from using your preferred weapon can give a significant boost. Additionally, be cautious about branching into too many ancillary skills. Stealth can be nice for a fighter but getting enough ranks in it to be effective can curtail your growth in hacking and fighting. Use your hacking, not your physical movement, to help you get where you want to be without leaving a trace. Engineering can come in handy for some hackers, letting them take their mastery of equipment to a new level, but that might be one branch of the hacker skill tree that you don’t need.
