## Nanohives

The key to using the current generation of nanotechnology augmentations is the nanohive. These devices are cybernetic implants designed to create and control hard nanite colonies inside a living body. Unlike previous versions of the nanohive that simply supported a semi-autonomous colony of nanites, current nanohives contain the systems and software to control the operation of the various nanite colonies, as they will not function without external control—in the form of either an active nanohive or a commlink running a nanite pilot program.

A nanohive contains a standard neural interface, processors to run the colony templates and the pilot-like colony control systems, as well as a miniature nano-forge with a storage reservoir for the cocktail of precursor compounds required for nanite assembly. A nanohive can generate any of the different nanite colonies it has the program template for. They store excess nanite volume, repair nanites as they start to wear down, and salvage unwanted or unrepairable nanites—breaking them back down into base components. In addition to the primary nanohive module, a nanohive system also includes several cybernetic screens used to limit volume loss. A nanohive’s primary module is typically implanted as a single unit near arterial and lymphatic junctions, or it can also be installed into a cybertorso or a full cyberlimb. The primary module may also use a distributed construction to conceal its nature from cyberware scans.
