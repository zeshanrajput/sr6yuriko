## Elements

The first thing you need isn’t the coin, but the news, just like a real speculator. The difference is that you’re not digging quite so deep. Instead, you’re listening for the proverbial tom-toms in the street. Look at what’s sensational in the news, the kind of thing aggregators are pushing to the front of news screeds and headlines. You want your mark to have heard about the events taking place in relation to the currency. Wars, revolutions, anything that drums up the idea that a struggling economy’s wealth has dropped in executive function.

- Another nice touch are the various amateur vids and blogs posted talking about the currency, too. A lot of marks will look at noproof professionals’ testimonials and take it as insider knowledge, at least if you get enough of them in support of your con.

- **>** Dr. Spin

- And of course, there’s nothing stopping you from generating fake reports, yourself.

- Pistons

Then comes investment. You’re going to buy a bunch of this cheap money and handle the exchange cost as well. And possibly front the cost of the burner to do it, but that’s very little with all the smallfry anonymous trade companies you can sign up on. Some even have anonymity built-in as a feature, which should tell you how common this con can be.

Once you’ve got the material, you then need a customer.
