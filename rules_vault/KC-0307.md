## The Job

Kyrin Tannis was the president of facility management for the ARCHology when the blackout occurred. She lost a lot of corporate favor when she chose to leave the lights bright after the blackout, when regional management recommended she kill the lights to appear less of a target. She’s now looking for a win to bring to Villiers to earn not only her original lost face back but also move up in the corporation, taking the position of vice-president of St. Louis operations.

Tannis, also known as KT, is also a Monad, something her colleagues do not know. She has been struggling to maintain her own nanite supply and sees the runners’ need for access to her connections as a chance to send them on a little recovery job before agreeing to present an offer to her

LET’Speers. She offers 5,000 nuyen for a small recovery job. A Negotiations test increases the pay by 250 nuyen per hit, up to 8 hits.

The target is a tube in the offices of Noughten-Ames Best Life Clinic (NABLC), a small medical facility focusing on personal augmentations. The characters are given code number to identify the tube, but no other information is offered by KT. NABLC is a small clinic with a small-businessstyle name, but twelve layers up the paper trail it belongs to Evo. When entering, guests are presented a forty-seven-page electronic agreement to sign. Hidden within is Evo’s ownership and extraterritorial acceptance, though Evo’s logo is not in the document and the name is buried deep in the text. Any runner with an active commlink will have the document hit their AR feed, along with a request that they acknowledge reading it. Acknowledging it creates a link to the SIN on their commlink and likely burns the SIN. Any character with a Knowledge skill related to security will know this. Not acknowledging brings up a message requesting the character stop and wait for further assistance. It also alerts the office’s management, and the security officer on duty will be notified that this document wasn’t acknowledged. The message will also continue to pop up into the middle of the AR feed every ten seconds.

The coded tube is fifteen centimeters tall and six centimeters across with heavy seals on each end. The top seal has a four-digit code panel. The sides are clear and filled with an iridescent liquid that swirls around with the slightest jostling. This tube shares a chilled cabinet with three other similar tubes with different codes on them. The tubes have no other identifier as to what is inside the tubes. The office host for N-ABLC identifies these items as 420 cubic centimeters of unprogrammed nanomachine solution.

Security personnel at the facility consists of a single security officer (use Lone Star Patrolman, p. 206, _SR6_ ) from 8 a.m. to 8 p.m. During these hours, the clinic is staffed by eight individuals— usually two doctors, two admin staff, and four nurse/assistants. After hours, the security officer is not on site but is instead at home and able to log into the camera system if an alarm goes off.

The clinic is located at 3861 Gravois Avenue and has a small parking lot. The local McHugh’s across the road to the east and nearby Weapons World bring a lot of traffic. The front and rear door have maglocks (rating 4) with an ARO keypad accessible by touching a panel beside the door. Each has a camera (rating 3) that wirelessly feeds data to the building’s security host. Inside the rear door (the staff entrance) is another ARO-activating panel for the building’s security system. The front door has no such alarm panel and is unlocked during business hours.

Inside the front door is a small sitting area with a greeting window and a door to the back of the facility with a maglock (rating 3) released by a button at the greeting desk. The hallway behind this door has a window in one wall open to the greeter desk and a door into the office with the greeter desk and the office’s electronics system and device storage for the six medical commlinks used to share patient data. The hall leads to a check-in room with a scale and small desk with a chair.

Beyond this room is another hallway, parallel to the entrance with eight doors. The first two lead to restrooms, the next four doors are examination rooms, the last door on the right is storage (and the location of the target tube), and on the left is a small procedural room. The hall ends with the rear door of the facility.

The storage room has a maglock (rating 4) with another ARO keypad accessed by touching the panel next to the door. The storage room also contains a supply of drugs, dreamchips, simsense players, and trodes. Characters who look to steal other items can get 10,000 nuyen worth of goods to sell to a fence or street doc connection. This includes the three other tubes of nanites, which could be quickly sold to KT for 6,000 nuyen, leaving 4,000 nuyen of goods to fence.
