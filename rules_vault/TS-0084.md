**The evidence collected at a scene is the cornerstone of any investigation, hands down. Everything regarding a case always comes back to what the evidence proves.**

**Processing a crime scene is the most constraining and meticulous part of the job, and for some the most exciting. Each and every piece can be a revelation, a part in the greater picture. Which can also be a pain in the hoop because you don’t always know what that picture is going to look like until you collect all of it. And worse, you may not know when you’ve actually fnished collecting everything. The best you can do is make sure you cover as many of your bases as possible. That means the collection of all evidence, which includes physical, astral, and Matrix.**

**Regardless of the type of evidence, the procedure is the same. All elements must be identifed, itemized, collected, and secured. The last part, above all, is crucial. You need to be able to guarantee the integrity of the items collected by ensuring a chain of custody. A piece of evidence must never leave the control of an offcer before it is sealed to absolutely guarantee that it hasn’t been tampered with.**

- **Which is one of a defense attorney’s primary angles of attack. If an investigator claims they have solid evidence, challenge that notion. I’ve had evidence declared inadmissible because a first responder contaminated it, a crime scene tech didn’t properly secure it, or the chain of custody was broken (or at least called into question).**
