## The Job

As the PCs start the job, their work will be made more difficult by a few concerned citizens who were monitoring police activity and have decided to check out what’s going on. The runners will have to navigate through these gathered citizens as well as police, and they won’t be able to drive into the park. Skates, scooters, bikes, and other such options are allowed.

In the area of the still-played-upon statue of Alice, the Mad Hatter, and the Rabbit can also be found a trio of individuals in medical pajamas seemingly engaged in the tea service somehow, with a quartet of NYPD’s finest working to keep them in some semblance of order. These are not the only people around, however, and locating everyone who is part of this odd arrival from Neverland is part of the fun. There are no fewer than six individuals around in similar state, though the gamemaster is free to add a few if they choose. None of them seem to have their full awareness about them, wandering as if recovering from mind-altering drugs or magical aftershocks, perhaps both.

The initial three individuals found at the statue’s famous play area are all human, two women and a middle-aged man. The other three individuals found in the vicinity are a male ork who believes he is currently a puppy sniffing around a hundred meters or so away at a fountain. He will talk, but in a manner that is very cartoon-like, over-exaggerated and puppy-like. He may even be proudly lifting a leg to mark a spot of his territory. The fifth individual is a male elf of indeterminate age (probably in his forties) with crewcut hair and an air of inferiority. He acts as if he is everyone’s lapdog and will respond to questioning in a way that he feels will make them happiest. The sixth and final individual is a female valkyrie (p. 81, _Sixth World Companion_ ) who at first may be hard to spot as she has taken up a position surrounded by cherubs at a fountain, placing herself in the middle of their many water streams. She currently is unable to take flight because she believes as long as she is wet, she is safe from the damning flames all around her (note that those flames are not real).

The individuals can be as unruly and chaotic as the gamemaster desires. They can be difficult to pin down, running away from the PCs or helping each other escape if one of them is pinned down.

The questions the PCs need to answer are: Who are these people, how did they get to the park, and what is going on with their heads?

Here are some paths to getting information about the people:

**Talking to them** : These individuals do not remember their own names or addresses, but they have some vague memories of how they arrived

in the park. If the PCs succeed on an Influence (Etiquette, Intimidation, Leadership, or Negotiation) + Charisma vs. Willpower + Intuition test against two individuals, they will hear them share similar stories about feeling dazed in the back of a cargo van, then being pushed out into some bushes. They don’t know where the van originated.

If the PCs have run through mission 6, **Cutting Edges** (p. 56), they will recognize the woman by the Alice statue as Becky Wu Ping with a Memory (1) test. She is dirty, and her hair is now cut in a short, uneven style, but it is recognizably her. She will not remember the runners or anything about who she is. The PCs may attempt an Influence (Etiquette) + Charisma vs. Willpower + Intuition test against her, and if they are successful, she will suddenly notice the clothing they are wearing and start talking about it. The way she talks about lines and seams shows she knows fashion details and should make it clear that she is in fashion design. This can give teams that did not meet her previously a way to discover her identity and pave the way for mission 10, **Pulling Memories** (p. 64).

**Assensing them:** An Astral + Intuition (4) test on any of these individuals will show a very unsettled aura around their mental energies, something that is at odds with the rest of their aura, as if it were imposed, not a part of them.

**Medical testing:** A Biotech + Logic (3) test done with a medkit shows traces of sedatives in the individuals’ systems. Five hits also identify a psychoactive drug used to augment simsense experiences. Six hits (or a Perception + Intuition [4] test when looking at them) reveal that the individuals have odd markings on their skin—a circle inside a square inside a triangle inside a circle. The men have it near the base of their underarm cavity, while the women have it at the bottom of their hairline on their neck.
