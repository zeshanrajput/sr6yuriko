## **Interacting** with Critters

## Please Don’t Eat Me

## New Influence Skill Specialization/ Expertise: Critters

When you encounter a potentially hostile critter, you usually have only two options: run or fi ght. However, there is a third option: make peace. There are two possible approaches to this: intimidation or appeasement. Even a hungry carnivore will seek an easier meal if they are convinced that fi ghting you is too risky. If you happen to have a snack for them to eat instead of you—or something else they might want—they might reassess their decision to eviscerate you.

The Infl uence skill is used for social interactions with critters, including attempts to tame and train them. Critters are very different from metahumans (and other sapient entities), and not just because they don’t understand spoken languages. But that doesn’t mean they don’t communicate—body language, sounds, facial expressions, and scents are the primary means that most critters use for communication. Motivating, training, and forming bonds with critters, understanding how they think and feel, and anticipating how they may interpret your own actions and behaviors requires a different focus than interacting with metahumans.

Intimidation is the most straightforward approach, and it usually involves making loud noises and appearing as large and dangerous as possible. Unfortunately, there’s no way to do this quietly, so if you’re hoping to sneak past some guard dogs, you might want to choose an alternate approach.



**Make an Infuence (Intimidation or Critters) + Charisma test opposed by the critter’s Willpower + Intuition. For every net hit, the critter will back off for one combat round. If you score as many net hits as the critter’s Willpower attribute, the critter will leave you alone indefnitely. Any hostile action taken against a critter interrupts and overrides this—an intimidated critter is not a defenseless critter.**

**Appeasing a critter is the quieter and more friendly approach. In order to attempt to appease a critter, you must present it with something it wants—food is the most obvious choice here. If you don’t have anything to offer, you can only attempt this with the Instinctive Connection Edge action (p. xx). Make an Infuence (Critters) + Charisma test opposed by the critter’s Willpower + Intuition. If your offering is particularly appropriate (raw meat for a guard dog instead of a soydog, for example), gain a point of Edge on this test. For every net hit you score, the critter will tolerate your presence for one combat round. Gain a point of Edge on any further interactions with the critter that take place within the next hour. If you score as many net hits as the critter’s - Willpower attribute, they are appeased indef nitely. Any hostile action taken against the critter breaks this appeasement, making all further appeasement attempts impossible for at least twenty-four hours.**

**You may also attempt to appease a critter that is afraid and likely to fee. In this case, a successful appeasement attempt will result in the critter remaining for one combat round per net hit—and if you score as many net hits as the critter’s Willpower, it will remain nearby indefnitely and allow you to approach it.**

**Attempting to intimidate or appease a critter requires a Major Action.**
