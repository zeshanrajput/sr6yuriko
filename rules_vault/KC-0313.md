## The Job

Talia Rodrigo is Aztechnology’s top black ops coordinator in St. Louis and has been looking for a way to move up in the corp for years. She is a powerful blood mage with a decent reputation as a Mr. Johnson. She sees an opportunity to get some work done that will not only prove the runner’s skills but also clean up a job that is on her personal hit list. In fact, it is a hit. The target, Conrad Hillson, doesn’t seem at all special on the surface. The runners will need to do a little digging to figure out why they are being sent after Hillson. They don’t _need_ this information, they really just need to pull the trigger, but runners often dig up that kind of dirt to soothe their moral bruises.

Conrad Hillson is a St. Louis aristocrat. He’s old money and lots of it. His family’s fortune has recently been consolidated and left to him after a series of unfortunate events took the lives of several Hillson family members. Investigations are still underway, which has Conrad restricted to St. Louis, making the runners’ job a little easier.

The easiest way to follow Hillson or know where he will be is to subscribe to his P2.0 feed. He has an announcement that he will be attending an avant-garde art show in a new club just inside the edge of the blackout zone near Biddle and North Sixth Street. It offers lessened security but still the threat of a nearby response. Yes, that address is

The club, Darkest Hour, is only a few weeks old but it has been pulling in a lot of big names. The show Hillson is attending is one of the nights shortly after the runners are hired and features the living artwork of Q Life. The art ranges from painted and decorated metahumans to tanks of genetically modified fish.

The bar itself is themed in black. The uniforms, walls, floors, doors, tables, etc. are all black. Each is a slightly different level of reflectiveness, and most of the lighting is the rich purple of UV blacklights. During the show, spotlights are used on several of the exhibits, but the light is kept narrow, leaving small pockets of lights. The layout of the bar is very open during the show. Beside the door is a coat and arms check room. Beyond that is an open square with booths lining three of the four walls, with the fourth wall containing the bar and a hallway back to the restrooms, storage room, and a door to the stairs down to the business office in the basement. A rear door is present at the end of this hallway, leading to a small outdoor sitting area. This area is currently partially filled with the tables from the bar that were moved out to make room for the show. This back seating area also houses the small shack where four generators are currently running to provide power to the club.

The event guest list is small and contained on the commlink (an Erika Elite) of the doorman. Blending into the scene requires high-end social skills and dress up, or the runners will be made as fakes (Influence [Etiquette] + Charisma (5) test; this can be done as a teamwork test, with success on the test covering the entire team). If they are seen as fakes they are ostracized and everyone makes small but visible gestures in their directions while they talk before getting in close and laughing with a fellow guest.

Outside the club is a typical urban street. Darkest Hour sits on the north side of Biddle between Sixth and Seventh Street. The club is only one story, though it is a tall single story. The buildings to the east and west are three stories, with the first floor as a retail space and the upper two set up as residences. The buildings across the street are four stories with a similar setup of retail at street level and residences above. This is the general set up for the blocks bordered by O’Fallon, Carr, Broadway, and Seventh Streets. A series of alleys separate the buildings, and all of them are built with fire escape stairwells in the alleys, rooftop access for maintenance, internal stairwells, and elevators.

Most vehicles for those attending the party are driven a few blocks away to park outside the blackout zone and away from trouble. A handful are parked along the nearby streets. They are a mix of high-end sports cars and luxury vehicles.
