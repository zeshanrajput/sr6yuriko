## Why Visit a Metaplane?

There are many reasons why people decide to visit the metaplanes. Magicians became aware of the existence of metaplanes almost from the very moment magic returned to our world. The most common reason for visiting the metaplanes is to gain a deeper understanding of magic. The earliest metaplanar travelers were magicians attempting to initiate, either guided there through study and meditation or with the help of their mentor spirit. Many hermetic magicians of that time believed that metaplanes existed within their own minds— elaborate thought constructs that existed only to express the deeper mysteries of their own subconscious. These hermetic wizards have even gone so far as to believe that the spirits they called from these metaplanes were mere extensions of their own consciousness. Oh, the hubris! Magicians of most other traditions were able to conceive of the metaplanes as other worlds, and some of these understood that the spirits summoned from the metaplanes are sapient entities worthy of respect and fair treatment.

While astral quests might be the first, and most common, reason that some risk travel to the metaplanes, it is not the only one by far. Another reason is to deal with a spirit. Most spirits that you are likely to encounter on the physical plane have been summoned or bound by a metahuman magician. Their will is not completely their own, for they have been compelled to perform services by the one that called them. Free spirits—spirits who are not bound to a magician and are capable of acting fully on their own—are rare in our world. On the



metaplanes, they are the norm. What does that say about the ethics of summoning? Many magicians dodge this question, even going so far as to deny that the spirits they summon are sapient—hermetics are especially egregious offenders in this manner, as many claim that spirits are created by their own minds. I get along well with very few hermetic magicians. And I have found that many shamans are just as likely to resent or dodge my question, citing the mysterious nature of magic or appeasing their conscience with offerings of reagents to the spirits they summon. This might seem to you to be a digression, but it is not. Spirits are sapient entities who call the metaplanes their home. They are tied to their home planes, and most never leave them.

Many of the entities encountered on your metaplanar travels will be spirits. Spending some time contemplating the nature of spirits is exactly what I meant when I said that both knowledge and humility are the two keys to surviving metaplanar travel.

Because spirits come from the metaplanes, and because they are mysteriously linked to their home plane, one of the most common reasons to travel to the metaplanes is to deal with spirits—one way or another. If you have the misfortune of making an enemy of a specific spirit, you will likely need to travel to its home metaplane in order to permanently resolve the conflict. Every spirit has a true name that can’t be expressed verbally or using text. A spirit’s true name is a collection of symbols and ideas that define their origin and nature. A biologist might compare a true name to a living creature’s DNA. A computer programmer may think of it as source code. Both analogies are accurate enough to help us understand what a true name means—and what can be done with it. Most spirits consider the circumstance of having anyone know their true name to be as disastrous as a dirty politician would feel about having proof of their guilt in the hands of blackmailers and extortionists. A powerful magician can use a spirit’s true name to summon them, compel them, and bind them to their will. They can also use the spirit’s true name to fundamentally change the spirit in some way, or even destroy them permanently. For when a spirit dies, it is almost never truly dead—only disrupted for a time, after which it will recover on its home metaplane (usually). If a free spirit has decided to kill you, the only hope that you have of permanently destroying it is by learning its true name and using that true name to destroy it on its home metaplane. Otherwise you can expect your spiritual enemies to return to haunt you again (roughly a month after you banish or disrupt them).

Dealing with spirits does not have to be a hostile endeavor, though. You might choose to travel to a metaplane to meet with a spirit in a much more cordial manner. Spirits have access to knowledge that can only be learned by speaking to them. Some of them can conjure up items of value—such



as rare gems—as easily as a magician might cast a spell. Others might even be willing to enter a binding arrangement with metahumans in exchange for providing specific services. Very few are willing to bind their life to a metahuman in a way that makes them vulnerable, but some have made deals to preserve someone’s life through magical means that we might never come to understand. This is the source of myths of ancient wizards living through impossible dangers by hiding their lifeforce within an object, like a lich with a phylactery or horcrux. In my experience, spirits who have made such a deal have learned to regret it.

One of the easiest services to gain from dealing with a spirit is finding a guide. This is the most common deal that I make with spirits, and I highly recommend that you find a local guide if you intend to explore a metaplane. It will cost you dearly, but not so dearly as the consequences brought upon by your own ignorance. As for payment, you might offer your services in return, but most spirits seem interested in taking a small part of you in return. There are many terms for this nebulous currency, but the most popular term in use is _Karma._ For those of you that focus on material wealth and physical rewards, it might seem like a small thing to give up. Magicians tend to consider this a very steep cost. To the skeptics out there who might consider this to be a whimsical and unimportant loss, I say to keep an open mind and avoid giving up too much. It might seem to you like you’re getting something for free by agreeing to pay in an imaginary currency, but keep in mind my advice about knowledge and humility. If a spirit takes something seriously—and you can bet they take the possibility of gaining Karma very seriously—you can bet that it’s important and valuable.

When a magician travels to the metaplanes by projecting their astral form there, they are not capable of bringing anything back with them (except knowledge, of course). If you travel to the metaplanes physically, without leaving your body behind, you can bring items and objects back with you. The metaplanes are magical places, which makes them a perfect place to go looking for reagents, magical artifacts, and even veins of orichalcum. Spirits are unlikely to allow someone to take things freely, and some of the most powerful or valuable things you’re likely to find will be well-guarded, which partly explains why massive corporate reagent and orichalcum strip-mining operations have taken so long to establish themselves and are not yet widespread. Some metaplanar travelers treat the metaplanes with the same respect a conservationist might apply when encountering an untouched wilderness, but most seem to think that the metaplanes exist only to be exploited for their abundant resources.

The most unusual reason why a metahuman might choose to visit the metaplanes is to travel

and explore. The metaplanes are full of strange sights, weird phenomena, and alien entities. The physical plane has been mapped, catalogued, and scoured for resources. A traveler who hopes to see the world needs clearance to cross borders, and mountains of nuyen for travel expenses and other necessities, and all they can hope to experience is another place already inhabited by other metahumans, all involved in the daily struggle to make enough money to live another day. It’s dreary, depressing, downright boring, and worst of all, it’s commodified so that it can be packaged and sold to you. To someone like me, travel in the physical world is an expedition filled with banal horrors. But I can travel to the metaplanes for free, either by projecting my astral form there, or (more commonly these days) using a gate. There are costs to be paid, but nuyen has little value. I am rich in magical power. I can create my own Karma (did you know that everyone can?). I can choose where to go and why and find things there that no one has ever conceived of, let alone built a gift shop next to. I seek and explore, and sometimes even go where no metahuman has gone before. Imagine the pleasure of encountering something strange and new, with the knowledge that you are the first person to experience it. This is what drives me to travel the metaplanes. To gain knowledge, and to experience and understand more of these worlds that are so mysteriously connected to our own.

There are likely more reasons to visit the metaplanes than I am capable of imagining, but only one more comes to mind: using physical transference passageways to travel from one physical location to another. This is incredibly rare because there are very few known reliable metaplanar passageways around the world—places where you can be certain of physically entering or exiting a metaplane. Shortly after the Awakening, there were almost none of these stable metaplanar passageways, but as the mana levels rise and more magicians experiment with powers they barely understand, they have become somewhat less rare. Most of these portals were either created or at least quickly claimed by the megacorporations or very powerful magical groups, who then restrict access to these passageways except for their own uses. Some free spirits offer their own abilities to create gateways connecting their home metaplane to the physical world in return for Karma. These gateways open directly to a metaplane, and you must travel through that metaplane to get to another passageway in order to return to the physical world. Even if you have a spirit with the metaplanar gateway power on your side, you can’t simply step into the metaplane, make another gate to where you want to go, and then step out somewhere else on the physical plane—it doesn’t work that way. Metaplanes seem to be linked to the physical plane in some way, and if you want to



get somewhere specific, you’ll have to find the right spot for where your spirit should make a gateway. Finding the right spot can be very tricky (or perhaps even impossible, if the metaplanar links don’t exist). If you try this, I highly recommend learning a thing or two about astral geography, geomancy, and divination. All of these factors make using the metaplanes as a means to travel to another physical location an inefficient and unpopular method of conveyance. As metahumanity finds more gates and encroaches further into the metaplanes, it will likely become more common—but don’t expect it to affect suborbital ticket sales. Metaplanar travel is always risky, and carries with it a great deal of unpredictability. It will likely always be something that is only undertaken by the foolish, desperate, or greedy.
