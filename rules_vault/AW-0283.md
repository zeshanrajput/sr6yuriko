## Second Hunt

It took a few days to arrange and involved the in-laws, but eventually I found time to go back to the Citadel. Only this time I had backup: Covenant, Exile, and Torch all agreed to come with. When we arrived, I explained the technique I’d devised to get out of the tower and then back in to go home again. Then we headed off into the keep.

With the additional firepower, we were able to get through the golems with ease. The traps were another thing, as while we had read up on archaic traps and mechanical devices designed to incapacitate or kill, they were still a problem—none of us were experts at the time. Due to repeated practice, we’ve all become quite proficient at trap finding, but that first trip was an entirely different matter.

- I’ll say! Nearly lost an arm that first trip to the Citadel. We didn’t see a swinging blade trap in time, but thankfully dad was able to body check me out of the way. A heavily bruised and partially dislocated shoulder is far better than losing an arm.

- Torch

- Knowing about ancient booby traps has been a lifesaver on so many occasions. I’m shocked more runners don’t take the time to learn about them. 5/5, would recommend.

- Kalbruin

During my previous visits, I’d not entered a lot of the rooms in the keep. Some because of traps, some because of golems, but mainly because I wasn’t sure I needed to at the time. Now that there were four of us, we explored all of the rooms fully. This brought us into conflict with golems even more than just prowling the passageways (it was after one of those fights that Exile identified the supposedly brass golems as orichalcum golems), which gave us the true purpose of the barracks that seemed to not shift locations. It wasn’t just a barracks or room to store golems in—it was actually spawning them. Like IC in a host, there was a set sequence, but then as they were destroyed, they would come back in the order we had destroyed them. An interesting turn of events, one that I’ve not seen on any other metaplane, and one that implies the golems aren’t native to the Citadel.

Without anywhere else to look for anything interesting, we decided to take the large stairwell down into the basement. We walked down that huge spiral staircase for a good ten minutes before we came to a large door that was guarded by two steel golems, two silver golems, an obsidian golem, and an orichalcum golem.

Torch and I dove straight into the pack of golems, while the other two hung back a little and supported us from the stairwell. It was a very tough fight, as I was finally able to witness the defensive sorcery capabilities of the orichalcum golem. They’re potent, knowing the Shielding and Reflection techniques. But even on my best day, I don’t think I could use them as often as that golem was—it was almost as if they were innate abilities that didn’t cause as much drain. Don’t get me wrong, our combat spells were landing. Just not as well as before, and we would need to be mindful of them being reflected back at us.
