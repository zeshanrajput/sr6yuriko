## **(Vulpes toxicus)**

**The regal white fur of the arctic fox is so beautiful among the pristine snows of the north. Too bad that’s also the twisted case of the copper-andgreen tones of the sickly foxes I was studying near the former MCT dig-turned-strip-mine called Red Chris up in Athabaskan Council lands.**

**They weren’t quite ready to head out on their own, but if a few of the larger siblings turned on the runts, they’d certainly bulk up to the big boy fying league. As you’d expect, I ditched after I got some good notes and footage. It had nothing to do with one of those aforementioned runts catching my scent and doing this hideous crawl movement over to me while I was jotting down height counts.**

**I went expecting this to be an Awakened subspecies of the arctic fox, similar to other species I’ve studied. Instead, though, the sickly fox, as its name implies, has been corrupted by the pollution of its habitat in both the physical and astral planes. The bulk of the pollution came in the form of sulfde-ore mining in the early years, but when MCT came in, they went full strip, which made the initial ecological damage look like a leaky oil pan on an old petrol Viper. The stains of red ponds became conical craters with narrow strips of land between. Many of those have collapsed over time or sit sunken and ready to collapse at any moment.**
