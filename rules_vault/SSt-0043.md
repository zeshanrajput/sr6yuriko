## **Who You Know**

**Contacts are the face’s most important resources. Street samurai have augmentations and weapons, mages and adepts have their magic, riggers and deckers have their tech, and your greatest assets are the people you know. Your contacts are highly useful for gathering information, acquiring equipment, watching out for you, and providing the skills and services that your team lacks but needs for a specifc job. From your very frst run, you should be leaning on the contacts you have as much as possible while also looking for opportunities to expand and develop your network for the future.**

**Contacts come in a wide range of types, and their uses are as varied as their occupations. I like to think of them in terms of categories that defne their potential uses: academic, corporate, criminal, engineering, government, magic, Matrix, media, medical, and street. While some contacts might easily ft into one of these categories, many of them are less easily defned, or are better described as a mix of two or more contact types. The ones who solidly fall into a single category tend to be specialists who are particularly well-versed in one feld, while contacts who ft two or more categories have a wider range of knowledge, experience, and available services. It’s a good idea to cultivate a network of contacts that covers all of the categories if you can, because they’re all going to come in handy eventually.**

**Academic contacts are primarily useful when it comes to fnding out information on a topic that fts their area of expertise. This information tends to be related to an established feld of knowledge rather than current situations, though there are plenty of exceptions to this. In some cases they might be able to provide you with useful intel that relates to the location where they work, though you will want to be very careful not to implicate them in your illicit activities during your run. If you have been hired to carry out a run that relates to their feld, reaching out to an academic contact can help you put together a better picture of the greater context that your run fts into. If you’ve**

**acquired a strange object such as an artifact or prototype, an academic contact can help you fgure out what it does and what that means. Accountants also fall within this category, and they can help you parse out useful information from fnancial documents that you would otherwise easily miss. Lawyers can also be useful for their legal advice and services, but it’s probably best to avoid the courtroom as much as possible in your line of work. Understanding the complexities of megacorporate and government jurisdiction can sometimes be very useful, however.**

**Corporate contacts are individuals who work for corporations. They are useful for fnding out what is going on in the corporate world, particularly as it relates to recent or current events. They are also likely to have up-to-date knowledge of corporate culture. If you are going to be attending a social event as part of a run, it’s a good idea to reach out to your corporate contacts to bring yourself up to speed on corporate culture, as well as put together a few useful talking points that will help you blend in more successfully. Some corporate contacts will also be able to provide you and your team equipment produced by their employer. An Ares contact might be an excellent source for weapons and armor, a Shiawase or Evo contact may be able to acquire augmentations, and an MCT contact could be a great source for combat drones.**

**Government contacts include politicians, bureaucrats, and agents working for government entities. They can be useful for gathering information that relates to their bailiwick, but they are also highly useful for acquiring and editing public records. If you need to reduce your heat or lay a false trail for individuals or organizations that are looking for you, these people are your most relevant resource. Some government contacts are an ideal source for fake SINs and licenses as well.**

- **Bureaucrats are easily overlooked as useless wageslaves, but they know how to navigate through red tape like no one else and are great at digging up information no one else would know how to find.**

**Magic contacts include talismongers and enchanters, as well as other magicians. Talismongers are the people you come to for foci and reagents, and enchanters can supply you with useful alchemical preparations. These people are most useful for buying and selling magical or magic-related items. Other magicians may be able to perform valuable services such as setting up wards, astral reconnaissance, summoning a spirit to help you, performing a ritual, or casting spells on your behalf. Hopefully you have someone on your team who can provide most of this, but sometimes you need a little extra magic on your side. When it comes to information, they can be called upon for identifying artifacts or providing insight regarding Awakened phenomena or critters. If you know you’re going to be dealing with a magical threat, give them a call frst.**

- **Even if you don’t do magic, it’s useful to have a talismonger contact. It’s good to have someone who can help you fence the foci that you find along the way.**

- **Wingman**

**Almost every shadowrun involves dealing with the Matrix , so it’s important to cultivate some contacts who specialize in that feld. Deckmeisters and programmers may also fall into the engineering category, and they’re most useful for acquiring, modifying, and fxing up your Matrix tech—but the decker on your team probably has that side of things covered. Hacker groups, data havens, and technomancer societies can provide you with useful information and advice on recent and current events that relate to the Matrix, but they’re also excellent sources for fnding out almost any other information. These people live and thrive in a world made of data, so they should be your go-to info brokers. If the hacker on your team is out of commission or is going to be too busy to handle everything that needs to be done on the Matrix, these contacts can be relied upon to carry the load—for a price. And it’s always useful to have someone else watching your back on the Matrix.**

**Media contacts include a wide range of occupations, from reporters and podcasters to performing artists and athletes. No one is going to be a better source of information on topics that relate to their interests, but you need to be careful not to bring your business into the limelight when dealing with them. These contacts are most useful for providing relevant information, but they can also be used to lay false trails or cause a distraction.**
