## The Meeting

The meeting between Silver Nail’s parents and the possible Animus operative is supposed to take place at a Commerzbank Café, where you can drink soykaf and talk to a virtual financial adviser twenty-four hours a day. It’s a good spot because it’s in the business district, so it’s safe, and no one ever actually hangs out at places like this, so it’s fairly private.

The Jameses have a small table with a short, balding man in a navy-blue business suit who they call “Stuart.”

Eavesdropping on the Jameses and Stuart will not be difficult as long as the PCs are not glaringly obvious about it (being rowdy shadowrunners or clearly pointing a directional mic at them would count as “glaringly obvious”; situations like those will cause the Jameses to end the meeting early and go home). They depend on the fact that they know how to keep their conversations sounding entirely innocuous so that no one will pick up any crucial information. They talk about their families and social outings in unremarkable ways, and when they talk about work, it’s about making sure a contract is signed and a report is filed, without any details on what might be in the contract or report.

The critical part comes at the end of the conversation. Mrs. and Mr. James stand up to leave and say, “So we’ll hear from you on the depreciation matter?” Stuart says, “Absolutely. In fact, I need to visit the office now, so I can update you later tonight.” The Jameses agree this would be nice, and they depart.

The PCs should understand that Stuart will lead them to an Animus office, which would be a valuable piece of information. So they should follow him.
