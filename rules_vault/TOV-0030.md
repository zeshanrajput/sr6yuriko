## Cyberw are And Bioware In Corpses

So where else can you get bodies? Luckily for you there is a strong and active body trade. Having a mortician, grave robber, or carcass hauler as a contact is going to go a long way towards getting your supply. The corpses are still illegal—you won’t fi nd them for sale at your local Stiff er Shack! There’s the bonus that the dead body isn’t one that died because of you. The downside is that their services aren’t free. There are bribes to pay, paperwork to forge, and of course keeping tabs on who might miss that corpse.

Corpses purchased through a contact lack cyberware and bioware—that stuff is just too valuable to leave in the dead body. But if you are going through other means, the augmentations that provide bonuses to physical attributes will benefi t the zombie. Any other augmentations do not function.

For example, if a corpse has muscle replacement (rating 3) and a base Strength attribute of 3, then the resulting Strength of the zombie would be base Strength 2 (original Strength/2, rounded up) + muscle replacements (rating 3) for a total of Strength 5.

Why grande zombies are different from regular zombies hasn’t been fully determined, but the grande zombie shows intelligence and skilled in sorcery. In addition, it must continue to consume Essence (via its Essence Drain critter power) to sustain its magical Force. When its Essence ceases, the grande zombie will collapse and rapidly decompose.
