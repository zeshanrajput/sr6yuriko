## TRICKS OF THE TRADE

## Posted By: Fianchetto

Some runners like to use stealth. Some like to go in guns and spells blazing. There’s a lot of ways to pull off a run, and a lot of ways for those ways. Personally, I’m a huge fan of social subversion. With a little improvisation and psychology, you can pull off the job with a minimum to zero body count, no ammo costs, and barely a blip on law enforcement’s radar. Even the corps appreciate a well-executed job against them. Sure, they’d rather you failed, but success with zero residual presence spares them embarrassment and interruption of business. And frankly, it makes you a possible candidate for future work instead of someone who needs termination.

The grift, the con, the caper. Whatever you call it, the art of lying for your own ends has existed as long as the concept of falsehoods. Its application and countermeasures represent a social technology. And like all technology, it’s evolved into certain formats and principles that have continued to work for literally a millennium. The names change,

the factors shift, but each depends on certain patterns in metahuman psychology. They hide in plain sight and sneak through mundane occurrences or prey upon biases and desires. In other words, they’ll keep working as long as metahuman consciousness exists in its current form. And seeing as that’s unlikely to change any time soon, let’s open the floor to some time-tested cons, how they work, how they’ll spot ‘em, and how you can pull it off.

Deception is the oldest game. The first lie must have been in the name of acquisition, whether it was told to someone else to get what they want, or to oneself to bargain for comfort or peace of mind. Yet we remain enduringly, even endearingly vulnerable to it, both the telling and accepting. And why not? It’s the easy way, the cheat code for metahuman existence. Lie, and you get something you don’t deserve, probably quicker and cheaper than by honest means. Everyone lies, and the world has rewarded the liars with the highest echelons of power, prestige, and wealth.



Honest work is a sucker’s game. You wanna win? Cheat.

A liar’s work in the 2080s is harder than ever, thanks to the Matrix and magic. The twentieth century must have been a breeze, no constant net presence, no magical scans, just the bustle of the hustle. There was a gradual rise, it’s true, with the Internet and the increasing prevalence of the surveillance state, but that granted time for cons to evolve and counter new security measures as well as find new ways to weaponize changing tech in their favor. Not everyone kept up with the times, and the first quality to recognize in a mark is ignorance.

Ultimately, the nature of the con remains the same, and the sheer division of wealth means environment is a factor wherever the game is played. Although it’s with a degree of disparity never before seen in history. But the roots are fundamentally unaltered, preying upon the metahuman element. So a lot of what follows is somewhat familiar to students of history. The differences and details are where you’ll find value here.
