## **VAA Estrada-77**

**Like most of Vehículos Automotores Aztlamos (VAA) vehicles, the Estrada (Aztlan Spanish for “road”) is a gas-burning machine, but it’s one built for endurance and comfort more than speed. Used in the vast empty territories in and around the NAN, as well as California’s long highway system, the Estrada’s large gas tank and effcient burn lets it outlast any other vehicle on the road. An Estrada doesn’t run you down and force you off the road; it simply follows you until you run dry. With eight-hour rides normal, and twelve on occasion, it was built with a comfortable ride for the offcer in mind, rather than as a high-speed crotch rocket. The large “saddlebags” on the side also give it ample storage space.**
