## I Don’t Appreciate the Attention

Getting a visit from a faction isn’t usually a pleasant experience for a contact, and they likely don’t want to spend their time trying to smooth things over. Worse yet, the visit could draw attention to the contact’s own illegal activities. The contact’s reaction to this experience will vary based on Heat, Loyalty, and Favor Points. They might stay silent about the matter, covering for the team—or maybe they roll over on the team and sell them out. Another, more nuanced, option is the contact stays quiet but feels the team owes them a favor or else they’ll sell them out in the future.

If the contact flips on the team, the team may decide to seek revenge. If the contact stays silent, it could cost the team Favor Points or result in a loss of Loyalty. How the contact feels about the encounter is up to the gamemaster and could be influenced by the involved factions and any contact modifiers such as Loyalty ratings or Favor Points. The player character could call them back after the run and attempt to smooth things over using an Influence + Charisma or Logic Opposed test. The

number of available Loyalty or Favor Points can be used as a bonus for this test.

Optionally, if the gamemaster wants to roll when deciding if the contact is angry with the runners for leading an HTR team to their door, roll dice equal to the Loyalty of the contact. In the case of a critical glitch, the Loyalty is reduced by 1. In the case of a glitch, the runners owe the contact 2 additional Favor Points. For a test with no successes, the team owes the contact 1 additional Favor Point.
