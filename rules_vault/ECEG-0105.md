## **Mitsubishi Heziba**

**O** r **i** g **i** na **ll** y a rewor **k** e **d k** noc **k** o **ff** o **f** Mostran’s KVP-27T, Mitsubishi has won over the market with their Heziba models by undercutting the competition. This hover-van is intended to be part delivery van, part mobile storefront designed to be able to run into cities on wheels for the road before switching to hover mode to continue in outback territories. Those needing faster transit across the swamps tend to use the Chrysler-Nissan G12C (p. 34, _Double Clutch_ ), but while those may be what many think of as fanboats, they don’t take much with them, other than the driver.

Some things have always been done differently in rural areas, and Louisiana is no different. A lot of folks who live outside of the Crescent City have transportation needs that urban dwellers just don’t have to deal with, and one of the big ones is getting people and cargo around when all you’ve got to drive on is either dirt roads or no roads. We’ve got details on two of the more common ways swamp people get their goods from point A to point B. These, of course, aren’t the only vehicles you’ll see outside the city. The ever-present Gaz-Niki is around as you might expect, although most of the folks driving them are still using the older model P-179, so don’t expect a brand-new vehicle to blend in as well as you might be used to in the sprawl.
