## Trans-Reality Filter

Other than the scientists who created these devices, no one is exactly sure how they work. Every attempt to crack the shell and reverse-engineer one has resulted in catastrophe, destroying both the device and the investigator. Several observations about how they operate provide a few clues. First, they likely contain a Disian memory crystal, as that’s the easiest way to connect to their magic-based communication networks. Second, they likely use some type of metaplanar translation— maybe a pinhole metaplanar gate that both data streams are sent through and then converted into a common format. Third, and most disturbing, these devices are likely powered by burning a spirit’s Essence. Like all Dark-Tech, spirits will refuse to go near one. Using a trans-reality filter, or even holding one for a length of time, leaves a lasting stain on the astral signature that every spirit can see.

Physically, a trans-reality filter is a shiny, featureless, 10-centimeter black cube constructed of an unknown material. In astral space, it’s surrounded by an impenetrable dark haze that obscures its actual aura. In the Matrix, the device icon is a dark cube that can be connected to but provides no information about itself or its function.

Connecting a trans-reality filter to a PAN with a cyberdeck allows a decker to perceive, connect to, and use the magic-based information networks of the Disians as if they were a standard Matrix format. The device does not, however, grant any understanding of the Disian language, so they’re usually paired with the Disian linguasofts now in development. The downside of these devices (other than the reaction of any surrounding spirits) is that once actively connected to a Disian network, it creates power surges that rip through the connected Matrix equipment. As the trans-reality filter burns through the circuits of the connected device, it also generates biofeedback through the VR connection.

**Game Rules:** This device allows Matrix tech to interface with Disian crystals in their magic-basedinformation network. This process follows the same rules as metaplanar translation on (p. 171, _Astral Ways_ ), and makes Disian information networks follow the same rules as standard Matrix devices.

As the user connects to a Disian information network, this device causes 1 point of unresisted Matrix damage per turn to the cyberdeck it’s networked to, and the user has to resist 1 DV of biofeedback each turn.

Possessing or using this device causes a drop in Astral Reputation (p. 63, _Street Wyrd_ ).
