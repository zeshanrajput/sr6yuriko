## Cracking BTL and Ripjack Copy Protections

You’ve been using the Upper BTL for the last few weeks and have been wondering why you need to pay each time you use it. It’s just software, right? There’s gotta be some way to just make a bunch of copies of it and have them ready for when you want to use one. The big thing stopping you is the copy protection on the software. Removing the copy-protection on a BTL or p-fix is a Cracking + Logic (24, 1 hour) Extended test. Any glitch on the test sets the BTL or p-fix as consumed, requiring you to purchase another chip to start all over.

For ripjacks, the process is the same, but since the systems are constantly being updated with new ripjack protections, the cracked ripjack has a very limited shelf life before it is effectively blocked by the off-the-shelf anti-ripjack on the target machines. Removing the copy protection requires a Cracking + Logic (24, 1 hour) Extended test, and any glitch sets the ripjack as consumed. A critical glitch causes the ripjack to attack your system. Once the ripjack has been cracked, each subsequent use causes the Power to decrease by 1, making it more likely that the target system will be able to resist the effects.

Cracked BTLs and ripjacks can’t be modified to increase the Power or effects.
