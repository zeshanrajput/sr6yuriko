## Hacking the Metaplanes

Translating a hacker to a metaplane needs a little bit of extra work. Specifically, decisions need to be made about what type of information systems exist on a given metaplane. While hackers will always be able to interact with anything that’s taken in with the team as normal, having something they can access inside a metaplane will depend on the local technology. Lower-technology metaplanes with high levels of mana may have Matrix equivalents that operate with magic as effectively as higher-technology systems operate with electronic and optical processors. Metaplanes using stonepunk or swordpunk systems may have magically operated Matrix equivalents, perhaps networks of memory crystals accessed with seeing stones. As long as the decker’s gear is properly adapted by the reality paradigm and able to interface with the local Matrix equivalent, all the normal Matrix rules apply. Some metaplanes may instead have limited Matrix equivalents where networks of devices exist, but there is nothing as complex as hosts. Perhaps there are individuals with magically adapted data-storage ability such as the coimeádaí of the Seelie Court with the “silver books” that commlinks and cyberdecks translate into. This equipment can hack the data stored by the coimeádaí—and in other silver books—but these information networks are simply interconnected devices on a common grid, not a full Matrix equivalent.

At lower levels of magic, deckers may start running into other issues with steampunk and dieselpunk technology metaplanes, as the technology and mana needed to support a virtual reality interface just isn’t there. The AR and VR generated by a hacker’s PAN still works, there is just nothing to access. Typically, at this level the decker will be dealing with difference engines made of cogs and gears, the information storage being the physical position of these mechanical devices. The interface will be mechanical displays with clacking rotating plates and inputs performed by inserting hole-punched cards. In cases like this, physical connections to data-storage devices will allow the data to be read, and the decker’s equipment causes physical changes within the mechanisms. Once the technology reaches the level of cyberpunk, a working Matrix is assumed to be a part of things, and all the normal Matrix rules apply, regardless of the ambient magic. Obviously at atompunk levels, a version of the Matrix will exist—the question is whether the decker is skilled enough to interact with such advanced systems.

Matrix-using entries moving from the metaplanes to the physical realm are presented with a different, more serious problem. Since the physical plane does not cause any translation or enforce a reality paradigm on travelers, any technology used in the metaplanes to access Matrix equivalents will not work on the physical plane. It’s also not just the non-translated equipment—the Matrix skills and knowledge gained in the metaplanes do not carry over and must be relearned to be effective. When it comes to technomancers, a different set of rules apply, as even primitive data-processing systems generate a noosphere that can be accessed by their ability. Since a technomancer interacts with the nous-particle field constructs created wherever data is processed, the technology that actually processes the data becomes less important. Any complex data system, regardless of whether it’s based on magic, mechanical devices, or electronic/optical processors, generates the same type of noosphere, and a technomancer can enter AR/ VR easily in any location. What changes is the ease of altering the data in the systems. Physical, mechanically operated systems are quite “sticky”

and resistant to being changed by Resonance. The effect can be represented by doubling the effective firewall in such systems when it comes to making changes to the data.

Another advantage technomancers have shows up in places with untranslated data systems— like Dis, or when entities come into the material plane—that would normally prevent equipment-based hacking. These systems still generate nous fields, and they can be read and altered by technomancers through the noosphere without requiring any understanding how the actual data-processing devices work.
