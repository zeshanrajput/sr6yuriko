## **Vice/Undercover officer**

**Uses: criminal activity, criminal organization Places to Meet: bar, local hangouts Similar Contacts : face, fxer**

**The responsibilities of a vice or undercover offcer extend far beyond the glamorized portrayals often seen in popular trid series. We‘ve all seen them as working on investigations and hanging out with seedy criminals in bars and BLT houses. But few people outside the force know what a vice cop is in real life. Nor do most people realize how the Matrix or magic has made their jobs harder. For example, an undercover offcer is pretending to be a “junky,” but their aura is clean, or they are accidentally made because a Matrix agent fnds an old picture of them on some long-forgotten social networking site that shows them going through Lone Star training.**

**These law enforcement professionals engage in covert operations aimed at tackling illegal activities, such as drug traffcking, organized crime, and other related offenses. Their work involves blending into various environments and social settings to gather crucial intelligence and evidence, often putting themselves at great personal risk. The role demands a unique set of skills, including deception and willingness to act fast in dangerous situations with little or no information. Ultimately, vice and undercover offcers play a vital role in maintaining public safety by targeting and dismantling criminal networks.**

**For a runner, having a vice or undercover offcer as a contact could prove valuable for several reasons. These operate discreetly, providing a unique perspective on criminal networks and activities. As a contact, they provide access to a source of information and expertise of criminal investigations and criminal organizations. Their insights, knowledge of the underground world, and ability to gather intelligence covertly can be benefcial in various situations. Also, like many runners they work in many areas, and a vice or undercover offcer can offer an introduction or protection in both.**
