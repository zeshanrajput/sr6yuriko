## Turning a Contact

There are times when a faction or an individual will attempt to get one of the runner’s contacts to sell out the team or set the team up for failure. This can occur during or between the sessions. Depending on how your group has decided to use these rules, this could be something that happens as a storytelling beat with no dice rolls necessary. This section will explain how to resolve the contact’s crisis of loyalty if the table has decided to use dice to do so.

When someone, or some faction, reaches out to the contact about the team’s recent activities, use the information in the section **Heat: When the Pressure’s On** to deal with Heat-related conflicts. There are also times when a contact is approached for information even though they have nothing to do with the current situation, run, or accumulated heat. These approaches will be driven by an opportunity for more nuyen, power, or influence in the shadows. This could be a rival team, or a future Mr. Johnson who wants to “adjust” the team’s finances, and by doing so, encourage the team to work for them. The contact could also be used as a distraction while something else is occurring. The first step is finding the contact and their relationship to the team. The opposition would need to perform the legwork to track that information down— the faction should use the Legwork rules for players while they track this information down between sessions.

Once the opposition has identified the parties in play, it’s time for them to apply the pressure to the contact. As part of the legwork, the opposition may have found leverage against the contact. There are some example mechanics for points of leverage if you’re using Contact Keywords, but for everything else, points of leverage will grant the opposition a point of Edge to use when attempting to turn the contact.

The test involved in turning the contact depends on the approach being used by the faction or individual. These should be resolved using either an Influence + Charisma or Intuition Opposed test ( _SR6_ , p. 96), or a Con + Charisma Opposed test ( _SR6_ , p. 94), whichever is higher. As stated earlier, Favor Points can be used to increase dice pool for the Resistance tests.
