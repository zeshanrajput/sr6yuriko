## Technology on the Metaplanes Posted by: Shade

The metaplanes are magic places, and everyone knows that magic and tech do not get along. As it turns out, this isn’t entirely the case when it comes to the metaplanes. Of course, astral forms can’t carry or use technology, but what happens when you bring your cyberdeck with you when you step through a gate? It comes with you, that’s what happens! Same case with your cyber, guns, drones, RCCs, and even vehicles. There’s nothing on (most) metaplanes that messes them up or makes them stop working. In most cases, the items look and work just like they do IRL. Some metaplanes have an unusual effect on manufactured objects, forcing them to conform to an aesthetic similar to how a UV host would translate your persona and software to match its vibe. Sorry about the decker jargon, but you’re going to have to put up with some of that. I’m a decker and a rigger, but I’ve also been around magic all my life. My first visit to a metaplane was accidental—I got sucked through an astral rift when I was thirteen years old—but I’ve been on a few jaunts on purpose since then.

The other times I’ve traveled to metaplanes have been work-related, and we got there by passing through a gate (courtesy of an overly friendly free spirit). Since I had been to the metaplanes before, I thought I knew what to expect, but it was different. When you travel to the metaplanes in astral form, it’s more dreamlike. When you’re there for real, it feels more real. Maybe it’s just that you’re still dressed in the same outfit you were wearing when you left the real world, and that you have all your stuff with you, but I think it’s more than that. I think that when you’re on a metaplane for real, that you bring more of your own sense of self and reality with you, and that makes you more real. Does that make sense? Well, if not, remember that I’m a tech nerd, not a magic theory nerd, and take it with a grain of salt. What’s important about this is that your tech comes with you, and it works the same as it does on the other side—mostly. On most metaplanes, you and your gear will look and work how it’s supposed to. Most, not all. If you travel to metaplanes without high technology, like the Plane of Faerie, things get weird—guns turn into bows and crossbows, drones turn into pet animals, cyberdecks turn into magic wands, clothing turns into ren faire fashion, stuff like that. What’s most strange about this is that even though your stuff looks and feels different, it’s still useful, and you seem to have this intuitive understanding of how to use it. A rigger’s RCC might turn into a funky bracelet, but speaking into that bracelet allows you to give commands to your drones-turned-critters, and you can receive information by concentrating on one of the drones while wearing the bracelet. I bet you can even jump into your own drones there, though I never quite got up the nerve to try it myself. That’s just one example, but I hope this makes it clear to you that while your tech might be changed or affected by a metaplane’s influence, it’s still going to be useful.
