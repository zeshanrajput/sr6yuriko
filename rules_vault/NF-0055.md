## Fractal

Originally an experiment in SK programming, this



Horizon breakout genre is the fruit of one question: What kind of music would an AI want to listen to? The rumor mill of the shadows says data stolen from Transys servers and Renraku archives helped round out this complicated, complex sound that _WHAM Music_ called “an audio tiramisu, layer after layer of seemingly chaotic phonics that are like a magic-eye picture: Only when you let yourself let go from the individual elements clashing can you begin to appreciate the way the whole acts in such startling harmony.” _Pogo_ was more critical, calling it, “vaguely melodic feedback static. You’d have to be pretty damn high to find any kind of meaning or reason in this mess. Like, ‘thinking the lights of geodesics are Dunklezahn’s ghost communicating with you in Morse code’ high.” Still, it’s gained some popularity with technomancers, hipsters, and wannabes, who sometimes say they can hear the message behind the music.
