## Auravores

Welcome back, everyone. I hope you had a good lunch, because that’s what I’m going to cover now.

There has been some recent research on what I’m going to refer to as “auravores,” creatures that feed on Essence or on an aura. To be clear, I’m talking about those who have been affected by HMHVV to such a degree that they require the Essence of others to survive. These people and creatures (of whom vampires are the most visible) are known for consuming the bodies (or blood) of other metahumans, but it’s apparent that this is not the substance that sustains them. You may remember a large data breach of secret research that was made public three years ago. I reviewed that information and found that the “donor” corporation had been conducting magical experiments with these auravores. The results of this organization’s research appear to have led to some of the more virulent FAB strains that were produced, but from an academic perspective, it has been interesting to discover that the researchers found what they thought was the mechanism for this—they discovered how vampires and other such creatures consumed the Essence of the victims.

The short explanation for this is that the auravore has a corrupted aura. This is probably a result of the changes HMHVV makes to the Essence of the person infected, and when you see a lot of it, it becomes obvious when you perceive an aura.

When an auravore absorbs Essence from a victim, it actually rips away part of the aura and incorporates it into its own aura, fundamentally changing the Essence of the auravore. Studies have shown that this lasts for a while until the original aura corrupts or overcomes the absorbed Essence, at which point the aura moves on to attempt to corrupt the body of the auravore, forcing it to absorb more Essence or eventually die.

This is also not good for the person whose Essence is being absorbed. Even those who recover from this process have “Essence scars” that a discerning aura-reader can pick out as places where an aura was damaged. It’s unknown how much this damage and these scars permanently change the personality and nature of the person being fed upon. In most cases, people who survive a vampire attack are just happy to be alive, and the trauma of such an event will probably make bigger changes than the feeding, but this is an area of open research.

It’s an academic exercise at this point, but with the development of Essence-repair technologies, it’s now possible to have an auravore feed on a small group of people undergoing treatment. This would be prohibitively expensive, but it’s a way to have a more ethical vampire. It would still be problematic to attack the Essence of other people, but there are some in the world who would be happy to live in an Essence restoration spa in exchange for one night of terror a year when their Essence
