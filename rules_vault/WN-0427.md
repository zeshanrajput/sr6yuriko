## The Factory

After as many or as few encounters with the locals as needed to feel immersed in the metaplane, the team should wander into “the Factory.” This structure is next to a large void—in fact, the corner of the building is already crumbling into non-existence. As the team approaches, Mr. Seven states that there is a large data structure here and they want to investigate. Deckers on the team will not be able to detect anything, but a technomancer will be able to enter VR in this area. But whatever type of Matrix structure this is seems to be empty, lacking any defined iconography or even data.

When the team enters, they discover the building was abandoned by its owner and operators but several of the “workers” have been left behind, chained to their workstations, surrounded by rotting food mixed with their own waste. This is a food factory, where magically enslaved mages conjure a steady stream of exotic foods and drinks to be packaged and sold to the other residents of the city. Some of those residents are trying to extract and relocate the mages for their own use. The looters will attack the runners on sight, assuming the team is there for the same reason. Use a number of Traders and Apocalyptics (p. 129, _Slip Streams_ ) equal to the number of runners. After the looters are driven off, the team has the run of the facility.

Once inside the building, Mr. Seven will leave the drone and enter the Matrix structure. After an hour or so the xenosapient moves to “somewhere else.” Mr. Seven will be gone from the drone for over two days—requiring at least one relocation due to the expanding void—leaving plenty of time to explore the building. If the captive mages are examined, they are found to be under the same type of enchantment used to create a Living Mana Well (p. 141, _Slip Streams_ ), and they can be brought out of this trance in the same way. If the enchantment is removed and any of the severely traumatized mages are freed, they will flee the area as fast as they’re able. At the opposite end of the factory from the void is a series of smaller rooms, offices of some sort, and stairs to a second floor containing a room with another different type of workstation. This semicircular desk contains a series of large, roughcut gemstones mounted in it. In the center of the desk is the slumped corpse of a tripedal being with six arms ending in four-fingered hands. The fractured gemstones have a faint glow; assensing will

reveal that they once held powerful enchantments, and a technomancer examining them will discover these are the source of the empty data structure. If the team spends some time examining the room, they may eventually discover that this was the Disian equivalent of a data center. The gemstones are Disian memory crystals, but the dropping mana level has left them fractured and useless. Whatever data they once contained is now gone, leaving an empty shell of a data structure in the local noosphere. The lethal biofeedback from the fracturing crystals must have killed the operator where they sat.

When Mr. Seven finally returns to the drone, they will state that they’ve found what they were looking for and are ready to leave the metaplane. If questioned, the Null will still refuse to enlighten the team on exactly what they were looking for or what they found.
