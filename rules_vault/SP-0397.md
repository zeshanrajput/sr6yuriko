## **Power Hammer**

**A power hammer has a two- to three-kilogram steel head attached to a 50- to 80-centimeter-long haft. Power hammers are heavy and unbalanced, generally requiring two-handed use, but a wielder with exceptional strength may use it single-handed. The standard confguration has one fxed striking face—sometimes taking the form of a large spike—and one foating plate that employs the explosive charges. When the foating strike plate registers an impact, a number of charges may be set off, driving the strike plate forward and increasing the power of the blow. For the power hammer, this can be either one or two charges, with that number set by the user. The noise made by detonating charges is equal to the noise of a shotgun blast.**

**A standard power hammer contains six explosive charges, though double-sided versions exist that use a second foating striking plate on the back side instead of the fxed face. The double-sided versions hold twelve charges, six under each face, but cost substantially more. Charges are basically very short, caseless shotgun shells without a projectile. They ft into a wide thin clip that inserts into the weapon just behind the strike plate. Some power weapons also include a smartgun system to wirelessly eject spent clips, making the reloading process faster.**
