## Move-By-Wire (Rating 1-2)

Cyberware that deliberately puts its user into a permanent epileptic seizure rightly seems like a radical idea. In fact, move-by-wire systems are among the oldest cybernetic technologies designed to enhance combat reflexes. Originally inspired by hundred-year-old aircraft fly-by-wire systems, move-by-wire has since been supplanted by less radical wired reflex systems. Thousands of computer-directed corrections are issued to the user’s nervous system every second, taming the seizure down to a state of small twitches and tremors. However, the cybernetic processors assist rather than dampen movement in the direction the user wishes to perform, resulting in preternaturally quick and graceful fluidity of motion. Each point of move-by-wire rating grants +2 Reaction, +2 Agility, and an additional two Minor Actions per round that can only be spent on actions that involve physical movement. Move-by-wire is incompatible with other augmentations that modify Reaction. Once implanted, a move-by-wire system cannot be deactivated. If move-by-wire is bricked or otherwise rendered nonfunctional, the user suffers the Stilled status (p. 53, _SR6_ ) until the system is repaired.

**Cyberware overdrive** : Move-by-wire grants +4 dice to a test involving Agility or Reaction. One of these dice must be a wild die.

**Wireless bonus** : Move-by-wire’s bonus to Reaction stacks with reaction enhancers (p. 287, _SR6_ ) but is still subject to the usual augmented maximum of +4.
