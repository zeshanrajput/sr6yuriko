## Sma rt Clothes

Just what a shapechanger needs to blend in the big city! Default setting is a basic neck to wrist to toe thin covering. Not particularly modest, as the material tends to be thin and clingy. A half suit will provide fl ip fl ops, shorts and a t-shirt. A quarter sized suit will cover about as much as typical swimwear. Electrochromatic coating is standard. Takes about ten seconds to swap between settings, but as long as you’re not in a hurry, you can go from tiger to two-legs without an issue. Sudden changes will tear the material, but the smart fabric will reattach itself if you pinch the pieces together. The material can change from dress to pants to shorts to collar to whatever. A full-sized suit folded down to a collar gets a little thick but not uncomfortably so.

- Obviously useful for more than just shifters, but don’t count on these to stop a butter knife, much less a bullet. These will absolutely not pass as any high-fashion material, but trying to make it into jumpsuits or coveralls for caterers or cleaning staff? You won’t even get a second glance. If the uniform you need to copy uses a jacket, you probably need to buy a second half piece, though.

- **>** Thorn
