## Distant Metaplanes

The planes of Kin, Faerie, Plants, Beasts, and the elements are all relatively close to the physical plane. Each of them is close enough to the others to allow for rifts and portals to connect them. Beyond these metaplanes, deeper into the Astral Sea, are perhaps an infinite number of other metaplanes—the farther afield you travel on the Astral Sea, the more bizarre and often hostile the planes become. Few of these distant metaplanes are directly connected to Earth via astral portals and rifts, but some of them seem to be reaching out hungrily toward us.

**The Hive** is the home of the Invae, a species of spirits more colloquially known as “insect spirits.” The Hive is one of the closest distant metaplanes, and it contains a vast multitude of warring insect-like spirits that are engaged in a constant struggle for the dominance of their home plane. They are usually busy enough with their unending fratricidal conflict, but sometimes they break loose in great swarms, seeking to invade other planes. Their closest enemies are the denizens of the Web, a metaplane woven seemingly of spider silk. The spirits of the Web resemble spiders and are one of the main reasons that the Hive has not yet spread out and overcome other metaplanes. The spirits of the Web are powerful and terrible, and they are probably the most serious threat the Invae face. They seem to have little interest in interfering with the physical plane or other metaplanes, and the Web is a relatively safe place for the metaplanar explorer to visit— provided that you are polite and respectful. It is said that the spirit who rules over the Web—the Empress Neit—has great powers of divination and can provide a truthful answer to any query she chooses to respond to. One day I hope to secure an audience with her. Getting to the Web requires passing very close to the Hive, however, and that is a dangerous journey that none should take lightly.

If, while traveling the Astral Sea, you encounter a large void-like metaplane with a glowing red orb at its center, I implore you to flee immediately. This metaplane is the Hungry Void, and it is ruled over by the Daemon Sultan, a malevolent spirit of unfathomable power. The Daemon Sultan is trapped within its own metaplane and can’t harm any outside of the Hungry Void, but those who stray too close may be drawn in. Once you are within its domain, there is no hope for you.

Far beyond in the Astral Sea is a massive nebula of violet gas. There are some who claim that this is not a metaplane, but rather a vast entity. I have not, however, seen anything in this regard that I consider credible. Those who claim to have gained answers from it have refused to share any of this knowledge with others. I think it is highly likely that their claims are false. But if they are not false, I find fault in anyone who would tease at the existence of mysterious knowledge and then refuse to share it with others. I would not place my hopes in any venture to make contact with, or get answers from, the Violet Gas. It would take an astral traveler many days, perhaps even weeks, to travel to it. Such a long journey is unhealthy for the body, even if careful measures are taken to maintain it.

Beyond the Violet Gas are undoubtedly countless other metaplanes, as distant from one another as they are from our own physical plane. These planes can likely only be reached by traveling through the Astral Sea, but the distances required are impractical. There do not appear to be any astral portals, rifts, or other devices that connect them to closer metaplanes for easy travel. Until someone discovers some other means of transit (a discovery that would be akin to inventing fasterthan-light space travel on the physical plane), they will likely remain unreachable.
