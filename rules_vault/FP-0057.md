## **Opposition Report**

**==> picture [233 x 4] intentionally omitted \<==**

**There is a long list of people they’ve pissed off, as one would expect from a group that generally thinks it can take whatever it wants, but it’s surprisingly lacking many truly powerful individuals. It’s not devoid of them, but it’s a short list of scary folks and long list of general nobodies. The thing is, the scary folks aren’t about stopping the entirety of the Echoes or bringing them down, they’re mostly just out to keep stupid metahumans from doing stupid metahuman tricks and fragging up the world. Those nobodies on the other hand tend to have a lot of personal vendettas against individuals, and fanatics can go a long way to piss someone off to the point of no return. While I’ll talk about the three scary entities I know of, I don’t think I can really cover all the personal drama of the Echoes, just know that you might get some trouble coming at you from out of left feld if you’re working with this society.**

**Scary fragger numero uno is none other than the Sea Dragon . She has it out for all of the Echoes because something on the cards scares her, and that’s a near impossible thing to do. While I think Seakeller is her agent in the Echoes and he’s mainly a non-violent player, I know for a fact the info he feeds out has gotten several Echoes killed, but the efforts were focused. Her talon-prints are all over the hit contracts that took down Eva Lee and Darryl Frombenter, but the pair were hit at an Echoes gathering, and no one else was touched. There is something very specifc about her efforts even though she seems to have a dislike of card collectors in general. It must be the specifc cards those two possessed or sought, but no one else seems to know what their agendas were.**

**Number two on the “I Hate the Echoes” list is the Shifting Pyre . This is not a single individual but rather an opposing group that hunts the cards as well. Instead of wanting to use them or faunt them, the Shifting Pyre wants to destroy them. As Echoes often possess cards, this group actively goes after them any time they can confrm a card exists. Sometimes their efforts intervene at the wrong time on a run, and sometimes they make a counteroffer to the runners, but usually they go straight at the Echoes, turning a simple hand-off into a protect-the-golden-goose gig. They aren’t large in number, but they match the Echoes in their fanaticism. Getting in between them and an Echo should defnitely be an extra payday, so don’t forget to renegotiate after you save their hide.**

**The third scary fragger with a grudge against the entirety of this society is Nightmare . If you aren’t familiar, Nightmare is a former leader of the Halloweeners who was rumored to have a card in the** _**Book of the Lost**_ **data drop. Problem is, he didn’t, but the data made him a mark for several Echoes. They came down hard going after him, and his branch of the gang paid the price. He barely survived thanks to a DocWagon contract, and rather than rebuilding, he gathered the only four surviving Halloweeners from his stable and formed a runner team that calls themselves the Street Haunts. They can’t hide most of their gang tattoos and tend to take loud jobs, but every extra nuyen they gather goes to taking down another Echo. From what I know, they’ve killed six Echoes, and while the others Echoes understand the threat, they still see them as a sort of bogeyman. Nightmare and the Street Haunts are a real and active problem for the Echoes, and they don’t have issues with collateral damage or making a giant mess to get just an Echo. To them, it’s worth it.**

**> Rumors claim Nightmare collects cards from the dead Echoes if he can and keeps them as trophies. It makes him an enemy and a target all at the same time, but no one can confirm the rumors, so no one wants to risk losing this face-off. Yet.**

**> Matt Wrath**

**As I pointed out, the list of people with a personal beef against the Echoes is long. Two individuals have come to my attention who don’t qualify as scary fraggers, but they’ve managed to make waves for the Echoes and counter some of their attempts to acquire cards. Neither seems to want to eliminate any specifc Echo. Both work to undermine the acquisition of cards rather than the lives or structures of the Echoes.**

**The frst is Alexi Storpova , a member of the Russian mafa, the Vory. He was working with an Echo on smuggling a card when his main warehouse was raided, and he blamed the Echo. Since then, he’s made it a personal goal to undermine the**

**The second is Joseph Mnguni of Izolo Inc. He used to be a collector with a great rep. Now he’s a disrupter with just as good a rep for his payments and no change in how he treats those who welch on a deal. He was a member of the Echoes up to about six months ago, when he suddenly changed his focus. Rather than acquiring cards for himself, he now gathers them to keep them from the hands of other Echoes. The falling out occurred when Mnguni had all four aces but refused to sell any of them or activate them. He was suddenly persona non grata, and the social shunning left a bad taste in his mouth. He left the Echoes and undermines his former peers at every possible turn.**

**Lastly I’d like to simply mention a few other groups that still deal in the cards and thus act in opposition to the Echoes, even though it isn’t always a head-on contest. The Romanov clan still consider their ties to the tarot fundamental and see the cards as something to protect rather than exploit. They tend to operate at gathering cards rather than eliminating card holders, but they are not pacifsts if push comes to shove. They pose the biggest threat when there is a race to acquire a card, as they have “family” all over the globe and can often get someone active in a region faster than an Echo.**

**Convergence has an interest in the tarot, but they try to avoid competing with the Echoes. If the Echoes do the legwork of gathering cards and Convergence can pay to have a look, that’s fne for Convergence. It helps that Convergence is focused on divination while the Echoes are more interested in activating cards, so Convergence can present their activities as not being in confict with the Echoes’ goals.**

**The Hands of Fate have dwindled in number but grown in skill and devotion to their cause. They gather cards but now seem to do so only when some blood price comes along with it. Murdering to acquire a card seems a requirement, rather than a side effect. They don’t specifcally target the Echoes, but they have exacted cards and a life toll from several.**

Several key members of the society were targeted on the Night of Death, and over half were successful. This put a lot of cards back into circulation, but it eliminated most who knew what they were looking for in the cards.**
