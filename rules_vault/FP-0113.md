## **The New Alpha Site**

**Over the decades that the Nexus has been operating in Denver, it has maintained a constant search for new places to hide banks of optical stacks and leave secure data backups. That process has included fnding possible facilities to relocate the entire operation if needed. These searches produced the old Beta Sites at the Fitzsimons Army Hospital, destroyed by the ZFD in 2078, and the NOAA facility in Boulder, compromised by MCT in 2082. The holy grail of abandoned facilities within the FRFZ was the NORAD complex under Cheyenne Mountain—the trick was fnding a way to use it.**

**the most advanced equipment, and then bringing down the side of Cheyenne Mountain to bury the main entrance. The interior remained mostly intact, and over the years several ways into the abandoned complex had been found by scavengers and explorers. Most are almost impossible to access, like air shafts high in the mountainside or water and power connections in tunnels too small for a metahuman to access. But eventually the searchers from the Nexus found the plans for a top-secret and well-hidden emergency transport tunnel that ran from the NORAD complex to the (also-abandoned) Evans Army Hospital, south of Colorado Springs near the very edge of the FRFZ.**

**After locating a reliable and secret way into the Cheyenne Mountain complex, it took years of careful work to quietly reconnect power and utility lines, run new Matrix hard lines, and secure the facility. The high-speed car inside the seven-kilometer tunnel can carry up to 20 people or about 10 cubic meters of cargo. Security at the Evans Army Hospital end of the tunnel is extremely heavy, with turreted machine guns and enough explosives to collapse a kilometer of tunnel. Not happy with one entrance (or exit), the Nexus has a half-dozen mining drones hard at work creating new tunnels through the mountain with their exits past the perimeter of the FRFZ, just in case the rail line is compromised.**

**Currently, the facility is operational, with Perri and a few other staff living there full-time. The rest of the Nexus staff are working from remote locations, and it’s undecided just how many people will eventually move to the new Alpha Site. While connections to the distributed network of the Nexus are in place and the Matrix side of the facility is fully functional, the lack of backup escape routes and the generally sparse conditions are giving a few of the staff some hesitation. Without actively being pursued by the ZDF, many see the extreme level of security provided by the new Alpha Site as unnecessary.**
