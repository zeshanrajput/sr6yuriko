## Failure

When a critter fails a Comprehension test, it stops what it is doing and returns to its owner or handler to await further instructions. On a critical glitch, the critter misunderstands the orders without realizing it and does something other than what it was commanded to do. It’s up to the gamemaster to determine what happens, but it should never result in behavior that would harm the critter.

When properly calibrated and used within its design parameters, critter comms make it possible to converse with an animal.

**Game Information:** A critter comm has one program slot per rating. The only programs it is capable of running are rating 1 linguasofts. In order for a critter comm to function, it must be running a rating 1 linguasoft for each language the critter may be able to understand and respond to.

In addition to being able to communicate using simple words and phrases, a critter comm provides a bonus point of Edge on any Influence tests to train, command, or interact with the augmented critter. When a critter must make a Comprehension test (see sidebar) to interpret complex orders, it gains bonus dice equal to the critter comm’s rating. On Influence tests that affect the critter as well as the critter’s Comprehension tests, one of the dice must be a wild die. If the wild die rolls a 1, the result counts as a critical glitch—removing any hits that were scored.

A critter must also receive special training in order to make use of the critter comm, but once the training is complete, the critter may be given additional commands beyond what it has already been trained for. Issuing commands the critter doesn’t already know requires a Major Action and an Influence + Charisma (3) test from the person giving

the command. The critter must then make a Comprehension (3) test in order to understand the command. On a failure, the critter becomes confused and does not attempt to follow the command. You can try to issue the same command again with another Influence + Charisma test (with another Major Action), which then allows the critter to make another Comprehension (3) test. There is no penalty for making multiple consecutive attempts, other than the time it takes to keep trying again.
