## **How to Use Psyche**

**Psyche is not intended as a being with whom the characters should engage in combat or battle, at least not directly. Psyche is motivated by its interest in farming and harvesting thoughts and the inspirations that lead to them. While it works comfortably within the Resonant side of the Nous feld (see Resonance Wells and Dissonance Pools, p. XX) , it is not impeded by the Dissonant side of the polar energies. What directly agitates Psyche are individuals who actively work to destroy thought and knowledge; such actions can bring player characters directly into Psyche’s radar.**

**Individuals who pursue actions that expunge data and information, regardless of form, from the Deep Resonance can potentially attract one of Psyche’s agents. And while Psyche doesn’t go out slaughtering such individuals out of habit, it has been known to assign its agents to recover or reconstruct the expunged information, often resecuring it someplace else unknown to others. This is refected in a 1D6 roll, with a one indicating that the action has been noticed by one of Psyche’s agents or even by Psyche itself.**

**When new information of a signifcant nature is created, or when the potential for it exists, Psyche may choose to grant a boon, either directly or through one of its agents. Such was the instance described by Onstage, where Psyche felt the driving desire combined with other matters of its own interest. It aided the performer to create a master work, something that would further infuence not just a few persons, but potentially millions, or even billions, of other sapients who would listen to it.**

**This is not to say that Psyche is behind every single inspirational thought—far from it. Exactly how to gain a favor or boon from Psyche or one of its agents is left as a tool for a gamemaster.**
