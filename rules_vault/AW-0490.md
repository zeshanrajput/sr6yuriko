## Blake’s Alchera

Located on Ketron Island, Seattle, Blake’s Alchera is a displacement alchera connected to the underwater section of the caverns there. The maze-like caverns that make up the alchera include a stable metaplanar rift leading to a hyper-metaplane associated with the elemental Plane of Water. Submerged crystal cities can be found there, filled with all types of water spirits. Both the alchera and the rift have been stable for years, but as with all things magical, there is no guarantee they will remain that way.

Some travelers have reported seeing a sun in the sky on rare occasions, usually on the horizon at dawn or dusk but also at midday with the stars still visible. It’s unknown what the appearance of the sun on the Astral Sea means, or even what it could actually be.

A traveler will always manifest a metaplanar form if entering the Astral Sea through astral projection, and the form taken is a close copy of the material body. Anyone traveling there physically also keeps their native form, exactly as it is on the physical realm—the Astral Sea does not enforce any metaplanar translation on the traveler. Time passes on the Astral Sea at different speeds—weeks spent crossing the sands may only be hours to the body left behind, or they could actually be weeks. Unlike the astral plane, time spent in the Astral Sea is not limited by Magic or Essence and follows the same rules as being in the metaplanes proper.

The Astral Sea may seem devoid of life at first glance, but there are always a number of travelers on it for various reasons. Sometimes it is simply someone on their way to a distant metaplane— maybe a free spirit who has decided to make the Astral Sea their home for a while, or you may see those who have been pulled into the sea by accident and simply want to survive and find a way home. Other times the encounters may be with bandits, pirates, or other types of outlaws roaming the dunes and looking for easy victims.

The borders between the Astral Sea and the metaplanes are not readily apparent while on the sea. Spotting one before stumbling into it takes some skill (an + Astral + Intuition + Initiate Grade

[3] test). Determining if a given metaplane is already known, or understanding some of its properties without crossing the boundary, is a more difficult task (Astral + Intuition + Initiate Grade [4] test; each hit past the threshold gains one piece of information about the metaplane). Crossing over the border into a metaplane from the Astral Sea moves the traveler into that metaplane. It doesn’t matter if they are materializing a metaplanar form or are physically in the Astral Sea, and it does not require anything beyond walking across the boundary. However, getting back to the Astral Sea may be difficult, as it will require an astral gateway ritual—or a portal/rift leading there—to enter the Astral Sea again directly from a metaplane. Returning to the physical realm from the Astral Sea, or a metaplane that’s been unintentionally traveled into, occurs in the same way as returning from any metaplane.
