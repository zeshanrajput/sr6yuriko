## Hacking Cyberware

When cyberware is targeted by a Matrix Action or complex form, its device rating (see sidebar) enhances the resistance test. If the cyberware is part of a PAN, this rating is added to the network’s Firewall but only for actions targeting the cyberware specifically. If the owner has no PAN, then cyberware’s Firewall equals its device rating. If the cyberware is not wirelessly enabled, the wouldbe hacker must first establish a direct connection. When cyberware is bricked, it does not deal any damage to the user by shorting out unless the device explicitly says otherwise. Certain implants can still function after being bricked, such as dermal armor, bone lacing, and goring horns. Gamemasters determine when cyberware falls into this category. Note that cyberware housings for implanted commlinks, cyberdecks, or RCCs do not modify the Firewall of those devices housed within them, nor to the Matrix personas or PANs they contribute to.

All cyberware is ultimately devices that have been implanted in a person’s body, and as such they are eligible targets for any of the Matrix actions that can target devices. However, due to cyberware being integrated into the user’s nervous system, it is difficult to force the augmentation to do something against the user’s wishes. Gamemaster discretion determines what the effect of standard Matrix Actions such as Control Device or Spoof Command could be on a given augmentation. The following Matrix Action is recommended for use instead when resolving attempts to hijack cyberware.
