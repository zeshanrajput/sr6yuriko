## Chidi Ené

Ené leads the Igbo tribe and, by default, the Area Boys, easily earning him a seat on the Lagos Council. Ené is an older ork male with impressive tribal scarring across his face, and he is the righthand man of King Nnamdi. He’s an ambitious man—some say too ambitious, which is why he’s in Lagos, far away from the king. Ené doesn’t seem to mind his pseudo exile, as Lagos has made him a very rich man. Ené oversees Igbo interests from his compound on Victoria Island, an elegant white mansion surrounded by vibrant grounds hiddenbehind double-thick walls kept gleaming white by an army of groundskeepers. Ené won himself a position on the Lagos Council almost twenty years ago and has held it with a heavy-handed grip. Most of Ené’s power comes from his indirect control of the Area Boys, which boasts many Igbo members and is easily influenced by the vast amounts of cash at his disposal.

Behind the power of the king and Ené are the oil corporations battling for control of the Lagos pipeline. This power position is threatened immensely by Okoro’s efforts to call these corporations to court to face the music for their ecological offenses.
