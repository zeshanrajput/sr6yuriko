## **Motion Sensors**

**Motion sensors are tricky to deal with. The best way to avoid detection from them is to not be within range. These devices use active ultrasound to detect motion within a limited feld, and they rarely have a range greater than twenty meters. If you have an ultrasound sensor, you can detect them. If you have an image link, you can set up an ARO to represent them visually to avoid accidentally straying within their radius. The problem is that motion sensors are usually set up to detect an area that you need to get through. If you can avoid them, great, but what if they’re in your way and there’s no alternative available?**

**My frst choice would be to rely on hacking. If your team’s hacker can gain access to a motion sensor, they can tell it to pretend not to notice you. These things are often hardwired to a security network, so you might not be able to access them wirelessly. A good way around this is to stick a data tap on some other device that’s part of the network: a door lock, camera, security console, etc. No system is completely secure.**

**If the hacker is too busy handling other things, or if their skills aren’t up to the task of overcoming the security system’s frewall, there’s not much else you can do.**

**Option one is to move very, very, slowly. Most motion detectors will ignore movement under a specifc threshold. If you’re patient enough and don’t have to worry about other means of detec-**



**tion, you can move slowly and carefully through a motion detector feld without triggering an alarm. It’s very hard to do, so it should pretty much be your last resort. I recommend picking up a motion sensor and practicing this at home before you even think about trying it on a run.**

**Option two relies on misdirection and distractions. Motion sensors are probably the most likely sensors to trigger a false alarm, especially when used outdoors. Insects, squirrels, and other little critters can set them off, and there’s no reliable way to set up an auto-flter without using much more expensive equipment. If you can trigger a bunch of false alarms over time before your run, you might be able to get security to look the other way or even ignore the output from a motion sensor. This has the potential of making security hyper-vigilant instead, though, so it carries risks.**
