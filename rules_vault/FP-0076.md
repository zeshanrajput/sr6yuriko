## **Recruitment**

**Many people will tell you that having two diviners in a room is one too many, unless you’re enamored of hearing them fght about their competing interpretations of the future. Convergence isn’t of that opinion. They believe that even a vision completely opposite of one they have on fle can be useful, as it might tell you that you had misinterpreted the frst vision, or that things are not as you thought they were. They do not believe there is such a thing as too many visions, as long as the seers have a good reputation. They will dedicate all the human and AI power they need to build connections and make the information useful.**

**That’s what’s in it for Convergence—but what do the people they recruit get out of it? Unfortunately for Convergence, the people they recruit are getting less than they used to. It wasn’t that long ago that a position with Convergence brought good pay but better prestige. Convergence was thought to be recruiting the best of the best, so if you were targeted, you must be good at what you do.**

**But time has passed, and Convergence is on the third or fourth wave of recruitment, so can they still be targeting the best of the best? Maybe, because sometimes people hold out for a long time, but that’s simply not the impression most people have at this point. They fgure Convergence is on their third and fourth choices, so these people can’t be as prestigious as the frst wave. Whether it’s fair or not, a job with Convergence will no longer raise your status as high as it did.**

**Which does not make it a bad job. If you’ve never worked for an organization that doesn’t need outside revenue, try it sometime. The vibe is completely different than any other workplace. People still worry about budgets and money sometimes, because everyone does, but the stress and scramble for nuyen is simply not there. Year in and year out, you know where your budget is coming from, and you don’t have to do anything to get it. As long as the global fnancial system doesn’t collapse, you’ll have the money you need. Not only does that tend to make the atmosphere of these organizations pretty laid-back, but it allows them to focus on larger issues more often. They can take the 10,000-meter-high view, look at trends, and just take a step back and** _**think**_ **. You’d be surprised (or maybe not, depending on how many corporate jobs you’ve held) at how few businesses get a chance to do that.**
