## **Critter Security Trainer**

**This is the most basic level, in that it should be pretty clear that critters don’t maintain themselves. They need food, they may need some grooming, and they usually need someone to clean up whatever area they’re drekking in. Not only that, but many of their security procedures are not automatic. Does a noise pigeon automatically mess up Matrix signals? Yes. Do they always stay where you want them to be or move in the patterns you want? Absolutely not. They need training to learn and maintain those patterns.**

**So if you want to mess with security critters, one fun way to do it is to fnd the trainer and mess with them. It can be a long-term operation, because critter discipline doesn’t break down immediately, but there is one advantage to messing with the trainer instead of messing with the critters—the trainer usually leaves the facility at some point, while the critters don’t (you’re not going to be able to intercept a hell hound during their evening commute). That leaves a point of vulnerability.**



- **It may go without saying, but I’m going to say it anyway: In order to make this kind of plan work, you have to identify that there are security critters where you’re going, and you have to identify the trainer. No facility is going to go out of their way to make either task easy for you.**
