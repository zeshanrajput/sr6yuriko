There are few environments on the globe that contain more mystery and mysticism than the jungles and rainforests. Bursting with life, they thrill the scientists of the world who delve into their treacherous depths to discover new forms of life and document the hidden wonders of Mother Nature. Less poetic are the corporate-sponsored endeavors that seek out new resources to exploit, bringing the world both new cures and calamities.

Defi ned by precipitation, the rainforests of the world receive in excess of three meters of rain per year, and some even more. This deluge of water brings about an environment teeming with abundant life. The vegetation of the rainforest is primarily evergreen plants, with trees that grow tall to create a canopy that limits light reaching the forest fl oor. This creates an immense variety of lower-level plant life that is exceptionally hearty and adapted to a life with less sun. Many Awakened plants thrive here, especially those that have increased their potential nutrient sources to include fauna as well as sunshine. Along with fl ora, fauna here is abundant and well adapted to a variety of niches. Each level of the rainforest is its own private ecosystem, fl owing and feeding into the cycle of life.

The Amazon. The Congo. The Daintree. The Sundaland. The Indo-Burma. These are the largest and most well-known of the world’s rainforests, but they are not the only ones. Rainforests now cover sixteen percent of the land on Earth. This growth from last century’s deforestation is thanks to the return of magic and the efforts of ecologically friendly nations to protect, expand, and learn to develop within rather than without the thick vegetation. It’s spurred on by the metahuman shift
