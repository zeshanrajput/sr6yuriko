## **National Divisions**

**Typically, these divisions are separate policing organizations within a national police force structure, but for city-states where the national boundaries are not much larger than the metropolitan boundaries, these duties are often incorporated into the local police force.**

**Anti-Terrorism divisions, departments, or forces will focus on tracking extremist groups and their members that are active within a force’s territory. This includes both Matrix and physical surveillance, with anti-terrorism forces leaning heavily on Matrix and drone assets. National anti-terrorism forces incorporate these assets into their own structure while local police will draw them from other divisions. These organizations are known for using undercover investigators to infltrate suspected terror groups, who will then report back on the group’s operations once they’re embedded.**

- **People working in this division are generally very keen at developing contacts in the shadows. Compared to other units, they care far less about procedure and admissibility in court. What they want is to know when Green War will strike the oil rig of Gaeatronics, east of Vancouver, and who is providing them with the gear to do so. For this kind of intel, they are more than happy to use their far-reaching access to numerous other divisions and unit databases and trade with you their little secrets …**

- **Sim-Eon**

**Border Patrol is like many other types of patrol divisions in structure and operation, except that border patrol will focus their attention on the edges of a national territory. Normally, this force, or division when talking about the local government version, focuses on stopping illegal border crossings and smuggling through a mix of vehicle patrols, sensor nets, and drone assets. Some also employ magical assets such as watchers, patrolling spirits, and astral patrols. As with anti-terrorism, these are internal assets on a national force or drawn from other departments in local jurisdictions. The offcers in border patrol will also work closely with other national-style departments, especially customs and immigration.**

**Customs offcers are a common fxture at any point of entry within a sovereign territory. At airport terminals they can be found inspecting luggage, and at ports customs offcials will spot-check incoming cargo for contraband. Reliance on drone assets and an assortment of sensors is common, with customs typically having a small number of agents relative to other national forces. In fact, many locations**

**Fish and Game is a common force in nationalities with undeveloped wilderness areas, or territory containing protected species. This force will be similar in structure to border patrol or immigration but focuses on internal wilderness areas, checking licenses, looking for poachers, and making the occasional wilderness rescue. As with any force operating in isolation, interception of smugglers is also part of the job. Just as with other specialized national forces, it’s often combined into other forces or departments.**

**Immigration offcers focus on non-nationals traveling into and out of sovereign territory, removing those who come in illegally, and processing applications for national SINs and citizenship for those who qualify. Immigration divisions work closely with both customs and border patrol, occasionally being merged with one or both. A lot of work for immigration is Matrix background searches and SIN verifcations that rely heavily on Matrix assets. The existence of corporate SINs and extraterritoriality can make the job of immigration enforcement harder, with megacorporations less willing to give up information on their citizens than other nation-states.**
