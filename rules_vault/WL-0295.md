## **> Leech**

**Somewhat surprising for such a large and capable predator, megalodons have been spotted in packs of up to fve individuals. These packs have been known to take on pods of leviathans and come out on top. We have even observed meistersingers with megalodon bite scars on them.**

**Megalodons are, like their progenitors the great whites, unable to pump water over their gills, so they must remain in motion. They also are slightly negatively buoyant, so they must continually actively swim in order to survive. Though there have been some specimens observed lying on the bottom in a current while they enter a catatonic state, allowing the current to push water through their mouths and over their gills, most megalodons are seen swimming while resting, which is not uncommon among sharks. More research on the great white and the megalodon is most defnitely needed.**

**As a pelagic shark, megalodons can be found anywhere in the world. They rarely dive below 750 meters, at least as far as we have been able to determine, but megalodons can be found in any ocean or sea at any given time. Like many species of shark, megalodons have a very advanced sense of smell, allowing them to detect a few drops of blood in the water dozens of kilometers away. When they detect a large amount of blood, they are often driven into a feeding frenzy, where literally anything and everything that moves is seen as prey, even each other when grouped into packs.**
