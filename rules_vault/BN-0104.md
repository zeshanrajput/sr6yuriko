## Mitsuhama Helping Hand

## (Small Anthro Drone)

The Bust-a-Move series of drones was a huge global hit for Mitsuhama, and not only with the target market of children. When the kids moved on to the next thing, the old Busta-Moves found a second life as a helper drone. As the fads came and went on the Bust-aMoves, Mitsuhama re-purposed some of their production lines to create the Helping Hand, which was designed as a helper drone instead of a children’s toy.

Gecko tips were added so they can get those hard to reach places, but the rest of the drone was scaled back to save costs. These drones compete directly with the Shiawase Bi-Drone Butler. The smaller frame is marketed as convenient and more discreet, and the climbing ability adds a capability the Shiawase drone lacks.

Personally, I have two. They’re pit crew, chef, loader, back scratcher, door opener, all in one. I keep one on my main drone carrier and another back at the shop. Solves all manner of minor inconveniences for someone without opposable thumbs.
