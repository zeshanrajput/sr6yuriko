## Train

The 2005 quake transformed New York transit forever, and the majority of underground lines were damaged to the point of abandonment. Instead of digging out the tunnels, the MDC invested heavily in an SK-designed monorail system that moves along the outer rim of the city and crisscrosses through several major streets. Transfers between El trains are usually free, as the only checkpoints are when you enter the elevated terminals.

The Seventh Avenue “Freedom Tunnel” Line is the primary surviving underground rail line, though a few others have since been dug out. Parts of the Freedom Tunnel were damaged in the quake, but enough of the main tunnel remained intact to rebuild as a shortened maglev route that begins at West 59[th] Street’s Lenape Circle and ends at Columbia University on the edge of New Harlem.
