## Matrix Support

## Posted by: Shade

Most hackers (technomancers as well as deckers) tend to be more focused on the Matrix than developing their social skills. There are, of course, exceptions out there, but you can’t ignore how much effort and attention are required to get—and stay—good enough at hacking to (a) be useful, and (b) survive. It doesn’t leave time for much else. But that doesn’t mean your hacking skills aren’t the most powerful support asset the face (or anyone else on your team, for that matter) has going for them. A good liar can trick a guard into believing they’re supposed to be let in, but they can’t convince the local host to add their name to a list or get the weapon and cyberware scanners built into the doors to keep quiet about your team’s hidden hardware. They can’t edit security camera footage to back up their lie. Not like we can. We work best when we have someone else to do the talking, and they work best when we’re on top of our game and turning their lies into reality—or rather, virtual reality.

And is there any other kind, truly?

The first important service you can provide your team’s face with is Matrix overwatch. Tie their comms into your PAN so your better firewall (and other systems) can protect them from getting hacked. Have an agent program or sprite keep vigilant watch on what’s going on in the Matrix around you, especially the ones that are running silent. If the opposition or mark gains access to your face’s files or intercepts their comms, any negotiations are going to be at a severe disadvantage. And a con can be completely ruined by having the wrong information revealed to the mark.

Your usefulness doesn’t end there, however. Covering your team’s sensitive gear with your PAN is just the bare minimum. Another tool in your kit is your ability to find information: current events, social media profiles, public tracking data, and private files. Your face will be much better prepared for an encounter if they’re armed with all the available information you’ve gathered for them before the meeting, assuming you’re communicating with them during the meeting. You may be able to look up and provide additional information on the fly. Make sure your face is equipped with an image link (contacts and earbuds will do if they don’t havecyberware—glasses and headphones would be too obvious) so you can share audio and visual data as needed. Ideally, they’ll also be able to send you text information via DNI. If they don’t have a datajack, make sure they at least have trodes hidden under a hat, wig, or otherwise concealed on them. This allows them to send you messages with just a thought. I know the Mindlink spell is all the rage these days, but you can’t transfer a file using a spell.

The most critical application of your talents is hacking, of course. In this, you and your face complement each other perfectly: You hack the electronics while your face hacks the people. No amount of hacking is going to convince a suspicious guard to stop worrying about the stranger who wants to enter a secure location, and no amount of social cajoling is going to convince a file to change its contents to reveal that the stranger should be allowed to enter said secure location. When the face lies, it’s up to you to make the computers lie to support them. Look for the most critical and vulnerable devices to hack. For example, you certainly aren’t going to be able to manufacture a fake SIN on the fly or hack the SIN registry to lay a false trail, but when that cop runs your face’s SIN, you can hack their commlink to edit the response to report whatever details you need it to. And that’s just one possibility: You can send false messages, fabricate or sanitize video footage and audio recordings, hack sensors to report a false positive, create distractions by setting off alarms elsewhere—to name just a few. We can accomplish

much by hacking, and the face can go far with a smile and a few words, but a face and hacker working together are an unstoppable force.
