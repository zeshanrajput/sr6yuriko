## Biosculpting

Biosculpting is a medical specialty that combines plastic surgery, body shaping, cosmetic alteration, and bio-implants. Throughout history, (meta)human social behavior and insecurities have been profoundly linked to personal appearance and ideals of beauty. In the 2050s, physicians gained the tools that allowed them to make the metahuman form as malleable as clay in the right hands. Those tools and skills continue to be improved and refined.

As I said previously, there are more clinics than police stations in most areas. So many biosculpting options are available that it would be impossible to list them all here. Instead, we have grouped them into gender/ethnicity, minor, moderate, severe, metatype reassignment, and troll/ork reduction categories. The gamemaster determines what category a particular cosmetic change falls into. Costs

can vary dramatically, and may also depend on the specific surgeon’s reputation or standard clientele, and so are they also under the gamemaster’s purview. Additionally, a player should never receive a dice or Edge benefit from biosculpting. Several specific biosculpting mods are also noted below.

Biosculpting and cosmetic surgery use the standard surgery rules for Cosmetic Surgery/Biosculpting. Biosculpting does not come in the Used category.

**Gender/Ethnicity Modification:** Universal Omnitech brought us the revolutionary Zimmerman Method of biosculpting and has expanded its techniques so it covers both ethnicity modification and gender affirmation. While previous approaches in this particular field were based on speedy rebuilds, adding bio-material while stripping away the unnecessary, the Zimmerman Method is a slower, more holistic method that’s garnered rave reviews and is now the method used worldwide.

The entire process takes a week of vat-time and remains expensive, but demand has been high enough that the core process is now available in most body clinics. It should be noted that the patient’s genetic structure remains unchanged, and modern law requires the person’s biometrics to be linked to their SIN both before and after the procedure for comparison, so this isn’t a good way to lose pursuit unless you plan on burning that SIN afterward.

**Minor Modification** : These cosmetic changes typically involve a mere injection, removal of fat layers, or similar light procedures. Fuller lips, nose jobs, hair transplants, ear tucks, liposuction, decorative scarification, fingerprint removal, and smaller or larger breasts are all considered minor modifications, requiring only light cosmetic surgery.

**Moderate Modification** : Moderate biosculpts require more extensive changes and surgery. This can include extensive face or head reshaping (i.e., making your face look like someone’s who roughly has the same head shape), the breaking and resetting of bones, and/or minor cosmetic biotech implants. Typical moderate modifications would include altering apparent ethnicity, giving a face an anime idol appearance, replacing hair with feathers, or adding animal or unusual-looking ears.

**Severe Modification** : Cosmetic surgery and biosculpting on this scale completely changes the appearance of the recipient. With this procedure, anybody can be made to look like anybody else, including altering the height of the person by adding or removing leg or upper body length.

**Metatype Reassignment** : This procedure modifies the body, allowing for one metatype to pass for another as long as the metatypes are similar. This allows an ork to pass for a human or a human to pass for an elf. Because of the unusual nature of some of the variations, metavariants are unable to take advantage of this surgery. Similarly, trolls and dwarfs are unable to use this adaptation.



Any basic elf, human, or ork (not metavariants) can have their body sculpted to resemble an elf, human, or ork (not metavariants), thus resulting in a metatype poser. Only the looks of the character change; genetically, their original metatype will still be readily identifiable. The changes are purely cosmetic; no metatype bonuses or penalties are obtained with this treatment.

**Troll/Ork Reduction** : Troll or ork (not metavariants) reduction involves a multi-stage process of dermal-plate removal, tusk reduction, hair removal, symmetrical re-balancing, and a host of other little tweaks to try and make a troll more accepted within mainstream society. Because of the unusual nature of some of the variations, metavariants are unable to take advantage of this surgery. The process is more expensive but less invasive and pretty much turns a troll or ork into a big human.

The patient using troll/ork reduction finds they generally appear human at first glance and gain +2 dice on Con (Disguise) tests to hide their metatype and appear more human. Trolls also lose the Dermal Deposits racial quality.
