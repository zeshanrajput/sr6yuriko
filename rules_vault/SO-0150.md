## Obstacles

Here’s the problem. As I stated before, you can find anything on the Matrix. That includes medical information. It’s plentiful and the reputable sources are well-established. So your marks should be the folks who can’t really grasp the scientific theories behind medicine or blatantly disregard them. Simplyput, you’ve got to make sure that you know more than your mark does about medicine and pharmacology. And you’ve gotta be able to spin anything that they read on the Matrix so that it supports your sales pitch. Most importantly is to know when to drop it. If somebody is side-eyeing your pitch and begins to ask the critical questions, find a polite and unobtrusive way to drop the subject. This person isn’t your mark. So just drop it. Being pushy leads to suspicion, and suspicion can frag up the whole operation.

Secondly is selecting your price point. If you’re wanting to set up a recurring venture, you want to price it appropriately. Not too cheap but not too expensive either. You want them to think they’re getting a deal, not getting ripped off. And you still want to turn a profit. So let’s take a page out of the corporate playbook and do what some pharmaceutical companies do. New expensive product? Here’s a coupon. It makes your product seem very valuable yet affordable. Then, after some time, drop the price and stop the coupons.
