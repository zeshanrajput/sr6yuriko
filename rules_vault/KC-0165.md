## Physical Location

The two main Ayeowo hawala offices are just outside the airport and in the heart of the Ajegunle slums. Ayeowo can also be encountered (and evidence can be found) at her apartment in Mushin, between the two offices.

The airport office is actually a broken-down bus and a storage container on a trailer base shoved up next to each other and covered in earth. It looks like a little mound house but is really just dirt tossed over tarps, sheet metal, and canvas blankets to create a cover so the outside looks natural. The windows of the bus are still visible, but the earth is piled up and shaped around them. Plants grow over the whole mound, and the front door (the former bus door) is covered in vines, making this hawala office difficult to locate from astral space, concealed beneath living auras. The door of the bus has a heavy padlock (rating 4) on it after hours but no lock during the day. The interior of the front portion of the bus has potted plants hanging in front of the windows, blankets over the walls, pillows and cushions all along the floors for seating, and a metal partition welded into the middle with blankets hanging on it and a heavy gate in the center. The gate has a heavy bar to lock it from the inside and gets another heavy padlock after hours. Behind the gate is Ayeowo’s office, where she meets those looking for loans or currency. These bus windows also have potted plants, and the walls are covered with blankets. A trio of swiveling seats sit beneath the windows in front of a desk. Behind the desk, a section of the bus has been replaced with a heavy gate that leads into the storage container. The container is full of shelves and cabinets and is used for storing items to be fenced, as well as a small shop where Ayeowo’s hawala tokens are made from bits of specially treated bark.

Beneath the desk in the bus, hidden under a rug, and in the shop under a big couch are secret passage panels leading to a space below the trailer and bus. This crawlspace is intended as a hiding spot, not a spot to hang out.



The office in the heart of Ajegunle Slums is Ayeowo’s original office before Okoro funded her operations. It’s not used as frequently as the airport office, but Ayeowo is in this office at least one or two days each week for a few hours to keep her connection to the people of Lagos. When she is not here, the office is run by Andiya Basimlake, her trusted assistant. This office is in transition. It was originally an old bus, similar to her airport office, but this one is in the midst of a similar conversion. The last attempt to bury it was mostly washed away in heavy rains. The bus is currently half-buried and covered in netting and seeds for growing roots to hold the soil. The door has a padlock (rating 2) after hours and can be barred from the inside. The front area of the bus is converted into a small sitting office with a little sleeping area in back, behind a hanging curtain of blankets.

Ayeowo’s apartment is located in Mushin, about five kilometers from the two offices. It’s a single-story expansive estate she recently moved into. The whole space is surrounded by a twometer-high adobe wall with an iron double gate up front and a smaller iron gate on the side. Between the wall and the house are five meters of open ground that is a mix of mud, local ground plants, and shallow pits that catch water in heavy rains. The house is a rectangle, thirty meters by twenty meters. The section near the side gate is a ten-meter by twenty-meter barracks for the guards who stay with Ayeowo. It has a sleeping area, kitchen room with dining table, and a large recreation area. The other section has three bedrooms, a kitchen, dining room, sitting room, and an entertainment center. Restrooms are external, with a quartet of them near the back wall for the guards and a more nicely appointed pair for Ayeowo and guests at the house set closer to the rear door of the private quarters. The overall aesthetic is eco-chic with real vines, tons of plants, large overhanging trees, and numerous birds and small animal species present.
