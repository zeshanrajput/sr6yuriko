## burned-out mage

## Playing Advice

The burned-out mage is a fairly unique archetype in the world of _Shadowrun_ . Unlike most other archetypes, it is not highly specialized in one specific area. They’re not as deadly as a street samurai when the lead starts flying, nor are they as capable of drawing upon the diverse range of arcane power as a mage or a shaman. They’re not as charming as the face, nor do they tap into the Matrix like a decker. In short, they can’t do any of these things as well as the experts above. Instead, their strength lies in their ability to do a little bit of everything. This means when the drek is hitting the fan, the burned-out mage might be able to pull some tiny talent out of their unique blend of cybernetics and spellcraft to save the day and earn the paycheck.

Magic and technology are diametrically opposed in the Sixth World, but somehow the burnedout mage walks with a foot in each world without living in either. This means that their diversity of understanding makes them a powerful asset to any team. They might not know everything about

arcane critters the way a shaman might, but they probably know something. They’re not going to have their reflexes wired to the bleeding edge like a more chrome than flesh street samurai, but they’ve certainly got a technological trick or two up their sleeve when the lead starts to fly. The burned-out mage, above all, is able to adapt to the situation at hand regardless of what that situation might be.

Moreover, burned-out mages are often older and more world-weary than other runners. They began as Awakened spellcasters but were pushed out of that path and moved to cybernetics. This means they took the time to understand both magic and technology and have firsthand experience with both. That kind of experience takes time to acquire, and the fact that they survived it speaks to the fact that unique insights were gained along the way. Those keen insights and honed instincts are both intangible and invaluable when teamed up with younger, more focused runners who think that all their perfectly polished areas of expertise make them some kind of untouchable master.

The burned-out mage knows better. They know that the mean streets of the Sixth World will kick their ass, and they’re not afraid to play dirty—and they’ve got a really, really large playbook. When playing a burned-out mage, players are encouraged to find unique and creative uses for seemingly innocuous or apparently subpar spells and cyberware. The burned-out mage’s true power is in their ability to blindside their foes with the tiny, unexpected spells and enhancements that no one saw coming—or at least not until it’s too late.

Their real-life experience breeds cynicism, and burned-out mages have been around the block enough times and survived to know when things aren’t on the level. Beyond spells and cyberware, burned-out mages can be surprisingly skilled at seeing a bad deal or double-cross a kilometer off. This can easily be reflected in increased Perception or Intuition, often making them highly effective negotiators when it’s time to get real and talk nasty. The burned-out mage has eaten enough bulldrek sandwiches for a lifetime and a half and won’t tolerate a steaming pile of it slapped down on their plate only to be told by a smiling employer to gulp it on down like a fine meal.

Above all things, the burned-out mage is unorthodox and a survivor. No one sees them coming, they’re unpredictable, and they’re not afraid to get down and dirty. But that ability to survive leaves scars that no cyberdoc can cover, along with regret for past mistakes that hang like foci from their belt and chrome in their skull. It colors every choice they make today as much as it did in all the yesterdays gone by.
