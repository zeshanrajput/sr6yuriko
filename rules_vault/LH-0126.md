## New Blood

Chris eventually convened with scions of other American Mafia families. United by their disillusionment with the old leadership, the young heirs hatched a daring plan to stage a coup to seize control of their families’ empires and usher in a new era of dominance.

Their first move was subtle and calculated. Leveraging their access to the inner workings of their respective families, they used the funds and resources they’d siphoned off to build a war chest for their uprising. Under the guise of legitimate business dealings, they pushed nuyen into fronts and shell corporations, which shielded their activities from prying eyes. Coincidentally, Chris had been using this same strategy for years, and many now look back and wonder if this was his plan all along. If anybody within Q-gene-9 knew, they were either too disillusioned, or too committed, to question him.

As tensions simmered beneath the surface, Chris and his cohorts planted seeds of dissent among the ranks, whispering promises of a brighter future and rallying support to their cause. Slowly but surely, they gained the loyalty of key capi and soldatis, forging alliances that would prove crucial in the days to come. Some of these alliances, such as the Romero family in New Orleans, ended up working against them. The Kozlowski family, a progressive and female-led group, didn’t have members in Q-gene-9, and as soon as the turmoil started, they moved against the fracturing Romero family. Still, for many of the other Q-gene-9 members, the plan would work beautifully.

Their greatest challenge lay in confronting their own blood. Don Morelli, a patriarch steeped in tradition, proved a formidable adversary with an unyielding grip on power. Still, beneath the veneer of respect and loyalty, cracks began to appear, as whispers of discontent echoed through the ranks. The night of reckoning arrived like a storm on the horizon, and Q-gene-9 all made their move at the same time. Orchestrating a coordinated assault on their families’ strongholds and striking with surgical, brutal precision, they seized control of key assets and infrastructure, crippling their parents’ ability to retaliate. It’s now known as “ _La notte è giovane,_ ” or “The Night is Young.”

When the dust had settled, over forty of the fifty-six capo who sit on the American Commissione had been replaced. With everything else going on, there’s been little fallout. The Kozlowski family in New Orleans came out unscathed, and many assumed their capa, who was respected by many of the new Dons and Donnas, might try to be the new Capo di Tutti Capi. Nobody in America had held the titlefor over one hundred years, the last being Salvatore “Lucky” Luciano. Yet, she made no play and even contacted Chris about him assuming the role. She even suggested a new nickname to solidify his place. A short time later, Christopher “Slap Patch” Morelli became the Capo di Tutti Capi for the Americas.
