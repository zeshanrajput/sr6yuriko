## **The RCC Advantage**

**If you’ve got a decent RCC running Virtual Machine, you probably have six program slots available. They’re pretty quick and easy to swap out as needed, so you might as well have a wide range of programs and autosofts ready to load. Besides Virtual Machine, I almost always run Encryption. As far as autosofts go, Clearsight, Evasion, Navigation, and Tracking are great to have because they aren’t drone-specifc. Maneuvering and Stealth can really take up a lot of space because you need a separate autosoft for each drone type. Targeting is going to be a necessity for combat, but you need a separate autosoft for every specifc weapon. Some drones beneft from the Close Combat autosoft, but getting stuck up close is a good way to lose them. Drones with a drug or toxin injector like the**

**MCT Hornet can make very effective use of that one, though. Just don’t ever expect a combat drone to last more than half a second against a street samurai or adept. If you can track down that pesky technomancer that’s been hacking your drek, though, you can do some hacking and slashing of your own—I can tell you from personal experience that there’s nothing more satisfying.**
