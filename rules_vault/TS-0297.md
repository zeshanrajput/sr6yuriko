## **Cr ime Scene Investigator**

**Uses: recent cases, evidence, forensics (respected feld) Places to Meet: coffee shop, offce, street vendor Similar Contacts : beat cop, detective**

**The crime scene investigator’s role is to methodically process crime scenes, gathering and preserving evidence that could be critical to solving a case. From documenting the scene to collecting evidence and conducting detailed forensic analyses, a member of the CSI team plays a crucial part in piecing together the puzzle of a crime. They do this by meticulously examining the evidence, then contributing their valuable insights and expertise, which can aid in solving cases. But a CSI isn’t just about solving cases; they also provide accuracy and reliability of evidence, as well as adding context as an expert witness in court cases.**

**Like many other law enforcement providers, Crime Scene Investigations is broken into three areas: magic, Matrix, and mundane. Each feld will search for and collect information from their respective area. For example, if someone is killed via a Powerbolt spell while talking on their phone, the magic investigator will look for astral signatures and things of that nature, the Matrix investigator will track commlink information to see who the victim was talking to or look at their Matrix search history, and the mundane investigator handles the physical crime scene.**

**Their frsthand knowledge of criminal investigations and forensic science provides unique insights that can be of use to those interested in understanding or bypassing the crime-solving processes.**
