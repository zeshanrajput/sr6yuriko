## Regulatory Capture

Once in place, the state can be used to maintain corporate supremacy. It’s not enough to just create and dominate markets through sheer size and direct action against one’s opponents, but the megacorporations engage in a number of systems to maintain their power through legal, economic, and political means. By creating the laws and rules that everyone within a given space has to abide by under the force of arms or economic ruin, they are also able to maintain systems that, at the least, protect their market shares at the size of when the law and rule is enacted. They are also able to establish economic and legal barriers to prevent intrusions by outsiders without the capital to invest in jumping through every hoop and crossing every moving goalpost that the existing powers had already met, because they’re the ones who knew where to install the hoops and goals.

There are other values as well. It gives corporations cover to avoid legal liability from each other and from third parties, especially _government_ parties, because they they can fall back on the defense of “We were just following the law.” It’s also a way of maintaining standards and dumping a lot of the busywork and detail-oriented work onto outside bodies, which they can then use to justify their own “research” and data. The same process is done with non-profits, NGOs, IGOs, and other entities—all of which exist because of the state, in chartering and often in funding. When you want to create a story, it helps to have a paid chorus repeating everything you say and giving you secondary confirmation to repeat the story.

- Just a note to say that this is not the way it **has** to be—NGOs, in other systems, can serve as important checks to the government—but in our world, this is the way it **is.** It sounds hard to believe, but there are circumstances where power is shared with the intent to elevate those it is shared with, rather than trying to get another excuse to control them. We just overlook that possibility because we rarely see it happen.
