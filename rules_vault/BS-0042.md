## Optional Rule:

## The Bleeding Edge

The opposite of obsolescence. With this rule, you spend time remaining on the very forefront of cutting-edge technologies and techniques, and you are rewarded for that.

To remain on the bleeding edge, a runner must dedicate time and money to staying up to date. If a runner spends twenty percent of their downtime staying on the edge, they can nominate a skill, piece of ’ware, gear item, spell, spirit, complex form, or sprite. A runner with a day job probably has time to keep up to date with one item. A runner with no other downtime activities can keep ahead of up to three (four if the runner has a sleep regulator).

There is also a monetary cost to this. Subscribing to the latest magazines, buying the latest tools, or bribing the right people is not always cheap. A runner can expect to spend around 500 nuyen per month for the privilege of staying on the bleeding edge for each item.

Further, the state of the art doesn’t happen in a vacuum. The reason that something is obsolete is because other people are doing things differently. For this reason, a runner will need at least one contact who is connected to the community that makes whatever they’re focused on.

For the nominated item, choose one specific type of test (for spirits, it might be resisting drain, or the initial summoning; for firearms, it might be a standard shot or a called shot). That roll has +1 die; that die should be a wild die, unless there already is a wild die in the roll.
