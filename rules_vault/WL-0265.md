**Locating these regions is easy—it’s in the name. While most of what we focus on here is whether** the critters are friend or foe, these parts of the world deserve a special mention about the environment trying to kill you, too. Between the cold, the massive variations in solar exposure, and the unstable glaciology of the icepack, just walking around is a potential death sentence.

How is the ice trying to kill you? Well, besides being cold and a ridiculous heat sink that pulls energy from everything around it, it is also often unstable and unpredictable. In the northern region, the icepack can range in thickness, meaning it can be anywhere between, “this drek is so thick it will rip a hole in the hull of your boat if you hit it” to “oops, my gengineered microhusky broke through.” Add in the fact that those thick packs could have gaps and holes in them, and you can never be quite sure where it’s safe to tread.
