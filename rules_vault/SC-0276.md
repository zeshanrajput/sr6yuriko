- **Skills:** Engineering (Gunnery) 5, Perception 3+, Piloting 6, Stealth 3+

- **Knowledge skills:** (Area) Knowledge, Drone Customization, Drone Sports

Such expensive cyberware would have normally been unaffordable, but they are often included as part of a long-term military contract. When you came of age, you became eligible for such a contract. Not long after receiving your first augmentations, you were placed into active duty as a military drone rigger. You had access to a military-grade RCC and all the drones you could hope for. As an officer, you also learned how to lead and direct personnel as well as drones. You used a combination of reconnaissance and combat drones to provide intelligence and direct support to a team of soldiers, and you were very good at it. The most surprising thing to you is how easily you seemed to get along with your superiors and subordinates. Your technical aptitude is matched by a talent for dealing with people, which made you a valuable officer. Life was interesting, mostly the way you liked it, though you could have done with fewer tragic losses. Soldiers die. Your last mission was an absolute disaster. Only moments before the worst happened, the hacker on your team sent you a frantic message. You only had time to glance over it before the disaster struck, but you learned just a moment too late that one of your superior officers had betrayed you and the team under your command. You were meant to die as part of a complicated scheme that you didn’t have the time to comprehend, because that’s when the hammer dropped. You did everything you could, but you couldn’t save them. Lives and drones were lost, and you couldn’t save them. But you survived, with the knowledge that someone with power and authority over you sold you and your team out, and expected you to be dead. You took advantage of this assumption and left your old life behind. With your skills and augmentations, a fresh start in the shadows seemed like the only path open to you. Maybe someday that path will lead to revenge or vindication. Which one do you hope for?
