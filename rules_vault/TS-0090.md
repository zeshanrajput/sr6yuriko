## **Searching the Matrix**

_**For any act, or suspected act, of electronic warfare, the lead detective must immediately transmit the elements to the Department of Matrix Security, which undertakes to produce a report within 72 hours.**_ **—Lone Star Security Services Procedure Manual, revised 2080**

**In a world as interconnected as ours, a detective would be foolish not to look into all these data trails. Without having the infated ambition of becoming a complete Matrix expert, it’s essential to understand the basic principles, to know what to look for and what to follow. And above all, you need to know when to accept a call from the GridSec expert who’ll start his support after ten minutes of pretentious monologuing about their superiority and why we’re just mediocre piles of meat.**

- **McCarthy isn’t overdoing it. There’s a real inter-departmental cold war going on in just about every LEP. Each department sees itself as indispensable yet despised by the others. Case in point is Lone Star’s Department of Matrix Security (GridSec), which is made up of more technical experts from top schools and hackers on probation rather than of police officers, and thus suffers from a severe case of impostor syndrome. Police officers in the field feed this sentiment with childish pranks, like calling them for tech support … as a way to say to them that they are “just the IT Department.”**

- **> LSPD**

**> The aforementioned DPI, which mainly contains Awakened personnel, has a similar issue. Officers are often reluctant to call on magicians, and procedures have evolved to force their hand in many cases. For their part, magicians have always argued they should be a separate corporation. The debate was reignited with the merger with OmniStar, as moving the entire department to Manadyne was a real possibility. They found a compromise, however, and obtained the desired level of autonomy and annual budget, while remaining part of Lone Star. For now. > Elijah**

**At the bare minimum, you need to understand that every piece of tech is supposed to be transmitting and receiving data through an enabled wireless connection to the Matrix. These data trails can be captured live, as part of active surveillance, or retrieved later by searching the logs of any device connected to it. This requires some Matrix skills, time, and real expertise.**

**For example, most people don’t bother shutting down the automatic transmission of their digital loyalty card to Stuffer Shacks, which facilitates purchases and credits them with fdelity points. And these little stores are everywhere. Which means that the loyalty service logs contain information on who was in the vicinity at what time. In this particular case, accessing this information, we’ll need the agreement of the parent company, but you get the idea.**

**Of course, this only applies to people who aren’t “silent” all the time. But most people, even you runners, only switch to silent mode when it’s time to be discreet. It’s not really that you don’t leave a Matrix trail, it’s just that it’s incomplete. And the part you do have, we know how to exploit.**

- **It’s also unusual for someone to not broadcast. So if you’ve got all your tech and toys off or silent, that’s going to set off alarm bells for some guards.**
