## Viper

Revenge is a dish best served cold, and it’s all the more satisfying when the target isn’t looking for it. The face fatale isn’t just an obstacle—they’re out to get one or more of the characters, either for a personal affront or on behalf of an employer. Unlike many other threats, the face fatale isn’t going to let the players see them coming unless it’s too late, if they can help it. The work of the face fatale should be hidden while the players are at least somewhat occupied by something else. Unlike the previous plotline, though, this should be the major threat to the players and not a diversion from a larger mission.

The face fatale should size up the PCs’ weakness first. Be sure to reward players who work hard to hide those weaknesses (such as a secret spouse) from those who may be out to harm them, but find where to start hurting the characters on the edges. Suddenly contacts are drying up, suppliers seem to not have what the characters need, and jobs are becoming hard to find. Perhaps a young wageslave who is easily led astray puts a hold on one of the character’s accounts.

Once the players realize something is happening, the face fatale should make their next move. They should either slide into the life of one of the players as a rescuing angel or sidle up to one of their enemies as a vengeful demon. Don’t make it obvious; the face fatale works hard to use guile and distraction to keep the focus off of them, especially if they’re in with the team and can help direct their efforts to figure out this unknown enemy. A way to get the face fatale in with the team is to pull them in as an asset, having a part-time or temporary new player play the role, though this time working against the team while embedded in it.

Once the face fatale’s trap has been sprung, the players should be at a disadvantage, off balance and on the defensive. Many of their contacts and support systems should be compromised, at least for the time being, and they are going to need to scramble to get a clear picture of what is happening. The face fatale need not be out to kill off the players; they are a master of manipulation and guile, not a psychotic go-gang leader. In the end, their goal is not only to accomplish their mission but also protect themselves by either vanishing completely or having some sort of leverage over or respect from the PCs.
