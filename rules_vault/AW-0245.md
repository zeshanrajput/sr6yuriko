## Viable Markets: Hive Pathogens

While the idea of importing alien diseases back to Earth should be considered a suicidally foolish way to turn a profit, the fact remains that it is literal alien life and has untold possible value to a number of biotechnical associations and megacorporate divisions. The fact that a live host might prove the best incubator is one most runners will likely not want to consider …

On a more mundane level of concern is the environment itself. The basic concept of the Hive as a seemingly endless series of claustrophobic tunnels holds true at the basic level. Point of order: None of the personnel or probes stationed in the Colony have ever seen a “sky” here. That being said, there are exceptions and variations within those environments.

**Tunnelworks:** The tunnels comprise roughly eighty percent of the experience of travel through the Hive. Passages range from 1.5 meters to as much as five meters at their widest, though certain strains of bugs may use larger paths, and experienced teams have learned to identify local breeds by this information combined with local material, tracks, and other signs. A tunnel may go any distance, seemingly lacking any pattern in where it branches, though certain breeds like bee spirits have been noted to show more regularity when in good health.

**Chasms and grottos:** Some areas provide a larger span, whether because over-tunneling led to collapses due to lack of structural integrity or other unknown factors. Drops of unknown depth have been documented, descending into darkness at such a distance that few probes have found their bottom or were otherwise lost (time displacement, insect interception, signal loss, etc.). Grottos are wider expanses, measuring from twenty to fifty meters. These regions often show signs of battle, including fallen insects and significant structural damage, perhaps a part of the formation at the location. More rarely, grottos seem to form around or as a result of xenoarchaeological formations and leavings, perhaps in response to as-yet-unknown properties of radiation or lingering native metaplanar attunement, or a kind of passive metaplanar rejection syndrome, much like flesh forming around an alien presence. The Colony itself has seen a slow regression of Hive material from its border, forming a grotto around us.

**Oxygen and other gasses:** It is a curiosity that the Hive seems to have a native supply of oxygen, particularly in the absence of any recognizable flora for a carbon dioxide exchange. While there is some debate at the labs whether this is some kind of manifestation of the environment accommodating an outsider (notable in that metaplanar travels to closer regions seem to accommodate the needs of the visitor vis-à-vis breathing, etc.), it should also be noted that the Hive is home to a number of novel gaseous matters of unidentified origin. While a number of these are terrestrial as we understand them (sulfur, methane, ethane, helium, etc.), several others have been detected, captured and are under analysis and awaiting classification.
