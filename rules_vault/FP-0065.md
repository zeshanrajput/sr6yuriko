## **Access Request**

**Cromwell Reeves has Two of Blades, Seven of Coins, and Comet cards but needs the secrets to their activation. He has managed to research far enough that he knows what he needs for the activation, just not the how.**

**Or so he claims.**

**The truth is the runners are bringing him three people, each related to imagery on a card, and he will then start questioning and testing on them to fgure out the secrets.**

**To cover his ruse, he tells the runners that each of the three people he needs is depicted on a card and has a birthmark that is a clue to the activation. One of the three has a birthmark, but it’s nothing special. The other two have tattoos but no birthmarks.**

**The pay will be slightly above average because kidnapping is a rather large crime and Reeves is ready for a renegotiation at the drop-off if the runners see through his ruse.**

**The three targets are all in Denver but in very different neighborhoods.**

**Denika Fasylu, the woman on the Two of Blades, lives in the barrens. She’s a squatter with little to protect her besides her stench and a trickle of magic she still has left, but she’s still whip-crack smart. She’ll be easy to grab but will be hard to keep and will constantly ask for things like food, clothes, a shower, etc. from the runners while doing annoying little magic things to piss them off or risk blowing their cover.**

**Olivia Boyd from the Seven of Coins is a corporate princess who lives downtown and goes to a private school. Security at both locations is decent but more focused on protecting corporate assets than spoiled brats.**

**After she’s grabbed, her parents apply pressure, and the hunt begins. They have ritual samples and face tracking, so keeping her hidden won’t be easy. The team will need to get a ward around her and keep her face off the public grid.**

**Rashinder Setaj, the nartaki on the Comet, is a corporate shark who is in the middle of a deal that will make or break his career. He’s working the deal from his home in Glendale, rather than his offce, because he needs to keep it under the radar. He’ll be desperate to get out and is a fantastic social manipulator. He’ll try to talk himself free with bribes and deals (far more money than the job) and will keep trying to gain access to communication if the team doesn’t block his headware link with a jammer. If they leave the headware running, he’ll be able to appear asleep and be in the Matrix making deals. If the headware is blocked, he’ll try to snag their comms, try to borrow from others he encounters, and even try to slip off for short stints to keep working the deal.**

**Once they are all brought back to Reeves, he will thank the runners, pay them, and gauge his next step on how they acted on the run. If they seem squeamish, it’s a polite separation. If they seem ruthless, he will put out another job to hang as security while he starts asking questions to get to the activation.**
