## Optional Rule: Simplified Contact Reputation

There are two ways to simplify contact reputations. The first is to have only a single faction: shadow community. This lets you focus on a contact’s reputation score with the group that’s most likely to come into play.

Second, you can use all the factions in the game, but instead of using a score, just define the contact’s relationship between each faction as positive, negative, neutral, or unknown.

**What are your flaws, weaknesses, or vices?** This is a broad question meant to identify possible leverage against the contact. Maybe the contact has an addiction to gambling? Or a family member with addictions? Maybe a family member who could be kidnapped? The contact might have a soft spot for the Damsel in Distress con. A good amount of detail about the flaws, weaknesses, and vices of the character provides a greater opportunity to define some reasons why these flaws exist. These reasons can, in turn, allow for deeper storytelling by the gamemaster.

**Who has leverage on you?** Someone may already have leverage on the contact. Or maybe a faction has dirt on them. In a lot of cases, this leverage is related to the character’s flaws, weaknesses, and vices, but it’s really about who holds that leash. Maybe they have a bunch of gambling debt that’s held by the Yakuza? Or their spouse works for MCT and controls any insurance and benefits for the family, so the spouse is essentially held hostage through the workday? Ultimately, knowing if someone already has leverage on the contact provides the gamemaster with opportunities for adventure hooks that can be discovered through legwork.

**What is your biggest fear?** Your fear can be used against you, and this is true for NPCs as well. A faction could use psychological torture to drive the contact to do something they wouldn’t normally do. Maybe they’re afraid of spiders, and someone has threatened to lock them in a room with large poisonous spiders unless they provide information on Mr. Johnson’s identity. Perhaps the contact has a deep secret they desperately want to stay hidden.

**How do you like to get paid?** Everyone likes nuyen, right? Maybe, but there are lots of different currencies that make the shadows move. It’s possible the contact operates within the containment zone and likes being paid in product if ammo is in short supply. Or they prefer to collect favors they can cash in when the need arises. Knowing how someone likes to be compensated is key to influencing them.

**What groups don’t you trust?** Maybe it was an experience when the contact was just getting started, or perhaps something much more recent. Whatever it was, there’s some group they just can’t trust. Whether that’s trolls, cops, wage slaves, the elderly, or some other group. It’s just hard for the contact to deal and work with them. This has an impact on whether they’ll be willing to rat on that specific group if they get any information regarding them.

**What behavior do you dislike?** Everyone has a pet peeve. Something that could adversely impact any of their relationships. Maybe the NPC can’t stand people who leave the lights on when they leave the room. Or they can’t deal with the person who simply must show off his flashy new gun to everyone. Maybe the character refuses to negotiate with people who chew with their mouth open.

**What groups are you a member of or just trust implicitly?** Most people in the Sixth World have a network or belong to a group. The contact could be a member of the Illuminates of the New Dawn. Or perhaps they are part of a small group of fixers who work together within a community to make sure they can fill out a roster for Mr. Johnson. Try to describe the trust network the contact belongs to. In the end, if the trusted group needs help, this contact is going to do their best to help them out.

**What is your biggest secret you are afraid will get out?** Not everyone has a secret they want hidden, but the contact might have a secret they feel is particularly damning. That secret collection of dragon reagents or Fifth World gold coins … it’s a total lie! It was totally someone spreading rumors! But the secret could be much darker. The character could be a blood mage, or they’re protecting a family member

who was turned into a vampire. Those secrets can be used against not only the contact, but any player characters who associate with the contact as well.

**What is something that you love?** The quickest way to a person’s heart has just as much of a chance of influencing them as using their weaknesses. The character could be a huge Seattle Screamers fan, and if the runners have playoff tickets they can pay for gear with, that will definitely get the contact’s attention. What if it isn’t the runner team who has what they want, but someone looking for dirt on the runner team? The contact could also be collecting rare musical instruments, and the team has found a silver bass trombone they’ve been looking for. This isn’t always about leverage. Building loyalty is also about knowing what they care about.

**What do you think of the shadowrunners in the game?** It’s good to know what the contact thinks about the team. Maybe the contact feels the team has done their jobs, but not without some failures. They could think of the team as good people they’d totally hang out and grab a beer with. Or maybe the contact has a crush on the team’s face. Their attitude toward the team and how they feel about them will change over time and could drive a lot of the story and interaction between them.
