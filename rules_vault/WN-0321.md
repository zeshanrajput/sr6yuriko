## The Job

Beneath the Quik-E-Nap facility is the abandoned 18th Street subway station. Rumors said a corp used it for some secret research that was lost in the earthquake. The station is accessible now, though, and its secrets are ready for plunder.

One of those secrets is that the research facility below Yves was government, not corporate. The condominium complex held a rather large number of CIA safehouses, often used to hold test subjects before they were transported to the lab via a hidden elevator shaft. The other access to the lab was in the rail tunnel off the 18th Street subway station, another victim of the quake, lost for years.

Now, the lab is accessible from a newly reopened access tunnel off the 18th Street subway station from the Underground, but few have dared explore it due to its high rat and devil rat (all variants) population, as well as the basilisk that was

The runners’ tip directs them to the area beneath the Quik-E-Nap (formerly the Yves Condominium building) and an access door off the subway tunnel.

The door clearly reads “DANGER: High Voltage—Authorized Personnel Only.” Its lock has long since rusted shut, and only brute force will open it—use a Body + Strength (16, 1 Combat Round) Extended test.

Beyond the door is a twenty-meter corridor with short side tunnels staggered every five meters containing dilapidated infrastructure elements such as gas, water, and electrical conduit pipes. At the end is another door, this one locked and sealed as well. The lock is controlled by a rusted wheel and an old numeric keypad. Bypassing the door takes powering up the keypad (Engineering + Logic (3) test), bypassing the code (Engineering or Hacking (4) test), and then forcing open the rusty mechanism (Body + Strength [24, 1 Combat Round] Extended test).

As the door opens, a hiss of air rushes in, and the runners hear a distant mechanical rumbling as a generator comes to life and powers up the longclosed facility. Several emergency lights slowly glow into life but most stay dark, giving the place an eerie feel. After they break the seal, the runners can smell stale, sterile air with a hint of a metallic tang that scrapes at the back of the nostrils.

The lab is only a single floor but still sizable. It is built around the central elevator that opened in four different directions to four separate sections of the lab.

The door the runners enter through is the only one in the outer perimeter of the facility. It leads to a three-meter-wide outer ring hallway that surrounds and connects the four labs. At every quarter of the circle, a hatchway door on the inner wall leads into one of the labs. Between lab doors, pairs of double doors in the outer wall lead to four suites of rooms: (A) a small living area set up for four people; (B) food storage and a mechanical room (generator, air filtration, water purification, etc.); (C) a medical suite with first aid, surgical, and full-spectrum scan capabilities based on then-current technology; and (D) a living area and armory for three security personnel.

Each lab section is identically laid out. A eight meter by two meter hallway leads from the elevator to a trio of doors—forward, right, and left.

Forward leads to a hexagonal room with four plain bunks at the center, four seats with straps to tie down subjects facing the mirrored side walls, and another door on the far wall. That door leads to the same place as the left and right doors from the first hallway.

Left and right lead to a ring for observation, as the side walls of the room are transparent. They

have another door opposite the elevator that is just slightly offline with the observation room door so subjects can’t see that door.

The labs were used for brainwashing and mind-control experiments of various sorts. Some involved drugs, others natural hormonal dosages, some torture, others long-term, repetitive audio and visual input, and many other techniques.

Information on the experiments is located on old comm terminals and pocket secretaries from the lab workers. The comm terminals are in three of the four outer living areas (the fourth was for the security detail on site). The pocket secretaries (predecessors to the commlink similar to how camera-equipped flip phones were predecessors to smart phones) rest in one of the footlockers in the security quarters, as they were gathered by the survivors after the quake.

What survivors, you ask? The vampires, of course!

Who also just woke back up and are hungry and anxious to escape.

One of the experiments performed here was on mind-control abilities of vampires as a means to understand brainwashing.

After the quake revealed they were trapped, a doctor and two security personnel plotted for their survival. They had captured a rare pre-Awakening vampire to use in their experiments. Using the vampire’s blood, the trio became Infected, but as it was pre-Awakening, they did not gain all of her abilities. They then killed the vampire, as they feared they would be controlled by her.

They used their new abilities to take charge of the survivors, rationing food and water well enough to keep their food source alive. This lasted a few years, but eventually they had to force the other survivors to eat one another. Long story short, they ran out of food near the time of the Awakening. They sealed themselves in, entered a coma-like state, and waited for the doors to open.

Decades later, the doors open, they’re hungry, and the runners are their first meal option.
