## Seed

There’s a certain Korean restaurant in Auburn that’s _definitely_ a front for the Choson Ring, but they sell the best bulgogi tofu in Seattle and are also a popular hangout for local hackers. I was sitting at their bar the other night trying to dig up info on a Mr. Johnson I’d been sent a job for, when I happened to notice that a nearby Renraku Kitsune had been pulled into a large local network, and they were all trading equities at a furious pace. Out of curiosity, I started looking for the deck’s operator, and I noticed her at the other end of the bar; she was an elf like me, laser-focused on something I couldn’t see in her AR display, but she looked up just long enough to notice me. I thought I was fragged until I realized she apparently thought I was just checking her out, and she ordered us a bottle of peach-flavored soju. I figured that the flirting was _basically_ the ideal cover, and we got to talking while I threaded a Static Veil on my persona and tried to figure out what the hell she was getting up to with all that trading. My attempts to con her into talking about it didn’t work well, but the _distraction_ did, and she didn’t seem to notice or care about what I was doing with her deck.

I noticed that the trading was focused on a lot of MCT subsidiaries, and along with the trading, there were a lot of P2.0 posts with observations about what was happening to the stocks. There were also plenty of projections on the side, with running tallies of how much further damage might be expected, or what kind of profits might be realized. I started to get nervous; while I’m not bad at facing, it was becoming increasingly apparent that the woman was clearly a trained grifter, and I desperately calculated how much soju I should drink to look casual while staying sober enough to avoid falling into one of the traps she would inevitably lay down. And just when I started digging through my companion’s recent messages to try to determine how she got involved with this network, and who else was on it, and whether it was Kechibi pulling all their strings, she shoved her deck into a holster, put her hand on mine, and asked if I want to leave there and go somewhere better.

- So why are you telling us about this? You met some woman who was probably running the Kechibi Code, and she conned you into going home with her. Who cares?

- **>** Glitch

I hadn’t been able to read any of these messages yet, and I scrambled to thread Editor and keep it up long enough to copy the whole archive onto my commlink. I desperately tried to come up with some excuse to why I couldn’t go with her, when she shoved me into an alley and told me she noticed every single thing I did in that bar— _and_ that I did it all without a cyberdeck.

- Oh, no.

- **>** Netcat

Now, I stood there, trying to hide my panic, expecting to be murdered, and I kept my concentration on Editor long enough to get those files downloaded. But _then_ she started telling me how _impressed_ she was with what she saw me do, and she told me that there was a home in her organization for people like me, and wouldn’t it be _great_ if we could crush oppressive groups like MCT together? I was kinda taken aback by this, and I didn’t really know what to say to get out of it, so I just ran.

I lay low for a couple days and took some time to skim over the messages, and I found out that the organization she was talking about was a cyberterrorist group called Seed.

- Wait, aren’t you in Seattle? I didn’t think they were **that** widespread.

- Kia

- I definitely encountered one of their agents here. What their plans are in the Emerald City specifically is beyond me.

- Vip3r

Seed had its beginnings as a radical anarchist group in Korea, an offshoot of Ex Pacis. In previous years, their goal was to topple the Korean government, but as their ranks have grown, their aim seems to have become more widespread and ambitious. They’re an aggressive anti-corporate movement, with a particularly heavy bias against the Japanocorps, and they champion causes like technomancer acceptance and AI rights. While their members possess unquestionable hacking prowess, they’re also highly skilled social engineers who tend to first obtain the information they want through charm and manipulation; and, yeah, trying to seduce their marks is a pretty common tactic.

Regarding their use of the code itself; obviously I’ve been a personal witness to one of their agents using it in an attack against Mitsuhama. How effective that ends up being, what the next step in their plan is, or when or how MCT plans to fireback at them all still remain to be seen, but given their typical MO, I doubt this is going to be an isolated hit. Seed breaks into their target systems, compromises them in some way, and then unleashes massive and lethal destruction inside. Whether the attack on MCT was simply a distraction or was their ultimate goal I can’t say, but I’d bet every last nuyen in my account that I didn’t see the whole picture.

Additionally, some of the messages I read from my acquaintance from the restaurant seemed to indicate that she and many of the other members who had encountered the Code believed that it was some sort of protosapient AI, and they intended to offer it some degree of freedom once it accomplished their goals.

- The Kechibi Code **isn’t** sapient. I’ve already stated that.

- Shell

- Oh, sure, while it may not be sapient in its **current** form, some of Seed’s members believe that it has the **potential** to be. And in all honesty, I’m not sure I disagree with them. It’s an incredibly advanced program capable of analytics and calculations that are beyond the scope of metahuman thinking, and if given the capacity to evolve, I think it could absolutely become an AI.

Pacis. This is the kind of manipulation that sets its claws and **really** digs into your mind, no matter how wrong you want to think all of it feels.
