## Biomedical applications

Although nanotechnology plays a significant part in manufacturing, some of the most important applications are in the realm of metahuman augmentation. Cybernetics would be far more intrusive without nanoscale engineering and nanites to continuously maintain the bridges to the body’s neurological system. Nanites are also used in almost every type of contemporary surgery. They weave the fibers of synthetic polymers used in dermal sheathing and create the retinal displays of cybereyes. Nanotechnology is commonly used to treat diseases and neutralize toxins, and it has also been a core driver in improvements to geneware. One of the major leaps forward for genetech was the use of nanotech to assemble the genetic code used in gene therapy—then make sure that the right genetic material is inserted in the correct tissue. Surgical nanites are also used after the genetech insertion to hunt down genetic mistakes or miscopies in the gene therapy.
