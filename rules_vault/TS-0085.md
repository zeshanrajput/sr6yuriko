## **> Legal Eagle**

- **At a crime scene, samples should be sealed and sent by forensic specialists to the nearest forensic laboratory. However, investigators have a wide variety of field scanners and drones. This situation is the result of a long tradeoff between specialization and polyvalence. Crime scenes are too important to be mishandled and need a specialized hand. But there are also situations where time is a factor (such as kidnappings), so a balance must sometimes be struck.**

- **LSPD**

**The biggest difference between my method of investigating and yours is intent. I don’t do it for a client, nor to simply fnd the guilty party. I do it to bring justice through legal means. This means preserving** _**all**_ **evidence, including the seemingly irrelevant drek, because I have to convince the judge and/or the people called upon to decide, beyond any reasonable doubt, that the evidence shows the person before them is guilty.**

**It’s through our procedures and protocols that I can do this. And that’s why investigations take so long. You and I do not have the same agenda when we investigate.**

**> McCarthy says he’s a cop driven by a sense of justice, but don’t let him fool you. He doesn’t work for Seattle, he works for Lone Star, a corporation concerned with security, not justice. They are an improved, paid-up version of the vigilante neighbor militia. They talk about due process, but in the street they trample it with their boots.**

**> Jimmy Kincaid**

**Each criminal, from the foolish one who leaves their commlink on, broadcasting their SIN while committing a crime, to the modern Artful Dodger, leaves a trace, however infnitesimal, of their passage. It could be a lost hair in the carpet, a drop of blood stuck in the victim’s fngernails, Matrix traces, or an astral signature. All you have to do is fnd those to get on their trail.**

**> Because most of us are SINless, we often don’t realize how much their SINs and general Matrix signatures track people’s moves. In a recent case, a wageslave who killed his ex-boyfriend thought he’d be okay because he shut down his commlink before doing the deed. However, scanners at the local Stuffer Shack where he waited for his prey recorded the perp’s comm ID when he entered. Long story short, his SIN activities were traced from his home to the vicinity of the shop where he procured the murder weapon, and also near the crime scene. Note that the scary part is NOT the “Big Brother is watching you,” but how easy it was for the runners who framed the poor slag by fabricating this virtual trail (plot twist!).**

**> Sim-Eon**

**> Ironically, magical evidence is often discarded because it is deemed unreliable, despite the fact that it’s far more difficult to tamper with. On the other hand, a Mind Probe spell or even just reading the aura of the poor sod while being interrogated would have shown he was truly innocent. Obviously, this spell, like any kind of mind manipulation, is forbidden under the laws against self-incrimination. But some are willing to look the other way if it points the investigation in the right direction.**

- **Matrix evidence is the opposite. Unless the data is thoroughly scrubbed, it doesn’t disappear. But it’s a needle in the haystack. Lone Star and other security corps have powerful algorithms to comb through the data, and more importantly, know what to look for. So it’s crucial in our line of work to be mindful of this. - **Netcat**

**In addition to the advice of not touching anything, an investigator should always take note of** _**everything**_ **. Write down names, everything seen at a crime scene, what witnesses tell you, the weather, and even the fragging wind direction or the smell of the nearby bodega. Keep notes of everything. The new generation of detectives is using all kinds of sensors on their commlink, and that’s a good idea. But that’s only one piece of the puzzle. Contextual data that relates to all that sensory data is still necessary.**

**Even then, forensics will take 1,000 years to send in reports, and they may still contain errors (you know, the glitch between the chair and the device), so factor that in, too.**
