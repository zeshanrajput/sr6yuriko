## Cease Fire!

## \[Influence (Intimidation), Major\]: 5 Edge

When you’ve gained an advantage in a firefight or other form of mass combat, sometimes the best way to end the fight is to give the adversaries a way out. To attempt this Edge action, the player characters must outnumber their adversaries by at least two to one—or the adversaries must at least believe they’re outnumbered. For example: Trid Phantasm spells and trid projectors might appear to inflate player character numbers. Or the player characters might have another clearly decisive advantage in play.

Make an Influence (Intimidation) + Charisma test opposed by the highest Composure dice pool among the enemy forces, adding +1 bonus dice to the Composure test for each additional enemy. This must be attempted against all enemies at once, and you can’t choose to affect fewer enemies to reduce the dice pool. If you want to attempt this Edge action against fewer enemies, you’ll just have to remove more enemies from combat first.

If successful, the enemy forces agree to stop fighting and either withdraw or let the player characters leave the area or location without further violence, as long as all hostilities cease immediately.

If player characters get as many or more net hits as the highest Willpower attribute amongtheir enemies, the enemies agree to surrender. This can allow the player characters to take their enemies captive or continue the attack. However, the latter could cause the enemy forces to renew their hostilities.

Per the GM’s discretion, some enemies such as toxic magicians, dragons, free spirits, and particularly powerful and influential people may be immune to this. Generally speaking, this includes most Awakened critters and NPCs with a Professional Rating of 6 or higher, as well as delusional megalomaniacs and combat monsters. If the target is unsusceptible to this Edge action, the GM will refund any Edge spent attempting it—though the action will automatically fail and still use up a Major Action.
