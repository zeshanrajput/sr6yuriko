## **There Is No One Right Way to Be**

**There is a lot of talk in runner bars and BBSes about the right personality for running the shadows. Should you be a taciturn, hardened professional? An improvisational agent of chaos? A motor-mouthed punk? A stoic survivor?**

**These debates are studded with the alleged strengths and (especially) weaknesses of these different types. The hardened professional is a poor communicator. The agent of chaos can’t stay focused on the ultimate objective of the mission. The punk talks too much to get the job done. The survivor is shell-shocked and will shut down at critical moments.**

**Knowing your personnel also affects recruiting. Know what you need and don’t be afraid of admitting weaknesses. Don’t think you’ve got the whole negotiations thing down just because you managed to convince a customer service representative to lower your Matrix subscription bill by two nuyen a month that one time. Know where you need help and be active in getting it.**

**That’s all crap. I’ve met good runners who ft each of those descriptions and bad runners who ft each of those descriptions. A shorthand personality description is not going to tell you how effective someone is going to be. If you want to make snap judgments about people based on artifcial personality profles, go work in corporate HR. If you decide to look for only one type of personality, or even eliminate some broad types from your consideration, you could keep yourself from working with some skilled people.**
