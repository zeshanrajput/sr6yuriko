## **Command**

**The Command of a department can vary, but someone is always in charge no matter the organizational structure. For corporate police forces this may be the CEO, or in larger corps it could be a VP of Operations that has day-to-day control. Municipal police tend to use titles like Chief of Police, Commander, or Chief Constable. Directly below whoever has overall command may be an Advisory Council or a Board of Directors. There will also be the various department heads who each manage the operation of their respective divisions. These may be Junior VPs, Division Chiefs, or Lieutenant Commanders. The largest divisions, such as Patrol or Investigations, may have additional layers of command, such as Regional Commanders, Precinct Chiefs, and Lieutenants. Eventually these chains of command end with the Sergeants or supervisors directly in charge of the rank-and-fle offcers.**

**Support divisions are less likely to have a formal multi-layered command structure and are typically organized like a corporate offce or a government department. Division managers, unit supervisors, and team leaders will oversee the technicians and clerks that make up the rank-and-fle of most support departments.**
