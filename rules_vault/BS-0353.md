## Resonant Drugs

If you’re in the market for drugs that let you do amazing technomancer things on the Matrix, there’s now a line of products that can help you do that. For an extortionate price, you could make a Resonance link with someone else, or even go on

a trip into the Resonance Realms. It doesn’t matter if you’re a hacker, a punk rocker, or a rat shaman. Of course, these things are rare—just barely leaking into the streets, really—but when you run the shadows, sometimes you need to be able to do things that just aren’t possible for a normal person. And sometimes those things are drugs that let an actual technomancer hyper-pump their Resonance for an hour to get a big job done.

These drugs seem to be coming directly out of the deeply secret labs that megacorporations prefer not to acknowledge, lured out by the very large asking price. Sometimes they come in vials that are hand-labeled from some sort of study; other times, they are delivered in a lunch box with makeshift padding. This is evidence that these experimental drugs are so valuable that the people who are prototyping them are willing to risk the wrath of their megacorp to smuggle them out. The supply is patchy—it’s likely that those scientists, cleaners, or lab assistants who are actually stealing the drugs either get caught or succeed enough to retire pretty quickly. Luckily for the people on the street, that just means that the next person to work in that lab may be open to a similar arrangement. After all, who wants to live in a corp that captures and kills its own scientists?

Resonant drugs are very rare and expensive at the moment. The supply is very limited, and they seem very useful, which means they command a high price. As the supply increases, the prices are likely to come down. While it’s possible that a normal street fixer might luck into a supply of these drugs, it’s much more likely you’ll need specialized contacts to get your hands on some. Direct corporate contacts are very unlikely to have access; the best people to contact are specialist drug importers. A fixer will be good for getting the phone number of such a person, perhaps, but at that point the importer will know that they have you over a barrel—they know that only they can get you what you want or need. Most of the time, this just increases the price of the drugs, but other times these importers will prefer to barter for services or information. Perhaps not surprisingly, these highend drug dealers tend to be much more arrogant than those who import (for example) high-grade cyberware. It’s much harder to get addicted to a new set of cyberspurs.

It is not at all clear where these drugs are coming from. Obviously, they are being produced in some corporate research facility, but if anybody knows whether it is one facility, a small group of independent labs, or a wide manufacturing base, they aren’t talking. This isn’t unusual for new drugs, or anything experimental really, but it raises questions. One of the questions being, “How are these made?” Now, we know that these drugs somehow contain Resonance, which is another thing we don’t understand. I’ve never heardof someone just finding a lump of Resonance on the ground—all the Resonance we know about is in technomancers, sprites, or a small variety of Resonant creatures. So, if Resonance has somehow been put into these drugs, where did it come from? The most charitable hypothesis is that some lab has found a species of Resonant worm or bug and is farming them, harvesting them to create the drugs. The least charitable hypothesis is that technomancers are somehow having their Resonance removed and infused into these chemicals—or even worse, that the technomancers are being somehow killed and distributed as very profitable drugs. It’s one thing to logically know that corporations sell you and your labor for a profit; it’s another thing to look at a vial of clear liquid in your hand and wonder if it has some ground-up technomancer in it.

As yet, there are no antidotes to any of these, at least nothing that’s hit the street. If you take some, fasten your seatbelt, because you’re not coming down ’til it works its way through your system. It goes without saying that these early prototypes are experimental. They haven’t refined the formula perfectly, so they don’t always work for very long, and some people are able to resist the effects.
