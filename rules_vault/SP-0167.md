## **10,000 Daggers**

**While units like MET2000 and Tsunami were respected, units like 10,000 Daggers were** _**feared.**_

**It’s not uncommon for mercenary commanders to refuse contracts or enact escape clauses when they discover they’re going up against 10,000 Daggers. Experts in special operations, asymmetrical warfare, and intelligence gathering, 10,000 Daggers was the kind of unit you’d never see coming until their knives were at your throat or sticking in your ribs.**

**Another major player in the Az-Am War, 10,000 Daggers spent the years since in “training mode,”**

**growing their ranks by taking on several small but lucrative contracts where they could hone their unit’s core skills and largely stay off the world’s radar. Unfortunately, 10,000 has been ensnared in the IMA contract debacle and is currently mired in legal proceedings with multiple contract holders, which include multiple Middle Eastern conglomerates, so they can move on to other contracts. Until then, 10,000 Daggers are offcially stood down.**

- **10,000 Daggers has a clause in those contracts that allows them to take on supplementary “advisor” contracts with other mercenary groups, governments, or security forces, even during their current litigation woes. Funny thing, in recent weeks, several of these advisors have been working with security specialists from Spinrad Global. SG lawyers have also been working (pro bono by the way) on 10,000 Daggers’ behalf at several of their IMA hearings.**

- **Legal Eagle**

- **Turns out that Spinrad Global and the Daggers share familial ties; several regional directors and vice presidents are cousins to Dagger operators. Who knew?**
