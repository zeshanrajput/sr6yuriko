## **That Special Kind of Leverage**

**So, what does blackmail have to do with law enforcement? Oh, so many things. But something important to note, it’s** _**never**_ **blackmailing. Because that’s a crime and law enforcement doesn’t commit crimes (heh). In LE circles, it’s better known as** _**leverage**_ **.**

**The more important thing to know about using blackmail as leverage is that it creates an antagonistic relationship between the blackmailer and their target; this is not the kind of tactic to use against someone with whom you have a positive or neutral relationship. This is an ultimatum, a “do what I say, or else” situation where the consequences of not going along with the program are dire.**

**I wish I could say that blackmail is used infrequently, but I can’t. It all depends on the offcer or investigator in question and sometimes the situation. Some don’t care; they see blackmailing a suspect or subject as the most effective and effcient means to get the job done. And sometimes it is. When you’ve got this kind of damaging dirt**
