## Behind the Scenes

Their office is a former mosque with the central hall converted to a meeting room, a collection of cubicles with desks, and a trio of offices for the partners. The right wing was converted to housing for the homeless with two dozen beds. The left wing was converted to a meeting hall with a small stage and large seating area. The gardens in the rear are now used as a large public meeting area where they host cookouts, trid shows, theatrical shows, and public announcements. They are the most trusted of Okoro’s allies, and she has given them a copy of every bit of confidential data she has, including a decaying copy of the Kechibi Code and the RFID tracking for Code Eco. This has a twofold purpose. First, she wants them to act as her legal team, and they need all this in order to keep them ahead of any legal attacks against her. Second, they are an insurance policy capable of releasing scads of damaging data against a plethora of Lagosian power brokers as well as corporate movers and shakers around the sprawl should something happen to Okoro.

All of this data is located on an offline host. This host is on the terminals in their offices, which look old but are filled with modern electronics.
