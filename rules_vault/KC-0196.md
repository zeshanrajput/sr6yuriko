## Out of the Past, Into the Future

As with every major metroplex on the planet, in Hong Kong it is necessary to keep looking to the future and balancing that against the influences, customs, and traditions that keep it alive. Everything from good management, crime prevention and control, up-to-date services, and new opportunities must be constantly reviewed and reconsidered. As one of several independent sprawls of the Sixth World, Hong Kong relies on the will and drive of its population and the influx of resources—financial and otherwise—to keep it going. Having an AAA megacorporation headquartered here certainly helps.

Wuxing works endlessly to ensure that it remains the HKFEZ’s financial leader. And now that it is back on the executive council (much to the chagrin of others who were seeking to get better footing against them), they are pushing a muchstronger agenda than ever before. Chief among this is to revitalize the efforts toward cleaning up many of the HKFEZ’s astrally hazed areas and strengthen the position of the Pacific Prosperity Group—a challenge given Aztechnology’s agenda, which is often at odds with Wuxing’s.

Regarding governance and its role on charting a course for the HKFEZ’s future, let’s take a quick look at the Executive Council representatives in 2081.

**Wuxing:** Tan Jing, a male elf who has been part of Wuxing’s Hong Kong operations for a couple of decades. Had more than a few close calls with death in his time, but each time he just seems to come back wiser and a lot less relaxed. He played a major role in getting offshore piracy to back off much of the HKFEZ’s trade. No one knows how he pulled that off.

**CrashCart:** Tanya Grün, female human. She is a face only death could love, known for ice-cold negotiations and matter-of-fact responses. This is her second time on rotation for the HKFEZEC, so she’s got enough experience to outdance most of the newcomers. She was instrumental in securing access for CrashCart’s Raphael EMVs deployment in Hong Kong—a major advancement. Tanya Grün and Tan Jing have a game going that can be best described as “Go, but with chess queens.” Nasty and not people to be caught between when they disagree. Overall, it’s been a good relationship on the governing council.

**Ares:** Geoffrey Weston, male human. Weston is about to step down from his role on the HKFEZEC. He is one of the longest-lasting council members, having served on the executive council four terms in rotation. When he steps down, Ares will be cycling off the HKFEZEC as well.

**Mitsuhama:** Kia Rhys-Harding, female human. The daughter of former council representative Akiko Rhys-Harding, she has retained her parent’s talent for magic and is currently petitioning to be one of Wuxing’s pupils for the geomantic arts.

**Shiawase:** Hayate Yuudai, male dwarf. Hayate is the youngest representative on the HKFEZEC at the age of 31. An engineer first and a negotiator second, he’s no doubt attempting to oust Monobe from their council seat and step in on the power generation contracts for the HKFEZ.

**Malaysian Independent Bank:** Wen Yang Sung, male human, changeling. Malaysian Independent Bank is one of HKB’s largest competitors in the HKFEZEC and a Wuxing subsidiary. “Mr. Sun,” as some refer to him due to his bioluminescence

quality, is one of the more optimistic members of the HKFEZEC.

**Shibata:** Ming Hu Peng, human male, Monad. A mainstay company of the HKFEZEC, Shibata remains active in its production of parts and supplies for various high-tech and space-research development. Peng defected from Evo almost two years ago and seems to treat them and their growing monad-enriched developments as challengers.

**Monobe:** Iseul Joon-Ho Gim, male ork, hermetic magician. Monobe’s newest representative, Gim is in his first term serving on the HKFEZEC. He is very placid in discussions and debates, but his ally spirit (which takes the form of a vibrant ivory and gold koi that circles his person) has been known to cause other members of the council to lose their focus.
