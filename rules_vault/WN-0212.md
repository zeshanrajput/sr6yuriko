## Meet El Guapo

Carson Place is a middle- to upper-grade hotel. It has a business-friendly area on the second floor where executives can rent a conference room. When you get to the room, you find the place is immaculate but hasn’t seen a facelift in several decades. Beside the door to the meeting room is a plaque that reads “This room is dedicated to Klubbs, businessman and caring father. Taken before his time. Love, Cheli. Paid for by Klubbs and Cheli Brokerage.”

Sitting in the center of the room is a large, ovalshaped table with a holoprojector and white-noise generator (rating 6). El Guapo sits with a serious pose and says, “My employer is attempting to gain favor with a rival. A key person there is a collector of antique vehicles who has recently had one stolen. There were other items of value that could have been taken, but they took a 1967 Chevrolet Impala—an antique in perfect condition, I want the car found and returned to me. I don’t want it damaged in any way. If things turn violent, only use non-lethal force while on this job.

“The car in question has been seen at two places. If you accept the job, I will send you info on those spots. The job pays 5,000 nuyen apiece.”

Each net hit on a Charisma + Influence Opposed test increases the payment figure by 250 nuyen, to a maximum of 7,500 nuyen per runner. Once an agreement is made, El Guapo will send the team info on the two locations where the car was seen: a magic shop called “Arcanna’s Cabana” and a food truck called “Fluffy Duck’s Gourmet Chicken Biscuits.”
