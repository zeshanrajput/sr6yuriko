## Sand Ship

No one is sure what the origin of the sand ships in the Astral Sea may be, but the theories are as different as the sand ships themselves. These elaborately carved wooden creations may be as small as a one-person rowboat or as large as an enormous galleon with dozens of sails capable of carrying a hundred men. Occasionally, one may be found abandoned in the dunes, but more often they will be in the possession of a band of marauders—or more correctly pirates—who prey on other travelers in the Astral Sea. Sand ships have a rating from 1 to 12, and while the base speed across the dunes is only a walking pace, they all have the ability to use a Movement power equal to their rating. Sand ships cannot pass through the barrier surrounding a metaplane; attempting to take it into any metaplane will destroy it.

Using a sextant and map to successfully travel to a specific metaplane—or area of the Astral Sea—requires an Intuition + Astral + Initiate Grade (distance number) test, where the map used reduces the threshold by its Force, but never less than 1. Conjunctions and eclipses can both affect this threshold in the same way, as when traveling through ritual gateways. If a traveler braves the Astral Sea without maps or sextant, they are likely to end up in a random location— that is, unless they decide to ask another traveler for directions.

Metaplanes can also be searched for in an area and can be either known or unknown. The test to spot a metaplane from the Astral Sea is listed above. Deciding if the discovered metaplane is already known is a function of the map used, set by its quality (its Force) and the skill of the observer. Use a Astral + Logic + Initiate Grade test with the threshold set by the gamemaster. Typically, you would use 2 x the distance number and reduce it by the Force of the map/grimoire consulted and the net hits made by observing the metaplane.
