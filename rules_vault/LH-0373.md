## Operation Seraphim, Phase II

When a war ends, people don’t just call it a day, pack up, and go home. There’s always the aftermath and cleanup that has to be done. After July 23, we (as in the Defenders) still didn’t know how many Disians and their allies had survived the battles or what kind of threat they might still pose. As far as we knew, there were still an unknown number of powerful and possibly entrenched enemies still out there, maybe planning some kind of alternate plan, or there might be a final retaliation strike we missed.

And not knowing things the last time almost got everyone killed.

To this end, the second phase of _Operation: Seraphim_ was initiated on July 28. Those who survived the first phase were joined by select individuals and groups from other operations to hunt down and neutralize any remaining Disian elements. I’d say it was successful only because the operation was ultimately unnecessary.

Reports soon confirmed that all targets were in fact already dead. It took a couple of weeks to confirm this information, but every Disian that had been caught on the wrong side of their portal, their Faustians, and every chimera that didn’t originate from the material plane (re: Earth), had simply expired. The reasons why are still being examined (for other reasons), but the general consensus is that once cut off from the energies of their home metaplane, the energy-based Disians simply faded away and took their thralls with them. Although there is evidence to suggest that some Disians survived for a time by actively draining their thralls in a last-ditch effort to live. Regardless, there was no big event, no massive catastrophe, nothing to mark their passing. They simply withered and eventually died wherever they ended up after the final battle. Can’t say I’m choked up about that.

Additionally, any technology that integrated with Disian magic didn’t work. It was as if we no longer had the right batteries to power the stuff. The Disian crystals, once a powerful link from a

Disian patron to their chosen, were no better than the cheap stones you get at the corner magic shop.

**>** And I wish I could say I’m surprised, but a market for secondary Disian items and tech has opened up. Some are trying to reverse-engineer equipment, others want it for the association value. I don’t say this often, but I refuse to deal in such items. **>** Red Anya

This initially caused a concern among certain Defender circles, as there was honest worry about what would happen to the liberated chimeras. Thankfully—and as far as I know—other than some initial health concerns, they pulled through and are as alive and well as the rest of us.

**>** When the portal to the Disian metaplane was cut off, we all thought we were going to die. And yes, it was painful, and similar to what I imagine going through severe withdrawal feels like. Yet, we survived.

**>** Liberator

Ultimately Seraphim Phase II was a bust, but nobody complained, and frankly we were grateful for it. We had other problems to deal with because there were elements out there who wanted to be absolutely sure the Disians were gone.
