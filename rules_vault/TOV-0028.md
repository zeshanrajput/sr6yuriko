## R itual: Zombie Creation (Minion)

## Threshold: Force

Zombies are a special purpose version of a summoned spirit which features more permanence at the cost of being less powerful than traditional spirits. In addition, the Voodoo practitioner can have more zombies than what they would normally be able to have as a traditional summoner.

- There are multiple traditions and forms of traditions that use possession. Some of them have their own form of zombie creation and even use living vessels.

- Elijiah

After acquiring a corpse that is fresh (see Fresh Corpses), the summoner must spend time and nuyen to prepare the body. The ritual to prepare the vessel is an Astral + Logic (Force, 1 day) Extended test. What defi nes a body as fresh is that it cannot be older than ten days dead at the completion of the preparation ritual. This can be extended through the use of a magical preservation or other preservation techniques, which can increase the time available by up to fi ve times normal before being unusable. The ritual must take place inside a hounfour with a Force greater than or equal to the desired Force of the spirit. In addition, the summoner needs to spend Force x 500 nuyen in reagents. During the preparation, any glitch or critical glitch results in the corpse being unusable—zombies require a correctly prepared vessel.

The summoner then makes an opposed test using Conjuring + Magic vs. Force x 2 to summon the spirit to use. Once the summoner has dealt with any drain, the spirit automatically succeeds in possessing the vessel. If the mambo or houngan drops unconscious for any reason during the summoning test or drain soaking, the zombie breaks free. The recently freed zombie then makes a Force x 2 (6) test. If the zombie is successful, the freed zombie becomes a grande zombie. If the zombie fails, it goes berserk for 1D6 combat rounds, attacking any nearby living creature before collapsing.

After successfully creating the zombie, the corpse is animated with the spirit. Its physical attributes are half (rounded up) that of the original person it is now possessing, and all mental attributes are equal to the Force of the spirit. The new life span of the zombie is equal to one month per net hit on the Conjuring test. Once the zombie’s time is up, the spirit departs, and the corpse rapidly decomposes. Until that time, the zombie will follow the orders of the summoner or those that the summoner designates. A zombie must be in line-of-sight to receive new commands.

A houngan or mambo can control multiple zombies. These don’t count against the summoner’s number of summoned spirits. The practitioner can control up to their Charisma in zombies. If they wish to animate new zombies, they must banish existing zombies to make room.
