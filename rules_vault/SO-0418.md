## Magic Support

## Posted by: Hemdale

Quite a few mages also dabble in social skills, particularly those of the shamanic tradition. Many hermetics and those following paths that don’t relyon personal charisma for their magic tend to pull their own weight in a conversation. Mages of all types understand the use of power. In many ways, magic becomes an extension of our natural talents, and in other areas we use it to compensate for our shortcomings. If you’re the primary face as well as the magician on your team, most of the rest of this document is going to be relevant to you. I believe there is an earlier section that specifically relates to the magic tricks available to the Awakened face. This section, however, is for the less socially inclined magicians out there—you know who you are. Your focus on other talents has left you a bit socially inept, perhaps even awkward. But there will be times when your assistance with a con or negotiation could make or break a plan, or at least make things a little easier and less dangerous for the rest of your team.

Assuming you have the ability to cast spells or create alchemical preparations, it behooves you to learn a spell or two that can provide your face with some magical support—and I don’t mean watching out for other mages and astral threats, which is something you should already be doing as the mage on your team. There are several spells that are particularly useful for assisting with social situations, and they don’t require you to spend time developing your social skills. By picking up a spell or three that can benefit your team’s face, you avoid having to waste your precious time and effort learning another skillset and you get to focus on what you do best: magic.

This is a definitive list of the most useful spells for assisting your face. There may be other spells that could come in handy for this purpose as well, but they’re likely to be more niche, and therefore of limited use. Once you have learned the spells I’ve recommended, I encourage you to consider other creative options. Until then, you’d do well to learn what I suggest. Let’s go through the spells by category.

Combat spells are not going to be of much use in assisting your face, except when things go wrong, and they require violent backup. That is outside the scope of this post, so I will leave it at that.

Among Detection spells, I have three suggestions: Analyze Truth, Mindlink, and Mind Probe. Analyze Truth is an incredibly subtle spell, and confirming with certainty whether someone is telling the truth or not might not be as flashy as a fireball, but it may help you avoid situations in which a fireball is necessary. Mindlink can establish a telepathic communication link, which can’t be jammed or intercepted. This is ideal for securely sharing information (other than computer files, of course) without letting on you are in communication; something that’s necessary for operational security, as well as coordinating a complex negotiation or confidence game. Mind Probe is infinitely more reliable than any other interrogation technique and tends to be much quicker as well. Some of you may have ethical concerns about invading a sapient individual’s mind against their will, but even you should be able to admit that this

method is preferable to resorting to most other interrogation methods. Against strong-willed targets, it is of less use.

Next are Health spells, which can do so much more than healing injuries. If you only select one spell from my entire list of suggestions, choose Increase Attribute. There are very few other means by which an individual’s personal charisma may be artificially enhanced, and none of them are as effective as this spell. By enhancing your face’s abilities, you can provide invaluable assistance with very little attention and effort on your part. Increase Attribute can also be used to enhance any other attribute— physical or mental—so you’ll find it useful for other purposes as well. The drawback, of course, is that it can be a particularly draining spell to cast. Keep your stim patches handy.

Illusion spells bring many possibilities, but you need to be careful and creative to use them effectively. Recent advancements in spell techniques have made them much more difficult to detect and recognize from the astral plane, which expands their usefulness considerably; however, they still work best when there is little to no astral overwatch to potentially observe and identify them. Mundane disguises and technological distractions work best against a target who is protected by significant magical security. Getting through a ward with an Illusion spell upon you is a difficulty for a magician, but an impossibility for a mundane face who relies upon your enchantments. When magical security is on the lighter side, or in rarer cases, nonexistent, Illusion magic will serve you very well. Generally speaking, when there are physical or mana versions of a spell (such as Mask and Physical Mask), don’t bother learning the mana version, as only the physical spell will work against cameras and other sensors. You want your illusions to be consistent whether viewed by living eyes or cameras. There may be some rare cases in which you would prefer a living target to see an illusion while the security cameras pick up something else, but those situations are incredibly niche. Now, on to the specific spells. Physical Mask is likely to be of the widest use when assisting a face, as it allows for instant disguises that can be removed and quickly replaced with another casting. Trid Phantasm creates illusions, so it’s ideal for selling a con, creating a distraction, or screening for some other activity. Improved Invisibility can be used to hide or smuggle items as well as individuals, but opening doors is a dead giveaway that something invisible is lurking about. I’ve found that having a smooth operator in the open providing distractions and approaching doorways is an excellent way to sneak a well-armed team past otherwise vigilant security forces. But I suppose that’s a bit of a tangent, as it’s an example of the face helping the mage, and not the other way around. An honorable mention goes to the Silence spell, whichcan be of great use if a conversation goes horribly wrong and sudden intervention is required. Follow it up with an Improved Invisibility spell to hide the resulting carnage, and you might just avoid botching the mission by raising an early alarm.

Manipulation spells can be almost as useful as Illusion magic when it comes to assisting your face, though they tend to be a bit more intrusive—and in some cases, controversial. The more scrupulous magicians avoid using magic that invades or subsumes an individual’s mind. While I applaud the sentiment, when it comes to surviving in the shadows, I prefer to make any and all tools available. And I’m not one of those idealistic fools who freely fireballs security guards when violence could have been avoided with a temporary violation of an individual’s agency. To that end, I highly recommend Control Thoughts, which allows you to take nearly complete control over a person’s mind for a time. Even the weakest mind will eventually rebel against this spell, and an average individual is unlikely to be held for more than a minute or so. Afterward, they’ll be most uncooperative, so this spell is of no use against the primary target of a confidence game or negotiation. It comes in handy when things go suddenly wrong with an underling or guard, and you can prevent them from raising an alarm or get them to come with you so you can take their place. Another favorite of mine is the Fashion spell, which allows you to permanently alter an individual’s garments. It’s less effective than Physical Mask for a disguise, but it has the advantage of creating a permanent effect that won’t show up in astral space or prevent the wearer from passing through astral barriers. When going up against a target who’s well-protected by magical security, the more subtle touch that Fashion brings is worth it. And it saves me so much money on my wardrobe. Be sure to consult with your face before casting the Fashion spell on a run, as they’ll undoubtedly have useful advice that’ll make the garment look as it should.

Most magicians also have access to spirits. These servant entities are incredibly useful for a wide range of applications. But when it comes to social situations, they require a subtle and delicate touch. A spirit materializing during a conversation is likely to raise an alarm and end all discussion. I find spirits to be quite useful for creating sudden distractions nearby—they can prevent a mark from having the time to think too closely about the story they’re being sold, or to corroborate a lie regarding potential danger or insecurity. To that end, the Accident, Confusion, Fear, and Psychokinesis powers are some of the most useful for your spirits to use. That said, spirits of fire are quite effective at setting off a fire alarm. When you need an extra person to play a simple role, a kindred spirit can sometimes masquerade as a metahuman; however, they lack the more delicate social skills and are likely to give themselves away in a direct conversation.

As for adept powers, I have little to say. Adept powers generally affect the adept and are poorly suited for directly assisting others. Adepts with the right mix of social and disguise powers make excellent faces and are better equipped to take direct action themselves rather than support someone else. Though they can accomplish much greater things than they could on their own if they work in tandem with another face.
