## Can’t Have One Without the Other

The HKFEZ is not without those who would oppose, restrain, or just remove adversarial elements. Yes, the Triads are so entrenched as to require the equivalent of a root canal combined with a colonoscopy to remove them. For years, Knight Errant and the HKPF tried to manage this, and in some parts of the city KE is still trying. But ever since Middle Kingdom Security (MKS) won the security contracts for nearly all the districts north of Victoria Harbor in 2079, the shifts have become almost frenetic. And after KE absorbed the HKPF in 2076, they held the contracts for Hong Kong Island and the Islands District west of it. MKS found enough backing to make the whole thing seem plausible. For the first year, MKS went on a massive sweep of all things violent and intrusive, and multiple Triad clans and societies suffered from the swift strokes. This could have been catastrophic for the Triads—but then, all at once, the raids ended.

Good thing, too—MKS was on the verge of triggering an all-out conflict not seen since the Boxer Rebellion. Multiple Triads were joining forces and applying pressure in new ways by pushing for MKS predecessors to come back in. Representatives from Red Dragon Triad, Yellow Lotus, White Lotus Society, Phoenix Lodge, and 14K persuaded the FEZEC that MKS was damaging Hong Kong’s culture and people. While not entirely convinced, some of the council members verified some of the issues their local operations had been suffering and decided to propose legislation intended to rein in MKS’ “Campaign of Sorrow.” Exact details of the agreements never came to light, but MKS’ crime-killing spree came to an abrupt halt.

- And what a shame that is. We support the people of Hong Kong and desire them to be free from the entrenched culture of corruption the Triads have put upon them. Middle Kingdom should have been allowed to succeed. Yes, the streets would have been bloody, but it would’ve been the blood of the criminal oppressors, and it would have faded with the rain of new promise.

- I didn’t think they’d show their like around here again.

Beginning with the new year, Middle Kingdom adjusted its actions to something less restraining. They started their own program of “protection,” not only maintaining their contracts with most of the FEZ but also offering individual security for persons of importance such as judges, politicians, and members of the FEZEC. Their campaign has continued to show signs of pushback, but Knight Errant and OmniStar (through DocWagon) have started to press their cases for the necessity of a zone-wide program to protect and provide for Hong Kong’s citizenry. How this might work is anyone’s guess.

- Along with the negotiation, Middle Kingdom had a personnel change. Hai Chun Kwok is now the director in charge of Middle Kingdom’s HKFEZ operations. One of the first things he undertook was reaching out to each and every outstanding HKPF officer who was still in the area and not working for Knight Errant. It took the better part of a month, but Kwok managed to expand his entire tier-three advisory board by more than eighty members, with nearly a dozen added to the tier-two commanders. It’s massive.
