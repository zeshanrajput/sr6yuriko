## Effects

Now, the simplest outcome is that they don’t raise a fuss. You sell them piss and call it whiskey and they are either too proud, too stupid, or too afraid to say anything about it. You drive them right up the walls wanting in and then cut them out in the blue, and they take it out on themselves later. Maybe it’s the muscle or their own lack of attack instinct, but they’ll go easily into that good night keeping their disappointments to themselves, and the best thing you sold them was a lesson on buying outside their corner of the yard. You can think of it like a commodities broker mixed with a government entity: buy low, sell high, and boss your way out of trouble.

- I have never heard of any hybrid so abominable in my life. It’s disgustingly apt.

- Mr. Bonds

The best part, like so many, is that the marks come to you. The disappointed element of value on service is so ingrained into the subconscious that most punters are going to just shrug it off. Hell, you might get lucky. A lot of folks simply chalk it up to an expensive life lesson or walk away believing everyone gets swindled that way. The prizes are the ones who buy into the illusion and become regulars.
