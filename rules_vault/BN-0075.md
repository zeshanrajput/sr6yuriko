## Design Your Shifter Examples

The Shifter quality counts as one of your (up to) six qualities that are selected during step three of character generation as described above. The positive and negative add-ons you take are a part of your Shifter quality and don’t count toward this limit. Think of the Shifter quality like a framework or chassis that allows multiple options. It always counts as one and only one quality, no matter how many additional add-ons, positive or negative are selected. The add-ons only modify the Karma cost of the quality starting from the Base cost of 15 (18 for Featheries). A summary would be something like Base 15 plus 25 positive add-ons and minus 18 negative add-ons for a total Karma cost of 22.

Mermaid is using Life Path to build her character. Mermaid pictures her metahuman form as a smooth social operator who can change faces and shapes. During the Born This Way step (p. 32, Sixth World Companion), she chooses shifter and to be an Awakened adept, and increases her Magic to 1. She completes the rest of Born This Way and Growing Up steps. At the Coming of Age step she chooses a skill and an attribute to increase as described, and when it comes to picking one or two qualities she chooses the Shifter (Fin) positive quality. Fins are a minimum base cost of 15 Karma. Looking over the rest of the add-ons for Fins, she doesn’t see anything that really fi ts her character concept. As being a selkie is largely an role-playing choice, that is just fi ne with her. She then completes the Coming of Age step and moves on to Adult Life Modules as normal.

Gator Girl is using Point Buy for her shifter. She has chosen Mundane and spent her CP. She is now in step three and chooses Shifter (Scaly) and notes the minimum base cost of 15. Gator Girl intends to get her teeth bloody, so she also selects one level of Built Tough (4 points), one level of Enhanced Movement Speed (1 Karma), Sharpened Natural Weapons (8 Karma) and Shifter Might (4 Karma) for a total so far of 32 Karma. She also selects Apex Predator (12 Karma) and Cold Blooded (5 Karma)l which brings the cost for the Shifter (Scaly) quality back down to 15 Karma.

Raptor is an elven falconine adept using the Priority method. He has completed step one and two as normal and is now picking qualities. He double checks, and his Body and Strength are both 3, so he is able to select Shifter (Feathery). He notes the minimum base Karma cost of 15. He reviews the add-ons and selects Shifter Modesty, because it’s a crime to hide abs like his. Since he’s already at the minimum cost for Shifter (Feathery) this selection does not change anything, so Raptor selects Enhanced Senses (Visual Acuity) and Enhanced Senses (Hearing) to complement his elven low-light vision. The total positives are 8 and balance exactly with the 8 negatives so the cost for Shifter (Feathery) remains 15. Because Raptor has already increased his Magic during step two, there is no adjustment to his Magic attribute from the shifter quality.

Scraps is a canine human detective, and he is a good boy. He has completed steps one and two of Priority and is now on step three. His Body is 5 and his Strength is 4, so he is able to select Shifter (Furry) as a quality. He notes the 15 point minimum base cost and reads over the add-ons. Enhanced Senses (Vomeronasal), Enhanced Senses (Hearing), and Enhanced Running Speed (2). This increases the cost to 27. The city is a dangerous place, and Scraps like to travel with friends, so he picks up Pack Animal reducing the cost to 20. Scraps also has a golden retriever tail no matter the form. This is a Shifter Tell, which reduces the cost by 10, but the minimum cost for Shifter (Furry) is 15. Scraps pays 15 Karma.
