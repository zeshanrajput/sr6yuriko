Major City The Coliseum

\*Spirits cannot be summoned nor banished/disrupted. Spirits and Awakened lose the ability to astrally project. The Metaplane of Battle is an aethereal prison.

and managed to assense it well enough before disrupting it to trace its native plane. This time, I was alone when I embarked on an astral quest to deal with the spirit once and for all.

I was certainly surprised to find myself back in the volcanic hellscape that is the Plane of Battle. The sky was just as red and broody, the volcanoes were still lobbing lava bombs like they were going out of fashion, and the ground beneath my feet was still the same nasty cloying red dirt. One thing was different, though: The grand melee in the valley had more clearly defined sides, and no bugs of any kind among the combatants.

It’s hard to describe a lot of metaplanar entities, as their home planes don’t always conform to the laws of physics as we know them. Tentacles and limbs connected to body parts that look, at first glance, to have no rhyme or reason. It’s only once you start trying to kill many things that you realize what you thought was a head is actually a stomach (not abdomen, but stomach), or what you thought was their thoracic cavity is actually their genitals. So I hope my fellow JackPointers will forgive me for saying that one side of that fight was a mind-boggling mix of tentacles, limbs, and randomly combined body parts that defy categorization. In all the years since, in all my repeated trips back to this plane, I’ve never encountered that species again, and I’ve lost countless days trying to accurately describe what I saw. The written word just cannot do them justice.

The force they were fighting are what I believe to be the dominant native life forms of the Plane of Battle—the minotoads. Minotoads are strange creatures, akin to a cross between a minotaur and a toad. And when I say minotaur, I mean the metahuman minotaur that one can see walking around the streets of many sprawls, especially those in Greece and Western Europe.

As I watched the battle below, it became clear to me that the mass of body parts army, for lack of a better term, was slowly but steadily losing. The

minotoads were just too tough and strong, able to leap over multiple ranks of fighters of the loose block formations they were in and utterly decimate their enemies around them before either leaping clear or being brought down by sheer weight of numbers. I didn’t have a dog in this fight, so I stood on the clifftop I’d appeared on and watched the battle unfold. The minotoads were fantastic fighters—far quicker than their bulk would suggest and capable of rudimentary tactics and strategies.

I’ve learned that large, set-piece battles cause one to lose track of time on the Plane of Battle, and this instance was no exception. Once the fight was over, I was on my way to tracking down the shade I’d come to kill. Wolf was pleased when I returned to my body at the end of my quest.
