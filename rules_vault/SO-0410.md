## Professional Note 05: Timing

A Charisma-based test doesn’t necessarily have to be one of how good you are at manipulating people, it can simply be, “How good is your timing?” With the receptionist, The Professor did not need to use deceit at all. The goal was not to convince the receptionist of anything, it was merely to occupy them. A properly timed commlink call, a loud sneeze, or even the perfectly timed flush of a toilet can make all the difference.

He slowed his pace as he approached the ORC suites. Timing was everything at this moment. A half-step too fast, and this all comes crumbling down. The automatic doors to the office suite swished open as I passed him on my left without acknowledgment and sauntered up to the receptionist at the front desk.

“Hello, Madam I’m hoping that you can help me. I have an appointment with Director Naka. And I’m afraid that I have gotten turned around. Could you direct me to the main security office?” I did notice a slight shift of nervousness when I mentioned Naka. But the young dark-haired receptionist was extremely polite while giving me directions to the security offices and only slightly glanced at the sleek steel briefcase that Incogno was carrying as he breezed by the desk with nothing more than a curt nod.

Cloaked in an exquisite magical glamor of Dr. Ken Ishihara, I moved with a confident stroll toward the heart of the building’s magical endeavors, the eXperimental Research Division. Although the entire skyscraper is dedicated to magical research and development, the laboratories in the XRD are reserved for the most potent and dangerous experiments.

Two guards stood at the entrance, one managing the biometric scanner and the other with his SMG at a casual low ready. As I walked through the doorway-sized scanner, the standing guard casually remarked, “So ... 9:30 and back to work? I’m guessing the date didn’t go so well.”
