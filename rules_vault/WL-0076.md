## **Fireworm**

## **(Vermes saxi infernus)**

**I’m happy to say I’ve never had a freworm make an active effort to kill me. That said, this still falls under my collection of species that have nearly ended my scientifc career. I was working in the Carib League down in St. Vincent, checking out La Soufrière for the local governance in hopes of fguring out how soon its growing lava dome was going to pop. They had already detected the presence of “rockworms” in the area and thus reached out to a parazoologist rather than someone with a volcanology expertise. The quotation marks indicate this was not in fact** _**vermes saxi**_ **, but was instead a new subparaspecies,** _**vermes saxi infernus**_ **.**

**Similar in size but drastically different in coloration, the freworm’s skin is orange and black, rather than the pallid, feshy color of the rockworm. I don’t know how any of the locals with a Matrix connection couldn’t have simply looked it up and known the difference, but they didn’t, and I have another new paraspecies discovery attributed to me.**

**But the initial point here is to tell the tale of my near death. As I stated, I was studying La Soufrière, and while not a volcanologist, I knew enough to tell something was off. The recorded pressure dome growth rates and the subaquatic lava fow quantities seemed strange, mainly because both varied greatly, including several negative growth reports without any indication of eruption. I was working around the crater, taking soil samples and looking for signs of paraspecies activity along with keeping a general eye out for anything arcane in nature, wondering if maybe spirits were active in the area. I discovered several “rockworm” tunnels, another indication this was something else, as rockworms do not leave consistent and sturdy tunnels. Each of these tunnels was also still generating heat and venting gases. Now, I was so excited I stopped being as careful as I should have been, and I managed to crumble the top of a tunnel I had not noticed yet. The recent rain had found a route in, and the walls of the tunnel were almost as smooth as glass. Needless to say I fell in and lost my bearings as I began to slide in the dark. I ended up a good 100 meters underground when I stopped. The chamber I was in was steamy and only survivable thanks to a few quick protective spells and Focustak, an earth spirit I had been working with. It was here I spotted four freworms, all resting. I did my best to study them quickly and then began to work my way back out with Focustak’s assistance.**
