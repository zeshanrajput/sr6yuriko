## “Verstanden.”

Briefly, the aging dwarf with the red mohawk turned to the Ausguck, the armed lookout at the edge of the block’s roof, to show her that he was heading off. Zoé had given him to understand—in perfect Kiezspeak, a lingo that changed way too fast for the corporations to keep up with any chipsoft—that she was in deep drek. She had a box of unknown content (turk. sürpriz kutusu, surprise box) from a factory (fac) in what used to be called “Industriesektor Ost” (ISO) to be delivered to an address along Kurfürstendamm—the Q-Mall. Apparently, she had been warned by her corporate contact (the babo salak, or “client idiot”) that she was about to enter the Kreuzhain anarchist zone and needed support (aldeam).

By a wink, he called up the as-popular-as-illegal rigger mapsoft panzAR and plotted possible courses, meeting points, and hangouts of people who owed him something. The Schattenhafen am Urban would have to do.
