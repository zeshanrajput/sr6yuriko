## The Job

## Meeting Mr. Johnson

Mr. Johnson, Malcolm Sutton, flies out to wherever the runners reside to meet them. He rents out an abandoned storefront for the day in order to provide a secure place to meet. He explains that he needs outside talent to complete a potentially difficult but highly profitable job. He is willing to provide the runners with the proper clearances to get them in the city and set them up with a fixer who can get them a place to stay and access to the supplies needed to pull off the job.

Sutton is a salesman. He stresses that this could be an opportunity for the runners to establish themselves in a new market and that his ability to provide them with clean IDs makes the work impossible to pass up. He is evasive about specifics of the work other than to explain that it involved breaking into a secured facility, performing surveillance, and planting data. If pressed for specifics, he will only add that the work will take place in NYC during normal business hours.

He understands that the runners likely will not be able to bring any of their standard gear into the city. Therefore, the pay for the job is 1,500 nuyen per runner plus 8,000 nuyen total for team expenses, over and above the CityPass IDs. Each net hit on an Influence + Charisma Opposed test increases the individual figure by 100 nuyen and the expense fund by 250 nuyen.

Once the runners agree to the job, Sutton gives them the details. He needs the runners to surveil and record a meeting inside the ultra-secure Goldwater Social Club in Manhattan. Once the runners have the footage, he needs them to get that footage to a specific spot below Times Square where they can

splice it into the Times Square Billboard feed, and play that footage across all the billboards at once.

Once it looks like the runners are finished asking questions, he tells them he will arrange for tickets to be waiting for them at their local airport in whatever names they want to use for the flight. Those will also be the names on the CityPass IDs waiting for them when then land at Newark Airport. He explains that this will be the only time they see him and that his compatriot, Eager, will meet them at the airport and handle business the rest of the way.
