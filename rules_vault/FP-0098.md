## **History of the Nexus and the Shadow Matrix**

**The Nexus is universally acknowledged as the largest data haven and has held that status since its creation. It is also the oldest, having existed in one form or another since 2037. Named by virtue of being a data haven operating in Denver, it also became known as the center, or Nexus, of the early Shadowland system of pirate hosts.**

**The Nexus—and the Shadow Matrix itself— started on the grounds of the abandoned U.S. Air Force Academy south of Denver. Later, this would become the Alpha Site. The equipment in the data center there was connected to the world-spanning system of hard datalines created by the old United States of America. This network was used to securely connect all U.S. military bases with its command centers, and it radiated out from the NORAD Cheyenne Mountain complex. The system was abandoned after the breakup of the Unit-**

**This host was eventually mirrored in dozens of cities around the world and became the primary access points to the Shadow Matrix. Each Shadowland node was set up for a local shadow community, but the important data was mirrored on all the Shadowland nodes, and the Nexus became the central hub and backup, archiving all the data. Other pirate hosts also took advantage of this hidden network, adding connections to the Shadowland nodes, the Nexus, and each other. These included a few that were archiving data in the same way as the Nexus, so they also became data havens.**

**The original Nexus/Shadowland network ended on November 2, 2064, during the series of events known as Crash 2.0. The doomsday cult Winternight set the Jormungand virus, created by the Dissonant technomancers of Ex Pacis, loose on the world’s Matrix infrastructure in an attempt to destroy it. While the Shadow Matrix fared better than much of the corporate Matrix, the damage was still extensive. The Nexus suffered major hardware damage and lost key personnel in the attack. Despite the damage, the virus was eventually driven out of the host, and the archived data stored in the Heap remained intact. The rest of the Shadow Matrix did not do quite as well, and most of the Shadowland hosts were destroyed. The Seattle node was one exception—it survived and eventually became the ShadowSEA data haven.**

**After Crash 2.0, the rebuilding started immediately on both the Nexus and the Shadow Matrix system. Funding and new equipment started quietly fowing into the Denver Data Haven from an assortment of patrons. The Shadow Matrix took longer to repair, with many of the nodes in its hardline network destroyed. The Second Universal Matrix Conference in 2065 established a new wireless protocol for rebuilding the commercial Matrix and the Nexus took full advantage of the new technology. Hidden wireless network routers were used to repair and expand the Shadow Matrix in the late 2060s. However, the backbone of the system remained wired and hidden from both GOD and the operators of the corporate grids.**

**In January 2075 the new Matrix protocols introduced Foundation technology and that changed everything again. The move toward a distributed network that started after Crash 2.0 accelerated exponentially. The host holding the Heap and the Ring was translated to a Foundation host. Untethered from physical servers, it became immune from real-world attacks (but it introduced new attack vectors that would eventually be exploited). Some of the specialty host structures like the killing jars and trapdoors continued to run on physical servers, but for the most part almost everything else changed to virtual foundation-tech, and the accumulated hardware was mothballed or relegated to creating physical data backups.**
