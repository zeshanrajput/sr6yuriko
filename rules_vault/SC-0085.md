## Counter-cultural

If following the culture you were raised in to reach this path is a thing, going against one’s upbringing happens almost as often. The rebellious teen corp kidwho finds they can touch the spirits and gains an affinity for those things they see so little of. The old-timer raised in the heart of Hong Kong, who has only seen pictures of anything besides vermin or food but feels such a deep connection to something so foreign that they embrace it fully. The island native who becomes drawn to the creatures of the land or the landlubber who learns to love the sea. These individuals embrace their rebellion so deeply they slide into the shadows— not just to oppose the tenets of their culture, but eventually to contest the status quo of the world itself. Their personalities are built on a defiant spirit but tempered by a spiritual connection to all things animalistic and simple in the world. These souls understand the corporate shark (or wolf of Wall Street), but by the same token know that even apex predators eventually can overhunt their territory or be brought down by a rival or even another top predator. Their unique perspective lets the character function within a society that seems foreign but is still governed by the laws of nature.
