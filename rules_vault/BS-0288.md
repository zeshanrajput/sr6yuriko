## Types Of Nanite Colonies Cosmetic (And Covert) Nanites

**Dynamic Features:** Using the same nanites as nanopaste, the dynamic features colony collects just under the skin of the user and is extruded through the pores when needed. The nanohive controlling the colony can be programmed with preset disguises from either pictures or biometric data. A dynamic features disguise creates a point of situational Edge that can be used on any Con test influenced by physical appearance. The colony requires thirty seconds to extrude the nanites through the pores and one combat round to set the disguise. The disguise can be altered to another preprogrammed disguise, requiring a combat round to reconfigure. The effect lasts for eight hours before the extruded nanites decay. Dynamic features can alter skin texture and color as well as facial features but not fine details such as fingerprints. It has no effect on eye color, hair length or color, teeth, tusks, or fangs, and it cannot cover or reproduce horns. If used for a full-body disguise, it cannot make any alterations to body size. Dynamic features cannot be used on an area where the skin has been replaced, such as with dermal plating or a cyberlimb.

- **Nanite Volume:** Dynamic features is a free-floating colony that reduces nanite volume as it functions. Extruding a single point of the colony’s nanite volume is sufficient to cover the hands and head with a disguise. A full-body disguise requires using three points of the nanite volume, and an upper-torso disguise consumes two points of volume.

- **Overdrive:** Using overdrive reduces the extrude time to a single combat round. While this does not burn out any nanite volume, it causes 1 DV of unresisted Stun damage per point of nanite volume used and creates significant bruising under the disguise, visible after the nanite cover is removed.

**Dynamic Handprints:** Extruded through the pores when needed, this colony is used to reshape the ridges and lines of a user’s fingerprints and palms to match a preset pattern. It is similar in function to a cellular glove molder and follows the same rules. The nanohive controlling the colony can be loaded with data from a biometric scanner. When used against a biometric maglock, make a nanohive rating vs. the maglock rating Opposed test. The colony requires thirty seconds to extrude the nanites through the pores and one combat round to set the prints to the correct pattern.

- **Nanite Volume:** Using dynamic handprints reduces the colony’s nanite volume, and extruding a single point of nanite volume is

sufficient to cover the hands and create a new print pattern.

- **Overdrive:** Using overdrive reduces the extrude time to a single combat round. While this does not burn out any nanite volume, it causes 1 DV of unresisted Stun damage.

**Nanotattoos** : A nanotattoo colony can generate the same range of color and variable display of the older LED tattoos, plus the colors and patterns can be changed on demand. Nanotattoos can be located anywhere on the body, moved to any other location on command, or deactivated—disappearing entirely. Neo-anarchists have deduced that many stylized facial tattoos have the effect of confusing facial recognition systems, though this was not part of the design’s intent.

The rating of the nanohive controlling the colony sets the quality of the nanotattoo’s fine detail and sets the threshold for automated systems to discern your natural biometrics or detect forged identity tattoos.

- **Nanite Volume:** Nanotattoos are a free-floating colony. The nanite volume indicates the percentage of the body that can be covered; each point of volume can cover approximately one-sixth of the body’s total surface.

- **Overdrive:** Using overdrive produces an effect like a flash-pak (p. 263, _SR6_ ). This is only effective if at least one-sixth of the user’s skin is exposed. For each combat round the overdrive effect is used, one point of the colony’s nanite volume is burned out after that turn.
