## Yippie Ki Yay

This adventure hook is designed for the maverick background but would be a good adventure for any team of shadowrunners. On Christmas Eve (or the evening of some other significant holiday of your choice), the runners are invited to meet one of their fixers (ideally one with a high Loyalty rating) in a very posh public place, high up in a building. Seattle’s Space Needle or Fox Plaza in Los Angeles are good choices. The key points are that the PCs are requested to meet for a potentially lucrative job, and they won’t be able to bring much of anything illegal into the building. Due to the holiday, most of the building is empty of its usual numerous occupants. When the PCs reach the meet location, a feast is already set out for them in a private dining room. The PCs are told to begin eating and that their contact will be joining them shortly. A few minutes after they begin to eat, gunshots and screams are heard coming from nearby. A team of violent criminals have just seized control of the building and taken its remaining occupants hostage. Unfortunately for the PCs, their contact’s bodyguard was gunned down, and the contact is now one of the hostages. The hostage-takers are quick to kill the guards and round up everyone else for use as hostages. The attackers have also sealed the building and activated its electromagnetic shielding, effectively isolating everyone from the Matrix. Several powerful spirits search out and disrupt any astral forms that attempt reconnaissance. Anyone who shows the slightest resistance is immediately killed, but the hostages require very few examples in order to become completely compliant.

Fortunately for the PCs, the private dining room they have been waiting in has not been noticed by the assailants. There’s a back door that leads to the kitchens—and past that, to the maintenance hallways, ventilation shafts, etc. With the exception of whatever they managed to smuggle in or what can be found in the kitchen, the PCs are going to be unarmed. It won’t be long before the hostage-takers realize that the runners are around, and they’ll initially announce over comms their intention to kill hostages until they get what they want. But they’ll

also send combat teams to hunt and kill the PCs. It’s a game of cat-and-mouse, with the runners at first being the mouse. But as each enemy is defeated, guns, ammo, stim patches, and medkit supplies are added to their arsenal. If the PCs choose to evade and hide, they might be able to avoid the matter altogether and make good their escape, but their contact will certainly die if not rescued. If the PCs take on and oppose the hostage-takers, any law-enforcement officials will help them avoid public scrutiny (and therefore charges) and allow them to leave with their thanks. If the PCs manage to successfully save the hostages, each PC gains a new contact with the corporate type, Connection 5, Loyalty 4, and one favor.

As for the hostage-takers and their motives, it has something to do with accessing a vault somewhere within the building. The hostage demands are a blind, a bluff designed to buy them time and misdirect suspicion away from their true target. The loot they’re after is a collection of offline data storage devices full of megacorporate paydata worth millions of nuyen to the right buyer. If the PCs flip the script and negotiate with the hostage-takers, it’s possible they might share in the spoils.
