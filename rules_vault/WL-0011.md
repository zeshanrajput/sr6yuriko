## **Awakened**

**Awakened critters (us old-timers call them paracritters) are magical. When the Awakening began, some mundane critters began to express magic powers—as well as physical changes, in most cases. The century ferret is the frst known discovery, but the great dragon Ryumyo’s famous fight near Mount Fuji in Japan was probably the most eye-opening. The powers, temperaments, and behavior of Awakened critters varies so greatly that I fnd it diffcult to summarize them here, so I’ll leave that largely to be covered by the individual entries of this book.**

**There are a few things worth mentioning in general terms, however. The one that stands out to me the most is what we call dual-natured. Some critters are capable of astral perception, with the drawback that they can’t turn it off. These critters exist both in physical space and in astral space, much like a magician using their astral perception ability. As far as I know, there are no known instances of critters that are capable of astral projection—unless you count spirits that can de-materialize into an astral form (which I don’t—apples and oranges). Dual-natured critters trained to spot and report unauthorized astral activity are among the most common paracritters employed for security work—they tend to be much cheaper and easier to work with than security mages. This power has drawbacks, though, the frst of which I already mentioned—they can’t turn it off and leave astral space. This makes them easy to spot on the astral plane and vulnerable to attacks from the astral. Their astral forms are stuck in the same location as their physical body, which means they can’t get to an astrally projecting mage or spirit if you stay out of their reach.**

**Elemental attacks are also worth mentioning. Many paracritters have some form of ranged magical attack that is somewhat similar to an indirect combat spell. Hell hounds, for example, can project fames. This is why it’s so important to do your legwork and know what you’re getting into—you should always bring armor with the appropriate elemental protection for the critters you’re going to be going up against. Some critters also have an ability to wreath themselves in an elemental aura—if you see this, take them down with ranged attacks, because getting close is going to hurt.**
