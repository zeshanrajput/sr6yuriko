## AutoVoice

The concept of a cyber-augmented throat has been around for decades and is present in some highly illegal hardware that allowed for voice modulators to be used to duplicate other people’s voiceprints. Amore musical-aligned (and legal) version has been around since the early ’70s. Known as “AutoVoice,” it led to a brief flurry of cybernetic-voiced artists who, before the addition, couldn’t carry a tune. Used at a normal setting,

the AutoVoice controls airflow through the voice box, and the voice box’s own vibrations, to hit and hold notes as needed. With some software tweaks, this can be turned into a more mechanical sound (creating that distinct AutoVoiced sound of a few years back) or used to pitch the user’s voice up or down but still recognizable only as their own. Software hacks to allow more illegal operation now circle the Shadownet.

AutoVoice replaces the user’s own limit for singing performances with its rating.

_Wireless:_ The AutoVoice allows the user to broadcast to a local audio network without a mic.
