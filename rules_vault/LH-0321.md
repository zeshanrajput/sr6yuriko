## Krime Sprayer

The engineers at Krime Industries are experts at finding niche markets not served by other weapon manufacturers, and then dominating those markets. The Krime Cryo-Sprayer and Krime Chem-Sprayer are two recent examples. Shortly after cryo-grenades hit the market, Krime reverse-engineered one, then adapted and upscaled the technology into a flamethrower design. The Krime Cryo-Sprayer, which shoots jets of liquid nitrogen, was born. The basic rifle-sized weapon system accepts a single fuel tank that’s good for five shots, or the tank can be replaced with a hoseand-tank carrier combo with two larger tanks and a capacity of twenty shots.

Shortly before the Cryo-Sprayer hit the market, Krime R&D realized the components required for the cryogenic flow were also extremely chemically resistant, so they began to experiment using different chemicals. The Krime Chem-Sprayer was soon released and almost immediately its use was designated a war crime in twenty-three countries. Krime responded by adding a time-released neutralizing agent to the mix, making the spray harmless after approximately sixty seconds. This reduced the war-crime list to five countries.

**Game Rules:** While the Cryo-Sprayer and the Chem-Sprayer are marketed as separate systems, they have the same list price, and all the components are identical. Either version can accept either type of fuel tank or hose-and-tank carrier backpack without issue. Krime Sprayers follow the standard rules for changing tanks and using backpack carriers.

Both versions of the Krime Sprayer use the Spray Attacks rules (p. 117, _SR6_ )—the Cryo-Spray causes Cold damage (p. 110, _SR6_ ) and the ChemSpray causes Chemical damage (p. 109, _SR6_ ).
