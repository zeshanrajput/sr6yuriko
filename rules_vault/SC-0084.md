## Cultural

In a world as modern as the Sixth World, many older cultures and traditions have had a resurBEASTgence. From the gamut of Native cultures across the Americas that honor nature and animals, to the revitalization of the druidic faith and community, and across the globe with the return of ancient African and Aboriginal cultures, the world has seen new paths to power that once seemed lost. But it is not just the old but the new, with nations devoted to Awakened, metaplanar exploration, and even newer religious and cultural movements that, while not necessarily mainstream, are still paths to power through the right vessel.

Let’s talk about a few. While we cover some basics and overviews here, this is a great place to add flavor, flair, and a personal investment in a character by digging a little deeper into the cultures we touch on here.

The ancient cultures of **Egypt** revered many creatures as aspects of their gods, and as those ancient beliefs now come with the shaping of power, many turn to this culture for shaping a character background that summons beast spirits from the pantheon of Egyptian lore. Add in elements of modern and Sixth World politics, and you can create a character with a drive for summoning but an aversion to magic. Sounds strange, but it makes a great explanation for why they simply summon and refuse to touch the manipulation of mana for spells. The spirits and powers of the past come to their aid, but reaching out and shaping mana risks a taint on the soul that’s not always easy to balance.

The wide variety of **Native American** tribes, from the northern polar Inuit all the way to the furthest tribes of southern Chile, offer a gamut of beliefs that revered, feared, and honored the animals they regularly encountered. This type of character background is a great opportunity to dig into the rich history of these groups and see how the actions of European settlers may or may not have affected a modern viewpoint of the tribe. Loss of life and identity en masse could skew a summoner to the point of hovering at the edge of a toxic path, while the return of arcane powers to the world help hold them at the brink with the belief it is their duty to make right the wrongs done to their ancestors. Access to the power of the spirits is a strong anchor that holds them from slipping off into the toxicity and anger of those bent on revenge. They focus on asking the spirits for aid in order to avoid the fear and hubris that drives a wedge when one shows greater power than their kin. Or they honor their tribe and family by not stepping past that boundary. They honor the past and the spirits of their ancestors but refuse to shape power for power’s sake.

Whether it’s the elephant as a symbol of Ganesh, the divine cow in Brahma, or the predators connected to Shiva, animal worship and appreciation are abundant in the Hindu religion and the **Indian Union** . This means the beliefs could be based in faith, culture, or even popular mythos and lead a summoner to focus on the realm of a single or small collection of beast spirits. This narrowing of their view, that the

spiritual powers are to be honored and drawn from rather than the mana itself, offers a reason to be a summoner alone and eventually develop into a runner who grasps at every string of power because they know they are barely holding on by a thread.

If the **Russian** bear isn’t your main spirit guide here, oh well, I guess any one of the other hundred animals of Russian lore could fill the gap. The stories told to children of this stolid nation are filled with animals teaching lessons. This alone should be enough to create a spiritual connection and a cool series of spirits. The limits placed on individuals could be the crutch that keeps a summoner from expanding their range to spells. Asking the spirits for aid rather than placing oneself above their peers makes an interesting social border to overcome when moving to a different nation or city-state. The cultural frameworks of Moscow and Seattle are vastly different and allow growth of a character after creation. Or it allows conflicts to be resolved or escalated as you cling to your beliefs in a world that has gone soft and weak.

There may be no snakes in **Ireland,** but Irish legends are full of animals and spiritual guides. The nation’s modern history is one of mystical acceptance, and building a summoner who focuses solely on the beasts of Irish lore offers wild and mystical options rooted in ancient legends and both wellknown and barely remembered tales. The summoner deeply rooted in Irish legends may feel the need to avoid touching mana in a traditional form in fear of touching the fae realm too strongly.

While Ireland may be mentioned specifically for its rich legendry, the bulk of **Europe** carries just as many legends and cultures with animal worship. From the fenrir of the north to the Greek and Roman mashups in the south, all of Europe offers a rich background for character development as a beast summoner. Focusing on one specific region can not only open up a world of understanding for the culture and history of that area but also allow a player to tweak their own history or add a twist to the Sixth World psyche with a story of their own pulled from the past, popular fiction, or even just a whole new tale they want to add to the world of Shadowrun.

The culture and history of **Hawai’i** have changed a lot in the Sixth World, and shamanism, as well as general nature worship, has returned to favor. This means a rich history to draw on for both cultural and counter-cultural options. From turtles to geese (yes, geese), the cultures of Hawai’i are rife with opportunities to make beast-oriented summoners. In the Sixth World Hawai’i, goose shamans are often security specialists and a collection of summoned beast spirits in the form of a gaggle of geese seems both terrifying and humorous.
