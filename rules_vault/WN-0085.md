## The Hands That Feed

Bottom-feeders like us runners can’t be too selective about who we work for. Odds are you’ve done most of your shadow work for the A-level corps scrambling to get to the grown-ups’ table. Don’t overlook smaller companies looking to make the leap. They lack the resources of the megas, but that also means that we are less likely to be as expendable to them, and as a result get more repeat work. You’ll find more than enough work in NY from corps that aren’t A-list—be it newer start-ups, collaborative efforts, or what-have-you.

- Green-Collar Technologies is worth mentioning here. The corporation operates with a barebones staff, but has an HR roster that lists five times as many employees. They are becoming known for bait-and-switch sanitation contracts that promise the consumer a low rate, which then jumps suddenly a few months into the deal. When customers don’t pay, they lean on shadowrunners as heavies to make it look less like the mob front they most assuredly are.
