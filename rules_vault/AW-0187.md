## Disian Dogma and the “Art of The Deal”

Disians don’t have an organized religion, yet their society is governed by complicated sets of social norms, customs, and traditions. We still don’t understand most of these. We have determined that Disian society is based on a concept called the “Art of The Deal.”

In Dis, everything has a price—goods, services, and most of all, knowledge. Like our own capitalistic systems, the goal is to get as much as possible for as little as possible. Disians take this to extremes—even the smallest transaction is fought for like a treasure. Not so much for the gain, but for the reputation as having the power to get what you want through deals, guile, and manipulation. The most powerful Disians are those who’ve also accumulated a vast amount of knowledge and know



how to use it. Taking something from another Disian by force is considered cowardly and in some instances is punishable by death.

This does not extend to “lesser,” expendable beings (i.e., anyone other than Disians). Disians are simply superior to all and no other explanation is needed. Because of this, the Disians feel no empathy or conflict in what they do. They take what they want because that’s the way of things. And if they cannot take or control it, they simply eliminate it.

No matter the situation, Disians will always put Dis before _any_ outsiders, no matter what they may promise or say to your face. Cooperation is not meant for the benefit of all Dis, but for those who benefit the most. Things get done, because to fail at one’s caste responsibilities is almost as much a sin as not being able to bargain.

To be forced into a direct or physical confrontation is considered a great insult, because the potential loss of a Disian is unacceptable. We’ve yet to learn what happens in this situation between two Disians. However, we have firsthand accounts (myself included) of Disians utterly destroying non-Disians with their magic.
