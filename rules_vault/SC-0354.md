## The Phoenix

The phoenix has always been able to charm those around them, and they have built a strong network of supportive and loving people. They blossomed, seemingly destined for some other great role in life—until their spirit was crushed. Perhaps it was romantic, perhaps a professional relationship, maybe even both, but their trusting nature let them open themselves up for a devastating betrayal. Rather than fold and join the multitudes of corporate drones, they rose up from the ashes and earned vengeance all by themselves. Since then, they have put up walls, and they utilize the same betrayal they suffered as a weapon to keep themselves from ever being hurt again.

Regardless of how they got to the role of face fatale, the player has plenty of room to find their morality. Killing may be off limits for a face fatale, or perhaps they are okay with killing in self-defense. Taking down a few street samurai in a firefight is regrettable but isn’t the heinous intentional choice of ending the life in an execution. A face fatale might find the trafficking of people to be despicable, or they might blame the victims for allowing themselves to be enslaved by someone else. One area of morality that should be easy is prejudice. A good face fatale shouldn’t let metatype impede them any more than any other physical feature; for them, egalitarianism is borne out of pragmatism, as prejudice gets in the way of their work.

Perhaps the biggest choice in morality is how they handle emotional matters. Does the face fatale let the mark know it was all a con at the end of the run? Do they not care, or perhaps want to keep them on the hook in case the face fatale might need that target in the future? Does the individual matter? Perhaps the face fatale lets nice people down easy but uses an emotional assault cannon on those poor examples of metahumanity who deserve it (according to the face fatale’s personal evaluation). The level of callousness and style of charm are up to the player to establish.

Another important background decision is whether the face fatale has a home. This isn’t so much about whether they own real estate, but rather if they have a spot that serves as home base if instead they live out of a few suitcases, bouncing around after each job. Having a stable life with contacts and friends (at least as far as they think) can make life easier, but it also leaves them more open to vengeance from someone they have burned in the past. The face fatale is more vulnerable than most runners; unlike an ork physical adept, when the face fatale tears someone’s heart out, the victim remains alive and might seek vengeance.
