## Reproductive Replacement

The desire to pass genetic material to future generations has been a driving force for metahumanity for thousands of years. While exo-wombs have been the primary form of clone production for decades now, they were predated by primitive endo-womb tech by half a century. Development along those lines stopped around 2050 as the process was effectively perfected, and it continues to be offered today. While it remains an option for males, the longer life of the male reproductive system means that most men who undertake the procedure are simply replacing parts damaged in accidents. The far more invasive female reproduction replacement is used more often, for those suffering from disease or for those who wish to have childbearing options.
