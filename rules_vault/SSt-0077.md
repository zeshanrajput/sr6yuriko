## **Aircraft**

**If you have your heart set on being an aircraft rigger, you’d better start out rich enough to retire from the beginning. When it comes to aircraft for shadowrunners, you can probably only afford drones. I suppose there are some decent fxed-wing aircraft that might ft the budget, but what good is something that needs a runway to a shadowrunner team? If your specialty is transporting people and cargo from one city to another, it might work, but that’s about all they’re good for. When it comes to aircraft for shadowrunners, you need VTOLs—helicopters or vectored thrust aircraft. If you’re dead set on going this route, I suppose you could spend most of your money on a Northrup Hornet—these helicopters are rugged and have enough room for a full team with all of the gear. A cheap option might be to go with a Northrup Wasp or Yellowjacket and expand the seating and cargo capacity—just don’t get shot by anything, as these craft are defnitely on the fragile side. If you can save up enough cred, perhaps by convincing other members of your team to pitch in for one, the Ares Venture is the ideal vectored-thrust aircraft for runners. No other vehicle of its type is anywhere near as inexpensive or commonly used as the Venture, though its cost is still outside the price range of most starting riggers. You’re going to want to mod it out, though, as the stock version has pretty poor handing and sensors. The good news is they are highly customizable. The biggest diffculty with owning aircraft of any kind is making sure you have a place to park it that won’t stand out too much. Make sure you have a hangar space of some kind before you even think about buying one.**

**I might sound like I disapprove of running with aircraft, but I really don’t—the only real drawback is the expense, but it’s an insurmountable drawback for most. The speed and versatility you get in return can defnitely be worth the expense—especially if you run in a place like Denver, with plenty of mountainous terrain to maneuver in. Just don’t ever forget**
