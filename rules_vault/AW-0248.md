## Viable Markets: Xeno Artifacts

Evidence of extraterrestrial civilization, let alone technology, would be of incalculable value. The purpose and function of any artifacts may be impenetrable to any but the most specialized laboratories, but the potential for research is limitless. Using them is impossible, and they may be host to any number of radiological, metaphysical, or biological hazards if kept as keepsakes. Their price would be astonishing, though likely a very small fraction of its real worth, not least because there is no open market for them, and because the corps would literally kill to have them. Accept the price offered or be killed before you can sell it to anyone else.

The implications of the xenosites are far-reaching and grandiose. While it is impossible to gauge the size of a parallel dimension by any reliable terrestrial standard, it is quite possible to hypothesize that the Hive has assimilated other planes into itself. One theory is that if enough insects manage to convert or consume the native biomass of a plane, their overall aspecting of that plane would drag it into the Hive. If true, this would suggest that the Insects have conquered other planes before, and only these lingering monuments remain of their people. It would also suggest that all survivors only exist now as the nightmarish residents of the Hive today.
