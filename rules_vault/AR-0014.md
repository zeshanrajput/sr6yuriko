## Item 5: Email intercepted as the result of a run on Stabler NanoGro 

More than any specific policies, what we need is stability. The past eight months have been hell, as you know, with the situation changing almost daily. We need that to stop, but we need to learn the right lessons from all of this, and that means remembering the proper place of government. Government is a prop. Government is a show we put on to appease the masses and make them think they have a role. 

We don’t need to worry about who the next governor or next mayor is. If we have the right behind-the-scenes infrastructure, we can deal with any shifts in who’s in charge, because the underlying approach will remain the same. That structure has been lost with all the recent changes; that’s what we need to rebuild. 

The first step is getting people on the same page. A lot of the people who are still here are pulling apart instead of together. We need to be unified in building the future instead of fighting over scraps. 

This means figuring out what Taub’s about. No one’s buying his story—he started a new business here because he saw an opportunity? Of all the places to drop a few million on a new startup, I have trouble believing someone would choose here. And I love this town. He didn’t come here for the business climate. Is he seizing power? Aligned with someone? We need to know. And we need to get him straightened out, 

—Hampton
