## Why Not Trust No One?

Operating on the other side of the law makes it especially hard to trust anyone. But that’s the point to all this. Trust is, or should be, hard-earned. Many runners have tried to go the route of operating solo and not trusting anyone, thinking that every transaction or communication can or will be exploited. But without any contacts, it’s just going to be hard (more like impossible) to get biz done. And then there’s dealing with a Mr. Johnson.

Usually, you can trust those folks enough to give the information you need to do the job. They’re paying you to complete objectives on their behalf, so it makes no sense for them to try and screw you over. Right?

Cue the next story.

So, we’d been hired to do a job in Seattle by one Tiffany Bellefort. I don’t mind using her name since she’s dead. Ms. Bellefort wanted us to break into a Gaeatronics facility and sabotage their power distribution network for Bellevue. The pay was solid, and she even tossed in a few thousand nuyen up front so we could pick up any explosives we needed. Now, we had never worked with her, but word on the street was she’d done well by a couple other teams that’d worked for her. She just needed extra help because her normal teams were tied up.

The truth is those teams were tied up doing the real work for her and she just needed a disposable team as a distraction. She didn’t even care if we were successful, since she had another team hitting the real power distribution hub and a second team doing a big theft. She was even banking on us not making it out alive so she wouldn’t have to pay us for a job she didn’t care about. So she tipped off Gaeatronics corporate security that we were coming.

The rough part was we lost a couple of our crew that day, but we finished the fragging job. Imagine her surprise when we called about getting paid. Of course, she didn’t answer the Ghost-damned commlink. But that didn’t prevent the decker from finding out where she was at. Funny thing, no one’s seen her since.

- Honestly this is all solid advice. Trusting others gets you killed. I contract work out to others, but I never depend on them to not screw me over. It’s kept me alive this long. The only thing you can depend on is the tech you bring to the fight.

- Clockwork

Shoot … I was trying to make a point about needing to find ways to trust others. The basic premise is, even if you keep it professional, that’s not going to stop some arrogant prick from trying

to avoid paying you. Could it have gone differently if we had some trust? Maybe it would have changed things. First, she could have told us about the other teams. Granted, it’s on us for not figuring out we were the decoys, but that’s another point and off topic. We didn’t have a proper network in Seattle at the time to help us, and being a decoy isn’t bad—as long as you know what you’re signing up for up-front. Trusting no one means you have no solid way to do your legwork. And if you tell me you’d rather trust the Matrix than someone working in the shadows, then you deserve what you get. Second, access to resources can be hard. That emergency bolt-hole isn’t just going to show up for you, and that dodgy Matrix site you can rent by the hour is probably a Honeypot set up by Lone Star or another law services provider.
