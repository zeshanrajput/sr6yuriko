## **Critter Commands**

**==> picture [233 x 4] intentionally omitted \<==**

**A tamed critter can follow very basic commands such as “come,” “stay,” and “don’t bite me” without requiring any special training, but anything more than that requires a specifc command. A critter can learn a number of commands equal to its Logic x 2 (this can be improved by qualities from the Critter Bond quality path).**

**A command requires both a trigger and a prescribed action. The trigger may be any stimuli the critter is capable of perceiving, such as a spoken work, gesture, or even an AR object. Whatever form it takes, triggering a command takes a Minor Action. Once the trigger has been performed, the critter attempts to perform the action.**

**Teaching a critter a new command requires one week of effort—during which time you must spend at least one hour with the critter each day—and a successful Training test.**

**If a critter has already learned its maximum amount of commands, they may unlearn an existing command as part of learning a new one—this requires no additional time or effort.**
