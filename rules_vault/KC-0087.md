## (Ahigbe Gang Leader)

Cintra Ime’s origins are mysterious. Her gang, the Ahigbe, appeared in Lagos more than fifteen years ago. Composed primarily of assassins for hire and high-end thieves, the gang made money through some of the oldest professions on earth. Cintra herself is a human of mixed tribal heritage but carries no specific tribal affiliation. The gang expanded since arriving, and they make most of their nuyen through human trafficking.

She’s infamous in Lagos for how she got her seat on the council. The story goes that she asked the other members politely and was turned down. She supposedly smiled and gave them her business card, asking them to call her if they changed their minds or an opening came up. A week later, a council member was assassinated. A week after that, to the minute, same thing—another council member iced. Before a third week passed, Cintra was on the council.

She sat on the sidelines for years before making a push to edge out Tamanous, but it didn’t stick. She’s since redoubled her own flesh-trade efforts, putting her at odds with Tamanous, but she has little concern about their opposition. On the council, she’s content to allow the others to bicker, as she plays the long game. She uses her ample free time to operate as a fixer and is known to be one of the most fair and honest council members, operating with an “honor amongst thieves” mentality.

She’s keeping to the sidelines of current events, as Okoro has not hindered her business dealings at all, and if her rivals are weakened, she can come in after the dust settles.
