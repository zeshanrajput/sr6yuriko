## shadow stock 


Attributes are increased by way of dracoform adjustment points, which are invested similarly to Metatype Adjustment Points (p. 63, _SR6_ ). They may only be spent on the attributes listed for your drake type (see the Dracoform Adjustment Points column), and you _must_ invest at least 1 point into each attribute. Once invested, these points cannot be changed, and they do not vary from one transformation to another. Dracomorphosis allows (and often results in!) exceeding a drake’s regular metahuman attribute maximums set in the Metatype Attributes Table (p. 64, _SR6_ ). Dracomorphosis also allows a character to exceed the usual limit of +4 modification to an attribute, but only for Strength or Body; some powerful drakes have grown large and mighty enough to be mistaken for young dragons. 

Note that this increase will likely result in additional Condition Monitor boxes. If this damage is untreated when shifting back to their metahuman form, the drake can progress into overflow, etc, as damage remains and Condition Monitor boxes vanish. 

_Example:_ The first time she shifts forms, that (human) eastern drake receives 6 points to invest into Body, Strength, Agility or Logic, adding to her existing metahuman attributes when she is in her dracoform. She must assign one point to each, so she begins there, and with her four remaining points she decides to specialize in precision and clarity of thought, inspired by the sinuous, flowing style of her “big sister” eastern dragons; splitting her remaining points between Agility and Logic means that from now on, in her eastern drake form she has +1 Body, +1 Strength, +2 Agility, and +2 Logic. These may exceed her usual human attribute maximums of 6, and if she later indulges in Dracogenesis and chooses to improve her attributes even further, these bonuses—in Body and Strength only—may exceed +4.
