## **> Lanyap**

- **I hate to be that person, but we have to stop with this idea of ubiquitous and persistent data trails. It’s not wrong, but it’s just as volatile as physical or astral traces. Can you imagine the cost of storing all those logs? I’m not saying there aren’t corporations paranoid enough and rich enough to keep everything for as long as possible. I’m just saying that the cost/benefit ratio is lower than generally believed. If you want to know who entered the local Stuffer Shack three days ago, I think you’ll have better luck using the cashier’s memory, rather than hacking a gazillion devices on the off chance that one kept a trace of the individual you are after.**
