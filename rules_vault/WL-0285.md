## **Snow Owl**

## **(Bubo frigidus)**

**Easily confused with the snowy owl (it is the progenitor species) from a distance, the snow owl is triple the size with quintuple the wingspan, so once you are up close, there can no longer be any confusion.**

**==> picture [18 x 765] intentionally omitted \<==**



**==> picture [19 x 765] intentionally omitted \<==**

**The special trait of the snow owl is a narrow zone of weather control, where it can cause snowfall from its wings. While this may seem an odd ability, the snow owl has been shown to spiral around and douse an area in snow and then use its keen thermographic vision to hunt prey in the cold. Frequently, three to ten snow owls will get together and blanket an entire region for all of them to use as a hunting ground. When this occurs, larger prey also become targets of group attacks.**

**That snowing ability doesn’t necessarily have a limit, and a few rather intelligent owls have fgured out how to fy repeatedly back and forth over narrow mountain roads to create slicks, bumps, and even complete blockages if enough of them work together. When they attack, snow owls bleed their prey. They make deep lacerations with talons that secrete an anticoagulant. Even a single good scratch can bring down a large buck in under an hour.**

**The trick then becomes getting to the food before other mountain predators, but the snow owl has a surprise for them. They expect interference and often lie in wait to attack the scavenger. Or the poor gnome who just came over to help out a buddy who’d been scratched by an owl.**

**Yup, that’s the tale here. Checking on piasma activity in the Rockies and a mate, Truffes, got slashed by a snow owl. Thing just silently glided by, slashed, and moved on while still dropping snow. Truffes played tough, but when his platelet system went bonkers trying to stop the bleed and plugged his arteries instead, I ran over to help him when he dropped.**

**Maybe it was some gut sixth sense or just knowing the frst attack seemed odd, but I spun and unloaded my Slivergun into the poor bird. He went from snow to blood real fast.**

**In the end, we got Truffes back up with a medkit, and I now wear a four-inch talon as a trophy. Not my usual thing, but I genuinely felt like a winner in the game of natural selection.**
