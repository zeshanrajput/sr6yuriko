## **The Unseelie Court and Dis**

**As you should understand now, there is no offcial Unseelie Court opinion on Dis, since there is no offcial Unseelie Court position on anything besides opposing the Seelie Court. So all we can look at are tendencies.**

**We know that the Disians seem to have the aim of harming our manasphere in some way, and it’s also likely that the type of damage we’re hearing about would do signifcant damage to the Seelie Court, or at least the Seelie Court’s connection to our realm. The Unseelie Court would like that. The downside is that the Unseelie Court essentially possesses the same nature as the Seelie Court, so it would damage them as well. The question each operative must ask themselves is this: Are they willing to suffer if it means the Seelie Court also suffers?**

**I’m sure the answers to this question are far from unanimous, but I’m also sure of this: A significant number of Unseelie operatives would be very willing to damage themselves and their operations if it means doing the same to the Seelie Court. This means the Seelie Court could be entering a very dangerous time where powerful forces are purposefully lined up against them.**

**You may be asking yourself, why do I care about what happens to the Seelie Court?**

**==> picture [21 x 19] intentionally omitted \<==**

**It’s a valid question. They’re a different plane (mostly) and are often imperious. Why worry about whether or not they are harmed? The answer lies with the Yellowstone Anomaly. It wasn’t just happenstance that led to the connection with the Seelie Court. The Court has always had a different sort of connection with our plane than any other metaplane, in part because it’s not a full metaplane. It’s more of a halfway point, though that fraction is likely wrong—calling it a three-quarters point might be more accurate. As anyone who has visited the Court can attest, it serves as a gateway, a gathering point for all sorts of spirits and metaplanar creatures. So if something happens to the Court, at best we lose a learning resource that gives us easy access to perhaps the widest range of sentient beings we could ever fnd.**

**The worst-case scenario is—well, much worse. If the Court is harmed but the connections remain, we may fnd out that we have an unguarded connection to a wide range of metaplanes who now have an easier way to access our plane. I can’t believe it would be a good thing to have little to no check on creatures approaching our plane. The bugs and the shedim hint at the scope of the dangers that could be waiting for us out there. Whether we’re aware of it or not, the Seelie Court serves as gatekeepers to our realm, and we may not like what we see when the gatekeepers are gone.**
