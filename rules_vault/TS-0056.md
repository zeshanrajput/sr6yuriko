## **Standard-Issue Drek**

**European states have their own form of training, often put on by private companies based on local laws. But no matter where you go, after basic training there’s training for their individual**

**As far as common gear cops tend to carry, the standard kit has come a long way since the days of “nightstick, gun, and cuffs.” In fact, any cop who**

**shows up to work with just that will most likely be sent back to the lockers; maybe even reprimanded or fred. The gear a cop carries is often the difference between catching a perp and going home in a body bag. And that’s just the bare minimum.**

**First, obviously, is the uniform itself. Can’t be a beat cop without one. Like I said before, a lot of what a beat cop does is make themselves seen. As such, they stand out, with most LEPs still preferring dark blues or grays. Lone Star incorporates gold on the shoulders for that extravisible look. Knight Errant uses a black uniform with red accents. A corporation like Eagle Security favors earth tones. Most of the time, these uniforms are matching shirts and pants, though sometimes, depending on the duty, they incorporate an armored jacket and helmet. On uniformed offcers, badges are generally pinned to the breast, and a precinct-specifc patch is on the left shoulder.**

**Built into the uniform, right where the badge mount is, is what is known as the “Man Down” system. It’s a simple orientation switch tied into the offcer’s commlink that detects if the offcer is standing, sitting upright, or prone. Should an offcer become prone for any reason (heh), they have a short window of time to report an “okay” status to the dispatchers before backup is automatically routed to the offcer’s last known location. This little thing seems like it would cause a fair few headaches, but since its implementation, it’s been a standard bit of gear for over ffty years. You never do lose the habit of saying that “okay” status after the frst couple of weeks of wearing one of these things.**

I knew many beat cops who decided it’s too hot outside and took off their armored vest once away from the precinct house. In the last few years, after too many fatalities and lawsuits, its mandatory use is tracked by RFID tags, so headquarters already knows if anyone decides not to wear it while on the beat.**

**The armor itself is nonconductive, though it isn’t subtle, and defnitely bulks out the cops who wear it.**

**One of the more recent (as in the last forty years or so) developments in tech for the average beat cop is the inclusion of personal optics that house an optical suite. Each cop is issued a pair of smartglasses, or a smart monocle, tied to their PAN. These incorporate an image link, a camera, and a smartlink as standard.**

**The intent is that the glasses continually download a stream of information of what the offcer is specifcally seeing to the precinct via their commlink. This doesn’t replace the traditional body cam, but rather supplements it. An offcer can hold up an ID or other object in front of their glasses, request information, and the dispatchers can look at what they have and try to fnd the necessary details. These sensor suites are also built into helmets along with commlink earpieces.**

**Speaking of body cameras, all offcers are required to carry one that automatically switches on under certain parameters, such as drawing a weapon, jarring movements such as running, or jerky movements like a fght. It can also be switched on manually. It cannot be switched off, however, except by the appropriate dispatcher or road supervisor, as the footage captured from the body camera is often used as evidence in a case down the road. It has internal storage for up to ten hours, but it is continually downloaded in a data stream once active. As long as a Matrix connection exists, this camera will record indefnitely.**

**All offcers are also required to wear a wristmounted biomonitor, which is tied into their commlink. Should their vitals be compromised while on the beat, a medical unit is automatically dispatched to their location, and backup is sent as well. If the offcer cannot wear a normal wristmounted biomonitor because, say, they have a pair of chrome arms that prevents this, any biomonitor will do. The wrist one is simply a standard and cheap solution.**
