## The Code of Wuxia

- May not harm the innocent

- May not accept bribes or misuse authority

- May not serve the corrupt

I have to say, I like these guys. There’s a lot of cross-pollination between followers of the Code of Wuxia and disciples of Dragonslayer. Most of them aren’t afraid to have a drink or three to celebrate a shared love of fighting the wicked and corrupt powers that rule society, and the ones who have been around for a while are worldclass ass-beaters. There aren’t many of them in the shadows, mainly because they consider taking jobs from corporate or syndicate Johnsons “serving the corrupt.” On the contrary, many of the Wuxia devotees I’ve met have teamed up with anti-corp or neo-anarchist activist groups.

- Since we don’t have Sticks around to bring it up again, I’ll mention it for him: Many followers of Wuxia get funding and other material support from Wuxing, usually through Buddhist temples in the Chinese nations. That funding makes its way all over the world, including into Aztlán, where a Wuxia follower named Monkey Mocks the Donkey from the Treetops (or “Mocking Monkey”) has been leading the charge against Aztechnology.
