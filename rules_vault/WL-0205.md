## **Tunche**

## **(Panthera tunche)**

**This was probably the best-known myth of the Peruvian jungle. Tunche, which was an indigenous expression meaning “fear,” is the name given to this critter that stalks the Peruvian jungle at night. Legend says it can take the form of anyone the victim knows, thus making it easier to approach them. Its methods aren’t unique, as many other critters create illusions to draw in prey. What makes the tunche special is its ability to read the sensory memories of its victims and use those to form their trap.**

**The parazoological nature of the tunche was in question, as many thought it was a hungry spirit, and I decided to head into the Peruvian jungle and do some of my own research. It wasn’t easy, but getting in with some local tribes helped me get a line on where to start. Most of the locals didn’t care about the critter’s origins, only that it was dangerous, but they were fne with me potentially upsetting the spirit as long as I didn’t bring its torment back to them.**

**Little did they know that in order to hunt and track the tunche, I needed to use them as bait. I set up my fake hunting camp about a half kilometer from a village and then snuck back, used invisibility, and tracked villagers who left. I discovered the tunche was actually an Awakened jaguar with abilities similar to the incubus but with the additional ability to**

**read memories. The critter was all black, though the hint of the jaguar’s color pattern was there.**

**I stayed out too long. The tunche got wind of me and turned its powers and attentions toward me. While I should have known better, as it couldn’t be the voice of Dr. Patterson calling for help, the call also has a compulsion within it. I walk right toward the voice, and suddenly I’m engulfed in water as my summoned spirit materializes and basically slaps me back to my senses. Came out of it in a staring contest with the tunche—and thanks to the spirit being around, I won.**

**The tunche slipped back into the brush, and it stayed clear of me and all my efforts after that.**
