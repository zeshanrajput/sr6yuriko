## Hook

The runners all get an early-morning chime on their commlinks. It’s a message from Cat asking the team to grab lunch with her at the McHughs next to Penn Station in Terminal. That seems like a real downgrade from the Waldorf-Astoria, so it must be about a job. Why else would anyone deliberately go into a McHughs? When the team arrives, two extremely large orks in cheap suits with the bulges of barely concealed shoulder holsters are pushing the restaurant’s sole patron out the door as he tries to finish off a soyburger. After turning the door sign to “Closed” and giving Cat a thumbs up, they take up a position just outside.

Another pair has cleared out the kitchen, and they give Cat an “all-clear” as they shut the back door.

Cat, Spyder, and Mr. Seven are seated around one of the plastic tables bolted to the dining room floor, and she motions for the team to pull up a few more of the restaurant’s cheap plastic chairs. Several trays of greasy Hugh soyburgers, HughFries, and soyshakes sit untouched in the center of the table. After everyone is settled in, she starts by saying, “You’ve done great work so far, and events are rapidly moving toward a conclusion. Mr. Seven is planning to use the portal you located and needs a team to go along. I won’t kid anyone—from what I’ve learned, this metaplane is dangerous, so if it’s a ‘no,’ I’ll understand. If it’s a ‘yes,’ the pay is 10,000 nuyen per person. It also needs to happen soon, as my new contact at the warehouse says something is happening, lots of people and equipment coming up without anything going back down.” Mr. Seven
