## burned-out mage

## Plot Hooks

Because the burned-out mage has a foot in both magic and machinery, they find themes drawn into situations and jobs that are strange—even for the Sixth World. This, combined with the fact that most burned-out mages have already been running in the shadows for a long time and therefore have a lot of friends, enemies, and everything in between, means they find themselves often sucked into situations tied to their past or that blend magic and machinery in ways that don’t typically appear to other shadowrunners. Below you’ll find several plot hooks and adventure seeds that are particularly appropriate to the burned-out mage.

- A cyberdoc who has previously worked on a burned-out mage comes to the PCs with a job. Rumor has it that a local cyberdoc running a street clinic has a new way to patch up Awakened customers with special cyberware that promises not to diminish magical affinity and capability. But local mages and shamans who visit the clinic are beginning to vanish, and no one knows where they’re going. As the player characters investigate, they discover that spells are being used on this strange cyberdoc’s implants that prevent Essence loss in their hosts but also eventually drive the patient mad, before transforming into some kind of ghoul. This cabal of Awakened, cybernetic ghouls is gathering beneath the city. The corps, gangs, and locals are all starting to take notice, each for their own

purposes—and if the player characters don’t put a stop to the mad cyberdoc’s schemes and find a way to either hide or destroy this new ghoul pack, it’ll be war in the streets over this strange new tech.

• A retired runner who now owns a Seattle-area pizza joint hires the player characters to go undercover as delivery drivers deep into the Redmond Barrens, where his former partner, a burned-out mage named Versengen, is being held hostage by local Halloweeners. The retired runner wants Versengen back alive and unarmed, fearing for his old friend’s safety. After breaking into the Halloweeners’ lair and rescuing Versengen, the player characters discover that their employer has already set things up to sell Versengen off to a team of Aztechnology corporate runners who plan on pumping him for information on an old job he did more than thirty years ago. What did Versengen and his treacherous friend do for Aztechnology to hold a grudge for three decades? Why did their employer want the player characters to go into the barrens in disguise? The players must decide whether to walk away and leave the old burned-out mage to die, take the payoff from the Aztechnology agents, or fight it out and unravel the secrets of this thirty-year-old mystery.

- A new gang has sprung up in the local slums, combining both magic and cybernetics: the Famulus. Their spells are too advanced to be simple street magic, and their cyberware is too bleeding edge to have come from some street-level chopshop. They’ve been hitting local talismongers and cyberdoc clinics with no discernible pattern. The player characters are hired by a local talismonger whose shop was burned to the ground by the Famulus to discover who is backing them, providing them with spells and cyberware, and why.

- The runners are hired by an eccentric antiquities collector to pose as bidders at an underground auction where several ancient magical talismans are being offered, along with early cyberware—including the left hand of none other than Leonora Bartoli, the famous violinist who supposedly received the first cybernetic limb. The player character must navigate the social delicacies of the auction while remaining undercover, discover the location of Bartoli’s hand, and steal it. However, they’re not the only ringers at the event and must deal with high-end security (both magical and technological), guards, other potential thieves, and the web of social intrigue that permeates the event. Rumors even persist that one of the elderladies of high society present at the auction is Bartoli herself, who some claim faked her own death and wants to get an artifact of her past back—at any cost.

• A long-retired burned-out mage (perhaps Versengen from the plot hook noted above) reaches out to the runners because a new drug seems to have hit the streets and taken hold in a very select portion of the shadowrunner community: spellcasters who have opted to enhance their bodies with chrome. In short, mages are being burned out by this new drug known, known as brain-burner. The retired burned-out mage hires the player characters to investigate where the drug is being manufactured, who is financing its production, and why this tiny subset of shadowrunners is being specifically targeted. Investigation of some local (and very disreputable) street docs reveals that a biomedical and cybernetics corporation is paying them to use their postop patients as guinea pigs for a clinical trial. Unfortunately, the drug is driving those who use it mad, resulting in unpredictable magical outbursts and even cybernetic rejection—a combination that almost always results in the death of the one taking brain-burner. The runners must find the hidden local lab where it’s being manufactured and shut the operation down before it spreads across the city and kills more than just down-on-their-luck spellcasters looking for help.

• A once-famous burned-out mage who earned a reputation for his arcane talents and brutal efficiency in combat before disappearing in the late 2050s has suddenly reappeared in the city. Whispers on the street say this enigmatic agent who was believed to be long dead has somehow risen from the grave to extract some secret vengeance on those who wronged him more than thirty years ago. The player characters are hired by one of their contacts acting as a middleman to maintain the secrecy of their true employer to hunt down this strange assassin and put a stop to the killings. There appear to be no links between the three that have been slain so far other than the assassin, so it’s up to the runners to find out what connects them and locate the killer before he strikes again. But time isn’t on their side, and as the nights go by, more and more people are dying—including friends and contacts of the player characters. Before long, the burned-out mage turns his eyes toward the player characters themselves, and the hunters become the hunted. What drives this strange murderer? Why is he back now, and what’s the connection between the victims? And most important of

all, how do they stop him when he seems to have more cyberware than seems possible for a mage of his power?
