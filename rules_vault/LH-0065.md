## Ares Seattle: Wayward Children

Not everyone in Ares was happy with Bishop’s stance on the Disian situation. One particular firebrand, Karen King, took matters into her own hands. As the head of Ares Seattle, she rallied enough support within her own division _and_ the new Free Seattle government to break away from the parent corporation, sending a now-infamous message to Bishop that read: Frag you, Bishop. I’ll do this myself.

Since then, King has turned Ares Seattle and the surrounding area into a massive anti-Disian stronghold by negotiating mutual aid and defense treaties with local governments _and_ rallying the disproportionately large number of shadowrunners in the region. Realistically, it shouldn’t have worked, _but it did_ . Through what can only be described as pure grit, Seattle and the surrounding area has remained out of Disian control and is currently acting as a major hub for anti-Disian forces for the entire Pacific Northwest.

- Although not without cost. We didn’t hear a lot coming out of Seattle or the Pacific Northwest after the Week of Death, and that was by design. King, backed by the Seattle Military, pretty much ordered a Matrix blackout and had her people go oldschool with radios and, in some cases, couriers. Eventually, the Disian influence was either eliminated or at least contained. Now, we’re learning more about what happened and it’s not pretty, but it could have been much worse. Ten percent of Downtown was damaged or destroyed, there was major damage to the Tacoma docks, and moderate damage throughout the remainder of the sprawl—not to mention the loss of life.
