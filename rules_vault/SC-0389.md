## Law Firm Investigator

Law firms are great clients for private investigators, and the larger ones bring those functions in-house. A law firm has plenty of things to look into—witness testimony needs to be verified or disproven, financial records need to be dug into, health status needs to be uncovered, environmental conditions need to be investigated, and more. There is plenty of work, and the pay and benefits are usually good, so the real question is, why would anyone leave that job and go freelance?

Often, they don’t, or at least not fully. A number of legal investigators in the shadows are there on a part-time basis, often doing favors for people. Once they have the skills, people know, and they reach out to them. If your brother is getting sued and you know a legal investigator, that’s who you call. If you need to look into the background of someone who is dating a family member, again, you call your legal investigator friend. And if you want to know what that slimy purple stuff oozinginto your water supply is, that friend is definitely who you call.

Sometimes, the investigator finds this side work more rewarding than their day job, and they look to find a way to move into it full time. Other times, their side job becomes too distracting, their quality of work suffers, and they are pushed out of the steady job. But then there are those who keep the balance, dipping their toes into the shadows on evenings, weekends, and vacations, and returning to their safer world when the pressure of the side job becomes too much.
