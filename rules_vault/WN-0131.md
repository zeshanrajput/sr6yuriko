## Hotspots

The Mitsuhama Arcology is a 120-story arcology located in New Harlem. The first floor is a public mall, and the top thirty floors contain a Tenrai luxury hotel. In between are offices for MCT subsidiaries and in-arcology housing for employees of those subsidiaries. The basement levels are split between parking and mechanical systems. The sub-basement levels seem oddly unused; it appears that whatever was here has been removed, and the space has not been repurposed yet. There is a cargo elevator that runs the entire height of the building along with two sets of stairs that do the same. Security seems to be focused on the street-level entrances and access between the pub2. CLEANlic areas and the private floors; there is no security at the sub-basement level. Assuming the team goes in the middle of the night, they will not encounter any security moving from the sub-basements to the twenty-ninth floor. If the run goes in a different direction, more information on the arcology can be found in mission 15.

Once there, the team finds a standard office layout featuring perimeter offices, common support areas in the center, and cubicle farms between. Each desk has two dataterms. One is a rating 3 device tied into the public-facing rating 3 foundation host, and that has already been investigated by Spyder. The other is a rating 6 dataterm linked to a rating 6 framework host physically located in a data center on this floor. The framework host has directories and employee files listing the offices and the thirtieth-floor residences of the three targets. Oddly, the file lists them as being at this location for more than twenty years. There is also a shell connection to a hidden rating 8 foundation host that, when entered, seems as if it’s never been used. While sculpted as a parkland of koi ponds and pagoda-style Shinto shrines, there is no indication that there was ever any data stored here. There is no paydata in either host.

The offices of the three targets are also strangely absent of data; no information about any projects, past or present, is there. A single secretarial desk serves all three offices, and if the players search it, they will discover a handwritten journal, the kind teenagers use to keep things private from parents with commlink codes. A search of it reveals notes about the owner’s strange new bosses that transferred in a few months ago but act as if they have known her for years, who spend their days playing AR games in their offices. There are also comments about files disappearing from the host all the time and an entry that the entire management staff went on a training retreat a few months ago, but no one seems to have any details on where they went or what it covered.

If the runners break into the apartments of the targets, they will discover that all three insist they have never been to Denver and have worked in this building their entire careers, but they can’t actually list any project they have worked on. They will stick to this story regardless of contrary proof presented or interrogation techniques used, including torture or magic. They have no memory of ever being anywhere but here.
