## **Augmented**

**Cybernetic and bioware augmentations were originally designed for metahumans, but it didn’t take long for many corps to start applying them to critters. Early attempts were disastrous, resulting in critters that went berserk or became self-destructive. It’s been decades since those days, though, and advances in technology, training, and other techniques have made critter augmentation viable. Augmented critters may be more diffcult to train and keep in line, but they’re much less likely to lash out at their handlers or try to chew off their cyberlimbs. And some augmentations actually make it easier to interact with critters, such as Evo’s Critter Comm implants. I won’t go into much detail on this topic here, as I’ve already covered it for a different download (p. 133–148,** _**Body Shop**_ **), but I do have one more point to make.**

**Just as with metahumans, cybernetic and bioware augmentations severely degrade Awakened or Emerged capabilities. For this reason, it’s extremely rare to see augmented paracritters and technocritters—and even when you do, the augmentations are kept to a minimum. Mundane critters are much more commonly augmented, usually to improve their physical capabilities to make them much more dangerous against intruders.**
