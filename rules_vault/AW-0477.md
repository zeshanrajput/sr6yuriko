## Gateways, Portals, Rifts

## Astral and Metaplanar Gateways

Over the last few decades, the astral gateway has become the main alternative to the astral quest as a way into the metaplanes. These passageways can be created by a free spirit with the Astral Gateway power (p. 66, _Street Wyrd_ ) or opened with ritual magic. The gateway can lead directly to a metaplane or the Astral Sea, or it can even be used without a passageway to provide non-mages access to the astral plane through forced projection. Astral gateways appear as tears in the fabric of the astral plane but are not visible in the physical world. The main advantage of this method of metaplanar travel is that there is no encounter with the Dweller on the Threshold or any requirements to fulfill an astral quest. As a negative, the traveler is on their own to find what they’re looking for on the metaplanes. Opening an astral gateway causes every object in the area of the gateway to become dual-natured, subjecting anyone in the area to forced projection, even the mundane. Once projecting, those affected can travel the astral plane—within the rules that normally apply to forced projection—or they can enter the opening

of the gateway and proceed to its metaplanar destination. Traveling to a metaplane with an astral gateway generates a metaplanar form for the duration of the stay.

Something unheard of just a few short years ago are metaplanar gateways that allow for twoway physical transference to and from a metaplane, the Astral Sea, or even the astral plane. A metaplanar gateway is visible in the physical world as a tear or opening in the fabric of reality, with the destination visible on the other side. Stepping through physically transports the traveler to their destination and subjects them to metaplanar translation. Unless the effect is inhibited in some way, the traveler is instantly adapted to the new reality they have entered. While extremely uncommon, a free spirit or other entity with access to the Astral Gateway power may choose to have the Metaplanar Gateway power instead, or rarely some may have both abilities. Black market copies of rituals used to create a metaplanar gateway also exists, but opening a metaplanar gateway is far more difficult than opening an astral gateway, and the various formulas for the ritual are products of ongoing research that have a reputation for unreliability. The metaplanar gateway ritual creates a physical transference passageway to the astral plane, the Astral Sea, or the metaplanes. Opening metaplanar gateways to the distant metaplanes requires teams of mages with skill levels and resources only found at the largest corporations or initiate groups of significant resources. It’s also worth noting that Disians use ritual magic to open their metaplanar gateways, including the kilometer-wide gateway responsible for the disappearance of III Corps and the city of Dutchville, Pennsylvania. The knowledge and power required for ritual magic on this scale is far beyond anything possible from the residents of the Sixth World, and the Disians are guarding that knowledge carefully. The larger gateways generated by the Disians also have their own additional rules as outlined on p. 63, _Slip Streams_ .

The destinations available for astral and metaplanar gateways opened by spirits or other entities are limited to metaplanes that they are from or at least have visited. For example, a free spirit of fire couldn’t open a gateway to the Metaplane of Water or the Hive. For gateways created with ritual magic, the ritual leader must have visited the target metaplane before, have a spirit from that plane act as a guide and escort the mage there, or have studied a spirit or creature from that plane sufficiently to understand its metaplane of origin. The locations of some metaplanes—with enough detail to use in this ritual—are recorded in an assortment of sources, but the informationcan be hard to find and even harder to use.

One final note on gateways: The exact location where a gateway opens is not set by the ritual leader or the spirit using the power. For example, if you opened a portal to a metaplane and then later opened another portal on that metaplane back to the material world, it would open to your starting location, even if you wanted it to open somewhere else. However, if you move a few kilometers inside the metaplane to a location with a different harmonic connection to the physical realm, you may open a gateway perhaps leading to the other side of the world, or just as likely ten meters away from the first one. Figuring out where a gateway may open from a given starting point requires an Astral + Intuition (8) test that takes 10 minutes to perform. Up to three related knowledge skills such as astral geography, metaplanar theory, or geomancy can be used with the test, and each one reduces the threshold by 1. Failure to meet the threshold produces a deduced location that is (1D6 x100) kilometers away from the actual destination for each hit below the threshold.
