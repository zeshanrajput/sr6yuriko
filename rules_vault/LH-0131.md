## shinobi clans

First, we have the **Koga** , rumored descendants of Japan’s ancient Koga and Ida Mountain shinobi clans. The Koga operate as an autonomous group under the legendary assassin Ryochi Musashi. Koga assassins accept jobs exclusively from those personally connected to Musashi, including top figures at Hisato-Turner Broadcasting, JETRO group, and Yakuza leaders like Maasaki Watada and Hiro Yamajima of the Mita-gumi. The Koga are celebrated for their exceptional stealth and infiltration skills, favoring members who blend in easily without relying on conspicuous methods.

The **Oni Do** , emerging exclusively in the Sixth World, are Awakened assassins cultivated from Japan’s unnoticed metahuman masses. This modern adaptation echoes the historical tradition of ninja recruitment from societal outcasts. Utilizing adepts or mystic adepts who follow the Invisible Way, they employ magical stealth and assassination techniques alongside traditional shinobi-jutsu. The group continues to operate under Jonin Daiken Tengu, who oversees a network of training safehouses in Kyoto.

Balancing mercenary work with idealism, the Oni Do serves various employers, including corporations and kumi, as long as the price is right. Yet, they significantly reduce their fees for missions aiding metahuman rights in Japan, often collaborating with the Four Oyabun Rengo, New Way clans, Evo Corporation, and the Imperial Household to advance their cause.
