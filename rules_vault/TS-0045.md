## **—Name Withheld, Boston KE Undercover**

**Runners with a longer and more pragmatic view seek to cultivate police contacts. This is one of the best kinds of connections to foster when conducting biz. A friend on the force is one who can warn you when bulls are coming, could get you access to police fles, make evidence go away, and oh so much more.**

**And of course, if they’re willing to work with you, odds are they’ve got a whole network of reprobates just like you. So don’t be afraid to ask for some introductions. Just remember, if they’re willing to work with you, they’re already crooked, so make sure you know if they’re crooked in a way that fts your style. A cop with a strong moral streak might see a partnership with hooders as a way to make sure the best public good is served, offcially or unoffcially, while an out-and-out dirty cop probably sees you as expendable, an alliance of convenience.**

**Keeping this in mind, don’t forget that the police make for potential shadow work as well, and the moral dichotomy stays frmly in place. Good cops need help solving the case and keeping the streets clean when their own authority fails. Meanwhile,**



**bad cops are going to need enforcers, thieves, assassins, and spies to silence witnesses, throw IA off their trails, and fll in the gaps where powerful criminal elements make demands they can’t keep up with. You can collect decent cash payouts, but favors are by far the more valuable reward, so long as you trust them to deliver.**
