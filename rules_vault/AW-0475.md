## shadow cloak

This focus-like magical device is useful during astral projection and is made in the form of some type of garment, typically a hooded cloak or robe. It is simple to bond, and even the mundane can bond to it. Its astral form can be carried along into astral space just like any normal focus. When worn in the astral plane, it adds its rating to the Magic (or Essence) of the projecting traveler to determine the length of time that can be spent in astral form. It does not increase the Magic rating in any other way. The shadow cloak’s effective Force is temporarily reduced by 1 point for every two hours spent projecting, and the temporary Essence loss of the traveler does not occur until after the shadow cloak’s temporary effective Force reaches 0. The shadow cloak’s effective Force returns at the same rate that temporary Essence loss does.

The shadow cloak has other lesser-known functions useful for metaplanar travel. When any rating shadow cloak is carried along by physical transference into the Astral Sea or another metaplane, the wearer has no need for food or water and will not become hungry or thirsty. As long as the shadow cloak has any Force and is worn, the traveler can survive any length of time in the metaplanes. If the shadow cloak is lost or destroyed, the traveler’s need for food and water will return. In the deep and very deep metaplanes, a shadow cloak can protect the wearer from evanescence (p. 175). The cloak can shield the traveler from Essence loss, but these extreme energies permanently erode the enchantments of the shadow cloak. Force reduced by evanescence shielding is permanent and does not regenerate on returning to the physical realm.

A shadow cloak has no known effect in the physical realm beyond the normal function for the type of garment.

Since the astral form is merely a projection of the traveler’s mental attributes, any cyberware, drugs, or spells that affect the mental attributes of the physical body are carried over to the astral form. Sustained or quickened physical spells remain with the body, but any mana spells may be left with the body or carried along with the astral form, in which case it no longer affects the physical body. For example, a sustained mana spell of Invisibility may be left with the body and will continue to affect it, or it may be taken into the astral plane and will then affect the astral form. As a side note, any Detection spells left with the body will not send their information to the astral form, as there is no information sharing between the two bodies beyond the shared pain of being wounded. Activated foci may also be left behind or be taken into the astral plane. The physical part of a focus with its astral form separated will be inert until its astral form is reunited with its physical shell. The choice to take or leave the astral form of a focus

or a spell must be made when initially projecting. Changing your mind about taking something after projecting requires a Major Action with the astral form and physical body in the same location. The astral forms of foci and spells may be dropped at any time, and they will return to the physical body on their own. Picking them back up requires a return to the physical body’s location (as noted above) to retrieve them.

Just as mages can’t wander the astral plane indefinitely, mundanes—or adepts—who have been subjected to forced projection also have a limited amount of time in astral space. Non-mages can spend [Essence (rounded up) x 2] hours in the astral with their Essence temporarily reducing by 1 point for every 2 hours spent projecting. Essence returns at the same rate after being reunited with the body. If the Essence reaches zero, the astral form crumbles into non-existence, and their physical body dies.
