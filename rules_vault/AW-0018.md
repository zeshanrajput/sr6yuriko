## How to Visit a Metaplane

Travel to a metaplane is not as simple or predictable as traveling from one location to another on the physical plane. The key difficulty is that metaplanes are not physically connected to the physical plane—they are connected astrally via the Astral Sea. This means that the metaplanes are accessible to astral travelers—magicians capable of astral projection—provided that they have completed their first initiation.

Not all practitioners of magic can undertake an astral quest to the metaplanes in this way—only full magicians who are capable of astral projection are able to leave their body behind. While a number of methods of inducing forced projection exist, an initiated mage must still act as the guide to lead the quest. This form of travel is not easy or without cost, for every time a magician visits the metaplanes in this way, they must face the Dweller on the Threshold. The Dweller is a mysterious entity of immense power, who seems to have taken on the job of testing every would-be metaplanar traveler. Sometimes it appears before you and asks direct, piercing questions. Sometimes it pulls a traveler into a pocket metaplane of its own creation in order to test you through an experience. It is always unpleasant, forcing you to face something about yourself that you would prefer to forget. Unless you lose your resolve, you will pass the test. I often wonder the reasons why the Dweller chooses to challenge astral travelers. Is it attempting to guide metahumanity to make better choices by forcing us to face our own faults? Is it trying to scare off those with weak and unprepared minds



so that they are not destroyed by the greater dangers that can be found on the metaplanes? Or does it gain some sustenance or power from inflicting these tests upon us? Only one thing is certain: if you intend to project your astral form into the metaplanes under your own power, you must face the Dweller.

Beyond the astral quest, there are other ways of travel that avoid a visit to the Dweller: gateways, portals, and rifts. Or, if you don’t know the exact location of a metaplane, you can also search for it in the Astral Sea. Astral versions of these three types of passageways are openings in astral space that create a direct connection between two metaplanes—or between the Earth’s astral plane and a metaplane. Less common—or more correctly, slightly more rare— are metaplanar versions that do the same thing but in the physical world. Astral gateways are created by ritual magic or by some free spirits, while astral rifts tend to be pseudo-natural astral phenomena, and astral portals are gateway-like passageways generated by artifacts, usually man-made but there are instances where they appear to have been created by some ancient magician or spirit. Many astral portals are predictable and open with command triggers, but some operate on their own schedule, opened by unknown triggers that can be difficult to predict. Most astral rifts are temporary in nature. Some of them only appear for a short time, others reappear at chaotic or predictable intervals. Others move gradually over time, shifting across the land with the passage of each day. If you intend to engage in regular metaplanar travel or exploration, I highly recommend familiarizing yourself with as many known astral passageways as you possibly can. They are difficult to track down on Earth, but are a constant source of gossip among the spirits of the metaplanes. Be polite, ask around, and make a few friends, and you’ll find it much easier to get around. That’s good advice for the physical plane as well as for metaplanar travel, now that I think of it.

But what are passageways, other than a hole in reality? They can be thought of as connection points, thin zones, permeable membranes, or doorways, that can be used to travel immediately from one plane to another. Astral passageways are the less obtrusive of the two, as they are typically only visible on Earth via astral perception—they are not visible to the physical eye. While astrally perceiving a passageway, you should be able to see what is on the other side—and what is on the other side is able to see you in return. To travel through an astral passageway, all you need to do is push through in your astral form. This takes a little effort but is not difficult or dangerous.

An astral rift is similar to an astral shallow and may simply be a stronger, more powerful version of the same phenomena allowing travel through the astral barrier. Perhaps the most famous astral rift is the one that was created at the site in which

Dunkelzahn met his end. You do not need to be an astrally projecting magician to perceive and make use of an astral rift—they are quite obvious to the physical as well as astral observer. Anyone who passes through, or even comes too close to, an astral rift will find that their astral form is forced to project through it.

When visiting a metaplane, you may encounter portals and rifts that connect from one metaplane to another. Anyone can travel through these without the hazard of needing to pass back through it to return home. Astral portals seem to be fairly common on most metaplanes, and the more stable among them are likely to be popular hubs for travel and trade.

There are some significant advantages to using astral passageways for metaplanar travel. First, if you know the location of your destination—or are lucky enough to have a portal leading to where you want to go—they are quite a lot faster than traversing the Astral Sea. Second, if you know what you are looking for, it avoids an encounter with the Dweller and the need to perform an astral quest. As an example, in the Tranquil Gardens located within the Plane of Plants, you could use its astral portals to travel instantly to any nearby metaplane. Traveling from the Plane of Plants to the Plane of Fire via the Astral Sea instead of using the portals there would take you many hours. For this reason, many metaplanar denizens refer to these portals as gates or doorways.

Visiting the metaplanes via astral projection is the most common and least hazardous means of metaplanar travel, either on an astral quest or by using an astral passageway, but it has a significant limitation: You can’t bring anything other than knowledge and experience back with you. To the magician, the power and knowledge gained through an astral quest are reason enough to travel the metaplanes. But the metaplanes are magical places full of resources that can only be gathered and exploited by visiting them in physical form.

To this end, many have searched for something more powerful than an astral gateway or portal—an opening that allows physical objects and individuals to pass through onto a metaplane. Various phenomena throughout recorded history point to the possibility that physical forms can disappear into the metaplanes, from the ancient legends of the Bermuda triangle to the more recent vanished UCAS military forces sent to relieve Detroit of its invae infestation. And some have been found, the most wellknown being the Yellowstone Anomaly. Naturally produced metaplanar rifts, outside of the three stable ones near Denver, are rare, unpredictable, and not likely to be of any practical use to the metaplanar traveler. A stable gateway can be built, but it requires significant effort from multiple magicians, along with an immense exHOWpenditure of radical reagents and orichalcum. A number of these gateways have been established by megacorporations, but don’t expect to be able to buy a ticket to use them anytime soon. These gateways are highly protected corporate secrets. They will almost certainly become common knowledge over time, but for now they remain accessible only to those who financed their creation. Rumors are also constantly circulating about ancient portals that were created in the time of the Fourth World or even earlier. That said, I’m sure any of these that are found and reactivated are quickly claimed and secured by the most powerful groups and individuals in the Sixth World.

If you don’t have access to a portal leading to where you want to go, the next best way is to open a gateway with the aid of a free spirit. Most free spirits are capable of creating an astral gateway to their plane of origin, and a very few can open gateways that allow physical travel. Additionally, it doesn’t seem to tax them to do it. Free spirits are aware of the value of their abilities and are highly unlikely to allow free passage. You’ll have to make a deal of some kind, and if it’s a physical gateway, I highly recommend that you include arrangements to return home as part of the deal. Because if you travel to the metaplanes in the flesh, you must return to the Earth via another gateway or remain on the metaplanes indefinitely. Unlike an astrally projecting traveler who can return from the metaplanes whenever they choose, you can be trapped indefinitely on the metaplanes if you have no return gateway. You will also need to find local sources of food and drink—which is easy on some metaplanes and nearly impossible on others. Though the price required is often much higher than you might be inclined to pay, arranging for the spirit who provided the gateway to act as a guide and remain with you until it is time to return can be well worth the expense.
