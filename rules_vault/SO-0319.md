## Game Information

The blur mask does not interfere with natural vision or throwback optical cameras, but it does affect cybereyes. When blurred, the user’s face is completely indistinguishable by electronic means. No amount of hits on a Perception test (or any other method) will change this situation. The only way to see through an active blur mask is to use

natural vision. And the only way to capture an image of a blurred face is using a retro-tech camera that uses actual film.

The best way to get past or defeat a blur mask is to hack it. It can be deactivated or bricked if the hacker in question doesn’t care about being obvious. By default, blur masks are set to run silent on the Matrix, and higher-quality masks are equipped with additional and more effective countermeasures to further prevent detection. The mask’s dice pool to avoid being detected by Matrix Perception tests is its rating x 2. The user can manually override this and use their own Logic and Sleaze attributes to hide their mask from the Matrix if they choose.

Blur masks can be purchased as separate devices. They must be worn uncovered on the user’s face, usually affixed directly to the skin with an adhesive. These are obvious when worn by themselves, so blur masks are often integrated into a device or accessory that can be worn without drawing suspicion. A blur mask can be installed in glasses, goggles, headphones, ear buds, or helmets. Or they can be installed in an outfit’s collar or hood, be it normal clothing or armor. In either case, a blur mask requires 1 capacity.
