## Cybergills

Bottled air works as advertised at atmospheric pressures or in shallow water. But once you dive deeper than twenty meters or so, the water pressures require more and more air to supply each lungful, so breathing consumes air more quickly.

Cybergills avoid this problem, as well as other problems such as nitrogen narcosis involved in

breathing atmosphere at depth. With this augmentation, the user draws water into the lungs, where oxygen is extracted from the water directly. While breathing water in this way, the lungs and vocal tract are completely full of water, making regular speech impossible. It costs a Minor Action to take water into the lungs and begin breathing it, but it takes a Major Action to forcefully expel all the interior water in a somewhat painful fit of coughing before the user can resume breathing air.
