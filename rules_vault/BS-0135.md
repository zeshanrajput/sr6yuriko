## Articulated Weapon Mount

A common desire among those seeking additional arms is to have more hands that can hold more guns. Articulated weapon mounts serve this niche without the expense and invasiveness of adding full limbs to the user. Little more than a cybernetic smart firing platform mounted on the shoulder, these systems consist of a retractable, articulated basic arm thirty centimeters in length when fully unfolded. This arm terminates in a weapon rail that can accommodate a firearm no bigger than a shotgun, automatically tracking the weapon in the direction of the user’s focus. The user can attack with a mounted weapon as if it were equipped in their hands. It takes a Minor Action to deploy or retract an articulated weapon mount, and it takes an additional Major Action to snap a weapon in or remove it from the mount. The system cannot retract while a weapon is attached. The user can only implant up to two articulated weapon mounts.

**Cyberware overdrive:** An articulated weapon mount can deploy while the user is wearing sleeved clothing, pushing hard enough to tear through fabric. Armor torn through in this way grants no Defense Rating until repaired (see **Build/Repair** , p. 95, _SR6_ ).
