## Jonathan Blake

Blake and his behind-the-scenes partner, Miles Lanier, have invested a lot in developing Animus technology for their own use, and they’ll be damned if they’ll let it just get away. They also know that the technology they have worked so hard for is very powerful and should not be spread too widely. This means Blake is willing to spend to keep the status quo.

Lanier, who suffers from cognitive fragmentation disorder, is not all that interested in the Evo plan to use Animus technology to cure monads. In his view, there are other viable cures out there, so one this invasive is not needed. He’s more worried that some other negative mental plague might develop if Animus’ technology falls into the wrong hands (how he defines his hands as being right and others being wrong in this matter is another subject entirely).

Blake and Lanier are certainly not poor by shadowrunner standards, but they also don’t have the resources to compete with the Big Ten. Any plea Blake makes will hinge on the dangers of this technology being shared too broadly—while also looking at the negative effects of losing it entirely.

_Sends_ : Blake

_Resources_ : Some personal cash, but Blake and Lanier do not have a shadowrunner team on call to run interference for them in this matter.

_Tactics_ : Honesty and a little cash. Blake will genuinely pay the runners and will tell them straight out that any corp that offers them a big score is going to kill them.

_Offer_ : 75,000 nuyen

_Conditions for Attack:_ None

_Ways to Play off Other Factions:_ Obviously, Blake’s interest overlaps with the CIA and Mitsuhama, and if he feels like he’s struggling with the PCs, he’ll turn to them to help do some persuading. He will not increase his offer; instead, he’ll push his allies to the fore and hope _they’ll_ increase their offer.

_Aftermath Attack Risk:_ None
