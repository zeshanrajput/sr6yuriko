## **Leverage Is Hard to Find**

**This is perhaps the real sticking point. If Lofwyr could make an ideal plan from this point, he’ll likely assist Dis for a time while he accumulates leverage against them, then deploy that leverage at the right time to stop the Disians in their tracks. That would be perfect, and that’s the way rich people and corporations work.**

**Think of it. You often hear people marveling over the vast amounts of wealth the richest people in the world have and how they could never spend all of it in a lifetime, in a hundred lifetimes, in a thousand lifetimes. That’s because that’s how most of us see money—it’s a means to buying things we want. But once you get to a certain level, money is not about sustenance. It’s about keeping score. Get wealthy enough, and it’s about playing big-stack poker.**

**Those of you who play Texas Hold ’Em know what I mean. When you have the most chips at the table, you can control the fow of play. You especially can bet aggressively before the fop, using your stack to intimidate people from making a bet at all. If they never see the three cards of the fop, they won’t be emboldened to think they have a chance in the long game. If you keep them worried about losing everything, they keep their ambitions limited.**

**That’s how the corps play the game. Deploy resources to keep us focused on what we might lose so that fear makes us timid. That’s how they** _**always**_ **want to work.**

**But they don’t know how to use their resources against Dis, because the one thing they know the Disians want—mana—is something they cannot give them. They have yet to identify anything else they want. Money, political power, luxurious resources—the Disians occasionally fnd uses for these things, but they don’t truly** _**want**_ **them the way so many people on Earth do. Lofwyr has not been able to dangle various trinkets in front of them to make them dance to his tune. He keeps looking, and he’s becoming increasingly desperate to fnd something. And we know where that kind of desperation leads.**

- **The more desperate we are, the more primal we become.**

- **> Many-Names**

**So yes, Lofwyr is ready to enter into a sort of arms race with the Disians. He needs to know how much he can hurt them, and he needs to deliver that pain in a quick, effcient manner. Not that he**

**wants to actually do it—he wants to use it as a bargaining chip and get a classic cold war going.**

**> Since we were already talking poker, let me continue the analogy this way: This is not an area where you should try to bluff. Too much bluffing with a big stack is a good way to transform it to a short stack. If you want the Disians to be scared, you need something that scares them. And you probably will need to give it a few test runs on your opponents—just to make an impression.**

**> Sunshine**

**I think that, given his druthers, that’s what Lofwyr would do—fnd a weapon and wave it around threateningly. Not everyone agrees, though, which has led to two novel things—some dissension in the ranks of S-K, and Lofwyr being open to the idea that the dissenters might have a point.**

**Here are some of the arguments he’s hearing and where they’re coming from.**

**Professor Mesut Sahin of the board approaches this from a practical angle. Weapons development takes time and money, and it takes a whole hell of a lot of more money if you don’t have time. Sahin knows a signifcant investment would have to be made, with no guarantee that it would produce anything worthwhile (they might not fnd a workable weapon, and even if they do, they don’t know how the Disians will react, or whether they will have already prepared for it). He doesn’t want to see waste when they could just pick an ally somewhere and advance a strategy.**

**Board member Evelyn Westlake has no interest in a high-stakes contest with metaplanar beings. In her point of view, Saeder-Krupp’s involvement should basically be over. They can disengage and let other people deal with any direct conficts. There are major corporate and magical powers in the fray, and Westlake argues that they can handle the matter themselves. Sadder-Krupp can just step back and let things go however they go.**

**According to many reports I have seen, Lofwyr is tempted by this direction. What better for a diffcult situation than to just walk away and let it work itself out? In the best of all worlds, the other parties might wound each other, leaving wreckage to walk into. And it’s always better to walk into the wreckage than be part of it.**

**The biggest weakness here is uncertainty. Can the various other parties handle things? Will they end up weaker, or could victory make one side all too powerful? What will be the ultimate nature of the confict, and what collateral damage might result? Stepping back means not knowing the answers to any of these questions, and that bothers Lofwyr, especially the collateral damage part—he wants to infict collateral damage, not experience it.**
