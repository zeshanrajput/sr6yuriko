## Nano-Critter Matrix Relay

An injection of specially programmed nanites (developed with the assistance of monads, of course) wires itself into a tiny critter’s nervous system and uses it to amplify Matrix signals. This has a minimal effect on its own, but swarms of these critters will overcome Matrix noise within their immediate vicinity. Some megacorporations have implanted millions of insects, pigeons, rats, and other local vermin in order to improve Matrix coverage in areas that are difficult or expensive to maintain more traditional hardware relays, such as urban barrens where these devices are frequently tampered with, destroyed, or stolen. The most profound application of this technology is in the ocean, where plankton are “infected” with nanites that have been observed to spread to uninfected plankton. When a swarm of at least ten nano-critter Matrix relays are together, all Matrix noise within 10 meters of the swarm is reduced to a minimum noise rating of 2.
