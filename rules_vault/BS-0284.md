## External Control of Nanite Colonies

While nanohives are a requirement for most nanite users, it is possible to have a limited volume of nanites in the body without one. A single transient colony can be controlled by a commlink running a nanite pilot program. Controlling a different colony needs a different pilot program. The program rating is limited to the Data Processing attribute of the commlink, and the nanite volume of a transient colony is limited to the Program rating. The Availability and legality of a nanite pilot program is the same as a template program. Colonies run from a pilot program function normally, but without the cyberscreens of a nanohive the nanites are rapidly flushed from the body. Transient colonies injected without an active nanohive system will last for one week after the initial injection, then have their nanite volume drop by one point per day.
