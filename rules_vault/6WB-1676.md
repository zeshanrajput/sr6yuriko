## Hellcome to Berlin

When _Shadowrun_ was first conceived and published, Seattle was the developers’ choice of the city where players’ running careers would begin. Since then, countless other locations for _Shadowrun_ campaigns have been described in detail and continuously updated and expanded over the course of various editions. One of these locations is the former German capital Berlin, which is discussed on the following pages.

Berlin is Germany’s largest city (the largest sprawl being the Rhine-Ruhr-Megaplex with Saeder-Krupp’s Neu-Essen) and a booming business location today. That was not always the case:

**Act I: Downfall (1990–2039).** Already structurally burdened at the fall of the Berlin Wall in 1990 by rampant corruption in the West and an ailing East, Berlin was completely dependent on federal funds at the beginning of the millennium—and therefore more affected than any other German city by the disintegration of the old German Federal Republic. The upheavals of the Awakening, race riots, the VITAS epidemic, two EuroWars, and excesses of violence by police forces desperately struggling for control culminated in ongoing civil war-like conditions in the city that caused countless citizens, businesses, and ultimately the federal government to flee from Berlin. Abandoned and forsaken, the neo-anarchist forces, with the help of organized crime gangs, deserters from the collapsed Neo-Soviet army, and the heavily armed Sharia militias concentrated in the Kreuzberg “Emirate,” finally gained the upper hand: the state government was overthrown and anarchy declared via the Final Law.

**Act II: The Flux State (2039–2055).** After a period of total chaos and violence, a balance between warring factions and alternative communal structures emerged. Under the so-called “F-State” (F for

“fluxus,” meaning fluid, ever-changing), whenever a shift in the power structure seemed likely to establish a new status quo, the rest of Berlin’s factions banded together to utterly strip this threat of the F-State from power, often by killing its figureheads and redistributing their possessions. Under this system, only a true political mastermind could hope to build a firm power base without attracting attention, but the constant shift in alliances and rivalries designed to promote the Flux State made such political manipulation all but impossible.

**Act III: The New Berlin Wall (2055–2072).** The F-State lasted until an alliance of corporations, backed by the fledgling Allied German States (AGS), decided to “liberate” the city through a coup. While the campaign was successful in the west of the city, the pro-anarchist forces were able to rally in the east and use guerrilla warfare to prevent a full corporate takeover. The result was a renewed division of Berlin into a western Corporate Sector and an eastern, walled-off Anarcho Zone.

**Act IV: Berlin Unity (2072).** Severe anarchist terrorist attacks against the West Sector and escalating punitive expeditions by corporate forces into the walled-off East Zone led to negotiations between corporate representatives and moderate anarchists in 2072. Mediated by AGS diplomats, an agreement was reached and fixed in the Berlin Treaty. Since then, corporations and the main alternative groups have participated in government and administration. Corporations and alternatives were granted extensive freedoms in self-government, their respective territories were mutually recognized, and the border fortifications around the former Zone were removed.
