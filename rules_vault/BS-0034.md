## optional rule: minor prosthetics

Minor prosthetic attachments bridge the gap between a separate (or detachable) prosthetic and a cyber- or bio-implant. They do not cost Essence, and they restore some level of functionality, at least cosmetically. They are also a lot cheaper than a full implant, and it is faster to heal from the surgery, as you are not rewiring your nervous system to accept different kinds of input.

Most of the time, a minor prosthesis will allow a person to attempt and complete tasks that would otherwise be impossible without some sort of attachment. For a prosthetic leg, this would allow the person to walk and run. For a hand prosthesis, a person could carry an item with both hands.

Note that each item has a recovery time listed, which is the time needed for surgical wounds to heal before the prosthesis can be used. Items may also have a training time. During this time, an additional –1 dice pool penalty is applied to any test that uses movement or manipulation as the person learns to trust their new body.
