## **Asian gargoyle**

## **(Gargoyle saxi velox)**

**They were initially categorized as a new species, but this was quickly changed once specimens were captured. In most of Asia, they are called onigawara, which I am told translates to demon tiles. All Asian gargoyles or onigawara have heads of lions or onis, and when relaxing lie down on all fours like an animal. Their wings are completely transparent when gliding or motionless. They are the smallest of the gargoyles, having a height of 1.25 meters and weighing 125 kilograms. Some speculate their small size is due to their unusually low caloric intake. Others believe that their speed and grace outpaces all other gargoyles, causing them to burn more calories, which reduces their growth.**
