## The Edge of Oblivion

Cybermancy depends on a few factors. First, the procedure must take place at a delta clinic or better. Treat it as having to find a piece of Availability 9 gear in order to find a delta clinic that can handle this procedure (p. 244, _SR6_ ). The locations of such clinics are well-kept secrets, so many contacts will have to be compensated heavily to reveal them. A runner who wishes to find one will likely end up owing more than a few favors.

Once a clinic is found, the runner must negotiate with whoever owns it to come up with some sort of arrangement. Commonly, these deals entail the runner agreeing to be kept on a tight leash. If that is undesirable, often the runner can offer themselves up as a subject for advanced research and experimentation. Or course, if that road is taken, the cyberware installed as part of the cybermantic procedure may not be exactly what was expected.

As far as magic is concerned, cybermancy requires a massive amount of mana to manipulate, and often entire initiatory groups are dedicated just to this function. At the minimum, the cleansing, sacrifice, and invocation metamagics must be known for this ritual to function, in addition to the unique metamagic of cybermancy. While this technique can be taught, it’s almost always a closely guarded secret of these sects, making those who can perform this metamagic a very rare commodity.

Among the pieces of cyberware required for the procedure, primary among them is the invoked memory stimulator (IMS). Implanted directly into the recipient’s hippocampus, the IMS uses chemical signals to effectively kickstart memories in the brain. This process seems to be crucial in preventing someone’s life force from slipping away, and those with these augmentations have reported a feeling of safety and security while the implant is actively stimulating these memories. On the downside, the experience of these memories tends to put the subject in a state of reverie while the rest of their body more or less resets.

The next pieces of necessary cyberware to be installed are auto-injectors to fill what meat is left with the necessary life-sustaining drugs. These drugs must be replenished every month, which is part of the regular maintenance and price of being something that should already be dead.

The first actual step in the cybermantic procedure is for one of the initiates present to create the formula that will be used. Each one of these is unique to the procedure being done and must be drawn up for every subject. This requires a successful Enchanting + Magic (8) test. This threshold may be reduced by destroying objects or even people important to the subject as part of the creation process.

The subject must then be prepared as a magical vessel to accept the impending magic. This entails a cleansing rite, and often inking or tattooing magical sigils onto the subject. After the cleansing, one of the magical initiates present must undertake a metaplanar quest to a difficult-to-reach metaplane. What this metaplane actually represents is unknown, but some say it is the metaplane where spirits of metahumanity go after death. What is known is that it can only be reached after crossing a great void. To reach this metaplane, an initiate must be accompanied by a guiding spirit, which must be summoned and Invoked as part of this quest. No one knows what exactly the initiate goes to find and bring back from this distant and mysterious place, but the cybermantic ritual cannot commence without it.

Once the quest is successfully completed, the cybermantic ritual begins. Simultaneously, the surgeries begin. During this time, the subject has no vitals and has no immune system at all, so the procedure must be performed only within the most sterile of environments. Any interruption or infection from outside sources causes the ritual to automatically fail. How this ritual plays out is completely up to the gamemaster, as players are never expected to have the requisite resources to perform it themselves.

Cramming all those implants into a body to force it below zero essence isn’t easy. The implantation process requires a Biotech (Cybertechnology) + Logic (20, 1 hour) Extended Test. All implants are likely to be at least delta grade, with many structural components being of cybermantic grade. Teamwork bonuses can be applied to this test. Along with everything else installed during this procedure, an invoked memory stimulator and auto-injector must be included.

Throughout the course of the procedure, the dark, powerful emotions that are part of the ritual create a sort of astral pollution in the vicinity of the operation. This functions as a high mana ebb (p. 167, _Street Wyrd_ ) aspected toward the magicians conducting the ritual. This occurs because the procedure is going against the natural flow of existence, suspending entropy, and forcefully binding the waning energies of a metahuman spirit desperately trying to flee its physical form. To prevent disruption of the ritual, cleansing metamagic is often used. All of these efforts combine to make sure a subject’s spirit does not flee during implantation when zero Essence is breached. At this point, the cybermantic ritual forces the spirit to inhabit the empty shell of its body.

Following the actual procedure, the subject is then kept sedated and closely observed for one week, plus an additional day for every point or partial point of Essence below zero. At the end of that time, the subject (hopefully) awakens as a cyCYBERMANCYberzombie. When they do so, they will have gained a Magic rating of 1 from the ritual.

When they awaken, the subject must make an Intuition + Willpower test. The lead magician of the ritual’s Conjuring (Binding) skill may be added as a positive dice pool modifier on this test. The threshold for this test is 3 + 1 for every point or partial point of Essence that has been taken below zero. If the character succeeds, the procedure is a success, and they gain 10 Karma worth of negative qualities. If the character fails but still gets at least 1 hit, the procedure _mostly_ succeeds, but they gain 10 Karma worth of negative qualities plus 10 Karma for every point they failed to reach the threshold by. If the test fails, the procedure fails, and the character dies. Worse, on a glitch, something has gone horribly wrong with the ritual, and a gateway has opened, bringing a nasty hitchhiker back from that dark place traveled to during the metaplanar quest. What that something is, is up to the gamemaster, but it should be sufficiently terrifying.
