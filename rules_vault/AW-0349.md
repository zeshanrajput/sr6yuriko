## Arrival at the Island of Tandora

The Island of Tandora is large—roughly the size and shape of Madagascar. The first thing you’re likely to notice as you approach is the smoke rising from the massive volcano at its center. Next to the volcano is a huge lake that is partially obscured by mist and steam. Two huge rivers lead out from this lake, heading in opposite directions toward the island’s edge. The second thing you’re likely to notice is the terrain, which is divided into four roughly equal regions: a jungle to the south, a forest to the north, a desert to the east, and a savanna to the west. It’s almost like someone decided to have four distinct biomes and made sure they each got fair representation on the island.

The next thing you can’t miss are the dinosaurs. Before you get too excited, no, they aren’t extinct animals from Earth. Like most entities in the metaplanes, the dinosaurs here are spirits. But they definitely look like dinosaurs. Huge, long-necked sauropods roam the savannas, endlessly grazing. Vicious two-legged carnosaurs that you’d be well within your rights to describe as a t-rex stalk the forest and its edges in search of prey. Stegosaurs, ankylosaurs, and ceratops all make for difficult prey. The colorful flocks of archaeopteryx (we started calling them lizard-birds before long) share the sky with pterosaurs—all of which are carnivorous by nature. Even the water is full of beast spirits from a prehistoric age, with alligators that would give the Ghost Gator a run for his nuyen, plesiosaurs with necks long enough to attack creatures on the shore without leaving the water, and razorfish that make piranhas seem as docile as goldfish.

The eagles set us down on the northern shore of the island, and it wasn’t until we reached ground level that the gigantic scale of the foliage became apparent. We soon found shelter under the roots of a really big tree, and there was plenty of room for us to set up camp. We scouted a small perimeter—roughly a kilometer radius— and started gathering material to construct ourARRIVALselves a new boat. Kai had removed the outboard motor from our previous craft before our flight to the mountain. Shade carried a wide range of tools and equipment, and there was ample raw material to work with. We’d initially planned to build a raft, but the trees were much too large to produce logs of the right size. It cost us an extra couple of days searching for suitable materials, but we eventually found a patch of reeds growing in the river that would be perfect.

And this is when we found out about the razorfish. We didn’t lose anyone, but it was close. Aspect was nearly killed, but it was nothing a Heal spell and a medkit couldn’t make better. We considered traveling on foot, but the pace would be far slower. With some care, we collected the reeds we needed for our raft and took some extra time to be sure that the craft was sturdy. Hemdale’s ally spirit provided some additional protection using its guard power, and the rest of us kept careful watch for threats in and out of the water.

Even with the assistance of the outboard motor, it took us days to travel upriver on our way to the volcano at the island’s center. It would have been nice if the eagles had dropped us off there instead, but as a general rule I don’t complain about the little stuff when powerful eagle spirits offer me a ride. Well before we got there, we made an incredible discovery: There are people living on the Island of Tandora.
