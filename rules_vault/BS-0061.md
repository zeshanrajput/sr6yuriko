## Paying for Cyberware

## Posted by: The Smiling Bandit

Cyberware isn’t as expensive as bioware, but it can still be a quick way to drain your bank account. I’ve known some runners who started off with a simple prosthetic when they lost an arm during a run. But as it is so often with tattoo or piercing enthusiasts, that one augmentation doesn’t seem to be enough over time. So a couple of months later, they show up with a hand that can crush the bumper on my car. Then a few more months later, they come back with one that looks more subtle and doesn’t make the general populace run screaming when they show up at the fancy restaurant for the meet. Sometimes, you do some work for the street doc, and sometimes you just scrimp and save your nuyen until you can find the right contact to get that cyberware.

The corporate workspace can be just as cutthroat as the streets, and obtaining a little chromecan give you a critical edge. But then, it’s never just a little bit of chrome, is it? And the corporations encourage this behavior by offering promotion incentives and payment plans. Joe Wageslave can’t afford a skilljack that would secure his nomination for management, so he takes out a loan from the company credit union and has his monthly payments conveniently deducted from his paycheck. Or even worse, he borrows against whatever retirement plan the company might have, running on a treadmill to load those proverbial sixteen tons of coal. It’s a fraggin’ terrible cycle. Almost makes you feel bad for those cops who have some chrome; if they get killed or medically retired while carrying the debt for their wired reflexes, their family is going to be out in the cold.

So, how do the SINless or criminals get their hands on this stuff? It’s not like you have an IRA to borrow from, after all. You can search the Matrix and see who’s selling. It’ll find you what you need if you trust the seller. But most folks are going to need contacts to help them get the gear they need, and they’re going to need _trusted_ contacts to install that fancy chrome inside you. Most of the cyberware is made by megacorporations, so unless you want used, you’re going to need someone connected with a corporation who can smuggle you what you need. If you’re lucky, you’ll find a streetdoc who happens to have some corp connections, but you might be carrying some experimental chrome that is broadcasting back home based on your performance.

The other two main groups I see sporting a lot of chrome are athletes and military. Not all athletic leagues are cyberware-friendly, but there are enough leagues that allow it that it’s not unusual to see a basketball player or urban brawler sporting some heavy-grade ’ware. And our military and mercenary groups will definitely pop some augments into their soldiers for battlefield advantage. They are little more than hosts to company-owned hardware that’s only “temporarily” being housed within their body during their contracted service. It’s no fluke that you see so many former military and merc backgrounds among street samurai. What’s a cheaper way to get chromed than on someone else’s dime, and then disappear rather than turn it back in?
