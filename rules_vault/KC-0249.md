## Triads

The Mafia and the Yakuza might dominate the press, but the Triads outmatch them in terms of both age and size. Dating back to the White Lotus Society’s start in the thirteenth century, the Triads

have been around twice as long as the Yakuza and four times as long as the Mafia, and they boast millions of members worldwide. Their widespread presence allows them to play a major role in the global drug and smuggling trade—both of goods and of people—and they lead the pack in brand counterfeiting, talislegging, and BTL production. The term “triad” is said to derive from a translation of San Hé Huì (Triple Union Society), which refers to the union of heaven, earth, and humanity, which also serves as the possible origin of the triangular imagery that the Triads frequently use.

One important thing to note is that Triads don’t have a central authority, so they’ve all developed pretty much independently of each other as a result. Though they have evolved different traditions and practices, they remain much the same in many ways at their core. For instance, all Triads have the same hierarchy, and each member of the hierarchy serves much the same purpose, with little variance from Triad to Triad. But there are distinctions. For instance, every Triad has a White Paper Fan in their ranks to keep their accounting in order and preside over their Matrix activity, but the importance of the position varies. Some Triads, like Seattle’s Eighty-Eights and Hong Kong’s 289s, are more active in Matrix crime than others.

- Numerological associations are common in Triad practices and go far beyond the names chosen by the two listed here. Each rank in the hierarchy has a number attached to it, and each digit in those numbers add to something that indicates the meaning of the rank. For example, the digits for the White Paper Fan (415) adds to 10, which is representative of money. 438, the rank for the Deputy Lodge Master, Incense Master, and Vanguard, adds to 15, which sounds like a word for “boss” or “elder,” among other synonyms.

**>** Jimmy No

The Triads with greater Matrix assets engage in BTL production and media piracy, but the real bulk of their income comes from identity theft and false identities as they move people and their SINs independently of each other. SIN forging is a highly involved process that requires a lot of friends in high places, and if you want your fakes to hold up under any level of scrutiny, they’ve gotta look like they have a history behind them. A good fake SIN needs a record of activity attached to it, like bill payments, tax records, and stock investments. So here’s where Kechibi comes in. Triad SIN forgers can use it to identify stock market trends, make bulk purchases of stocks that are on the decline for ridiculously low prices, and attach those to fake SINs to craft a more convincing identity.

- Those stocks still get controlled by Triad forgers, and the owners of the fake SINs they’re attached to don’t get to benefit from them.

Triads keep their illegal operations well-disguised and below board, and many of their members keep up appearances as respectable businesspeople by day. Higher-ranking members like lodgemasters or deputy lodgemasters typically manage small businesses such as restaurants or independent shops, but many other Triad members work in respected corporate positions by day. While the people they come face-to-face with on a daily basis often remain totally unaware of their syndicate involvement, these Triad members’ corporate and underworld connections both work together to benefit them in the other area. Triad resources can be used to connect them with hackers, who can find and use the code in their interest, stock prices soar in response to their projects, and paths to promotion open in both worlds.

And stock purchasing and trading is far from Kechibi’s only use. Those working in Triad counterfeiting operations can deploy the Code ahead of the release of in-demand items to catch a glimpse into the future and decide what’s going to be most worth reproducing to gain the greatest return on their efforts.

- It’s not uncommon for Triads to have counterfeit versions of hot items produced and ready for sale before the real ones are released. Imagine how much the data from Kechibi can streamline and accelerate this process, making their operations even more profitable.
