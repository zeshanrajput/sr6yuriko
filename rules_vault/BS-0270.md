## Dangers of Symbionts

Symbionts are ordinarily quite safe. The base form is chosen for the low impact on the host, and none survive if the host dies. I expect it is because they share the host’s biosystemic stability. That said, when the life form is damaged, it tends to react somewhat erratically, usually pulling extra blood and nutrients from the host to repair itself. The process of turning parasitic is gradual and can be easily overlooked for days or weeks before the effects become clear. In this situation, a medical procedure is needed to extract it. Once removed, most providers will apologize and replace it for free with a new, non-parasitic version as a token of good faith. Assuming you still want one of the things in you.
