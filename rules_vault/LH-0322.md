## Notes on using tanks and backpacks

Replacing a fuel tank (or capacitor pack) on a weapon requires a Reload Weapon Major Action, even if the weapon is equipped with a smartlink system. Replacing two tanks requires two Major Actions to replace both.

The tanks in backpack carriers are also changed with the Reload Weapon Major Action, and a carrier can support between two and eight tanks, depending on the weapon. The tanks in the backpack can’t be changed by the user while the carrier is worn. Shouldering a backpack carrier and attaching the fuel hoses (or power cables) to the weapon takes approximately thirty seconds. Hoses and cables attach at the weapon’s normal connection point, so the backpack carrier’s capacity always replaces the normal ammo capacity of the weapon.

Most spare fuel tanks are filled and pressurized when purchased, and disposable after use. Capacitor banks are an exception and need to be charged before use. Vapor gun tanks also need to be filled by the user with whatever liquid is being aerosolized. Loading and pressuring each two-liter vapor gun tank requires an Engineering + Logic (4, 10 minutes) test and an engineering toolkit.

Tanks mounted on a backpack carrier can be targeted with a Called Shot: Break Weapon action. Instead of reducing the weapon’s AR, the attack ruptures the tank and causes the wearer, and anyone else in melee range, to take damage equal to the weapon’s DV. These tanks, however, have built-in armor and a Structure rating of 16, requiring a modified DV of 8 or more to rupture.
