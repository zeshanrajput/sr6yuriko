## Pride

Saved for last, Pride is the ultimate sin, the most dangerous by traditional standards. I’d argue its influence is felt among all the others, and ultimately is the single most important ingredient to any mark: the idea that they couldn’t be fooled. It’s Pride that blinds the mark to seeing the con for

what it is, and Pride that makes every other sin possible. Which means that understanding Pride is understanding the mark.

Where every other sin is recognizing what motivates the target to best manipulate them, Pride is their blind spot, the place they think they’re safe. Everyone has instincts toward self-preservation, ranging from the passive to the paranoid, but we all have varying levels of insecurity. And where there’s a hierarchy, there’s a low-rank qualifier— the one we take for granted. It’s the thing we think we’re good at, and maybe we are. The place where we can’t possibly fuck things up is where we slip, where we slack, and where we ignore. Pride is, above all else, a false sense of security.

As runners, you look for this all the time on a tactical level. But take a moment and recognize that Pride, worse than active ego, can be passive. While ego consumes and can be provoked (leading to a self-defensive version of Hate), real Pride doesn’t come from a place of insecurity. As the opposite of humility, which encompasses self-awareness, Pride is ignorant of the self and rests in a concept of self rather than reality. Understanding someone’s Pride doesn’t just tell you where they won’t see you coming, it’ll also tell you who they are, and who they think they are. Speaking to someone, to who they really are inside, is an intimate and honest affair. Playing to their idealized self-perception legitimizes it in their minds and reinforces their trust in you and in themselves, opening them up to manipulation.

In short, find someone’s Pride, and you’ve found your way in.
