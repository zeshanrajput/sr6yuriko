## **What sets the Matrix attributes for a PAN when I have multiple devices?**

A PAN is run by a Persona. A Persona can run on a single device except in the case that they are running a device with Attack/Sleaze coupled to a device with Data Processing/Firewall. This Persona controls the PAN and any other devices (e.g. commlinks, RCC, cyberdecks) are controlled by non-leader Persona. The lead Persona’s device is where the damage for Matrix attacks ends up landing.

In _SR6_ , a device only has two of the four Matrix attributes. So, there is a special case for when there is a cyberterminals or cyberdeck involved where the lead device consists of a cyberdeck or cyberterminals are paired with a cyberjack, M-TOC, or commlink. You cannot mix and match Matrix attributes from any device on the PAN (counter to what’s stated on p. 174, _SR6_ ). You can only mix and match Matrix attributes for the primary Attack/Sleaze device and the primary Data Processing/Firewall device.

For programs, the only programs that are active are the ones on the primary device. Other programs can be stored on other devices on the PAN, but they aren’t active in defending the PAN. The primary device is effectively the virtual host that an enemy decker is attacking. Using an M-TOC or commlink to connect to the Matrix does prevent you from using Matrix Edge boosts or taking advantage of Initiative Dice bonuses that would come with a cyberjack.

Technomancers, AI, and EI all use a Living Persona which uses a separate set of rules. You cannot mix and match Matrix Attributes between the Living Persona and Matrix devices.
