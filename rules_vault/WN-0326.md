## The Job

In mission 13, **I Did What?** (p. 74), the PCs should have learned about some frightening research to mess with metahuman minds. The previous mission, **Digging for the Truth** (p. 87), pointed the PCs toward the Octagon. Now they need to find out what that means. Any of the Mr. Johnsons from past missions can ask them to look into this, but the PCs might be inspired to look on their own, knowing whatever they find will be valuable to the city’s power brokers.

A simple Matrix Search (1) test shows that New York’s most famous Octagon is a five-story building on Roosevelt Island (the PCs might have found information pointing them to this location). Roosevelt Island sits in the East River across from Midtown and the Upper East Side and is accessible by train, tram, or boat. The Octagon is on the northern end of the island, and the current use of the building is unclear. Which is why the PCs need to explore!

Arriving on the island should not be a problem, though note that the only road connecting the island to any other location is a bridge to Queens— there is no road to Manhattan. Conversely, the tram runs from Manhattan to the island, with no other connections. The subway connects to both Manhattan and Queens.

One primary street, the appropriately named Main Street, travels most of the length of the island. The Octagon is on Main Street, on the north end of the island.

Getting to the Octagon should pose no problem unless the PCs are already wanted by the police, in which case having only Main Street to traverse could add some challenge, as they’ll have fewer ways to avoid them. Given that the island is filled with prisons, it’s also filled with police.

Getting inside the Octagon is a different matter. As described in the Hotspots section, security is high at the Octagon, and the information the



PCs need is not exactly waiting at the front door. Stealth or a good con are called for—unless the PCs feel they can force their way in fast enough and grab what they want. That would require, of course, knowing what they want.

The PCs are looking for confidential patient files. These files are not stored in the Octagon itself. A broad, three-story extension attaches to the west end of the Octagon and connects to a larger annex to the west. On top of that is a narrower passageway that attaches to the fourth, considerably narrower floor of the Octagon. That windowless passage leads across the extension and into the fourth floor of the large building to the west. There is a file room there that is only accessible through that hallway—it is closed off from the rest of the building’s fourth floor. This room holds patient files that are not allowed on any sort of wireless-enabled device.

To get the files, the PCs will have to connect to a wired rating 11 host. The file room has no one in it from 8 p.m. to 7 a.m.; at other times, two admin staffers (use Corporate Wageslave, p. 166, _Sixth World Companion_ ) are in the room. Four guards (use Elite Corporate Security, p. 208, _SR6_ ) are in the hallway outside the room at all times. A Force 6 spirit of air also constantly circles the room. While there is not remote Matrix security for the host the PCs need to break into, a Professional Rating 8 decker (p. 209, _SR6_ ) monitors the building and looks for Matrix activity they don’t recognize.

Once they are in the host, the PCs can get any patient’s file. When they get a chance to look it over, they’ll see some technical language and details about brain sections that they may not understand, but they’ll see that the thrust of the project is to erase real memories and put false ones in their place. They’ll also see a familiar name on the files: Animus Construction.

They should realize that the implications of such a power being in the hands of anyone in the Sixth World are quite frightening. The question is, who is behind Animus Construction? The next job will be to examine the data for hints on who owns the Octagon and who is performing this work.
