## CITADEL

## Posted by: Canis

A few years ago now, I found myself in an odd position over the summer. All of the paperwork and prep for my day job—this whole professor gig—was up to date, and I didn’t have any research projects that needed my direct oversight (top tip: it helps to have awesome TAs). After making a few calls, I found that there wasn’t much biz going on, certainly nothing that anyone would pay my rates to get involved in. So I found myself at a loose end. As anyone who knows me will tell you, me being at a loose end usually ends up in shenanigans and hijinks. Well, I’m finally ready to announce to the shadows what I did.

**>** Finally! **>** Vixen

My first thought was to head off and catch up with some chummers overseas I’d not seen in a while, but with my mates due relatively soon, I

decided against that. So I did the next best thing: I headed out into the Salish lands for a spot of hunting, maybe go on a vision quest or two, and generally try and reconnect with Wolf like I did in my twenties.

So there I sat, drinking soykaf and eating a freshly killed white-tail while thinking about my next project. The academic world is as cutthroat as the shadows have ever been, and if you don’t publish, you very quickly fade into obscurity. I’d also been working with some archivists, helping with translations for a research paper and digitizing the documents we were going through, as that would help them (both were technomancers) once they got the data into a format they could manipulate more easily. Plus, they both were big fans of having the data in the Resonance Realms where anyone with the skill and power to access it could get it.

I remembered Vixen and Paladin talking about the Resonance Realms—how they’re a vast repository of every piece of information that’s ever made its way to a digital format. And that got me wonCITADELdering: Is there something similar for magic? The idea rattled around in my head for a while, and about the time I returned to Seattle, I decided to do some exploring. Because one place kept coming back to me … the Citadel.

Despite my best efforts, far too few of my students know of the Citadel. And to be honest, I’m not surprised, as at the time I remember not having been there in a while myself. That struck me as a particularly good project to delve into—a quantitative study of how Unified Magic Theory affected the field (for the worse, in my opinion). I mentioned it to my mates, and they all thought it was a great idea.

**>** High time he put his money where his mouth is! I’m not even a mage, and I’ve heard his rants hundreds of times. **>** Vixen

I’ve gathered some good data on that project, but I’m still trying to gather more. But one piece of data was the Citadel itself, because despite astral quests at the time rarely even approaching the Citadel, I found I was able to go back there. Took some work, and a couple of stints in a medically induced coma while my wetware repaired itself (turns out, metaplanar exploration can lead to injuries very similar to running up against some mean biofeedback—but that’s another paper for another day too), but I managed to get back to the Citadel.
