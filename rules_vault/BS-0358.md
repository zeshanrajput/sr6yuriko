## OPTIONAL RULE:

## TOLERANCE

The repeated use of addictive substances and activities changes you. The thing that used to make you feel good no longer has the same sort of high that it used to, and you find yourself progressively needing to increase the amount you take or the spend you need to make. Maybe it’s gambling that is your vice and you’ve been finding that the low-stakes tables no longer give you the same thrill they used to, so you move up to the higher-nuyen tables, putting more on the line.

There are many different types of tolerance, such as pharmacodynamic, pharmacokinetic, and behavioral tolerance. But the general idea is that it takes more of a substance to give you the same high. In all three types of tolerance, your body and brain become accustomed to the drug and the chemical reaction, leaving your response to the addictive substance muted.

This tolerance is not permanent. If you give up the substance, your body will change again, and if you are to take the drug after the adjustment period, you will be able to have that high again. This is only made harder by the fact that tolerance and addiction often go hand-in-hand, so while trying to get that high back, you are also suffering from withdrawal.

To use tolerance in your game, players will need to track a new counter based on the number of consecutive weeks they have used a specific drug. Each week they take the drug, the Consumption Count for the drug increases by 1. For instance, if Orbital Burn has been drinking alcohol for the last three weeks in a row, their Tolerance (Alcohol) is currently 3. For each week that they skip partaking in the drug or addictive behavior, that value reduces by 1.

To determine if you have gained a tolerance, compare your Tolerance Value against the Power of the addictive substance or behavior. Your Tolerance is equal to (Consumption Count/Power, rounded down). For you to gain the benefit of a drug or BTL, you need to increase the Power of the substance by your Tolerance. For drugs, this requires you to potentially take two doses, which increases the Power by 1 (see Concentration, p. 122, SR6). For BTLs, it would require you to increase the rating of the BTL you’re taking. For sex, gambling, and other behaviors, the amount of nuyen spent would need to be increased. All these effectively raise the Power and make it easier to be addicted, but beware of overdosing. So, if Orbital Burn continued drinking for thirteen weeks, his Consumption Count (Alcohol) is now 12, which is twice the Power of alcohol, which means he now needs to drink three doses to gain the benefit of the drug.

For simsense, foci, and similar items, while you continue to track a Tolerance for the item, it doesn’t prevent any benefits of the item. Instead, the user needs to make a Composure (Tolerance / Power, rounded down) test to not use it on a relevant test. For instance, ChaOS has been using only hot sim for the last seven weeks, so when he thinks of going in using cold sim, he needs to succeed at a Composure (1) test or else needs to use hot sim.



The runner could also check themselves into a rehabilitation facility or even work with a street doc to help them recover. Both have psychological treatments options as well as access to drugs to aid patients in dealing with withdrawal symptoms. If you stay with a street doc, it costs 100 nuyen per day, but you get to reduce recovery time by ten percent. If you stay at a specialized clinic, it costs 1,000 nuyen per day, and the recovery time is reduced by fifty percent.
