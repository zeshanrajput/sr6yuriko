## **Uncovering Infiltrators, and Their Work**

**At this point, it’s clear that agents operating on behalf of Dis have gained footholds in several organizations. Speculation says that the whole**



**deal with them to disappear III Corps was initiated by Disian agents in corporate ranks. This means they have been infltrating for years, which in turn means no one knows how deep their infltrations have run. The work to discover and remove these infltrators will long and diffcult, but it’s work Horizon could be a very useful part of. A key part of any PR campaign is good information, and Horizon has that in huge quantities. Thanks to the Dawkins Group, they’re also skilled at using information in ways that go far beyond conventional marketing.**

- **This stuff gets incredibly sophisticated (and invasive). The basics of any scheme to use data for marketing is to look at trends. Do a high percentage of people who buy product X buy product Y? Then market product Y to people right after they buy product X. But then you try to anticipate and influence. Can you convince people of a certain demographic to buy product X, then move to product Y? Who are the people you can convince? How much noise do you detect in your patterns? Can you explain it? The outliers become really interesting, especially if they start**

- **influencing others. You can get outlier pockets, where a group of people who should be behaving one way, according to your data, are not. Can you explain the pocket?**

**To do this work, Horizon will need data that goes far beyond purchasing habits. They need travel habits, work hours, entertainment habits, and anything else they can get the Matrix to measure for them. Then they’ll look for patterns and outliers, then try to explain the outliers. It will take a lot of work to figure out which outliers might be Dis-connected, but that’s the work Horizon is willing to put in.**

**> Dr. Spin**

**The primary things Horizon will need in this effort are information and people willing to act on their analysis of the information they obtain. The list of possible information sources is fairly simple to make, as it consists of the world’s top information agencies—Aegis Cognito, the UGB, Saeder-Krupp Prime, Shiawase’s MIFD, various government agencies, etc. The problem Horizon will encounter is that none of these groups want to share. Convincing them to do so will not be easy, but stealing information from them and getting away with it will be harder.**

- **Allow me to speak of the Tiding for a moment. This is a small, shadowy organization—even by our standards—who have surfaced in the recent activities in Barcelona and Manhattan. They’re specialists—they do not have as broad a knowledge base as any of the organizations Cosmo lists, but when they’re given an assignment, they have a strong ability to dive deep into it and penetrate levels they shouldn’t be able to reach. They’re very nimble and don’t seem to be beholden to anyone. They will be involved in this information-gathering effort, but I have no idea what they’ll do with anything they find.**
