## Seoulpa Rings

When the now-defunct Watada-rengo began establishing themselves in Seattle, they did so mostly by sending recent Korean recruits, who were highly successful at expanding Yakuza influence and pushing back Mafia territory. After that, the oyabun, Akira Watada, was somehow _not satisfied_ that his goal had been accomplished. Like any insecure Yak on a power trip, he began to suspect his Korean officers of having more ambition than loyalty.

- Something tells me you really don’t like the Yakuza very much.

- **>** Slamm-0!

Watada began implementing more and more stringent restrictions on Korean officers. The Koreans and their loyalists became more and more defiant, because being punished for a job well done was complete and utter bulldrek, and Watada became more and more paranoid, even going so far as to blame the Crash of ’29 on the Korean Yakuza. Finally, in 2043, Akira Watada called for a purge of all Korean members to make the rengo ethnically pure.

A few of the rengo’s Korean members survived the purge, and angry at the Yakuza and hungry for revenge, they formed the Seoulpa Rings—small,

clandestine operations that depend on secrecy, since they couldn’t overtake the Yakuza by force or depend on someone else for protection. Though the Rings are loosely affiliated with each other, they’re decentralized, which makes it nearly impossible to locate them and wipe them all out as a whole. The Rings also skew pretty progressive as a general rule, recruiting capable individuals regardless of race or metatype (which I suspect stems from their roots as second-class citizens in the Yakuza). This varies by ring, as a ring’s leader may hold certain prejudices and refuse to recruit from certain groups.

Seattle’s Choson Ring deals heavily in cybercrime, operating on an almost entirely remote foundation of email dead drops and anonymous datahavens that are nearly impossible to find unless you know where to look for them. They have been one of the main Seoulpa Rings looking at ways to use Kechibi against the Yakuza near Fort Lewis. The stranglehold over some of the meatspace vice business has pushed the Choson Ring to rely on gaining a Matrix advantage.

- So, some small cells of unorganized organized crime chumps want to go after a highly organized and violent crime organization? That’s not going to end well in Seattle.

The Seattle Choson Ring has one of the most creative uses of the Kechibi Code I have seen. They have been targeting the software used by bunraku parlors near Fort Lewis. From what I hear, those businesses had a severe drop in customers when the puppets started manipulating their clients’ emotional states in a very nuanced way. Local brass had to get involved when their soldiers started having mental breakdowns after visiting some of these establishments. Other customers are looking at therapy bills a lot higher than what they spent for a good time. Serves them right for hitting one of those places.

Beyond that, the Choson are looking to use Kechibi to completely disrupt online gambling for the Yakuza, messing up one of their major revenue streams. Everything from Matrix scams to info-brokering is on the table for the rings to go after. Once they hurt the Yaks, they move on to selling information about how others can do the same, which turns into a win for the ring and the independents they deal with. They’re being subtle about it, making it seem like a software glitch. And who’s to say if they have the Kechibi Code or if they’re just using their talented hackers to get the job done?
