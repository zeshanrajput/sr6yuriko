## **Intangibles**

**It should come as no surprise that the authority derived from the badge constitutes its own special kind of perks. In the case of civilians, this might**

**range from a cowering deference to free stuff (food is a favorite). Motivations range from genuine gratitude for the folks in blue to currying favor in the hopes they’ll get the best service possible.**

**For the more proactive, it’s entirely possible to utilize one’s status and authority as a threat, whether implicit or explicit. The fact is, a civilian has less credibility than most offcers, and when the cops are robbing you, there’s not a lot of places to turn to. At its most minor, a free lunch or lower rent with the threat of a health inspection or asking if licenses are current if they don’t comply tends to be successful (I wouldn’t eat what comes out of that kind of kitchen, personally). Taken to extremes, it’s easy to set up sideline protection rackets, effectively only doing your job if the neighborhood pays in. Of course, this can lead to problems if they’re already paying for the Yakuza, the mob, or any number of** _**other**_ **criminal outfts.**

**Those same outfts can provide another steady stream of secondary, off-the-books income. After all, what syndicate or even gang doesn’t like having someone on the inside who likes bribes and doesn’t mind being in the wrong place at the right time for money? With the kind of access security agents have, crime can stay one step ahead of punishment, and the cop is all the richer for it. This comes with its own brand of risk, as any crook worth their street cred keeps a nice little dossier on all their interactions with crooked badges, which makes for excellent leverage. “If I go down, you’re coming with” can motivate a lot of dirty cops into erasing evidence and silencing witnesses.**

**Perhaps a little more esoteric but infnitely valuable is the training. Corpsec and municipal enforcement alike are educated in more than rules and regs. Firearms use, investigation, high-speed pursuit, tactical procedure, and a plethora of other skills make for attractive additions to their jacket, whether seeking a lateral career move with another employer or going freelance, criminal or not. Considering how valuable corpsec fnd exshadowrunners for handling security or even just consultation, it’s more than reasonable to think any former offcer is going to be just as valued for their insights.**

- **And that’s before you take Matrix, magic, or HTR training into account. Mages may be able to write their own ticket when it comes to finding jobs, but coming with training just means adding zeroes. Matrix coders can jump from cubicle to office the same way in the open market.**

- **Kia**
