## The Haunted Mansion

This adventure works best with the charlatan background, but any team skilled in deception and misdirection with access to spirits and illusion magic should have fun with it.

The charlatan is contacted by a fixer who is familiar with their reputation. They would like to hire the runner team for a very unusual job. A megacorporation is in the process of attempting to buy a property that Mr. Johnson would prefer they not acquire. The deal is nearly complete, however, and only a final assessment by the megacorporation must be done before the deal is concluded. This assessment will occur in two days, and the runners are tasked with the job of scaring off the megacorporate assessor by convincing them there is a dangerous haunting at the site. The team is welcome to use whatever means are available to them to pull this off, but the job will be complicated by the assessor’s security entourage, which consists of several heavily armed grunts and an Awakened lieutenant.

Pulling off a magical con under the watchful eye of a security mage will not be easy. If they can pull it off, however, the fixer is impressed. As for that, the gamemaster must ultimately gauge the responses of the assessor and security team appropriately. The assessor and security mage are both pragmatic and difficult to trick, so they gain a point of Edge on any Composure, Judge Intentions, or Perception tests against the runners. The guards are fairly superstitious and gullible people, and so in this scenario, they cannot gain or spend Edge on Composure, Judge Intentions, or Perception tests. The runners must be careful to avoid direct contact, though releasing a pack of captured paracritters is a valid alternative tactic, albeit a very risky one. If successful, the team gains a fixer contact of the Criminal or Magic type, with a Connection 5 and Loyalty 4. If the gamemaster used a fixer who is already a contactfor the team, raise the contact’s Connection and Loyalty ratings each by 1.
