## Prosthetic Cyberlimbs

If a critter loses a limb—or is born without one—and their owner wishes to outfit them with a cybernetic prosthetic replacement, a variety of options are available. A crude prosthetic cyberlimb is the cheapest option, which encourages people to typically choose it for their pets. These models are simple motorized prosthetics fitted with the most rudimentary synthetic nerves, and no expense has been added to make them appear to be natural. Crude cyberlimbs restore a critter’s ability to walk, but they are clumsy devices—better than nothing, though.

Generic prosthetic cyberlimbs are designed for a specific species of critter and can be fairly sophisticated. The cyberlimb is designed to function and appear as close to its natural equivalent as possible.

Custom cyberlimbs are rarely employed except by very wealthy individuals who have a strong attachment to a favorite pet, though inexpensive and less-effective options have become available as well. Critters that are used for security or labor are generally replaced entirely rather than repaired if they are too badly maimed to continue to perform their employed function.

**Game Information:** Crude prosthetic cyberlimbs are not much more complex than a peg leg. They restore basic mobility, but the critter permanently gains the Hobbled condition, which reduces all movement speeds by half.

Generic prosthetic cyberlimbs are a little better, restoring basic mobility without the Hobbled condition, but the base threshold for all Athletics tests (including the Sprint action) is increased by 2—this means that on a Sprint action, only the number of hits that exceed a threshold of 2 count towards increasing their movement speed.

Custom prosthetic cyberlimbs are designed for an individual critter and mimic the natural limbs that they replace. They function and appear as if they are natural limbs and have no disadvantages other than the nuyen and Essence cost.

No additional bonuses or capabilities are provided by prosthetic cyberlimbs. Unlike standard cyberlimbs, they do not provide additional boxes to a critter’s condition monitor and cannot accept any cyberlimb modifications. Prosthetic wings are only

available as custom prosthetic limbs, and they restore full flight function—but only for a critter that would be naturally capable of flight. Sticking prosthetic wings on your dog doesn’t allow them to fly.
