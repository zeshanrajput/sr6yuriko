## **See No Evil**

**Not getting spotted by eyeballs and cameras is the most basic aspect of stealth. If someone hears you, or if a motion sensor picks up your movement, that doesn’t necessarily mean that your cover is blown—if you react quickly enough and hide before security can verify the contact, you might be okay. But if you get spotted, you’re almost defnitely hosed. Avoiding visual detection should be your highest priority.**

**The frst and best way to avoid getting spotted is to stay out of line of sight from guards and sensors. Opaque solid objects are sometimes all the cover you need. When it comes to getting the job done for a shadowrun, we aren’t going to have the luxury of sticking to locations that will always remain unobserved, but if you can get a good idea where the cameras are pointed and where the guards are stationed, you might be able to work out where their blind spots are and take advantage of them. Mostly these are temporary blind spots, such as when a camera pans from one side to another, or a guard patrol moves to another area. If you can get directly under a wall-mounted camera and lay fat against said wall, you might be able to avoid its gaze. Time it correctly, and you can advance from one direction while it’s looking the other way and get beneath its visual radius before it swings back your way. This doesn’t always work, but sometimes you can pull it off. If cameras are hardwired to a building’s security system and you can get close enough to attach a data tap, it’s a great way to get access to not just that camera but the whole network. At the very least, having a hacker available to edit your team out of the camera feeds makes it a lot easier to sneak into a place.**

Once I managed to score some, I never go on a run without it. This drek makes you nearly invisible, without the drawbacks of having an active spell on you.**

**Another aspect of camoufage is uniforms or attire that help you look like you belong there. With electrochromic clothing or a Fashion spell, you don’t even need to acquire stolen uniforms—you just need to get some good images for the hacker or your mage to use. Just make sure to get images from several angles so you don’t miss any key details. A uniform on its own won’t be enough to let you sneak around without arousing suspicion, but combined with the right demeanor and some Matrix support, it can take you far.**
