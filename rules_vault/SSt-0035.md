## **The Hidden Workshop**

**Here’s an example from my experience. I’ll do my best to share the relevant details without giving away too many specifcs. A few years back, my team was hired to break into a corp facility and acquire a prototype from an R&D workshop. Mr. Johnson didn’t provide the exact location of the target, probably expecting us to fnd it as part of our legwork. The frst thing I did was look up their Matrix host, which was online but not publicly advertised. That took me a while, but I found it. It helps to have an Agent program running as well as Browse, but it still took me an hour or so to track it down. From outside the host, I took my time to analyze what I could, just to see what kind of frewall I’d be going up against. Before I hacked my way in, I made sure that my persona was running silent (stealth mode). Once inside, I ran a few searches: 1) Were there any security spiders (deckers) keeping tabs on the host? (There was one.) 2) Was there a current foor plan of the inside of the building? Yep. It was encrypted, so I decided to save downloading that for last. 3) Where were fles with security guard rosters? Those were less obvious, but some digging into the payroll fles and extrapolating from those details would tell me what I needed to know. 4) What devices were slaved to the host, such as cameras, locks, elevators, and such? I decrypted the foor plan, scanned it for a data bomb (there was none), and downloaded the fle. The spider and IC were taking their time tracking me down, so I hacked the system logs to erase the record of the unauthorized decryption action—that used to always set off alarms back in the day, but it’s a little easier with more recent tech.**

**I logged off with my ill-gotten gains and shared the fles with my teammates. We spent a couple of hours studying the data, and we were able to ascertain the exact number of security guards on hire. That didn’t tell us how many were on duty at any given time, but it helped. I also memorized the foor plans as best I could, then made another run on the host the next day. This time there were a couple security spiders on duty, so either they were feeling a bit paranoid over yesterday’s intrusion, or they were more lightly staffed before. Null persp—I just made sure to prioritize my Sleaze settings and got it to work. I wasted no time in accessing the cameras and other sensor feeds one at a time, which allowed me to confrm the foor plan and make some notes about who was where and what was what. It let me fgure out how many guards were on duty, and I was able to pinpoint the security offce (which was unmarked on the foor plan). I was also able to make a pretty good guess on where the R&D workshop was, though I couldn’t completely confrm this because there were no cameras in that area that were slaved to**

**the host. Even with my wizard deck and skills, it would only be a matter of time before the spiders or IC would catch me, but I risked a fairly long visit so I could properly evaluate patrol patterns and other security systems. Good thing I did, because I was able to spot a number of drones on patrol that were not visible from the host—they were slaved to a security rigger’s RCC.**

**While I was busy taking a look from the Matrix, the adept on our team snuck near the facility and made some analog observations. They were able to study the two-person guard patrols outside the building, mark the position of the fence, pick out the external camera sightlines, and note the existence of a pair of airborne surveillance drones.**

**Our mage took a peek from the astral, projecting from the safety of their warded lodge back in their doss. Most of the place was pretty dull on that end, with a single spirit patrolling the grounds and interior, and only a single ward further in the building. When we compared notes, that ward lined up with the area I had pegged for the R&D lab. They were also able to sneak around for quite a while before the spirit fnally noticed them. That gave them the opportunity to assense the guards. One of them was Awakened but not astrally active. The rest had auras with lots of dull grey sections (their words, not mine), which told us the mundane security personnel were fairly heavily cybered.**

**With the updated foor plan marked with locations and sight-lines of cameras and sensors, the locations of the guard station and patrol routes, details about the drones in play, and awareness of their external surveillance drones, we were fairly certain we knew who and what was watching the place. That gave us enough intel to make our plan. When we went in, I started by hacking the drones to make sure we wouldn’t be reported from the air. I had my agent maintain that hack while I subverted the cameras as we went, and our mage kept an eye out for the spirit on patrol. It wasn’t easy, and it didn’t go completely smoothly, but we got in and out with our target widget without too much of a mess. With so much security in place, it was unlikely we’d avoid detection for the duration of the run. What gave us away was the late-night worker in the workshop. Once we got in there, they got a few shots off and didn’t do us the courtesy of using a silenced weapon. We were ready, though. Our mage sicced their own spirit on the enemy spirit, I shut down the drone network and gave the rigger a very bad headache, we grabbed the prototype, and only had to fght a fraction of the guards on the way out. I would have preferred a clean full-stealth op, but it very rarely seems to go that way. And it’s always nice to let your combat adept let off some steam every once in a while.**
