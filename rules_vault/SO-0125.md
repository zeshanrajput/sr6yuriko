## Elements

First, you need a large pool of marks, ideally one divisible by the power of two. You’ll be splitting it in half at every stage. It’s not hard to get high numbers, since you’ll be hitting them via the Matrix and not by mail or in person.

- By sheer numbers, I’d avoid going so high as, say, 1,073,741,824, since that’s a lot more chances to be caught out as spam or a scam (and this is both). I’d probably settle at 65,536.

- The Smiling Bandit

- As tempting as it might be to just hit the billions, the balancing act is that you’re going to be spotted by filters anyway. So a lot of marks won’t get it in the first place, but you need enough of them that you can be “right” enough times to matter. This is a numbers game in more ways than one. The thing is, if your send-outs are a low enough spread, it’s possible that the filters won’t pick it up and wipe out the whole batch. Find the sweet spot where you have enough rounds to establish credibility without losing them all in the process.

- **>** Copperfield

- And of course, a lot of them are breakage. Plenty of marks aren’t going to bite, no matter how often you’re right.

- **>** Borderline

Next, pick your gamble. It could be stocks or the racetrack or major sporting events. Picking a consistent flow of bets or investments, such as a particular company or sports team, can result in a more stable pool of marks to choose from, though the psychological tribalism people tend to form around such entities can lose their interest when you bet against them.

- Worse for you, entities like that keep a closer eye on branded bets. Beware the guardians of properties, official or otherwise.

- **>** Copperfield

From here, it’s a binary game. Split your marks down the middle and advertise your certainty that things are going to play one way to one half, the other way to the other. For example, the Seahawks will win against the Rippers on one side, the Hawks will lose for the other side. See how the game goes, then eliminate the losing group of recipients from your mailing pool. Split the one in half and give them another binary win-loss gamTRICKSble. Do this again and again, every step offering them insight into your next big win.

- The Seahawks and the Rippers don’t even play the same game!

- **>** Slamm-0!

It’s also possible to do this without a binary, for example a horse race or other circumstance with multiple potential outcomes. This one will reduce your pool more significantly, but the benefit is that making the right pick this way over and over will build credit far more quickly by virtue of being more impressive.

- This isn’t bad to build a little following for a fake SIN or other cover as a bookie, broker, or lucky/gifted speculator. Obviously it can’t work forever, but if you cut and run with nothing but winners singing your praises, it’s not the worst way to build a temporary resume.

- Saturday Jones
