## Colonel Carmen Lopez y Cabrera (Picador)

## Posted by: Colonel Cobra

If one looks up the term “quiet professional” in the dictionary, they’ll see Colonel Cabrera and each one of the Seventy-Seventh Independent Rangers (El Cuadrilla) as an example. While other mercenary units are about fortune and glory (mostly fortune), Cabrera is more concerned with getting the job done effectively and fulfilling the contract.

This stems from her early days in the Seventy-Seventh, when she learned the craft from her first CO, Colonel Juan “Matador” Pererya. Through him she learned how to be not only an effective warrior and leader, but an ethical one as well. Once Cabrera takes a contract, she sees it through.

- Which made for a sticky situation during the Battle of Detroit, as El Cuadrilla was contracted by Ares to supplement corporate forces. This put her at odds with … well, everyone else in Motor City. But to her credit, her unit followed the international rules of warfare, limited collateral damage, and treated prisoners properly. Which slotted Ares off. But in the end, she found a way to legally break the contract and side with the good guys.

- Johnny Redline

It was this sense of professionalism, combined with a proven track record in multiple conflicts and engagements, that allowed El Cuadrilla to gain contract after contract, often beating out more prestigious units such as Tsunami and Combat Inc. Having been in command since 2062, Cabrera has constantly expanded the Seventy-Seventh’s strategic and tactical capabilities by upgrading their weapons and material, as well as continuous, rigorous training.

sister unit, the Sixty-First IR, and joined Grey Cell to fight against the Disian threat. At their peak, El Cuadrilla fielded an estimated 9,000+ troops, along with full support units. The Seventy-Seventh was the keystone of Grey Cell’s fighting forces for almost a year until additional units signed on. As a result, El Cuadrilla saw some of the heaviest fighting against the Disians and suffered the most casualties. Despite this, they never gave up, and she was given command of _Operation: Rampart_ , the assault on Site Triple-Zero.

During the final phases of that operation, Colonel Cabrera prevented a Disian war form from regaining control of the site’s metaplanar portal. In single combat, she was able to defeat the Disian, but she still suffered significant internal trauma. Despite this, she took up an LMG and laid down suppressive fire until timely reinforcements arrived. And even then she defied medics and prioritized the treatment of her subordinates. Unable to make it to the field hospital, she eventually succumbed to her wounds.

- We had our own … issues. But I have always respected her. I now find myself wishing we could have truly settled our differences.

- **>** Thorn

- There’s no doubt, Picador … Carmen was one of the best. And not just a professional soldier. She was also one of the most humane people I’ve ever known. She took Matador’s code to heart and made it her own. Even though at times it cost her and the Seventy-Seventh nuyen and even prestige, she held fast to her ethics and sense of loyalty. Because of this, she saved more lives than she took and fiercely protected her people. And even though she fought in many grey areas, she was a beacon of light. But most of all, she was my friend. Descansa en paz mi amiga.

- **>** Rifleman
