## Getting to Miggon

The simplest way to get to Miggon is through the Plane of Kin. All you need to do is head to the region of Id and scan the horizon—you’ll perceive the skeletal ruins of a city in the distance. There are many ruins, mines, temples, and fortresses within Id, and you will want to avoid all of those. Do not approach or enter any structures along your way, or your journey may end before you arrive in Miggon. Be sure that the ruins you approach resemble a more modern urban wasteland, and you’ll be on the right track. The city is more ancient than it appears to be, as its existence predates the Fifth World. It is called Hubris, and you must avoid as many interactions with the local population as possible. Travel through Hubris with care. Watch for signs of falling ash, feel the rising heat, and follow your nose to the scent of combustion. If you are wise and clever, you will have brought a radiation sensor—just follow the clicks as they increase. The metaplanar rifts that connect to thehyperplane of Miggon blend in so well with the surrounding wasteland that you may not notice your transition. When you see humanoid shadows on walls that have no obvious source, you will have arrived. Whatever you do, do not touch the shadows.

The city of Hubris is not fully within the plane of Miggon, and neither is it completely within the plane of Id. Whether that makes it two separate cities or one that straddles two separate hyper-metaplanes is a question for philosophers and aetherologists. Some speculate that the plane of Miggon has grown like a tumor upon the plane of Id as an extension of metahumanity’s newest atrocities against nature and itself—nuclear devastation. Some believe that it was created by the United States’ nuclear attacks in the mid-twentieth century, and that it attached itself to the Kindred Plane shortly thereafter like a radioactive barnacle. I believe there is some evidence to suggest that Miggon has existed since before the Fourth World, but the nature of Hubris (on the Miggon side) is rather damning. The ruined buildings are reminiscent of early twentieth century Japan, and all of the signs are in Japanese. Throughout all of metahumanity’s history, there have been no acts of mass slaughter and destruction as large and sudden as the bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. The Ten Minute War of 2004, 2030’s Kashmir War, the nuclear meltdown of Cattenom-GAU in France, Los Angeles’ Green Tide in 2045, and other incidents have expanded Miggon, adding remnants of each event like disturbing pieces of a doomsday puzzle. It is plain to any observer of this place that metahumanity’s atrocities have effects that ripple into the metaplanes. Most citizens of the UCAS have long forgotten the toll of wartime atrocities, but Miggon remembers. Miggon nurtures the memory of many simultaneous murders, which creates a toxic conduit that connects the Plane of Kin to the Plane of Fire—a corrupted aspect of fire that has been tainted by the hubris and murderous nature of metahumanity as they sought to establish dominance over atomic forces and use them as a weapon of war. Miggon is the toxic plane of radiation, the fear of nuclear annihilation, and a testament to the literal fallout from the twentieth century.

Miggon borders the region of Id on one side and the hyper-metaplanes of Carcean and Kadath on the others, and you may laugh at me when I say this, but these are evil places. They are born of evil, and they nurture evil, causing it to fester and grow so that it may be amplified. Laugh if you can, but if you ever travel beyond the borders of Id, you will find little motivation for levity and humor ever again.
