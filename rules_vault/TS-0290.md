**This position has a variety of titles, such as community outreach, incident management, loss prevention, or risk reduction. They are in place because someone in accounting or management realized it’s cheaper to do these things than it is to pay people to stop crime. A crime prevention specialist serves as a bridge between the law enforcement service provider and the communities it serves. Their role is to foster positive relationships, trust, and collaboration by actively engaging with residents and organizations. By organizing and participating in various outreach programs and events, these individuals work to address community concerns and educate the public about their law enforcement provider’s practices to promote a sense of safety and cooperation. Through a genuine commitment to understanding community needs, a crime prevention specialist helps build a stronger, more resilient bond between law enforcement and the individuals they are supposed to protect and serve.**

**A crime prevention contact typically knows everyone and sometimes everything going on in the area they serve. Though it may not be high-level information, it can provide a clear picture of events thanks to the breadth of data covering a local area.**
