## Obstacles

Paranoia is a double-edged sword—drive the client too far, and they’ll be more alert to what’s happening around them. Consider that potentially includes you. It’s not a stretch to make a connection between this fresh variable and suspicious events taking place in their life with you. This also opens you up for a counter-op or trap.

I mentioned before that larger corporate outfits and better trained private concerns are harder to work with because of their professionalism. They know how to spot Paranoia Protection. Corps occasionally pull these ops purely to frag with each other’s credibility. It’s somewhat less common among syndicates, but still occurs. They know how to handle it and keep the client out of the loop, or otherwise handle the client and keep them so in the loop it enhances trust. In the case of a major corp, they may even offer to rotate a fresh team in for the client’s peace of mind, and you can be sure the new team gets a thorough briefing on everything concerning the client, the layout, and you.

You also have to be ready for a security element to react to an approach with enhanced vigilance

or even immediate response. You start bandying vague threats or passing an unmarked credstick with thanks, they pull their sidearm and escalate. This can blow the op entirely.

Your sweet spot is the small-time outfit of freelancers, as smaller criminal operators or private citizens might use. Consider the arms merchant, the lesser noble, or the kinds of people who might hire a runner team like you to work for them. You are, in effect, looking for a brand of security equal to a runner team.
