## Bus

There are forty-five distinct bus routes covering the nearly 60 square kilometers of city. As a holdover from when NYC was more than just Manhattan, each bus route is labeled with an _M_ followed by a corresponding number. Over the years, the routes have been renumbered to reflect agreement with and movement through various corporate territories. As a rule, there are both express and local bus lines for every north–south avenue. Lines run east–west along major streets such as 14[th] , 34[th] , 42[nd] , and so on. Additionally, each corporate-run neighborhood has its own bus line circling or bisecting that neighborhood. Bus travel is the cheapest per-ticket way to move across the city, and buses are usually crammed to capacity during working hours.
