“Most of you were not.” He nodded again, this time toward the suit-clad end of the room. The _proper_ suit-clad ones. The ones with cars as nice as the BMW he had arrived in. The ones who received management allowances for gene-and-bio-work to be done so that they were universally Hollywood-flawless. The ones who had been granted stock options as part of advancement packages. The ones who took expensive vacations to expensive clinics and came back looking five years younger, fitter, and trimmer. One of them had only just recently been on such a trip, in fact. 

They were the ones he was here to chastise. The rest were only witnesses. 

“Mr. Schneider, step forward.” The executive who had tried to volunteer his knowledge earlier swallowed, but obeyed. 

“Mr. Schneider, my associate, Abbas, has informed me that there has been a breakthrough in investigating these recent incursions. Was that, pray, what you were about to inform me of, earlier?” 

“Nn-no, sir.” Schneider had a bead of sweat rolling down his temple. He had likely never (to his knowledge) been this close to a Prime agent before. “I was ju—“ Another lifted finger. Another ringing silence. 

“My associate, Abbas, remotely overrode and updated your facility’s Matrix protocols after the first incursion.” The man in the golden glasses didn’t flick a glance toward the Facility Director, but he didn’t have to. He could _taste_ her surprise, even as she tried not to hide it. She had not been asked for permission. She had not been informed even after the fact—until now. 

“And while the second incursion ran into substantially more physical security than they expected, and the initiative of those involved is admirable”—here he gave the _slightest_ inclination of his head toward the decorated security troopers flanking the door, a sign of respect both of them would carry to their graves—“the intruders did _not_ run into more substantial Matrix security. Or, rather, they did not _think_ they did. Abbas?” 

The Prime decker chewed gum and wore their tie looser than the Prime agent had ever worn a tie in his life. 

“Yeah, so, here’s the deal. I installed a whole-ass second layer of security protocols over the first, while your in-house guy just repaired the damage and did the standard up-tick in IC quality and aggression—which these chucklefrags seemed to expect. Long story short, they thought we patched things up by the book, so they got sloppy with cleaning up after themselves the second time.” 

“Which means?” The dangerous one in the too-sharp suit flashed his teeth again, a predator’s smile. 

“Which means we figured out how they were getting in,” Abbas, the Matrix warrior, flashed a smile, too. His was cocky and brittle by comparison—less an apex predator, more a smug competitor. 

“And how was that, Abbas?” 

“They bypassed every doorway thanks to the iris pattern of the building’s assistant director. Schneider, comma, Thomas Jay.” 

Abbas said it cheerfully, as though they weren’t delivering a death sentence. 

“What? N-no!” Mr. Schneider’s eyes went almost comically wide and his heart rate spiked. “I didn’t! I wouldn’t! There must be some mistake!” 

“Silence. There is no mistake.” The senior Prime agent took two steps towards Schneider, and the whole room took in a breath and tensed. He loomed half-a-head taller than the executive. Schneider’s propensity for Micronetics gene-treatments, his leònization treatments, his bio-genetically enhanced musculature and athletic regime—none made him even close to the agent’s equal. The golden frames of the agent’s smartglasses twinkled, the golden lenses seemed to shine with an impossible light. 

The Prime agent took in a deep breath. Schneider’s bladder began to empty..
