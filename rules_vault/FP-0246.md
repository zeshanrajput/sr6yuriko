## **Tactics**

**==> picture [233 x 4] intentionally omitted \<==**

**You would be forgiven to think that all Warpath does is blow drek up, ’cause let’s be honest, that’s not entirely wrong. But there’s more than that.**

**First off, you have the Leif-Killers, who are straight-up into murder, and they don’t particularly care about collateral damage. Well, it’s not so much that they actively seek to cause collateral damage like the Old Guard does, but they won’t try to mitigate it. So, for those who’ve dedicated their life to hunting down Winternight, and now Ex Pacis, it’s pretty much a balance of old-fashioned sleuthing to locate them. This is where the Coderunners do help them a lot, both in planning and in executing those plans.**

**Since they want to send a clear message, it’s rarely a clean bullet in the head from a sniper gun, although that does happen sometimes. No, we’re usually talking about drive-by shootings, ambush assaults, car bombings, or just blowing their hideouts to kingdom come while they’re inside. Since most of them tend to be initiates, a fair number of their attacks are magic- and ritual-based.**

- **A few months ago, they killed a former member of Winternight, a woman who, ridden by guilt, had actually become a nun. A group of runners were hired to track her down and deliver the address to their client. They were quite pissed when Warpath blew up the entire monastery a few days after that!**

- **> Fianchetto**

**A lot of what I said about the Leif-Killers also applies to the Old Guard faction. The biggest difference is that the Leif-Killers are more discriminatory in their killings. Primarily active in Winnipeg, western CAS, Florida, and the Tsimshian Protectorate, the Old Guard members are also into downright old-fashioned terror attacks. They favor busy areas like parks, shopping centers, casinos, and government or corporate buildings. They like to make a statement, usually blowing their target up (whether magically or with old-fashioned**

**> Cosmo**

**Another of their tactics is sabotage, especially against corporate or government-run utilities (such as energy, water, sewers, waste treatment, etc.), although bridges, ferries, and rails have been targeted in the past. In this case, it’s less a matter of direct casualties and more a matter of disrupting life and causing as much property damage as possible. Big fashy attacks, magical or otherwise, are still favored over “softer” types of sabotage, mainly because of the extended disruption they cause.**

**In both cases, their approach is messy and brings a lot of heat on all of us, but they have become quite adept at stealth and detection countermeasures. When it gets really bad, they’ll jump the border into the NAN, Québec, or the League and wait it out. They are also the ones most likely to be found on UCAS/CAS law enforcements’ Most Wanted lists.**

**Last but not least, the Great Ghost Dance Redux. This is a plan that is close to the Old Guard’s heart. Since the early years, they have been slowly and patiently researching the 2017 Dance, but a lot of information about it was lost after the Crash and the process had to be restarted in the early ’70s … with fewer records to study and fewer people to interview. They will pay dearly for this type of information, and this is one of the only instances in which they will willingly interact with or hire non-indigenous people.**

**To further hinder that particular plan, they are faced with two additional hurdles. First, this plan is not popular within the NAN. The vast majority of tribal councils, the Ute and the Tsimshian being the main exceptions, are not keen on it and will even actively oppose it. The last thing they want is to risk losing what they have gained or, worse, to piss off the great dragons living on the continent. The second is gathering enough people, shamans and mundane alike, to the cause. Many lives were lost back in ’17, and those who survived were never quite the same afterward. Finding a large number of shamans and mundanes willing to sacrifce**

**their lives in a massive Blood Ritual has been a slow process. Sadly though, they are slowly, very slowly, getting there, despite these hindrances.**

- **I’ve heard vague rumors that some of them also had an interest in the Yellowstone Anomaly recently. Maybe as an alternative to the Ghost Dance or maybe to try to forge an alliance with the Seelie or, more likely, the Unseelie Court, who knows? All I know for sure is that they’ve been causing some waves in Oceti Sakowin and Aztlan.**

- **Frosty**

- **I suspect the FBI have embedded several undercover agents in Warpath to accelerate this. The plan is to let them gather and prepare the ritual so that the UCAS can wipe out most of the ones crazy enough to attempt it in one air strike.**

- **Sim-Eon**

**On the fip side, we have the New Guard . Make no mistake, they’re no choirboys and they’re as bad as (maybe even worse, than) the Old Guard, even if their actions are more low-key. As indicated earlier, they believe that the old way, described above, is not the best approach against the Anglos and that maybe, just maybe, the pre-twentieth-century invaders had the right approach: buying the land where possible and committing biological warfare where money doesn’t work.**

**They are mostly active in Seattle, Atlanta, and Boston, as well as cities hit hardest by the blackouts and dealing with the corporate “Resource Rush” up north. In all fairness, it has proven somewhat effective so far. The Old Guard is far from being pleased by it, however, and there is quite a bit of tension between these two factions. So far, we have been able to avoid a schism, but there have been a few close calls.**

**Their main strategy is buying the land back from under the Anglos’ nose. Land isn’t cheap, and money doesn’t grow on trees. So, their frst order of the day is gathering funds. The easiest sources of fnancing are the pockets of the members, their sympathizers, and tribal associates. Most are aware of where the money is used, and those associations also act as fronts, or as middlemen, and the land is often bought under their names.**

- **Which is to say, if you are aware of a few runs that happen to cheapen the price of land, whether directly or indirectly, be aware that it might be due to some ops from Warpath’s New Guard.**

- **Sim-Eon**

**More diffcult (but potentially much larger) sources are fnancial institutions, government organizations, and corporate accounts. Whether from embezzlement, blackmail, fraud, or market manipulation, this requires a lot of work and know-how, mainly supplied by the Coderunners.**

- **Mr. Bonds**

**Having the money is great, but we’re not swimming in it—so why pay full price for the land when you can have it cheaper? And that’s the second part of their plan: They strive to lower the value of the land and maybe “encourage” people to sell. This is achieved in multiple ways: provoking gang wars, crimes against property (arson, vandalism, break-ins, robberies), crimes against individuals (mugging, assault), sabotaging infrastructure (water and sewers, electricity, Matrix), misappropriation of municipal funds, and more. It’s a tricky game, as we want to get it before anyone else, especially the corps, can get their hands on it, and we want the problem to go away once the land is in our hands. This is another case where we hire out runners and use gangers to take the attention away from us.**

**Regarding biological warfare, obviously pox blankets are not an option, and we are as susceptible as the Anglos to any modern bio-weapons, so we needed to tread carefully with this one. While not our main go-to, we have plans and stockpiles for anthrax, nerve gas, and a few other nasty surprises that have occasionally been used on a small scale against government buildings.**

**Up in the Matrix, the Coderunners are proving to be very supportive in their ability to gather information and manipulate documents and funds. But that’s not all they do. They make our manifest accessible, vet potential recruits, and make initial contact online. Not all of our recruits are contacted this way, but a fair number are, and it is a useful way of weeding out law-enforcement offcers.**

**They are also great at server sabotage. They mostly target governmental institutions and American corporations, but they will regularly attack and shut down any servers in their way, disrupting services and causing fnancial losses. This is often done in parallel with other actions to cause confusion and hinder any effective response.**
