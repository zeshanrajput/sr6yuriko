## Backgrounds

The idea of elven assassins goes back at least thirty years, with the legacy of such luminaries in

the field as Blackwing, Nightprince, and Ire written large in bloody letters wherever shadow operatives share tales of kick artists. Patience, grace, and keen perception are all assets to the would-be murderer for hire, and the politics and tactics of the Tír governments lend themselves to building the idea that no one makes the kill like an elf. This reputation isn’t lost on the population at large, either; both _Suki Redflower_ or _Hannibal: Hitter for Hire_ have dipped into elven hitman archetypes as rivals, antagonists, and even antihero forbidden romances, turning the concept into a trope, one which ironically works in real assassins’ favor. After all, if it’s on the trid, how true could it be? Just a harmful stereotype, surely. Utter nonsense …

No contractor simply steps into the role. Hired hits are as simple as a credstick to the right bunch of beetle-heads with two-by-fours or the local gang if you just want someone zeroed. A real sanction calls for an artist, and almost no one is simply born with the skills, let alone the moral flexibility necessary to make a career of it. Those who are,

Professionals are a blend of nature and nurture, often from a troubled childhood (much like any professional criminal), where the idea of solving a problem with decisive finality appeals and the concept of death is less a matter of ethics and more about expedience and precision. The vast majority are former military or intelligence spooks who are trained extensively in covert ops and likely performing liquidations as missions before entering the market as independent contractors. Not only does such a background prepare them with the skills and tactics necessary to complete the job, but their travel and experience mean they have a ready résumé, an established reputation, and an existing network of contacts to begin their new careers as quickly as they like.

The process of becoming infected is far more variable. Some hitmen like the idea of the additional advantages. None would be so naive to think that their work is carried out purely in shadows and night, no matter what the stereotypes say. A shocking number of hits take place in literal broad daylight, and the obstacles such a transformation presents are often seen as a turnoff. Then again, being able to rapidly recover from wounds means that more brazen tactics become viable. Mist form makes infiltration and quick exits extraordinarily simple in many circumstances. The very strong possibility of gaining magical talents is an incredible incentive, especially for those assassins who have gone up against magic without having any at their own disposal. Functional immortality is, perhaps surprisingly, a less attractive perk than one might think, as media has helped make people practiced in pondering the downside of an extra-long life. Still, for some, the guarantee of life is worth it, and their hunger for immortality means they have few compunctions about where their next meal comes from.
