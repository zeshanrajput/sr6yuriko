## The Astral Sea

The Astral Sea fills the void between the metaplanes and is substantially different from the astral space that overlaps the physical realm. It cannot be

accessed by normal astral travel, but a dedicated astral quest can be used to enter it. Normally, it requires a gateway set to it, but some portals and rifts lead there as well. It has some aspects of a metaplane and takes the form of an endless desert of white sand at twilight. It connects to, or perhaps contains, every metaplane, in the same sense that space within our physical plane surrounds and contains the planets. The Astral Sea is vast and has few defining characteristics of its own, beyond the stars overhead and the endless dunes. As you near a specific metaplane within the Astral Sea, you encounter the shore—the place that marks the metaplane’s boundary with the Astral Sea. The shore can be hard to notice—in fact, the metaplanes themselves are hard to notice from the Astral Sea, resembling little more than a shimmer in the air with a certain thinness to it. To enter a metaplane through the Astral Sea, merely approach it, step onto the shore, keep moving, and you will pass through the threshold. Be careful, though—not all of the denizens of metaplanes welcome travelers, and those who consider travelers to be intruders may be watching and waiting to destroy you.

When I refer to metaplanes being close to (or far apart) from one another, or of the metaplanes becoming close to our world, I am specifically referring to their proximity within the Astral Sea. The physical plane connects to the Astral Sea through what we call the “astral plane.” The threshold for this is not bound to our Gaiasphere. Do not attempt to travel to the Astral Sea by projecting your astral form away from the planet—you will die. The connection becomes available to any magician who initiates, but only through astral projection. Those magicians who are not capable of projecting their astral form outside of their own bodies can only attempt metaplanar travel with outside assistance, but any initiate capable of astral projection can simply visit the metaplanes using an astral quest. In the context of an astral quest, this invariably leads to a hyperplane known as the Citadel. This place seems designed for the advancement of magical knowledge, but doesn’t give up its secrets easily. The Citadel is highly reactive to the minds and goals of its travelers, and entering it always requires meeting the Dweller on the Threshold. For decades, magicians believed that the Dweller guarded access to the metaplanes themselves, but now we know that it only guards access to the metaplanes via astral projections.

One thing you must be careful about when traveling the Astral Sea is that it takes time. A traveler manifests a metaplanar form there just like in a metaplane, so unless you have made other arrangements, you will be limited to a walking speed. The nearest metaplanes can be reached within a few days travel at a walking speed, while the most distant ones may take weeks to travel to. If you decide to travel through the Astral Sea, you will need to



know where you are going, wandering aimlessly can be exceedingly dangerous. The stars are used to navigate between metaplanes, but it still takes some skill and a sextant along with the correct maps to find your way. Also, keep in mind that time doesn’t always follow the same rules within the metaplanes as it does on the physical plane, and this includes the Astral Sea.
