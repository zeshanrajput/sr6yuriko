## Spending Your Pool

Your Heist Pool was built through the hard work of the team to create the perfect plan on how to get the job done and bring everyone home alive. These points are spent while on the actual run to represent the effects of your planning. When you spend a point, you create a special effect that helps the team out and turns the tide in the team’s favor.

While a gamemaster can rule that a special effect from the pool matches the designation it came from, this isn’t a requirement. These points are meant to speed up play by providing teams the ability to retroactively create a situation as if they had really planned for it like the professional criminals their characters are.

When spending a Heist Point, this acts as if you were using the 5-Edge Boost: Create special effect (p. 47, _SR6_ ) retroactively.

Suggestions for special effects from spending Heist Points:



- **Redirect law enforcement or guard patrol.** You’ve either created a distraction or paid a little bribe to create a hole in the patrol pattern, allowing your team the perfect chance to slip by.

- **Exploit a lapse in fragmented jurisdiction.** Sometimes the dystopian state of the world can be used to your advantage, especially when you can get two different groups to fight a turf war while you get through.

- **Take a sensor out of service.** Whether you researched the MAD scanner maintenance schedule, or made sure a camera is deliberately brought offline, you create a blind spot in the sensor coverage.

- **Cancel a host alert.** Either you were able to set up a timed alert suppression, or the bribe to the spider worked perfectly. You’ve got one extra shot at breaking through.

- **Unlock a door.** It’s common that employees leave a “secured” door unsecure. Everyone needs to take a nicstick break just to deal with all the stress. Someone was nice enough to make sure the door you need is unlocked.

- **Gain employee credentials.** All that work paid off and provided you with the access you need. Either it’s login information for the host, or maybe an access card for a building, but at least now you look semi-legitimate.

- **Rig a trap or set up an ambush.** A little onsite recon provided you some time to rig a trap before you went in for the real run. Or maybe you found someone on the inside who was able to help you out for a couple playoff tickets.

- **Make a scanner malfunction.** Those darn faulty detection systems always seem to be on the fritz. And the guard was getting stressed out from the kilometer-long line to get in, so he just waved you through. Wiz!

- **Find an emergency egress.** Things are getting sketchy, but there’s some packed parachutes sitting on the helipad you can use to make a very quick exit or some ATVs in the drug compound you can quickly grab to get away.

- **Weaken the ward.** You did your research and knew this particular ward was reaching the end of its ritual time length, or this just happened to be one that was set up by the intern.

- **Bargain with a patrol spirit.** You parlayed with the spirit that guards the place and worked out a deal that benefits the spirit and doesn’t contradict its commands.

- **Delay an alarm.** Sometimes it just takes an extra minute or two before the alarm goes off to ensure your success. Perhaps you set it to have a delay trigger, or you paid someone off to help you out a little bit without risking their neck.
