## **Option 2:**

## **They Offer a Plea Bargain**

**Sometimes, a prosecutor might have a defendant dead to rights, but trials are still expensive, and juries are unpredictable. In those cases, the prosecutor might try to offer you a plea bargain, where you admit to committing a crime (sometimes the crime you’d be charged with, sometimes a lesser related crime), in exchange for a much lighter sentence than if you were tried and found guilty. The deal usually sounds something like, “We caught you breaking the window and climbing in on the security feed, and we found the stolen property in your car. If you plead guilty, you’ll serve one year. If you go to trial, you could serve up to fve. Which would you rather do?”**

**If you take a plea bargain, you still end up getting stuck with a criminal SIN, and you have to go to court and admit to whatever crimes you pled guilty to. On the other hand, you’ll get out of jail a lot sooner, if you go at all.**
