## Hotspots

## Ilhuicaatl Holdings Warehouse

Ilhuicaatl Holdings is an Aztechnology-owned shipping company. The low, fortress-like warehouse sits on the waterfront just north of the Clinton Castle and includes a pier jutting out into the Hudson. A number of bay doors allow cargo to move between the building and ships at the pier. Large, remotely operated cranes and cargo haulers line the pier to load and unload ships. There is a large, paved area to stage trucks; on the street side of the warehouse, bay doors allow trucks to move cargo in and out from the Manhattan streets. The warehouse only operates from 5:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., with numerous employees there during those hours.

The outer perimeter is ten meters out from the building, and the paved area consists of a pair of three-meter wire fences topped with razor wire. There is a gap of three meters between the fences, and the inner fence has high-voltage warning signs on it. A blockhouse sits next to a large, automated gate for vehicle traffic, and a smaller gate on the opposite side of the blockhouse allows foot traffic. The exterior of the building is well-lit at night, and cameras cover the entire grounds. There are six guards (use Lone Star Patrolman, p. 206, _SR6_ ) stationed in the blockhouse at all times, and three two-person teams constantly patrol the grounds. A separate guard station staffed by four guards sits on the pier, and two teams of two people patrol the pier. During off-hours, two additional two-person teams patrol the interior of the warehouse. When not in use, the bay doors are secured with rating 4 maglocks. Eight automated turrets can be seen on the warehouse walls, one at each corner of the building and one centered on each wall.
