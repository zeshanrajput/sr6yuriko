## Lust

First, let’s understand that Lust means a whole lot more than sex. You can lust for power, for money, for your neighbor’s husband, or his shiny new Phaeton-7, but if those are all Lust, I have no idea what Gluttony, Greed, or Envy are. St. Thomas Aquinas said that it’s an abuse of the same instincts (meta) humans share with animals, though I think of lust more as the dark side of passion—meaning passion has greater potential to achieve a goal that is benevolent to others, whereas Lust is more self-centered.

Lust, more often than not, isn’t about sexual gratification at all. Rather, it’s a stand-in, a proxy bypass to sate a deeper need. We don’t need the orgasm so much as we need to feel loved, wanted, powerful, brave, or successful. Lust is an injection of passion in a place where we stagnate, youth when we age, company where we are lonely, and so on. While Gluttony is about surpassing appetite into excess, Lust is a hunger that drives us to dangerous extremes of effort or vulnerability. Understanding the facets of someone’s Lust will give you invaluable insights into your approach.

Lust is as powerful as it is risky. Even in the anything-goes porn age of the 2080s Matrix, where you can do or be anything to any being, we’re still tempted by the real thing. We still live in a world where a certain shame about those feelings can have a tangible effect in the form of hesitation and suspicion toward those who offer us gratification. Confidence on the part of the mark doesn’t determine the degree of difficulty, but rather the nature of it. Those who lack selfworth may question why someone is so interested in them, while those who place an abundance of value on themselves may have so many suitors you have to compete with them, or simply show you’re worth their time. So we can break most Lust marks down into those who operate from a position of strength or weakness—or, drawing on the earlier Observation section, whether they are proactive or reactive.

A lack of confidence determines who’s going to approach the lure. You can doll yourself to hell and back and be everything they ever wanted, but if you don’t initiate the chase, they’ll just look at you longingly from across the hotel lounge. Of course, self-doubters are prone to constant auto-auditing, which means you’ll need a soft touch. It’s easy to overwhelm this kind of mark in a bad way, arousing suspicion when you want to inspire relaxation. Avoid “Why would they be interested in me?” and instead pursue “How did I get so lucky?” To achieve this, moderate your approach with a careful measure of their desires and their belief that you’d approach them. Research should give you an idea of what they’re into as well as what they believe they’re unworthy of. Not everyone is looking for a nova-hot looker in SecondSkin. The cozy sweater, shared hobbies, and idea of secret passion will usually get you further and in a much shorter time frame. They might also appreciate something a little more adventurous, like the artist who can liberate them from their humdrum life, for example, or lend philosophical beauty or passion to their work. This approach is rare for more action-oriented, violent types, and much better aligned with cloistered scientists, middle-manager programmers, and other low-profile archetypes. Confident and proactive targets may be the most dangerous. Much like the insecure proactive, finding their type is simply a matter of observing their patterns. Problems arise because they’re likely quite successful in their pursuits, confidence being one of the most potent aphrodisiacs out there.
