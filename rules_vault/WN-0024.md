## Private Transportation

Yellow cabs are so synonymous with NYC, you’d think they started there. They’re also easy to find—just raise your hand. Indy cabs hardly exist as most medallions belong to one corp or another and they log their fares with a corporate database detailing who went where, and when. Jitnee, the major ride-sharing service, is not as common here as in other sprawls, mainly because of the city’s various restrictions, but you can still find a ride if you prefer that to taxis. One step up from a taxi is a private car service. Dozens of these services exist in the city, and not all are linked to major corporations or security services. They provide a more discreet transport option, often with upgraded security—as long as you’re willing to pay the upgraded price.

If price is not a factor, the fastest way around the city is by air taxi. Roughly 10,000 of the city’s buildings—around a fifth, all told—are zoned for rooftop landing pads. Multiple corporate air services offer roofpad-to-roofpad transit, and like ground cabs, fares are logged with the company database.

- If a sedan or VTOL doesn’t fit your needs, service vehicles such as vans, trucks, garbage trucks, and even automated street sweepers are available if you have the right contacts. Local syndicates are embedded in the service industry and always looking for a way to make more nuyen.
