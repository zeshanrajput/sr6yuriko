## A Message from the Widow

In mission 30 (p. 114), the PCs might have heard directors of Animus Construction talking about a patient they referred to as “the widow,” hoping she would not recover some memories they had buried. The widow is, of course, Gabrielle Al Thani Spinrad, who took a week to plan the assassination of her husband, then erased the memory of doing so in order to bypass any Mind Probes used by her late husband’s security. Like Blake, Al Thani has the PCs on her radar, but she doesn’t know how much they know. She also doesn’t know everything _she_ used to know, but in line with the worries of the directors, her real memories are starting to bleed through. Animus had designed fake memories of a Moroccan vacation for her, but her memories of that vacation have become interspersed with memories of looking at trid stills of assassination victims and conducting high-pressure negotiations with unsavory individuals. She believes someone is messing with her mind, but currently she thinks the real memories of her assassination planning are the fake ones that someone is trying to insert into her head. Her networks have heard that the PCs are looking into mental manipulation, and since they offered her some help with the Gala, she would love to know what they know.

But she also does not want to let anyone know what she is going through, so she’s not going to directly deal with the PCs or let them know anyone is acting on her behalf. What she does instead is send an operative who finds the PCs at a restaurant or bar they are frequenting and joins them, buying a round of drinks for all of them. The operative is named Joy Tsung (use stats for Face, p. 85, _SR6_ ), and she is a cheerful, talkative woman. She makes sure she gets the PCs’ drink preferences correct, and she also makes it clear (without saying it directly) that she knows the PCs’ line of work and is not there to cause them trouble.

When the drinks are delivered, she demonstrates that she memorized exactly who got what drink. She says that’s not hard with a relatively small group, but she’s worked on various techniques to hone her memory over the years.

This launches her into a whole conversation on memory, its reliability, and how it shapes us as people. She posits that someone who could manipulate memory could reshape entire personalities, which is a scary kind of power. But also a useful one. If the PCs know of any work going on in that area, she’d be super-interested to know.
