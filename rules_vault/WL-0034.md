## **Posted by: G-Nome**

**The steppes are defned by colder grasslands either due to elevation or latitude. These plains have some of the highest ranges of temperatures anywhere** on the planet. Extremes measuring 45 °C in the summer and –55 °C in the winter have been measured in many of these, including Mongolia and the PCC. Subtropical steppes can be found in parts of Europe near Greece and Italy, or the fringes of the Thar Desert in India. The steppes of Ukraine and Patagonia of Amazonia are examples of cold steppes.

**Prairies, grasslands, and plains all share similar aspects** . **They are def** i **ned by their large stretches of** fi elds of grass with very few trees. These ecosystems are home to a variety of wildlife, plants, and insects. They often have a moderate amount of rainfall and moderate temperatures, although those can swing wildly in a single day because of the lack of windbreaks and bodies of water that can act as a heatsink.

There are other types of grasslands. This includes savannas, sedges, fl ooded grasslands, and herbaceous salt swamps. Some of these biomes are covered in different parts of this data drop.

Steppes tend to be drier than other types of temperate grasslands. This defi nitely impacts the types of animals that can be found here. Grasses are still plentiful even with the lower precipitation, and many of the larger animals are horses, deer, or other herbivores. The larger predators tend to be canine, such as the gray wolf, the fox, or the occasional bear.

The temperate grasslands cover areas of southern Amazonia, the steppes of Ukraine, and the central plains of North America. These grasslands contain mixtures of different types of grasses. During the spring rains, the grasses have young tender chutes that welcome many of herbivores that later attract predators. When summer and fall arrive, the lower amounts of rainfall coupled with hot weather provides risk of wildfi res during thunderstorms. At this time of year, the animals of the grasslands will often feed upon the fl owers and seeds of the various plant life. During the winter, much of the grass dies off while the root network stays alive, providing a tough area to burrow into for the many creatures like the prairie dog which live below ground and will often hibernate through the cold months.

There is another type of grassland that is usually constrained to smaller segments of the land. These are naturally occurring meadows. These stretches of grasslands are typically found on the border of other types of ecosystems. For instance, alpine meadows are grasslands located above the tree line at high elevations. Many of the creatures that subsist here are goats or other herbivores that use the nearby mountains to avoid predators but feed upon these grasses. Wet meadows are near the edges of rivers or other wetlands that will fl ood on occasion, but in most seasons maintain a consistent growth of fl ora to support the local animals.
