## Street Samurai Blade Fighter

## playing advice

Regardless of background or weapon style, the blade street samurai walks a warrior’s path. Their primary focus (at least in the beginning) should always be combat—specifically how to become a better warrior on the streets while staying focused on their specific style of fighting (it is a matter of honor and pride, after all). As the blade samurai progresses and improves throughout their careers, additional, and maybe unrelated, secondary skills and focuses can be added, but never to the detriment of skill with the blade.

On the tactical level, combat for the blade street samurai typically means getting in close to your opponent and doing as much damage as possible, as quickly as possible, while avoiding counterattacks. The first can often be challenging, as there are multitudes of ranged attacks a target or opponent can employ, be they firearms or ranged combat spells.

As such, speed is essential for a blade street samurai, as it will (hopefully) allow them to close

and act before an opponent. This means the best chrome they can get—wired reflexes, reaction enhancers, anything to boost initiative should their first pieces of ’ware. High Reaction ratings should also be considered. Secondary types of augmentations to consider should be anything that increases survivability, such as dermal plating or bone lacing, because the blade street samurai is a primarily a close combatant and will inevitably take damage at some point. Combining defensive augmentations with a high Body will help the blade street samurai avoid or deal with damage (medkits are always useful, too!). If for some reason the blade street samurai is not able to gain enough raw speed through augmentations, then investing in a high Stealth skill is advisable in order to close with an opponent.

Whatever method the blade street samurai uses, they will need to realize that among their team, they will be one of the main front-line combatants in any kind of confrontation. They’ll need to coordinate with the rest of the team accordingly.

But blade street samurai are more than a mix of edged weapon and chrome, to be pointed at a threat and told to kill it. They can come from diverse backgrounds, be of any metatype, and literally have been anything prior to taking up the path of the blade. How they reconcile their past with their present while they travel along their path is what makes the blade street samurai interesting.

This is also why a samurai’s code is so important. Anyone can pick up a weapon (even a blade) and install some chrome to become a deadly adversary. But it is their code that makes the samurai (blade or otherwise) stand out in the shadows. Inevitably, a blade street samurai will face with an opponent or situation that will challenge their code and force them to decide if they will uphold it in the face of opposition or even certain death or break it and suffer the consequences.
