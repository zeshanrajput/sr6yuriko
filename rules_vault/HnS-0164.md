## Cyberterminals

Many corporate security jobs simply do not justify the expense of an implanted cyberjack or the portability of a cyberdeck, especially those filled by low-level personnel who haven’t proven their ability or loyalty yet. Most employers prefer a low-cost option for facility and Matrix security work, one that can sit on a desk and be used by the next shift—or the replacement for the guy who just had his brains fragged. Cyberterminals provide full ASDF Matrix functions in one device, but are non-portable, requiring a stable external power supply to operate. They are physically much larger than a cyberdeck, about the size of a large briefcase or suitcase. Most include a supplemental flip-up flat display and a manual input device for accessing basic functions. For actual use, a cyberterminal can project standard AR overlay controls, and all models include a sim-module for full DNI connectivity if the user has a datajack or trodes.
