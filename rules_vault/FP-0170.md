## **Goals**

**First and foremost, Bishop has to establish his authority. Ares has always been a chaotic mess, and Vogel squandered the best chance in recent memory to straighten it out. Hell, he did so badly that I wouldn’t be surprised if it was intentional. Paying off old debts to the eco-side of things? Just wanting to salt the Earth that Knight used to claim? Cyberpsychosis? We don’t know. Regardless, Bishop’s now in the big chair, and he needs to establish how that’s going to work. He’s got a hundred small fres to put out and we don’t know which one he’ll get to frst. Off the top of my head:**

_**Deal with Vogel’s people**_ **: Vogel had a few years to replace Knight’s people with his own, but he never managed a clean sweep. There are still people more loyal to Daviar foating around, and, of course, Aurelius’ people as well. Vogel’s people are the biggest, and most recent, threat, so he’ll have to fgure out who they are, whether or not he can**

**use them, and if not, how to remove them with minimal damage.**

_**Pay off his debts**_ **: You don’t get rewards without promises. Some are patient and will get paid down the line, but some want their agreement resolved as soon as possible. His position isn’t secure enough to betray his backers, but he can’t afford to make enemies yet, either. It’s a tough balancing act, and he’ll likely get some shadow help to assist him.**

_**Improve relationships with the CAS**_ **: Ares’ new HQ in Atlanta has brought prestige and power to the nation, and the assorted contracts that have been signed for military support, and tax breaks in return, are bedrock parts of this. He’ll need to keep the good feelings growing, as he doesn’t have anywhere to go if things go, uh, south.**

_**Rebuild relationships with the UCAS:**_ **Vogel burned bridges with the UCAS and burned them badly, but the UCAS isn’t in a position to just shut Ares out. The Seven Council Fires war showed them how vulnerable they are without Ares’ help, and until the Treaty of Seattle gets hammered out, that vulnerability remains. With Colloton out of the Oval Offce and Vogel out of Phoenix Tower, they have a chance for a fresh start. Of course, a second chance isn’t a sure thing.**

_**Personal goals**_ **: The above are what he needs to do for the position, but the open question is what he wants to do for** _**himself**_ **. He’s always been ambitious, but not greedy. There’s really nowhere for him to go up from here, so now we get to see what he personally values. Bishop has a decades-long history in Philadelphia that shows his philanthropy and community support, but a CEO can make a much bigger difference if they choose to.**

- **I should point out that he doesn’t have absolute power. We tend to think of CEOs as kings, when they’re more like prime ministers, beholden to a parliament, or in this case, the board of directors. Knight and Vogel both had massive blocks of stock that let them have considerable influence in votes, asserting their will over the board. Bishop’s stock doesn’t even qualify him for a seat on the board. Significant power is in the hands of Aurelius as the chair of the board. She can play a considerable role in setting the corporate agenda and directing funds, while Bishop is, at the end of the day, a kind of glorified salesman. As long as they’re on the same page, he’s good, but that’s a short leash.**
