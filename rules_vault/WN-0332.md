## Altered States

The challenge is that Sigrid (use stats for **Squatter** , p. 172, _Sixth World Companion_ ) wants nothing to do with Brinkvale. She is living on the street and has done so for a long time. She has moments of lucidity, but also moments where she yells gibberish and nonsense.

She is easy to find because chefs at the Blind Pig regularly slide her food. She has her favorite crate by their dumpster, and she sits there and eats well. Between meals, she has nowhere else to go, so she sits on her crate and sometimes naps.

Since she likes her spot, she is not anxious to leave, so she’ll question the PCs’ intent in asking her to go anywhere. If they mention Brinkvale, she’ll absolutely refuse to go with them, as she wants nothing to do with him. She says he kept trying to push BTLs on her, and when she finally used one, it messed up her head for a week. Then he wanted to scan her brain. She is not interested in that.

Promises of a nice place to sleep will not impress her, as she doesn’t trust strangers who offer things like that out of the blue. She also doesn’t care about food, as she is fed well. If the PCs talk to her a little, though, they’ll find she has a significant interest in music. The chance to hear live music—or better yet, participate in it (she likes to sing and has a decent voice, all things considered)—will persuade her to follow the PCs anywhere.

The PCs always have the option of transporting her sedated or unconscious, but she needs to be alive for Brinkvale’s purposes.

Once the PCs get Sigrid to Brinkvale, she won’t be happy, but she won’t flee, mainly because she

21: THEknows she cannot outrun or outfight the PCs. She begrudgingly asks what he wants, and he says all he wants is a brain scan. He promises not to give her BTLs or harm her in any way.

He is good to his word. After he scans her brain, he lets her return, unharmed, back to the Blind Pig. Then he tells the PCs what he found.

Animus Construction is working on memories—specifically, erasing them, maybe even altering them. He warns them, though, that it’s an extremely tricky thing to attempt. To explain why he says so, he shows a series of scans he made of Sigrid’s brain, including when she used the BTL he gave her. The BTL was designed to implant a memory of relaxing on a sandy beach in the mind of the user—not just make them feel like they were doing it, but make a memory that they really had done it. For a time, it worked on Sigrid, but her mind rebelled. It recognized the falsity of the effort and rejected it. Sigrid briefly accepted it as a memory, but then realized it wasn’t, and she became angry at the effort to trick her. That’s the primary reason she wants nothing to do with Brinkvale. He says that what’s happening in Sigrid is what he has seen happening in some Animus patients (he got a look at scans from some of the people who turned up in Central Park). Their work, as revolutionary as it is, seems to lose effectiveness over time.

Brinkvale says this is extremely high-level—and expensive—stuff. If they want to learn more about who might be doing it, they need to talk to Brinkvale’ old boss: Dr. Sonya Peters at NYU. The bad news is, she’s dead. The good news is, they might be able to find an e-ghost of her.
