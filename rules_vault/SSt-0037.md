## **Mana Barriers**

**Mana barriers can be a huge pain in the hoop. A mana barrier or ward can be set up solely to prevent astral intrusion, but it is often dual-natured, preventing anything actively magical from going through it on the physical plane as well. They’re pretty obvious to spot using astral perception but can easily be overlooked by mundane senses.**

**If you’re Awakened and want to get through a mana barrier, you’ll have to carefully push through it. Any Awakened individual is capable of attempting this, though more powerful magicians are more likely to succeed. Pushing through a barrier doesn’t mean just slamming yourself up against it as hard as you can—that’s more likely to knock you out faster than a Narcoject dart. Pushing through a ward is more subtle and requires you to attune yourself to it so that it accepts you as an authorized user. It’s the magic equivalent of bypassing a maglock using a sequencer device. This can take a few tries, but failing to break through doesn’t set off any alarms or alert the security mage. Unless you’re really pressed for time, take a deep breath and make as many attempts as you need. Unless the barrier is very powerful or your mojo is very weak, you’ll eventually get through. For some reason, magicians from shamanic traditions seem to have an easier time of this than hermetics, but I couldn’t tell you why that is. The better you are at this, the more active spells, foci, spirits, and preparations you can bring with you. If you’re on an astral scouting run, don’t just push through a mana barrier frst thing, though. If there’s something alert and nasty on the other end, you can’t just zip away and escape back the way you came—you’ll need to push your way back through to get back to your body.**

**This might be obvious, but it’s worth pointing out that fully mundane people and objects can pass right through mana barriers without any trouble— even dual-natured ones. If they’re carrying an alchemical preparation or have a spell enhancing them (or hiding them with invisibility), they won’t be able to pass through. A physical barrier will get in their way, but those are not normally part of a security system unless they know you’re coming. In that case we wouldn’t be talking about stealth anymore, so it’s beyond the scope of this post.**

**There are some variations on wards that can trigger secondary effects such as activating a spell, but those are rare and require a specialist. Any security mage with some skill in ritual sorcery is likely to be tasked with setting up and maintaining basic wards, so they are fairly commonly employed. Most magicians would rather spend their resources and training on learning more widely useful things, so you aren’t very likely to encounter anything more fancy. Just be aware that such things exist, and take the time to properly assense any mana barriers that you encounter before you mess with them.**

**If you don’t have a magician on your team, you probably don’t need to worry about wards—unless you’re using alchemical preparations, have a quickened spell on you, or someone on your team is dual-natured. Your best bet is to avoid the ward or circumvent it, as tampering with the ward is almost certain to alert the security mages. If you can use an alternate route, do that. Most wards are designed to cover a whole room, and these will protect the entire space from all directions. This means using explosives or power tools to make a hole in the wall is just going to be a waste of your time. If what you need is inside the ward, deactivate your foci, put away your alchemical preparations, and tell anyone who can’t or won’t do that to wait outside. Unfortunately, that means any shifters, deepweed addicts, and vampires on your team are going to have to guard the door while the rest of you get the job done. If they absolutely need to come inside with you, you can disrupt the ward by damaging its physical anchors. Look for signs or symbols inscribed on the walls, which might be simply drawn on with a permanent marker but are more likely to be tastefully integrated into the decor. Mess these up with a knife or something (I like to carry a mini-welder for such occasions), and the ward will collapse. Here’s the bad news, though: if you pop a ward, whoever set it up will know it immediately. At that point, you can consider the stealth aspect of your mission to be over with, so be prepared to deal with incoming security. In my experience, the magician either arrives in their astral form to fnd out what happened or they send in a spirit or two to do that for them. Magical security response times can be very quick, often just seconds after the breach is detected. You**

**can bet that the mage will alert the mundane security forces of the breach before they investigate, so don’t expect to get rid of the alarm just by taking them out when they come for you. That’s why it’s so much better to go around or just send in the mundanes.**
