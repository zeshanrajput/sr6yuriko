## ENORMOUS DRAGON SEEN OVER SOUTHERN AZTLAN

_MEDELLIN —_ Airport radar picked up a flying object of immense size earlier today, initially thought to be an off-course passenger aircraft. After the object failed to answer all communication requests, two _Zeta-Bravo_ interceptors were dispatched. As they approached the mysterious object, increased turbulence caused the pilots to change course. In doing so, they flew past the unidentified object and flight recorders caught a glimpse of it within the thick clouds: a large western dragon followed by a phalanx of air spirits.

Neither the dragon, spirits, nor the fighters engaged each other.

Long-range imaging systems were able to get a better look at the dragon; its markings are conWORLDWIDE: BYsistent with Sirrurg the Destroyer, the dragon responsible for causing an estimated ten thousand deaths in an attack on the city of Cali in the Amazonian War, as well as the devastation of Borinquen. It also revealed several scars along its right side along wing and foreclaw, likely from its last known engagement with Aztlan Military forces in 2074, when it suddenly disappeared during the engagement, before Aztlan forces could deliver a killing blow.

After verifying this sighting, all air travel within 250 kilometers of Medellin was suspended, and incoming flights were diverted to other airports, complicating air traffic in Bogotá and as far north as Panama City.

Since the sighting, communities ravaged by the dragon have come forward, demanding answers from the Aztlan government.

“We remain vigilant in the face of this discovery. Should the Destroyer wish to test the might of Aztlan, he will not be so lucky to escape this time,” replied Gerardo Ruiz-Orozco, spokesperson for the Aztlán Party, in Tenochtitlán today.

All requests to interview Señor Ruiz-Orozco have been declined.
