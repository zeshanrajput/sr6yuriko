## Professional Note 02: Being the Wingman

In this situation, The Professor acted as lookout for his teammate and defused a situation with an interloping corporate employee that could have had dire consequences for the job. Going into the Matrix in hot-sim is an all-inclusive endeavor, whether a decker with an old-school wrist-mounted deck or a technomancer fully immersed. When operating as such, they’re completely vulnerable and need someone to watch or guard them. While a street samurai, adept, or other combat specialist could keep a decker safe by the sword or fist, they more often than not stand out or attract too much attention in most social situations. In situations outside of combat or ones that require a more delicate touch, is where faces are in their element. At first, The Professor came off as abrasive, but then switched gears to become more sympathetic to the jogger. This brought the jogger into The Professor’s confidence allowing him to pull off a minor con and defuse the situation.

While it was beneficial in this situation, being charming or even likable isn’t always the answer or solution. It’s about manipulating emotions, holding attention, and getting the desired outcome. Yes, a good face knows that you definitely catch more flies with honey than vinegar. But there are times when the flies simply need to go away.

ULTIMATEsecurity analyst, doing their horribly mundane job. Back in the real world (I detest the term “meat space”), I stood watch as Augury sat at the side of the brook with her eyes closed, seemingly deep in meditation or perhaps sleeping. I clocked the jogger who had passed us on the garden trail just a few moments earlier. The looping walking path was leading them directly back to the rest house where Augury was silently working.

“What are you two doing?” the jogger asked me as she continued to jog in place, presumably to maintain a constant heart rate.

“We are at the rest house and are simply resting.” I attempted to keep my tone pleasant, if not projecting a bit of annoyance.

“Shouldn’t you be working?” The jogger didn’t seem to appreciate my tone.

“Shouldn’t you be jogging?” I snapped back, “I don’t believe that is any of your concern, but my partner is meditating.” I gestured at the kneeling Augury. “She needs to meditate three times a day.”

“She can’t meditate on her own time?” the jogger still bounced up and down her pace steady, “I can’t jog on the clock.”

“Renraku Diversity and Inclusion Policy, section three, paragraph one ‘Renraku will make reasonable efforts to accommodate employees’ religious beliefs and practices. Accommodations may include flexible scheduling, time off for religious observances, and modifications to work attire or grooming policies.’ I read off the inclusion policy with practiced exacerbation before softening my expression and tone with a small sigh and slight bow.

“My apologies. We get this a lot. My trusted coworker here has asked me to come with her so I could talk to those who might approach so she can remain undisturbed, and we can return to our duties as soon as possible. Our supervisors are aware of her breaks; she filled out all the required paperwork, which has been approved by human resources.”

“It just doesn’t feel fair. Why does a grounds worker get an extra break in the middle of their shift, and I have to work through my lunch every day?”

“I’m going to be honest with you,” I replied before leaning in toward the jogger. “All you need do is fill out the proper 601-A form and submit it to your immediate supervisor. Tell them your run is part of your worship or something like that. You could get up to an extra fifteen extra minutes a day for religious exercises and expression.”

“You think that will work?”

“It’s their policy, not yours,” I said with a shrug and then attempted to take on the guise of corporate sage insider, “You simply have to learn how to jump through the right hoops.”

“Thank you, I just might do that,” she replied before resuming her run along the garden path.
