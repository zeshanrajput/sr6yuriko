## Meeting Mr. Johnson

A common misconception is that we only work for the corps or the criminals. The nature of shadowrunners as off-the-book agents of change means that we can work for practically anyone. Corps and crooks may be the groups that get play on the trideo, but shadowrunners work for anyone willing to pay. The thing is, we aren’t supposed to know who we work for. That’s part of the appeal to our employers. Most shadowruns happen through an intermediary known collectively as Mr. Johnson. Sexist, I know, but the gendered term helps reinforce anonymity. Not knowing who you work for means deniability on both sides. The way it works is, you or someone you know is contacted by Mr. Johnson. You set up an initial meeting where terms of the job are discussed. A small down payment is generally offered up front to secure your services, and then you go to work.
