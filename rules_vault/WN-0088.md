## Jurisdiction

Following the Municipal Services order, the NYPD performs all policing functions that don’t fall into the jurisdiction of corp-specific security. This include capital crimes such as murder, interjurisdictional spaces (sidewalks and anything outside of a building), and being responsible for city access through a subdivision of the organization known as MTA.

Other security forces exist—especially within buildings. If you commit a crime inside a building owned by Evo, their security can come for you. Now if you leave that building, the NYPD will get you; however, they need to cooperate with the internal security provider to get inside. That happens less and less these days. The charter of the NYPD holds them responsible for external threats, essentially meaning that a crime committed inside of an extraterritorial corp-owned building doesn’t go on their books, so they have less motivation to get involved.
