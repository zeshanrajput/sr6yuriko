## Hotspot

## Animus Construction: Clinical Operations

This facility is located beneath the rebuilt escarpments on the west side of the former Washington Street Bridge site. The information the PCs obtained gave the location and even an address (400 West 181st Street), but there is no building with that actual street address. They also have no pictures of the facility.

After the earthquake and subsequent reconstruction and reinforcement of the escarpments, many construction records were lost. A series of maintenance accessways built into the new escarpments have been since converted into what is now an Animus Construction location. These have been hidden by a complex array of measures, magical and otherwise.

There are two ways to enter the facility. A pedestrian access is disguised behind a bench alongside a public access path and bike route. There is a vehicle access by way of a parking garage two full blocks onto the island beneath what was once a U.S. postal facility, a spot now owned by CitiGroup. Both are camouflaged by combinations of high-end holography and advanced smart-material-infused ferrocrete combined with rotating service vehicles in the garage and the occasional metahuman-disguised anthroform traversing along the path and bike route. Triggers for gaining access to either location are by way of authorized, passive RFID technology for staff or vehicles.

Visually penetrating the camouflage requires


a Perception (16, 1 hour) test at either location, made more complicated by actual traffic or the wandering drones. Attempting to penetrate by way of an Astral (Aura) (3) test will only reveal that the area is consistently in use. MAD scanners and related surveillance technologies must defeat an Electronics + Logic v. twice rating (10) test to detect the presence of the RFID scanners. Police response time is rapid, and they can be on site within a minute of being summoned.
