## Prepared Gris-Gris

This gris-gris doesn’t yet have a spirit that has been called to help. The prepared gris-gris is meant for the busy houngan or mambo who need to be prepared to call upon the spirits to aid them. It has already gone through the fi rst ritual and has been imbued with reagents. The cost represents both the hours of labor involved in creating gris-gris and the reagent cost.

The world of these charms is wide and varied. They are often created to match one of the lwa that I talked about earlier. Many of the uses are built around bringing good fortune or protecting the wearer from foul magics. Other gris-gris are created to attack others as part of a ritual using material or sympathetic linking. The objects have taken on many different names besides gris-gris, including fetishes, talismans, and charms.

This is different from an expended gris-gris, which has no intrinsic value. In an expended gris-gris, the spirit has consumed the magic from the reagents. When it departs, what is left is just the bauble with no astral signature.

There are different forms of gris-gris beyond the classic mojo bag. Paket kongo is often a ball of spices tied in ribboned fabrics and decorated with feathers and sequins. If you encounter terracotta jars, these are known as govi. Wanga are a variety of objects that are physically bound in power through the tying of cords and ribbons. The size of the gris-gris has an impact on the power of the spirit that can inhabit it.
