**in that lwa’s entry below), they will also gain an additional point of Edge that may be used** to assist the Conjuring or drain resistance test.

Voo **d** oo spe **ll** cast **i** ng **i** s typ **i** ca **ll** y **f** ocuse **d** on ritual spellcasting with a heavy reliance on lwa for the magical needs of the community. This is deeply rooted in the traditions stemming back to before the Awakening. This doesn’t prevent the practitioner from casting spells, though. When selecting spells, use those that are closest to the selected nanchon.

Any food offering must be fresh—not just some krill-based substitute from Stuffer Shack. The cost of the ritual food offerings is covered under medium lifestyle or higher. At low lifestyle or less, you’ll need to spend at least 50 nuyen acquiring a proper offering.

Conjuring without both a summoning ritual and the correct ritual offerings is potentially risky. The lwa that appears may not be the one intended. Instead, the lwa could be a trickster whose goal is to antagonize the summoner. When summoning without these two things, the summoner must make an Edge test, and on two or more hits, they successfully call the desired spirit. If there is only one hit, the type of lwa is correct, but the Force is reduced by 1. With no successes, the lwa that appears will be of a different type. On a Glitch, the lwa will actively work against the summoner—on a Critical Glitch, they’ll be more direct and extreme about it. If the lwa is antagonistic, the lwa gains a point of Edge to resist any Social test used by the summoner attempting to convince the spirit to complete a service.
