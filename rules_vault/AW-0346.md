## LAND OF DINO BEASTS

## Posted By: Moreau

When I read Magister’s entry in _Aetherology_ on the topic of the Plane of Beasts, I was especially intrigued by an entry regarding the Island of Tandora. The interesting but brief entry describes the island as “a dreadful place of beast spirits … looking like various dinosaurs or beasts with fearsome scaly or feathered appearances.” I thought this seemed like the kind of place that the Draco Foundation might be interested in funding an expedition to explore, but I thought wrong. The interest might have been there, but their resources were stretched thin at the time.

But I couldn’t set the idea aside for long. I have plenty of equipment and nuyen stashed away, and no shortage of contacts in the shadows. It took me nearly a year to get the right crew together and complete our preparations for the expedition. Nearly all of the nuyen I had saved up for my retirement fund was gone by the time we left Earth for the Island of Tandora.

One of my contacts is a free spirit in New York named Germaine or Ginger-Melon (they answer to both) who was willing to help us find a metaplanar rift for a fee. This was how my team and I crossed over into the metaplanes, along with all of the carefully selected gear and supplies we could carry. One of the specialty items we brought with us was an electric outboard motor, along with some spare batteries and a few of those little hydroelectric recharge tubes. The rift was just large enough to pass through in a small boat, but we knew we couldn’t count on keeping it for long. Nancy figured that the portable outboard motor would give us some flexibility on that front.

I suppose I should introduce the team, now that I’m naming names. Nancy is a rigger from St. Louis who specializes in aquatic shenanigans. She’s probably able to pilot any vehicle ever manufactured but has a special reputation for knowing her way around anything that travels on or under the water. A hermetic mage by the name of Hemdale and a raccoon shaman named Bandit were therefor magical support and handling delicate negotiations with spirits. As for muscle, I hired Aspect (an adept) and Kai (a street samurai). I also brought along a decker named Shade, not so much for hacking purposes but for his data collection and engineering skills as well as his personal experience with metaplanar travel. His Fly-Spy drones were one of the most useful things we brought with us (he also dabbles in rigging). If you’re ever on any sort of scouting expedition, make sure to bring at least a half-dozen of those little buggers and an RCC with a signal booster. Even where there’s no Matrix coverage and you’re limited to line-of-sight range, they’re indispensable.

As soon as our boat was ready, we departed to find the rift Germaine had found. We had to take to the Schuykill River, as the rift was in the middle of it. We navigated to the tributary that would held the rift and made our way through.
