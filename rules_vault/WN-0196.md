## Kente Royal Gallery, New Harlem

This gallery stands as one of the oldest surviving galleries in New Harlem. Originally created as a touchstone to African American artists, the location fell into disrepair during the Newtown era. The gallery was resurrected by Horizon during the New Harlem revival and formally reintroduced to the NYC fashion circuit in 2079.

The building itself is over one hundred years old. The insides have been gutted and replaced to include a full suite of SOTA security technology, giving the building itself an AA security rating despite it being in a lower-rated zone. Rumors persist of basement connections to deeper underground tunnels that run as far uptown as 90th Street.
