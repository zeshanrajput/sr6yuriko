## The Black Lodge

## Posted by: Philostrate

- Yes, our resident Black Lodge … associate? confidante? whatever … is back, but I don’t think I need to put too many warnings or disclaimers here. I’m sure Philostrate has some opening remarks that will set things up better than I could regarding what the Black Lodge has been contributing to this little shadow war.

- Glitch

It gets easy to pretend it’s all a game. Or a puzzle. You get caught up in how this piece fits with that one, and another one slides in here. And when they’re all together, it’s not a picture, but a machine, and you make it whir silently and marvel at what it can do. You keep building out the machine and adding new functions. You put in a cog to deliver military contracts. A gear to provide access to industrial machinery. You play puppet master and giggle as you make the puppets dance.

**>** Great ghost, pick a metaphor and stick with it already! **>** Sunshine

These game pieces, these pieces of machinery, these puppets—they’re people. Occasionally they’re converts, people who have understood and fully bought into your cause, and at least they’re going into what they do with their eyes open. Everyone else has been subverted. Some are bribed, some are blackmailed, others are manipulated into serving a master they don’t understand at all.

You see the toll it takes. You talk to people who are nervous, stressed, feeling powerless, and you take satisfaction in their desperation, because it means they are yours. Any basic empathy is replaced by satisfaction that your will is advancing. It’s all just a game, a show, a machine.

You have two paths from this point. One is to continue as you are, to stay above everything, remote. Stay distantly amused at how you manipulate the people beneath you—because you can’t help but think of them as being beneath you. You develop names for them—drones, normies, NPCs—that gives voice to your contempt. Moment by moment, step by step, you lose your ability to connect to other people as you push them farther and farther away. You play your game because it’s all you have.

The other path is you slip and make a connection. You find someone you work with who is compatible with you, interesting to talk to, fun to be with. You look forward to any chances you have to interact with them, and sometimes you manufacture those opportunities.

And then something happens. Maybe you’re asked to do the type of thing you’re always supposed to do. Or maybe someone else does it. We know how it goes. We know that some people go along with us because they’re scared, but eventually some of them reach a breaking point and try to cut their strings. That cannot be tolerated. So, one of us, possibly you, makes an example of them in the way we do. Which leaves you to watch as someone you have come to care about is broken.

I’ve gone back and read what I wrote in the _Scotophobia_ upload. Factually, I stand behind it. Tonally, I no longer understand the mindset of the person who wrote it. I envy him, though. He treated all this as an academic exercise. I wish it were.

- Oh, no, he discovered a conscience. The Sixth World isn’t gentle to people who do that.

- X-Prime

I’ll be more direct in this entry. But you have to understand there are things I still can’t say. Truths I have to dance around. Lives are at stake—not just my own, but yes, also my own. Operations cannot just be blown up because I’ve had a change of heart.

After all, the fate of the world possibly remains in the balance.
