## The Basics

The Tiding gets its name from the collective noun for magpies, because that’s what this group is—people who collect bright, shiny things that get their attention for purposes known only to them. Since they are a product of the information age, the shiny objects they collect are bits of information rather than pieces of silver or gold. They are certain, of course, to only collect bits that are valuable.

They did not immediately form an organization, though. Instead, corp workers and runners alike continued with their existing careers, but they kept a careful eye out for any interesting data and started building a secret cache of it. Along with fi nding hidden data, these individuals were also skilled at the intuitive leap, making connections that others might have missed.

The Tiding is an interesting collection of former corp operatives and shadowrunners who share a talent for discovering things they aren’t supposed to know. They formed during the early days of the CFD crisis, when everyone was looking for data on what the new disease was and where it might have come from. **The business model quickly became clear: Gather information, make connections no one else has seen yet, invest wisely, and entertain inquiries from people who want what you know. So, the Tiding was born.**

**They still did not incorporate. Offcially, the Tiding does not exist. Any payments they receive or make are received and made by the individual members. Contracts are made the same way. It’s believed that some members of the Tiding still maintain a “day job,” drawing their salaries from some megacorp or other while discovering and feeding information to their Tiding compatriots.**

**The Tiding’s independence is its advantage—at least, that’s how they sell it, and that’s what their clients believe. They have no megacorporate interest to advance, no business advantage to obtain, or any other conficting interest. They want information, and if that’s what you want, they’ll fnd it for you, free of bias or personal agenda.**

**The Tiding has developed a small list of clients, but those clients are high-rollers, and the Tiding keeps them thanks to their track record. Pick a major recent event, and you better believe they know more about it than they’re letting on. The Blackouts, the Kechibi Code, Dis, the Denver catastrophe, the Spinrad assassination—you name it. That alone, though, is not what makes them special—as I mentioned, their true gift is for connecting various dots. Do they know who ordered the assassination of Johnny Spinrad? Maybe. Do they know how that assassination is connected to the rifts that have been popping up all over the world? Or to the Kechibi Code? Quite possibly.**

**So if you want to try to make sense of all the chaos in the world and ft it into one narrative, the magpies of the Tiding are the people to talk to. Just remember: They won’t share information for free, and they won’t tell you everything.**
