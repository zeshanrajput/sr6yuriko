## Minor Prosthetics

As you probably know, cyberware and bioware that connects to the body reduces Essence. One thing to bear in mind is that this mostly applies to advanced cyberware, the sort that replicates body function. Even non-digital items such as orthoskin or bone lacing have an effect. There are, however, many people in the world who do not have the funds to implant a fully functioning prosthesis, or who do not have access to a trustworthy cyberdoc to complete the procedure. For people who lose limbs or other body parts but still want some utility, a minor prosthesis is one way to get some function. Sometimes a temporary measure, these are also reasonably obvious to anyone who can see them. Examples of minor prostheses are glass eyes, wooden legs, and even the piratical hand-hook. On the downside, these prostheses are not nearly as capable as modern implants. For example, a minor prosthetic hand doesn’t have nearly the dexterity or control that a cyberhand would have, and an attached prosthetic leg doesn’t allow the user to move as fast as either their original leg or an attached bio-implant leg would.

As to _why_ these don’t damage an aura, this is still something that people are exploring. The best explanation so far is that these particular implants are not polished or functional enough to either restore functionality or add something to who you are, as a more advanced cyber- or bioware implant might. This explains the difference between these implants and others, but it has not been rigorously tested, so you might have me here next year with an update in this area.
