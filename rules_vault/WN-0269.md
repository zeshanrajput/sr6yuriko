## detecting the magpies

PCs on overwatch have several ways to potentially see some of the Tiding’s magpies in action. Individuals who are casing the central entryway to the building can spot the physical agents with success on both a Perception + Intuition (2) and Judge Intentions (3) test. Matrix overwatch will have to search for icons running silently per normal rules (p. 182, SR6).

they are and will be circumspect about who they work for, but otherwise they will be amiable, looking for common ground. A single Matrix Asset will approach the team with no Attack utility loaded, wanting to talk. They want to talk to DJ, preferably in a location of their choice, and they will exchange information they think the PCs and Rikki would be very interested in.

The Tiding knows who transported the confused individuals, including Becky Wu Ping, to Central Park. The individual uses the name Gramius, and he’s a technomancer who works occasionally with a company known as Animus Construction. He is still in Manhattan but is lying low. The magpies say Animus operates worldwide, and they say it’s secretive and seems to have a considerable bankroll. They also confirm that DJ has no comprehension of what he has done.

The PCs might have reservations about leaving DJ alone with the Tiding magpies. The magpies will assert that they have no intention of hurting him, which is true, but they also intend to dig around in his brain for a while, and the effects of that are uncertain at best. They would prefer to have no eyes at all on them, but they are also reasonable people who know a person might not be turned over to them, a group of strangers, without question. So they are willing to try to make the PCs comfortable with the arrangement while getting away with as much as they can. Here is what they are willing do, in order of preference:

- Interrogate DJ with no one else around.

- Interrogate DJ while being monitored over a video connection.

- Interrogate DJ with one of the PCs present in the room.

- Interrogate DJ with one of the PCs present in the room and the others in the next room.

The last option is clearly one they don’t want, and they will bargain hard for concessions from the PCs before they allow it. The concession they want the most is to be able to watch the PCs when they act on the information the Tiding is about to give them. Any other valuable information could help persuade them, but the gamemaster should remember what the PCs tell them and be ready to have the Tiding pop up and interfere in future missions.

Whatever deal the PCs come to, the Tiding will learn little they do not know from questioning DJ, other than his mind has been altered by Animus Construction, which they already suspected. The techniques will leave DJ unconscious, and they’re not clear when he’ll wake up.
