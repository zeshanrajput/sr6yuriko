## **>** Baka Dabora

For those living under a rock, pachinko is a game where you buy ball bearings (usually twenty for a nuyen or a hundred for four) and feed them into a machine, flicking each through a small vertical maze, generating more balls with good shots. Each machine has plenty of flashing lights and loud bells, which means the sound of a hundred patrons flicking ball bearings around is nearly deafening. You’ll hear it easily in any nightspot in Japan and internationally anywhere that Japancorps can be found. You can trade ball bearings in at a counter to buy overpriced prizes, like sake, pictures of idols, and so on. Since it’s just prizes and not money, this is technically legal.
