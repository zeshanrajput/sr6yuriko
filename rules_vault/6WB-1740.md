## Drek Vehicles

If you want to know what most of the vehicles in the alternative districts look like, all you have to do is go on an online image search for “rat car” or “rat bike.” The typical Berlin drek car either has the rusted chassis of some vehicle made before 2060, or is a recognizable patchwork that is just somehow meant to keep the acid rain off. The undercarriage and suspension are rock solid, impervious to potholes, and easy to repair. Such drek cars may raise alarms or be denied access in Berlin’s corporate and norm districts due to a lack of autopilot and equally lacking ALI system (the German equivalent of GridGuide), but they are the unmistakable standard of locomotion in the city’s anarchic zones. Certain scrap vehicles such as rat bikes, e-rickshaws, and tuk-tuks are tolerated outside the zones nevertheless, as they pose no real threat to traffic.
