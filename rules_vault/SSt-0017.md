**Shotguns. Coming in either pump-action or semi-auto models, shotguns are heavy-hitting close-range weapons that use either shot, fechette, or solid slug rounds. Their simple and rugged design allows them to easily load specialty rounds.**

**Hold-Out Pistols. Designed for maximum concealability and use at close ranges, hold-outs are good when your concealment options are limited, or as a backup to other frearms. Their general lack of ammo capacity and overall power keeps their uses limited. But specialty ammo types (such as capsule rounds) greatly increase their utility.**

**Sporting Rif es. Generally used for hunting, sporting rifes pack a large punch and are accurate at long distances. Their ammo capacity and rate of fre tend to be limited, and their lower price compared to sniper rifes makes them viable options when funds are short.**

**Sniper Rif es. Designed to put accurate fre on target at extreme ranges, sniper rifes are often paired with advanced optics and sensors. A poor choice at short range, sniper rifes are often large and fragile when compared to other weapon types.**

**Light Pistols. A good blend of concealability, frepower, and ammo capacity; solid general performers that can be modifed and customized. They have better range than holdouts.**

**Heavy Pistols. Designed frst and foremost with close combat in mind, heavy pistols are more powerful with greater ammo capacities, often at the expense of concealability. Heavy pistols are favored when concealability isn’t a factor and are popular as a primary personal carry or as a backup for larger weapons.**

**Enhanced Battle Rif es. EBRs straddle the line between assault and sniper rifes. Often slightly bigger and more powerful than assault rifes, EBRs typically have slightly less ammo capacity. EBRs can also operate at ranges shorter than sniper rifes but ofer burst-fre capabilities. These are efective as long-range fre support that moves with a unit.**

**Machine Pistols. Burst or sometimes full-auto capable, machine pistols can send higher volumes of pistol-caliber rounds downrange. As such, they tend to have larger ammo capacity but may be less accurate when compared to other handguns.**

**Machine Guns. Coming in light, medium, and heavy variants, MGs are designed to put large volumes of fre down range. Light MGs often serve as person-portable weapons for small units, while medium and heavy machine guns are often used as emplacement or vehicle-mounted weapons.**

**Submachine Guns. SMGs fulfl the same function as machine pistols at the same ranges, but typically with much better accuracy. Their compact size make them ideal for use in tight quarters such as hallways, on ships, or inside vehicles.**

**Assault Cannons. These single-shot weapons are basically metahuman-scaled, person-portable artillery guns. They fre large, high-velocity rounds designed to brute-force targets into submission.
