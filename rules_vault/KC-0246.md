## U.N. Commission on Megacorporate Affairs

You’re laughing. I can tell that you’re laughing. Who the hell is the UN Commission on Megacorporate Affairs? Once upon a time they were pretty powerful—they were one of the ways that nations used to influence and keep the megacorps in line. Glorious bureaucrats striding forth to do battle with the consumption-only megas, and occasionally winning a few battles. Of course, constant bribery of UN officials and members of the commission, as well as just ignoring the UNCOMA, quickly drained its power and authority. These days, the commission has no teeth to enforce its mandate and no power to enforce international law, so it might as well not exist. Just recently, though, there has been a change in leadership. Someone who is experienced and savvy has taken over the top spot in this tiny agency, and it looks like they have an axe to grind.

So why are they looking for the code? I’m pretty sure it’s spite. If the commission can’t enforce its rulings on the corps through law, then maybe the downtrodden and underfunded department can enforce their orders through illegal code. It could actually work, in theory, if the corp that had the lowest compliance experienced several financial misfortunes and suddenly had a lot less money. That might encourage those who remain to follow the rules. On the other hand, if a rule-following corp gets a bump in money, it could be a carrotand-stick thing.

I, personally, think the UNCOMA is looking to ambush a hellhound with a cardboard sword. Sure, if they pulled it off, it would be a hell of a surprise, but every mega lives its life striving against other megas, which are ten times as vicious, petty, and avaricious as the UNCOMA. You can’t just walk into a pit fight with a good weapon and expect to walk out with your arms still attached. History has proven that the only thing that a megacorp fears is other megacorps, and then, only when they band together.

Along with questions about their exact end goals, people are asking about who is in charge at UNCOMA. If it’s someone who is familiar with corporate politics, perhaps a recently departed (or scapegoated) executive, or someone who has worked in the shadows for years, then maybe they have an angle that could help their plan succeed. Someone who has defected from the leadership team of the megacorps, and who has chosen revenge over money, could be a real danger for the corps they left behind.

What does it mean for you? It probably means another over-confident shadow-player is about to go down, and there is a lot of money to be made when that sort of thing happens. There is also the chance that you could get in on the ground floor of something big. Be aware that if you work for this group, and it turns out to be very effective, you could be burning your contacts in other organizations, even more than normal.
