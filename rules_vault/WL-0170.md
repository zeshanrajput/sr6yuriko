## **Stymphalian**

**The stymphalian (** _**Ardea foedus**_ **) is a rare sight in the marshes of Spain, southern France, Italy, and Greece. Stymphalians are the Awakened cousins of the purple heron (** _**Ardeidae purpurea**_ **). The stymphalian stands nearly three meters tall and averages eight kilograms in weight. The largest specimens have a wingspan measuring up to 700 centimeters. The bird has black feathers on its body and grey and red plumage on its wings, with “arrow feathers” that are yellowish-brown calcifed contour feathers. The stymphalian’s serrated, rust-colored beak reaches a length of forty centimeters, and each of its four-toed feet has retractable talons. The stymphalian packs a toxic punch with its breath.**

**Like many others in the heron family, the stymphalians are carnivorous hunters. They feed on small- to medium-sized mammals and reptiles living in the wetlands but can supplement their diet with fsh and frogs. The typical family unit size for stymphalians is ten to twenty-fve adults and juveniles. They are very social and form hunting groups with a minimum of three members. The hunting groups work in concert by fying high above the region searching for prey. Once spotted, they spread out to surround the prey, cutting off all possible avenues of escape.**

**The stymphalian has several ways to take down its prey. The calcifed yellowish-brown feathers are colloquially called arrow feathers because they can be fred from a distance, although it takes roughly a week for the feathers to regrow. In addition to the arrow feathers, they have vicious claws and a heavy serrated beak to take down their prey. The**



**noxious breath can also be used to stun animals. In the end, the rabbits and fsh don’t stand a chance against a hungry fock of stymphalians.**

**Stymphalians are rarely known to attack metahumans unless provoked by entering their territory or coming too close to their nesting location. However, a major worry is that the birds are particularly aggressive once they start attacking and will continue until they kill the victim. A key defense for folks who live near their hunting grounds is to use loud sounds to drive the birds away. They are very sensitive to sound, having a threshold of pain at around 100 decibels.**

**Stymphalian nests are made of sticks and after mating season will typically contain two to four pale red eggs. The typical incubation time for these eggs is twenty-fve days. Once the fedglings hatch, both parents care for the young by regurgitating partially digested fsh and meat for them.**
