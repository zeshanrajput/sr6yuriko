## Elements

One of the best things about this con is that it simply requires time and maybe a couple hundred nuyen in frontage. The time is spent in observation, figuring out patrol patterns, mainly. If you can get some inside info on the outfit in question, and if you know the different protocols at work, you can be much more effective in applying the second element.

The next part is setting up the “evidence.” Simulate pressure, observation, subversion. Small scatterings of cigarette butts, fast food wrappers, the detritus you leave behind if you’ve been on a stakeout for days on end and get sloppy. Strike a balance between tidy pilesand being too spread out to be noticed. It has to look natural.

Your knowledge about the security is going to play a part in how much you can press them directly. We’re talking about point-defense units as opposed to regularly rotated large staffs, more akin to bodyguards and personal property patrols. If they are private, rather than a large corporate contractor, you have a higher chance of success. Pressure is applied indirectly, giving the security detail ambiguous threats or payments, taking small pains to make sure word gets back to the mark/client. Handled correctly, the staff you approach is either going to go to the client and report what they saw, or they’ll keep it to themselves taking the bribes/ threats, and the client sees it on surveillance. The result is them wondering which of their detail they can trust.
