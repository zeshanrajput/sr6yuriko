## Efficiency and Fuel Mods (Increasing Operational Time)

The baseline operating time of a vehicle is six hours—that’s for everything from crawling microdrones to cargo airplanes. It doesn’t matter if it’s batteries powering electric motors, a biodiesel ICM turning a generator, or straight-up antique carbureted liquid fuel internal combustion (although it’s probably modified to burn a synthetic fuel blend). Increasing operational time means adding more energy storage or increasing the efficiency of the motor.

These are actually two different ways to achieve the exact same result: extending the operational time of a vehicle. With this modification, the fuel consumption of a vehicle is optimized to use less, or the fuel capacity has been expanded with a larger tank of higher capacity batteries (or maybe both). Either way, each rank of this mod added to a vehicle increases the operational time of the vehicle by an additional 33 percent of the base value (normally, that’s an extra two hours). Mods that use extra power also reduce this extra capacity at the same rate.
