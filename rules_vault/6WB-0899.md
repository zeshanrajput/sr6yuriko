## Rigging and Dumpshock

Unfortunately, hackers also know the uses of the Reboot Device action, and they have been known to gain control of a PAN just long enough to forcibly reboot it, thus expelling a jumped-in rigger. And that’s not the only way to force a rigger out—bricking the device they’re using for remote commands, whether it’s a commlink or RCC, or destroying the vehicle a rigger is jumped into expels the rigger. And if you go old-school and directly plug into a vehicle or drone to run it, and someone yanks the plug— boom, you get dumpshock, as described on p. 176. You also lose control of the vehicle, and it continues on whatever course its was on until someone takes control or something stops it. If it has an autopilot, that will guide the device, except when it reboots.
