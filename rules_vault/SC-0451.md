## How It Came To Pass

The shadows have only known about social adepts since 2064. The world around them has taken even longer to recognize and accept them. However, that acceptance came rapidly because of their immense utility and versatility to all of those in power who need and want social adepts’ abilities. That has sparked a race to catch up and deploy this archetype in roles that would make them most profitable and politically significant. A character’s age plays a significant role in how, when, and to what extent they developed their stats and personalities. Indeed, older characters very likely grew up and may have started their adult lives without full awareness of their abilities, or even that they were magically active.

Magical testing by corps and developed governments is imperfect—and even less reliable than normal when attempting to recognize social adepts. Additionally, the nature of most social adept abilities means there are only rudimentary systems or institutions dedicated to their training. Given the scope of some of their skills, it’s difficult to describe just what these adepts can do. Think of the most charismatic politician or con artist you can. Powerful, trained social adepts can routinely accomplish feats those individuals achieved in their greatest moments. The greatest practical restrictions on social adepts, and what makes them so desired, is that they are still people who are limited by their other stats, contacts, connections, experiences, and general nature. As in real life, there is training in _Shadowrun_ that can replicate and provide practical support for “soft” skills and techniques related to leadership, negotiation, and cons. However, most of the training can only be measured effectively on the job. The threshold to increase one’s skills in leadership, for example, isn’t just a matter of Karma expenditure. From a role-playing perspective, a starting character who has the same effect as Lincoln giving the Gettysburg Address _every time they speak_ has to be that much better to improve even marginally as they progress.

If the character is a lifer in one of the worlds listed below, then it will be reflected in their skills, abilities, and nature. The larger institutions had a lead on the others to recruit and exploit social adepts once they gained awareness of them. Intelligence agencies saw them as the ideal spies. Media found them even more useful than their usual stables of stars and mouthpieces.

Others saw them as threats. Even as the diamond anniversary of the Awakening approaches, many people still fear and distrust magic, or they see it as cheating even when there are technological methods of performing many of the same feats that social adepts perform.
