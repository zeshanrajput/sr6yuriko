## **Standard-Issue Carry**

**The last things I wanted to go over in regard to standard kit are weapons. Every cop is trained in some form of close-quarters combat with emphasis on submission holds and subject compliance. Certain LEPs teach a proprietary form of martial arts to their offcers, and it’s often considered a bonus on a LEP contract. Bottom line, each cop is trained to hold their own in a fst fght and to turn a decent-sized goon into a screaming mess. For everything else, there’s the “Force Continuum.”**

**Basically, it’s a spectrum of how much force to apply in a certain situation, up to and including lethal force. The average beat cop certainly carries weapons—sorry “defensive tools”—to ft these needs, and sometimes a few more depending on what they can get away with.**

**> Before anyone says anything, know that the force continuum is not a step-by-step guide, as in they have to start with one, then go to the next, and so on. Nope, the force continuum is more of a legal tool used to justify what level of force an officer may need to employ. And the continuum helps articulate why they needed to go that level, even right to lethal force, and why that action was “reasonable.”**
