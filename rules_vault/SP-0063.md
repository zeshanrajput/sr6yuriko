## **New European Economic Community**

**Yes, Saeder-Krupp’s the dominant power in this body, but its membership is a list of people who are partnered with S-K, at least to a degree. This is not to say they all dance to S-K’s tune by any means—Spinrad Global, Maersk, Tír na nÓg, and Esprit-Dassault are hardly in S-K’s hip pocket, and others, such as Erika, Zeta-ImpChem and the Frankfurt Bank Association, have allied against him. But Lofwyr has historically played a strong role in setting the NEEC’s overall agenda, which means he is dictating terms, and others are left to just react. One of the major accomplishments of the NEEC—and one of the reasons Lofwyr has earned so much deference— is that they’ve made it diffcult for outsiders to have a strong impact in the European economy. They may have disputes about how the Euro-**

**pean economic pie is divided, but they have to admit that Lofwyr has helped ensure that they’re the ones who get to divide it.**

**Some theories—and they’re not bad ones—say that some of Saeder-Krupp’s foot-dragging on the Dis issue is an effort to gain more leverage within the NEEC. Obviously, Tír na nÓg, with its Seelie Court connections, would like Saeder-Krupp on their side. The Eurocrat faction of the NEEC, on the other hand, is pretty distant from this whole thing and would prefer to pretend it doesn’t exist, which means they don’t want any assets going to fght it. Lofwyr will likely play either side off the other and see what he can gain from the deal.**
