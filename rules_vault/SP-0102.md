## **The Interception**

**As a hotbed of intrigue, the Seelie Court is naturally full of people looking to intercept each other’s communications. Ever since the opening of the Yellowstone Anomaly, an arms race has been underway to either hack or protect the** _**coimeádaí,**_ **the ensorcelled people the Court uses as vessels for information. Attempts to hack** _**coimeádaí**_ **are made so regularly that it would be simple to plant some message in one that purports to have some Dis-related intelligence that the Court should act on.**

**The trick is to make this intelligence seem genuine. This technique is not novel, though, so intelligence offcers are perpetually on the lookout for junk messages purporting to be what they are not. The Black Lodge, though, is as skilled at subterfuge as any organization in the world, so serving up messages that authentically look like what they are supposed to be is not a problem for them.**

- **Let’s take a moment to appreciate the level of art on display here. We could take this at face value and say, hey, great, someone who knows about the Black Lodge has come to our private message board to share some inside information that will stay just between the members here. But we all know that information has a way of traveling, no matter how secure we think we are. Soon, operatives at the Seelie Court will get word that the Black Lodge is very confident that they could use coimeádaí to carry messages that look authentic but are not. And just like that, the Court is going to suspect that any intercepted intelligence they gain that looks authentic might be information forged by the Black Lodge to look authentic. And paranoia and uncertainty set in, which are things the Black Lodge loves.**
