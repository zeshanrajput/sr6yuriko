## Technology Level

**None (Tooth and Claw):** There is no tool use on this metaplane at all—even the idea of picking up a sharp rock is beyond the imagination of the residents. Technological items brought into the metaplane will still work, but the metaplanar

translation may be extreme. If the mana is high enough, gear will manifest as a feature of the travelers, weapons become claws or tail-thrown barbs, and scales replace body armor. Other gear may be represented as small animals in the company of the traveler or expressed as some innate magical ability. There is no organized data processing or data transfer possible at this level of technology, as written language does not exist.

**Stonepunk:** Tool use is limited to crudely shaped stones, with trees and branches or animal bones and skins, held together with sinew or maybe vine ropes. Refined metals and any power source beyond muscle power—or animal power —do not exist here. At low mana levels this may resemble Earth’s Stone Age, but as magic increases, the neolithic technology becomes more fantastic. Stonepunk with high mana allows for self-rolling carts, trained animals, and overall more efficiencies and labor-savings than would be expected. Creative magic use may allow for some type of information technology and even a Matrix equivalent. This could be based on memory crystals and seeing stones or some other form of magic-based information-transfer system.

**Swordpunk:** Refined metals can be found here, but the sources of power are limited to windmills, waterwheels, and muscle. As the mana level increases, this level of technology takes on aspects of the traditional sword and sorcery fantasy genre.
