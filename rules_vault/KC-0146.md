## Caleb Robertson

Caleb was formerly known as Tiku Agebwe. He is looking to move up the Ares hierarchy and sees solving this bit of unrest and trouble as a ticket to go help out his home while advancing his new life in Atlanta. He’s a twenty-seven-year-old male ork who spent his first fifteen years of life on the hard streets of Lagos. He was an Area Boy who saved an Ares arms dealer (Robert Hathaway) during

a sale gone bad. The dealer brought him into the Ares fold as private security. Life inside Ares was tough at first, but he convinced Robert to let him change his name to Caleb Robertson and spent half a year watching as many hours of CAS trid as he could in order to minimize his local accent. Caleb excelled at keeping street connections and making sure Hathaway was safe. Eventually Hathaway got transferred, and Caleb took over. Despite this, he managed to keep his finger on Lagos’ pulse. After several possible exposures of Ares’ business dealings by Okoro, local management has tasked Caleb with handling that situation rather than making arms deals. Behind the scenes, management thinks it will lead to Caleb taking the fall for Ares’ shady activities, but Caleb thinks this is his chance to get promoted.

The illegal (and environmentally disastrous) production and distribution of arms is a giant target for Okoro and one of the easiest for her to prove and prosecute. She pulled together terapulses of data on their operations and continues her efforts, meaning the runners will definitely be looking in the same arenas for a datatrail. Once the runners are involved, Caleb will approach them with a counteroffer rather than a direct assault. The offer should be a little less than what the team is getting paid for intel gathering to make it worth their while but not overly lucrative. The meeting also comes with blustering and threats that the money is their best option, as the other path leads to “unpleasant engagements with local megacorporations.” He won’t identify himself directly, but his dress and mannerisms should offer enough clues for the runners to see Ares behind the curtain.

If the runners refuse the offer, Caleb goes into guerrilla-warfare mode. He mentions the runners to street connections and smears their names, making it tough for them to operate in the city (cabs don’t stop, lodgings are full, taxes and bribes are higher, contacts aren’t as open with info, etc.). Though the runners may expect a direct assault from Ares, they instead get some social warfare (at least at first). During this, Caleb gets info from his connections on how the runners react. He finds out who’s the most aggressive, who can use magic, who keeps out of it, who does the hacking, etc. The answers all feed into his later efforts, which bring focused attacks on each member. This means spirits coming to attack the Awakened, focusing on active spells and using powers on them rather than a brute-force attack. A team of deckers will go after the team’s hacker and rigger. A group of toughs will pick a fight with one of the team’s more combat-oriented characters, doing their best to get them at an isolated moment like in a restroom (this may need to be orchestrated slyly, as runners rarely mention going to the washroom).



Caleb does not have an infinite supply of Ares goons to call on, but he has a decent bit of pull with some local talent and a good supply of cash thanks to being an arms dealer.

His street connections are all regular people who take a couple naira to just add a bit of struggle to the runners’ day, but they are everywhere.

He’ll hire four mages to summon spirits (spirit of kin, Force 4, Innate Spell: Decrease Attribute) and send them after the team’s magic assets. He’ll contract a trio of deckers (use Cutters Data Harvester, p. 205, _SR6_ ) to go after the team’s electronics specialists. They’ll focus on sneak attacks on low-end tech before coming down hard once some real cybercombat ensues. The street tough attacks will consist of eight Area Boys (use Sons of Sauron Brute, p. 205, _SR6_ ; add AK-97). Some of the Area Boys will attempt to grapple or focus on a defensive style to fight one-on-one if they can catch someone alone. If a runner manages to get cornered in a bathroom or some other spot, a few thugs will stay back to block the door while the others give a beatdown (or make a go at it and get beat down themselves).

If all of these deterrents don’t push the runners off this investigation, Caleb will make a second effort to buy them off, but an attack will be waiting in the wings. Caleb will invite them to a market for the meeting, and if they refuse him again, he’ll walk away and call his forces hiding in the crowd and nearby buildings.
