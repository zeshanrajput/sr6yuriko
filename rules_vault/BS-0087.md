## Morphing Fingerprints (Rating 1-6)

Much like retinal duplication cyberware, morphing fingerprints were designed for use by intelligence services and are always illegal outside of such agencies. Morphing fingerprints replace the fingertips of one hand with lifelike synthetic skin that uses myoelectric fibers to alter the fingerprint patterns. Any fingerprint verification systems roll their rating in an Opposed test against the morphing fingerprints rating to detect the falsified biometrics.

**Cyberware overdrive:** Gain +2 dice, one of which must be a wild die, on Opposed tests to fool a fingerprint biometric verification system.

**Wireless bonus:** Morphing fingerprints can adopt a pattern downloaded from the Matrix or a biometric reader.

The user can change their apparent skin tone, with the tone naturally darkening or lightening as appropriate to the part of the body. Hues beyond metahuman norms are possible, but the artificial nature of the coloration is immediately apparent. As with other electrochromic technology, it costs a Minor Action to change the current display but it takes two combat rounds for the transformation to complete. Skin toner cannot be combined with other skin augmentations.

A chameleon processor uses complex algorithms to enhance the coarse coloration. When mated to a chameleon processor, skin toner can broadcast more complex patterns and even use multiple colors rather than gradients of one. Tiger, leopard, zebra, Holstein cow, and many similar skin patterns are possible. The chameleon processor also permits the user to adopt a variety of camouflage patterns. The chameleon processor grants bonus Edge for Stealth tests while the user is camouflaged and most of their skin is visible.

**Wireless bonus:** Skin toner can download new colors and patterns via the Matrix by spending a Minor Action.
