## Other Animals

If you need a stat block for an animal that is not provided, either use the stat block for a similar animal, modify an existing stat block, or make one up yourself. For example, if you need a stat block for a raccoon, you might just use the stat block for a dog. If you’d like to represent a particularly large and dangerous grizzly bear, use the stat block for a bear, but add +2 to its Body and Strength, which also adds +1 DV to its natural weapon attack.

When it comes to making up your own stat blocks, just pick numbers that make sense to you. Most physical attributes are 6 or less, though Body and Strength are the most common exceptions to this. For mental attributes, a Willpower of 2 or 4 is common in animals, Logic is almost never higher than 2, Intuition ranges from 2 to 4, and Charisma rarely exceeds 3. All unaugmented animals start with an Essence attribute of 6, and they do not possess an Edge attribute. If an animal would gain a point of Edge on a test, it uses the Edge immediately to gain an extra die on that test.

For skills, all critters will have Athletics, Close Combat, Outdoors, and Perception. Predators tend to have a high Close Combat skill and a specialization in Perception, and common prey tend to have a high Stealth skill. Some animals have an Influence skill with a specialization in Intimidation. If you aren’t sure how many ranks to assign to each skill, go with 4 for average, and either increase or decrease that number by 1 or 2 based on your impression of whether or not the animal you are thinking of would be exceptionally good or bad at that skill.

More critter and animal stat blocks can be found in the **Wild Life** chapter in the _Shadowrun, Sixth World_ core rulebook, starting on page 215. _Wild Life,_ a critter sourcebook, will be published following this book, and will include many more critters.
