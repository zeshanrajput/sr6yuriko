If you want to see where the real magic happens, however, look no further than Bourbon Street. Sure, it’s a tourist trap, and you might catch the New Orleans locals arguing about who hates this part of the city the most, but don’t let that fool you. Behind the neon lights, throbbing music, and fl ashing AR advertisements, some of the oldest magic on this side of the world is here. You’ll fi nd plenty of tourist “voodoo shops” alongside the bars and corner stores, but real Vodou is hidden in the back shops and alleyways. Everything from fetishes to pre-Awakening artifacts that have a tinge of real magic in them.

It’s also home to Mardi Gras and the CAS Decadence Festival. Horizon capitalized on this by opening their very own club, Fleur de Lis—an expansion of their Orchid chain that caters heavily to these decadent celebrations wedged between the Midnight Flowers and Beautiful Dreams Bordellos.

An important place to know if you’re a shadowrunner or criminal is going to be Old Riverwalk. Not to be confused with New Riverwalk in downtown, this is the original deal that was closed down just as magic returned to the world. The offi cial line is that the increase in vagrancy and violence is to blame, but everyone knows the real story. Critters, vampire covens, ghoul hives, free spirits, and everything in between made it home, and everyone else booked it out.

- The Infected feel safe here, and even what’s left of the old vampire hunter guilds usually leave them alone after they saved the city from the shedim.

The CBD, south of the French Quarter, is where you go after you’ve already sacrifi ced the fi rst half of your life to the corporations. Once you’re solidly indoctrinated enough to be able to travel the Quarter without accidentally gaining a soul, you just might get promoted to work in the skyscrapers of the CBD.

There’s stuff here to enjoy. After all, the big corps need to sell this as your endgame—the wealth and comfort you get for being a loyal wageslave for decades of faithful service. New Orleans’ oldest theaters, most beautiful hotels, and the Superdome are all located here. It’s not worth selling your soul to live close to it, but if you’re a shadowrunner, there’s no avoiding that you’re eventually going to meet with Mr. Johnson at a fancy theater here.

Entregy Tower is also located here, now owned by Mitsuhama. Corporate citizens come from the world over to vacation here and look down at the rest of the city, and high-powered executives host their grandiose meetings here. More locally, it serves as an ideal recruiting center for young artists and magicians. They run their entertainment division and magical departments for the entire CAS out of here.

The culture here is the closest you’ll fi nd to a middle ground between the suits and everyone else. Sculptures are commissioned for the street. There’s plenty of fake corp-sponsored graffi ti with just enough color to almost fool you. Look a bit longer, however, and you’ll see just how safe the color choices are, how inoffensive the art is, and how re-sellable all the buildings are. Most of the wageslaves don’t even speak the local lingo, favoring corptaught “proper” English and French instead of Patwa (the local cityspeak).

- Hanibelle
