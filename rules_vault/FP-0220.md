**knowledge, but they have more than that. They know how to put two pieces of knowledge together, fnd connections, and make a third.**

**We all do this sort of thing all the time. You see a car speed past you on the street, then a minute later you see a police car, lights blazing, pass by. You connect the two and assume the police car is going after the speeder. Most of the time, you’re right.**

**Sometimes, you nail it on other connections. A company has a policy of not auditing employee expenses under 200 nuyen. One employee regularly submits expenses in the 190 to 199 nuyen range. That employee is very possibly submitting a bunch of fake expenses. The husband of a corp executive has a standing hair appointment every other Thursday at 3 p.m. One particularly attractive worker takes lunch at 1 p.m. most days, but she takes it at 3 every other Thursday. She’s probably meeting the husband for some reason or another, and that reason is likely not legit. Those are plausible connections.**

**Unfortunately, this doesn’t always work. Sometimes we see patterns that aren’t real or connect things that aren’t actually connected. The Kechibi code was used for all sorts of fnancial manipulation, tanking some stock prices while making others soar. Some people bought and sold stocks at the right time because they were taking advantage of the code. Others just happened to be lucky. Assuming anyone who made money was defnitely connected to the code and anyone who lost money was targeted by people unleashing the code is a sucker’s game. There are just too many traders for there to always be a direct connection. If you see someone making money off of Kechibi-infuenced stocks, that may be a sign that you should dig a little more into them, but that’s as much as you should assume.**

**This gets us to the subject of epiphany versus apophany. An epiphany is knowing there is a virus invading people’s minds, and that it seems to be related to AIs, knowing the Evo and NeoNET were experimenting on AIs, and understanding the two might be connected. You made a connection that helped you discover the truth.**

**An apophany is when you make a connection that isn’t real. You think you’ve discovered a truth, but you’ve only discovered illusion. In my experience, the average runner team has at least fve apophanies per run. They see a shady character near where they met Mr. Johnson and fgure the two know each other. A security guard and a mid-level executive have the same last name, and they convince themselves they’re related. And so on.**

**Runners leap to those connections because there was one time they were right, and it saved their life. The only cost of being wrong, they fgure, is they were a little extra cautious and did a little extra legwork, and what’s the harm in that?**

**So they indulge their bad ideas, and hopefully it doesn’t get to the point where they become one of those runners who takes two days to scout out each and every location they plan to go to and is paralyzed by caution.**

**The Tiding can’t afford apophanies. Their reputation depends on their information being solid. They need to tell people things no one else knows, and they can’t be wrong. That means they don’t just make those connections and hope they’re right. They make connections, then research like hell to confrm they’re legit. They’re also sure to do it fast, because information doesn’t always age well.**

**> Care to share an example of their ability to make connections? > Winterhawk**

- **Sure. Ryoko Iseri has deep knowledge of Mitsuhama from her time there. She knew MCT was buying a reagent research firm in Ethiomalian Territories—Qalabka Enchanting. She also knew that Mitsuhama Magical Services had just endowed a chair in Applied Enchanting at Cairo University. Separately, she learned of a package making its way through Sheba’s government about the care and handling of Awakened critters. She put it all together and determined that Mitsuhama was undertaking a major effort to funnel Awakened critters, especially those whose body parts can be useful reagents, from Ethiomalia and Sheba through Cairo. Her early jump on this helped her spy on some of MCT’s activities in Sheba, helping her learn some of the latest Awakened critter training techniques, and also led her to invest in some smaller animal-handling supply companies in the area, whose stock then did well in the next few years thanks to MCT’s investment.**
