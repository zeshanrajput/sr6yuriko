## Law Enforcement 

Omaha is a Lone Star town, but their secret weapon was their close relationship with the Offutt military police. Crimes that took place on the base were a small percent of overall sprawl crime, even less than the share of sprawl population the base represented. But the base police had valuable functions to provide beyond that. When crimes involving soldiers happened off-Base, the MPs would be involved, lightening the burden on Lone Star. They also gave significant assistance on some larger initiatives, such as anti-smuggling activities and unlicensed magic use. With the military police gone, Lone Star lost much of its capacity to be proactive. 

The bigger worry is losing their funding. Their contract, like many municipal contracts, is population-based, so the out-migration of 

citizens has led to some officers being laid off. Some have been transferred to other cities, but others are completely out of a job and trying to figure out what to do next. 

Even those who still have a job have some difficulty figuring out what happens next. The weaker the government gets, the more Lone Star’s authority dissolves. In other cities, the might of the megacorporations serves as a kind of backbone to governmental authority, at least when it comes to enforcing the law on the plebes. Without that, they’re left with some uncertainty about how to carry out their job. How heavy-handed should they be? Should they have any areas of emphasis? The higher ranks have been flailing a little, and that’s had an effect on morale across the force. If the government has trouble paying them, morale will rapidly get worse. What they do after that is unknown.
