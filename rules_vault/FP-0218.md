**They build up and rely on sources: As they work on a target, the Tiding builds an array of contacts wherever they go. Their goal is to continue communication with these people, even after formal operations have concluded. For this reason, the Tiding now has a couple of full-time people who do nothing but maintain communication with existing contacts. These handlers adopt an array of identities, generally impersonating the Tiding magpie who initially made the contact. The goal is to be ready in case they need more information, which leads to the next point.**

**They’re never really done: With the contacts they make, the cameras and bugs they leave behind, and the Matrix back doors waiting for them to use, the Tiding leaves a continual fow of information behind them. Even if they found a particular piece of information they were looking for, they don’t just pack up and move on. They keep up with the contacts, they keep listening, and they keep watching (though not with human eyes— that’s what bots and algorithms are for). You might think that the Tiding’s ultimate goal is to have ears in every sensitive room in the world, and you could well be right.**

**They use weapons primarily as a deterrence: Tiding magpies usually go into just about any situation with some sort of personal weaponry, but mainly because that’s how the Sixth World is. They do all they can to avoid using it, because bloodshed tends to bring unwanted attention. When things get tense, they’ll try to talk their way out of it, then fee if possible, and only pull out the guns if nothing else works. Usually, they don’t get to that point.**
