## Magic City 

The south part of the city, and one that does not really live up to its historical nickname. It got that name thanks to its rapid, stockyards-inspired growth in the late nineteenth century, and it’s been through a lot since then. Meatpacking jobs dropped severely in the mid-twentieth century, but the area survived and built a more diverse economy. The 2060s were unkind to it, though—Crash 2.0 was hard on everyone, but Magic City suffered an additional blow when an F-5 tornado touched down and carved a path along Q Street that furthered the devastation. After that blow, the city focused post-Crash reconstruction efforts on areas not as desperately broken, and serious disinvestment settled in to Magic City. There are still some areas in reasonable repair, especially as you get closer to Offutt Air Force Base to the southeast, but a distinct barrens quality has been spreading in the central part of the neighborhood. NORAD’s departure from Offutt is likely to accelerate this trend. 

Barrens have some distinct advantages in runner culture, and in Magic City, **L Street** is where you’ll find them, especially near Hitchcock Park. A few bars and restaurants manage to do decent business here—the **Seed and Feed** restaurant fills the need for runner-friendly dining (and has the best trog cuisine in the sprawl), while nearby **Winter Quarters** provides your late-night dive-bar services.
