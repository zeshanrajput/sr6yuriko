## Gambler

You know how to up the ante to gain a potential advantage, but luck doesn’t always have your back.

**Cost:** 10 Karma

**Game Effect:** Whenever you roll a test that includes one or more Wild dice, you may spend a point of Edge to re-roll the Wild die. If you rolled multiple Wild dice, you may re-roll as many of them as you like, but you must spend one Edge per Wild die. This does not count as your one available Edge expenditure on the test, you may still use Edge as normal to influence the results even when you use this quality.

If you don’t like the result of the Wild die, you may re-roll it again for another point of Edge. You may do this as many times as you like—provided you have the Edge to spend, and you’re willing to accept the greater risk.

The more you gamble, the riskier it gets. The first time you re-roll the Wild die, it cancels all 5s on a roll of 1 or 2 (instead of just one). This range increases by one for each re-roll, ignoring 5s on a result of 1

to 3 on the second re-roll, and 1 to 4 on a third reroll. On a fourth re-roll, only a result of 6 will avoid canceling the 5s rolled. After this, it doesn’t get any riskier for further re-roll, just more costly.

Note that normally re-rolling Wild dice doesn’t change the wild part of their result—this quality is an exception to that. When you re-roll Wild dice using the Gambler quality, you completely ignore any previously rolled result (including having 5s not count) and use the re-rolled result instead.
