## Using Nanite Colonies

Nanite colonies are volumes of assembled nanites built against a colony template. These colonies can be built by nanohives or can be transient colonies injected into the body. The colonies are controlled by either the nanohive, if one is installed, or by a commlink running a nanite pilot program. Having multiple colonies of the same template but with different volumes will combine their total volume, which is still limited to six. Combined volumes higher than six become uncontrolled, and any volume over six is ignored.

Colony templates include both the build instruction and the nanite pilot software. While injected nanites are ready to use within a few minutes, nanohives need time to generate the volume of nanites for the colony from precursor compounds. A nanohive can load any number of different templates, but the maximum number of active nanite colonies is limited to the rating of the nanohive. For transient colonies, the number of colonies that a pilot program can control is one.

Nanite volume comes into play in a variety of ways, and these are listed in each colony’s description. Many types of nanite colonies require a minimum volume to function, typically to maintain a nanostructure. Additional volume past this can be added to these structures, but with no additional effect other than creating system redundancies allowing for overdrive losses without degrading the basic functions of the nanostructure. Many types of nanites actively use up nanites to produce an effect, causing the nanite volume of a colony to drop.
