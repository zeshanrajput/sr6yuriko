## Dovv

At the cardinal pole (see p. 37) of earth and water, the city of Dovv gives the impression of sandcastles built on an ocean of mud. The elementals here quickly gather up mud to build intricate houses on basalt foundations. On the water side of Dovv, it’s perpetually dark, with volatile ocean frothing at the edge of the hemisphere. To the earth side, there is perpetual light and a jagged cliff that is slowly collapsing into the mud twenty meters below. One large basalt column refuses to budge from the slow push of the cliff’s march toward the sea. It is here that the community of Dovv has built a simple temple to contemplate the cycles of life and death. Dovv is the quietest place of all the cardinal poles, as there are no great trade routes here. It’s more a place for pilgrims to contemplate the greater pattern.
