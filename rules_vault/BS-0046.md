## The Counts

After the Dukes are the Counts, players in the market who are usually focused in only one small area, or thinly in many, and who aren’t a threat to the crown. That said, they struggle against the Dukes more than each other, always searching for that elusive Thing that can serve as the breakthrough to carry them higher or, at worst, keep them in the dance.

**OmniStar,** particularly in the **DocWagon** branch, produces little on their own, but is a major purchaser of cyber and the leading name in implantation. They get limbs, eyes, and ears in bulk discounts and pass the high cost on to the consumer, upcharging the insurance in the process.

**Universal Omnitech** was once at the cusp of greatness, but when they broke with Aztechnology, they lost market access in much of the world. They later cut their cyberarm to the bone, focusing on first bioware, then genetics, always trying to stay at the forefront of the game.

**MCT** has a core focus on rigger adaptations and radio transmission gear. They also do good work in non-metahuman senses, adapting their vehicular sensors to bodies not designed for them.

**Shiawase** has always been a big name in headware, but over the past decade, they’ve grown more holistic, moving away from cybernetics to focus on bioware. They still make amazing datajacks.

**Horizon** wraps us up, another headware-focused manufacturer, but their skillwire network broke whole new fields wide open.
