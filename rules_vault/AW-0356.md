## War

The arrival of Miko and the prospect of the coming Return sparked a cultural revolution among the Tandorans. Many of them began to prepare for the Return, but a significant minority were resistant to the idea of leaving the Island of Tandora. To them, it was their true home—not the doomed world their ancestors had been rescued from. They became known as the Kyoko faction, and taking the name of their most powerful and influential matriarch. Tensions rose, and the many fierce duels only seemed to ramp up the aggression rather than resolving it. Miko worked tirelessly to bridge the growing divide. She was assassinated while giving a speech in Tandora City. Though nearly all of the Tandorans from both sides of the conflict were horrified by Miko’s violent death, a great battle broke out almost instantly. Hundreds of Tandorans—a significant fraction of the population—died in the fighting. Eventually there was a lull in the battle, and Kyoko called for a truce.
