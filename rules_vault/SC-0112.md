## Everything But Your Life

This adventure hook works best with the Corporate or Whisper background but is easily adapted to any team with an eye for social engineering and complex planning.

The job is an extraction, but not a snatch and grab. More corporate espionage. It’s not enough to acquire the asset—the target has to want to leave. The client doesn’t specify why, and the reasons could be myriad, both for the player character’s imaginations and for the gamemaster. Additional loyalty can help make extraction easier, or perhaps there is some kind of magical reason behind their need for loyalty, or there’s a psychological trigger or kink bomb to bypass. Regardless, the team’s job is to effectively alienate the target from their cushy corporate life. If the players complete this first phase, they are paid. If they have managed to keep their own faces and fingerprints (digital and otherwise) away from the target’s sight, they’ll be offered a follow up to approach and extract the now-willing target. The better a job they did, the easier the second part will be. The real challenge is doing this in a week or less.

Alienation is a sandbox the gamemaster can customize to their players’ interests or, if they are up to the improv and response, a beautiful opportunity to let their group’s imaginations run wild. Sabotaging events, undermining their security or standing in their current employer’s eyes, or making them feel unappreciated or unsafe can all contribute to a more enthusiastic defection. The soft touch is slower but less likely to put the target (or their handlers) on alert. Overt threats to their safety (or that of their family, if the gamemaster wants to up the complexity and difficulty of the job) are likely to escalate their dependency on their employer’s security, which works against the team and tips their hand. Subtler methods of driving a wedge between them may involve causing their coworkers to dislike them, sabotaging their work and pinning it on a rival, glitching gifts and bonuses they feel they have earned, or arranging for promotions and accolades to be delayed or waylaid. Ruining the target’s life and making it look like their employer’s fault will win the day. Bonus points for making the PCs feel guilty about wrecking an otherwise happy life, if you want a dystopian flavor. From a rules perspective, consider the target to have 4 Edge usable in resisting a recruitment attempt. Every successful alienation removes one point of Edge, possibly reducing it all the way to zero as the target’s reluctance lessens and becomes token. For every overt threat or tipped sabotage, add one point of Edge, to a maximum of 6, and add one more security element to their protection. Should the recruitment go well, Mr. Johnson keeps them as a priority team in their rolodex, gaining them a Mr. Johnson contact at Connection 5, Loyalty 3. If using a long-running Mr. Johnson, increase each rating by one.
