## Special Rules

## for Translating to Dis

A few short years ago, the metaplane of Dis was unknown to anyone outside of a very small group of megacorporate insiders. But for many different reasons, the name of Dis is quickly becoming known. In fact, for those who pay attention to such things, it’s quickly becoming one of the most important of the known metaplanes. Dis is a metaplanar crossroads, and spirits and entities from countless metaplanes can be found here. Some are explorers who have just recently discovered the metaplane, while others come and go, trading with



Dis for various items but most commonly providing slaves. Permanent residents may be there for their own reasons, or they may have been pressed into servitude sometime in the distant past. Many of these residents have been on Dis so long they no longer remember where they originally came from and will claim to be Disian if asked.

Unless the traveler has access to an existing portal, traveling to Dis presents an assortment of problems. For ritual gateways, Disians themselves conceal their true form inside created vessels so the location of the metaplane can never be deduced from studying one. Formulas for detailing the location for a ritual gateway are also not available, at least not in the Sixth World. Currently, the ways from the physical realm to Dis seem to be blocked. It may be a grand eclipse or something the Disians are doing, but all tests for traveling to Dis from the material plane realm have a threshold of 12. Going there from an adjacent metaplane has the normal distance of 8, but a formula or map would have to be acquired on the metaplane. Traveling there by the Astral Sea is also at increased difficulty (both in finding it and perceiving it), and all tests regarding Dis having a threshold 4 higher than normal, as the maps to its location are inconsistent at best.

Travelers arriving at Dis will quickly find out that the entire metaplane is covered in enchantments to inhibit metaplanar translation. This affects both information technology and magic use.

Native Dis technology is based on magic, but other technology brought in still works, mostly. Devices have been brought in from an unknown number of other metaplanes over the millennium, and some of it was made with very different reality paradigms. All of this technology was untranslated on arrival and later adapted by the non-Dis residents of the metaplane—sometimes numerous times as it changes hands. So don’t expect to understand how or why devices found in Dis work. Machine languages and connection protocols do not readily translate, but many semi-magical devices may self-translate to a certain extent, which means a Matrix connection to any different type of technology found here may or may not work. Some buildings or even sections of the city may contain devices that generate a localized Matrix equivalent usable by some of the residents, and Sixth World technology may or may not connect to it. The memory crystals used by the Dis themselves for communication and data storage will never be translated, so a cyberdeck can’t access or communicate with Disian technology at all. However, as nous is a universal force (like mana), all information processing devices—including Dis memory crystals—generate a noosphere that technomancers can access. Currently, Dis memory crystal tech can be accessed by technomancers, but the untranslated language the data is written in is currently unknown.

Dis mana is “out of phase” from what is found in most other metaplanes, including the Sixth World. The Dis metaplane should be considered a high mana ebb for all non-natives or those who have not been acclimated to it through the chimera process. In addition, any test that uses the Magic attribute must include a wild die in the dice pool, and glitches happen on 1s and 2s for non-natives. For Disians and those who have been altered, the mana is much more accessible, and the metaplane is a low mana flow with no wild die or increased glitch chances.

A final note on Disian magic: It is highly dependent on Essence to make a connection with a target, and Essence reduction inhibits its effectiveness. Any Essence loss (down to a 4.0 Essence) causes a –2 reduction in net hits, applied after the test is taken. An Essence lower than 4.0 but 2.0 or higher increases the penalty to –3 net hits, while an Essence lower than 2.0 applies a –4 net hit penalty. If these penalties reduce the net hits to 0 or lower, the spell fails
