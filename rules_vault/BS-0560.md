## DIY Genetic Engineering

In order to create a genetically enhanced critter, a genetics engineering laboratory is necessary. You will also need at least one artificial womb, which is not included. Growth vats must also be purchased separately. Each growth vat allows you to quickly grow one specimen at a time and uses up a number of supplies equal to the critter’s natural Body attribute (counting any increases from genetic augmentations) per week.

Embryos are not as difficult to acquire, as many megacorporations produce them for R&D purposes within as well as outside the scope of genetic engineering. Frozen embryos of mundane critters can be purchased for 500 nuyen x the critter’s natural Body attribute, with an Availability of 4(L). Frozen mundane embryos of Awakened critters are expensive and more restricted, with a nuyen cost of 1,000 x the total of the critter’s natural Body and Magic attributes, and an Availability of 6(L).

Each genetic augmentation requires a specific formula, and these aren’t simply posted on the open market. Each formula costs 10,000 x rating in nuyen, with an Availability of (Rating x 2) (L).

Once you have the equipment, embryos, and formula, you can get to work. Designing an enhanced critter requires a Biotech (Genetic Engineering) + Logic Extended test with a threshold of the total of all the ratings of the desired genetic augmentations and an interval of one day. Once you have developed the formula for an enhanced critter, you don’t need to do this work again for multiple applications—you can reuse it as many times as you like.

When you are ready to begin applying the genetic augmentations to an embryo, make another Biotech (Genetic Engineering) + Logic Extended test, with a threshold equal to the total ratings of all genetic augmentations and an interval of one week. Replace one of your dice on each test with a wild die. On a critical glitch, or if the wild die ever rolls a 1, the embryo dies during the gestation period. On a critical glitch, the embryo dies during the gestation period. Make a note of how many regular glitches are rolled on the Extended test—if you roll more glitches than the critter’s natural Body attribute, it dies shortly after its birth.

In order for Awakened genetic augmentations to take, the Awakened Genetic Augmentation ritual sorcery must be completed once each day. If you skip a day or the ritual ever fails, the critter dies immediately. Only one ritual needs to be performed each day, but the more Awakened augmentations are used, the greater the threshold.

If the critter survives gestation, it will grow at its normal rate unless you accelerate the growth using a growth vat. Accelerated growth requires another Biotech (Genetic Engineering) Extended test with a threshold equal to double its Body attribute and an interval of one week. Add a wild die to each roll during the Extended test, and if it rolls a 1, the critter dies during this process. As mentioned above, special supplies are required each week of accelerated growth. The number of units of supplies needed is equal to the critter’s natural Body attribute.

If a critter is modified via gene therapy treatments (instead of engineered as an embryo), they must be placed within a growth vat for the duration of the procedure. This requires a Biotech (Genetic Engineering) + Logic Extended test with a threshold of the critter’s Body plus the number of genetic augmentations that are applied, and an interval of two weeks. If any test fails or results in a critical glitch, the critter dies during the process. Each glitch increases the chance of unintended consequences, as per the Genetic Mishaps table. The maximum number of genetic augmentations a post-embryonic critter may receive is equal to its Body attribute, and each genetic augmentation has an Essence cost equal to its rating x 0.1, or 0.2 for augmentations that do not have a listed rating. Each week of the gene therapy treatment consumes one unit of supplies. If the process is aborted before it can complete, the critter painfully reverts back to its original state over the course of oneweek—no augmentations will be applied. During this time, the critter gains the Immobilized condition. At the end of the week, the critter must make a Body test with a threshold equal to the number of genetic augmentations that were attempted. If it fails this test, it dies due to aggressive and incurable forms of cancer.

Pre-grown bioware augmentations can be installed in a baby critter. These grow with the critter, and their effective grade counts as one better than the grade purchased—used becomes standard, standard becomes alpha, alpha becomes beta, and beta becomes delta. Cybernetic augmentations may not be installed until the critter reaches adulthood.
