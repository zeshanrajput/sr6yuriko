## Corporate Court Artificial Resources Management

The picture shifts when we look at how the Corporate Court treats AI and other Matrix resources. The highest priority on their list is to track any AIs that are out there, so they work diligently to register as many as they can. They will never get them all—there are too many AIs who have no desire to cooperate, and too many places on the Matrix to hide. But if they at least get a good percentage of them, then they have some idea when a known AI is operating and when a rogue AI is involved, and that shapes their response to any incident.

And let’s be clear about this: There is nothing that worries the CCARM more than rogue AIs. One of the many concerns about rogues is we really don’t know the limits of their abilities, or even if such limits exist. We know certain things about biological beings. For example, if an animal weighs more than forty or so kilos, it will have a very difficult time flying unless it generates some extraordinary lift. Humans simply do not get beyond threemeters tall, because their bone structure and blood circulation can’t handle that size.

**>** <shakes head in troll> You poor, limited souls. **>** Beaker

AIs are something else. They have no physical structure to limit them, and Matrix structures are vast in their variety. It’s not only possible that AIs could perform functions in ways and at speeds that we never thought possible—it’s likely that such things are happening right now in the Matrix. That’s what keeps the CCARM from ever resting easy.

But at the same time they do not want this nervousness to translate into overt distrust of registered AIs. They want those beings to feel welcomed and accepted, for the specific reason that many of them are making the corps a lot of money. They take this far enough to offer classes and trainings to help AIs better integrate with meatworld society. If you want to open your eyes to the vast range of perspectives and definitions of reality that exist in the Matrix, sit in on one of their AI orientation sessions and just listen. You’ll hear beings trying to describe mindsets you never would have conceived.

- I love it when they try to describe the concept of families. Some AIs grok it immediately—code that is similar to my code may be seen as reassuring. To others, it’s a way of building associations among beings that is somehow worse than random selection. Humans don’t build their closest associations on complementary skills, or on seeking elite performers in selected areas? Instead, they’re close to code that happened to branch from theirs, regardless of whether the code is useful or even functional? What kind of beings **do** that?

Once you get past that warm, nurturing carrot part of CCARM, you get to the pointy stick. They put a lot of person-hours into searching out the deepest, darkest parts of the Matrix, and they’re not playing around. They’re quite capable of handling most beings they encounter. The ones they can’t handle, though, literally keep them awake at night.
