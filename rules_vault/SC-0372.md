## face fatale

## playing advice

The face fatale is a finesse character, best used with patience and an end plan. Their looks, wit, and charm are their best weapons, but not their only ones. There’s no need to spend a month luring a target into an involved romance when they have a dark secret to be blackmailed with. And sometimes, pointing a gun at someone instead of seducing them is the easier, more efficient way to proceed. The face fatale should take a prominent role in social interactions without sidelining the other players—everyone should have a role to play, and

the face fatale can help arrange those roles. Sure, the hacker may not have many social graces or people skills, but having them play a techhead in a long con should come to them naturally enough. Even flaws can be used—the character who stutters and freezes in awkward social situations can be a chance for the face fatale to show their compassion to a mark in a public gathering, earning an opening for an introduction where they can use their social powers.

The gamemaster should reward the character for spending resources on Charisma and Intuition just as they would a characters prioritizing Strength and Agility. The player may not be the most eloquent person in the world, but the face fatale should be, just as a troll merc is much stronger than the player running one. Try to balance roleplaying by allowing the character to use their skills and attributes when talking with NPCs. Give the character a chance to utilize their strengths, but challenge their weaknesses as well. As an NPC, the face fatale’s reach and resources should reflect the challenge they offer—they’ll never be a demon in combat, but their friends can be.
