## Scourge

- **Vector:** Injection (and Contact when mixed with DMSO)



- **Speed:** Immediate

- **Duration:** (12 – Body or Magic, whichever is higher) Hours, minimum 1 hour

- **Power:** 10 (5)

- **Effect:** Magic loss, Physical damage, Nauseated status

With the threat from Dis constantly increasing, the Grey Brothers have found their products in high demand. As production increased, toxic byproducts accumulated and research was started on a more concentrated (and deadly) variety of Blight, leading to the development of Scourge. This compound has proven to be slightly more effective when used against magical entities, but unlike Blight—which only targets magical ability—Scourge has proven to be highly toxic to everything alive.

**Game Rules:** Like with Blight, Scourge requires Awakened entities to make a drain resistance test instead of a toxin resistance test vs. the Power of the toxin. Any unresisted Power is converted to Physical damage instead of Stun. If the Power of the toxin is not reduced to zero, Magic use is inhibited, and the affected entity loses its connection to the manasphere for the Duration of the effect. Specifically, it prevents attempting any test requiring a Magic rating, or using any adept power or any natural ability generated by Magic. As a secondary effect of Scourge, the affected entity also gains the Nauseated status for the duration.

Note that Disians and some Faustians can channel mana into a drain resistance test, making both Scourge and Blight less effective against them.

Non-Awakened creatures exposed to Scourge must make a standard toxin test against the lower Power (5) listed with any unresisted Power also converted to Physical damage. If the Power is not reduced to zero, this also causes the Nauseated status.

When mixed with DMSO, both Blight and Scourge gain the Contact Vector. They are subject to the rules for DMSO-toxin mixtures and require a delivery method for aerosolized liquids such as a vapor gun or an aerosol grenade to be effective.
