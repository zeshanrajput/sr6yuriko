## Investigation Time

By this point, the runners have probably gotten the attention of several groups playing a part in this Manhattan mayhem. This leads the runners to do what every good runner should have been doing from the start; checking out who they’re working for. Though this time, they are technically checking out who Piper is working for.

Piper offers that her payment came via Iron Couriers, a secure courier service that delivers certified credsticks. She has an image, a name, and some basics on the person who did the delivery and suggests the runners start there, or with the Iron Couriers Matrix host.

The courier is named Jake Adams (use Tir Ghost, p. 209, _SR6_ , with all proper licenses to carry his weapons in Manhattan; add Ares Roadmaster and gel ammo for all weapons). His doss is on the edge of Southside, near Terminal. When confronted, Jake will claim his profession demands he maintain client confidentiality, but he can be convinced to give up the info through social, mental, or physical coercion or bribery (2,500 nuyen). He knows he did the pickup at a duplex at the corner of 3rd and 122nd. The person who gave him the package was a male, human, brown hair, mid-forties, wearing exercise clothes that revealed scarring from implant surgeries, and a right cyberarm.

The duplex at the corner of 3rd and 122nd is currently empty on both sides. The host for the homes are tougher than their neighbors, and all entries use maglocks (rating 6). The windows are all reinforced glass. Inside both is a large, openplan main floor with living room, kitchen, bathroom, and dining room. There is a small balcony off the kitchen area at the rear. The second floor of both units has a master bedroom with a balcony at the rear, two smaller bedrooms at the front (one of which is being used as a workout room), and a bathroom and laundry at the center. The basement/ lower floor has another maglock (rating 6) on the door from the main floor. It has a bathroom, single bedroom, and a sitting room at the rear with another maglocked door to an exit stairwell. The sitting room has a slate tile floor with a drain in the middle. The secret of this place is door hidden in the shower of the lower floor bathroom that connect both basements via a small secure saferoom/ armory. The houses are actually used as a UCAS CIA safehouse. This is something the runners can only discover if they find the hidden room via an astral scan or Perception (6) test made in the bathroom. Discovering how to open the door requires

a Perception + Intuition (16, 1 minute) Extended test.

Inside the saferoom is a small armory (six Colt Government 2076, six HK-227; four spare clips for each full of regular ammo). There is also a comm terminal that connects to a satellite link hidden on the roof inside a decorative chimney. The saferoom is the only place with real evidence of who operates from here, but accessing the terminal is dangerous. As soon as the terminal is activated, it signals the security forces on call for the safehouse and alerts the two electronic security specialists (use SEAL Team 6 Counter-Electronics Commando, p. 209, _SR6_ ) who monitor the satellite link.

The physical security forces (use one Marine Corps Special Operations Forces, p. 208, _SR6_ ; four Lone Star SWAT Officer, p. 207, _SR6_ ; one Lone Star Combat Mage, p. 207, _SR6_ ; Delta Force Logistics and Support Rigger, p. 209, _SR6_ ) were not prepared for a call from this site. They will not arrive for twenty minutes, but the clock is ticking as soon as the saferoom is accessed. They arrive via Ares Dragon and don’t hold back.

More ties to the US/UCAS CIA are popping up all over the place. While the UCAS may have taken some hits of late, this new tech seems to be pointing directly back at them. If they are in control of it, that could mean a major shift in world politics if all those corporations that keep stomping on them are suddenly full of executives and board members voting favorably for UCAS policies.
