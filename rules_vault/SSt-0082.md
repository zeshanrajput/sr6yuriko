## **Drone Selection**

**When it comes to drone selection, I tend to look very closely at the specifcations and included options for the stock model. You can modify and customize drones a fair bit, but you’re going to need a lot of them, and they’re both expensive and fragile. I fnd it best to work with what comes naturally, so to speak, and I keep my drone mods to a minimum. Autosofts can generally be copied around and reused, but hardware is very fnite. There are some exceptions to this, as I have a few precious favorites that I’ve applied some considerable modifcations to, but they are few and not the norm. You can’t get too attached to your drones for the most part, because they just don’t survive long in this biz. That doesn’t mean you should treat them like expendable trash—it’s just the nature of the job. Whether it’s from enemy hackers, corpsec trackers, or bullets, your drones are gonna get wrecked. Be ready for that—as ready as you can make yourself—and never put yourself in a position to die when a drone can do it for you.**

**When it comes to snooping and spotting, microdrones and minidrones are where it’s at. As a matter of fact, that’s pretty much all micros and minis are good for—but they’re perfect for the task. The thing I love most about ’em is that they’re cheap, which also makes them common sights—almost anyone can afford a Flying Eye or an MCT Gnat to follow them around and take pics and vids for their social media feeds or keep an eye on their doss, parked car, kids, or untrustworthy signifcant other when they’re away. Plenty of people other than riggers use drones, so you can make use of them fairly freely in public places without attracting suspicion. Because the corps make so much nuyen selling them to the masses, the laws restricting their use are pretty lax and rarely enforced. And if you need to snoop in a place that’s more secure, they’re small enough to avoid notice. The GMC Micromachine can slip under most doors and zip along the foor silently, and the MCT Gnat is indiscernible from an actual gnat unless you get a very close look at one—so even if it gets spotted, someone probably won’t bat an eye. The MCT Hornet is a bit too large to be overlooked—even though it looks like an actual hornet, you have to take into account that people get very nervous around stinging insects. But it’s still small enough to be sneaky, and the injector it carries provides some signifcant tactical fexibility. The Flying Eye**
