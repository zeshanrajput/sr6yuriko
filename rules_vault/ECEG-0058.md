## Crescent City Leadership

When you think of syndicates, New Orleans must look like it would be a hotbed of competing groups all making a profi t off the port, the river, and the international shipping border. In reality, that isn’t the case. Sure, there are a lot of small krewes doing business in the area, but as far as more organized, widespread business goes, the Mafi a has been the law of the land for generations.

The group has about a dozen people in the greater New Orleans area and can often be found at Foo Bar in the Abita Springs ’burb. While the local members mostly work on their own goals, they all look up to deBass, who keeps the organization together while pulling off some of the major heists.

This isn’t to say nothing ever changes. The current head of the Families is Pamela Kozlowski, capa of clan Kozlowski and dona of New Orleans. She’s the third generation of the only line of female dons in North America, and she has fought for every inch she’s taken in this city. There was an upset years back when she took over for her aunt, the so-called La Dame du Morte, when her right to run the Families was brought to question. In the end, Pamela was in charge, and the Mossino Family leaders were executed for staging a coup.
