## Obstacles

The paperwork is a bear. And unless you have your ducks in a row, it’s going to be very difficult to navigate. But the real hassle involves land ownership rights. Yeah. Boring to everyone on your team except the smuggler, whose slowly spreading grin and steepled fingers hint at the power borders and ownership have for street ops like you. But here’s the thing: Deeds are powerful things and have been since they were invented. It’s what wars have been fought over since time immemorial, and it’s gonna be war on a local scale if you frag this up. See, one of the big events that makes the twenty-first century so different from the twentieth and before is the Resource Rush, when the old American government (and, evidently, a lot of the rest of the world) just decided all kinds of land was up for sale to any big corporate entity that has the cash to pay for it. With the Shiawase and Seretech decisions, if you own the land, you make the laws. As long as you’re a big enough entity, corporate or otherwise. Now, flashback to the horror of Westward Expansion hundreds of years ago (or just slot in a Western, a lot of them are about this). Land rights and deeds are worth a lot, not just for what’s on them, but for what they connect to and what can be made on them. If you decided to salt the land with something worth big, big nuyen, the powers-that-be will find ways to fuck you out of it, whether it’s drawing you into litigation (which you, as an upstanding criminal, cannot handle), private investigation, previous claimant right (real, forged, or bribed, doesn’t matter, they’re bigger than you).
