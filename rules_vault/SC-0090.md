## Not So Free Spirit

Thanks to their known affinity for beast spirits, the character is hired to retrieve an escaped subject in their local sprawl. The free spirit of beasts is still bound in some form to our plane thanks to an artifact in the possession of the corp that wants it back, in this case, Manadyne (part of OmniStar).

The spirit, known as Lycusaurum, is a free spirit of beasts that takes the form of a large golden-furred wolf. The Force of the spirit is left to the gamemaster to suit their campaign and provide sufficient challenge in the climactic final encounter.

Lycusaurum fled captivity at a Manadyne facility where they were studying her exceptional ability to heal others. Her escape weighs heavily on her as she had to injure several guards to escape, and a part of her exceptional ability to heal comes from her reluctance to do harm. She was weakened in the escape and fled but hides among the local squatters offering minor healing in exchange for their company and care. In the time since escaping, she has recovered to her full potential and stays among the squatters out of loyalty.

The plot should follow an investigation in the barrens with only the initial description of the spirit, a golden wolf, to guide them. Their inquiries are a mixed bag based on how trustworthy they seem, but no one is willing to sell out their local “golden goose … err … wolf” to some bounty-hunting shadowrunners. They need to either work their way toward the information with violence, which would end in direct conflict with Lycusaurum and her squatter horde, or come in peace and seek to understand the spirit and possibly free it from its captivity on our plane.

In a violent encounter, the runners should face an overwhelming number of fanatic squatters that defend Lycusaurum with a fervor but only use makeshift weapons. They come in groups and fight as a mob rather than individuals. During this battle, Lycusaurum gains Edge for every injury done on those loyal to her with no cap on her Edge pool. Whenever a squatter takes damage—any number of boxes, Physical or Stun—add one point of Edge to her pool. In this scenario, Lycusaurum’s attack is based on the actions of the runners. If they try to knock foes unconscious, she’ll try to knock them out (using a Stun base spell); if runners do Physical damage, she will return it in kind (using golden Fire or Lightning).

If the runners seek a more civilized approach, they can gain an audience with Lycusaurum and learn how to set her free. Lycusaurum knows two ways to accomplish this. The first way is to gain possession of the artifact that binds her to the plane. This means a run against a Manadyne black-research facility in the barrens with Lone Star physical security alongside Manadyne’s mojo. The second way is a path of sacrifice. If the character can succeed at a Conjuring (Banishing) + Magic (12, 1 minute) Extended test and is willing to sacrifice a point of Magic, they can free Lycusaurum. This sacrifice has benefits, but they are not mentioned in advance.

The benefit is two additional tasks gained for free on all future Binding tests involving spirits of beasts. It’s a solid trade for a summoner but an unknown risk when Lycusaurum asks for them to sacrifice their “arcane potential.”

The team can also disrupt or banish Lycusaurum in battle. If this occurs, she will then reform at the Manadyne facility under the control of the artifact and shielded by wards, once again trapped. She will remember the team and the character, and she should pose a threat down the road. This can mean another escape, and rather than focusing on helping, she just seeks revenge, or she draws the runners into a dangerous encounter.
