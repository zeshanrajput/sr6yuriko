## Final Calculations

Once you have a chassis, powertrain, console, and design options all selected and sorted out, it’s time to make some final calculations.

The vehicle’s final Body and Armor values determine the vehicle’s final Acceleration and Top Speed. All calculations are rounded down when necessary.

The initial calculation is the speed modifier, which is (Body/2) + Armor. To find the final Acceleration, divide the speed modifier by 2 and subtract that from the Powertrain’s base Acceleration.

For Top Speed, multiply the speed modifier by 10 and subtract that from the Powertrain’s base Acceleration. Walkers, due to how they function, do not suffer the Top Speed reduction. Drones have one more step based on size. Small drones multiply their new Acceleration by 0.75, mini drones by 0.50, and micro drones by 0.25. Medium and larger drones are unaffected by size.
