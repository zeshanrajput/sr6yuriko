## **An Idiot’s Guide to Gunfighting: Lesson 6: Don’t Skip Weapon Maintenance and Prep.**

**Sometimes a gunfght is lost before it starts because some people don’t know how to perform basic maintenance or can’t be bothered with it. It’s simply idiotic to take any frearm or weapon into battle that isn’t at peak mechanical condition and operational capability. Just because some corp marketing dip-frag says a weapon “never needs cleaning,” do it anyway because they’re not the ones in the middle of a frefght getting their hoops shot of. Maintaining your frearm at peak efciency also means having more than one clip (or speed loader, for those who favor revolvers) of ammunition at your disposal. Because no one after a frefght ever said, “I wish I had less ammo.”**

- **I’ll give everyone a bonus lesson: never quit. This doesn’t mean fighting until the bitter end or stupidly going out in a blaze of idiotic glory performing some delusional last stand like you see in the sims or ’trid. This means not giving up at the first sign of trouble or hardship. It means powering through until you’ve exhausted every single option you have. It may mean shooting every single round you have, even if you’re bleeding all over the ground. It may mean working a new plan until someone or thing stops you from doing any more. It may mean holding on just a few more seconds, just to see what happens next.**

- **One thing about opinions on firearms is that they’re like hoops— everyone’s got one. You hit on it just now, saying that it feels good in your hand. Don’t second-guess that. The biggest hurdle for any new shooter is finding what works for them. And good on you for doing that. We can sit here all day and give our opinions. But at the end of the day, what matters most is that it goes “bang” when you pull the trigger and you hit what you’re aiming at. Down the line, as your skills improve, you may decide to upgrade. Such is the way with any warrior and their weapons.**

  - **What are the different types of these arsenals?**

  - **Pike**

  - **Depends on the job or situation. But typically, you’ll have two basic kinds: everyday and on-the-job carry. The first are the firearms and other weapons you carry when you’re not on the job. These are often small, easy-to-conceal weapons like pistols. Then there are loadouts for use on a job or mission. These are usually heavier, like SMGs, rifles, shotguns, and so on all the way to specialized weapons like sniper rifles, machine guns, and assault cannons. Both arsenals are usually dictated by the user’s personal styles and preferences or by the job/mission requirements.**

- **Thorn**

As you progress, you’ll start adding things to your arsenal, developing skills that encompass multiple firearm types. As that happens, you’ll also become more comfortable using types in different situations and create your own personal arsenals.**
