## The Job

Now for the reality. Swen is contracting for Aztechnology to make some particularly damning data on something called Project Renewal look not

3: SWAPso damning. Unbeknownst to Swen or the runners, the plan is to replace the data with an exact copy but have the runners caught doing it so it looks like planted data.

Project Renewal is a variation on monad and cloning tech working to create newer, younger bodies, and then move a mature mind into the form. All sorts of moral issues are twisted in this project, and tons of the data has been stolen and refined from several corporations and government projects over the years, including the UCAS CIA, Evo, Project Imago, NeoNET, monads, and many more.

On site, Aztechnology already alerted their subsidiary, Valentine BioSolutions, about the run going down. The runners are under scrutiny and surveillance the entire time, and when a very staged security response occurs or the runners notice the data is the same, they may realize they’ve been duped.

In order to not get burned and black-listed so early in their Rotten Apple career, the runners will then need to lower their profile. To do so, they need to do some counter-hacking and convince security guards to get forgetful as the games of data manipulation are on.

No matter what the runners do, the screamsheets will start running stories about a shadowrunner break-in at Valentine BioSolutions. The stories will claim the company is working on a cloning or body-swapping program, but corporate PR at Valentine BioSolutions will claim that is false data being planted by runners. The runners will not be identified if they manage to cover their tracks.

Valentine BioSolutions has a relatively small office in a rare short office building located at the southwest corner of 102nd and Central Park West, called 102CPW by locals. It has kept a low profile in order to preserve the views of Central Park from a beautiful pre-quake condominium tower beyond it. The office is the entire third floor.

During work hours (06:00-18:00), the building and office host are well-protected, but in the evening most of the offices have chosen the offline route for protection, so machines are turned off rather than watched carefully.

After hours (post 18:00), the building only has a trio of security drones (use GM-Nissan Doberman, p. 300, _SR6_ ; add Smartgun X, loaded with 100 gel rounds) that patrol the halls on pre-programmed routes. Unauthorized after-hours presences are fired upon without warning. If any of the squatter spies or the security spider signal them, they will divert from their pre-programmed course and head toward the runners.

The Matrix system for the 102CPW building is offline after hours, so the Valentine BioSolutions host must be accessed internally. There is currently a security spider (use Decker, p. 84, _SR6_ ) logged in and disguising himself as Patrol IC in order to monitor the team’s hacker when they get in. Once

he spots the hacker, he will just record and monitor their activities.

The real Patrol IC will be the clue, as the hacker will notice the two overlapping their efforts at some point, but that activity will not happen until after the data swap has been recorded.

The group watching for the runners consists of eight squatter spies (use Lone Star Patrolman, p. 206, _SR6_ ; add single cybereye with camera, headware commlink [rating 4], headware memory, datajack; Con 5 skill). The spies blend into the local alleys and streets, looking like failed corporate wageslaves with some headware.

The security response consists of three GM-Nissan Doberman drones that are released from their pre-programmed route once sent a signal by any of the squatter spies or decker. The drones are all equipped with non-lethal ammunition and miss every shot. Roll a regular attack test, but there is no chance of the attack hitting. Instead, the net hits (in either direction) show how close the miss is. Use the net hits as a threshold for a Perception test to notice something is strange.

Once the runners are onto the ruse, they can respond accordingly. The squatter spies will threaten to upload what they have immediately, but they can be bought off or intimidated with an offer of 500 nuyen.

The hidden hacker will certainly require a bribe as he threatens the data is already backed up and ready to hit the screamsheets.

When it comes to payment time, Swen asks for another meeting in Central Park, but no wandering. Instead, he wants to meet at the center of Sheep Meadow. It should feel uncomfortable to be in the middle of a large, open field, but nothing happens except the runners getting paid. And maybe threatening Swen and accusing him of knowing what was happening, which he didn’t.
