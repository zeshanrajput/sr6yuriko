## **Change and Consequences: The Price of Failure**

**For Aztechnology, things changed for the worse regarding their alliance with Dis.**

**First was their failure to prevent not one but** _**two**_ **key events: the extraction of former board member Oliver McClure and the breach of their ultraviolet data vaults. The latter not only confrmed the existence of the Disians to outsiders (re: us), but also revealed the depths of Disian infltration on the material plane.**

**As one would expect, the Disians didn’t take either of those failures well and forced Aztechnology to pay several “penances.” We can confrm at least one, but we’ve since learned that several other Aztechnology personnel, or their family members, were taken by Dis as compensation for Aztechnology’s failures.**

**Second, the Disians may have already gotten what they want from Aztechnology in terms of assistance; no reason to continue the partnership as**

**previously established, especially since MCT now seems to be the heir apparent in the “frst among equals.” But someone else will have to cover that. While that has some credit to it, the fact that the Disians still work with and heavily rely on Aztechnology suggests this is more likely a means for the Disians to keep Aztechnology in check and to prevent inter-corporation collusion against Disian interests. A sort of carrot** _**and**_ **stick situation.**

**Whatever the reason, this new shift in the status quo has caused the Aztechnology leadership to do whatever they can to regain Disian favor. This in turn is driving most of their current strategies and operations during this shadow war.**
