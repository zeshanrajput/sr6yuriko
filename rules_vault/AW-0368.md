## The Camp

The Tandorans of the Great Savanna are nomadic, traveling with herds of massive sauropods as they continue their endless grazing. Most Tandorans live in tents that are taken down and set up again every few weeks. Several of the largest sauropods have platforms and cabins built on their backs. The largest portable structures are gigantic cargo sleds that are pulled by sauropods when the camp moves from one site to the next.

While the camp spends roughly half of its time traveling from one site to another, there are a few sites that they routinely return to and remain at for as long as a month at a time. Some Tandorans remain at each site while the camp moves on, so that they might prepare and maintain the location for when the camp returns. The longest stay happens where the sauropods lay their eggs—the hatching grounds. This is a sacred time of quiet festivals, as the Tandorans of the camp celebrate new life and an easy time of rest. They are careful to do so without disrupting the sauropods.
