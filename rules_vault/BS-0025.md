## Revitalization

Perhaps one of the most complex operations is restoring lost Essence. Techniques for this have been developed over the decades. This is not strictly a genetic therapy—it includes genetics but also uses chemical and alchemical preparations. This means that the actual treatment can vary significantly depending on whether the Awakened person supporting the procedure is a Bear shaman, a hermetic doctor, or even a spirit.

For anyone except the mega-wealthy, the treatment has to be administered in a carefully set up facility. Most sprawls have a small handful of suitable places. A few places are over-served—for example, Washington D.C. has more than any other sprawl.

This exercise in blending together magic and science can be very frustrating to scientists, who generally want a repeatable, reliable, consistent technique, without Awakened therapists changing their crystals and herbs for every patient. By the

same token, the Awakened therapists become irritated when the un-Awakened members of the team want to blindly and stubbornly repeat the same exercise again and again, ignoring how the ebbs and flows of the manasphere change the world. As you might expect, with highly skilled medical, magical, and scientific experts working together on a common goal, there is near-constant bickering and a very high turnover rate as egos clash. On the upside, for those who are able to demonstrate even an ounce of humility, it can be a very rewarding and educational experience.

This fusion means that prices and times have not come down nearly as quickly for this procedure as for simple genetic repairs.

Even with the low corporate interest, ongoing research is robust. Two systems stand out: Shiawase’s Wakagaeri Program was the first major program to be standardized across an entire megacorporation. This particular program was brought about by elevating a Shinto priest to the board of directors and eliminating Leónization Type II, and it gained funding as Shiawase slowly moved away from cybernetics as a major corporate theme. Ares’ Gaea Approach is the other program of interest. This is more recent and happened with the elevation of Arthur Vogel as temporary CEO. It has continued under his successor, Michael Bishop, who doesn’t seem to care about it enough to cancel it. Ironically, perhaps, Vogel himself has yet to undertake it, as his implanted cybernetics are the only thing keeping him alive, but his desire to return to what he once was is the guiding philosophy behind this program, and he keeps a watch on its developments. When he can survive without the chrome, this program will be the thing that rebuilds the rest of him.

- I actually went through the Wakagaeri Program back in the day. It wasn’t in a specially set-up facility, just in a lab in the bowels of one of the big buildings. I’m glad I did it, but it was nice when they stopped poking and prodding me. The worst bit was having some wiz kid come and stare at my aura for twenty minutes twice a day. At least, I think that’s what they were staring at.

- **>** Fife


## revitalization

Revitalization restores 0.1 Essence per month. A person must spend at least twenty-four continuous hours per four weeks in a dedicated facility but can otherwise go about their normal life. This costs 40,000 nuyen upfront and an additional 10,000 nuyen per month of treatment.

Spending extra time during the week at the facility doesn’t restore additional Essence, but can be very pleasant.

Note that this doesn’t restore Magic lost due to Essence loss (see Auric Therapy). It also cannot restore Essence lost to currently implanted ’ware.

See p. 92 for complete rules.
