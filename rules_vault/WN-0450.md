## OmniStar

With both DocWagon and Lone Star in their portfolio, OmniStar is certainly plugged into chaos in the Rotten Apple (even if Lone Star only has a smattering of private contracts across town rather than responsibility for municipal policing activities). They also have ambitions to become an AAA company. While they’ve only heard rumors of what the Animus technology can do, they’re interested in it—and like Ares, they’re interested in anything they can use as leverage against any of the Big Ten. While Ares wants to deliver pain, OmniStar prefers blackmail, wringing favors in return for covering up certain pieces of information.

_Sends_ : A team of in-house shadowrunners. Their face will make initial contact and put in an offer for their employer.

_Resources_ : A six-member team of corporate shadowrunners with decent support resources.

- Two Shadowriders, p. 137, _Firing Squad_

- Minuteman Security Rigger, p. 206, _SR6_

- Lone Star Combat Mage, p. 207, _SR6_

- DocWagon HTR Officer, p. 207, _SR6_

- Hacker, p. 84, _SR6_

- The team has two Force 4 kindred spirits, a Force 4 spirit of earth, an Ares Roadmaster, two MCT-Nissan Rotodrones with two HK227s, two GM-Nissan Dobermans with Ares Alphas loaded with smoke and stun grenades, and an Aerodesign Systems LDSD64 Condor (p. 108, _Double Clutch_ ).

_Offer_ : 130,000 nuyen

_Conditions for Attack:_ Only if threatened. And that means threatened by actions, not just angry words.

_Offer_ : Free month of Gold DocWagon membership for each team member just for taking the meeting. Three months of Platinum membership and 20,000 nuyen for each piece of corporate data they bring in. They pay 150,000 nuyen for access to Animus Construction technology.



_Conditions for Attack:_ None, unless they are attacked. The OmniStar runners are looking for an opportunity, not a fight.

_Ways to Play Off Other Factions:_ The OmniStar team represents opportunity for the PCs. Not only could they get some cash and health care, but they should recognize kindred spirits in the team from OmniStar. If they do anything that might result in some interesting megacorporate gossip, the OmniStar team could be willing and able accomplices. This might mean repaying them some of the money OmniStar has laid out, but it might be well worth it.

_Aftermath Attack Risk:_ None, but the PCs should be aware of what happens if OmniStar gets hold of Animus’ technology. For a time afterward, the PCs will be living in a world where Lone Star has some of the powers they have long coveted. They could beat a confession out of someone and make them forget the beating. They could alter what witnesses remember to build a case to suit what they want at the time. Some innocent suspects will be railroaded, while some guilty but connected suspects will be cleared. It’ll come to an end before too long, as the weaknesses and failures of Animus’ technology become clear, but for a few months, the PCs will have unleashed one of the most frighteningly unethical authoritarian law forces the world has ever seen.
