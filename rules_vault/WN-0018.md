## Sea

While New York is listed as a major UCAS port of call, most New York shipping ports are actually located in the outer boroughs. The Port Authority Complex is in Lower Manhattan, and thus part of the city proper. Manhattan’s largest piers, located along the West Side and southern tip of the island, are used primarily as ports of call for ferries and cruise ships.

The East River flows around Roosevelt Island and Randalls Island, separating lower Manhattan from Queens and Brooklyn. The Harlem River divides upper Manhattan from the Bronx. And Spuyten Duyvil Creek wraps around the northernmost end, between the Hudson and Harlem rivers. One hundred twenty meters wide on average, the creek is the narrowest crossing into Manhattan, but it’s heavily patrolled by water and air.

- Manhattan’s shoreline is loaded with docks. Most are restricted to ferries, cargo ships, NYPD, and military vessels, but the rest are private-use docks owned by the megacorporations. It’s practically impossible to get an unlicensed ship on to one of these docks, but sneaking onto a ship that’s hitting port is a legitimate option.

- There is also the skimmer approach. Most smuggling in New York happens via high-speed skimmers sliding across the water at night. Be careful, though, because there aren’t many places that a skimmer can touch down, and the places they can are all controlled by one criminal organization or another.

Rail entry points are limited. Towers Station, Penn Station and Terminal Station service trainlines from the New Jersey side of the Hudson, while Grand Central Station and the Delancey Hub filter traffic from Brooklyn and Queens. Harlem Station in the Mitsuhama Arcology is the only rail access point from the Bronx.

After the quake of 2005, the MDC tore down most of the surviving bridges, creating an additional natural barrier to individuals trying to enter (or leave) the city. There are eight remaining bridges that connect Manhattan to the rest of the world. Anyone still alive from before the quake wouldn’t recognize most of them by name, because the corps changed every name they could.

Sony Bridge is the only bridge to access Randalls and Wards Islands. It connects the incorporated islands, with Manhattan on one side and Queens on the other.

The only bridge that crosses the Hudson into New Jersey is named in honor of Ares autocrat Damien Knight. Aztechnology tried that tactic when they rebuilt the Brooklyn Bridge, but New York revolted. A short walk from the Brooklyn Bridge is the Aneki Bridge, which also connects directly to downtown Brooklyn. Travel a mile to the northeast and you’ll find the Trans-Orbital Bridge bringing trains, cars, and foot traffic into the city from nearby Williamsburg.

Spinrad Bridge is directly next to the Roosevelt Island Tramway on the Upper East Side of Manhattan, linking New York to Long Island City.



CitiGroup Bridge at Third Avenue and 135[th] Street spans the Harlem River between the Bronx and the Mitsuhama Arcology. Only a kilometer away, the Prometheus Bridge provides the same service without having to cross through MCT space.
