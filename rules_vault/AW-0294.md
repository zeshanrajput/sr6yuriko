## Obsidian Golem

Yes, I am well aware that obsidian is not a metal, but you try naming something that looks like a very slightly built human made of dull matte black metal when you don’t know what the metal is. Should we manage to bring samples back to our plane any time, we will analyze them to determine what they are. But having seen these things in the flesh (so to speak), I think it’s an appropriate name.

Obsidian golems are not particularly fast, they’re not really what I would call tough—though they do have the same golem resilience—and they aren’t strong. When facing one you know is there, I wouldn’t call them very skillful, either. What they are, though, is fragging sneaky, able to cloak themselves and move without a trace. They are true terrors when it comes to ambushes. They also are unlikely to be on their own, and they often work with another golem to act as a primary focus while the obsidian golem gets into an opponent’s blind spot.

All the obsidian golems that have been seen so far are armed with the same short sword as the silver golems, a dagger that can be thrown or used in their off hand, and a light crossbow when they need to work at range.

This is the only type of the four that we know is an accurate name—I’d suspected the brass golems were actually orichalcum the first time I saw them casting spells, but Exile (as our resident expert on all things enchanting) confirmed it for us.

Standing roughly 1.6 meters tall and with a slight build, the orichalcum golems look like your average Joe Wageslave. If he were made of orichalcum. They aren’t physically imposing, but they show a lot more in the way of a spark of intelligence behind their eyes. And they’re also capable of speech, though to date we’ve not encountered one that can understand or speak English or Sperethiel.

As one would expect from the mage, the orichalcum golem is a force multiplier. They have been observed healing their allies (which is tremendously annoying when they do it on a steel golem, let me tell you) as well as casting offensive spells and using illusions and manipulation spells to good effect in battle. So basically, they do everything a shadowrunner mage is likely to do. The orichalcum golems are also capable of potent offensive and defensive metamagics. Spell shaping is one they like to use a lot, which I completely understand as I use it a lot to make my area spells more useful. They also use Shielding and Reflection quite a lot, which is annoying (did I ever mention I hate it when people use my tricks on me?). I have, to date, not seen an orichalcum golem using Absorption—that could be down to luck, or it could indicate that they are not capable of doing so in some way. I have also not seen orichalcum golems use Conjuring or any of the associated metamagics. Again, I don’t know the reason why.

Orichalcum golems are not stupid, though to be honest I’m not sure I could call any of the golem types stupid—their grasp of tactics, both individual combat and in small groups, is excellent. Regardless, the orichalcum golems carry one of those golem short swords, and they know how to use it. They are the worst swordsmen of their kind, but they at least know to carry a sword and know how to use it, despite them seeming to shrug off drain like it was water off a duck’s back.
