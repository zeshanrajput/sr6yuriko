## **Grimm’s Elephant**

## **Great Weaver Bird**

## **(unclassified)**

## **(Loxodonta malleus)**

**Due to the wings, beaks, and general body structure of these creatures, we call them birds, but they are actually extraplanar colonizers. They appear similar to the typical weaver bird of Africa but a bit more golden than yellow. They are slightly larger but not much, and they build similar nests. The difference with the great weaver is inside that nest. Their nests provide portal accesses to a separate metaplane. On this plane, the great weavers are hunted but have managed to hide and settle on our plane. The only problem is they have yet to fnd a nourishing prey on our plane. Instead, they need to cross through the portals within their nests to hunt and then return to our plane for safety.**

**While the bullyphant seems more the white knight, Grimm’s elephant is the anti-hero at best if not the occasional villain.**

**While the bullyphant targets those who wrong elephants directly, Grimm’s elephant is a toxic metahuman hunter through and through. They operate individually but tend to blend into larger herds when they are not off on a murder spree. They don’t look much different at frst glance. Closer inspection demonstrates a drastically different dental structure, with retractable claws on the fore and hind limbs, and sharp ridges along their trunk. Their dental adjustment is more predatory, with a dental array similar to a sabertooth tiger but longer lower tusks. All of the teeth are well-hidden within the head, much like a hippopotamus.**

**Their metaplanar doorway usually stays open when they are off hunting and is guarded by other great weavers, but sometimes other things slip through.**

**The ridges along the trunk are like a chainsaw and can hack through a tree, or a metahuman torso, easily. The retractable claws are the interesting part, as they serve mixed purposes. They can be used to attack, but hind claws are for grip or locking in to absorb an impact. Eyewitnesses have claimed a Grimm’s elephant can take a hit from a GMC Rhino dead in the face and not even budge. The foreclaws are for climbing when they need to work their way up a tree after prey, though the tree usually breaks under their weight.**

**The great weaver is sapient and will often offer valuable trinkets or minerals from their own plane as payment to assist them and keep unwanted guests out or hunt them down if they’ve slipped through.**

**My near-death experience didn’t specifcally come from a great weaver but from an accident near one of their nests. I was levitating over the area when a void croc shifted its power to me. My spell dropped and so did I, but I fell right into a nest. I crashed through the walls and fell across the metaplanar gate. Apparently, the weaver was on the other side, hunting.**

**They are a regular nuisance to hunting camps, research parties, exploratory expeditions, and any other group or individual out in the wilds and somehow on their list. They don’t kill everything they come across and have some strange and narrow sense of self-control, but that self-control evaporates completely when they come into contact with anyone who uses elephant tusk or bone in any way. This includes shamans who think it’s something sacred, Grimm’s elephants don’t care. They kill and take all of the elephant’s remains back to an elephant graveyard.**

**Because I knew my only hope of returning was to wait for the weaver, I stayed close and fended off several potential crossovers. It wasn’t easy and left me with several injuries I needed to treat upon my return. Fortunately, that happened quickly, as the weaver knew I had crossed though and came back to send me home. They have no interest in us coming to their metaplane—which makes me want to more!**

- **Rumor has it everyone’s bones go back, and the bone termite mounds are quite impressive.**

**> Ecotope**
