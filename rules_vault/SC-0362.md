## String of Favors

This run-in with the face fatale has nothing to do with the mission, though the players don’t need to know that. One of the characters has something the face fatale wants. It could be money, a possession, information, or maybe the face fatale needs them to do something for them. It doesn’t even have to be one of the PCs—an important contact or friend could work as well. This never happens in a vacuum, and the team will have to deal with one of them being compromised while the rest of the mission continues. This can be tough to roleplay and will require cooperation from the player. Alternatively, it could be an excellent way to temporarily sideline a character when a player is absent, allowing the rest of the players to deal with this problem without derailing the whole mission.

It should start out small, with the character being slightly distracted or occasionally late. This should start to build as the face fatale gets a deeper hold on the character. This will eventually build into the character missing assignments and wanting the team to delay things while they deal with what the character feels are more important matters. The team needs to first identify what is going on with their teammate. Next, they’ll need to figure out who is delaying the PC and how they’re doing it. This should help them discover that their teammate is in the thrall of a face fatale. Finally, the team will need to figure out how to fix the problem.

Removing the face fatale isn’t simple. They probably shouldn’t try to take on a street samurai directly, but the face fatale has been around for a while and is going to be looking for anyone trying to mess with their plans. The face fatale has their own assets, so if they sense any danger, they’ll be ready for fight or flight, perhaps with an assault team to back them up. A frontal assault may be a solution, but it shouldn’t be easy. Confrontingthe face fatale and negotiating is another possible way to remove them from the picture, but they’re a pretty good negotiator, and their timetable may or may not work with the team’s. Another answer might be an epic role-playing intervention with the character, making them see how lost in the claws of the face fatale they are.

In the end, the face fatale should be a challenge but not an insurmountable one. They aren’t the main storyline but rather a wrinkle to challenge the players. Even once they are gone, there should still be some emotional scars; their charms leave soul-damaging wounds, like a psychic assault cannon. It shouldn’t cripple the character forever, but it should take them a while to get back to normal.
