## **Upgrading Contacts**

**Building loyalty can take a lot of time, care, and work. The most reliable way to build up loyalty with a contact is to do business with them over a long period of time, gaining favors, and not taking advantage of them. Losing loyalty is easy and quick—a single betrayal is all it takes. If you demonstrate that you’re someone who can’t be trusted, or worse, bring danger and harm to your contacts, you’ll fnd that it’s nearly impossible to repair those relationships and make new ones. Sometimes a run requires you to do something that may inconvenience one or more of your own contacts—ideally, you’ll fnd this out while the Johnson is pitching the job to you, and you’ll have the opportunity to turn it down. If that doesn’t become apparent until you’ve already taken the job, you’ll have a tough decision to make: Do the job anyway and lose loyalty with your contact, or refuse to complete the job and hurt your reputation. If you’re careful and discreet, maybe your contact will never fnd out that it was your team that did the thing that inconvenienced them. Sometimes you can avoid this dilemma by giving your contact a heads-up about what’s about to go down so they can avoid the damage or even turn the situation to their advantage, but this carries the risk that if they can’t make out all right, they’ll defnitely know who to blame. The one major drawback from building up a large network of contacts is that there are far more opportunities to end up in a situation that benefts some at the expense of others. There’s no easy way to navigate conficts of interest—sometimes you just have to make a choice and pay the price.**
