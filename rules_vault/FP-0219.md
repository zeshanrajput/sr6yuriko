## **Stocks and Investments**

**After years of making informed stock-market trades, the members of the Tiding have amassed very healthy investment portfolios. None of this is held by the Tiding itself, as the Tiding is not incorporated, but the principals are willing to use their holdings as the Tiding requires. The holdings are vast enough that the interest and dividends alone are suffcient to fund the members’ day-to-day activities.**

**These investments include very little real estate besides the principals’ various homes. The Tiding maintains no permanent offces, instead renting space as needed.**

**Agents: Since the Tiding is not incorporated, it has no actual employees. Each principal has an array of “assistants” that they employ for various Tiding tasks, including the work of maintaining their network of contacts. The heart of the oper-**

**ation, though, is the Tiding’s core group of agents. Offcially, they are independent contractors hired by the principals, but by this point, they all know one another and are kept in the Tiding because they work well with the principals and each other. They have diverse skills that mirror and add to those of the principals, which means they ft the Tiding’s preferred mode of operation. They pride themselves on not being noticed and fnding ways to get eyes and ears everywhere.**

**Contacts: The Tiding has two contact lists: clients and resources. The names on the former are initially more impressive. It includes CEOs, governors, government department heads, drakes, and a dragon or two. They try to keep this list secret, but if you ever see it, you’ll get a sense of how highly regarded this group is—clearly, a lot of powerful people think the Tiding magpies know what they’re doing.**

**The second list has a lot of names you won’t recognize. It’s also a whole lot longer than the frst list. This list has personal assistants, physical facilities directors, paralegals—people who aren’t wellknown but spend most of their time getting things** _**done**_ **. They also have access to a wide range of facilities wherever they work. They are incredibly useful to know, which is why Tiding agents got to know them, and they put in the effort to stay in touch with them.**

**Skills: The Tiding is highly skill reliant. They want people who are the best at what they do. Since they don’t ever hire people as full employees, they have the freedom to hire people for a test run, then bring them into ever-more complicated jobs. This means that if the Tiding is interested in you, your frst job won’t be breaking into Celedyr’s lab. You’ll get much more of a milk run, then work your way up to the more diffcult tasks. The people who make it that far are quite good at what they do, so if you encounter a Tiding team in what’s supposed to be a secure space, expect their rigging, hacking, stealth, and all other skills (except, perhaps, combat) to be top-notch.**

- **Which tells you how to deal with them when you need to. If they’re weak in combat, that’s how you take them on.**
