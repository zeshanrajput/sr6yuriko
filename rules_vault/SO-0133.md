## Obstacles

Some secrets are worth killing to keep. And while the heat of the moment might carry them into the arms of temptation, the immediate remorse might just be powerful enough to motivate drastic measures to keep it quiet. This is more of a risk when you’re looking for leverage. If you lack morals, you could use a proxy to compromise them and leave them to take the consequences. Which, admittedly, can give you even more to use

against them. If it’s you on the spot, or you want to make sure your agent makes it out alive, be sure you have the means to neutralize any threats to making a clean exit.

**>** And for Ghost’s sake, don’t kill them. It defeats the whole purpose.

- Thorn

The higher the stakes, the more likely they have staff trained against this exact situation. That means counter-surveillance, vetting with background checks, you name it. You’ve got to either control the field or compensate to overcome it. Working with a lack of caution in the heat of the moment might get you past their own better judgment, in which case speed is essential and that remorse effect comes kicking in harder, so be ready to act on it either way.

The biggest killer of a Badger is that sometimes the mark has enough conviction to resist the play. Experience says no one’s truly immune, we all have one vice or another, some secret shame, but sometimes good sense or paranoia wins out. Whether you screwed it up or the mark just isn’t pliable to the play, you don’t have much choice but to pack up and try something else.

- Here’s the thing. Not everyone is willing to pay to keep secrets. If they don’t jump at the bait, then you may want to wiggle the line. Mention how bad this looks for them. Mention how much money a tabloid would pay for said image. See if that opens their wallets. In the worst case, where there’s no way to shake them down, reach out to a contact who feeds information to the tabloids and see if the photo is worth any nuyen.

- Haze

- Odds are they’ll brush you off early on, but be ready for complicated moments if they take it right to the limit and chicken out at the last second.
