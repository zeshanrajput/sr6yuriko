## Terminal Safehouse

The MCT team is there, but they have set up serious MCT-style defenses. Getting in is going to be a slugfest, but at least the location is buried deep in Terminal. As long as no explosive grenades get tossed, no one in the area is really going to care.

The site has Zero-Zone-style security but cannot extend that security out far, as they’re in tight quarters in Terminal.

Access to the site is a single alley entrance. At the foot of the alley are two beaten up dumpsters, both of which have seen better days, but both contain a surprise. Inside each is a remote turret loaded with a pair of SCK 100s and 100 rounds of standard ammo each. They have Pilot 4, Targeting 4, Body 3, and Armor 3, but the dumpster bumps their total Defense Rating to 10. If you don’t have the proper RFID FoF indicator, they open fire. As they open fire, garbage and trash fly into the air, and some of it is on



fire. Mostly for cinematic effect, but the runners could be distracted or use the fire to summon something.

The alley is twenty meters long with two chained (Structure 6) doors on the right wall and one on the left. The two right doors lead to stairwells down to storage. The one on the left opens to a missing stairwell, so instead of steps, there’s a three-meter drop into a soggy basement. This is important, as this is the only cover to take, since as the two dumpsters at the far end of the alley, flanking the doorway to the safehouse, also contain turrets (two Yamaha Raidens, APDS ammo [100 rounds], stun grenades [four each], Autopilot 5, Targeting 6, Body 4, Armor 4, dumpster bumps Defense Rating to 14).

Once the turrets engage, the team will join in from two upper-floor windows. They will not open the door for anything, knowing that the access for the door is a killbox from the side windows.

Inside, the runners will find a small safehouse with seven rooms, including a kitchen, sitting area,

two bathrooms, and three bedrooms. There are no obvious exits, but beneath and behind one of the toilets in the bathroom is a secret exit that leads out to an Ares Roadmaster set up for a quick run up to the street.

Inside the facility are seven members of the MCT Monad Hunters (all monads themselves, see p. 144). One is always in the Roadmaster, two are stationed near the front windows (which are accessed via a gangplank), two are stationed at the side windows (also on gangplanks), while the last two monitor the target (in this case Piper Jane). Piper Jane is in one of the bedrooms next to the guards, which has been made into a Faraday cage. It is the middle bedroom and has no exterior window. Piper’s wrists and ankles are held by plasteel restraints.

Piper will be very grateful to be rescued, but she still has work for the PCs. She will allow herself a little recovery time, then get back to work. She pays the PCs a lump sum of 10,000 nuyen to express her thanks.
