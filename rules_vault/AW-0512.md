## Base Environment And Terrain

The base environment and terrain of a metaplane work together, contributing to its appearance and general features. For example, depending on the terrain, a metaplane aspected toward Earth may be floating chunks of stone hanging in the void, scattered plateaus with unscalable cliffs surrounded by eternal clouds, grass-covered plains, gentle forested hills, broken scrubland of cliffs and canyons, steep mountain sides with narrow valleys, or endless underground caverns.

If you want a more varied result, roll two or even three times on each table, then either combine the results to make a hybrid environment or give the metaplane an assortment of terrain styles and aspects in different areas. For example, rolling Fire and Water combined with Badlands could produce a steam-shrouded metaplane of gigantic terraced hot springs with shallow boiling pools and mud flats dotted with eternally jetting geysers.

The local weather wasn’t included in these charts, but be sure to note it. Decide on the general temperature range and type of precipitation based on that range—a volcanic metaplane will be dropping pyroclastic ash, not snowflakes. And don’t forget that the runners will most likely translate to survive in whatever the environment is, so this will mainly be for defining the environment, not an obstacle to overcome.
