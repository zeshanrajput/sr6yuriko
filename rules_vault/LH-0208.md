## The White Wizard

The White Wizard, a.k.a. the Wizard of Olkhon Island, has been providing supplies and weapons essential in breaking the shapeshifters’ hold around Lake Baikal and further abroad. When the Saban Zaga had secured Olkhon Island, fortifications were quickly established, digging deep into the rock and sediment. Many shapeshifters and other Awakened agents have been displayed impaled or stretched out on the rock of Olkhon, creating a sense of fear and anger while strengthening the morale of the dissidents.

- Information about the Wizard is limited. They’ve remained incredibly reclusive and isolated from all but a select few over the years. The weapons were often items carved out of the bones of paracritters and imbued with venomous properties toward shapeshifters. Physical contact with the items always resulted in an extremely strong immune response in the target, nullifying shapeshifters’ heightened recovery and regeneration and inhibiting their ability to shift between their bestial and metahuman natures. The Wizard’s magic was powerful, and they seemed to be able to call upon any number of elemental or spiritual beings when they wanted to. In hindsight, all the signs were there; everyone was simply too distracted.

- Lei Kung

Back in late October 2082, a hunting party of Amur Tigers was engaged by a group of local



Awakened. These Awakened were led by Lumas and intercepted the AT near the town of Barguzin east of the lake. The entire AT hunting party was slain, while half the Awakened attackers were killed or wounded, including Lumas. Yakut sympathizers were able to extract the survivors back to the interior of the Awakened nation. It’s said that the items were examined by Vernya herself, who had come to try to save the wounded. She is said to be the one who explained the nature of what they were really up against.

The enchanting process was very advanced, and the venom used was an alchemical concoction with extreme potency. Further study confirmed that both were Disian in nature. It was the first real break to be made. It was probably the straw that also broke the proverbial camel’s back.

- A week after this incident, multiple assassination attempts were made against known Yakutian assets, leaders, and financial supporters. The location where Lumas had been recovering was found, and Oleg Vostozhian, one of the Amur Tigers leaders, claimed his skin. The raven Arlen was also slain using aerial toxins that poisoned an entire valley in the western highlands. Bzzz’t Cob, a dwarf rigger who frequently made the Trans-Siberian smuggling run was also found dead; his Venture light panzer was found skewered on several Pain Pines in the Eastern Region.
