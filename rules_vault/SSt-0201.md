## **Meet at a Hangout**

**This is easily my least favorite way to fnd someone for your team, but sometimes you need to fnd someone fast and someone present where you are, so you go to a runner bar or runner nightclub or wherever it is people like us gather, and you mingle and you appraise the people there, and you try to determine if someone is worth hiring.**

**Similar to when you go out looking for a quick hookup, don’t fool yourself into thinking you’re looking for Mr./Ms./Mx. Right. The person you fnd does not need to be the great love of your life, or a long-lasting relationship, or anything that lasts beyond the short amount of time you want it to last. They need to care about you and be reliable enough to look out for responsibilities connected to you for at least a few days. After that, you can break up and go your separate ways, and you can be as bitter about that as you want, as long as you got through the brief period you needed to get through.**

**So what can you look at that tells you what you need to know? The frst thing you need to do is watch. Get a scan of any room you walk into. Look at how people sit, how they take in the room. Look at how other people react to them. Is someone being avoided by nearly everyone in the place? There probably is a reason. Does the staff seem to treat the person with a mixture of kindness and pity? May not be the person you are looking for. Do people defer to the individual out of respect? Or fear? Learn to tell the difference, and learn which one you might want on your team.**

**Then, when you talk to someone, see if they change. Ask them to talk about themselves. See if they keep it about themselves all the time, and see if they elevate others. They’re obviously going to be evasive about some topics—that’s part of our job—but see if their evasions are smooth or clumsy. Ask them some direct questions about their pay, or about the last time they shot someone. See how they deal with discomfort.**

**Notice, though, that I’m telling you certain things to look for without saying which signs are the good ones and which are the bad ones. Sure, there are some clear tendencies—you usually want to connect with a good liar instead of a bad one, for example—but other parts are less defnite. Sometimes, you might want the more forceful personality, while other times you might want the person who can hover in the background. Body language isn’t always consistent—a person who keeps their arms folded the entire time they talk to you might turn out to be warm and engaging once you get past their initial barrier, and if you reject them based on an over-broad interpretation of how people carry themselves, you risk missing out on that. Behavior just isn’t consistent enough to know what a person is based on a few simple cues.**

**> And if it were, you can guarantee that people would start imitating certain gestures to try to imitate trustworthiness (which, in fact, happens all the time), and that would then make those gestures untrustworthy, so we’d have to find other ways of interpreting gestures, and we’d pretty much be where we are now, where nothing is guaranteed.**

**> Sunshine**

**More than any other area, this is where you need to go with your gut. It would be a good idea to ensure your instincts are well-honed. Or else send in someone whose instincts are.**
