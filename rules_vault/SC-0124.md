## bloodpanther

## playing advice

## Roleplaying

There are no unwilling Bloodpanthers. The path to becoming one is long, difficult, and fraught with a combination of physical and psychological trials. True, those who fail have a tendency to be consumed by their would-be sponsors, but those who reject the path and still remain viable members of AZT are often simply released back to their lives, compelled by threat or trauma to keep the tests secret. Those who succeed are ushered into a life of prestige, wealth, and freedom. A Bloodpanther bodyguard is almost as prestigious as a Jaguar Guard. Their bloodpanther pelts are worn with pride and distinction, and they are an honored member of Aztlan/Aztechnology culture, at least among those who recognize them. To all others, they are simply exceptionally impressive and powerful members of the megacorp. The reason you would seek this power is up to you, so long as it was a conscious choice and a path you not only survived but earned willingly. Immortality, riches, respect, freedom, strength, power—all are within the grasp of a Bloodpanther, and they tend to operate with astonishing latitude within the AZT structure. There is literally nothing stopping you from taking freelance work in the shadows, with all the accompanying opportunity to make money and contacts. The only two rules are first, never act against AZT interests; and second, make sure you are never traced back to the parent company by your actions. Failing either of these rules is grounds to be hunted and consumed by your fellow Bloodpanthers—a worthy motivation for them to succeed at any cost. There’s no leaving once you’re in, but at least you’re a part of the biggest, most powerful megacorporation in the world.

You have stock (perhaps literally) in its ascension to greater heights of power, and you’ll be able to enjoy the perks of it for all time. You cannot imagine the weakness of someone who wouldn’t seize such an opportunity by the throat and drink it in for all it’s worth.
