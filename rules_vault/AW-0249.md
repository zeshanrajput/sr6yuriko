## Local Fauna

There are no lifeforms of any kind beyond the microscopic—save for the insects. If there ever was any other kind of life here, it was gone a very long time before we got here, and there are no paleontological remains to indicate its existence. It stands to reason any such life would have been consumed or infested by the insects by now, which might account for the expressions of their manifest forms in this plane.

Unlike our own plane of existence, the insects are the natives and have no requirement to adapt themselves through investment. The majority of them express as large insects of their respective breed, though quite often manifesting largely superficial differences in color and chitin expression, usually denoting identity between one brood and another. These manifest as additional spikes, patterns, grooves, and sometimes alternative shapes, contours, lengths, and sometimes numbers of legs. If anything, it is akin to mutations in Earth insect species, though here such mutations are not weeded out or exiled but rather breed and grow.

The subject of breeding is one of curiosity. Infiltrated hives and Queen/Mother chambers lack any egg sacs as observed on Earth, only discarded physical mass that is usually broken down into Hive material. The architecture seems to indicate a metaphysical focusing lens or antenna, likely to serve as the focus for communicating with potential shamans in other planes. This lends credence to the idea that the bugs lack any ability to procreate without hosts, meaning we serve as a link in their natural cycle of life, as well as the theory that fully consumed universes are absorbed into the Hive.

This would further explain the hybrids. There are no metahuman hybrids on this plane. Indeed, those hybrids observed seem extraordinarily alien in composition, suggesting that the ranks of the Hive are replenished through planar absorption. This might seem encouraging to corporate directives initiating punitive actions to deplete their ranks, but I wish to remind the reader that this is literally an entire universe populated by the insects. Their numbers are beyond all counting, with theoretical additional universal populations bolstering them. It is, at present, inconceivable that we could, under any circumstances, eliminate their entire species in their native environment, even if we had access to every weapon available on Earth. I recommend we continue efforts at containment rather than reprisal. The bugs will never learn to fear us enough to overwhelm their instinct to use us, regardless of what Dr. Ellis’ Beta Program suggests.
