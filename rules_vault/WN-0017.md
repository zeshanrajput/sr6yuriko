## Land

Once upon a time, there were more than forty different ways into the city—today’s bridge-andtunnel crowd are limited to using eighteen. Mass transit provides some, vehicle tunnels provide a few more, and the rest are highly monitored bridge crossings, some of which can be traversed on foot.

Officially, there are four vehicle tunnels running under the waters and into New York City. The newest is the Ludmilla Reanka Memorial Tunnel, which only reopened in 2077 after a lengthy rebuild process plagued with setbacks that bordered on sabotage. The tunnel runs south from Battery City and into the fashionable Red Hook neighborhood of Brooklyn. On the West Side, the Lincoln and Holland tunnels both pass beneath the Hudson River into New Jersey. The Queens Midtown Tunnel is the only vehicle tunnel on the east side of the island.

- Queens Midtown runs parallel to the Steinway train tunnel, which used to service the old F, N, and R lines. The tunnel supposedly collapsed after the quake, but over the past few years there’s been talk that the tunnel is still intact. If you can find a way in and out on the other side, it could be a smugglers’ dream.
