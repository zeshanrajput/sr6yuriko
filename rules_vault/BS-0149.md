## Primitive Cyberlimb

Crudely articulated limbs that terminate in clamps or rubber stoppers rather than hands or feet are common sights on drones, but less so on metahumans. Such primitive limbs are built to provide basic functionality and little else. Since these prosthetics are built from solid materials without any regard for lifelike aesthetics, they are robustly constructed and always benefit from cyberlimb hardening (p. 291, _SR6_ ). Primitive limbs are much cheaper than other cyberlimbs, but you get what you pay for. A user loses 1 meter of movement for each full or partial primitive leg replacement, and the user can gain but may not spend Edge on any tests involving Agility using a primitive cyberlimb. Primitive cyberlimbs cannot be custom fitted (see below) and are not available in alphaware or higher grades.

**Cyberware overdrive** : A primitive cyberlimb gains +4 Strength for one test, which may exceed the augmented maximum.
