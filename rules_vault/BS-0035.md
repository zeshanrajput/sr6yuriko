## Minor prostheses are divided into categories:

**Category:** Sensory replacement (eyes, ears, nose, skin). **Use:** This is cosmetic only, like a glass eye that’s not able to see. These tend to be noticeable and harder to conceal than other prostheses. **Modifiers:** –1 die in social situations when you are dealing with someone with very specific body beliefs, or where displaying poverty would be an issue. **Cost:** 200¥ for materials, 100¥ for the surgery. Recovery time is overnight.

**Category:** Movement (feet, legs, and items such as wings or tails for metavariants that have them). **Use:** These restore most of the missing movement to a person. They are less smart than cyberware, and without the feedback from a biological or cyberleg, they can be more clumsy. **Modifiers:** –1 die in situations where a test is made that relies on careful placement of feet or legs while other things are competing for attention (such as combat). **Cost:** 50 to 300¥ for materials, 100¥ for the surgery. Recovery time is overnight. Training to use the limb properly takes 1 month.

**Category:** Manipulatory (arms and hands, but also prehensile tails, trunks, or any other manipulators). **Use:** These restore some functionality, but do not work as well as a person’s original equipment, cyberware, or bioware. **Modifiers:** –3 dice on fine manipulations where another manipulator is not available to assist. For example, trying to shoot a pistol with a prosthetic hand without another hand to steady it. **Cost:** 50 to 500¥ for materials, 100¥ for the surgery. Recovery time is overnight. Training to use the limb properly takes 1 month.

As one final benefit, minor prostheses are never affected by State of the Art rules.
