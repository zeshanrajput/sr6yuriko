## **Alliance and Conflict with Ghostwalker**

**The history of the Denver Data Haven is also intertwined with the history of Ghostwalker and his occupation of Denver. When the great dragon frst appeared in 2062 and started studying the Sixth World, it didn’t take long for the wyrm to understand the importance of the Matrix. Once he learned of a data haven operating within his claimed territory, he offered the Nexus an alliance (or ultimatum) in effect saying: You can stay as long as you work for me. Soon afterward, one of the admins, SilveryK—who had some history with Dunkelzahn—negotiated the actual agreement between the data haven and the dragon.**

**With Ghostwalker’s vast resources at their disposal, the Denver Data Haven soon built the wyrm an extremely powerful host, with top-tier security and a direct connection to the Heap. Christened the Dragon’s Lair, the physical location of the servers were rumored to be inside, or at least very near, Ghostwalker’s actual lair. Some of the Nexus personnel signed on to run the new host, and a Nexus admin using the name Crystal became the frst admin of the Dragon’s Lair. After her death during Crash 2.0, another Nexus admin, SilveryK, took over management of the dragon’s host.**

**This arrangement lasted for another ten years, with Ghostwalker providing protection and resources in exchange for unrestricted access to the Heap, plus whatever other Matrix services he needed. On October 10, 2074, an unknown group of hackers hit the Dragon’s Lair and opened it up to the Matrix. Despite the fact that a number of deckers and technomancers from the Nexus assisted in tracking down and recovering everything removed from the dragon’s hoard of data, Ghostwalker was enraged and claimed that the breach could only have happened with help from the Denver Data Haven. He withdrew his patronage immediately and violently, sending the ZFD troops to the old Air Force Academy to destroy the Alpha Site and arrest any Nexus personnel found there. Most of the admins and sysops had enough advance warning to escape, but SilveryK surrendered to the ZFD, claiming that she could explain things to Ghostwalker. A number of technomancers also remained at the Alpha Site, perhaps unwilling to leave their Matrix temple, and they were also taken into custody.**

- **I can understand why they wouldn’t leave the Temple voluntarily. Experiencing the Resonance coming off the well there is indescribable to someone who isn’t Emerged. I can believe that after a long enough exposure, a technomancer would become addicted to the pure power found there.**

- **Puck**

**While Ghostwalker’s attack on the physical location of the Nexus was unsuccessful in several ways, the wyrm seemed satisfed with the destruction. Perhaps it was only a warning, as there was no real effort made to track down and arrest the operators of the Nexus or to make any further attempts after the initial attack.**

**The data haven managed to get back online within days of the Alpha Site’s destruction through plans that were put in place after Crash 2.0, drawn up to manage any possible disaster. The Alpha Site had become an administrative center for the Nexus, with the actual host being generated by hundreds of small optical-stack mainframe clusters hidden across the Front Range Free Zone (FRFZ), the Pueblo Corporate Council (PCC), and even further across North America. This made the host virtually crash proof. Getting it back online was a simple matter of rerouting the network to go around the destroyed system elements at the Alpha Site.**

**After Dragon’s Lair severed ties with the Nexus, it was not clear how the Lair was rebuilt or how Ghostwalker received Matrix support. Recently, it has come to light that a secret society of technomancers, calling themselves the Followers of the Ancient Way, had begun operating in Denver in late ’75 or early ’76. From the few descriptions obtained, we believe this was organized by the hacker SilveryK, now using the name Prophetess. The frst disciples of the Ancient Way appear to have been drawn from the group of technomancers arrested at the Alpha Site. This group formed the core of a secretive technomancer tribe and pseudo-religious cult dedicated to serving Ghostwalker, who they refer to as the Prophet of the Seed. Considering the recent discoveries made by the Nexus with the Book of Salitura, it can be assumed that Ghostwalker had similar texts in his possession, and he used them to craft his own Matrix-themed theology, creating a cult of Emerged followers.**

- **While I resent the tone used by this author, the Prophet did indeed have a number of ancient scrolls describing the Seed of Knowledge from a previous age. His teachings from these texts have opened the eyes of many of the Emerged who now follow the great dragon as a leader and guide, despite his own lack of Resonance. The sacred scrolls taught us that shortly before their downfall, some of the ancients began to use magical devices for storing information. This practice created the Seed, or what some today would call the noosphere. The chosen ones who were able to perceive the Seed banded together and pooled their knowledge, writing the sacred texts. When the civilizations built on magic fell, the Seed of Knowledge faltered as well, never reaching its full potential in that age. But in truth, it was only dormant and has returned to us, growing stronger every year now. The accumulated knowledge of the Seed from the sacred scrolls remains true in this new age and provides a path to true enlightenment for those open to following their ancient way.**

- **> Prophetess**

**In 2078, Ghostwalker made a second attempt to remove the Nexus from Denver. This time, the ZFD troops surrounded and destroyed the Beta Site of the Nexus at the Fitzsimons Army Hospital, used as the primary center of the Nexus after the Alpha Site’s destruction. We now know that this was simply a small part of a wider plan to remove the Aztlaners from the FRFZ. But whether this was intentional or collateral damage, the Denver Data Haven had to relocate again to a new complex, perhaps to the location known as the Gamma Site, now called the new Beta Site.**

**After March 2079, Ghostwalker had succeeded in consolidating his power and driving out the last remaining foreign forces. It also appears that he decided that mending fences with the Nexus would be more productive than continuing to persecute them. A decker using the name Spyder started posting on the Nexus and offered large sums of nuyen to the data haven from an unnamed patron, with no strings attached. It was soon revealed the Spyder was in the employ of Ghostwalker, sparking a furious debate on both his membership and the offered money. While no apology or offcial acknowledgement was offered by the wyrm, it appears that the nuyen was eventually accepted and continues to be. Spyder has been allowed to remain on the data haven, but his actual status is unclear, as is Ghostwalker’s true level of patronage.**

**While perhaps not directly related to the Denver Data Haven, there has been a great deal of activity at the old Air Force Academy site, formerly the Alpha Site of the Nexus. Early in 2081, ZDF forces established a secure perimeter around the area. Construction equipment and personnel soon moved in. The wreckage of the original buildings was removed, foundations were dug out, and the framework of a massive structure soon started reaching skyward. As of the writing of this report, the purpose of the unfnished structure is unknown. Considering the current renewed patronage from Ghostwalker, this structure could involve the data haven in some way, but it is just as likely to involve the mysterious Followers of the Ancient Way.**
