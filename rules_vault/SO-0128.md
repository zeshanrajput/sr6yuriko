## Scaling

I’ve seen this used by actual brokers hoping to build their résumé until they make connections enough to be able to rely on good advice, but that often backfires. Still, every now and again you can sell your speculation stable to one of these low-level climbers and leave the mess in their hands. I suppose it might be possible for a bookie too, but they tend to be more conscious of out-and-out scams for sale.

It can be tempting to hang on to profitable marks, but mobility is an essential element of survival in this one. Every list is for one-use only, whether they bite or walk. If you make a career out of this con (which is anything but reliably profitable, but plausible through sheer odds), you could rotate and scramble over time if you use enough randomization in your marks and approach with a sufficiently different angle. That said, I really don’t think you can pull this off forever without getting caught, unless you’re extremely modest in your returns and patient.
