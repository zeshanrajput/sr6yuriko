## Bodyware

## Auto-Injector

The earliest auto-injectors were pre-cybernetic implants that released medication into a person’s body. Today, a cybernetic auto-injector releases pharmaceuticals on command rather than over pre-programmed intervals. Of course, nothing stops a user from replacing medicine with something recreational, or even a combat drug. A standard auto-injector stores one dose of a chemical, which is normally refilled via external access port. If the user also has a chemical gland (see p. 57) then that bioware’s output can be stored in this cyberware. Optionally, an auto-injector can have its reservoir expanded to hold more doses before requiring refill. A reservoir cannot mix doses of different kinds of drugs, but multiple auto-injectors can be implanted and loaded with the various components of a juicer’s cocktail. It takes a Minor Action to activate an auto-injector, and the drug or toxin effect takes place according to its Speed (p. 121, _SR6_ ).

A killswitch is a variant form of auto-injector where the user has no control over the implant. The killswitch dispenses its payload upon preset conditions being met, or upon receipt of a special wireless signal from its true owner.

**Wireless bonus** : An auto-injector can sync with a wireless biomonitor to keep the user updated with information about the drug’s effects, including an estimated time they will wear off.
