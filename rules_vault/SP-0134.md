## **Reagent Specialization**

**Increasingly, we’re learning that some reagents are better suited for some tasks than others. Let me be clear that as far as I understand, this is currently the exception rather than the rule. For the most part, reagents will remain their versatile selves, giving you a certain amount of bang per dram for a wide variety of tasks. Those outlier cases, though, suggest some interesting possibilities. Of particular interest are reagents that can provide a boost in banishing spirits.**

**The trick of this line of research is it has taken some dark paths in recent years. Not too long ago, talk circulated in magic back channels about a new reagent with exceptional mana-retaining properties. This helped it play a central role in making powerful preparations. The problem with this reagent was contained right in its name: veins of the adept. Yes, people were using bits of adepts’ bodies in their magic. And of course, researchers (though I use the word loosely when talking about this kind of investigation) determined that you either need to harvest veins**

**from a living person or from a corpse within a month of their death. The good news is that we aren’t going to have a bunch of people digging up long-dead adepts to use parts of their bodies. The bad news is, living and recently dead adepts became a target.**

- **Does that mean cash-poor adepts might make some scratch selling their veins on the magical black market? You bet! But be cautious. The more you damage a vein, the longer it takes to repair, and it might not ever regain full circulation. Your body might be able to grow new veins, but that can be a time-consuming process, too. Plus, some of the street docs who might try to harvest a vein aren’t especially skilled at avoiding nerve damage. They might think they’re going for an easily accessible vein in the wrist, but after the procedure, your hand doesn’t make a proper fist—which is bad news for a lot of adepts. Also remember than while the veins are powerful, few reagents can do much with a really tiny amount. You might find out the cutter plans to take more of your veins than you were comfortable offering.**

- **Ma’Fan**

**The use of veins of adepts in magic was both exciting and disturbing, and it led to an inevitable follow-up question: What other parts of the bodies of magical beings could be harvested and used? I don’t have to tell you that several parties have been enthusiastically exploring this avenue.**

- **And guess which Aztlan-based corporation a lot of these “researchers” work for!**

- **Frosty**

**While Horizon would very much like to not participate in this research, they’re quite willing to disseminate some results among those who would like to know them—Wuxing and MCT being the major interested parties.**

- **Cosmo states this somewhat causally, but this is a big, complicated deal. Aztechnology doesn’t do dangerous, unethical research just to share what they learn with others. If they discover new reagents, or new uses for old reagents, they want at least a year or two to benefit from it before anyone else catches on. Convincing them to share what they learn—and share it accurately—would be a feat worthy of Horizon’s PR prowess.**

- **Just remember that Horizon is the second-greatest PR power in the world—after Aztechnology.**
