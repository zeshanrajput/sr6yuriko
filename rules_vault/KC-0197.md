## I Love the Nightlife, I Like to Noodle

The HKFEZ is a glowing tip of the Southeast Asian coast, but it’s time to focus on some other locations of interest.

**Fortress Hill Station** includes the eastern edge of the Wan Chai and the eastern area districts on Hong Kong Island. At night, the area is a major social gathering spot. Noodle shops, NERPS Huts, Taco Temple, and the Bok Choi dance club can be found here. Bok Choi can contain nearly a thousand ravers and neon-kiddies with the latest music to make your (wannabe) teen ears bleed love. The Taco Temple found here is nearly as unique, offering an entire sit-down service along its five stories of patio-like decking on the outside of the building. The side streets in the area are filled with all the small clubs, restaurants, and dive bar delights you could want, with a lot less danger than its equivalents in Yuen Long or Sai Kung.

**Shaw Brothers Actors Studio** on the corner of Clear Water Bay Road and Ngan Ying Road, Southern Coasts District, Hong Kong Island. Offering in-person tours as well as virtual visitations, the entire property was entered in the Hong Kong Antiquities Advisory Board right after the Awakening and found new life as people visiting often encountered what have been described as “echoes.”



These are astral manifestations built up by the impact of the Shaw Brothers’ first studio. Studied for years afterward, the preservation actions dried up in 2020 after significant development interests expressed a desire to own the properties and convert them to luxury condominiums and high-rise townhomes. The movement met with stiff resistance, and in the aftermath of VITAS and the first Crash, the site remained relatively unused. In 2036, a collective of adepts and practitioners of Wushu, Opera, and Kung Fu gathered and petitioned for the right to have dojos and acting studios reinstated in the complex. Crowdsourced funding tapped into the nostalgia of the Shaw Brothers and the impact they had upon the Hollywood-style studio productions. The location to this date remains a mecca for many practitioners of Wushu, Jeet-Kun-Do, and Tàijí quán. Wuxing, SpIn Entertainment, Ares Global Entertainment, and Sony Multimedia have all made claims on this piece of history.

Some of them reflect the dates of release for the entertainment in question. Because of the actors and operatic guild that owns it, no one gets to come in and dig into the astral ebb and flow of the area.

The views from **Victoria Peak** overlooking the northern shore of the Downtown district have been a photographer’s paradise for generations. Community towers and adaptive office facilities abound here, nestled between the Tai Ping Shan medical complex and the University of Hong Kong. They attract bright young minds to mingle with experienced scientists and mid-level corporate suits. Research groups such as those with Shibata C&E, MIB Financial Futures, and Yingyong can be found here. Yakashima has also found a home away from home for its operations in Hong Kong and nearby regions. This area is one of the more heavily patrolled parts of Hong Kong Island because of the massive biotech and research industries found within. At the edges of the urban sprawl along the mountainous interior, the high-rises give way to smaller structures, many of which are over a century old and serve as homes to numerous Buddhist, Daoist, and Confucian temples and lodges. Victoria Peak Gardens on the southern slope of Victoria Peak remains open to the public, with large parts of the land restricted to allow restoration services to help maintain the vibrant forests and lands at the island’s interior.

- And on just the other side of mountains to the south stands Aberdeen Tower, Wuxing’s influential pride and joy. One of the ley lines routed toward the tower passes along the median of Lung Fu Shan park and Victoria Peak, strongly representing the forces of Earth. Those invoking these forces while in the area of influence definitely gain a boon to their work—if they manage the forces correctly.
