## Sigma

## Non-Binary Human

Sigma belongs to a special kind of corporate breed that is typical for Berlin and is called a Konzernhai (corporate shark, or corpshark in short). Especially in the years of Flux State, many managers, developers, and security guards from outside were dumped in the “anarchistic hellhole” because they were too radical or otherwise unbearable elsewhere. Other corpsharks were born in the muck of Berlin’s streets and fought their way up, position by position, through cold tactics, ruthlessness, supreme brutality, and an insatiable greed for success and wealth—fighting for opportunities they would never have had elsewhere, because Berlin’s insatiable hunger for people capable of executing the corporations’ lofty plans has always stood in stark contrast to the unwillingness of the “normal” managers to expose themselves to the chaos and higher lethality of Berlin.

What all Konzernhais have in common is that they bear more resemblance to crime bosses than to the private university graduates who clog up managerial jobs elsewhere. Most of this special breed have booted out more than one competitor by using shadowrunners—or by putting a bullet in the head of a competitor themselves.

Sigma works for Evo but, like any Konzernhai, is mainly beholden to themself. Speaking of which: Sigma prefers the pronouns they/them but doesn’t let this become an obstacle to business: Whichever pronoun a business partner (or runner) first uses toward the well-toned, visually perfected, androgynous beauty, they assume for the ongoing interaction. Should someone ask for the preferred form of address, they take positive note of it, but when push comes to shove, Sigma is just as quick to stab even the politest of parties in the back.

Sigma’s trademark—besides the goat eyes they sometimes shows by lowering their smart glasses, which can be very irritating—are black business cards printed with nothing but the Greek letter Sigma and an RFID with a call link for their commlink. Most of Sigma’s jobs focus on promoting biotechnology, genetic engineering, or trans-humanism.
