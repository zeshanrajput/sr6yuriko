## **Toxic Zones**

**Before we talk about how to survive a toxic zone, we need to talk about defnitions. It’s a common joke when something is denoted as a toxic zone to ask, “How can you tell?” Most of us live in environments with high levels of ambient pollution, and it affects the water we drink, the plants that grow around us, and the air we breathe. So how is something like the SOX or the Scottish Irradiated Zone different from <gestures broadly> all of this?**

**One common defnition is that a toxic zone is an environment that poses an immediate threat to all of the life within it, but again, how is that different from every place else? The Scottish Irradiated Zone is a toxic zone; Glow City is not, at least not offcially. What’s the difference? Perhaps the intensity and size of the radiated area, but both will shorten your life if you decide to hang out there too much. But let’s just say it: Declaring Glow City a toxic zone, moving people away from it, and acting like you might attempt to remediate it is a lot of effort. The Seattle government doesn’t want that level of bother, and the people there don’t seem too keen to be evicted, so the government lets them careen toward a shortened lifespan, and the residents keep boarding that ride.**

**So rather than try to draw a clear line between what is or is not a toxic zone, let’s just stick to the basics: A toxic zone is an area so polluted that spending a signifcant amount of time in it could directly lead to your premature death. Even worse, some species have adapted to this hostile environment, making them dangerous and usually quite grumpy.**

**One of the most common sources of toxic zones is radiation. The environmental upheavals of this century combined with cost-cutting corporations who thought safety concerns were a luxury, and this led to several nuclear plant meltdowns. The territory near these plants will remain toxic for a long time. How long? Obviously that depends on how close you get to the center of the event. Meltdowns create an exceedingly toxic substance called corium. At its worst, it will kill you if you spend just a few minutes near it. Radiation scatters the farther away you are from the source, but your risk of radiation sickness and cancer grows quickly the closer you get.**

**Radiation isn’t the only source of toxicity. Industrial, commercial, and residential waste production are all signifcant contributors. They create the low-level pollution we deal with as the background noise of our lives, but sometimes they combine into something bigger. Take Tsimshian. In order to gain a competitive advantage with neigh-**

**boring nations, they relaxed every environmental guideline they had, and when that wasn’t enough, they actively encouraged companies to come to their nation and pollute. This led to runaway mining and deforestation, and the land got worse and worse. This in turn affected the manasphere, leaving it twisted in some places, practically empty in others. In some spots, the only spellcasters who can operate are toxic shamans, which tells you a lot about the atmosphere of those places.**

**Wandering around such places is a nightmare. Their air is acidic, the water more so. Smoke and steam rise from unexpected places and always smell sulfurous. You don’t dare take a drink, even if you boil the water. What plants and animals there are are twisted and ravenous, as living on blighted land seems to infect them with an unending hunger. There is no safe place to lie down, no place to take a rest. The psychological burden of moving through such an area cannot be overstated.**

**But enough description. I’m supposed to be talking about survival. Obviously, a suit that protects you from whatever toxin is prevalent is a must. Don’t get a cheap one. If you can’t afford a good suit, don’t go to one of these places.**

**Even if you get a good suit, don’t overestimate its protective value. Even a good radiation suit will only give you a few hours of protection in a highly irradiated place. No matter what, you want to get in and get out in short order. You also need to remember that these suits are bulky and clumsy— any fne motor skill is going to be very diffcult when you’re wearing them. But don’t let that tempt you to, say, take the gloves off for a few activities. Any gap in the coverage of these suits dramatically reduces their protective value.**

- **What I’m about to say may be an overstatement, but not much of one: When Mr. Johnson sends you to a toxic zone, he expects you to die. The basics of such a job often aren’t difficult—a lot of the time it’s about going to a spot and fetching some item or another. It’s the sort of thing anyone could do easily, were it not for the terrain. But the terrain is there, and it’s so nasty that Mr. Johnson would rather spend money paying us to go there than buy some protective gear and go there himself. That tells you that he thinks the protective gear will not be enough—and whoever he sends in are sacrificial lambs.**

**Besides protection, you’ll want some antidotes to whatever you might encounter. Rock lizard**

**blood is perhaps the most versatile option you can use, as it gives a general protection against just about any toxin. Just be ready for it to wear off, though, as it has a vicious kick at the end of its effective time.**

**You may notice a common thread in what I’m talking about here—radiation suits, antidotes, and so on don’t last forever. You’re often talking about a matter of hours—perhaps two or less—to get the job done. This means you don’t have time to go in, scope out the area, and then sit down and make a plan. You also might not be able to do a lot of improvisation or last-minute adjustment of your plan. You need to make a plan that gets you in and out of the zone in your allotted time, and you need to stick to it.**

- **I can’t emphasize this enough: Send in drones to map the area before you go in. Don’t rely on anyone else’s maps, as these areas are highly changeable. A drone will stand up to radiation better than you will, especially if you apply some extra protection. Even if a drone or two get wrecked in the effort, it will be more than worth it to be certain you have a correct image of the terrain.**
