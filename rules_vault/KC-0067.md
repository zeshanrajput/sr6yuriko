## _Jalky:_ Why?

_Bungo:_ True. But doesn’t it also seem too convenient? Too easy?

_Jalky:_ Do we really have to go and complicate things?

_Bungo:_ We need to understand them, don’t we?

_Bungo:_ Now _that’s_ the real question. But look at the timing. Look how close their action was to ours. Can we believe the two actions were not related?

_Eligius:_ Correlation does not imply causation. Or connection.

_Jalky:_ I suppose.

_Bungo:_ McCann is tense when we first talk to him, but he becomes more relaxed when we tell him that the hackers didn’t really know what they were doing.

_Bungo:_ But if they were connected, wouldn’t that be something McCann would not want us to know? And wouldn’t he be relieved once he understood we weren’t looking at such a connection?

_Jalky:_ Maybe, but this is all really tenuous.

_Eligius:_ We tell him they’re not likely to be an ongoing threat, so he relaxes.

_Bungo:_ But we don’t know that they ever were going to be a threat to him. They hit the same target he did—that would make them potential allies.

_Jalky:_ They also might interfere with McCann’s work. Get in the way.

_Bungo:_ True. That would be a source of relief, to find out they’re likely not going to be a problem. But would that cause much tension? When we’re about to start a job, do we stress a lot that someone might get in our way?

_Jalky:_ I assume someone will be getting in our way as a matter of course.

_Bungo:_ Exactly. It’s so normal, it’s not worth extra tension. So what was McCann so worried about?
