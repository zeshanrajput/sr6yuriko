Accessing the library may even be possible with crystal balls or magic mirrors, seeing the information in the books without removing them from their shelves.

**Steampunk** : This is a well-defined genre that needs little elaboration here. Devices are either spring-wound clockworks or steam-powered devices with some integrated heat source. Electronic (or optical) and electrical devices do not exist, but the mechanical clockwork devices and controls at this level can be extraordinarily complex. As magic enters the picture, the rules of how devices operate expand, and what they can do becomes broader, as realistic physics no longer apply to their operation and mana-infused coal or self-winding springs provide the power. Steam-powered analytical engines using punchcard data entry generate enough of a noosphere for technomancers to read the data, but deckers will be limited to the manual interface used in these very primitive computing devices.

**Dieselpunk** : Internal-combustion engines operate trains, tanks, and ground vehicles. Zeppelins, jetpacks, and other flying machines fill the skies, while electric lights turn the darkness into day. Communication is through wired telephone systems or primitive radio signals. Information processing and computing is analog, based on building-sized analytical engines grinding out equations using endless banks of gears and cogs, the data shown on clattering mechanical displays. Increasing the magic of the metaplane moves everything away from realistic physics and allows the technology to be more fantastical in its applications.

**Cyberpunk** : The metaplane’s level of technology is on par with the Sixth World, including a version of the Matrix that will be very familiar to the runners. The level of magic available on the metaplane will be the main factor regarding the familiarity of the technology, with metaplanes having high magic making full use of hybrid magical/ technological devices.

**Atompunk** : A level of technology far advanced from the world the players know. Seemingly impossible technologies like floating cities, teleportation, anti-gravity in every household device, and sentient robots are commonplace. While the technology already seems to be almost magical, adding high levels of magic to the mix creates truly mind-bending possibilities. Hackers may have some difficulty accessing the data systems, as the technology will be beyond anything they have experienced.
