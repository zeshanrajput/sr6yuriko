## **Arang-Utan**

## **(Pongo arangus)**

**Mythology becoming reality is just something we need to accept in the Sixth World. These tales came from somewhere, and while Fifth World psychologists and anthropologists would have had you believing it was human’s simple mind trying to explain what they do not understand, we now have to accept that many of these stories were real. Glimpses of a previous world passed down through cultures that have not been coerced or coopted by a purely scientifc mindset during the waning era of magic. The arang-utan is a piece of that mythology. They are an Awakened orangutan that has returned to the world to fulfll the myths of Indonesia by punishing the bad. They are bogeyman of sorts, but they have not fully adjusted to the world at large. The world at large (a.k.a. megacorps), however, has certainly found a way to make use of them.**

**First off, let me just point out that arang-utans are even more intelligent than their progenitor orangutans. Their ability to learn and communicate is sapient, though not the same as ours. They are all magically active but not spellcasters or summoners. Instead, they infuse objects with power and give them as boons or banes. Truly interesting and relatively rare. Their truest power is one of insight. They are excellent aura readers, and I hypothesize that back in the era of myth, this is how they identifed “bad” people. Today, there is some level of that, but a more rudimentary and apparently primal sense has put them on the leash of a few megacorps: a deep dislike for damaged Essence. Any missing Essence, whether from enhancement, surgery, or damage from bodily abuse, puts them off. Truly low Essence, like that of street samurai or other heavily enhanced individuals, makes them actively aggressive. Security uses them at checkpoints to keep an eye out for runners or other threats.**

**The appearance of an arang-utan is very close to their mundane kin. The only exception is the eyes. The arang-utan has entirely white eyes. They**



**aren’t blind, but as they spend more time perceiving the astral, their eyes develop a natural defense against light damage that also provides them with excellent night vision. Males and females display the same gender variations as their mundane progenitors. Arang-utans live and still breed with mundane orangutans.**

**Indonesian cultures that still honor and revere the arang-utan will bring those suspected of crimes or wrong-doing out to the apes’ territory. From up in their arboreal domain, the arang-utan will pass judgment and drop a piece of bark, a leaf, or something else from their tree home. The accused must pick up the object to receive their fate or choose banishment and leave the object where it lands. No one else would dare touch it for fear of taking on the fate of the accused.**

**The object is obviously a preparation triggered by contact with the accused, but what spell it activates varies widely between arang-utans. Some are more violent, triggering a combat spell, while some are subtle and place an illusion or manipulation spell marking the accused or making them invisible and essentially shunned.**

**By the same effort, tribes perform rituals that alert arang-utans of a coming event. After the ritual, the tribe members will go to the arang-utans and receive boons, tokens given to them that grant them powers. As this critter is sapient, it cannot be easily trained or tricked into creating banes and boons on command.**

**While I was doing a study with some nagas of Indonesia, I was introduced to the Sarang family. They are a tribal family that has long held and passed down the myth of the arang-utan and now act as protectors of the returned species. They were welcoming and happy to speak with me about the majestic beings but would not bring me to meet them. I couldn’t get a reason why, so I took it on myself to sneak off and have a visit on my own.**

**The orangutans and arang-utans did not like that. I’m not positive why, but I have an idea that since I was told to stay away, my aura would give away that I was doing something wrong, so I made sure to leave my masking up. Turns out they can break through masking and likely don’t appreciate the falsehood.**

**Rather than a great chance to research, I got something tossed my way that exploded in a burst of force that sent me fying and gave me quite the hurting. After the locals found out, I was made to go back before the arang-utan, masking down, and apologize. It was humbling, but rewarding, because it got me in back on their good side and able to return when I have more time.**
