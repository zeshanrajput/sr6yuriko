## Item 2: Article from Omaha World-Herald Business Section, April 15, 2081 

OMAHA—A new corporation in the city is providing one of the first bits of good news for the beleaguered Omaha economy while also providing hope of an eventual rebirth for the city. Buzzard Transit has an ominous name, but CEO Brock Taub promises the name has nothing to do with the current economic downturn. 

“If I thought the city was about to be a ruin, I wouldn’t be starting a new company,” Taub said. “We’re a resilient city, and we’re going to bounce back. I plan to be a part of that.” 

Buzzard has an automated fleet of vans that can haul people, goods, and even food anywhere in the metroplex. Every van is highly adaptable, with advanced materials allowing any configuration—passengers, cargo, mixed passengers and cargo, shelves, and so on. The configuration of the interior can shift between pickups or even while in transit, with navigational algorithms allowing for maximum efficiency in pickups and drop-offs. That efficiency, combined with the automated pilot that allows the vehicles to be on the road constantly, keeps rates low. Taub says a trip from downtown to Council Bluffs using Buzzard would cost 40 percent less than a comparable taxi ride. It may not be quite as direct, but Taub says the time difference is minimal. 

“Naturally, when humans are on board, their destination is prioritized over goods, except in rare circumstances,” he said. “The goal is simple: Get everyone and everything where they should be as quickly and smoothly as possible.”
