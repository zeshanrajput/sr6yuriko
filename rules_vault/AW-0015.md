## SO YOU WANT TO VISIT A METAPLANE

## Posted by: Wayfinder

So you want to visit a metaplane—or so you think. While I’m certainly not here to dissuade you from your dreams of astral exploration, or to turn you away from a desperate course of action that your life or livelihood likely depends on, it would be careless of me to share what I know without a pragmatic warning or three. So before I get properly started, let me say this: do not enter the metaplanes lightly. They are dangerous realms that don’t often follow the laws of nature that you’re familiar with, populated by incredibly diverse denizens to whom metahuman ideas and motivations are strange—or even alien. Most of them are uninterested in harming astral travelers, and a great many of them can be very helpful. That largely depends upon where you go, of course—many regions of the plane of metahumanity resemble the physical world we know and are populated with beings that are aware of metahumanity’s history, current events, and even popular culture. If you happen to

stray into the Wastes of Miggon, you had best be on your guard against anyone you encounter. And good luck asking directions from a sapient cloud living in the border space between the planes of air and water—I believe that I will never truly know if those lightning bolts were meant as an attack or an attempt to communicate.

The very land itself (or in some cases, a complete lack of land) is also fraught with perils for the unwary traveler. Gravity seems to work differently depending on which metaplane you are visiting, and space and time are just as malleable. Places that are more stable, in which the laws of physics as we know them seem to have caught on, are in the minority. In these places, you can walk about as you would on Earth. You can breathe the air, drink the water, and find food that will nourish your body. However, I would caution you against ingesting anything you find on your metaplanar travels unless you are absolutely certain that it is safe.

There are many other places that will not make any sense at all to the unaccustomed traveler, andit can be very easy to get lost or fall victim to a hazard or predator. I have visited metaplanes in which the very world itself seemed to want to destroy me however it could—places where solid ground transformed into sticky tar, or where the storm clouds seemed to be aiming their lightning bolts directly upon me (they were), and others where every few steps brought me to a new environment with no apparent portals or gateways to warn me of a transition.

The twin keys to surviving any astral exploration are knowledge and humility. Do not visit the metaplanes lightly or without a clear purpose. Do your research and legwork as best you can to learn about the places you’re going to, but don’t trust your knowledge too far. You must temper any self-confidence that derives from obtaining this knowledge with an acceptance that it will be insufficient. The world that you come from is not the same as the metaplanes. The physical world is a place where our species has evolved to thrive in. The metaplanes are not. The very instincts that you rely upon to survive within that world could get you killed just as easily as they might guide you well. Keep an open mind, try to avoid forming expectations, and don’t get comfortable.

Astral quests always have a narrow, clearly defined focus, and rarely involve travel to the much more dangerous far reaches of the metaplanes. If you’re curious about the metaplanes and are a magician, I highly recommend that you begin your explorations with an astral quest ordeal as part of an initiation. I won’t promise you that it will be safe or easy—you will encounter the Dweller on the Threshold, and that’s just for starters— but I can promise that it is safest way to get your first taste of metaplanar travel. Just be sure to have excellent wards in place before you leave, and take measures to prevent interruptions from the physical world. While projecting to the metaplanes, as with any other form of astral projection, your body is helpless and vulnerable to physical harm and astral possession.
