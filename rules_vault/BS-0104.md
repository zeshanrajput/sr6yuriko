## Headware

## Active Control Rig (Rating 1-2)

Active control rigs are based on EuroWars-era military R&D. Because this early form of control rig does not permit jumping-in remotely or entering VR, it fell out of favor compared to the now-standard form of control rig. This older control rig technology hasn’t fallen completely into obsolescence due to its relevance in corporate sponsored and funded motorsports. Motorcyclists do not want to be VR-comatose while competing in combat sports like combat biker or urban brawl, and the inability to remotely pilot is no downside in racing leagues where remote control is prohibited. Naturally, it also sees use in the shadows thanks to go-gangers and wired samurai who also wish to turn a mean wheel.

An active control rig functions as a control rig (p. 283, _SR6_ ) but it does not require (or indeed, permit!) the rigger to be in VR to jump-in, and neither does it permit the rigger to remotely jump into a vehicle they are not physically driving. Active control rigs must employ a physical data cable to establish and maintain a direct connection between the implant and the vehicle’s control board.

**Wireless bonus:** An active control rig is inherently a throwback device and can never be wirelessly enabled.
