## MANAINFUSED MANNERS

## Spells

As a mage, players have many options at their disposal that could be used to manipulate a social encounter. Whether it’s using magic to your own benefit or an ally’s social graces or to deceive a target, it can be an extremely powerful tool in the kit of any face character. That’s if they can conceal its use (see p. 126, _SR6_ **Recognizing Magic** or p. 35, _Street Wyrd,_ **Illusion Terms and Conditions** ). Such uses include but are not limited to: Detection spells to case a scene or get the read on a subject, Increase Attribute to boost Charisma, using Illusion spells to change your appearance, the environment, or just sowing general confusion, or Manipulation spells to directly control your target.

There are several existing spells that can be used to apply the new Social Statuses (p. 44). While this is by no means an exhaustive list, below are examples on how to use specific spells by player and gamemaster as a roadmap for their creative ideas. In all cases the actual spellcasting

does not change, only how the effects may be used to your advantage.

**Mind Probe** is an obvious choice. After successfully casting the spell with 3+ net hits and allowing the caster to probe the target’s consciousness, you gain the Ace in Hand (#) status, with the number determined by the amount of net hits over the threshold. At the GM’s discretion, 5+ hits may be needed if the target is sufficiently guarded enough to keep any such weakness buried in their subconscious.

**Mask and Physical Mask** can be used to inflict others with the Fish out of Water status or used to negate it upon oneself. The Fashion spell can also be used in this way but is not illusionary.

**Control Thoughts** is quite insidious, it can allow the caster to inflict Misdirected, Follower, or Smitten upon a target, with net hits determining what level of the status is if applicable. If a target recognizes the spell’s effect on them, then at the GM’s discretion they may gain the Suckered status as well.

Double Image can be used to apply Misdirected, as the target must split their attention betweentwo different subjects unless they see through the illusion. Disregard is another spell that can be used to misdirect a target, but it is more subtle. In either case, the level of the status is determined by the net hits of the Sorcery test.

Mob Mood and Mob Mind can be used to apply the Smitten status to someone if the net hits of the spellcasting test are greater than the target’s Willpower.
