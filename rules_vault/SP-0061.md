## **Spinrad Global**

**Lofwyr and Johnny Spinrad had a longstanding rivalry, and there has been talk of Lofwyr actually missing his famboyant counterpart. That has not, however, stopped him from looking at ways to take advantage of the instability at Spinrad following their leader’s demise. He’s mainly focused on fnancial operations, having his people see what kind of revised terms they can slip through on any agreements while leadership is preoccupied with the transition. He also has made overtures about buying subsidiaries under the guise of helping the corp streamline as they fgure out what they are going to be.**

**So far, though, Gabrielle Spinrad has proven to be a savvy operator, making sure Lofwyr’s attempted revisions do not pass and politely refusing any deals he proposes. Lofwyr, of course, hates this, so expect him to get a little more aggressive, if only because he can’t stand a Spinrad being an obstacle to him.**

**None of this, of course, has much bearing on the Dis situation. The relevance is curiosity about where Lofwyr’s priorities stand. How badly does he want to strike out at his rival? Could pro- or anti-Dis forces offer Saeder-Krupp something in his fght against Spinrad to move Lofwyr to help**



**them? Or will he continue to evaluate the Dis situation and not let his rivalry with Spinrad sway him in any direction?**
