## **Changes due to the Matrix**

**The technology of information exchange and storage has been in a constant state of change since its inception. This affected every organization, not just law enforcement, as the**



**constant change required constant upgrades and retraining. In the Sixth World this change was accelerated by a series of rapid technological advances and just as rapid system-wide failures. The worldwide information system known as the internet radically changed with the advent of Artifcial Sensory Induction System Technology (ASIST), also known as simsense. By the 2020s the frst datajacks and cyberterminals appeared, allowing for rapid acceptance of the technology and increasing the utility of computer systems for everything from surveillance to data mining. The Crash Virus ended the internet in 2029, crashing the entire network and generating worldwide chaos. This included massive data losses in every system, and for law enforcement organizations this meant recreating criminal records and offender databases almost from scratch.**

**After the crash of ’29, the global grid was slowly rebuilt as a dedicated ASIST-based system, and the Matrix was born. A new paradigm that required new skills from both hackers and cops. By the 2050s, cyberterminals had evolved into portable cyberdecks and this new technology quickly made its way onto the streets. On-site hacks into vehicles, weapons, comms, and even cyberware made offcers vulnerable to criminal hackers, and law enforcement was forced to respond in kind. White-hat hackers were moved out from behind the desk and sent out into the feld with SWAT teams and other rapid response units. Armed with the latest cyberdecks, they were tasked with both protecting their teams and turning the tables on the opposition.**

- **Those early cyberdecks were a huge budget issue for the Matrix divisions in the ’50s. More often than not the SWAT team was there to protect the tech investment in the decker as much as anything else. I heard more than one story about runners trying to get a high threat tag on a call to take out the team’s hacker and lift a cyberdeck.**

- **The Smiling Bandit**

**In 2064 everything changed again. Crash 2.0 was a confuence of several events that included the doomsday cult Winternight’s release of the Jormungand virus, designed to destroy the Matrix. While only partially successful, it still left the wired Matrix system, and many existing information databases, in smoking ruins. In 2065, the Second Universal Matrix Conference established protocols for the new wireless Matrix, and by 2069 it was up and running. The system was promoted as the ultimate in user functionality, but ultimately it proved to be a little too functional. Hacking required little more than a commlink and the right programs. Matrix assets in police forces struggled to keep pace**

**with the changing technology. As a result, most offcers became responsible for their own Matrix defenses, using department-issued commlinks to protect their individual PANs.**

**That frst version of the wireless Matrix was short-lived. By 2075 the security loopholes of the wireless Matrix had to be addressed. The New Matrix Protocols were proposed and put in place by the Corporate Court using non-physical Foundation technology. These new protocols once again required specialty equipment, and cyberdecks made a return. Police departments were forced to re-equip and re-train their Matrix specialists. With the Grid Overwatch Division (GOD) of the Corporate Court handling most crime within the Matrix, the specialty divisions of most forces could focus on investigating the Matrix-related aspects of other types of crimes. Host technology evolved, and attaching department-issued equipment to the WAN (Wide Area Network) of a department’s host became the norm. Most forces centralized their Matrix assets, patrolling their own hosts as spiders and the frontline Matrix defense role diminished slightly. Matrix operations feld offcers were returned to primarily offensive roles in SWAT teams and other fast responders.**

- **All of the constant retraining and constant equipment upgrades cause a real cash flow problem for most police departments. As a result, some of the lesser-funded departments can be found issuing older, outdated equipment to their Matrix officers. So always do a little snooping on what the local law enforcement’s Matrix budget is and what they are issuing to their teams. Knowing what you may be up against could give you a big advantage.**

**The current version of the Matrix and how it’s used by both police and criminals is constantly evolving. For example, a cyberdeck now also requires the use of a cyberjack to defend from hacks hiding in the vast volume of incoming simsense information. Those who don’t have one are at a huge disadvantage to those who do. The minimum thresholds required for both gear and training are constantly rising for both police and black hat hackers, forcing a constant evolution of equipment and tactics.**

**As for the future of the new Matrix, cracks are already starting to show, perhaps heralding a new crash sometime soon. Over the last year, Foundation hosts across the world have randomly become unstable, with some even failing spontaneously, and Framework hosts have returned to the cutting edge of Matrix technology. One thing will remain constant, however: as information technology changes, criminals will fnd new ways to take advantage of it, and the Matrix divisions of police departments will have to change to keep up.**
