## Unnatural Instinct

A critter can be genetically programmed to start its life with an unnatural instinct that acts as special training or a command. The critter will not need to be trained to follow this instinct, and it does not count toward the critter’s maximum number of commands or special training. A critter may only receive one unnatural instinct augmentation, and it may not be removed by any means, whether through training or genetic therapy treatments. This genetic augmentation can only be applied at the embryonic stage.
