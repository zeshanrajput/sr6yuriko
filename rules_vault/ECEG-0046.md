## Broussard Vodou Tradition

This is a tradition of faith and resists drain with Willpower + Charisma. For more information on traditions of faith, see p. 104, Street Wyrd.

Vodou focuses on the unseen world which is populated by spirits and mysteries created by God. The primary goal of practitioners of Vodou is to serve the spirits (or loa). However, the Vodou religion has picked up a nasty reputation caused by out-of-control practitioners, ritual hexes, curses, and misinformed superstitions. Outsiders focus on these hexes instead of the charms and protection spells that are core to the tradition. True adherents of Vodou are deferential to spirits, hoping to gain infl uence with the major loa. You will never hear them refer to services owed, instead they will refer to gifts granted by the loa. As such, they would never deign to summon a spirit into an inanimate vessel or anything technologically advanced. While practitioners of Vodou can summon spirits to possess sapient creatures, it is taboo for adherents of Broussard Vodou.

Broussard Vodou was formed in the early 2070s from the teachings of Louis Broussard in Baton Rouge. Louis formed the movement at the direction of one major loa. According to Broussard, the loa directed him to not call upon them to possess the bodies of living, intelligent creatures. The cynical observers of the movement believe that this has less to do with the loa and more to do with the cultural norms and biases of those outside the religion.

The adherents cannot possess sapient creatures. You can possess a non-sapient creature or the dead. For non-sapient, living creatures, the possession works as specifi ed on p. 69, Street Wyrd. They can control the creature and interact with the physical world. In addition to living, non-sapient creatures, the spirits can possess the dead (Object Resistance 3), but this is distasteful to the spirit and the wider community resulting in a loss of 1 Reputation per successful possession. When possessing the dead, the spirit does not gain the benefi ts from any ’ware implants. The possession lasts a sunrise and sunset.

Dawn set up a location in New Orleans in the mid-1800s formed from members of the Freemasons and Societas Rosicruciana in Anglia. While the society went through tough times in the 1900s, nearly disappearing, the Awakening caused a resurgence in its membership as people sought to learn and control these powers. During this resurgence, the organization adopted the writings of Regardie taking on the name Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn.

The Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn follows the traditions of the parent organization while each chapter has a separate leadership and structure. These orders follow the teachings of Aleister Crowley, but there are variations based on the specifi c set of writings the chapters follow. The core principle of the order is the study of magic with the stated goal of achieving immortality. Many chapters follow this tenet to a fault, causing the resources of the group to be stretched thin where efforts to secure funding take a back seat to fi nding appropriate patronage.

The New Orleans chapter is located at the Thoth Temple of the Golden Dawn and consists of dozens of members, including adepts and shamans, organized into circles. The highest circle consists of high-ranking mages whose

membership is held secret. The second circle consists of highly skilled practitioners and is led by the master librarian, Claudette Laurier. The lowest circle consists of the researchers and other members of the order who are focused on expanding the organization’s knowledge. Claudette often acts as the primary contact for members of the third circle, offering up guidance on research as well as hiring resources to hunt down artifacts. She prides the library on having an extensive set of works talking about Christian mysticism, Kabbalah, hermeticism, paganism of Egypt, theurgy, alchemy, theosophy, Enochian magic, and medieval grimoires.

- Given how spread out the organization is around the world, any given chapter ends up feeling pretty small. The biggest chapters are Zürich, London, and New Orleans. These three locations have excellent libraries of material across a wide range of traditions, but they aren’t just going to share those with anyone.

- Ethernaut
