## **Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT)/High Threat Response (HTR)**

**The original Special Weapons and Tactics/ High Threat Response concept was for extremely high-risk situations such as barricaded suspects, hostage situations, high-threat warrants, or events demanding specialty weapons and tactics. Candidates undergo a series of intensive physical ftness assessments. These tests are designed to gauge an individual’s strength, endurance, agility, and overall physical ftness. Given the demanding nature of these special operations, physical prowess has traditionally been a fundamental requirement. This is usually followed by marksmanship evaluations. Candidates must demonstrate profciency with a variety of weapons, showcasing their ability to handle frearms effectively and make accurate decisions under pressure. When a member is commonly**

**out of harm’s way, such as astral breacher, surveillance rigger, and Matrix communication, marksmanship evaluations are lowered but more focus is placed on the skill in their appropriate area. If the roll starts with the word “tactical,” however, the individual must also reach the standard SWAT/HTR marksman qualifcation.**

**Psychological evaluations are integral to the selection process. Given the high-stress nature of these operations, candidates undergo assessments to ensure they possess the mental fortitude, emotional stability, and sound judgment required for the role. This step is crucial in identifying individuals who can maintain composure in intense situations. This includes a thorough background investigation conducted to scrutinize a candidate’s personal and professional history. Those who do not have a SIN (or a very high-rated fake SIN) should not apply.**

**Once an individual makes it on a SWAT/ HTR team, what’s next? The vast majority of units around the world are part-time teams, with individuals having duties and responsibilities outside of this position. This requires them to work full time in a variety of other assignments but may be called out on occasion as part of regular patrol or law enforcement operations. In larger cities or jurisdictions, a SWAT team is a full-time position.**

**To combat an unknown threat, SWAT/ HTR offcers must be ready for anything. That is why SWAT/HTR team structure generally requires a variety of positions and skills based on multiple threat assessments and layers of multiple contingency plans. This can typically be boiled down into three areas: magic, Matrix, and mundane. Each of these requires unique specialized training. Once weaknesses are discovered, a team must then ensure that the appropriate skill set is either available via staff or through mutual aid. Some examples of SWAT areas of specialization include astral observers, astral breachers, crisis negotiators, explosive breachers, magic breachers, long rife observers, Matrix observers, tactical Matrix communication, tactical medics (Awakened), and tactical medics (mundane).**

**As threats evolve, so do SWAT teams. Continuous training, the integration of state-of-the-art ’ware, and a commitment to learning from real-world experiences keep SWAT teams at the forefront of law enforcement’s assets. But sometimes, it’s not just about kicking in doors; it’s also about getting the drop on a suspect or gaining intelligence regarding the overall situation. To assist the door-kickers, SWAT teams also employ surveillance and tracking riggers to give them every possible advantage before they make entry or engage suspects.**
