## Translation of Decking on the Elemental Planes

A cyberdeck’s appearance translates into the elemental plane that the character traveled to—unless they have bonded a memory of water focus (p. 59), in which case the deck has the Water Plane characteristics regardless of the metaplane that the character arrived in. For Earth, Fire, and Water, almost all devices have no wireless connection, so they have to be hacked directly. The Earth Metaplane has induction technology, through for example the Nazca slipstreams. This allows indirect hacking of devices using this technology. The Water Metaplane has the concept of memory of water (p. 59), allowing the decker to hack structures and vehicles for sensory information.

Air, for lack of a better term, has cloud devices. Every sentient elemental has one. It can change form and density to whatever the elemental was thinking they need. A decker can hack such devices indirectly in an attempt to “brick” them, or in this case disperse the cloud. Treat the device as F+2 for rating. In all the elemental planes, the technology is riskier to hack, as they are not exactly the same on the material plane. Any glitches incur feedback in the form of some energy (thermal, electrical, etc.), causing 1 box of stun damage to the decker.
