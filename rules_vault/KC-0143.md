## Geoffrey Dakins

Geoffrey is a male elf in his mid-twenties in the midst of his field training for the Dawkins Group, Horizon’s intelligence and counterintelliTHEgence division. His assignment to Lagos was not a feather in his cap but instead a penance for his transgressions. During his training and education in the Seattle sprawl, the son of a corporate exec fell in love with Geoffrey. Though Geoffrey did not return the feelings, at least not to the same degree, he was supportive of his friend, while the young man’s executive father was not. Geoffrey’s relationship with the executive’s son was painted as manipulative, and the executive influenced Geoffrey’s assignment after his graduation. Geoffrey (not his real name) is trying to quickly make a name and get himself transferred from Lagos back to a different sprawl, preferably Seattle or Los Angeles. He’s made connections in Lagos that help complement his corporate resources through his social skills.

Geoffrey doesn’t have a lot of his own physical assets, but he has spies and eyes everywhere. He rarely has to follow close to anyone himself, instead using different assets to keep tabs on a target. Geoffrey’s subtle surveillance network enables him to listen in on conversations carried out above a whisper through active commlink microphones, faked phone calls, and even a few old-school long-range microphones hidden in the mass and mess of Lagos’ slums. With active spies, Geoffrey can try hacking into the equipment of the runners, looking for copies of whatever data they have gathered or piggy-backing on their own microphones during interrogations or discussion of their investigation. He can also call on others to create some physical encounters to get data from them, but he usually tries to build it into some better cover, like a toll or tax that he knows the runners might resist. For this, he borrows assets from an associate, because his own megacorporate supports are limited.

Geoffrey has three asset pools. He has himself and the collection of spies he uses around Lagos. These sources are all paid for their assistance, most in the form of hawala tokens from Dakin’s second pool, the hawala Kiniun the Lion. Kiniun provides Dakin with thugs as well as good rates for his hawala tokens that help Geoffrey pay his spies. Kiniun’s supply of warriors isn’t unlimited, and he needs to maintain security for his hawala efforts. So if Geoffrey calls, the size of the force Kiniun provides is based on how much closer this will get him to his goal of getting out of Lagos. If Dakin says he is going after them because they have a lot of data, up to ten warriors will be available, but if it’s just a roughing up or intimidation, he will only send four. Dakin’s last pool of assets is a pair of operatives in Lagos on retainer with Horizon. A rigger named Tricks (use Rigger, p. 86, _SR6_ ) and a mage named Coldwell (use Combat Mage, p. 82, _SR6_ ) can be called up to offer backup, but Geoffrey will wait until he has a line on all the data before he pulls out those aces up his sleeve.
