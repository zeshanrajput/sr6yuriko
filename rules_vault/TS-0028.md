## **Changes Due to Metahumanity**

**The changes to policing caused by metahumanity happened suddenly, quickly became deeply entrenched, ended up being slow to resolve, and still impact policing to this day.**

**At the very start of the Sixth World, some elements of society, and even some entire societies, were uneasy over the Unexplained Genetic Expression of 2011 that brought the births of the new metatypes dubbed elves and dwarfs. This, however, was only a preview, as that uneasiness exploded into fear in 2021 when Goblinization changed one in ten adults into orks and trolls. At best, these newcomers were discriminated against; at worst, they were openly feared and even hated, rounded up and placed in quarantine camps. This was especially true within the law enforcement organizations of the time. Almost every force quietly discharged their Goblinized offcers for “the public good” as they were deemed unft for duty. In the civilian population, all varieties of metahumans and their families were viewed with suspicion by the now all-human police, who treated them as secondclass citizens. Predictably, violence escalated against all types of metas, their families, and supporters, with law enforcement typically being slow to do anything about it, or even tacitly encouraging it.**

- **At worst?! The worst is they were hauled away and slaughtered. Unless they were one of the lucky ones living in what would become a sanctuary like the Black Forest, Yakut, the Tírs, or Amazonia. Classism and privilege also manifested in new and terrifying ways. Those with influence and money could even arrange to swap out their meta-born infants for a “normal one”—I’m looking at you, Charles Brackhaven! And the people who suffered through that early Goblinization, especially those becoming troll-kin, had horrible transformations or didn’t survive. Or maybe it was because when they sought out “medical treatment,” they were quietly put out of their misery. I could go on about this forever.**

**> Ecotope**

**> Let’s not, so we can try to keep this on topic. One hard truth from that time was that law enforcement organizations of every type were torn apart from the inside during Goblinization and in the years after it. The fear factor was devastating for morale; stories were coming in from all sides about what might happen if your squad mate of ten years suddenly twists into a threemeter-tall flesh-eating monster. Those fears empowered groups like Humanis well beyond anything the white hoods of the KKK had ever managed before.**

**> Hard Exit**

**It didn’t take long for metahuman rights groups to appear and start pushing back against the mistreatment. Progress was slow, but it was there, in some places more than others. Society slowly became more accepting of metahumans, and a few police organizations began to realize that an allhuman force was a bad look. While a small number of metahuman offcers appeared in some locations, other LEPs such as Lone Star remained frmly antimetahuman and refused to change.**

**In 2039, the social confict reached a climax. Some unknown spark caused metahumans and meta-rights groups to rise up, march, and riot, protesting their treatment as second-class citizens in what became known as the Night of Rage. The response from both governments and metahate groups was predictable. Hundreds died in Seattle after Lone Star rounded up metahumans suspected of protesting and detained them in warehouses on the Tacoma docks. Those same warehouses were then set on fre by the Hands of Five (although some say it was actually Lone Star offcers who started the fre and the Hands were simply scapegoats). This led to pro-meta and anti-meta groups rioting across Seattle for days. Violence continued with the Alamos 20,000 bombing that brought down the Chicago Sears Tower, killing 26,000 and creating the Shattergraves. These events and more proved to be too much for the majority of the population. Public opinion started to shift.**

- **Those early days were bad, really bad. The complete lack of understanding about Goblinization and meta-racism in general directly fueled the Night of Rage, with the fallout from it stretching out for generations.**

**> Bull**

**Metahuman rights initiatives took hold in nations and municipalities across the globe. It became increasingly unacceptable for law enforcement to be seen as anti-meta. Many, but not all, police forces added and quickly promoted many metahuman offcers during this time. Those that didn’t, such as Lone Star, started to lose contracts, eventually leading to changes in the boardroom and to their corporate culture. **Today, discrimination based on metatype is offcially illegal around most of the globe. In LEPs, both command and HR departments try to stamp out meta-racism wherever it raises its ugly head, but some will try less hard than others. Is it a problem from every cop, or even most? No, but it is an ongoing problem with enough to damage the public’s trust in all police everywhere. One big part of why it’s so hard to root racism out of police forces is that no one likes their dirty laundry displayed in public. When a charge of racism against an offcer is investigated and substantiated, more often than not it just gets covered up. There may be write-ups and reprimands, but more likely the offcers involved just get moved to a new area to become someone else’s problem. It also means they get a fresh start and a new opportunity to continue the same behavior.**

- **The integration of metahuman officers into forces soaked up the nuyen in budgets, which stretched departments even thinner. It became easy for unhappy people to latch onto “the cause of the problem” and for the blame to land squarely at the feet of metahumans. Money for recruiting. Money for training. Money for new gear. Money to modify vehicles. Hell—money for new ceiling tiles! It became an ongoing argument that kept anti-meta sentiment entrenched for decades.**

- **Butch**

- **There were still some significant long-term holdouts globally. Nearly all the law enforcement providers owned by the Japanacorps were resistant to change at the deepest levels. The old guards’ inflexible mindsets hobbled progress on metahuman issues. When Yamatetsu left Japan for Vladivostok, it only made things worse at the remaining Japanacorps. The HR departments of Renraku, Shiawase, and Mitsuhama used examples of Yamatetsu employees to show how they were tearing apart families. Eventually, however, even the most reluctant began hiring metahumans for some positions.**

**In addition to acts of overt meta-racism by the occasional offcer, there is also a general anti-meta bias from society itself that spills over into policing. You’ve all heard it: “orks are just naturally violent” and “trolls are less intelligent than other metatypes” and other drek like that. Trolls and especially orks are often subject to profling, or “walking while tusked,” and tend to be stopped and questioned at a higher rate than humans, dwarfs, or elves. They also tend to be arrested after a stop at higher rates, and the same statistical trend continues for conventions.**

**As a result, if you go out on the streets of any sprawl and ask people about meta-racism from the police, most likely you’ll hear that it’s still common. We could argue about causation vs. correlation all day, but that doesn’t really change the statistics. The numbers don’t lie, and they show that despite decades of progress, the effect that metahumanity’s appearance has had on policing is still far from being a thing of the past.**

- **The changing of the emperor served as a significant catalyst for a cultural shift in Japan, but other places like Russia remained a bastion of anti-meta prejudice, especially when it came to law enforcement.**
