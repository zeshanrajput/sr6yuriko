## Listening In

The next step is finding out what happens at the meeting. The meeting will take place in a conference room in the middle of the fourth floor of 44 Union Square. This room, naturally, has no windows (for more details on this building, see **Hotspot** ). The door is on the west side of the room and is watched by four guards. The east wall has no door, but two guards still watch it (use Elite Corporate Security for all guards, p. 208, _SR6_ ). Two Force 6 spirits of air circle around the room, one passing every fifteen seconds (optional powers: Elemental Attack (Cold) and Psychokinesis). Matrix security is provided by a decker in the meeting (use Seal Team 6 Counter-Electronics Commando, p. 209, _SR6_ ) who is continually scanning traffic in the area. No one in the meeting is supposed to have a wireless device active, making it easier to look for wireless devices being carried by intruders, though the guards have the wireless functions of their cyberware active. Additionally, one participant in the meeting, Maria Morel, is a shaman (use Sioux Wildcat Shamanic Support, p. 210, _SR6_ ). She will assense the room every ten minutes.

Next to the door on the west side is a floorto-ceiling glass plate, half an inch thick (Structure rating 9). The walls are two inches thick and sturdy (Structure rating 12), the floor and ceiling are concrete (Structure rating 15). The fifth floor hosts a lounge and café; at this time of day, it’s sparsely populated, but some people pulling long hours occasionally come up here for a soykaf (though not from the café, as that closes at 6 p.m.; there is a coffee station for common use against one wall). The third floor holds the offices of a lobbying firm (see **Hotspots** ).

The participants in the meeting are:

- Justin Bellflower, consultant

- Jonathan Blake, Centurion Services International

- Coleman Driess, Trojan Capital

- Maria Morel, Atlantean Foundation

- Dr. Rose Salinger, Animus Construction

- Dr. Sharmin Thakkar, Animus Construction

The participants will be addressed by their names at different times in the meeting, so the PCs will know who they are if they listen in for more than ten minutes. They should know Blake’s affiliation already; Driess and Morel’s affiliation can be found with a simple Matrix search (though the parent company of Trojan Capital is not immediately apparent). Salinger and Thakkar’s role with Animus will be clear as they talk. Bellflower remains a mystery, and no mention of his associates, co-workers, or anything else comes up in the meeting.

Morel is in the building well before the meeting begins. The others arrive minutes before 7 p.m., dropped off at the door by their drivers. They all immediately head to the fourth floor via elevator.

Dinner for the five has been loaded into two customized Vend-X drones (see p. 98, _Double Clutch_ ). The drones are operating according to their programming with no one controlling them. Given that they have nothing more complicated to do than serve food, this should be sufficient for the evening.

The meeting lasts one hour, with little small talk. The subject is the growing evidence that Animus’ treatments are not as permanent as hoped. Drs. Salinger and Thakkar lead the conversation, trying to strike a balance between a realistic assessment of the problem and optimism that it is solvable. Naturally, attention turns to Animus’ highest-profile client, who no one refers to by name. She is instead just called “the widow.” Driess and Blake are particularly concerned about whether the widow will regain any of her memories, because they insist that will be as bad for her as it will be for Animus. They discuss a wide range of options, including kidnapping and isolating the widow until a new procedure can be developed and even killing her to “save her from her own memories.”

Morel is mostly silent in this discussion. When she talks, it’s to ask questions, generally aimed at understanding the theories Animus is using on how the brain works, how it can be altered, and how these unexpected recoveries are happening. The business practice is a lesser concern for her.

The true enigma of the meeting is Justin Bellflower. Matrix searching will show he is a consultant, but he doesn’t list any clients. He doesn’t speak frequently in the meeting, but when he does, he commands the attention of the others, and his conclusions are rarely questioned. Bellflower believes the work of Animus needs to continue, and this issue need only be a speed bump, not a major delay. He discusses the possibility of “surreptitiously delivered boosters”—that is, procedures that could be administered outside the clinic at times when the subject does not expect it. Others in the meeting express concerns about the logistical challenges this presents, though they do not bring up any moral objections.

If the PCs manage to listen to the end of the meeting, they’ll get some clues about the affiliations of Bellflower and Driess. As the six individuals prepare to leave, Driess walks over, pats Bell30: ESSENCEflower on the back, and mumbles a few words at him. If the PCs have some way of picking up this exchange, they’ll hear him say, “Those spooks at Langley must be riding you hard.” Bellflower will look up sharply, as he does not appreciate any reference to the people with whom he consults. Then he mutters, “Tell your people it’s okay—I don’t intend for them to lose their top spot.”

The Langley spooks line is a clear reference to Bellflower’s clients at the UCAS CIA—he is at the meeting to represent the agency’s interests. His remark back to Driess is a reference to the ultimate owners of Trojan Capital—Mitsuhama, the world’s top-ranked megacorp. With this exchange, the PCs have information about the interests involved in Animus Construction.
