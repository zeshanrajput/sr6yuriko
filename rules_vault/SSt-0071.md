## **Critical Skills**

**The baseline skillset for being a rigger is being able to effectively pilot a wide range of vehicles. The control rig helps with that, but no matter what the trid shows portray, it doesn’t just make you an automatic ace. That takes time and practice—unless you have skillwires, I suppose. Next up is being able to modify and repair your tech. Things will get damaged, and even if they don’t, they’ll wear out. Your nuyen is already going to be stretched very thin, so hiring someone to fx your drek for you is going to break the bank. Another important skill is gunnery—shooting vehicle-mounted weapons is very different than handling a gun yourself. You’d think being a decent shot when not jacked in would translate, but it really doesn’t. That’s the basics: drive, fx, and shoot—though I don’t think these are enough on their own. Assuming that you’re a shadowrunner—why would you be reading this if you weren’t?—you’re going to need to learn some techniques for sneaking around without getting made by the security forces. This includes staying out of sight, but also blending in and being able to tail someone without making it obvious. Navigation and tracking skills also come in handy—you can always get a mapsoft, but knowing where to go in your bones will save your hoop when you just don’t have the time to carefully study a map.**
