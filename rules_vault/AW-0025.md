It seems that the denizens of the Plane of Faerie consist of not only spirits, but elves who have used it as a haven for their people since the Fourth World—the time of magic before recorded history—or perhaps even long before then. These elves (some of them, at least) appear to be truly immortal, and many of them remember those ancient times. This might be a dream for historians, except for the fact that the elves of Faerie seem little interested in enlightening others. They have kept secrets for thousands of years, and so they tend to be reticent when posed with direct questions. It seems likely that these elves have been interacting with the physical world for some time—perhaps even before the Awakening, though I can’t fathom how that would be possible—affecting the course of history to suit their own mysterious ends. Ehran the Scribe comes from this place, and it doesn’t take a shadowrunner to see how deeply he has affected events in our world.

Much like the Plane of Kin, the Plane of Faerie is close enough to the physical plane that it overlaps in some places. The Yellowstone Anomaly is currently the one most used, but other older faerie pathways certainly predate it. It is possible to travel physically to the Plane of Faerie through these faerie gates—some of which are ancient, some of which are new—that can be found within the physical plane. These faerie gates can be difficult to spot, but they also tend to be quite stable. They don’t come and go or move from one place to another like rifts often do. Once you’ve found one, you can be reasonably assured that you will find it again in the same spot the next time you need to use it.

If you must travel to the Plane of Faerie, be on your guard at all times. That’s not the most useful advice, since you should always be on your guard within the metaplanes, but it’s worth restating here because it is so much more important here. The Plane of Faerie seems like a peaceful and idyllic place of natural beauty and serenity. The denizens may seem friendly, whimsical, and even silly—but they are not harmless. This plane has been ruled by the same people for thousands of years, and they have established a totalitarian control over reality itself. It is a stagnant realm, in which every denizen

is placed in an unnamed caste system. These denizens seem comfortable and casual in their caste, but they follow rules so complex and ancient that I doubt any metahuman would have the time to learn them before dying of old age. There are hidden rules everywhere, with few or no signs that make them clear, but if you step wrong or behave inappropriately regarding a context that you might not be aware of, this plane will seem anything but serene. When in the Plane of Faerie, tread softly, speak very carefully, and be polite at all times—losing your composure is the fastest way to come to a bad end.

The aesthetic of the fae is imposed upon all who enter the Plane of Faerie. Visitors should expect their clothing and equipment to be transformed. Its function will remain much the same, but all modern appearances (particularly advanced technology) will be reshaped into an analogous lowtech alternative. Firearms, for instance, become crossbows. Drones are transformed into animals, medkits are replaced with an apothecary’s bag, and obvious cybernetics take on a gilded, handmade appearance. This can be upsetting for those of you who rely so much upon technology in order to do your work, but you will find that you can use your crossbow as skillfully as the machine gun you carried in with you—even if you have never fired a crossbow before. It might not make the loud reports you are accustomed to, nor will it fire crossbow bolts in huge bursts—though you will find that after each shot fired, a new bolt will be ready in its place without you having to go through an onerous reloading procedure. Matrix magicians will likely be at the greatest disadvantage, however, as there is no Matrix to interact with here (or, with some notable exceptions, on any metaplane).
