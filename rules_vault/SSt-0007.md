## **Teamwork**

**The trids are full of depictions of lone wolves— solo operatives with all the skills, talents, and gumption needed to pull off a job and survive. While there might be a few such individuals who operate like this in real life, they are rare, and almost all of them are short-lived. A shadowrun requires a wide range of capabilities, some of which are mutually exclusive.**

**Almost anyone can pick up a gun and learn how to shoot, but that’s just a sliver of the skill needed to become a combat specialist. Such individuals must rely on cybernetics or magic abilities in order to gain the edge needed to survive a combat engagement. These people rarely develop the social skills necessary to pull off a con or blend into a unique social environment. Faces—social specialists—are perceptive and charismatic individuals capable of reading social cues, emulating behaviors, and selling fabricated tales in order to get the job done. Such people are rarely capable of surviving serious combat without support from others.**

**And then there’s magic, something you’re either born with or not. In the early days, magic was a largely unknown factor that was well-suited to subverting authority. Now, every security force has its own magicians, willing and able to use their abilities to counter street mages and shamans. Almost every target, whether it’s a megacorp, gang, organized crime syndicate, or government agency, is going to have some magic on its side. It seems everyone has learned the lesson that the only reliable way to deal with magic is to have some magic of your own. That’s never more true than when you encounter spirits, which are incredibly resistant to mundane countermeasures. And while many magicians are quite capable in combat, they’re also singled out as a primary target in a fght.**

**Transportation is another concern for a shadowrunning team. This role is most often flled by a rigger, requiring expensive and invasive cyberware that allows them to pilot a vehicle far more effectively than anyone without such gear. Except in the extremely rare case of pulling off a mission**

**without alerting security, making a clean getaway is their primary job—but the addition of drone technology expands their capabilities far beyond just being a getaway driver.**

**And then there’s the Matrix, which is largely considered the sole domain of the powerful megacorps. If not for hackers—deckers and technomancers—it would be impossible to contemplate subverting a security system or accessing protected data. These specialists are also highly effective at countering the opposition’s technology in other types of engagements by disabling weapons and drones, sowing chaos by altering sensor feeds, and in some cases shutting down heavily cybered combat specialists entirely—all while preventing the other side from doing the same to their own team.**

**While it’s possible for one individual to cover two or three of these specialties, no one shadowrunner could possibly handle them all. Even if they had the potential to do so, they couldn’t possibly cover more than one or two roles at the same time. In order for any serious shadowrun operation to succeed, you’re going to need a team of individuals, each covering one or more specialties, in order to have anything resembling a reliable chance of success. It’s true that not all runs will require all of these skillsets, but it always helps to have as many bases covered as possible.**
