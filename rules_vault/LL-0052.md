These internal discrepancies between Talons are entirely that—internal affairs. To Saeder-Krupp Prime and the rest of Lofwyr’s sprawling corporate empire, the Talons simply _are_ . They serve a half-step removed from the traditional corporate chain of command and are generally free to requisition whatever corporate assets and commandeer whatever security forces they can justify after the fact should a mission succeed. 

Loyalty to the parent corporation is expected of each member, as well as a certain level of aggression (even between the agents 

themselves, as Scale believes in a rather Darwinist approach to keeping his servants sharp). Success and failure are all that matter within the Special Group, and the ends always, _always_ justify the means. Scale (and through him, Lofwyr) is notoriously disinterested in his agents’ magical tradition, chosen mentor spirit (or lack thereof), or even what side-jobs (or personal wealth) they acquire in their own time. All that matters is that they answer when summoned and serve well. 

If a Talon agent’s grasp exceeds their reach, however, and they waste lives or equipment needlessly, or fail to produce satisfactory results? Well, there’s always another brood coming up. They can be replaced.
