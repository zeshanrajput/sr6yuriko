## Lt. Col. Faith Raddick (Point Guard)

## Posted by: Rifleman

Faith Raddick is one of the finest officers and professionals I’ve had the privilege to work with. I first met her in the early sixties while commanding Bravo Company. We’d been tasked with recovering a downed allied helo in a very unpleasant part of the world. When we arrived, I saw (then) Lieutenant Raddick solo-operating a crew-served HMG and defending the surviving members of her team against enemy forces _despite_ having lost both of her legs in the crash.

That impressed me.

I later convinced her to join Bravo Company and eventually made her my XO, which transferred over when the Sixty-First reformed. Since then, she’s proven her value time and again not only as a warrior but as a leader. I’ll be the first to admit, I’m a field commander first and foremost. Give me a problem to solve and turn me loose. But Colonel Raddick is on another level when it comes to operational execution and coordination. Much like she is on the basketball court, she’s an expert in running multiple elements at once and adapting to whatever is thrown her way. This includes on the field or in a command center. Her ability to absorb and apply information in any situation made me wonder if she was a technomancer (she’s not). She’s also universally liked and respected by her subordinates. Cool under fire, she has the command presence few ever achieve.

- I’ve heard about a saying within the Sixty-First: When the lead is flying and people are dying, have faith, as Colonel Raddick will see us through.

- Scattershot

- I appreciate the sentiment, but deep down I hate that saying. It feeds the false hope I can save everyone.

- Point Guard

When Grey Cell was formed, Colonel Raddick was my right hand, and she made sure the newly formed cells and strike units had proper communication and logistics networks set up. She was also instrumental in recruiting and training new recruits, and in helping me create battle doctrine and SOPs. And I think _she_ should have been the operational coordinator in the beginning. After the Disians tried to end us during the Week of Death, I finally *ahem* convinced Cole to cut me loose and let Raddick take my place.

Best fragging decision he ever made.

After that, Colonel Raddick was in her element. With her coordinating operations like a championship coach, we were able to stop the Disians and force them into a stalemate because of her efforts. She also managed to save other cells across Europe and the Americas by finding ways to get needed reinforcements and supplies in, and when necessary, get people out—despite the Disians’ best efforts.

When I was laid up, Faith … Colonel Raddick took care of my task force and rolled them into a new one, TF-313, under her command. She was put in charge of dealing with the new Mobile Harvester Threat and she was instrumental with the destruction of over fifty MBs. But more importantly, she was able to make the call to trust the renegades within Aztechnology. That decision allowed for the destruction of not only all MBs, but allowed our forces in Antarctica to gain a desperately needed foothold and ultimately assured our very survival.
