## The Plane of Plants

In the Plane of Plants, you will find oceans of algae, mountains of fungal colonies, towering forests, verdurous swamplands, and idyllic gardens. The only structures have been carefully grown by the spirit denizens who tend to the plants here, and these blend with the surroundings so well that it would be very unlikely for you to notice unless you stumble into one. Most of the denizens of this plane are plant spirits, and unless they choose to reveal themselves, you will be unable to distinguish them from the other plants. Generally speaking, if you visit the Plane of Plants, it is wise to assume that any plant you encounter might be a spirit. This plane is rich with plant-based reagents, but harvesting those reagents is dangerous. Not only do plant spirits object to having their bits chopped off, they have been known to take offense at any metahuman who attempts to harvest reagents from other plants. If you hope to forage for resources in the Plane of Plants, you had best be prepared to encounter flora that are far more proactive than what you might expect from plants on the physical plane.



## THE PLANE OF PLANTS

**Technology Level:** None **Magic Level:** Normal **Base Level:** Plant **Terrain:** Plains/Rolling Hills **Population:** Dense **Society:** Varied **Distance:** Adjacent


For the astrally projecting traveler, the Plane of Plants is easy to reach via the astral gateways. If you wish to bring any items back with you from this plane, you will need to visit in the flesh. The Hudson Valley is one of the most reliable means to travel here, as it is easily accessed from the physical plane and passes through the Plane of Plants. However, the Plane of Plants is not particularly close to where the physical plane intersects the Hudson Valley, so it can take some time to travel there. Unless you have brought a vehicle with you, I highly recommend booking passage on a riverboat.

One of the places within the Plane of Plants is the Tranquil Gardens, strange with its manicured hedges and stone paths, dotted with small lakes and pools that act as astral portals—passageways to other planes, including the physical plane, the Plane of Beasts, and the Plane of Man. The other side of these astral portals can be found only in wild places untouched by the ravages of metahumans. Once one is discovered and used by metahumans, it will fade away. Within the Tranquil Garden, that pool will become overgrown and dry up. I believe that it is very likely that the pools regenerate over time, connecting to other untouched wild places once they have recovered, but it is entirely possible that these astral shallows are irreplaceable and might one day become extinct. I have spent considerable time and effort seeking to understand the nature of



this serene metaplanar nexus, and I can confirm that using the pools to travel from the Tranquil Gardens that connect to other metaplanes causes no disruption—only the ones on the physical plane seem to object to being used by metahumans. Unfortunately, we cannot be entirely certain of these revelations, as my sample size consists of a handful of observations. If the pools of the Tranquil Garden can be used without causing their destruction, it could become a busy hub for astral travelers. I wonder how long it will remain a tranquil place. The fact that megacorporate logging operations have already begun in the forest of Umbrage makes me fear that the Tranquil Gardens will soon become something like a metaplanar airport or train station. I wonder if the spirits of this plane would take action against that.

Due to its proximity to the physical plane, an astrally projecting traveler can utilize a ritual gateway to quickly arrive at the Plane of Plants. Other travelers most often make use of the Hudson Valley, or use one of the astral rifts or shallows most commonly found in wild places. The shallows and rifts that connect to the Plane of Beasts appear unpredictably—I believe that these occur when beasts enter the physical plane from their home plane, but I have been unable to confirm this through direct observation—and tend to close up or fade away in a matter of days. Some efforts have been made to establish more stable direct connections with some limited success, but the Plane of Beasts seems to actively resist these impositions. News of increasingly populous and dangerous wildlife might lead you to one of these passages, but I don’t recommend it. If you don’t have access to one already, use the Hudson Valley to get there from the Plane of Kin instead. It’s easier, safer, and will save you time.
