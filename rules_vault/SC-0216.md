## Assault on Host 13

Word has sprinted around the Matrix that a particular data haven known as Host 13, has assembled a very compelling and valuable set of paydata. The gamemaster can decide the nature of this paydata, but here are some ideas:

- A list of upcoming products to be developed—these could include weapons, drones, vehicles, or any other form is technology

- A list of executives a corporation plans to target for extraction

- Evidence that incriminates a number of very wealthy people

Host 13 has been under a massive assault since the rumors got out. The defensive decker has been contacted with an offer from the data haven admins to help protect it until the files can be sold. They are offering a considerable percentage of the sale price, which turns out to be a rather high-paying run.

The defensive decker can take any side in this fight, either joining the Host 13 to defend it from a vast array of attacks or by going after the files themselves. The battle will involve multiple factions as well as lone-wolf hackers. Locating the file requires an extended Matrix search test with a threshold of 13 and an interval of 1 Major Action. The file

is protected by rating 13(!) encryption using some rather unorthodox techniques. After each failed decryption attempt is made, the encryption rating goes down by 1 and the attempting persona is dumped from the Matrix (suffering dumpshock, of course!) and their physical location is reported—almost as if GOD had converged on them, but without being reported to GOD.

That’s where the runners come in—what all of the non-hackers on the team will be used for. Host 13 admins have hired runner teams to be on standby in various places around the world, and the defensive decker’s team is the one hired to cover their own town. Most of the attackers will be from other places in the world, but one of the most persistent ones is in their area. This hacker seems to either have many cyberdecks or can repair them impossibly quickly. Unless the runner team can stop them in the physical world, they will just keep coming back and eventually get the files. The target is defended by some muscle—either some gangers, drones, or other shadowrunners—so it won’t be as easy as knocking on their door and telling them to stop. A diplomatic solution isn’t impossible, but the runners can handle this problem a variety of ways.

As for the fight within Host 13, it’s going to be messy and chaotic, as deckers, technomancers, and sprites from around the world converge to defend or attack the host. Rather than playing out every possible interaction, the defensive decker should declare what their general role or activity will be. Describe the constant battle between personas, sprites, and IC, but don’t resolve every possible encounter. Instead, play through an encounter against an enemy decker. After that encounter, Host 13 alerts the runner team of the location of a hacker who almost broke through its encryption. As the runners mobilize, the enemy hacker returns and faces off against the defensive decker. Just as the runners arrive at the target location, the enemy hacker returns to Host 13 again.

The defensive decker will have just enough time for one test to repair Matrix damage between each encounter. If their hardware becomes too damaged, or take too much biofeedback damage, they may not be able to return to the fight. Assuming they participated in the defense well and didn’t just fold in the first round, the runners get paid. If the runners defeat the enemy hacker, the team gets a significant nuyen bonus. If the defensive decker was able to fight on through until the end, another nuyen bonus is forthcoming. Unless the team fails in defeating the persistent enemy hacker, Host 13’s defenses will hold. Otherwise, the file encryption will eventually be broken. If the file is protected, everyone on the team gains a special contact: Host 13. This represents a data haven and its core admins, who will be very grateful to the runners who came to their defense. The contact has the types Criminal Matrix, with Connection 6 and Loyalty 2.
