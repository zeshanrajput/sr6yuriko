## Turret Mount (Rating 2-6)

One or more turret mounts can be added to a critter exoframe. Critters are not capable of learning how to aim and fire turret-mounted weapons—these are essentially stationary drones that use a drone-like pilot system and autosofts or are operated remotely by a sapient controller, such as a rigger, AI, sprite, or technomancer.

**Game Information:** A turret mount counts as a drone that acts on its own initiative or on the initiative of its operator. A mini-turret is capable of mounting a taser or hold-out and is the only turret mount that can be attached to a plastic exoframe. A small turret can mount a dart pistol, light pistol, or machine pistol. Medium turrets can mount a light crossbow, heavy pistol, machine pistol, super-squirt, or submachine gun. Small and medium turrets require an aluminum or titanium exoframe. A large turret can mount a rifle, shotgun, or standard crossbow, and requires a titanium exoframe. And then there’s the heavy turret, which can mount machine guns and launchers. In addition to the capacity requirement, a heavy turret must be mounted on a titanium exoframe and can only be used on critters with Body and Strength attributes of 6 or higher.

The drone pilot system for a turret mount doesn’t require the same level of sophistication that a regular drone needs (all it needs to do is aim and shoot) and is less expensive—but incompatible with other drones. The internal pilot system is capable of running a number of autosofts equal to half its rating, and the rating of the autosofts is limited to the rating of the pilot. The only autosofts that are compatible with a turret mount pilot are clearsight and targeting, and a separate autosoft is required for each specific weapon.

In order to attach a weapon to a turret mount, it must be specially modified for this purpose and can’t be operated normally unless modified back to its original form. Modifying a weapon for a turret mount requires an Engineering (Armorer) (4, 1 hour) Extended test. Attaching or swapping out a weapon requires an Engineering (Armorer) (4, 1 Major Action) Extended test with a threshold of 4 and an interval of a Major Action.

Unless an ammunition storage module is also installed, the weapon must rely on its internal magazine or clip. Crossbows require extra modifications that double the listed nuyen cost of the weapon and provide an ammo clip that holds five bolts and a reloading mechanism. Crossbows are not compatible with the ammunition storage module.
