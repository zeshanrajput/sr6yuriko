## Vendetta’s End

Mr. Johnson has a vendetta. Their rival commands prestige and resources comparable to their own, and the two have stepped on each other’s interests in the poorly delineated shadow community numerous times in the past. These transgressions began innocuously enough, but somewhere along the line one side didn’t satisfy the other’s expectations for courtesy and the slights became deliberate. The feud has been going on for some time, but for the most part it has remained professional— until now. Mr. Johnson has decided to end the rivalry permanently. Naturally Mr. J requires assets who cannot be linked back to them by their rival. However, Mr. J wants to use their own henchman to pull the trigger for any number of reasons: the killing must be done in house for reasons of honor,

Mr. J doesn’t want to rely on an unknown sniper’s skill, or perhaps the runners are known or expected to balk at cold-blooded murder while still being amenable to taking some blood money watching a hitman’s back. The team must assist the sniper in stalking the prey, preparing the ambush, and then getting away clean.
