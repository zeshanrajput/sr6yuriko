## Running Interference

Animus is gaining a lot of attention, and the PCs are not the only team interested in what’s going on in this meeting. A second team is trying to execute a plan to eavesdrop on the meeting.

The team consists of a covert ops specialist (p. 83, _SR6_ ), street shaman (p. 88, _SR6;_ add Shape Stone spell), technomancer (p. 89, _SR6_ ), and weapons specialist (p. 90, _SR6_ ). The technomancer and weapons specialist are staying out of the building, wedged between the front of the dome and the pediment on top of the building’s façade. The shaman and covert ops specialist are dressed in businesswear, complete with fake IDs to get them into the building. They arrive on the fifth floor at 7:20 and relax and drink soykaf for ten minutes. Then they stand up and slowly amble across the area. When they cross the middle of the floor, the shaman drops her soykaf, and she immediately falls to her knees to clean up the spill. The covert ops specialist moves to the floor to help.

At that moment, the shaman casts Shape Stone on the floor to make a narrow hole, less than a half-centimeter wide. The covert ops specialist then slips a small microphone into the hole. They proceed to finish cleaning up the spilled soykaf, then move on. They quickly leave the building and go to a nearby van; once they’re out, the technomancer and weapons specialist descend from the roof and join them.

These runners have been hired by OmniStar, who is trying to dig up dirt on the other corps in order to try to force their way to AAA status. Once the meeting is over, they will deliver any information they have to their contact, meeting them in a warehouse on the Hudson around 18[th] Street.

The gamemaster can decide how interactions between the runner team and the meeting security works out (including Morel’s astral perception). They can make tests for each group as the runners attempt to insert their camera, or they can make a decision for what happens based on how they

would like the story to proceed. Naturally, any interactions between either group and the PCs should be handled through dice rolls.

If the runners from OmniStar are detected, the microphone will be removed, and the spirits and four of the security guards will be dispatched to chase the OmniStar runners down. Two guards will remain at the door to the conference room. They won’t try to eliminate them, though; once the guards are convinced the runners have fled, they will return to the fourth floor. The participants want to finish their meeting, and the OmniStar runners don’t want to interrupt the meeting, so it will continue.

If the runners are on the fifth floor after 7:20 or the roof after 6:50, they should make Perception + Intuition vs. Stealth + Agility Opposed tests to see if they notice anything unusual. If they notice the OmniStar runners, they can always opt to interfere with them, but since they are all interested in the same thing, an alliance might work out best. The OmniStar team will share what they learn for 15,000 nuyen, but they will decrease this to 10,000 nuyen if the PCs offer substantial help (like making sure their microphone insertion is not detected). This can work the other way—if the PCs can quickly convince the OmniStar runners that they have a solid plan and might have a longer recording of the meeting, they can extract 10,000 to 15,000 nuyen from them for sharing what they know.

Alternately, the PCs might decide the OmniStar runners are rivals who need to be dealt with, which could lead to an extra complication. If open fighting breaks out between the two teams, the meeting on the fourth floor will hastily adjourn, and the participants will quickly leave the building.
