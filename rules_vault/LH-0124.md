## Vodka, Sake, and French Wine

After some quick investigations and inquiries regarding the cyberdeck’s data, Novikov believed he also had enough proof to connect the Disians to other organizations, not just MCT. With this knowledge in hand, Novikov attempted to get in touch with the Four Oyabun Rengo, who were rumored to have lost their love for MCT, to inquire about a possible sale, or alliance.

They were interested, but they wanted to verify the data themselves. Most of the it appeared to be normal accounting and business research, so the Four Oyabun families wanted to investigate further, ideally in a location where the Vory had little power, but where there was at least one MCT subsidiary in the area—they decided that Paris, France, was the best option. So they sent one of their most trusted Saiko-komon, Hisoka Morita, to investigate. Somehow, the Disians and MCT learned of this meeting and increased their operations against all Vory in France, and Novikov in particular.

When Vory assets within France started to disappear or change allegiances, Novikov realized the Disians had connections that far exceeded anything he had been expecting. He met with hismost trusted advisors to come up with a plan and discovered an opportunity. The daughter of one of his advisors, Anastasia (now going by Anna), had moved her family from the California Free State to France at her mother’s request. Anna had also been the one providing crucial intelligence to Novikov, who became reliant on the information she provided and wanted to keep her in the area.

The only problem was Anna’s husband, Boggs, wasn’t aware of her family’s history. Boggs had previously been a rigger in law enforcement, so they arranged for him to get rigging work in Paris and stay home with their four daughters while Anna did her work for the Vory. When things became serious regarding the Disians targeting the Vory, Anna revealed her family history to Boggs, who didn’t care; he simply wanted to help protect his wife and family.

As Boggs became more integrated into the family business, he started working for Novikov, who eventually introduced him to the Metal Angels’ main Yakuza contact in Paris. Several deals and a few jobs later, Boggs began to work closely with one Hisoka Morita, and the two developed a mutual respect and trust for each other.

While Hisoka and Boggs were investigating Mitsuhama, the Disians realized that the pair had uncovered information that could cripple MCT and expose how much control the Disians had over the world’s largest corporation. The Disians became desperate to end the pair’s efforts and took a scorched-Earth approach (pun intended), overrunning a rural area of the city with chimeras.

Novikov needed Hisoka to present his findings to the Four Oyabun Rengo and couldn’t afford to lose her. He also came to respect Boggs and realized the White Vory would eliminate him and his family once things were over. Novikov’s people instructed Boggs to sneak his family and Hisoka out of Paris and to an area where the Red Vory had more influence. There, they met Mashina, who escorted them to Moscow where Anna, Boggs, and their daughters were welcomed with open arms.

After a successful meeting with the Four Oyabun Rengo, where a kind of mutual-aid and alliance against the Disians was formed, Novikov now had the power and nuyen he needed to make the Wolf’s prophecy become reality. The ranks of the Red Vory swelled into the hundreds, along with the Red Wolf’s influence.

Novikov then took the fight directly to the Disians and their allies.

After piecing together rumors from several sources, Novikov was confident the tide had changed and decided to press his advantage. So when a powerful Vory within the Caribbean League called to discuss an alliance, he quickly agreed. Upon arriving at the meeting location, Novikov and Mashina went in to negotiate the terms. Thankfully, Boggs not only flew them in

with his helo, but was also running overwatch with a small army of MCT Gnats, some Nissan Roto-Drones, and a Nissan Oni (all with his signature stickers saying, “I love my coconut”). One of Boggs’s drones detected a strange signature under the table, and he attempted to warn them. Mashina grabbed Novikov and tried to cover him just as the bomb detonated. The blast shredded most of Mashina’s body, and ruined Novikov’s hands and part of his face.

Using his drones to provide covering fire, Boggs was able to extract them to safety—he also leveled the meet location for good measure. Mashina and Novikov survived, but they were in bad shape. In addition to Novikov losing his hands, his lower jaw was mostly gone, and he had burns that covered most of his head. Worse, he had also lost his eyes.

Mashina, being more machine than meat, had a relatively quick recovery. Novikov, however, required extensive surgery and augmentation replacement. He received high-grade replacements for his eyes, ears, and jaw, as well as his hands. When he awoke, he stated that Wolf had come to him once again and given him a message. He’d been blind, believing others had to pay the price with their souls. Now, Wolf would destroy Novikov so that something new could be born—a Wolf, born from his blood. A Red Wolf, not just by name, but from the blood he will spill with his claws and fangs.

Novikov declared that much like his Metal Angels, his old self was dead, and he would now be known as the Red Wolf, and his Vory would be known as “The Pack.” After his recovery, the Red Wolf learned those responsible for his assassination attempt were hiding on the island of Nevis. The Pack descended on the island like avenging angels and brutally eliminated all those involved in the assassination plot. For reasons unknown, the Caribbean League didn’t react to the incursion, and Nevis has become the Pack’s second-largest operation outside of Moscow. They also control all illegal—as well as a large portion of legitimate—business in Nevis.

Novikov continues to work against Dis and their allies. He’s become something greater than before, and the Red Wolf and his Pack are sitting in a position of power that few can imagine, and even fewer can maintain.
