## The Blackout

St. Louis was one of the major cities that was hit by the blackout. They suffered what seemed a twofold hit. A section of the downtown core of the city was pummeled, damaging the infrastructure to such an extent that even now the area does not have power and suffers frequent blackout spikes that deactivate electronics and stunt communications systems. This section of the city had a diameter of approximately three kilometers and is referred to by locals as the Deep Blackout Core, or the DBC. The surrounding region, in a circular pattern about twenty-three kilometers in diameter with the same epicenter, suffered a similar blackout, but the damage to devices, infrastructure, and systems was not as extensive. It has managed to recover to a far greater extent then the DBC.

During the chaos of the blackout, the seeds of independence, long planted, began to sprout, watered and fed by sources both internal and external. In truth, most St. Louisans think independence would have never happened if not for the blackout, and they’re probably correct. The issues with NeoNET’s fall and the loss of a primary Big Ten megacorp for the ARCHology stifled independence efforts earlier, but the blackout allowed loads of shadowy money and a gargantuan influx of insurance cash to feed and fuel the process.
