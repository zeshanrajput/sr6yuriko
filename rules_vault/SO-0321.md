## Game Information

There are three separate inert chemicals that make up this explosive: one masquerades as lipstick, the second is a liquid component disguised as eyeliner, and the third looks like a finishing powder. The inert chemical components are so effectively concealed that most sensors are incapable of identifying them. Only by analyzing the chemicals using a chemistry kit will reveal their true nature. This requires a Biotech (Chemistry) + Logic (3) test that takes ten minutes in the field with a kit but only one minute with a shop or facility.

Combining the chemicals is a delicate process that requires precision measurements. First, the desired amount of lipstick is selected and separated from the rest. Then a precise amount of the liquid eyeliner must be added and mixed into a paste. Finally, mixing in the finishing powder activates the explosive.

The amount of lipstick used determines the base DV of the explosive (one DV per gram, with each stick containing four grams), which is then increased by adding the precise quantity of eyeliner. This requires an Engineering (Demolitions) + Logic (3) test. If the user has the chemistry knowledge skill, the user gains a point of edge to the test. Each net hit adds +1 to the explosive’s DV, to a maximum of double the base DV. If this test fails, the components are ruined. A critical glitch causes a significant amount of toxic vapor to be released [Power: 6, Vector: Inhalation, Speed: 1 Round, Effect: Stun Damage and Dazed]. Once the lipstick and eyeliner have been combined, a chem-sniffer sensor device will be able to detect the compound as an explosive.

The finishing powder is what activates the explosive. The time delay before detonation is determined by how much is used and the method used to apply it. Mixing it directly into the paste accelerates the reaction, while just dusting the paste with powder causes a slower reaction. Applying the precise amount of finishing powder requires an additional Engineering (Demolitions) + Logic (3) test. Failing this test also ruins the explosive compound. A glitch causes the actual time delay to be adjusted. Roll a die: on an odd number, the delay is

increased, and on an even number it is decreased. The amount of time the delay is affected is 1D6 combat rounds. A critical glitch causes an immediate detonation.

When purchased as a kit, the explosive includes an emergency “deactivator” chemical that terminates the explosive reaction, but also ruins the explosives in the process. The deactivator is a clear liquid, usually concealed as nail polish remover.

Each of the explosive compounds can also be used as regular makeup.
