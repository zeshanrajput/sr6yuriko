## Compiled by: Red

The Daedalus station portal is, supposedly, the most secure one in the world. Not really surprising, given that it’s one bad day of gardening away from collapsing. The arboretum that surrounds it provides just enough of a localized manasphere to sustain it and the mages and bug shamans who utilize it. Security in and out is the kind of hyper-secure you can only find in orbital locations, every milligram scanned, assensed, cataloged, and accounted for, a range of weapon platforms ready to turn and burn the whole thing to scrap. If Ares ever did anything right in this series of wrongs, it’s this.

With the switch-up in Ares management at the highest executive levels, the new heads got a good look at all of old man Knight’s black-on-black ops, and this topped the charts. A massive and seemingly bottomless budget for the express primary purpose of sending suicide squads of elite troopers to stage bombing runs on an overrun parallel universe. Maybe it made Knight happy, but that was like pouring beer into the ocean and saying one day all the fish would have measurable alcohol content. Oh, and the beer costs six figures a drop.



This is a terrible analogy, but I hope it works. The point is that Ares was looking at a massive investment waste from a purely profit-minded perspective … and then they found out about the Colony.

An untold sum, somewhere in the billions of nuyen, had gone into establishing a beachhead in the Hive itself: a self-contained fortress with its own metahuman-friendly ecosystem. Labs, containment facilities, living quarters, hydroponics— everything needed to theoretically colonize the Hive.

So it wasn’t just the operations on the ground, or the Daedalus portal labs, but an actual facility posted on the other side, a slice of regular meatspace thrust into a deep metaplane for the first time known in the Sixth World. If it wasn’t so irresponsible, it’d be worthy of a Nobel Prize. But it was treated specifically as a castle; built for war. The containment facilities were there to examine the bugs to discover how to kill them more efficiently more than anything. R&D wasn’t especially turning out exceptionally profitable products, and any potentially marketable data was a side effect or stored away for later use. Even those nuggets of gold were held on old-school data servers, cold storage completely cut off from the modern Matrix and hoarded by the black-ops personnel who constituted the project.
