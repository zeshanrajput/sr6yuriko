## Gene Therapy Treatment

A critter that has already been born can receive genetic augmentations, but the process is more time-consuming, limited, risky, and disruptive to their bodies. The critter must be placed in a growth vat for the duration, which takes weeks—or sometimes even months—during which time its body adapts to the altered genetic template. Sometimes, the critter’s body rejects the generic alterations, and in some cases it suffers from aggressive cancers instead of properly adapting. For these reasons, most megacorporate laboratories prefer the approach of embryonic alteration, but there are enough customers willing to pay the steep prices required to have their favorite critter modified to their desired specifications to support a small market for gene-therapy treatments.
