## \<06-02-83/2217:05>

For the diversion, Spitfire and her drones went out to the west flank along with Swiss, a powerful dedicated spellcaster, and Incubus, one of the most powerful physical adepts I’ve ever met. The Unseelie went with them, and a party of about a dozen or so tore into the chimeras. Watching through the drones’ sensors with the team’s M-TOC, Incubus and his ally spirit, a golden lion, were putting a serious hurt on them. Swiss was throwing some devastating combat spells into their ranks and the drones were advancing in unison, blasting anything in range. But the chimeras kept coming. Still, the attack produced the desired result, and the chimeras near our position went toward the sounds of battle. Arcane provided some help with his fae abilities and we covered the open ground quickly and without drawing any attention.

As we were moving, I sent the signal for the distraction team to withdraw. And that’s when things started to go wrong, really wrong. Something broke through the inner tree line—a Disian war form. I’d heard they existed, but this was the first one I had seen. Twenty meters of muscle and scales, hexapedal with four clawed arms on the upper torso and a mouth full of katana-sized teeth. It reminded me of some terrible god of war.

The Unseelie scattered, along with Swiss, who probably understood its nature better than the rest of us. For some reason Starfire and Incubus stood their ground. In a flash, a half-kilometer swath of trees was instantly incinerated with a spell from

the war form. I think Spitfire went with it, but I’m not sure. The drones were all untouched and continued firing under their previous orders. A massive air spirit started manifesting and began hammering the drones with lightning attacks. The last I saw of Incubus, just before the last drone went down, he was charging the Disian.
