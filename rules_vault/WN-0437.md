## The Job

Being at the heart of this battle for information is going to have its fair share of troubles. Even if the runners use a contact to play middleman, the

streets point right back to them and the target they have on their back for having such juice.

The basics of the job are simple—find a way to use the data you have. The devil is in the details, though. First, the PCs remember it’s not enough to get paid—they need to survive long enough to spend the money. They should be careful to extract some concessions that will provide protection, or at least promises that certain parties will not try to kill them.

They also might be asked to keep some data from ever being reachable by destroying it. For the Dis portal, this means destroying the chamber under Governors Island. For Animus’ memory-erasing technology, this means destroying the Octagon and the secret facility near the base of the Washington Bridge.

The PCs also might want to do more than just look after themselves. There are some important questions at stake, such as:

- Do they leave Animus Construction and the powers behind them free to continue manipulating people’s minds?

- Do they spread awareness of the Dis portal under Governors Island?

- Do they expose various corporate wrongdoing to hurt the corps? Do they try to damage one more than another?

- What do they do with information about risky investments made by Mitsuhama?

It’s a complicated web, and the gamemaster should have fun making the PCs navigate the factions. The gamemaster should be aware of how discreet the PCs are in their negotiations—the less word of their activities spreads, the fewer other parties might become aggrieved at their decisions. If the PCs do a good job of making sure word of what they are doing does not leak out, reward



them by having some people who might be opposed to their actions leave them alone since they don’t know what happened.

The end of the mission comes when the PCs have made all their agreements and move on— possibly out of Manhattan, because it’s often a good idea to not linger long at the scene of a crime.
