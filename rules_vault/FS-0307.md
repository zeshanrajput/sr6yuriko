## Choices, Actions, and Consequences

With reputation being important in the shadows, it can be beneficial for GMs to have PCs deal with the consequences of their actions. For example, runners known to “shoot first and often” could become pigeonholed by Mr. Johnsons, offered only certain runs, or develop a negative reputation for being bloodthirsty. Also, corporations and governments take a dim view when their personnel die. Or a civilian who happens to be related to someone powerful could end up as collateral damage.
