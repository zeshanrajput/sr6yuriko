## The Metaplane Of Dis

Once outside the facility, a dark red sky with a massive black moon hanging overhead can be seen. The directionless light washes out colors, leaving everything shades of greyish purple. The Topside facility is located on the crest of a large hill and currently provides a clear view to the center of the metaplane. The intervening terrain consists of gently rolling hills covered in structures, but numerous areas are shrouded in fog or low-hanging smoke. The center of the city is built around something that could be either an elaborately

carved mountain or a giant stone arcology. Flashes of light flare around this structure and arcs of energy jump between it and the tallest towers surrounding it. Viewed in the astral, the effect is even more pronounced, almost blindingly bright. The architecture of the city varies widely from building to building; the different styles seem to be drawn from a vast number of different metaplanes. The layout of the streets is equally chaotic and patternless, rarely traveling straight for very long. Wide boulevards suddenly transition to narrow, twisting alleys that open onto large courtyards. Near the facility, a 250-meter-wide void in the fabric of the metaplane can be seen, darkness obscuring its center. A few structures along its edge are collapsing in slow-motion, sections of masonry, wooden beams, and large chunks of bedrock are breaking off and drifting away into the void, winking out of existence before they travel very far into the darkness.

Initially there will be no encounters with the residents of the metaplane, as they have all moved away, fleeing the decaying edges around the void. As the runners move toward other areas, especially heading toward the center of the metaplane, encounters will occur—infrequently at first, but they will become unavoidable. These encounters may be with any type of spirit or entity from any metaplane (see _Astral Ways_ for the range of what’s available) and can vary from overtly hostile—a mugging or run-in with a “street gang”—to being approached with unsolicited offers for goods and services. Dis is a crossroads for metaplanar trade, and the majority of residents are not actually from Dis. That can be hard for newcomers to realize, as many entities have been here so long they consider themselves Disian and have adopted the local culture and customs. The **Denizens of Dis** detailed in _Slip Streams_ and the Disians presented in _The Third Parallel_ (Fidian Serverson and the Refugee) are prime examples of this. They are not native Disians, just long-term residents of the metaplane who consider it their home.

If the team decides to scout out astral space, they will discover that access to the center of the metaplane is blocked by insanely powerful spirits—at least twice as powerful as anything previously thought possible—who have been summoned by Dis and are preventing any non-Disian from approaching in the astral. Physical travel to the center of the city is similarly blocked with ranks of chimera (p. 73, _Astral Ways_ ) and bound spirits from numerous other metaplanes who are turning away any non-Disians who attempt to enter.

There is no organized Matrix on the metaplane, but millennia of metaplanar immigrants have brought their own technologies with them. Occasional Matrix access will pop up, but most often the signal will not connect to the team’s equipment, as the interface is incompatible, or the data is in an alien language unknown to the runners.



Regardless of the usability of the connection, Mr. Seven will insist on stopping so that it can examine the data structure. The Null will leave the drone for anywhere between a few minutes to a few hours and then state a desire to search other areas. If any of the runners offer to assist in whatever it’s looking for, Mr. Seven will refuse to provide any information to the team.
