## Example Three

When Haze approached Tara Michaels at Dante’s Inferno, he scored a whopping six net hits on his Influence (Etiquette) test—he’s very good at this. Not only does this provide him an opportunity for conversation, but it also sets him up with a circumstantial point of Edge from a momentum advantage for his following social skill test: a Con (Seduction) + Charisma test to gain Tara’s romantic interest. After the test is made and the Edge points are spent, he ends up with three net hits. Tara is intrigued by Haze and decides to spend more time with him at the club.

Note that seduction is not simply a matter of scoring net hits on social skill tests. If someone is potentially interested, they may be susceptible to seduction attempts. Someone’s orientation, values, interests, chemistry, or other influences may prevent a seduction regardless of how lucky and skillful the seducer may be. That was not the case for Tara Michaels in the above examples; howevGAININGer, based on the GM’s discretion and Haze’s Matrix-assisted people-watching skills, she was a potential match for Haze—or at least who he was pretending to be. If Haze had chosen to seduce the wrong person, he might have made a new friend— or a new enemy—instead of a lover.
