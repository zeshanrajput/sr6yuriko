## Modular Systems

There are construction toys where pieces can be interchanged with some snaps of plastic. Modular cyberware systems are no toys, but the plug-nplay aspect remains the same! A modular mount is implanted into the body or into a cyberlimb, establishing a mechanical joiner that can mate with adapted cyberlimbs or partial replacements. With a modular system, a user can replace a cybernetic body part as quickly as changing their clothes. A

modular mount only works with a chosen, specific cyberlimb style. For example, a modular mount for a forearm replacement cannot accept an entire cyberarm, nor can a mount for a full arm accept a leg. Lower body replacements (see above) require two modular connectors to interface with two modular mounts, but these mounts can also accept a full cyberleg each while the lower body replacement is not in use. Modular mounts may also be installed on drones of small size or larger where they consume one standard weapon mount and grant a user the utility of trading arms with their drones! For those who enjoy the mecha motif, even a smart firing platform may be modified with a modular connector designed to mate with a full cyberarm modular mount. This permits a gun to be tacked directly onto the side of their torso or drone’s hull where an arm should be. It takes a Major Action to attach or detach a modular connector to or from a modular mount.
