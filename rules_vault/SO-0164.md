## Effects

The sale can be anything from posing as a pro to a casual trader or hobbyist. What makes the sale is your insider knowledge. You’ve got a cousin who works with the International Trade Commission who knows that a major corporate buyout is about to happen. Or your boss is being put in charge of the juicy military contract they’re still locked into. Or the UCAS (which you work for, of course) is investing heavily in their infrastructure. The value is about to skyrocket, and the banana republic is about to be wealthy as hell, their currency going with it.

So why are you selling? Well, you might tell them you’re willing to part with some because you’re friends. Or you might tell them if you sell, you’ll have to charge them the additional cost oftrade, because that’s coming out of your end. Regardless, you’re just going to take it and buy more. Make sure you have a story about why they can’t do this without you. Maybe it takes a trade license, or there’s some tax process that they can’t navigate, or you need an insider to get the currency without breaking some laws. Or … you can get it a lot cheaper than they can. As with all of these, you’re the irreplaceable bottleneck that looks like a unique provider of opportunity. You sell, you walk, and they sit, waiting for the price to go up. And hey, maybe they’ll fluke hard and get lucky, in which case … welcome to semi-legitimate currency speculation!
