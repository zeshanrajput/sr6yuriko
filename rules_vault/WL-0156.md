## **Corpselight**

**Researchers will often talk about the millions (if not billions) of different types of animals that exist on Earth, but that is just a drop in the bucket to what is out there in astral space. Some of the astral beings can visit our plane or even feel at home. One of those is the legends of ghostly lights deep in wetlands. Before the Awakening, it was thought to be swamp gas or hallucinogens, but years of research by scientists have identifed an astral being called the corpselight or will o’ wisp.**

**When the corpselight appears on the physical plane, it looks like a glowing ball of light that is**

**==> picture [18 x 765] intentionally omitted \<==**

**roughly thirty centimeters in diameter. On the astral plane is a whole other story. It appears there as a twenty-tentacled cephalopod that latches onto its victims. While the ball is often described as eerie, it doesn’t appear to be threatening, but make no mistake—it is. The amount of light that is cast by the corpselight is generally equated to that of a torch or around ten meters of low light.**

**==> picture [36 x 696] intentionally omitted \<==**

**The corpselight is a solitary hunter in most cases. When they do operate in the same hunting space, it’s not as a team, but as independent entities. Some of the more science-focused have noted that the corpselight will continue to feed even though it has reached some measurement of full. Based on this information, it appears that the creature is mindless. The will o’ wisp will typically either draw its prey in with a comforting light or will chase its victim if especially hungry.**

**Once the creature touches its victim, it triggers some sort of dopamine and serotonin response in its prey. Their victims often describe it as one of the most wonderful feelings in the world. With its prey immobilized with pleasure, it will feed upon their essence until interrupted or the victim has died. While the astral being can feed on other creatures, it appears naturally drawn to sentient beings.**

**We still haven’t discovered how the corpselight reproduces. There have been some pretty wild theories out there, but the most plausible one we have is that the corpselight buds after consuming suffcient amounts of life force. Nothing has confrmed this, but it does seem plausible.**
