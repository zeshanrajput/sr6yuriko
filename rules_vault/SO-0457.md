## Planning the Con

The first step in any long-term project is to define the scope. In the case of the very long con, that means deciding who you’re targeting and what you hope to get out of them. Is your target an individual or an organization? Is this about getting something valuable from them, such as money, artifacts, or paydata? Or is it something personal, such as getting revenge for a past misdeed or righting a wrong? Of course, there’s always the possibility of dabbling in politics—corporations, governments, secret societies, magical groups, and even charitable organizations all have their agendas which may require some long-term duplicity to achieve. There’s an incredible range of possibilities that you may find overwhelming to consider, but it all boils down to answering those two big questions: who are you going to scam, and what do you want to get out of it? Don’t worry about the details at this stage—right now it’s just about setting a goal and choosing a target.

Once you’ve chosen your target, it’s time to do some legwork. Find out everything you possibly can about your mark—from the big stuff like where they came from and what their life goals are, to the little things like their favorite flavor of ice cream and where they like to go out for dinner. Anything and everything you discover could turn out to be useful, so cast a wide net—your goal is to understand your target as much (or more) than they understand themselves. Find out what they’re really like—and what they really want.

In game terms, that means spending some Karma on a very specific quality: the Long Con Pool. You must gain at least one level of the Long Con Pool quality at this time, though you may gain up to two levels if you choose. If you spend at least one



week per level of Long Con Pool studying your target as thoroughly as possible (this merely requires spending the time—no test is required), the Karma cost for purchasing the quality is not doubled.
