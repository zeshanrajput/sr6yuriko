## CHROME LIFE

## Posted by: /dev/grrl

The way that we augment our bodies comes in a couple different flavors that you need to know about before investing some serious nuyen into making you better. Confusing matters further, the bridge between some of those types of ’ware is pretty narrow. I’m going to spend my time teaching you about cyberware specifically, which ones give you the best bang for your nuyen and offer a quick fix to improving your capabilities.

What is cyberware? Cyberware is a broad class of prosthetics or components that are installed in the metahuman body and can range from a basic replacement to serious capability enhancements. They can come in a range of looks, styles, sizes, and purposes that you can pick to suit you. That is, if you have the nuyen to spend.

Now, I know I said that it was the best value option to enhance your abilities, but then I go and tell you that it’s going to cost you. Really, the story there comes down to the grade of ’ware you buy.

There are two factors behind cyberware’s cost: The first is just the quality of the hardware and software, and the second is the quality of the clinic installing the gear. So when your buddy says they got some solid betaware cyberwings installed, they really mean that they got both quality equipment and a proper configuration into their system.

The bottom end of the cyberware spectrum is omegaware, often called “used grade” on the streets. This is the factory-seconds, experimental-gone-wrong, pile-of-drek level of gear. Omegaware is notoriously unreliable and glitchy, and it is prone to erratic behavior at the most inopportune time. But you get to save some nuyen and still come away with the same level of enhancement. It may not look as pretty, and there’s probably a security vulnerability that never got patched, but beggars can’t be choosy. And really, you’re just using the gear until you can afford the latest NERPS ’ware to install.

As the quality of the gear goes up, the amount of tuning required to align your biological systemsalso goes up. With higher-quality grades, cyberware will look better and be better integrated into your nervous system. Just geeking some chummer who has deltaware and trying to get that installed doesn’t mean you have some delta. Your neural network isn’t theirs, and the damage done to the gear from when the previous owner flatlined, not to mention any damage done by removing it from their body, means you are living on used ’ware.

Another grade of cyberware is exoware. It got its name from the fact that it looks like it has a huge exoskeleton of structure. Depending on who you ask, it’s also called Neo-Soviet, industrial, or dieselpunk ’ware in certain circles. This stuff is cheap, provides the same performance as standard cyberware, and tends to be rugged, but unless you’re into an industrial look, it’s a bit of an eyesore. Polite society isn’t going to dig your exposed metal, but lots of runners care more about saving some nuyen than looking pretty.
