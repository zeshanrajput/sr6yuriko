## Lennox-Sullivan Square Towers

The Towers have sunk their former first floor and basement into the silt and mud beneath the



water. Each tower has two new doors on the second floor; one beside the bridge and one facing towards the city. These, along with a small dock on each building facing the river, are the main access points for the towers. None of them have locks or cameras as the building has very limited power. Even at night, most of the activities that require light are performed near windows exposed to the ARCHology and its abundant light. The daylight is used for solar charging of electronics and banks of batteries.

Each door has a pair of River Rat gangers (use Eye-Fiver Go-Ganger, p. 204, _SR6_ ; add SCK Model 100 and stun baton) keeping an eye out for trouble, or playing the latest Matrix games on their commlink until the battery runs out. The dock has two River Rat gangers doing security and four more waiting inside to help handle cargo. This floor on both buildings has plastic crates and rusty metal shelving that keeps everything off the frequently damp and perpetually moldy floor. The ebb and flow of the river regularly shifts this floor from almost dry to thigh-high water, depending on the day.

Stairs connect floors inside each building, and a pulley and hoist system has been installed in each of the six elevator shafts in both buildings. The bridges also connect the floors, though you need to bounce back and forth between them and still use the stairs to get the exact floor you intend. Floors three and four are used for goods storage, floors five through seven are communal living spaces, floor eight is private living spaces for the leaders of the various River Rat gangs and special guests, floor nine is a collection of meeting rooms for negotiations, auctions, and private viewings of high-end items, and floor ten is storage for all high-value items that come into and through the towers. A total of thirty River Rat gangers can be found between the two towers. They could be patrolling, sleeping, hanging out, entertaining themselves, or just working.

Below the water are the former first floors, which housed the entrance foyer, a small café, an office space, and a small gym in each tower. Below those, and accessible from the elevator shaft or the stairwell, is the basement. The basement included a public laundry, the boiler room, a small maintenance office, a set of storage rooms, and an electrical room. In the north tower, the wall at the rear of the storage rooms and boiler rooms collapsed. This wall was along the small river inlet that separates the buildings and provided an ingress for the Sea Dragon to have a case of Confederate gold dropped off.

These lower-level areas don’t have any River Rat gangers swimming around, but they do have a small group of devil jack diamonds (an Awakened version of the pike). The “artifact”

that the runners are after is located in the north tower boiler room. Four Confederate gold bars were dropped from the case the Sea Dragon had dropped off here to pay city officials and underworld figures in order to further the efforts of St. Louis to declare its freedom. The gold bars are inside one of the boilers, but all of the boilers are half buried in muddy silt from the river that has settled in this room. A Perception + Intuition (20, 1 minute) extended test can be used to search for the items.
