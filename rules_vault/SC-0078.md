## banshee hitman

## playing advice

## Roleplaying

Hitmen are, by their nature, very specialized and often solitary. When presented as opponents, their confederates are often easily categorized in one of two camps: heavies hired as distractions and tools, and partners who are their equals and usually share their focus, such as Nightprince and Blackwing during their infamous days executing sanctions in the ’50s. When it comes to runners, this generally means the whole crew specializes in wetwork or that the hitman is moonlighting outside their usual modus operandi. The fact that the assassin is also Infected only isolates them all the more, again unless the whole team shares their nature. This makes integrating them into a more traditional game more challenging, necessitating an agreement at the table that all the players are onboard for the challenges, both in roleplaying and dice rolling, that a character like this presents.



Done well, it opens up deep veins of potential plot and exciting challenges. Done wrong, then lone wolf syndrome sets in, where either the group or the assassin are doing the work, but they don’t function all together. Perhaps the safest way to handle this is to let the hitman be functionally “retired” from exclusive wetwork, branching out to other areas of crime for profit. Alternatively, they might take contracts on the side, using the team to get close to a target and performing the sanction while in the vicinity of the job proper. Again, this requires careful handling but has enormous potential for both tension and profit.

Banshee hitmen walk a tightrope of cool professionalism and sadistic hunger. Banshees crave life force tinged with terror, which does not easily lend itself to silent, elegant ops. Fear, after all, is emerging chaos, and it takes effort to shape such unpredictable energy in the opposition in your favor. Those who eschew the terror effects may find themselves roughly comparable to most vampires, relying more on their skills to survive than any specialist tricks of being a banshee. Those who embrace their nature must learn how to channel it into something productive, or their team will start thinking of them as a paycheck more than a liability. You’re all too aware that plenty of places in the world still offer handsome bounties for Infected.
