## Near and Intermediate Metaplanes

Farther out from the planes I have already described, yet near enough to be easily accessible to the astral traveler, are countless other metaplanes that are typically iterations or combinations of those nearest to the physical plane, but become more strange and alien as you move further out. Though it is possible to reach new, unknown metaplanes here by traveling through the Astral Sea, it’s not a task to be undertaken lightly as it requires covering great distances and introduces the hazard of getting lost. Once you have arrived at a new, unexplored metaplane, you are far from safe. The farther one travels from the physical plane, the environments become ever stranger, and the inhabitants even more alien than our frame of reference has prepared us for.

The Hudson Valley extends out as a tendril from the Plane of Kin, where it touches each of the elemental planes, passes along the edge of the Plane of Shadow, and ends abruptly at the borders of the Plane of Death. As far as I am aware, no living astral traveler—whether present in the flesh or as an astral projection—has succeeded in passing through the barrier to the Plane of Death (though I must admit that I know of very few who have even attempted it). Sometimes things come out to travel other metaplanes on their own errands—or to make their way to the physical plane—but these are the only entities that seem capable of returning to that foreboding plane.

Some have theorized that the Plane of Shadows (or something within it) is the source and cause of all magical diseases that have arisen since the Awakening—in particular, HMHVV. Perhaps there is some merit to this theory, though I doubt any astral travelers are powerful enough to survive exploring deeply enough to confirm or refute it. I’m certainly not planning on spending any time there. And because there are few, if any, valuable resources that can be harvested from the Plane of Shadow (except perhaps for those who pursue toxic traditions), megacorporations and magical groups have very little incentive to visit them.
