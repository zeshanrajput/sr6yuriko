## Vory v Zakone

If I had a nuyen for every runner who’s come to me asking if I could wipe that one malware infection from their comm that bombards them with ads promising to help them meet “Hot Russian MILFs in Your Area”… I mean, I’d only have six nuyen, and I charged each of ’em way more than that for my expert services, but it’s weird that it’s happened this many times in a profession where we’re supposed to care about opsec, right? Anyway, I mention that not just because it’s funny. It’s one of the most prevalent Vory scams out there, and you’d be surprised how many people still fall for it—today, in 2082, when online scams have been a thing for almost a century. They trick or harass you into signing up for this dating site that steals your information and sells it to other scammers.

The Vory are commonly thought of as the Russian Mafia, but in reality they recruit from a variety of Eastern European ethnicities. The name translates roughly to “Criminals who follow a code,” and that “code” boils down to “Do whatever works.” They’re one of the most violent and brutal syndicates globally, and they’re not a power you

want to run against unless you’ve got the resources to avoid the absolute hell that the Vory will unleash upon anyone who crosses them. They deal in virtually every type of crime, from smuggling and metahuman trafficking to drug production and cybercrime—and, in fact, their Matrix presence is one of the most prolific of any syndicate. The Vory are masters of credit fraud, DDOS attacks, data theft, and as I’ve already mentioned, scams.

Vory hackers have been using Kechibi as a way to add weight to ransomware attacks against companies large enough to possess stock to trade. They gain entry to corporate hosts to flood them with ransomware, which frequently appears as a wall of fire or a lock and chains around the host icon. Popups demand a large sum of money within seventy-two hours, or the host will be deleted, all its data will be stolen, and Kechibi will wreak havoc on their stock prices or portfolios.

- This kind of ransomware attack isn’t new for the Vory; this version involving Kechibi is just a new form. They’ve targeted both companies and individual users with the same tactic.
