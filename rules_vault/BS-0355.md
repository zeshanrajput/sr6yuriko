## Game Information

## Addiction

Using drugs for performance enhancement or pleasure brings the real risk that you become addicted to their use. This includes being physically addicted to the substance or having a psychological dependence. And it’s not only drugs or BTLs that you can get addicted to. You could become addicted to draining Essence (pp. 106–107, _Sixth World Companion_ ), gambling, foci, and more. For magical focus addiction or augmentation addiction, you need to make an Addiction Resistance test when you use the augmentation or focus on a test. This addiction check only needs to be made once in a twenty-four-hour period regardless of the number of pieces of ’ware or foci in use. The Power is listed in the Vice Table.

Whenever you engage in potentially addictive behaviors or use addictive substances, roll 2D6. If the value of the roll is less than the Power, then you need to make an Addiction Resistance test. Failure on the Addiction Resistance test means that you gain the addiction, or the addiction level increases.

The Addiction Resistance test for drugs, augmentations, and magic is a Body + Willpower + Lifestyle (Comforts) Opposed test vs. the substance’s Power; protective gear does not apply. The addiction resistance test for BTL is a Firewall + Willpower + Lifestyle (Comforts) Opposed test vs. the software’s Power; protective gear does not apply. For both types of Addiction Resistance test, you add the Lifestyle Points spent on comforts if you are using the Lifestyle rules from _Sixth World Companion_ , or the level of Lifestyle (Street adds 0 dice and Luxury adds 5 dice) to the test. While addicted, the character needs to spend nuyen to get their fix. For addictions that don’t have a cost, such as augmentation addiction or focus addiction, the runner must make a Composure (Addiction level) test to not use their focus, cyberware, or bioware. For other purchasable addictions, a simple approach is to use the costs found in the _Sixth World Companion_ (p. 154), where you pay 100 nuyen per level per month or five percent increase per level of addiction to your lifestyle, whichever is higher. Alternatively, work with your gamemaster to determine what you need to pay and how you want to deal with this in the game.

If you have reached the maximum level of addiction, failing the Addiction Resistance test results in you taking permanent damage to your attribute maximums. On failure, runners reduce their racial maximum for Body by 1. When the racial maximum reaches 0, the character dies.
