## The Job

The first part of the job is easy—Silver Nail buys the PCs a meal to thank them for their efforts on his behalf. He tells them that he’s learned that maybe he’s not cut out for the shadows, so he’s getting out. If he gets any calls for new work, he

25: THEpromises to forward them to other runners—like the PCs, perhaps.

Then he gets to the special bonus part of the meal. He says that he started his path into the shadows because while his parents were ostensibly Spinrad employees, he noticed them acting with increased secrecy, and he heard murmurs of some decidedly non-Spinrad work they were involved in. He decided to learn more about what they were up to, and he spent a fair amount of his shadow career looking into it. He is now going to turn over what he learned to the PCs.

He tells them that his parents were working for an organization named “Animus Construction.” That should, of course, immediately grab the PCs’ attention. Unfortunately, most of what he knows will not be new to the PCs—he knows the ownership is shadowy, that there is a lot of medical expertise involved, and they seem to meddle with people’s brains. He does, though, have two pieces of information of particular interest:

1. He overheard conversations between his parents about some of the procedures not working as well as they thought. He says they compared it to how the brain sometimes develops new pathways after someone has a stroke—some things Animus thinks they are closing off are finding ways to resurface.

1. His parents are meeting someone tonight, and he thinks it’s related to Animus, so if the PCs surveil the meeting, they might pick up some useful information.
