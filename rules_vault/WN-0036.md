## Midtown

- **Council Member:** Abe Giordano

- **Security Rating:** AAA

- **Typical Host Rating:** 6/3/6

- **Primary Corporate Funding:** Horizon, Aztechnology

Midtown is what the average person thinks of when they hear New York. The district officially covers all real estate from the west shoreline to Third Avenue, and between 34[th] Street up to the tip of Central Park at 59[th] Street. However, a previously walled-off section of that footprint, known as Terminal, has been sheared off to become its own district. The fact that this area, like New Harlem and Washington Heights, gets only one vote is a testament to how much of a rubber stamp the city council and the mayor actually are.

Midtown is known for streets teeming with corp suits moving in the shadow of a thousand massive skyrakers. The Aztechnology Pyramid, Rockefeller Center, and the Empire State Building are part of the history of this district, as is the flagship New York Public Library and adjoining

Bryant Park near Times Square. The jewel of the Midtown skyline is the one-hundred-sixty-story Horizon building, whose four exterior sides also double as laser-lit billboards that simulcast advertisements in AR.

Virtually everything at ground level is commercial space offering anything your credstick can afford. Zoé’s flagship store on the corner of 5[th] and 57[th] kicks off a stretch of high-fashion stores that continues to the beginning of the Grand Central Terminal Arcology at 47[th] .

- Be wary of the Going Out of Business signs. It’s an old ploy the shops use to convince you their prices are slashed to near-greymarket levels—they aren’t. In truth, they’ve marked their prices up 500 percent, and then knocked around 75 percent off that new figure. You’re better off shopping at the superstores at either end of the island than anywhere in Midtown.

- Mr. Bonds

- A strip of brightly lit buildings north of Times Square known as Neon City is the best place to get your hands on BTLs and other controlled substances and activities. The stores there operate legally, checking CityPass licensing and whatnot. In my experience, they aren’t looking too closely. Ask the right questions and they can even show you stuff the MDC won’t legalize.

- Kat o’ Nine Tales

Midtown is among the safest places for average citizens to go and a must-see for tourists. That safety comes at a cost to privacy. While in Midtown, your CityPass profile is constantly scanned, and personalized AR advertisements are thrown back at you. The NYPD patrols these streets in two-officer cars, but the fixed cameras and drone patrols are the real threat. In the evenings when the offices empty out and the population recedes like the tide, the NYPD patrols are reduced, creating a small window of opportunity for the Night Market street vendors to make their way to the surface.

A fraction of the million-plus corporate suits who work in the district also live here, packed into mixed-retail residential buildings where the apartments’ housing starts thirty stories off the ground. Below them, Midtown evenings thrum with the nightlife of a hundred clubs catering to any personality or fetish you can imagine.
