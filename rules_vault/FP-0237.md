## **A Bit of History**

**In a nutshell, Warpath is a response to a great injustice. The root cause of this organization stems from the Ghost Dance, or more specifcally its aftermath. Even today, a fair number of Native Americans feel that they shouldn’t have stopped when they did and should have asked for more during the First Treaty of Denver, but back then … oh, man! There was a lot of hatred and anger on both sides.**

**The terms of the settlement were unequal, and no one got a worse deal than the eastern tribes. Not only were their ancestral lands not returned, as was the case for the western tribes and the northern Indigenous people, but the United States savagely uprooted us (again) and shipped us to the newly created Native American Nations. Now the NAN and the Sovereign Tribal Council globally welcomed their eastern brethren, but we were still basically uprooted refugees.**

**It wasn’t too long before the eastern tribes started pushing for further negotiations and for additional land claims, but they were summarily dismissed by the STC. Around that point in time, some decided to take matters into their own hands. While we don’t have an exact date, we know that Warpath was formed sometime in the late ’30s around a shamanic lodge in the Boston area. Their goal was, and still is, straightforward: boot the Anglos out of North America and reclaim the ancestral lands of the eastern tribes.**

**However, it was a few years before they came to the world’s attention. On Christmas day 2040, a Connecticut casino, built on a former reservation appropriated from its original tribal owners, was targeted by a magical explosion. It was the frst strike of a long and bloody list of terrorist acts.**

- **Kay St. Irregular**

**At frst, our numbers were low, and over the course of the ’40s and ’50s, we struck many blows against the governments and the corporations that stole our lands. These actions rallied a few to our**

**cause and attracted the attention of many. While the UCAS (and later the CAS) continued to refuse to negotiate or return our land, they could no longer afford to ignore us.**

**That changed in the mid ’60s, when we sided with Winternight. In hindsight, it was a terrible idea, but at the time, it seemed like an opportunity we could not say “no” to. Here was a group willing to share their resources and to help us strike a blow at the UCAS … well, they do say: “Beware of Greeks bearing gifts.” What they really needed from us was to be the fall guy. And we jumped in with both feet. Crash 2.0 was disastrous for us. Our leadership and our ranks were wrecked. Only a few of us survived the aftermath of the attacks on Boston, and everyone went to ground.**

**It was during the Az-Am war in the early ’70s that we made our return. A generous backer contacted some of the remaining members in the CAS and provided us with money and equipment. With those, we were able to start an aggressive recruitment campaign, rebuilding our numbers, exceeding what they were before Crash 2.0. At last, we could resume our activities. Back north, the Lockdown of ’76 gave us an opportunity to snatch some of our land back and paved the way to a new approach that has proven effective.**

- **It was Aztechnology, through Aztlan, that financed Warpath and others, such as New Revolution, back in ’73. It had less to do with generosity and more to do with keeping the CAS occupied with domestic terrorism, allowing them to focus on the Bogotá front.**

- **Pyramid Watcher**

**And then the events of summer ’80 happened. Like everyone, the Detroit bug war caught us by surprise, but when the blackouts hit and the NAN invasions came in, we were quick to join in the fray. We kicked the UCAS while it was down! We struck blows in pretty much every city that was in the dark. We were able to set our own pieces in place, generating further chaos and acquiring more land. Those gains were minimal, but that’s still better than none. It has been two years, the UCAS is still scrambling about, and we’re still there, slowly chipping away at it.**
