First, manufactured value is always good stuff. With a team, not only only can you be what they’re looking for, you put their doubts at ease by establishing that you’re wanted by others. Whether they’re comforted by having their desire verified by outside parties or get a rush out of being the one who wins in a competition, you can create an arena where they are more at ease. Insecure targets can be chosen over others, which makes you someone who reinforces their sense of self, lending intimacy. Confident marks will enjoy overcoming rivals, giving them a sense of “winning.”

Another is early and escalating disclosure. There was a study almost a hundred years ago that showed opening up early and showing vulnerability engenders reciprocity, so the more you lay on the table, the safer they feel. For some ops, this might be all you need to get some dirt. For something deeper, you make yourself look like you’re vulnerable. If they return the favor, you are establishing intimacy. If they don’t, they feel like they have leverage, which makes them feel safer. And you want them to feel very, very safe.

Kino is a tactic used to get the mark feeling more comfortable with you. Little touches, bits of physical contact without breaching their limits, every time testing how long you can get away with it. It works verbally, too, seeing what they’re comfortable with. For cons and predators alike, this has the dual function of scouting boundaries and subtly testing and expanding them. You see this both with players and genuine courtship.
