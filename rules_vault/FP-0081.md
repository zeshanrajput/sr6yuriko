## **A Growing Power Base**

**If you think a boatload of money and insight into the future can help you build commercial and political infuence, then you’d be absolutely right. Just don’t think that’s all you need.**

**Once you start in the infuence-building game, you have a certain honeymoon period where your main competitors are low-level people who don’t know what they’re doing and often are under-resourced. Competence and a non-zero amount of resource support put you above them, and you can make progress rapidly.**

**Until you get noticed. You can gain a certain amount of infuence for a time, but once the real power players see that you’re starting to make things happen, they’ll be aware. And they’ll try to counter you, which means you’re now playing an entirely different game. It’s beyond just fguring out what people want and how you can deliver it to them. They’re going to throw every counterintelligence move in the book at you—double agents, hacking, infltration, opposition research, you name it. They’re skilled at what they do, so if you’re not ready for them, you’ll get buried.**

**This is where Convergence now fnds themselves. The honeymoon is now over, and some of the real powers of the world—namely, Horizon and Aztechnology—are looking at keeping them contained. Their future depends on how well they can rise to the challenge. But that leads me into the next section.**
