Downtime Protocols & Common Rules

This section aggregates core cheat sheets and templates for downtime registration, rigging, decking, and resonance network benefits.

Registering Sprites

  • Minor downtime action: Register 8 levels of sprites (up to R7 sprite)
  • 1 per night’s rest (7 hrs registering, 3 hrs restoration). Must buy hits (SRMG p19)
  • Outcome: R7 Modular sprite with 3 services/powers for 3 missions

1. Compiling Phase

  • Pool: Tasking (6) + RES (6) + Resonance Focus (4) = 16 dice → 4 Bought Hits.
  • Sprite’s Defense: Level 7 × 2 = 14 dice → 3 Bought Hits.
  • Net Hits: 4 - 3 = 1 → 1 base service.
  • Technoshaman Stream Modification: Grants +1 additional service on a successful compiling test. Total compiled services = 2 services/powers.
  • Fading Value (FV): Under core rules, compiling fade is exactly equal to the hits received on the sprite’s defense test = 3.
  • Technoshaman Mitigation: Reduces compiling fade by a flat 2 (3 - 2 = 1).
  • Fading Resistance: Willpower (6) + Charisma (4) + Submersion Grade / Sprite Conduit (6) = 16 dice → 4 Bought Hits.
  • Compiling Damage Taken: 1 FV - 4 hits = 0 Damage.

2. Registering Phase

  • Pool: Registering (8) + RES (6) + RES Focus (4) = 18 dice → 4 Bought Hits.
  • Sprite’s Defense: Level 7 × 2 = 14 dice → 3 Bought Hits.
  • Net Hits: 4 - 3 = 1 → 1 base service.
  • Combined Total Services: 2 (compiled) + 1 (registered) = 3 total services/powers.
  • Fading Value (FV): Under core rules, registering fade is equal to twice the hits received on the sprite’s defense test (3 x 2 = 6).
  • Fading Resistance: Willpower (6) + Charisma (4) + Submersion Grade / Sprite Conduit (6) = 16 dice → 4 Bought Hits.
  • Registering Damage Taken: 6 FV - 4 hits = 2 boxes of Stun damage.

3. Resonance Focus Fading

Because a data structure/focus was used to boost both tests, it incurs its own independent data-structure fade (4 / 2 = 2).

  • Focus Fading Pool: WIL (6) + LOG (4) = 10 dice → 2 Bought Hits.
  • Damage Taken: 2 FV - 2 hits = 0 Damage.

To understand how an Artificial Intelligence (AI) character with the Pilot Origins quality pilots a drone, we must distinguish between two distinct methods of drone operation: Jumped-In (Rigging) and Remote Control (Un-jumped) [Hack and Slash, p. 119; SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 198].

The rules handle these two scenarios differently regarding whether they use standard mental attributes or the AI’s Matrix Equivalent Attributes [Hack and Slash, p. 118; SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 198].

1. Jumped-In Operation (Why Matrix Equivalents Are Bypassed)

The Pilot Origins quality states:

“The AI may jump into drones and vehicles, controlling them like a rigger. Each level of this quality grants the bonus of a control rig of an equal rating” [Hack and Slash, p. 119].

When an AI character chooses to jump into a drone using this quality, standard rigging rules take over:

  • According to the Control Rigs and You rules, a rigger who is jumped into a device uses their mind instead of their meat body [SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 198].
  • Physical attributes are replaced entirely by Mental attributes for all jumped-in tests [SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 198].
  • The conversion table for jumped-in riggers dictates the following replacements [SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 198]:
Standard Physical Attribute Jumped-In Mental Attribute
Body Willpower
Strength Charisma
Agility Logic
Reaction Intuition

Since an AI character natively possesses standard Mental attributes (Willpower, Charisma, Logic, and Intuition) in their character block [Hack and Slash, p. 118], they use these Mental attributes directly when jumped in.

  • Piloting: When piloting a jumped-in drone, the test uses Reaction. Because the AI is jumped in, Reaction is replaced by Intuition. The AI rolls Piloting + Intuition [SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 198].
  • Gunnery/Firing: When firing a jumped-in drone’s weapons, the test uses Agility. Because the AI is jumped in, Agility is replaced by Logic. The AI rolls Firearms + Logic [SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 198].

Because the standard rigging rules bypass physical attributes in favor of mental attributes, the AI does not need to refer to its “Physical Attribute Matrix Equivalents” (Firewall, Sleaze, Data Processing, Attack) in this state.

2. Remote Control / Un-jumped Operation (When Matrix Equivalents ARE Used)

If the AI is not jumped into the drone (e.g., they are issuing remote control commands to a drone, or the drone is operating autonomously as an “AI-driven drone” without the AI jumped in), standard remote control rules dictate that the controller’s Physical attributes are used [SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 201]. If the AI is controlling the device via VR they use their mental attributes, but if the AI is controlling the device via AR they use their “physical” attributes [Devices, AR and VR, SR6 Core Rulebook, p. 180].

Because an AI lacks a physical body, they must substitute their missing Physical attributes using the Matrix Equivalent Attributes table [Hack and Slash, p. 118]:

Standard Physical Attribute AI Matrix Equivalent Attribute
Body Firewall
Agility Data Processing
Reaction Sleaze
Strength Attack

In this remote-control scenario, the AI does use its Matrix Equivalents:

  • Piloting: When remotely piloting a drone, the test normally calls for Piloting + Reaction. The AI substitutes Reaction for Sleaze, rolling Piloting + Sleaze [Hack and Slash, p. 118].
  • Gunnery/Firing: When remotely firing a drone’s mounted weapon, the test normally calls for Firearms + Agility. The AI substitutes Agility for Data Processing, rolling Firearms + Data Processing [Hack and Slash, p. 118].

Network Benefits

Permanent Benefits

  • -6 Noise Reduction: Designer home device -2, signal scrubber -2, ECM warrior -2
  • +1 Sensor Rating: Added to any connected sensor (sensor upgrade)
  • Edge Storage: Capacity (5)
  • Resonant Coordination: Connected characters who use the Send Message action (a Minor Action) among themselves gain a bonus Minor Action immediately afterward.
  • Smartsoft Array: Drone Target Locks are shared among networked drones (smartsoft). Target Lock victims cannot spend edge against attacking networked drones.

Available Benefits

  • Tasking Tests: Electronics (+5) and Cracking TW (+7)
  • Rigger Support: Engineering (+3) TW
  • Tactical Software apps:
    • Artillery Barrage: +1 to Launch Weapons, no rifle/cannon penalties
    • Mobile Medic: +3 Biotech (First Aid) + Harmonize hits
    • Sneak-Sneak: +2 Stealth
    • Target Artist: Attack w/o LOS on painted targets
  • Sensory Array: Social HUD/Thermal Mood Reading/Vocal Tension Detection: +1 Edge on Judge Intentions
