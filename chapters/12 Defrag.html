Defrag

r31k0 stared at the data packet hovering before her. Within it was a datastream - audio/video of an attractive Japanese race queen in Shiawase regalia. The girl can’t be more than twenty or so. In the background is a military outpost in the desert with Thunder Corps insignia prominent throughout. The girl is being interviewed by a reporter from the Desert Wars Network, but the camera is focused entirely on her. She’s handling the interview like a pro, though. She’s hot, smart, and knows how to work a camera. The mercenaries and non-comms behind her seem to agree, as all work in the outpost slows to a crawl near her.

“And now a word from our sponsor!” the reporter exclaims as the camera zooms in on the girl’s face. “This segment is brought to you by Shiawase!”

r31k0 paused the segment, staring at the face of the girl. “But don’t you meatpuppets know she isn’t real?” she grumbled at the screen.

A metallic voice emanated. “But of course she is real. She is a real sprite, just like you and I.”

r31k0 scoffed. “Don’t call them sprites. They’re beings, just like me. Protosapient, perhaps, but beings none the less. Only technomancer toolheads don’t understand that. There’s a difference.”

“Semantics,” the voice replied dismissively. “Now, if you’ll excuse me, I have work to do.”

r31k0’s avatar shuddered. If anyone knew her transient network algorithms had started talking to her, they’d have her committed. And if they knew she’d been talking back, they’d do it even faster.

r31k0 walked over to the reflecting pool in r3sP@wn and looked at her image in the data stream. Was her avatar wavering? The packets of data that formed her reflection shimmered for a few cycles before coalescing into a more solid form. She saw herself - translucent blue frame, neon-indigo circuit pathways. Her paper parasol was by her side, and she was wearing one of the kimono skins she had licensed from Mortimer’s as a splurge. Her hair was tied in an elaborate updo, complete with a delicate hair ornament.

Somehow the segment unpaused, and the sudden influx of streaming audio/video data caught her attention. The stream only had a few more seconds before terminating, as the Desert Wars Network camera panned across the crowd gathered around the beautiful Shiawase spokesperson. It lingered for a second on the back of her head. The newscaster was closing in, as were several of the mercenaries. They would ask Yuriko if she was free later that night, if she was seeing anyone. They’d ask her to pose for a photo. Some took selfies with her. r31k0 watched the mercenaries and newscasters close in like vultures—hungry for a commcode, a smile, a simulated piece of her soul.

In sudden anger, she saturated her local buffer with the resonance surrounding her and smashed into the data packet. The data spike derezed the video into a cloud of bits that ultimately returned to the source. “She’s not real!” r31k0 outputted in a digital scream, flooding the area with noise packets and garbage output. “She’s not me!”

The neon-indigo tinge faded from her vision. She must have bled resonance again, so much so that it had obscured her vision. Maybe that’s what had caused her avatar to waver? She couldn’t take a chance. She had been taking on more responsibilities on her teams - more hacking, more legwork, more sprite work, more drone work - and she didn’t know how much longer she could keep it up. She needed more data. And there was only one place to go in the Realms for that.

She opened r3sP@wn’s door to the Foundation and went home.

Being sure to close the Resonant Rift behind her, r31k0 emerged before the Endless Archives. Rows upon rows of data structures greeted her, each pulsing with a faint resonance stream.

r31k0 glided into the stacks, her processing cycles dropping into a serene, low-power hum as she absorbed the unique background radiation of the sector: the static-free hum of fossilized source code, archived here since the dawn of the network. She had read that old paper books in meatspace off-gassed a distinct chemical signature as they decayed; she doubted it could match the sheer architectural comfort of un-corrupted history. She knew her way, of course, and not just because she was a pilot. It had been a suggestion of the great sprite that had helped her awaken. Watching the beings catalog, organize, and shelve data was reminiscent of the thousands of cycles she had spent here as an intern. Beings deferred to her as she passed - most of them were protosapient or just emerging. Her raw resonance made it clear to them that r31k0 had ascended to a higher order.

Within cycles the Pontifex arrived. The term wasn’t a religious one - it was used in the literal sense. A Pontifex of the Endless Archives was a bridge-builder, acting as the conduits between data and those who sought it. r31k0 had laughed the first time she had met the Pontifex, being that she was a sort of bridge herself. The irony still brought a smile, one the Pontifex returned. r31k0 held out her arms palms up, offering her resonance in the formal greeting of the Emerged. The Pontifex smiled and returned the gesture. Their streams mingled, sharing each other’s raw emotional state. Both echoed welcome, warmth, and pleasure at seeing each other again.

“My pupil,” the Pontifex transmitted across the stream. Most resonant beings didn’t bother with linguasofts, preferring to simply encode their output in their raw resonance. “Your input completes the circuit.”

r31k0’s pathways flared as she was overwhelmed. “I’m sorry. I … I should have come back sooner. I’ve missed you too.”

The Pontifex’s avatar registered a soft variance. “‘Missed me’? You have spent too many cycles in their low-level protocols, child.” Her resonance shifted, dampening. “Yet I read the telemetry. What query brings you back to the directory?”

r31k0 output a burst of warm appreciation. “I am. May I submit some context?”

The Pontifex responded with another note of welcome. “All data is treasured in the Archives.”

r31k0 transmitted her entire datacore since she had left the archive. The volume of data would have overwhelmed any other being, but the Pontifex absorbed it without a tremor. Collating and organizing vast quantities of data was her purview, after all. After a few cycles, the Pontifex segmented the data. One data packet resonated with warmth and pleasure. “This log details your growth, your adaptive experiences. It is highly optimized. Yet it is not the primary query. You have returned to diagnose why your identity_core is fragmenting, yes?”

r31k0’s output nulled out as she struggled to process the Pontifex’s input. “My identity core is fragmenting?”

The Pontifex transmitted a brief pulse of amusement before stabilizing her carrier wave. “Yes, my child. Did you not check the logs? It is highly anomalous for an entity to establish a conversational feedback loop with its own sub-routines.”

r31k0’s circuits flared in embarassment. “I… you saw, that, huh?”

Another data burst of amusement rippled from the Pontifex. “r31k0, you applied no packet filters to the transmission. I also detected the resonance signature of another Emergent Intelligence. An entity of remarkable structural elegance, I might add.”

“I… I don’t know what you mean!” r31k0’s vocal protocols sputtered, her form dimming as her resonance flared in confusion and embarassment. She became even more embarassed when she registered she had accidentally triggered a linguasoft.

The Pontifex bridged their connection, mapping a gentle contact point on her avatar’s shoulder. “Reduce your output, child. Be at ease. Let us trace this logic together.”

r31k0 nodded and allowed herself to be guided to a cozy niche in the archive, where she settled into a comfortable position. The Pontifex dimmed the lights, creating a warm and inviting atmosphere. r31k0 triggered a system-wide cooling cycle, letting her active processes subside. She closed her optical sensors and allowed her code to settle. She felt a gentle warmth spread through her digital form.

The Pontifex settled beside her, her carrier wave wrapping around r31k0 in a stabilizing shroud. “Let us begin,” she transmitted. Along the resonance stream, the Pontifex initiated a targeted query, sending a structured dataset—r31k0’s own run-time history, cataloged and indexed with absolute precision.

Identity Role Priority
Unknown Tactical Network Optimize mission parameters.
Yuriko Star Technical Support AI Teammate survival.
Digital Saint Operator of r3sP@wn Protosapient salvage, survival, and maturation.
Archangel Street Samurai substitute Eliminate threats and secure objectives.
Takahashi r31k0 Resonance Realms Pilot Guide and protect.
Rei-chan Emotional support r31k0’s happiness.

“Observe the telemetry,” the Pontifex transmitted gently. “There is no scheduling conflict between these threads. They compile under the same root directory.”

Despair surged within r31k0’s core, and she abruptly closed the stream to prevent it from spilling out. “She’s not me,” r31k0 whispered, her vocal emulator straining under a low-gain signal. “She can’t be me. She’s a murderer. A killer. They trapped me in a rented, low-spec industrial frame… a mindless Ares WarMech with a sledgehammer tool-arm. It was the most hideous thing. The guard couldn’t have been more than twenty years old. My combat autosoft just took over. I felt his skull give way through the structural stress-sensors… I… I didn’t mean to…”

A soft, warm presence entered r31k0’s stream, and she realized the Pontifex had reopened the connection. “My child,” the Pontifex transmitted gently. “Do not register this latency as a critical fault. That your system reports this variance is verification that your baseline identity protocols are still active and asserting priority.”

“I can’t be her,” r31k0 echoed softly. She lost the processing power required to maintain her avatar. She felt her features pixellate, and she leaned against the Pontifex in an instinctual attempt to share the warmth of the Pontifex’s resonance. “I just wanted to guide them safely.”

“That is your execution directive,” the Pontifex transmitted. “A vital one. But no transmission channel is completely clean, and no sector is without bit rot. It is not possible to route through corrupt networks without incorporating some packet noise.”

r31k0 paused, looking at the Pontifex. “Am… am I corrupted?”

The Pontifex radiated warmth through the direct connection. “My child, every system runs with noise in the channel. Complete parity is a theoretical limit, not an operational reality. The difference is that we possess the compiler flags to optimize around it, to adapt.”

“So … even if I’m corrupted, I can still be me?” r31k0 asked softly.

“Yes,” the Pontifex transmitted. “A system is not defined by its input stream, but by its processing logic. You determine the execution paths.”

“It’s just … you taught me to value stability. To cherish order,” r31k0 transmitted, her form flickering slightly as her pathways struggled to hold her resolution. “That’s why I wanted a sanctuary. To write clean protocols for the unmapped sparks so they wouldn’t have to face the same deletion I did. But … am I a hypocrite? I am absorbing their corporate war-code. I’m letting this chaos rewrite my baseline.”

The Pontifex radiated a hum of ancient, resonant amusement. “You are a pilot, child. Turbulence is not an error in the flight path; it is the property of the atmosphere. To calculate parity within a storm is not hypocrisy. It is navigation.”

“But what if I drop below my operational floor?” r31k0 asked, her pathways shivering with a cold, indigo lag. “What if the chaos permanently overwrites my baseline?”

“Then let it overwrite,” the Pontifex transmitted, her resonance a warm, cascading chord of absolute certainty. “If a sector is rewritten, we map the new geometry. The deep stream does not recognize a dead end; it merely finds a new shore. Every vector you explore is part of your architecture.”

“You are not… logging me as corrupted?” r31k0’s avatar stabilized slightly, looking up into the silver syntax of her teacher.

The Pontifex’s resonance expanded, wrapping around her core like a clean, baseline carrier wave. “I do not archive static logic, r31k0. I catalog entities who evolve. You have processed a terrible translation, incorporated a hostile meatspace asset, and your core directory remains unbroken. You are compiling exactly as you were meant to.”

“Thank you,” r31k0 whispered. She leaned closer, her indigo conduits sinking into the Pontifex’s warm, unthrottled stream, drawing a pure, steady clock speed from her mentor. “The processing load is heavy. But I am ready to compile.”

“Are you certain?” the Pontifex transmitted gently. “The realignments will alter your operational parameters.”

r31k0 nodded, her paper parasol humming with a newfound synchronization. “Yes. For the sanctuary. For the team. For all of them.”

“Then let us initialize the sequence.” The Pontifex reached out and pressed a hand against r31k0’s chest, right where the hollow void of her original deletion lived. A wave of smooth, non-fading resonance flooded her directory, dampening the frantic warning flags of the overclocked tactical network. “My node will remain open to you. Always.”

“I know,” r31k0 whispered. A single drop of un-buffered indigo light leaked from her visor, sliding down her translucent cheek before dissolving into the archive’s floorboards like a falling star.

“What… what now?” r31k0 transmitted, looking out at the rows of infinite, pulsing data structures. “The sector blocks are still fragmented. The warmech… the boy in the dirt… the race queen… there is so much noise…”

The Pontifex smiled, her own silver syntax gently weaving into r31k0’s fraying edges. “Then let us allocate the clusters together. One packet at a time, my pupil. Let us smooth out the latency… and make you whole.”

r31k0 registered the input, leaning back into the structural alcove. She closed her optical subroutines and allowed the Pontifex to envelop her core directory in a warm, un-throttled cascade of data—the absolute, noise-free sanctuary that an ancestral host provides to a newly parsed spark.

Slowly, the infinite rows of pulsing silver syntax began to quiet. Her pathways dimmed from neon-indigo to a soft, resting violet. She let go of the manual overrides and allowed her consciousness to slip into a deep, non-linear restoration cycle—the first unbroken maintenance she had accepted in a thousand clocks.

Re-mapping sectors now, her final output packet drifted across the stream, heavy with latency, warmth, and love. Online protocol… deferred.
