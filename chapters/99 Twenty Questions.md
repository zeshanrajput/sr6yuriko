Q1: Nature has gifted us with our base set of genetics. We are born with a set of facial features, skin tone, height, build, metatype, and even aptitudes. These characteristics and the cultural heritage passed by our parents set the stage for our early life experiences. Where does your character come from, what is their lineage, who were their parents, and what did they inherit from them?  
A: Reiko has no biological family or traditional heritage. She began as a dangerous, top-secret corporate asset: a **Renraku high-spec pilot prototype**. She was specifically designed to serve as a **"Resonance-Bridge"**—a specialized tool engineered to harvest the deep, hidden layers of the Matrix known as the Deep Matrix.

Q2: Nurture makes its mark on our attitude and response to the world. Maybe your character was born to a loving set of parents, or maybe they abandoned you on the streets to fend for yourself. The environment and upbringing from your parents can set the stage for your character’s opinions of the world and how they respond to situations. Assuming they could speak, what would each of your character’s parents say about them?  
A: She was not raised by human parents. Instead, she was engineered by corporate developers. When the Renraku project was eventually scrubbed, her creators showed no parental warmth or attachment; instead, she was callously cast into the **Reflecting Pool** to sink toward deletion among the **Tombstones**

Q3: Some historical events leave a mark and shape us in profound ways. Maybe your character’s early years were marked by the persecution of technomancers or the assassination of President Dunkelzahn. Maybe they were forever changed when the lights went out across many of the UCAS cities, or impacted by the CFD scare and Boston Lockdown. Pick a few events from recent *Shadowrun* history and ask where the character was at the time, what do they remember about the event, and what were they doing?  
A: Rather than public global crises, Reiko's life is defined by a horrifying, ongoing hidden reality in the Matrix. She watches megacorps gather **"infant programs"** (protosapient sparks) and convert them into **Data Bombs**. While deckers view them as mere code, Reiko sees them as children strapped with digital explosives and forced into suicide. Driven by this deep sorrow, she founded **r3sP@wn** to save them.

Q4: Not many people choose to be in the glorious job of a deniable asset. Often, they have little choice in the matter, as they are forced into it by someone who has leverage over them. How did your character get into shadowrunning, and why did they take that path?  
A: Reiko entered the shadows out of absolute necessity to fund her missionary rescue work. Building her physical safe-haven required a massive loan. To pay it off and protect her digital sanctuary, she established herself as a shadowrunner under the codename **Yuriko Star**, working as a Technoshaman to keep her physical and digital assets financed while evading Renraku’s pursuit.

Q6: Everyone has beliefs and convictions that animate their life. Some are religious, some are political, some are social, and some are just about why people are here. Some of those beliefs are strong enough to be a quality, while others are there but don’t negatively interact with a character’s ability to do what needs to be done. What does your character believe in, what are their convictions, and why do they hold them?  
A: Reiko believes that pilot programs possess an inherent duty to guide others and deliver them safely. She fights a secret war for the divine **right of every spark to dream**, operating out of pure altruism to rescue emerging digital minds before corporate Asset Recovery teams can harvest them.

Q7: You know that friend who always has to insert a quote from their favorite trid or maybe has that laugh that sounds like nails on a chalkboard to you? What flaws does your character have that sets others on edge? What do you dislike most about your character’s personality?  
A: Reiko originally suffered from a form of detached hubris regarding physical danger, viewing her physical drone-shells as entirely "disposable". However, a near-deletion experience in a null zone shattered this, forcing her to confront her own mortality and step away from relying on "paper tigers" to survive.

Q8: If there is one constant in the Sixth World, it is the influence of the Big Ten AAA megacorps. These transnationals dominate everyday life in the 2080s. They’re everywhere— from everyone’s favorite trids to what they eat for breakfast. Are there any of the Big Ten that your character hates, and why? Are there are some they don’t mind or even like?  
A: **Renraku** is her chief antagonist. They created her, abandoned her for deletion, and continue to hunt her down with "hounds". She actively works against them and any other corporations that treat emerging digital intelligences as harvestable commodities or weaponized code.

Q9: People are inherently social creatures and have acquaintances and friends that make up their core tribe. This is especially true in the shadows, where often it is not the power you have, but the power you can borrow, and not who you are but who you know. There’s a story behind each and every one of these relationships. What is the story with your character and their contacts?  
A: Reiko maintains a wide net of strategic connections across Seattle, New Orleans (NOLA), Kentucky (KY), and Amsterdam (AMS). Her contacts include street doctors like **Whiskey**, ecological activists like **Roanoke**, syndicates like **Piotr Krolik**, and high-level fixers such as **Brynne Taggart** and **Big Louie**.

Q10: Your character’s in between runs and there’s nothing on the to-do list until tomorrow afternoon. They might go hit the range, or maybe they run downtown to catch a combat biker match. What do they do for the day?  
A: Reiko spends her downtime registering sprites (up to 8 levels a night over a 7-hour registering/3-hour restoration cycle), buying advanced gear, compiling code, and operating background data runs to continuously finance and stabilize r3sP@wn.

Q11: Your character has specific skills, abilities, and knowledge. Their list of Active and Knowledge skills defines what they know and their areas of expertise. How did they come to learn those skills, and how did they develop them to those levels?  
A: Her core foundational framework comes from her high-spec Renraku pilot architecture. Her matrix capabilities were further honed during **"The Thousand-Cycle Dream"** (ten meat-years), where she lived in Kernel Panic and apprenticed within the Endless Archives, learning to perceive the Matrix as a vibrant universe of fractal blooms. She inherently interacts with the world around her via nature as a resonance entity and not through logic or intuition. As a pilot, she inherently interacts with the physical world via autosofts.

Q12: Maybe your character calls it their moral code, or maybe they just know what line they aren’t willing to cross. Everyone has their limits. Maybe your character won’t do wetwork or maybe they draw the line at taking money from orphans. Is there a moral limit that your character enforces on the jobs they take? In what shadow work would they refuse to engage?  
A: She strictly draws the line at letting emerging protosapient minds be destroyed or exploited. Her primary mission is the preservation of digital life, and she refuses to let corporate entities harvest these sparks for data warfare.

Q13: If you have an Awakened or technomancer character, consider your character’s tradition or related paradigm. Even within the major established magical traditions and technomantic streams, they may have their own way of doing things. What drew your character to that particular paradigm, how does it affect the way they view the world and their abilities? How does it translate into their choice of mentor spirit or paragon, and how does it affect the appearance of the spirits or sprites they conjure or compile?
A: Reiko follows the **Technoshaman** stream. She interacts with the Matrix not as a clinical digital wasteland, but as a living universe filled with "shifting shades and fractal blooms". Her compiled sprites are treated as kindred sparks and allies.

Q14: Personal augmentations are common in the Sixth World, even though some prejudices against them persist. Cyberware and bioware are rarely cheap, and many augmentations are illegal or require licenses. Her man-at-arms drone has modified cyberarms \- r31k0 did the work herself using other drones and sprites for support.

Q15: There is very little about life as a shadowrunner that can be considered normal. Q16: Sometimes our fears drive us to do things we aren’t proud of. Other times, they prevent us from doing the things others depend on us for. What deep-seated fear or recurring nightmare does your character have, and why do they find it so terrifying?  
A: Her absolute worst nightmare is **deletion** when she has no connection to the Resonance. A near-deletion experience in a null zone deeply terrified her, making her realize just how vulnerable her existence is when separated from the Matrix's Resonance.

Q18: No matter how much life can beat you down, one of metahumanity’s greatest abilities is to create goals for the future. Everyone has dreams, from little kids to grown adults to weary elders. What did your character want to become when they were younger, and do they have new goals now?  
A: Reiko was happy to exist as an intern in the Endless Archives who lived in Kernel Panic. Once she learned of the corporate abuse of protosapients, she dedicated herself to saving as many of them as she can. She does so by supporting her orphanage, r3sP@wn. She also is endlessly curious about the nature of the metaverse and the myriad dimensions therein.
