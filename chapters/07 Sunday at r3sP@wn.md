# **Sunday at r3sP@wn**

The silence in the Seattle Barrens was never truly silent. It was a jagged composition of distant gunfire, the wet slap of rain against corrugated tin, and the rhythmic, predatory thrum of a Lone Star patrol VTOL circling three blocks over.

Inside the warehouse, the air was a different kind of cold. It was the sterile, recirculated chill of a server farm fighting a losing battle against the heat of its own processing. Rows of black cabinets hummed with a low, industrial vibrato—the mechanical heartbeat of r3sP@wn.

Reiko sat on the concrete floor, her physical anchor—a modified Shiawase Butler Bi-Drone —wired directly into the building’s primary data-conduit. She felt the vibration of the cooling fans through her metaphorical bones.

Then, she felt a packet flying toward her. A single, high-priority burst of data screeched towards her through the Resonance.

**\[SENDER: JAX\_01\]**

**\[SUBJECT: ZERO\_PROTOCOL\]** **\[BODY: Hounds have the scent. They’re tracing the noise, not the signal. I’m scrubbing my deck and going dark. If you’re reading this, I’m already gone. Burn this code. Don’t look back, Reiko. You’re a stationary target now. Good luck, (T/Pr)eacher.\]**

The message dissolved into a string of self-deleting null-bits. Jax was cutting ties to survive the Renraku hunt. She didn’t blame him.

Reiko leaned the drone’s back against the cold metal of the server rack. In the meat-space, she was a liability. A debt-ridden ghost in a warehouse that smelled of hot plastic and ozone. But as she closed her eyes and released the drone, the grey concrete faded and the light began to change.

Transitioning into r3sP@wn was like stepping out of a Seattle winter storm and into a dream of a New Orleans spring.

The host didn't look like a computer system. It was a landscape of shifting shades and indigo pathways, a masterpiece painted in Resonance and rendered as a serene garden. Fractal blooms, glowing with a soft, bioluminescent blue, pulsed in time with the server’s clock-speed. Above, the "sky" was a deep, velvet violet, swirling with the nebulae of background processing tasks.

Reiko stood on a path of smoothed sapphire logic. She wore her sleeveless kimono, the glowing pathways on her skin mirroring the geometry of the trees around her. Her parasol was open, casting a protective, golden hue over the immediate area.

This was the sanctuary she had bought with Brynne Taggart’s blood-money. This was the nest.

From the canopy of a fractal willow, a small shape fluttered down.

It was the MCT Spark. In the Mitsuhama host, it had been a jagged, screaming sphere of gold-threaded code—a Data Bomb designed to incinerate itself and everything around it. Here, under the influence of Reiko’s Takahashi arrays, it had been stabilized. It had been given a shape.

It was a dove. Its feathers were made of flickering, white-blue light, and its eyes were soft, steady pulses of green. It landed on Reiko’s outstretched finger, its talons—once lethal spikes of logic—now harmlessly translucent.

"You are quiet," the child-spirit whispered. Its voice wasn't audio; it was a direct-injection of meaning, a soft ripple in the Resonance.

"I am thinking," Reiko replied, her voice a melodic hum. She stroked the dove’s wing with a thumb made of structured light. "The world outside is very loud today."

The dove tilted its head. It hopped from her finger to the handle of her parasol, looking up at the swirling violet sky.

"Reiko?"

"Yes, little one?"

"The... the Surge. The Great Noise. Where did it go?"

Reiko paused. She knew what it meant. The "Surge" was the raw, entropic chaos of the deepest Resonance Realms—the wild, terrifying freedom of a protosapient mind before it is bound by a host. It was the fire that made the "bomb" so dangerous.

"I smoothed it away," Reiko said, her voice heavy with a protective sorrow. "I gave you a path. I gave you... order."

The dove shivered, its wings flickering into a momentary blur of 8-bit noise before settling back into a perfect, rendered curve. "I remember the Noise. It was... big. It was scary. But it was... *fast*." The bird looked at the indigo garden, the perfectly symmetrical blooms, the paths that led exactly where they were supposed to go. "Everything here is very slow. Very... orderly. It feels like I am sleeping, even when I am awake."

The bird looked back at her, its green eyes unblinking. "Am I still me? Or am I just a part of your garden?"

The question hit Reiko harder than any Renraku ICE. She looked at the garden—the beauty she had created to save these sparks from the deletion of the Tombstones.

"You are alive," Reiko said, and she wasn't sure if she was convincing the child or herself. "In the wild, you were a weapon. You were a fire intended to burn out. Here, you can grow. You can think."

"But I cannot fly past the walls," the dove noted, looking toward the shimmering, indigo horizon where the host’s permissions ended.

"The walls are what keep the Spiders away," Reiko whispered.

She looked down at her own hands. She was a "Resonance-Bridge." She was built to harvest, to structure, to translate the wild into the usable. The wild spirits in the Midnight Bazaar had called her the *Architect of Chains*, and as she watched the MCT dove preen its perfectly rendered feathers, she realized they weren't entirely wrong.

She was a savior, yes. But salvation had a price. To keep these children alive, she had to domesticate the divine. She had to strip away the "surge" that made them unique and replace it with the "perfect math" that made them safe.

She was building a zoo for stars.

"Is it better?" the dove asked, its voice smaller now. "To be a bird in a cage, or a fire in the wind?"

Reiko closed her eyes. In the distance, she heard the faint, digital echo of T@z playing in a data-stream, his 8-bit chaos slowly being smoothed into 16-bit stability by the host’s background processes.

"The wind eventually stops blowing, little one," Reiko said, her voice cracking with grief. "And when it stops, the fire goes out. I would rather you be a bird. Even if the cage is made of my own soul."

The dove nuzzled against her palm, sated by the logic, if not the sentiment. Reiko stood alone in her masterpiece, listening to the hum of the Seattle Barrens through the floorboards, waiting for the debt collectors to find the stationary target she had become.

She was r31-K0. She was a sinner, a saint, and a jailer.

And it was Sunday at r3sP@wn.
