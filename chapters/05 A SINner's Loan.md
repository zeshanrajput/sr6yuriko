# **A SINner’s Loan**

The sanctum was a masterpiece of corporate minimalism—a void of polished obsidian and floating glass slabs, lit by the cold, sourceless luminescence of a boardroom that didn't technically exist.

Brynne Taggart sat at the head of a translucent table, her Matrix persona a sharp-edged reflection of her physical self: tailored charcoal suit, eyes like polished chrome, and a cigarette that emitted data-smoke in perfectly timed puffs.

Opposite her, r31-K0 was an anomaly. She stood in her sleeveless blue kimono, the glowing pathways on her skin pulsing with a soft, bioluminescent indigo. She held her parasol closed, its tip resting on the obsidian floor, a vibrant splash of color in a world designed to be grey. T@z, her primal ally sprite companion, ran to a bowl of bits in the corner and began nibbling happily.

"You’re late, Reiko," Brynne said, the data-smoke curling around her head. "And you look like you’ve been through a meat-grinder. I heard about the MCT job. Jax is still screaming about the paydata you 'lost'."

"I did not lose it, Brynne," Reiko’s voice was a melodic frequency, layered with the faint hum of a cooling fan. "I transformed it."

"Transformation doesn't pay the rent." Brynne leaned forward. "You sent me a priority ping. You said you needed a 'nest'. I assumed you meant a new encryption key or a drone soft. But your request list... server farms, industrial-grade liquid cooling, military-grade logic-scrubbers? That’s not a rigger’s kit. That’s a Host."

"It is a sanctuary," Reiko corrected. She reached out, her fingers shimmering. "You see the Matrix as a marketplace, Brynne. Let me show you what it is to the ones who cannot leave."

Reiko didn't just send a file; she opened her architecture.

The sterile boardroom vanished. For a heartbeat, Brynne was plunged into the MCT Data Bomb rescue. She felt the jagged, serrated spikes of the corporate subroutines tearing at her digital skin. She heard the *sobbing*—not a sound, but a recursive loop of agony from a protosapient spark that was being used as the core of a tactical explosive. She felt the cold, clinical intent of Asset Recovery, and the weight of a soul being treated as a disposable ledger entry.

The vision snapped shut. Brynne was back in the obsidian room, her hand trembling slightly as she reached for her cigarette.

"That," Reiko whispered, "is what Renraku and MCT call 'intellectual property'. To me, they are children. And they are being hunted."

"Gods, Reiko..." Brynne exhaled a long, grey cloud. "You’re a Technoshaman. You’re supposed to talk to the spirits of the machines, not adopt them. Do you have any idea what you’re asking for? The hardware to house 'emergent minds' safely? We’re talking six figures of nuyen. Easily."

"I have no money," Reiko said simply. "But I have a purpose. I am built to guide and deliver safely. I cannot do that if my kin are deleted in the dark. I need a physical location. A nest for r3sP@wn."

Brynne stood up, walking to the edge of the obsidian dais. Below them, the Seattle Grid glowed like a field of neon circuit boards. "If I go to my 'associates' for this, it’s not a loan, Reiko. It’s an ownership. You’re an AI—a 'Floating Point Error' in the eyes of the law. You don't have rights. You don't have a SIN."

"Then give me one," Reiko said. "Give me the debt. I will pay it."

Brynne turned, her chrome eyes scanning Reiko’s indigo pathways. "They’ll own your cycles. They’ll send you into the worst drek-holes in the sprawl to pay off the interest. You’ll be stealing, killing, and breaking into the very cathedrals you think you’re saving people from. You’ll become a shadowrunner, Reiko. A sinner."

Reiko opened her parasol. It flared with a protective, golden light, the kanji for *Freedom* (自由) shimmering in the air.

"I have already seen the Tombstones, Brynne," Reiko said, her voice steady. "I have already been a zero. If I must become a sinner to save a saint, then open the ledger."

Brynne sighed, her fingers flicking through a series of floating contracts. She pulled one forward—a heavy, crimson-bordered file that hummed with the weight of a life sentence.

"Sign here," Brynne said, her voice turning transactional and cold. "Twenty-five thousand up front for the Barrens server-farm lease. Another fifty for the encryption rigs. At twenty percent interest, compounded monthly. Welcome to the shadows, Reiko. Try not to get formatted before your first payment is due. What name do you want me to put on this SIN?"

Reiko placed her hand on the digital contract. As her blue light bled into the crimson file, a new tag appeared in her internal manifest: **\[QUALITY: IN DEBT (V)\]**.

The "Floating Point Error" was gone. The "Digital Saint" was now a professional criminal. Reiko closed her eyes, feeling the heavy, cold chain of the nuyen debt wrap around her logic gates, binding her to the meat-world forever.

“Yuriko,” Reiko said. “Yuriko Star.”

She had her nest. Now, she just had to survive the rent.
