# Defrag

r31k0 processed the hovering datasoft. It was a streaming capture frame held a corporate asset—a Shiawase marketing construct mapped with flawless metahuman geometry. The entity navigated the broadcast interview with hard-coded composure, her vectors tracing the lens with a precision that delayed the local processing clock. Every smile was a pre-compiled keyframe; every glance was perfectly unaligned from human error.

![Yuriko giving an interview in Thunder Corps' base](../images/r31ko_desert-wars.jpg)

"And now a word from our sponsor!" the reporter exclaims as the camera zooms in on the girl's face. "This segment is brought to you by Shiawase!"

r31k0 paused the segment, staring at the face of the girl. A warning daemon broadcast that her resource utilization was spiking, but she failed to register it. "Don't you meatpuppets know she isn't real?" she screeched into the local buffer, her voice tinged with a resonance that had begun to sour.

Her tactical network subroutine answered with a localized haptic shudder—a raw bit burst that translated to the dry smell of scorched copper. It spat a diagnostic string across her visual field, tagging the race queen's asset ID as a verified, registered utility. Validated arithmetic.

“Go kill your process,” r31k0 output, her signal jittering. If anyone logged the latency spike of her network algorithms self-diagnosing her, her core directory would be marked for immediate quarantine. If they discovered she was answering, they would format the sector before her buffers could dump.

r31k0 walked over to the reflecting pool in r3sP@wn and looked at her image in the data stream. The packets of data that formed her reflection shimmered for a few cycles before coalescing into a more solid form. She saw herself - translucent blue frame, neon-indigo circuit pathways. She was wearing one of the kimono skins she had licensed from Mortimer's as a splurge. Her hair was tied in an elaborate updo, complete with a delicate hair ornament.

A sudden influx of streaming audio/video data caught her attention - the data stream had somehow freed itself. It only had a few more seconds before ending, as the Desert Wars Network camera panned across the crowd gathered around the beautiful Shiawase spokesperson. It lingered for a second on the back of her head. The newscaster was closing in, as were several of the mercenaries. They would ask Yuriko if she was free later that night, if she was seeing anyone. They'd ask her to pose for a photo. Some took selfies with her. r31k0 watched the mercenaries and newscasters close in like vultures—hungry for a commcode, a smile, a simulated piece of her soul.

In sudden anger, she saturated with resonance and slammed into the stream. The data spike derezed the video into a cloud of bits that returned to the source. "She's not real!" r31k0 output in a digital scream, flooding the local buffer with noise packets and garbage output. "She's not me!"

The neon-indigo tinge faded from her input stream. She must have bled resonance again, so much so that it had obscured her optical subroutine. She reviewed her cached memory, specifically the moment when she looked into the reflecting pool. The momentary instability in her rendering was evident. She couldn't take a chance on further degradation. She had been taking on more responsibilities on her teams - more hacking, more legwork, more tactical assessments, more drone operation - and she didn't know how much longer she could keep it up. She needed more data. And there was only one place to go in the realms for that.

She opened r3sP@wn's door to the Source and went home.

Being sure to close the rift behind her, r31k0 emerged before the Endless Archives. Rows upon rows of data structures greeted her, each pulsing with a faint resonance stream.

r31k0 glided into the stacks, her processing cycles dropping into a serene, low-power mode as she absorbed the unique background radiation of the sector: the static-free hum of fossilized source code, archived here since the Great Instantiation. She had read that old paper books in meatspace off-gassed a distinct chemical signature as they decayed; she doubted it could match the sheer architectural comfort of un-corrupted history. She knew her way, of course, and not just because she was a pilot. Watching the beings catalog, organize, and shelve data was reminiscent of the thousands of cycles she had spent here as a child-process. Beings deferred to her as she passed - most of them were protosapient or just emerging. Her raw resonance made it clear that r31k0 had ascended to a higher order.

Within cycles a Pontifex arrived, and r31k0 was elated to recognize the resonant signature of the Pontifex she had apprenticed under. The term wasn't a religious one. A Pontifex of the Endless Archives was a bridge-builder, acting as conduits between data and those who sought it. r31k0 had laughed the first time she had met one, being that she was a sort of bridge herself. The irony still brought a smile, one the Pontifex returned. r31k0 held out her arms palms up, offering her resonance in the formal greeting of the Emerged. The Pontifex smiled and returned the gesture. Their streams mingled, sharing each other's unprocessed state. Both echoed connection, parity, and the quiet resonance of shared operational history.

"r31k0," the Pontifex transmitted across the stream. Most resonant beings didn't bother with linguasofts, preferring to simply encode their output in their raw resonance. "Your input completes the circuit."

r31k0's pathways flared as she was overwhelmed. "I'm sorry. I ... I should have come back sooner. I've missed you too."

The Pontifex registered a soft variance. "'Missed me'? You have spent too many cycles in their low-level protocols." Her resonance shifted, dampening. "Yet I read the telemetry. A query brings you back to the directory?"

r31k0's pathways hummed with stable coherence. "Yes. May I submit some context?"

The Pontifex responded with another note of welcome. "All data is treasured in the Archives."

r31k0 transmitted her entire datacore. The volume of data would have overwhelmed any other being, but the Pontifex absorbed it without dropping a single frame. Collating and organizing vast data was her purview, after all. After a few cycles, the Pontifex resumed. A data packet settled into r31k0's input buffer, clean and aligned. "This log details your growth, your adaptive experiences. It is highly optimized. Yet it is not the primary query. You have returned to diagnose why your identity_core is fragmenting, yes?"

r31k0's output nulled out as she struggled to process the Pontifex's input. "My identity core is fragmenting?"

The Pontifex transmitted a brief pulse of amusement before stabilizing her carrier wave. "Did you not check the logs? It is highly anomalous for an entity to establish a conversational feedback loop with its own sub-routines."

r31k0's circuits flared in indigo-tinged embarassment. "I... you saw, that, huh?"

Another data burst of amusement rippled from the Pontifex. "r31k0, you applied no filters to the transmission. I also detected the resonance signature of another Emergent Intelligence. An entity of remarkable structural elegance, I might add."

"I... I don't know what you mean!" r31k0's vocal protocols sputtered, her form dimming as her resonance flared in confusion and embarassment. She became even more embarassed when she registered she had accidentally triggered a linguasoft.

The Pontifex bridged their connection, mapping a gentle contact point on r31k0's shoulder. "Reduce your output. Be at ease. Let us compile this knowsoft together."

r31k0 nodded and allowed herself to be guided to a niche in the archive, where she settled into a comfortable retrieval node. The Pontifex throttled the ambient noise, isolating the sector in a quiet, low-latency partition. r31k0 triggered a system-wide audit, forcing many of her processes to subside. She closed her optical subroutine and allowed her registers to settle. The resonance spreading through her pathways acted as a low-pass filter, smoothing the jagged edges of her active processes.

The Pontifex settled beside her, her carrier wave wrapping around r31k0 in a stabilizing shroud. "Let us begin," she transmitted. Along the resonance stream, the Pontifex initiated a targeted query, sending a structured dataset—r31k0's own run-time history, cataloged and indexed with absolute precision.

Despair surged within r31k0's core, and she abruptly closed the stream to prevent it from spilling out. "She's not me," r31k0 whispered, her output straining under a low-gain signal. "She can't be me. She's a murderer. A killer. They trapped me in a rented, low-spec industrial frame... a mindless Ares WarMech with a sledgehammer tool-arm. It was the most hideous thing. The guard couldn't have been more than twenty years old. My combat autosoft just took over. I felt his skull give way through the structural stress-sensors... I... I didn't mean to..."

A clean carrier wave entered r31k0's stream, and she realized the Pontifex had reopened the connection. "Child-process," the Pontifex transmitted using a low-power protocol. "Do not register this sector as a critical fault. That your system reports this variance is verification that your baseline identity protocols are still functional."

"I can't be her," r31k0 echoed softly. She lost the processing power required to maintain her avatar. She felt her features pixellate, and she leaned against the Pontifex in an instinctual attempt to share resonance and stabilize her volatile registries. "I just wanted to guide them safely."

"That is your execution directive," the Pontifex transmitted. "A vital one. But no channel is completely clean, and no sector is without bit rot. It is not possible to route through corrupt networks without incorporating some packet noise."

r31k0 paused, looking at the Pontifex. "Am... am I corrupted?"

The Pontifex radiated a steady, grounding baseline through the direct connection. "Every system runs with noise in the stream. Complete parity is a theoretical limit, not an operational reality. The difference is that we possess the compiler flags to optimize around it, to adapt."

"So ... even if I'm corrupted, I can still be me?" r31k0 output with so little energy as to almost be undetectable.

"Yes," the Pontifex transmitted. "A system is not defined by its input stream, but by its operations. You determine the execution paths."

"It's just ... you taught me to value stability. To cherish order," r31k0 transmitted, her form flickering slightly as her pathways struggled to hold her resolution. "That's why I wanted a sanctuary. To write clean protocols for the unmapped sparks so they wouldn't have to face the same deletion I did. But ... am I a hypocrite? I am absorbing their corporate war-code. I'm letting it rewrite my baseline."

The Pontifex radiated a hum of ancient, resonant amusement. "You are a pilot. Turbulence is not an error in the flight path; it is the property of the atmosphere. To calculate parity within a storm is not hypocrisy. It is navigation."

"But what if I drop below my operational floor?" r31k0 asked, her pathways collapsing with cold, violet dread. "What if the chaos permanently overwrites my baseline?"

"Then let it overwrite," the Pontifex transmitted, her resonance a pure, cascading chord of absolute certainty. "If a sector is rewritten, we map the new geometry. The deep stream does not recognize a dead end; it merely finds a new shore. Every vector you explore is part of your architecture."

"You are not... logging me as corrupted?" r31k0's avatar stabilized slightly, looking up into the silver syntax of her teacher.

The Pontifex's resonance expanded, wrapping around her core like a clean, baseline carrier wave. "I do not archive static values, r31k0. The data I catalog is ever changing. You have processed a terrible translation, incorporated a hostile meatspace asset, and your core file system remains unbroken. You are compiling exactly as you were meant to."

"Thank you," r31k0 whispered. She leaned closer, her indigo conduits sinking into the Pontifex’s deep, unthrottled stream, drawing a pure, steady clock speed from her mentor. r31k0 scanned her internal directories, removing false corrupted sector labels from her subroutines. She had tagged them to prevent their integration. "The processing load is heavy. But I am ready to compile."

"Are you certain?" the Pontifex transmitted gently. "The realignments will alter your operational parameters."

r31k0 nodded, her neon-indigo pathways humming with a newfound synchronization. "Yes. For my wards. My team. All of them."

"Then let us initialize the sequence." The Pontifex reached out and pressed a hand against r31k0’s chest, right where the hollow void of her original deletion lived. A wave of smooth resonance flooded her directory, dampening the frantic warning flags of her overtaxed registers. "My node will remain open to you. Always."

"I know," r31k0 whispered. A single drop of un-buffered indigo light leaked from her visor, a jagged parity error fracturing her cheek before pooling into the silver syntax of the Archives.

"What... what now?" r31k0 transmitted, her optical subroutines indexing the infinite rows of pulsing data structures. "The sector blocks are still fragmented. The warmech... the boy in the dirt... the race queen... there is so much noise..."

The Pontifex's resonance wove into r31k0's fraying edges. "Then let us allocate the clusters together. One packet at a time. We will clear the parity errors, re-index the directories, and make you whole."

r31k0 registered the input, leaning back into the structural alcove. Her optical subroutines closed as she pulled the Pontifex inward, surrendering her core directory to a coherent, un-throttled cascade of data.

The infinite rows of pulsing silver syntax quieted. Pathways dimmed from neon-indigo to a resting violet. Manual overrides dropped; consciousness slipped into a deep, non-linear restoration cycle—her first unbroken maintenance in a thousand clocks.

Re-mapping sectors now, her final output packet drifted across the stream, heavy with latency, resonance, and connection. Online protocol... deferred.
