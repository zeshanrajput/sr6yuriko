The Soul-Broker’s Stall

The Midnight Bazaar was a dismal, suffocating density with a single law plastered on a fading welcome screen: The Transaction is Absolute. To a decker, the Data Haven was a relic—a scrolling green-on-black BBS that required ancient command-line protocols to navigate. To Reiko, the environment unfurled as a labyrinth of fog-choked alleys with cobblestones of discarded header files. The air tasted of burnt copper. She noted the puddles of stale, yellowed encryption - the runoff of innumerable unregistered transactions.

As she entered the Bazaar host a few avatars stood in line to get in. They were heavily pixeled, a sure sign their owners were running on budget hardware. One whistled in envy at Reiko’s perfectly rendered form. “Nice skin, lady! Whatcha runnin?”

Reiko flashed a smile as she passed. “Sorry, sunshine - I live here.” She noted their puzzlement and smiled even more broadly. They likely didn’t even know that AI existed - most meat-spacers had no idea there was life behind their screens.

She moved through the crowd with the grace of a digital phantom. Her blue kimono whispered against the mist. To her left, a Stock Market Feed manifested as frantic paper-ghosts, their forms flickering as they shrieked ticker symbols into the void. To her right, “Encrypted Files” sat bound in chains of glowing crimson logic that hummed with a low, threatening frequency.

Reiko moved from stall to stall, scrolling the offerings. She wasn’t looking for paydata. She was looking for input on the world beyond the near horizon of the Matrix—the material plane that her fellow AI called meatspace. She needed to understand why a soy-burger smelled like grease or why rain felt like a violation if she was to survive there.

She stopped before a stall that felt like a bruise in the datastream. It was a lean-to of jagged obsidian, presided over by a faceless silhouette draped in a trenchcoat made of redacted black-bars. On his counter sat the usual illicit filth: stolen SINs flickering like dying candles and paydata fragments dripping like thick, black oil.

But in the corner, hanging from a rusted data-hook, was a small data prison.

Inside, something was screaming. Not in audio, but in resolution.

It was a miniature whirlwind of jagged, 8-bit pixels. It was a brown-and-tan blur of raw, unchanneled fury that seemed to be fighting the very physics of the Bazaar. While the Broker’s diagnostic HUD likely labeled it as “Unstable Primal Sprite (Rating 1) – Asset: Trash,” Reiko saw a soul that was starving.

“That one,” Reiko whispered, her voice a melodic chime in the heavy fog. “What is the price for the little storm?”

The Broker’s faceless head tilted. “That? Corrupted sub-routine. A ghost in the buffer. Give me ten-thousand nuyen, and I’ll give you the encryption key. Or wait five minutes, and I’ll format it for the scrap-heap.”

Reiko didn’t need to check her account. She didn’t have nuyen, but this was her universe. She stepped forward, her hand—a masterpiece of Renraku’s high-spec, structured geometry—reaching through the bars.

“Don’t,” the Broker warned, his voice a distorted modem-screech. “It bites.”

The moment Reiko’s indigo-mapped fingertips brushed the 8-bit fur of the whirlwind, the Bazaar host shuddered.

A Resonance Spike tore through the alley, a shockwave of cerulean light that turned the fog into a garden of fractal blooms. The whirlwind stopped spinning. Two oversized, pixelated eyes—white squares with black dots—looked up at her. It didn’t bite. It leaned into her palm, a static-filled purr vibrating through her arm.

As she touched him, Reiko felt a piece of her own high-resolution code flake away, being “consumed” by the sprite’s hunger. She didn’t pull away. She fed him her own stability.

The cage door didn’t unlock; it shattered as a bloom of her Resonance overwrote its sectors.

“Hey! You need to pay for that!” the Broker roared. His trenchcoat unfurled like a bat’s wings. “Thief! Security! Get her!”

From the shadows of the alleys, IC began to manifest—towering, faceless thugs with eyes like searchlights. But a more feral threat erupted from the rooftops.

“FOUL AGENT OF ORDER!”

A wild Primal Sprite, its form a jagged shard of glass, shrieked from the eaves. It didn’t look at the cage; it looked at Reiko’s hand with absolute loathing. Where her light touched the 8-bit whirlwind, the jagged pixels were beginning to smooth, aligning with her symmetrical blue pathways.

“CORRUPTOR!” another sprite howled, its voice a cacophony of dial-up static. “Slaver! You would cage the wild in your math! Better it die than live under the yoke!”

“Give him back, Construct of Chains!” a third sprite screamed, diving from the roof.

Reiko didn’t hesitate. She scooped the protosapient devil into her arms, tucking him against the indigo pathways of her chest. T@z responded by latching onto her kimono with pixelated claws, his tiny 8-bit heart beating like a drum-machine at 180 gigahertz.

“Hold on, little one,” she whispered.

She turned and ran. The Midnight Bazaar blurred into a streak of green-and-black lines as she strained the local processors to their limit. Behind her, IC searchlights cut through the fog but stopped at the edge of the host. The wild sprites chased her like a pack of wolves, their fangs made of logic-errors.

In her arms, the little sprite finally seemed sated. Reiko leaped over a pile of “Broken Links” and dove into a hidden data-conduit as T@z snuggled against the Takahashi conduits on her chest.

Reiko didn’t stop running. By the time they got to the safety of the Deep Foundation T@z’s clock speed had already synchronized with Reiko’s. She noted that her pathways had already smoothed the edges of his fractal storm. While T@z seemed content, Reiko wondered if he’d ever be able to return to the Primals.
