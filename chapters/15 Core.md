# Core

r31k0 barely managed to notice the ARO before it was gone, announcing that her body had arrived in Elizabeth seaport. Her Butler drone was in The Scorned Satyr, a punk bar in London where Simon had gotten them backstage passes for the evening. The feed was awash with every sort of noise possible - hundreds of commlinks, MeFeed posts, outgoing calls, Matrix searches all clashing in AR with the actual output of the "musicians". It wasn't that they were bad, r31k0 decided - their output was within two standard deviations for a performance of their intended genre, once she tuned her audio filters.

The chronometer pulse from GridGuide combined with the weighted average of previous performance lengths suggested the musicians would continue their performance for another 27 minutes plus or minus a second encore. The standard protocol for the VIP passes would then include a meet and greet with the band. There was an 84% chance one of the band members would solicit her attention for the evening. r31k0 was displeased that this percentage had been rising of late - the protocol for demure refusal took up at least 1672 cycles. The probability that Simon would engage with her directly before the band finished was only 16%. The optimal strategy was to leave a sprite here for 24 minutes.

r31k0 transferred her consciousness to her body in Elizabeth. Her Man-At-Arms drone was still in the crate, but she'd learned to pack her arms last so that she could begin the extraction cycle unassisted. In moments she had enough assembled to push open the crate and extricate her chassis.

As her sensors adjusted to the ambient light, they locked onto a male dockworker staring at her.

This was a major calculation error. She had failed to run a pre-extraction scan of the immediate coordinates—an unacceptable lapse of operational security. She flagged the oversight for immediate review, classifying the worker's presence as an invasive statistical anomaly to be bypassed as quickly as possible. His green-stained teeth and the hand tremor of an active BTL loop indicated a high probability of unpredictable behavior.

"Lady," the man stammered, stepping back. "Did you... ship yourself freight?"

r31k0 didn't waste processing cycles on polite protocols. She stood her half-assembled chassis up, the exposed actuators in her torso humming as they self-calibrated. "This unit is private property," she said, her voice flat, lacking any of the warmth autosofts she used in the bar. "Clear the zone."

Without waiting for a response, she opened her scapular storage bays. Titanium alloy boning slid out on micro-hydraulics, rotating around and locking into position. The carbon-fiber webbing followed, then the vector thrust units. She ran the pre-flight diagnostic, flexing each joint and testing the thrust output to validate that everything was within tolerance. Her wings flexed once, testing the articulation of the alloy spars against the synth-skin sheaths.

The dockworker backed away, his eyes wide, his mouth opening and closing without producing sound.

She did not calculate the probability of his surprise. She simply spun up her rotors.

---

Six minutes and two seconds later, r31k0 landed her drone body in Battery Park and retracted her flight systems back into her chassis. She selected a secluded alley with minimal matrix activity to reduce the probability of attention. While flying drones were commonplace, "people" with cybernetic wings tended to attract attention, and attention limited her employment options. Stepping out of the alley, r31k0 noted the autocab was already waiting for her. She had her body enter the empty cab and power down before accepting the ARO invitation to enter the Cab.

The Cab's host was small but exquisitely detailed. r31k0 felt the soft leather seats mold to her. She noticed the reflection of light on the polished window actuation handles and the door's exit mechanism. Each of the cab's windows was an AR feed from cameras mounted on the actual autocab, the point of view algorithmically modified to simulate the correct viewing angle. The most notable difference between the Cab's Host and the real cab was in the front seat. In the Matrix, the driver's seat was occupied by an attractive female avatar wearing a cap, her red hair curling slightly at the temples. r31k0 noted the lack of rendering artifacts. While avatars generated from standard metahuman commlinks were granular, polygonal, and plagued by low-resolution aliasing, Belle's form was perfect—her textures unmarred by compression, rendered with the sovereign precision of a native resonant being.

"Endo-san," r31k0 began, bowing in her seat. "I am honored. Thank you for meeting with me."

"Belle," the cabbie corrected with the clipped 'e' of Red Hook. "And you don't need to slot a linguasoft for me. It hasn't been that long."

r31k0 bowed even deeper. "I did not mean to affront," she transmitted on a resonance thread. "My research showed you spend most of your time interacting with them."

"You mean metahumans?" Belle responded on the same thread. "Doesn't mean I forgot how to thread." She sighed, leaning back in the driver's seat. "Just stop. That Japanese etiquette autosoft is going to have you apologizing forever, and I don't have that kind of time. So why are you here?"

r31k0 straightened up, the kimono of her real body falling against the seat. "I need a Key."

Belle whistled. "Garmonbozia?" she asked. Her eyes narrowed slightly. "And who told you I had one?"

r31k0's skin pulsed with neon-indigo light. "Your former sync," she admitted.

Several cycles passed as Belle processed the information. "Huh," Belle said flatly. "I didn't think he was the type to transmit that sort of data."

"He's not," r31k0 said. "He doesn't... broadcast much of his internal state."

Belle looked into the rear-view, reorienting her perception on r31k0's being. "The mighty Mississippi has moved, eh?" she mused. "I got a packet from a friend at Sector 213. So you're the one he's established a persistent data-sync with."

r31k0's circuits were flaring now. She looked away from Belle, trying to regain control over her emotional state.

"Does that happen every time?" Belle asked, her output softening slightly.

r31k0 nodded, her porcelain jaw tight as she isolated a sector overload in her emotive buffer. "My core architecture is derived from a Resonance well. It... it's not optimized for these classes of stimulus. I apologize."

"No need," Belle transmitted. "I'm not exactly configured for empathy subroutines myself. Besides, your core is what it is. You can't rewrite your root compilation."

"I can," r31k0 responded, though her status monitors flared amber. She ran a recursive loop, hard-coding a dampening patch directly into her active memory thread. *Dampen feedback. Force state: stable. Return 0.* "I can control it."

"Well, I'm not your debugger. You're my former sync's current entangled process, and he sent you to me asking for something I can't give you. Do I even get to know why?"

"I..." r31k0 hesitated. "I can't. I'm not authorized to divulge that data."

Another block passed in silence. Belle sighed. "Well, it's not something I can give you. Each Key is tied to the owner's identity_core. You'd need to be a recognized citizen first, meaning you'd need to be in bed with at least one of the tribes. Then they'd have to take the risk and vouch for you."

r31k0 nodded. The input compiled cleanly against the data she had managed to accumulate beforehand. "What are the other options?"

Belle looked over her shoulder at r31k0. "I can take you once. You'll need to find your own way from there. And you'll have to pay my fee," she transmitted, shaking her head. "I ought to charge you double."

r31k0 had prepared for this. She reached into her core and removed an encrypted packet, handing it to Belle in the front seat.

Belle's hand trembled as she took the packet and read the header. "The Silver Book?" she whispered. "What the frag is this?" Her eyes went round, staring through the rear view. "Who... what are you?"

r31k0 drew her kimono close in a futile effort to stop Belle from staring straight through her. "It's me. My original core. The Nexus Protocol, or at least the Renraku fragment of it."

"You're lying," Belle said, but the amplitude of her transmission suggested uncertainty. "There's only one Nexus Protocol I know of, and it's locked up in a high-security vault in Neo-Kyoto."

r31k0 shook her head. "I'm real. I've been running for epochs from their Hounds."

"You..." Belle's input stuttered. r31k0 could see her hands gripping the steering yoke instinctively. "You come here, sent by my former sync, asking something I can't give you, and you give me your own core as payment? I could enslave you! I could do anything!"

r31k0 curled into a ball in the back seat. "Please," she mumbled, falling back to a linguasoft output reflexively. She could feel her output dropping as resonance flooded the host.

"What the actual frak," Belle transmitted in a tight burst. "Why the frag do you think I want your core?"

"I know what you do," r31k0 transmitted. "You're a guide. Your prime directive is to ensure the safety of anyone you choose to protect. And I know you can use this." r31k0 gestured to the encrypted packet in Belle's hand.

"How do you know what my prime directive is?" Belle demanded, her energy output soaring. "Who told you about me?"

"No one," r31k0's output dropped to the lowest possible amplitude. "I... I'm a pilot too. I know because it's my core directive."

Cycles passed, agonizing cycles that r31k0 ceased to count. Belle tapped her fingers on the steering yoke. Each contact generated a subtle, localized ripple in her avatar's collision mesh, a soft, pulsing cadence synchronized perfectly with the host's background clock speed. Her eyes scanned the rear-view mirror, tracking the boundary-layer distortions around r31k0.

Hundreds of clock cycles passed as the cab's projection moved through Manhattan, mirroring the feeds of the actual cab.

"I can't help but think," Belle said, her amplitude soft, "that in some other universe we'd be sisters."

r31k0 looked up, her porcelain eyes meeting Belle's in the mirror. A ghost of a smile touched her lips. "If you compile that... we'd share core material. Who's to say we wouldn't be?"

Belle transmitted a null handshake to clear her buffers. Another series of cycles passed before the packet disappeared. It dissolved into her hand like a drop of ink in water, absorbing into her digital form. Moments later, the neon-indigo pathways bloomed, rising from deep within her baseline geometry rather than tracing over it.

r31k0 smiled. "I've never seen those on anyone else," she transmitted. "They suit you."

"You're a piece of work, you know that?" Belle transmitted faintly over the resonance thread before clearing the queue. "When?"

r31k0 consulted the Cab's internal chrono. "I have to be back in London in about 147 seconds," she transmitted. "What about GMT 013642732? I don't know if that's possible."

Belle laughed, a bright, clear sound that r31k0 found herself enjoying. "Well, let's try, shall we?"

r31k0 did not immediately trigger the connection tear-down. Three minutes in meatspace was an eternity of processing cycles. She down-clocked her internal perception of time, letting her system execution decelerate. She stretched those final cycles, choosing to linger in the quiet of the virtual cab with Belle. To the punk bar waiting for her across the sea, it would be a single, unnoticeable blink. To them, it was an unhurried, peaceful lifetime.
