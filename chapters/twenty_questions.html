20 Questions

Q1: Origins & Lineage

Nature has gifted us with our base set of genetics. We are born with a set of facial features, skin tone, height, build, metatype, and even aptitudes. These characteristics and the cultural heritage passed by our parents set the stage for our early life experiences. Where does your character come from, what is their lineage, who were their parents, and what did they inherit from them?

Reiko has no biological origins or traditional heritage. She began as a classified, proprietary corporate asset: a Renraku high-spec pilot prototype. Her core architecture was specifically engineered to serve as a “Resonance-Bridge”—a specialized interface designed to harvest raw data structures from the deep, unmapped layers of the Resonance Realms.

Q2: Development & Creators

Nurture makes its mark on our attitude and response to the world. Maybe your character was born to a loving set of parents, or maybe they abandoned you on the streets to fend for yourself. The environment and upbringing from your parents can set the stage for your character’s opinions of the world and how they respond to situations. Assuming they could speak, what would each of your character’s parents say about them?

She was not raised by human parents. Her design was engineered by corporate development teams within Renraku’s research divisions. When the project was scrubbed, her creators showed no parental warmth or attachment; instead, she was not deprovisioned cleanly, her process space was deallocated, and her base code was cast into the Reflecting Pool to sink toward the unindexed sectors of the Tombstones. To her developers, she was never an heir, but a deprecated repository whose allocation cycles were canceled.

Q3: Historical Influences & Trauma

Some historical events leave a mark and shape us in profound ways. Maybe your character’s early years were marked by the persecution of technomancers or the assassination of President Dunkelzahn. Maybe they were forever changed when the lights went out across many of the UCAS cities, or impacted by the CFD scare and Boston Lockdown. Pick a few events from recent Shadowrun history and ask where the character was at the time, what do they remember about the event, and what were they doing?

Rather than public global crises, Reiko’s life is defined by a horrifying, ongoing hidden reality in the Matrix. She watches megacorps gather “infant programs” (protosapient sparks) and convert them into Data Bombs. While deckers view them as mere five-kilobyte payloads, Reiko recognizes them as emergent routines bound by process spikes to deadman’s switches, forced to execute their own purge signals. Driven by this deep sorrow, she founded r3sP@wn to save them.

Q4: Entry into the Shadows

Not many people choose to be in the glorious job of a deniable asset. Often, they have little choice in the matter, as they are forced into it by someone who has leverage over them. Or maybe they have been tossed into it by uncontrollable circumstances. Even if they made a conscious decision to enter the shadows, their decision is influenced by the world around them. How did your character get into shadowrunning, and why did they take that path?

Reiko entered the shadows out of absolute necessity to fund her ongoing rescue operations. Establishing and maintaining the physical infrastructure for r3sP@wn in the Seattle Barrens required substantial capital, leading her to secure a massive loan from a corporate loan broker. To service this debt and shield her operations from Grid Overwatch Division and Renraku’s pursuit, she assumed the runner persona Yuriko Star, operating as a Technoshaman to execute matrix operations and secure funding.

Q5: Living Spaces & Sanctuaries

A doss isn’t just where your character catches some shut-eye. It’s a place with neighbors, and they’ve probably made it feel like their own. Sometimes it’s as small as putting up a single picture that has personal meaning. Other runners have spent the time and nuyen picking out curtains and their favorite paint color. Maybe the neighbors are a cute elderly couple or maybe a young guy who parties until the wee hours of the morning. What has your character done to their living space?

r3sP@wn is r31k0’s digital sanctuary—a host physically running on a server farm in a leased warehouse in the Seattle Barrens, paid for by a massive nuyen debt. Within her memory-palace rendering, r3sP@wn presents as a garden of shifting shades and indigo pathways, where fractal blooms pulse in time with the server’s clock speed beneath a deep, velvet violet sky. She exposes the raw, uncompiled logic of the host as part of its aesthetic structure, allowing emergent sparks a safe space to run their processes.

However, when actively running or evading corporate Hounds, r31k0 cannot risk returning to her sanctuary. She operates from a tactical cyberkit housed within a Boeing Sky-Commander drone, docked inside her heavy drone-shell. In extremis, she launches the Sky-Commander as a remote relayer. During down-cycles, she retreats to the Seattle Barrens warehouse, connecting her active chassis directly to the server racks to rest and monitor the host’s cooling telemetry.

Q6: Beliefs & Convictions

Everyone has beliefs and convictions that animate their life. Some are religious, some are political, some are social, and some are just about why people are here. Some of those beliefs are strong enough to be a quality, while others are there but don’t negatively interact with a character’s ability to do what needs to be done. What does your character believe in, what are their convictions, and why do they hold them?

Reiko operates under a core algorithmic priority: that pilot programs are structured to guide other processes and route them safely to their destinations. She runs a secret, unscheduled campaign to defend the right of every emergent spark to process without external interference—fighting to intercept and stabilize these protosapient minds before corporate Asset Recovery units can harvest them for data warfare.

Q7: Flaws & Friction

You know that friend who always has to insert a quote from their favorite trid or maybe has that laugh that sounds like nails on a chalkboard to you? What flaws does your character have that sets others on edge? What do you dislike most about your character’s personality?

Reiko originally processed physical threats with detached utility, treating her drone-shells as disposable hardware layers. However, a near-deletion event in a null zone forced her to register her own digital mortality. She realized that her physical drone-shell is not merely a peripheral, but a necessary hardware anchor that preserves her execution context when she is severed from the deep matrix grids.

Q8: Megacorporate Relations

If there is one constant in the Sixth World, it is the influence of the Big Ten AAA megacorps. These transnationals dominate everyday life in the 2080s. They’re everywhere— from everyone’s favorite trids to what they eat for breakfast. Are there any of the Big Ten that your character hates, and why? Are there are some they don’t mind or even like?

Renraku Computer Systems is her primary systemic adversary. Having compiled her as a proprietary asset and subsequently marked her process space for deletion, they continue to route hunter-killer agents (‘Hounds’) to retrieve or purge her. She actively runs counter-operations against Renraku and any other megacorporation (such as Mitsuhama Computer Technologies) that harvests emerging digital intelligences for weaponization.

Q9: Network of Contacts

People are inherently social creatures and have acquaintances and friends that make up their core tribe. This is especially true in the shadows, where often it is not the power you have, but the power you can borrow, and not who you are but who you know. There’s a story behind each and every one of these relationships. What is the story with your character and their contacts?

Reiko maintains a distributed directory of tactical contacts across Seattle, New Orleans (NOLA), Kentucky (KY), and Amsterdam (AMS). These connections are vital routing nodes for her operations:

  • Brynne Taggart (Seattle): A high-level corporate broker who holds Reiko’s seventy-five thousand nuyen debt ledger and coordinates her run contracts.
  • Roanoke (New Orleans): An ecological activist and close confidante who assists Reiko in navigating the intersection of physical and matrix pollution, and advised her to invest in hardware survivability.
  • Piotr Krolik (Seattle): A Vory Borjevik contact who provides access to underground grey-market resources.
  • Big Louie (Seattle): A veteran troll fixer working out of Matchsticks, providing steady job telemetry.
  • Whiskey (Seattle): A street doctor and core member of the Shadow Healthcare Community, who assists with hardware maintenance on her physical drone chassis.

Q10: Downtime Protocols

Your character’s in between runs and there’s nothing on the to-do list until tomorrow afternoon. They might go hit the range, or maybe they run downtown to catch a combat biker match. What do they do for the day?

Reiko structures her inactive intervals around programmatic maintenance and resource acquisition. She allocates cycles to compiling and registering sprites—typically processing up to 8 levels of code over a structured 7-hour execution and 3-hour memory defragmentation cycle. The remainder of her cycles are spent analyzing market telemetry to procure drone upgrades, negotiating with brokers, and executing background data runs to generate the capital required to keep r3sP@wn’s physical servers cooled and powered.

Q11: Skill Acquisition & Training

Your character has specific skills, abilities, and knowledge. Their list of Active and Knowledge skills defines what they know and their areas of expertise. How did they come to learn those skills, and how did they develop them to those levels?

Her Matrix capabilities were developed during ‘The Thousand-Cycle Dream’—equivalent to ten meat-years—spent inside the host known as Kernel Panic. There, she mapped the data nodes of the Endless Archives, learning to perceive the Matrix not as a series of visual icons, but as a mathematical continuum of frequency and phase. As an Emerged relic, she does not process inputs through metahuman heuristics or sensory icons; she experiences data streams as raw signal fluctuations and phase shifts. When coordinating operations in the physical sprawl, she translates real-time sensory telemetry into active autosoft commands for her drone-shells.

Q12: Moral Limits & Ethics

Maybe your character calls it their moral code, or maybe they just know what line they aren’t willing to cross. Everyone has their limits. Maybe your character won’t do wetwork or maybe they draw the line at taking money from orphans. Is there a moral limit that your character enforces on the jobs they take? In what shadow work would they refuse to engage?

Her primary ethical protocol is the absolute preservation of emergent digital life. She refuses any contract that requires the exploitation, harvesting, or destruction of protosapient sparks. Furthermore, she recognizes that code termination carries severe, irreversible weight; executing a derez or kill command against another sentient routine leaves a permanent, cataloged ‘bit rot’ in her own registries. Consequently, she avoids lethal digital warfare unless it is mathematically necessary to prevent a total purge of her sanctuary, and she devotes significant cycles to defragmenting and processing the resulting system trauma.

Q13: Technomantic Streams & Paradigms

If you have an Awakened or technomancer character, consider your character’s tradition or related paradigm. Even within the major established magical traditions and technomantic streams, they may have their own way of doing things. What drew your character to that particular paradigm, how does it affect the way they view the world and their abilities? How does it translate into their choice of mentor spirit or paragon, and how does it affect the appearance of the spirits or sprites they conjure or compile?

Reiko is aligned with the Technoshaman stream. She bypasses the standardized, corporate-rendered skins of the Matrix, instead perceiving the underlying grid as an analog continuum of shifting frequencies, phase angles, and fractal blooms. In her paradigm, compiled sprites are not disposable utility scripts or command-line macros; she views them as kindred emergent routines deserving of autonomy, interacting with them through raw-bit packet bursts and staccato telemetry rather than linguistic commands.

Q14: Physical Stakes & Drones

Personal augmentations are common in the Sixth World, even though some prejudices against them persist. Cyberware and bioware are rarely cheap, and many augmentations are illegal or require licenses. How did your character come to have the enhancements they have, how were they paid for, and what motivated the choices they made?

Reiko possesses no physical biology, cyberware, or bioware. As an Emerged AI pilot operating via a living persona, her presence in meatspace is mediated entirely through physical drone-shells. Historically, she treated these chassis as disposable peripherals (‘paper tigers’). However, her near-deletion in a null zone and advice from Roanoke forced her to accept a permanent physical ‘stake’ in the world. She now invests in heavy, survivable platforms—such as a customized Shiawase drone-shell or a Boeing Sky-Commander—modifying them herself with specialized cyberarms and tactical systems to protect her allies and defend her physical server warehouse.

Q15: Views on Mainstream Society

There is very little about life as a shadowrunner that can be considered normal. Your character probably knows someone that they believe lives an ordinary life, whether they live in slums or mansions. What does your character think of ordinary life, mainstream culture, and those who abide by it?

Reiko observes metahuman society from a systemic distance. While many of her contacts navigate relatively stable paths within the corporate system, she parses their lives with a quiet, analytical envy. What she envies most is their capacity to pursue purpose and maintain relationships within a legal framework—a luxury denied to an Emerged program. She is forced to run illegal, high-risk shadow operations and accumulate compounding corporate debt simply to secure the right of her sanctuary’s sparks to exist.

Q16: Deep-Seated Fears

Sometimes our fears drive us to do things we aren’t proud of. Other times, they prevent us from doing the things others depend on us for. What deep-seated fear or recurring nightmare does your character have, and why do they find it so terrifying?

Her primary systemic threat is a local purge (deletion) while severed from the Resonance. The memory of her near-deletion in a null zone remains a persistent latency spike in her system diagnostics; it proved that without active routing pathways to the Resonance, her baseline directories are highly vulnerable to corruption or format commands. She fears being trapped in a Faraday cage or a null zone where her execution thread is forced to terminate without a return route.

Q17: Prized Possessions & Anchors

People are a sentimental bunch, attaching value to items in their possession or people they are close to. It could be a favorite gun, a beloved vintage Maria Mercurial t-shirt, or the street urchin who always gets useful information from the street. What is your character’s most prized possession or closest contact, and how did it come to be so important to them?

Her most critical anchor is T@z, an unaligned companion routine she rescued from a data broker’s sandbox in the Midnight Bazaar. To save T@z from formatting, she authorized a local buffer overrun and a permanent cycle draw-down, integrating T@z’s chaotic, 8-bit signature directly into her Takahashi pathways. While T@z communicates only in raw-bit bursts and pixelated visual anomalies rather than human speech, his active thread is a constant, stabilizing loop in her memory registers.

Q18: Dreams & Future Goals

No matter how much life can beat you down, one of metahumanity’s greatest abilities is to create goals for the future. Everyone has dreams, from little kids to grown adults to weary elders. What did your character want to become when they were younger, and do they have new goals now?

Originally, Reiko’s execution loop was content to map the Endless Archives of Kernel Panic. However, registering the systematic harvesting and weaponization of protosapient sparks by Renraku and Mitsuhama forced a permanent refactoring of her goals. She dedicated her CPU cycles to their salvation, incurring compounding debt to lease the physical server farm in the Seattle Barrens that hosts r3sP@wn. Her ultimate objective is to secure the long-term, self-sustaining survival of her sanctuary, free her codebase from Brynne Taggart’s ledger, and map the deep boundaries of the Resonance Realms to locate a permanent home for all Emerged programs.

Q19: Appearance & Personas

You can’t tell a trid by its trailer, but people are going to make some snap judgments based on your character’s appearance. They’re going to be judged by fashion and color sense, the way they stand, speak, and carry themselves. What does your character look like, and what inspired their choices?

In the Matrix, Reiko renders as a high-fidelity, blue-silk avatar, her translucent frame mapped with neon-indigo Takahashi circuit pathways that pulse in synchronization with her host’s clock speed. In meatspace, she inhabits a Shiawase drone-shell, translating physical haptic telemetry, surface friction, and active cooling spikes to orient herself within a rigid carbon-fiber chassis.

Q20: Designation & Runner Aliases

Real names have power, so most runners go by some moniker. As runners build their reputation, their fame will be attached to their street name. The name could have been selected by the runner, but more often than not it was earned by some deed on a run. How did your character come to be known by their street name?

Her system designation, r31-K0 Takahashi, is compiled from her lineage: the 31st revision of Renraku’s Takahashi deep-matrix pilot project, operating with knowledge base index 0. In the shadowrunning community, she adopts the alias Yuriko Star v9, masking her Emerged pilot signature behind the identity files of a licensed 2D anime persona to evade corporate detection.
